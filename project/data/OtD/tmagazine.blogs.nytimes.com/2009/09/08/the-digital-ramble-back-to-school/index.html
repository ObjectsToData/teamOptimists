The Digital Ramble | Back to School

By ROSECRANS BALDWIN
September 8, 2009 4:19 pmSeptember 8, 2009 4:19 pm
Bettmann/Corbis The sweet agony of back-to-school day.


The Digital Ramble explores aesthetic topics through materials found online.

In my book, the calendar year should kick off in September. I loved going back to school: even when I was acne-covered and misunderstood, school was still appealing. I’d be miserable, but so was everyone else.

Among the Web’s obscure corners that summon that new Trapper Keeper smell, this collection of “red-hot and filthy library smut” is spot on. Less titillating than it sounds (but not much), it features sumptuous photographs of library interiors, shot by Candida Höfer. As the site’s writer notes, “It’s like porn for book nerds. Seriously.”

The Nonist Eye-popping “library smut” photographed by Candida Höfer.

I always hated grammar. But I like this collection of misspelled signs, and also The “Blog” of “Unnecessary” Quotation Marks. Another site, The Wonder of Whiffling, gathers interesting words and phrases (“push the peanut – to progress an arduous and delicate task forward”), and even better is this clever illustration of American Sign Language using matchbooks. I feel schooled — or like I just witnessed a triple lindy.

JK Keller Didactic matchbooks.

In the September issue of Harper’s Magazine, Mark Slouka makes a ferocious defense for the humanities (and pillories its apologists) that should warm the cockles of any sad English major in debt. Highly recommended. For example:

By downsizing what is most dangerous (and most essential) about our education, namely the deep civic function of the arts and the humanities, we’re well on the way to producing a nation of employees, not citizens. Thus is the world made safe for commerce, but not safe. We’re pounding swords into cogs. They work in Pyongyang too.”

A lot less high church than Slouka is the image-pool Web site What’s for School Lunch?. Self-explanatory: pictures of school lunches from around the world, proving that every child is miserable around noon (except the Japanese).

What’s For School Lunch A truancy-inspiring school lunch.

The platoon behind AVGeeks.com rescues “old 16mm school films from Dumpsters and obscurity.” They have over 22,000 educational videos available online. Productive hours can be lost learning, for example, about proper playground courtesy:

My classroom memories are wallpapered with enormous world maps. This assortment of African soil maps is selected from a much bigger project. I may not know my Pleistocene from my Pliocene, but they’re beautiful.

Can’t have school aesthetics without athletics. Rugby doesn’t have much of a following in the United States, but it was big when I was a student in South Africa, and many around the globe would like to see it reintroduced to the Olympics. In the movie below, the filmmaker Keith Lutit uses “tilt-shift” filming to get a super-flat look, as though the world is his miniature train set; here’s his recent rugby project, “Origin II.”



I grew up close enough to my schools to walk or bicycle to class. For the ladies, here are 10 “Gorgeous Bicycles for Stylish Ladies.”

As the fall rolls forward, weekend warriors of all stripes will be mothballing their gear. The following trailer for “Dear & Yonder,” a new surf movie about “an ocean odyssey of the female kind,” has a great melancholic vibe. Even the soundtrack, “Been So Long,” by Vetiver, suits the mood. So long, summer.
