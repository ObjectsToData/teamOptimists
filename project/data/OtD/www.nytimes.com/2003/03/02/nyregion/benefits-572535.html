BENEFITS


Published: March 2, 2003

Families at the Library

TODAY -- A family party at the main New York Public Library will raise money for its book collections. Activities will include dancing, storytelling, a puppet performance and a chance for children to make their own books. The party begins at 1 p.m. with refreshments and concludes at 4. Tickets, $125 for adults and $75 for children, at the door.

On Pointe

TOMORROW -- A black-tie dinner and performance by students of the School of American Ballet, at the New York State Theater at Lincoln Center, will raise money for scholarships to the school and for its programs. Violette Verdy, the prima ballerina, will be honored. Cocktails at 6:15 will be followed by dinner at 7:15 and the performance. Tickets, $500, from (212)769-6615.

Open Spaces

TUESDAY -- Attorney General Eliot Spitzer will be among those honored at the Seamen's Church Institute, 241 Water Street (Peck Slip), to raise money for the Neighborhood Open Space Coalition, which advocates increasing green space in the city, and for Friends of Gateway, which supports New York's Gateway National Recreation Area. Cocktails and hors d'oeuvres will be served beginning at 6, and the party concludes at 8:30. Tickets, $100, from (212)352-9330, or $125 at the door.

For the Humanities

TUESDAY -- Joan Ganz Cooney will be honored at the W New York Hotel to raise money for the New York Council for the Humanities and its programs, including teacher training, sponsorship of humanities projects at cultural institutions throughout the state, support of family literacy efforts and the awarding of college scholarships. Cocktails at 6:30 will be followed by a black-tie dinner at 7:30. Tickets, $500, from (516)681-2037.

A Dance Premiere

TUESDAY -- A premiere of Paul Taylor's ''Promethean Fire'' and other works at City Center will raise money for the Paul Taylor Dance Foundation. The organization's efforts include classroom residencies and performances for families and children. The black-tie evening begins with the performances at 7:30, with dinner and dancing to follow about 9:30 at the Waldorf-Astoria. Transportation between the show and dinner will be provided. Tickets, $500, from (212)431-5562.

Singing Outdoor's Praises

WEDNESDAY -- Harrison Ford will be among those honored at a dinner at Pier 60, Chelsea Piers, to raise money for the Natural Resources Defense Council and its environmental advocacy. Caroline Rhea will be the host, and Vonda Shepard will perform. Drinks at 6:30 will be followed by dinner at 7:30. Tickets, $600, from (212)727-2700.

Against AIDS

THURSDAY -- An opening-night cocktail party at the Architectural Digest Home Design Show, at Pier 94, 12th Avenue at 55th Street, will raise money for Design Industries Foundation Fighting AIDS, which provides support to H.I.V. and AIDS service and education programs. The show opens at noon, and the cocktail party begins at 7. Tickets, $50, from (212)727-3100.

Animal Health

THURSDAY -- A black-tie dinner dance at Doubles, in the Sherry-Netherland Hotel, will raise money for the cancer care unit at the Animal Medical Center. Cocktails at 7 will be followed by dinner at 8 and dancing. Tickets, $500, from (212)874-5457.

Preview of New Art

THURSDAY -- A preview of the International Fair of New Art, at Piers 88 and 90 (12th Avenue, between 48th and 50th Streets), will raise money for the exhibition fund of the Museum of Modern Art. Cocktails and hors d'oeuvres, which will be served throughout the evening, begin at 5 p.m. for $1,000 ticketholders, and at 5:30, $500 ticketholders may enter. People with $250 tickets may enter at 7. All tickets, and information, from (212)708-9680.

Helping Pregnant Girls

THURSDAY -- A dinner-dance at Essex House will raise money for Inwood House and its services to pregnant teenagers and their families. Drinks at 6:30 will be followed by dinner at 7:30 and dancing to the music of the Kit McClure Band. Tickets, $300, or $200 for those under 30, from (212)861-4400 ext. 239.

A Winter Dance

THURSDAY -- Educational programs at the American Museum of Natural History will benefit from a black-tie dinner dance at the museum. Cocktails at 7:30 will be followed by dinner about 8:30 and dancing at 9. Tickets, $500, or $175 for dancing only, from (212)769-5165.

No Mind Wasted

THURSDAY -- A black-tie dinner at the Sheraton New York will raise money for the United Negro College Fund, which provides scholarships and fellowships to African-American students. Drinks at 6 will be followed by dinner at 7. Tickets, $1,000, from (212)838-2660.

Backing Up the Force

THURSDAY -- A dinner-dance at One Police Plaza will raise money for the New York City Police Foundation and the police programs and equipment it provides to the police force, which in the past has included bulletproof vests and horses in the mounted unit. Drinks at 6:30 will be followed by dinner and dancing to the Emery Davis Orchestra at 7:30. Black tie or dress uniform. Tickets, $1,000, from (212)751-8170.

Antiques on Parade

THURSDAY -- A preview of an international antiques show at the Seventh Regiment Armory will raise money for the pediatric service programs of the New York University Medical Center supported by the Kids of N.Y.U. Foundation. Cocktails and hors d'oeuvres will be served beginning at 6. Tickets, $150, from (212)404-3683. The show, with admission of $15, opens to the public Mar. 7.

A Hand for Sick Children

THURSDAY -- A dinner at the New York Marriott Marquis will raise money for the Starlight Children's Foundation of New York, New Jersey and Connecticut, which works to improve the lives of critically, chronically or terminally ill children through entertainment programs, the granting of wishes and outpatient activities. Drinks at 6 will be followed by dinner at 7 and dancing. Tickets, $600, from (212)354-2878.

A Black and White Ball

SATURDAY -- A black-tie ball at Cipriani 42nd Street will raise money for the volunteer programs of the New York Junior League. Al Roker and Deborah Roberts will be the hosts. Drinks at 7 will be followed by dinner at 8 and dancing. Tickets, $350, or $150 for dancing and dessert after 10 p.m., from (212)288-6220, ext. 267.

Aiding Mental Health

SATURDAY -- A dinner-dance at the Hilton Garden Inn, 1100 South Avenue in Bloomfield, Staten Island, will raise money for the Staten Island Mental Health Society and its diagnostic, treatment, education and prevention programs. Drinks at 7 will be followed by dinner at 8. Tickets, $185, from (718)442-2225, ext. 215.

Educating Young Actors

MARCH 10 -- A black-tie dinner at the Plaza will aid the National Actors Theater and its educational programs, including high-school teaching. Tony Randall will be master of ceremonies. Drinks at 6:30 will be followed by dinner at 7:30. Tickets, $1,000, from (212)748-3511.

Bidding on a Tango

MARCH 10 -- Artwork by Al Hirschfeld, James Rosenquist and others, as well as a private performance by Peter Duchin and tango lessons with a member of Ballet Hispanico, will be among the items auctioned at Christie's to raise money for the Center for Arts Education. The center works to enhance arts education in the New York public schools through program development and financial support. Drinks and hors d'oeuvres at 6:30 will be followed by dinner at 7:45 and the auction at 8:50. Tickets, $750, from (212)414-2993.

Party at F.A.O. Schwarz

MARCH 11 -- A bunny-hop at F.A.O. Schwarz will raise money for the pediatric department at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. Activities will include a discothèque for children, entertainment by the Rockettes, face painting, magic shows and a tattoo booth. The party begins at 7 with refreshments for children and adults, and concludes at 9. Tickets, $125 for adults and $50 for children under 17, from (212)639-7975.
