THE GUIDE

By ELEANOR CHARLES
Published: September 28, 2003

Guilleux Exhibition

The paintings of Herve Guilleux with their rough brushwork, lots of black, maybe a little white, evoke French farm life that few people ever see, except for those who live it. The harsh reality of family farming that is light years away from global agribusiness is portrayed with compelling honesty and artistry by this young artist.

Mr. Guilleux's work received its first exposure to the public when he showed it on the streets of Paris eight years ago. Since then his exhibitions at the PKNY Gallery, Station Square in Kent, have garnered glowing reviews and sold out. His current show will run through Oct. 12. The gallery is open Friday through Sunday from 11 to 5 or by appointment. Information: (860)927-4152.

Reiner's Bedtime Story

Carl Reiner, a 12-time Emmy Award winner, comedian and, among many other projects, the creator of The Dick Van Dyke Show, is now directing his attention to children. His new work, a book inspired by his grandson who likes scary bedtime stories, is ''Tell Me a Scary Story -- but Not Too Scary.''

Mr. Reiner will read passages in his own comical way to children who attend his appearance at the R.J. Julia Bookstore in Madison at 2 p.m. today. The shop is at 768 Post Road and admission is free. Reservations are required: (203)245-3959.

Lipizzaner Stallions Show

An infrequent opportunity to watch the graceful white Lipizzaner stallions perform their intricate dressage drills is scheduled Saturday at 2 and 7:30 p.m. at The Arena at Harbor Yard in Bridgeport.

The small but powerful and agile horses, of Berber and Arab descent, began to be trained in dressage in Austria around 1580 in what became known as the Spanish Riding School. By the 18th century the school was established as the Austro-Hungarian Empire's Imperial Stud in the town of Lipizza, with Vienna as the seat of the Lipizzaner dressage demonstrations.

In recent years the horses have performed for more than 20 million people in North and South America, Europe, Australia and Hawaii.

Ticket prices range from $17.50 to $26.50 through Ticketmaster. Information: (203)345-2400.

Arlo Guthrie in Bridgeport

Arlo Guthrie, a folk singer and songwriter like his father, Woody Guthrie, came into adulthood in the volatile 1960's, a happenstance that imprinted his career with a commitment to music that embraced social causes. He will appear today at 4 p.m. at The Klein auditorium, 910 Fairfield Avenue in Bridgeport, with guest artists Abe Guthrie, his stepson; his daughter Sarah Lee Guthrie and Johnny Irion.

Arlo Guthrie's early claim to fame was ''Alice's Restaurant,'' a comic monologue set to music in 1965 at the Newport Folk Festival, for which he earned a Gold Record. Tickets are $25, $30 and $35. Box office: (203)345-4800, extension 150.

Strega Prize Readings

Readings by Enzo Siciliano, Domencio Starnone and Melania Mazzucco all Strega Prize winners, will open Yale University's celebration of the 57-year old Strega Prize, Italy's most prestigious literary award, on Friday at 4 p.m. in the Beinecke Library, 121 Wall Street, New Haven.

A symposium Saturday, beginning at 10 a.m. at the Whitney Humanities Center, 53 Wall Street, will feature a panel of speakers addressing the subject ''The Twilight Zone: Contemporary Italian Narrative Between Prose, Poetry and Art.''

Panelists include Arnaldo Colasanti, Mr. Siciliano, and Raffaella Morselli, with Paolo Valesio, professor of Italian at Yale, serving as moderator.

The Strega Prize originated in 1944 in Rome after World War II, when a group of writers, intellectuals, artists and musicians calling themselves the Sunday Friends got together for discussions about Italian cultural life.

Guido Alberti, owner of the Strega liqueur company, provided the money and the name for the prize in 1947.

The public is invited to the readings and symposium free of charge.

Jazz by Elvin Jones at Yale

The Elvin Jones Jazz Machine will appear under the auspices of the Duke Ellington Fellowship at Yale Friday, 8 p.m. at Sprague Hall, College and Wall Streets on the New Haven campus. Mr. Jones, one of the most innovative drummers in contemporary jazz, will be joined by Gerald Cannon on bass; Anthony Wonsey, piano; Mark Shim, saxophone, and Delfayo Marsalis, trombone.

Mr. Jones, who backed Charlie Parker in Detroit the late 40's, migrated to New York, playing with most of the jazz legends including Miles Davis, Tommy Flanagan, Milt Jackson, Lou Hayes, Yusef Lateef, Charles Mingus, Bud Powell, the Pepper Adams-Donald Byrd Quintet and John Coltrane.

Tickets are $20, $30 and $35, students $10. Information: (203)432-4158.
