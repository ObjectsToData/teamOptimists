BENEFITS


Published: April 28, 2002

When Actors Need a Hand

TOMORROW -- James M. Nederlander will be honored at a black-tie dinner at the Sheraton New York to raise money for the Actors' Fund of America, which helps entertainment professionals who have fallen on hard times. Cocktails at 6:30. Tickets, $750, from (212) 245-6570.

Aiding the Cancer Society

TOMORROW -- Queen Noor of Jordan will be an honoree at a dinner at the Pierre to help the American Cancer Society. The black-tie evening begins at 7 with cocktails. Marvin Hamlisch will perform. Tickets, $500, from (212) 237-3888.

Harvest for the Needy

TUESDAY -- Members of the New York Giants are to be honored at a black-tie dinner-dance at the Pierre to raise money for City Harvest, the antihunger group. Cocktails at 7. Tickets, $500, from (212) 874-5457.

Cultural Scenes

TUESDAY -- Excerpts from ''The Guys,'' an Anne Nelson play now at the Flea Theater, will be performed at a benefit dinner at the Regent Wall Street, 55 Wall Street, for the Lower Manhattan Cultural Council. The black-tie evening begins with cocktails at 6. Tickets, $550, from (212) 683-4023.

Premiere to Aid Guggenheim

WEDNESDAY -- The premiere of Matthew Barney's film ''Cremaster 3'' at the Ziegfeld Theater, 141 West 54th Street, will support the Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum. Cocktails with Mr. Barney at 6:30, with the screening at 7:30. Tickets, $500, or $100 for the screening alone, from (212) 423-3581.

To Help Violence Victims

THURSDAY -- The National Domestic Violence Project, an effort by the American Academy of Facial Plastic and Reconstructive Surgery to provide free surgery to victims of domestic violence, will benefit from a party at the Boathouse in Central Park. Drinks at 7, with dancing to the Sultans of Swing. Tickets, $175, from (914) 779-0066.

Dinner With the Dinosaurs

THURSDAY -- A black-tie dinner at the American Museum of Natural History will raise money for the museum's educational and research programs. The evening begins at 6:30 with drinks. Sumner M. Redstone will be honored. Tickets, $1,500, from (212) 888-7003.

Helping Jewish Research

THURSDAY -- A dinner at the Pierre will raise money for the YIVO Insti tute for Jewish Research. Cocktails at 6, dinner at 7. Frank O. Gehry, the architect, and Representative Nita M. Lowey will be honored. Tickets, $600, from (212) 246-6080.

Children With Children

THURSDAY -- A buffet at the New York Marriott Brooklyn, 333 Adams Street (Willoughby Street), will aid Project Teen Aid Family Services, which helps homeless teenagers who are pregnant or have children. Drinks at 6, dancing after dinner. Tickets, $100, from (718) 330-0845, ext. 10.

An Evening of Tasting

THURSDAY -- A tasting dinner and a raffle at the House of the Redeemer, 9 East 95th Street (Fifth Avenue), will aid the Yorkville Common Pantry's program to give food to the needy. The evening begins at 7. Tickets, $250, from (212) 410-2264.

Reading Favorite Things

MAY 6 -- Julie Andrews, David McCullough, Margaret Atwood and Jonathan Franzen will read from their works at a black-tie dinner at the Vivian Beaumont Theater at Lincoln Center to help the programs of Literacy Partners. Cocktails at 6. Tickets, $700, from (212) 573-6933.

Performance for City Center

MAY 6 -- City Center, at 131 West 55th Street, will raise money for its presentations and educational programs with a performance of ''The Pajama Game.'' The evening begins at 6 with cocktails and dinner, with the show at 8 and a party with the cast at 10:30. Tickets, $1,250, or $500 for the show and the party alone, from (212) 247-0430, ext. 202.

Honoring a Producer

MAY 6 -- Dino De Laurentiis is to be honored at a dinner and screening of Fellini's ''Nights of Cabiria'' at the Brooklyn Academy of Music. The event will aid the academy's repertory film program, festivals and film education, and Audrey Cohen College in Manhattan. Cocktails and dinner at 6 precede the screening at 8 and a dessert reception at 10. Tickets, $500, from (718) 623-7800, ext. 3.

Fighting Antigay Bias

MAY 6 -- The Lambda Legal Defense and Education Fund, which fights discrimination against gays, will benefit from a dinner at Regent Wall Street, 55 Wall Street (William Street). Anna Quindlen is to be honored. Cocktails at 6:30.. Tickets, $300, from (212) 809-8585, ext. 221.

'Success' and the Humanities

MAY 6 -- A benefit for the New York Council for the Humanities will include the 8 p.m. performance of ''Sweet Smell of Success'' at the Martin Beck Theater. The evening begins with dinner at 5:30 at Barbetta, 321 West 46th Street. Tickets, $250, from (212) 233-1131, ext. 28.

Baskets of Goodies

MAY 6 -- Picnic baskets of treats from New York restaurants will be served at a benefit for Housing Works, which helps homeless people with AIDS. The evening, at the Altman Building, 125 West 18th Street (Seventh Avenue), begins with cocktails at 7. Lea Delaria, a comic and jazz singer, will perform. Tickets, $250, from (212) 645-8111, ext. 110.

Learn the Merengue

MAY 6 -- Ballet Hispanico will present an evening of merengue at the Plaza to raise money for scholarships to the organization's school and for its educational dance and Hispanic cultural programs. The evening begins with drinks and merengue lessons at 7. After dinner, members of the company will perform. Tickets, $500, from (212) 362-6710, ext. 25.

Clinton's Work for Peace

MAY 6 -- Bill Clinton will be honored at the New York Hilton for his work for international peace at a dinner given by the Interfaith Center of New York. The event will benefit the center, which works to promote understanding among people of different backgrounds. Cocktails at 6:30. Tickets, $500, from (212) 675-9474.

A Tribute to Coppola

MAY 7 -- Al Pacino, Diane Lane, Matt Dillon and Martin Scorsese will offer tributes to Francis Ford Coppola at a black-tie benefit at Avery Fisher Hall for the Film Society of Lincoln Center. Drinks at 5:30 p.m., followed by the screening and the tributes at 7. Dinner will be at Tavern on the Green. Tickets, $900, or $75 for screenings alone, from 212 875-5630.

For Community Center

MAY 7 -- Marvin Hamlisch will perform at a dinner-dance at the Pierre to benefit the Educational Alliance, a social services group and Jewish community center in Lower Manhattan. The black-tie evening begins with cocktails at 6:30. Tickets, $500, from (212) 838-2660, ext. 18.

Teaching for America

MAY 7 -- Peter Jennings will be the host of a dinner at the WaldorfAstoria for Teach for America, which recruits college graduates to teach in low-income rural or urban schools. Cocktails at 6:30 p.m. Tickets, $600, from (212) 675-9474, ext. 10.
