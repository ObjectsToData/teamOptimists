LONG ISLAND GUIDE

By BARBARA DELATINER
Published: October 25, 1998

Best Bet

Greenlawn was once the ''Pickle Capital'' of the state, with Pickles on the Vine, a variety of cucumber, covering its agricultural acreage and dozens of processing plants along the railroad tracks. Now only one local farm is raising the crop, but that doesn't stop the Greenlawn-Centerport Historical Association from holding its annual Pickle Festival today. The fair, featuring pickles prepared the old-fashioned way by association members, as well as other homemade delicacies, is from 1 to 4 P.M. at the local library on Broadway (754-1180).

Free Choices

TODAY To celebrate Humanities Month, the Long Island Arts Council presents ''A Pilgrimage on the Road of Stars to Santiago de Compostela,'' a lecture by Dr. Alfonso J. Garcia Osuna, who teaches Spanish culture and history at City University of New York and Hofstra University. The talk details his experiences on an ancient road in northern Spain that dates back to biblical times. The talk is at 2 P.M. at the Freeport Library on South Ocean Road (223-2522).

TUESDAY Nassau Community College begins ''Truth and Consequences in D.C.: A Series on the Clinton-Lewinsky-Congressional Issue,'' with Elizabeth Holtzman, the former Representative from New York, who served on the House Judiciary Committee during the Nixon impeachment hearings. She will speak at 11 A.M. at the College Center on the Garden City campus (572-7158).

With a new book on the market, ''The Virtues of Aging,'' former President Jimmy Carter is making the obligatory book-selling tour, which brings him to the Book Revue on New York Avenue in Huntington at 1 P.M. (271-1442).

THURSDAY Joining in Share Our Strength's ''Writers Harvest: The National Reading,'' Barnes & Noble outlets on the Island hold readings at 7 P.M. and will donate a percentage of sales during the programs to groups fighting hunger and poverty. Bernard Waber, the creator of the ''Lyle, Lyle Crocodile'' books, appears in the Carle Place store on Old Country Road, while students from the Manhasset Middle School read at the Manhasset branch on Northern Boulevard (747-7234).

SATURDAY ''Music for Montauk'' returns with a concert featuring Stephanie Chase, violinist; Alan Stepansky, cellist, and Max Barros, pianist, in a program including works by Bach, Beethoven and Handel. The free performance, at 7 P.M. at the Montauk School on South Dorset Road (668-2474), is to be followed by a benefit reception and dinner at $85 (668-2196).

At the Libraries

Today's musical lineup includes ''From Broadway to Bach,'' performed by the Choral Pops of Long Island (formerly the Choraleers) at 2 P.M. at the Bethpage Library on Powell Avenue (931-3907); ''Around the World in Music and Song'' from David Goldman, singer and songwriter, at 2 P.M. at the Lindenhurst Library on Lee Avenue (957-7755); ''Latin and Afro-Cuban Music,'' by Napoleon Revels-Bey and the Rhythm Kings, at 2 P.M. at the Hicksville Library on Jerusalem Avenue (931-1417), and chamber music, presented by the Hamptons Bays Library, at 3 P.M. at St. Mary's Episcopal Church on Ponquogue Avenue (728-6241). A lecture, ''How to Read 5,000 Books a Year'' by Christopher Lehmann-Haupt, senior daily book reviewer of The New York Times, at 2 P.M. at the Long Beach Library on West Park Avenue (432-7201).

Tomorrow, at the Port Washington Library on Library Drive, John O'Hagan screens and discusses ''Wonderland,'' his documentary about the 50th anniversary of Levittown (883-4400). On Wednesday, Robert Schapiro, an antiques dealer and a member of the International Society of Appraisers, conducts an appraisal clinic (two items per participant) at 7:30 P.M. at the Half Hollow Hills Library on Vanderbilt Parkway in Dix Hills (421-4530). On Friday, the New York Grand Opera Company performs ''An Evening of Opera'' at 8 P.M. at the Port Washington Library (883-4400).

From the Chambers

Colleagues, collaborators and former students of the founder of the Bach Aria Group gather today for a ''Memorial Tribute to Samuel Baron.'' Preceded by a panel discussion at 12:30 P.M., the concert at 2 P.M. features performances of Bach arias by members of the Bach Aria Group; Mr. Baron's transcription of the Brahms Piano Quartet in G minor (Op. 25), performed by Gilbert Kalish and the New York Woodwind Quintet, and works of George Crumb, Mario Davidovsky, Meyer Kupferman and Ezra Laderman performed by former students of Mr. Baron. The concert is in the Recital Hall of the Staller Center on the Stony Brook campus of the State University

(632-2787).

Five more chamber concerts make it a banner week for devotees of the genre. Also today, under the auspices of the Garden City Chamber Music Society, Judith Pearce, flutist, and Nelson Padgett, pianist, play Handel, Ravel and Schubert at 3 P.M. at the Unitarian Universalist Congregation on Stewart Avenue in Garden City (248-8855). North Shore Pro Musica offers Mozart, Holst and Schubert at 5 P.M. at the Three Village Garden Club Sanctuary on Bates Road in Setauket

(584-8945). The Staller Center's Recital Hall has ''Computer Music at Stony Brook VII'' at 8 P.M. (632-2787). Friday brings the Haydn Quartet, joined by Rosario Marciano, pianist, and Wolfgang Harrer, double bassist, playing Goldmark, Schubert and, of course, Haydn, at 8 P.M. at the Monroe Lecture Hall on the Hempstead campus of Hofstra University (463-6644), and, in a change of pace and music, Le Trio Gershwin, a Parisian group, playing, you guessed it, Gershwin, at 8 P.M. again at Staller Center's Recital Hall

(632-2787).
