Choose Some 'Good Old Days' And See How They Really Were

By J. D. BIERSDORFER
Published: June 4, 1998

WANT to prove to your wisecracking, history-challenged progeny that ''Roll Over Beethoven'' -- and not old Ludwig himself -- was all the rage when you were born?

For an instant dose of personal nostalgia, just beam your Web browser to the dMarie Time Capsule (dmarie.com/asp/history.asp). Once there, type in a birthday or other significant date. The Time Capsule will reward you with a quick recap of the top headlines for the three months surrounding that date and the sports achievements and most popular songs from the year. You can find out other ephemera from that period, including average income, the prices of a stamp and a gallon of milk, and Academy Award winners. The site offers specific data for the years 1900 to 1997, although some facts from 1995 to 1997 are not available yet.

The Time Capsule comes courtesy of dMarie.com, a do-it-yourself site that lets its users compile and lay out their own virtual scrapbooks and also features chat rooms, discussion boards and a poetry database. ''The information here is to serve as a fun, reminiscent stroll through past days,'' reads a disclaimer on the Time Capsule page. ''Use it to put together a heritage page for your scrapbook -- NOT for your school history report!''

While the Capsule provides a soft-focus-lens look at a certain time, surfing scholars looking for events occurring on a particular day may need to dig a little deeper. The History Channel's Web site has an on-line version of its popular ''This Day in History'' television spot (historychannel.com/thisday). After selecting a date, users are treated to a page of historical and pop-culture events that took place on that day. For instance, on this day in 1939, the first grocery-store shopping cart was introduced in Oklahoma City. June 4 is also the birthday of England's King George III (in 1738), and ''Love Me Do'' by his future countrymen the Beatles was topping the American pop charts on this date in 1964.

Yahoo also boasts an extensive list of time-specific tidbits on its Today in History page. It includes a link to the Library of Congress's Today in History site, illustrated with items from the library's collection. More focused efforts, like the Literary Calender, Mathematicians Throughout the Year and Today in Movies, also round out Yahoo's collection (www.yahoo.com/Arts/Humanities/ History/TodayinHistory).

The brief synopses these history pages offer -- particularly if you search, say, on your high-school graduation date -- are enough to transport you, emotionally at least, back in time. And if you can't remember all the words to what were your favorite songs, you might find them at the Lyrics Library Homepage (web2.kw.igs.net/wgarvin/ lyrics.html). You can find all the words to ''American Pie'' there.
