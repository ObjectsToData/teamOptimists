BENEFITS


Published: March 1, 1998

Fostering Futures

WEDNESDAY -- The Community Literacy Research Project and more than 50 fashion companies are sponsoring a dinner and dance at Avery Fisher Hall. The money raised will help the organization build a youth center in Manhattan. The evening will begin with drinks at 6 and will include performances by two dozen young people who have participated in the All Stars Talent Show Network, a performance program run by the literacy project. Tickets, $1,000, from (212) 941-9400.

Cards for a Cause

FRIDAY -- The North General Foundation will hold its third annual Bid Whist Tournament to raise money for the North General Hospital in Harlem. The evening at the hospital, 1879 Madison Avenue (122d Street), will begin with cocktails and a buffet at 5:30. The card games will begin at 7. Tickets, $250 to play, or $150 to watch, from (212) 423-4468.

Helsinki on the Hudson

FRIDAY -- The Finlandia Foundation, which sponsors cultural and educational exchanges between the United States and Finland, will benefit from a black-tie dinner and dance at the Waldorf-Astoria. The evening begins with a reception at 7:30. Tickets, $175, or $105 for full-time students, from (212) 983-5272.

Networking

SATURDAY -- The junior committee of the Partnership for the Homeless will hold its annual fund-raiser from 8 to 10 at System, a dance club at 76 East 13th Street (Broadway). The event will raise money for Network, the partnership's job training and placement program, and will include an open bar, hors d'oeuvres, live music and dancing. Tickets, $50, from (212) 645-3444, ext. 102, or $65 at the door.

Gunplay at Lincoln Center

NEXT SUNDAY -- Patti LuPone, Peter Gallagher and Eli Wallach will participate in an evening of Western-themed musical theater at the Vivian Beaumont Theater to raise money for the musical theater programs at Lincoln Center. The show at 6:45 will be followed by a barbecue dinner at Tavern on the Green; transportation to the dinner will be provided. The organizers are asking guests to wear Western or casual attire. Tickets, $600, from (212) 501-3251.

Songs for Tibet

MARCH 9 -- Sheryl Crow, Natalie Merchant, Patti Smith, Philip Glass and the Drepung Loseling Monks will be among those who will perform at Carnegie Hall at a benefit for Tibet House. The evening will raise money for the organization's Tibetan cultural preservation efforts, for the construction of its new center on West 15th Street and for its free public educational programs. The concert at, which begins at 7:30, will be followed by a buffet supper at the Waldorf-Astoria. Tickets, $300, from 212 807-0563, or $25 to $100 for the concert alone from (212) 247-7800.

A Talent Show

MARCH 9 -- Tyra Banks, the model, will be the master of ceremonies for a talent show at Roseland, 239 West 52d Street, to benefit Safe Space, a program for homeless young people run by the Center for Children and Families. The evening will begin with Champagne and hors d'oeuvres at 6, followed at 8 by the show, which will feature performers who have participated in the Safe Space program. Tickets, $50, or $20 for the performance alone, from (212) 226-3536, ext. 226.

Preserving a Brooklyn Oasis

MARCH 9 -- The Prospect Park Alliance will hold a wine and antipasto party at Cucina Restaurant, 246 Fifth Avenue (Garfield Place) in Park Slope, Brooklyn. The evening will begin at 6, and the money raised will aid the organization's efforts at restoring and maintaining the park. Tickets, $50, from (718) 965-8988.

Wishes Granted Here

MARCH 9 -- The Starlight Children's Foundation, which grants wishes to seriously ill children and sponsors entertainment in hospitals, will hold its 13th annual dinner and dance at the New York Hilton. Richard Kirshenbaum and Jon Bond, advertising executives in New York, will be honored. Drinks at 6. Black tie. Tickets, $500, from (212) 354-2878.

Florentine Fun

MARCH 12 -- The Young Friends of the New York Public Library will hold a dance party with decor and entertainment inspired by ''The Decameron'' at the library's Center for the Humanities, Fifth Avenue and 42d Street. The event, which will run from 9 P.M. to 1 A.M., will raise money to buy novels and story collections for the research libraries. Wandering minstrels in period costume will entertain. Black tie; the library is also asking guests to wear 14th-century Florentine-style hats of their own design. Tickets, $125, from (212) 930-0730, or $175 at the door.

Royal Honors

MARCH 16 -- The Simon Wiesenthal Center will present King Hussein and Queen Noor of Jordan with its Humanitarian Laureate Award at its annual black-tie dinner. The evening, at the Waldorf-Astoria, will begin with a reception at 6.Tickets, $750, from (212) 888-7003.

Tribute to Dustin Hoffman

MARCH 19 -- The American Museum of the Moving Image in Astoria, Queens, will honor Dustin Hoffman at a black-tie dinner at the Waldorf-Astoria. Among the actors and directors who will reminisce about their work with Mr. Hoffman are Katharine Ross (''The Graduate''); Teri Garr (''Tootsie''); Barry Levinson (''Rain Man,'' ''Wag the Dog''), and Justin Henry (''Kramer vs. Kramer''). The event will benefit the museum's collections and its education and exhibition programs. Drinks at 7. Tickets, $1,000 from (212) 245-6570.
