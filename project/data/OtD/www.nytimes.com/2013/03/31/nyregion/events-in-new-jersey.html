Events in New Jersey

Comedy

MORRISTOWN Colin Mochrie and Brad Sherwood: The Two Man Group, improvisational comedy. April 4 at 8 p.m. $49 to $79. Manhattan Comedy Night, featuring up-and-coming talents. April 12 at 8 p.m. $25. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

PRINCETON David Sedaris, humorist. April 8 at 7:30 p.m. $42 to $56. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

RED BANK David Sedaris, humorist. April 4 at 8 p.m. $35 and $49.50. Colin Mochrie and Brad Sherwood: The Two Man Group, improvisational comedy. April 5 at 8 p.m. $19.50 to $65. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

Film

NEW BRUNSWICK “Cavalleria Rusticana/Pagliacci” in HD, recorded at the Teatro alla Scala. April 10 at 2 and 7 p.m. $7 and $12. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

NEWARK “Wattstax,” a 1973 documentary film by Mel Stuart that focused on the 1972 Wattstax music festival and the African-American community of Watts in Los Angeles. April 5 at 7 p.m. $23 and $25. Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

For Children

EAST RUTHERFORD “Disney Live! Mickey’s Music Festival.” April 14 at 1 and 5 p.m. $25 to $80. Izod Center, 50 Route 120. (201) 935-3900; izodcenter.com.

NEW BRUNSWICK Sesame Street Live, “Elmo Makes Music.” Through March 31. $14 to $75. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

RAHWAY “Fancy Nancy: The Musical,” based on books by Jane O’Connor. Through April 7. $25 and $30. Hamilton Stage, 360 Hamilton Street. vitaltheatre.org; (212) 579-0528.

WAYNE “If You Give a Mouse a Cookie and Other Story Books,” presented by TheatreWorks USA. April 2 at 10 a.m. $8. Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University, 300 Pompton Road. (973) 720-2371; wplive.org.

Music and Dance

CHATHAM An Intimate Acoustic Evening with Dave Mason and guest Caleb Hawley. April 5 at 8 p.m. $30. Sanctuary Concerts, Presbyterian Church of Chatham, 240 Southern Boulevard. sanctuaryconcerts.org; (973) 376-4946.

ENGLEWOOD Jerry Lewis, with film clips and songs from his career. April 4 at 7 p.m. $39 to $149. Charlie Daniels Band, with special guest the Dusty Gray Band. April 5 at 8 p.m. $39 to $99. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

MONTCLAIR “Danceworks 2013,” on the theme “Myth and Transformation.” April 4 through 7. $15. Shanghai Quartet with guest artists Ying Huang, soprano, and David Witten, pianist. April 12 at 7:30 p.m. $15. Alexander Kasser Theater, 1 Normal Avenue. (973) 655-5112; peakperfs.org.

MONTCLAIR Revolver Road to the Golden Gods tour, Stone Sour, In This Moment and Hell or Highwater. April 5 at 8 p.m. $32 and $35. Wellmont Theater, 5 Seymour Street. (973) 783-9500; wellmonttheatre.com.

MORRISTOWN The Beach Boys. April 6 at 8 p.m. $59 to $99. “Don Juan,” presented by the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra with Jean-Efflam Bavouzet, pianist, led by Susanna Mälkki. Works by Richard Strauss, Debussy and Messiaen. April 7 at 3 p.m. $20 to $88. For tickets: (973) 624-3713; njsymphony.org. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Boyz II Men, R & B. April 5 at 8 p.m. $30 to $65. “Don Juan,” presented by the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra with Jean-Efflam Bavouzet, pianist, led by Susanna Mälkki. Works by Richard Strauss, Debussy and Messiaen. April 6 at 8 p.m. $20 to $88. For tickets: (973) 624-3713; njsymphony.org. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

NEWARK Alicia Keys, R & B. April 8 at 7:30 p.m. $49.50 to $125. Prudential Center, 165 Mulberry Street. (973) 757-6000; prucenter.com.

NEWARK “Don Juan,” presented by the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra with Jean-Efflam Bavouzet, pianist, led by Susanna Mälkki, conductor. April 5 at 8 p.m. $20 to $88. For tickets: (973) 624-3713; njsymphony.org. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

NEWTON The Grand Slambovians, rock. April 6 at 8 p.m. $24 to $34. The Newton Theater, 234 Spring Street. (973) 383-3700; thenewtontheatre.com.

RAHWAY Engelbert Humperdinck, Anglo-Indian pop singer. April 7 at 7 p.m. $49.50 to $104.50. “Romeo and Juliet,” performed by the American Repertory Ballet. April 12 and 13. $35. Union County Performing Arts Center, 1601 Irving Street. (732) 499-8226; ucpac.org.

RED BANK Robin Zander, lead singer and rhythm guitarist for the rock band Cheap Trick. April 6 at 8 p.m. $19.50 to $39.50. Monmouth Symphony Orchestra performs Rachmaninoff’s Piano Concerto No. 2, April 7 at 3 p.m. $35. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9000; countbasietheatre.org.

SOUTH ORANGE Viva Spring, presented by the American Repertory Ballet. April 5 at 8 p.m. $15 to $30. Ben E. King, soul singer. April 6 at 8 p.m. $25 to $50. Juilliard at SOPAC: Allant Trio, composed of three musicians from the Juilliard School, Alina Lim, cellist, Beth Nam, pianist, and Anna Park, violinist. April 7 at 3 p.m. $15 and $20. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-2787; sopacnow.org.

STANHOPE New York Ska Jazz Ensemble. April 12 at 6 p.m. $12 and $15. The Stanhope House, 45 Main Street. (973) 347-7777; stanhopehousenj.com.

VINELAND Samuel Hernandez, Christian singer. April 5 and 6 at 7 p.m. $20. David Bromberg Quintet. April 12 at 8 p.m. $30 to $60. Landis Theater, 830 East Landis Avenue. landistheater.com; (856) 691-1121.

WAYNE Sounds of Korea, featuring music and dance from Korea. April 5 at 8 p.m. $10 to $15. Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University, 300 Pompton Road. (973) 720-2371; wplive.org.

WEST LONG BRANCH John Hammond, blues guitarist, with Billy Hector. April 5 at 8 p.m. $30. Pollak Theater, Monmouth University, 400 Cedar Avenue. (732) 263-6889; monmouth.edu/arts_events.

Spoken Word

MORRISTOWN An Evening With Jerry Lewis: “Dean and Me,” a career retrospective, with film clips, a question-and-answer session and behind-the-scenes home movies taken by Jerry Lewis with Dean Martin. April 5 at 8 p.m. $59 to $89. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org. Outdoors

MILLVILLE “Paws for Art,” featuring a WheatonBarks dog show and parade and various animal-themed activities. April 7, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. Wheaton Arts and Cultural Center, 1501 Glasstown Road (Route 55). (856) 825-6800; wheatonarts.org.

Theater

HACKETTSTOWN “The English Bride,” drama by Lucile Lichtblau. April 5 through 21. $17.50 to $27.50. Centenary Stage Company, Lackland Center, 715 Grand Avenue. (908) 979-0900;

centenarystageco.org.

LONG BRANCH “Noir,” drama by Stan Werse. April 4 through May 5. $25 to $40. New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater, 179 Broadway. (732) 229-3166; njrep.org.

MADISON “The Comedy of Errors,” by William Shakespeare. April 13 at noon. $16. F. M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University, 36 Madison Avenue. (973) 408-5600; njshakespeare.org.

MAPLEWOOD “Having Our Say: The Delany Sisters’ First 100 Years,” adapted by Emily Mann from the book of oral history by Sarah “Sadie” L. Delany and A. Elizabeth “Bessie” Delany with Amy Hill Hearth. Presented by the Theater Project. April 4 through 21. $10 and $30. Burgdorff Center for the Performing Arts, 10 Durand Road. (973) 763-4029;

thetheaterproject.org.

MORRISTOWN “Hair,” a rock musical with a book and lyrics by James Rado and Gerome Ragni and music by Galt MacDermot. April 13 at 8 p.m. $47 to $77. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK “Rich Girl,” a comedy by Victoria Stewart. Through April 7. $35 to $67. George Street Playhouse, 9 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7717; gsponline.org.

PRINCETON “The Winter’s Tale,” a play by Shakespeare. April 2 through 21. $20 to $62. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

RED BANK “The Electric Baby,” by Stefanie Zadravec. April 6 through May 5. $24 to $45. Two River Theater, 21 Bridge Avenue. (732) 345-1400; trtc.org.

SUMMIT “Shakespeare in Vegas,” comedy by Suzanne Bradbeer. April 4 through 21. $25 and $30. Dreamcatcher Repertory Theater at Oakes Center, 120 Morris Avenue.

dreamcatcherrep.org; (973) 378-7754 (ext. 2228).

TRENTON “Roundelay,” drama directed by Adam Immerwahr. Through April 7. $28 and $33. Passage Theater, 205 East Front Street. (609) 392-0766; passagetheatre.org.

WAYNE “Exquisite Potential,” by Stephen Kaplan. April 4 through 28. $10 to $18. Hunziker Black Box Theater, 300 Pompton Road.

wpunj.edu/wplive/hunziker.htm; (973) 720-2371.

WEST ORANGE “A Moon for the Misbegotten,” drama by Eugene O’Neill. April 6 through May 5. $25 and $35. Luna Stage, 555 Valley Road. (973) 395-5551; lunastage.org.

Museums and Galleries

BOONTON “Illuminato,” paintings by Brenda A. Edwards. Through March 31. Friday and Saturday, 11 a.m. to 7 p.m.; Sunday, noon to 4 p.m. S.H.E. Gallery, 819 Main Street. (973) 335-0943; shegallery.com.

BOUND BROOK “Dwell: A State of Habitation,” works about the places where people live. Through April 18. Hamilton Street Gallery, 6 Hamilton Street. hamiltonstreetgallery.com; (732) 748-2092.

CAPE MAY “Celebrating Community Service: Black Fraternal, Social and Civic Organizations of Cape May County.” Through April 14. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. (609) 884-5404; capemaymac.org.

DEMAREST “Ursula Schneider: earth, air, water,” works by the painter and printer. Through April 26. Reception: April 4, 6 to 8 p.m. Mondays through Fridays, 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. Mikhail Zakin Gallery, 561 Piermont Road. tasoc.org; (201) 767-7160.

ENGLEWOOD “Paint Work,” new works by Daniel Rios Rodriguez. Through April 14. Mondays through Thursdays, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. One River Gallery, 49 North Dean Street. onerivergallery.com; (201) 266-5244.

GLASSBORO “Cosmobilities,” group show. Through May 11. Mondays, Tuesdays, Thursdays and Fridays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Wednesdays, 10 a.m. to 7 p.m.; and Saturdays, noon to 5 p.m. Rowan University Art Gallery, 201 Mullica Hill Road. (856) 256-4521; rowan.edu/colleges/fpa/artgallery.

HAMILTON “Heaven, Hell and the History of Punch,” Robert Taplin. Through April 10. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. groundsforsculpture.org; (609) 586-0616.

HIGHTSTOWN “Sustenance,” installation and exhibition by Andrew DeCaen at the Mariboe Gallery. Through April 17. Mondays through Fridays, 8 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Saturdays 8 a.m. to noon. Peddie School, 201 South Main Street. (609) 944-7500; peddie.org.

HOBOKEN “Mapping the Territory: Hudson County in Maps, 1840-2013.” Through June 30. $2. Tuesdays through Thursdays, 2 to 7 p.m.; Fridays, 1 to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Hoboken Historical Museum, 1301 Hudson Street.

hobokenmuseum.org; (201) 656-2240.

JERSEY CITY “Avatar: The Exhibition,” interactive installations and original props from the movie. Through May 19. Free with admission, $12.50 and $16.75. Liberty Science Center, 222 Jersey City Boulevard. (201) 200-1000; lsc.org.

JERSEY CITY “Ray Smith: Here | Now,” over 100 works spanning three decades. Through May 5. Mana Contemporary, 888 Newark Avenue. manafinearts.com; (201) 484-1495, ext. 672.

LINCROFT “From Plate to Paper: The Art of Printmaking,” juried exhibition. Through April 28. “Bruce Waldman: New Works,” monoprints and etchings. Through April 21. $7; members and children under 2, free. Tuesdays through Thursdays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Fridays, 10 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Monmouth Museum, 765 Newman Springs Road. (732) 747-2266; monmouthmuseum.org.

MAHWAH “Les Paul in Mahwah,” about the longtime Mahwah resident, inventor, musician and self-taught engineer. Through June 1. “Nike Missile Base in Mahwah: Nuclear Warheads in Our Backyard,” models of the base, which operated from 1955 to 1971, and of the missiles; vintage footage; and other artifacts of the period. Through June 30. $3 to $5; members and children under 5, free. Wednesdays, Saturdays and Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Mahwah Museum, 201 Franklin Turnpike. (201) 512-0099;

mahwahmuseum.org.

MILLVILLE “Wheaton Glass: The Art of the Fellowship,” an exhibition that celebrates the 30th anniversary of the Creative Glass Center of America at the Museum of American Glass. April 2 through Jan. 5. “Paul J. Stankard: Celebrating the Native Flowers of Southern New Jersey,” an exhibition and sale featuring recent works by Paul J. Stankard. April 2 through May 5. $7 to $10; children 5 and under, free. Tuesday through Sunday, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Wheaton Arts and Cultural Center, 1501 Glasstown Road (Route 55). (856) 825-6800; wheatonarts.org.

MONTCLAIR “Patterns, Systems, Structures: Abstraction in American Art.” Through May 19. “The New Spirit: American Art in the Armory Show, 1913.” Through June 16. “Oscar Bluemner’s America: Picturing Paterson, New Jersey.” Through June 16. “Redefining American Art: Organizers of the 1913 Armory Show in MAM’s Collection.” Through June 16. “Bloomfield Avenue Hotline,” featuring a joint project by the artists Karina Aguilera Skvirsky and Liselot van der Heijden. April 4 through June 16. $12 adults, $10 seniors and students; members and children under 12, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Montclair Art Museum, 3 South Mountain Avenue. (973) 746-5555; montclairartmuseum.org.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP “Immortal Structures: Here and There After,” contemporary realist paintings by Maria Mijares. Through March 31. “Victorian New Jersey: Photographs by Guillermo Thorn.” Through March 31. “Reflections: Contemporary Studio Art Glass,” 40 works by Kathleen Elliot and Paul Stankard. Through April 28. “Line, Color, and Pattern: Exquisite Textile Designs of Central and South America.” Through June 16. “Once Upon a Dime: The World of Money,” an interactive exhibition. Through Sept. 3. $7 to $10. Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 11 a.m. to 8 p.m.; and Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3700; morrismuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN “Thomas Nast: President Maker and Campaign Breaker,” illustrating presidential campaigns from 1864 to 1884. Through June 1. “Fine Collection: Treasures From the Vault Exhibit,” Oriental rugs from the museum’s collection. Through June 30. $6 to $8; children ages 6 to 12; $4 and children under 5, free. Wednesdays, Thursdays and Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Macculloch Hall Historical Museum, 45 Macculloch Avenue. maccullochhall.org; (973) 538-2404.

NEW BRUNSWICK “Lynd Ward Draws Stories: Inspired by Mexico’s History, Mark Twain and Adventures in the Woods.” Through June 30. “Leonid Sokov: Ironic Objects,” about 50 works. Through July 14. “Henri-Gabriel Ibels,” works on paper. Through Sept. 8. “Stars: Contemporary Prints by Derrière L’Étoile Studio,” featuring significant contemporary prints by many of the artists who defined the American art scene after 1980. Through Sept. 29. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum, 71 Hamilton Street.

zimmerlimuseum.rutgers.edu; (732) 932-7237.

NEWARK “Expanding Africa at the Newark Museum: New Visions, New Galleries,” contemporary and historical works. Through May 1. “In Her Eyes: Women Behind and in Front of the Camera,” photography. Through spring 2013. “African Cosmos: Stellar Arts,” about African cultural astronomy and arts. Through Aug. 11. $6 to $10; members, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. newarkmuseum.org; (973) 596-6550.

OCEAN TOWNSHIP “Loved Ones Go to War: Local Stories of World War II,” the war’s effects on local soldiers and those they left behind. Through June 1. Tuesdays and Wednesdays, 1 to 4 p.m.; Thursdays, 1 to 4 p.m. and 7 to 9 p.m.; and first and second Sundays of the month, 1 to 4 p.m. Free. Township of Ocean Historical Museum, 703 Deal Road. (732) 531-2136; oceanmuseum.org.

PRINCETON “The Pine Barrens: A Legacy of Preservation — Photographs by Richard Speedy.” Through April 15. $5 and $6. Tuesdays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Morven Museum and Garden, 55 Stockton Street. (609) 924-8144; morven.org.

PRINCETON “1913: The Year of Modernism,” prints, drawings, photographs, rare books and periodicals from the collections of the Princeton University Art Museum and the Princeton University Library. Through June 23. “Revealing the African Presence in Renaissance Europe,” paintings, drawings, sculpture and printed books of the period. Through June 9. “Picturing Power: Capitalism, Democracy and American Portraiture,” 50 portraits. Through June 30. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 10 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Princeton University Art Museum, Princeton University Campus. (609) 258-3788; artmuseum.princeton.edu.

PRINCETON “A Republic in the Wilderness: Treasures of American History From Jamestown to Appomattox,” featuring some of Princeton’s holdings of American historical manuscripts, autograph letters, rare books, maps, broadsides, prints, photographs and other original materials. Through Aug. 4. Mondays through Fridays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Princeton University Library, 1 Washington Road. (609) 258-3184; princeton.edu/rbsc/.

PRINCETON JUNCTION “Energy and Motion,” photography. Through April 27. West Windsor Arts Center, 952 Alexander Road. (609) 716-1931;

westwindsorartscenter.org.

SHREWSBURY “Eyesights 2013.” Open Juried Photography Show. Through April 3. Mondays through Fridays, 8 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Thursdays, 8 a.m. to 7 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Guild of Creative Art, 620 Broad Street. (732) 741-1441; guildofcreativeart.org.

SPRINGFIELD “Visions Within,” Boltaire V. Cruz. Through May 1. Mondays, Wednesdays and Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 8:30 p.m.; Tuesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 4:30 p.m. Donald B. Palmer Museum (Springfield Free Public Library), 66 Mountain Avenue. (973) 376-4930;

springfieldpubliclibrary.com.

TEANECK “Seeing the Future From the Past: Art Work by W.P.A. Artists.” Through April 4. Tuesdays through Thursdays, noon to 4 p.m. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. puffinculturalforum.org; (201) 836-3499.

TRENTON “New Jersey Artist Series — Jon Naar: Signature Photography.” Through May 5. Tuesdays through Sundays, 9 a.m. to 4:45 p.m. Suggested admission, $5. New Jersey State Museum, 205 West State Street. newjerseystatemuseum.org; (609) 292-6464.

UNION “Advocacy Quilts: A Voice for the Voiceless,” 281 artists. Through May 10. Mondays, Tuesdays and Thursdays, noon to 7 p.m.; Wednesdays and Fridays, noon to 4 p.m. Kean University, 1000 Morris Avenue. kean.edu/~gallery/; (908) 737-0392.
