SUMMIT IN WASHINGTON; White House Dinner: The Guest List


Published: June 17, 1992

Following is the list of guests at the Russian-American state dinner tonight, as made available by the White House:

President Bush and Barbara Bush.

Vice President Dan Quayle and Marilyn Quayle.

President Boris N. Yeltsin of Russia and Naina Yeltsin.

Prime Minister Yegor T. Gaidar.

Foreign Minister Andrei V. Kozyrev.

Defense Minister P. S. Grachev.

President M. E. Nikolayev of the Republic of Sakh.

Vladimir N. Lukin, Ambassador of Russia, and Mrs. Lukin

V. V. Ilyushin, first assistant to the President.

Y. V. Petrov, chief of presidential administration.

Y. V. Shokov, Secretary, Security Council of Russia.

P. O. Aven, Secretary, Security Council of Russia.

V. N. Khlystun, Minister of Agriculture of Russia.

D. B. Ryurikov, assistant to the President.

S. A. Kovalev, chairman, Supreme Soviet Committee on Human Rights.

M. M. Molostvov, member, Supreme Soviet Committee on Human Rights.

Robert E. Allen, chairman, A.T.&T., and Betty Allen.

Terry Anderson, chief Middle East correspondent, The Associated Press, and Madeleine Bassil.

Dwayne O. Andreas, chairman, Archer Daniel Midland Company, and Dorothy Andreas.

Dexter F. Baker chairman, Air Products and Chemicals Inc., and Dorothy Baker.

Secretary of State James A. Baker 3d and Susan Baker.

Ronald H. Benson, President, Phibro Energy Inc., and Kristy Kay Benson.

James H. Billington, Librarian of Congress, and Marjorie Billington.

Nicholas F. Brady, Secretary of the Treasury, and Katherine Brady.

Representative William S. Broomfield and Jane Broomfield.

Dean Burch and Patricia Burch.

R. Nicholas Burns, director for European Affairs, National Security Council.

Defense Secretary Dick Cheney and Lynne V. Cheney, chairwoman of the National Endowment for the Humanities.

Leonard Cohen, Union of Councils for Soviet Jews, and Pamela Cohen.

E. Gerald Corrigan, president, Federal Reserve Bank of New York, and Linda Corrigan.

Mitchell E. Daniels Jr., vice president, Eli Lilly & Company, and Cheri Daniels.

Kathleen DeLaski and Edward Grubb, White House Correspondent, ABC News.

Kenneth T. Derr, chairman, Chevron Corporation.

Senator Bob Dole, and Elizabeth H. Dole, chairwoman, American Red Cross.

Robert H. Ellsworth, chairman, International Institute for Strategic Studies, and Vivian Ellsworth.

Commerce Secretary Barbara Franklin and Wallace Barnes.

Robert W. Galvin, chairman, Motorola Inc., and Mary Galvin.

Louis V. Gerstner Jr., chairman, RJR Nabisco Holdings Corporation, and Robin Gerstner.

Daniel S. Goldin, Administrator, NASA, and Judy Goldin.

Robert F. Greenhill, president, Morgan Stanley Group Inc., and Gayle Greenhill.

Alan Greenspan, chairman, Federal Reserve, and Andrea Mitchell, NBC News.

Gov. Judd Gregg of New Hampshire, and Kathleen Gregg.

Representative Lee H. Hamilton and Nancy Hamilton.

Senator Mark O. Hatfield and Antoinette Hatfield.

Edward A. Hewett, special assitant to the President for national security affairs, and Nancy Hewett.

Henry L. Hillman, the Hillman Company, and Elsie Hillman.

Representative Henry J. Hyde.

Housing Secretary Jack F. Kemp and Joanne Kemp.

Donald M. Kendall, chairman, Pepsico Inc., and Sigrid Kendall.

Morton Kondracke, senior editor, Roll Call, and daughter Alexandra Kondracke.

Parker B. Ladd.

Agriculture Secretary Edward Madigan and Evelyn Madigan.

Howard P. Marguleas, chief executive officer, Sunworld Inc., and Ardith Marguleas.

J. Willard Marriott Jr., chairman, Marriott Corporation, and Donna Marriott.

Arch McGill, president, Chardonnay Inc., and Jeanne McGill.

Gerald McRaney, actor, and mother, Edna Lenore McRaney.

John J. Murphy, chairman, Dresser Industries Inc., and Louise Murphy.

Thomas M. T. Niles, Assistant Secretary of State for European Affairs.

Senator Sam Nunn and Colleen Nunn.

William F. O'Connor, Chicago Board of Trade, and Mary Jane O'Connor.

James C. Oberwetter, vice president, Hunt Oil Company, and Eleanor Oberwetter.

Gen. Colin L. Powell, Chairman, Joint Chiefs of Staff, and Alma Powell.

Ahmad Rashad, sports announcer, NBC, and Phylicia Rashad, actress.

William H. Rehnquist, Chief Justice of the United States.

Condoleezza Rice, associate professor of political science, Stanford University.

William B. Walsh Sr., president, Project Hope, and Helen Walsh.

John G. Weinmann chief of protocol, and Virginia Lee Weinmann.

Betty White, actress, and Wayne Ferguson.

Kristi Yamaguchi, figure skater.

Zach Zachariah and Rachel Zachariah.

Gordon Zacks, chairman, R. G. Barry Corporation, and Carol Sue Zacks.

Fred M. Zeder 2d, president, Overseas Private Investment Corporation, and Martha Zeder.
