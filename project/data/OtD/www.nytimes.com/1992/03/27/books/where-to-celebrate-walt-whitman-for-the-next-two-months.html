Where to Celebrate Walt Whitman for the Next Two Months


Published: March 27, 1992

"Democracy's Poet: A Walt Whitman Celebration" will feature a variety of events around New York City and on Long Island through May 31 to commemorate the 100th anniversary of the death of Walt Whitman. The Museum of the City of New York has organized the celebration, with the help of many cultural and educational organizations. Grants were provided by the Lila Wallace-Reader's Digest Fund, the National Endowment for the Humanities and The New York Times Company Foundation.

Unless otherwise noted, all events are free. Times and dates are subject to change.

For self-guided walking tours of Manhattan neighborhoods associated with the writer, two publications are available: "Four Literary-Historical Walks," available for $6.50 from the Academy of American Poets, (212) 427-5665, and the spring issue of Seaport, a magazine published by the South Street Seaport Museum. Information: (212) 669-9400.

Here is a schedule of events. Locations

BROOKLYN HISTORICAL SOCIETY, 128 Pierrepont Street, Brooklyn Heights, (718) 624-0890.

FEDERAL HALL NATIONAL MEMORIAL, 26 Wall Street, lower Manhattan, (212) 264-8701.

GRADUATE CENTER OF THE CITY UNIVERSITY OF NEW YORK, 33 West 42d Street, Manhattan, (212) 243-5822.

JUDSON MEMORIAL CHURCH, 55 Washington Square South, at Thompson Street, Manhattan, (212) 477-0351.

MERCANTILE LIBRARY OF NEW YORK, 17 East 47th Street, Manhattan, (212) 755-6710.

KATHRYN BACHE MILLER THEATER, Columbia University, Broadway at 116th Street, Manhattan, (212) 854-7799.

MUSEUM OF THE CITY OF NEW YORK, Fifth Avenue at 103d Street, Manhattan, (212) 534-1672.

NEW YORK UNIVERSITY, Tishman Auditorium, Vanderbilt Hall, 40 Washington Square North, Manhattan, (212) 995-3336.

POETS HOUSE, 72 Spring Street, second floor, Manhattan, (212) 431-7920.

QUEENS COLLEGE, Kissena Boulevard and the Long Island Expressway, Flushing, (718) 520-7238.

ST. MARK'S CHURCH IN THE BOWERY, Second Avenue and 10th Street, Manhattan, (212) 674-0910.

SEAMEN'S CHURCH INSTITUTE, 241 Water Street, lower Manhattan, (212) 349-9090.

SYMPHONY SPACE, Broadway and 95th Street, Manhattan, (212) 864-1414.

WALT WHITMAN BIRTHPLACE ASSOCIATION, 246 Old Walt Whitman Road, West Hills, South Huntington, L.I., (516) 427-5240. Today

"WALT WHITMAN AND NEW YORK," Museum of the City of New York. An exhibition of photographs, paintings, lithographs and three-dimensional objects depicting New York City during Whitman's days. Through Aug. 30. Wednesdays through Saturdays, 10 A.M. to 5 P.M., Sundays, 1 to 5 P.M. Suggested admission: $5 ($3 for students, the elderly and those under 12; $8 for families).

READING, Graduate Center of the City University of New York. A reading of Whitman's works by the writers Gordon Thompson, Ann Lauterbach, Armand Schwerner, Angus Fletcher, David Reynolds and Charles Molesworth. 9 A.M. to 5:30 P.M. Information: (212) 243-5822.

"WALT WHITMAN: IN LIFE OR DEATH FOREVER," New York Public Library, Berg Exhibition Room, third floor, Fifth Avenue and 42d Street, Manhattan. More than 200 items including manuscripts, books, letters and photographs from the library's permanent collection, including Whitman's own "Blue Book" and first edition of "Leaves of Grass." Tuesdays and Wednesdays, 11 A.M. to 6 P.M.; Thursdays through Saturdays, 10 A.M. to 6 P.M.; closed Sundays and Mondays. Information: (212) 869-8089. Tomorrow

"LEAVES OF GRASS," Symphony Space. A marathon of works by Whitman, read by Stockard Channing, Stephen Lang, Elizabeth McGovern, James Naughton, Tandy Cronyn, Viveca Lindfors, Marian Seldes, Roy Blount Jr., Representative Ted Weiss and City Council President Peter Vallone, among others. Also, world premieres of commissioned works and music by the composers William Schuman, Virgil Thomson and Norman Dello Joio, set to words by Whitman; performers include the Dessoff Choir, the New York City Gay Men's Chorus Chamber Choir and the New Amsterdam Singers Chamber Chorus. 11 A.M. to 11 P.M.

MARATHON READING OF "LEAVES OF GRASS," Walt Whitman Birthplace. Readings of Whitman's work by Sue Kain, Vince Clemente, Maxwell C. Wheat Jr., Cornelius Eady, Raymond R. Patterson and others. 9 A.M. to 9 P.M. Also Sunday, 9 A.M. to 6 P.M. Tuesday

"FORGING A NEW VOICE," Brooklyn Historical Society. Whitman's position as a gay poet in the 19th century, discussed by Robert K. Martin, an English professor at Concordia University in Montreal. 7 P.M. Thursday

"A TRIBUTE TO WALT WHITMAN," Kathryn Bache Miller Theater. A musical program that includes Whitman settings sung by Paul Sperry, a tenor. 8 P.M. Tickets: $15. April 4

"I HEAR AMERICA SINGING," Brooklyn Historical Society. A family workshop in which participants will take tape recorders to the streets of Brooklyn Heights to record the neighborhood sounds that may have influenced Whitman. 2 P.M. April 5

"SCENES OF WALT WHITMAN'S BROOKLYN," Museum of the City of New York. A tour through Fort Greene and the Fulton Ferry area, led by Arthur Marks. 1 P.M. Advance registration and meeting place: (212) 534-1672, extension 206.

"WALT WHITMAN: A LONG ISLANDER," Walt Whitman Birthplace. Opening of an exhibition of photographs, prints and text depicting the writer's life. Through Dec. 31. Wednesdays through Fridays, 1 to 4 P.M.; Saturdays and Sundays, 10 A.M. to 4 P.M. April 6
