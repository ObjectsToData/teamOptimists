PROBABLE HEADS OF SENATE PANELS

Published: November 9, 1986

LABOR AND HUMAN RESOURCES

Edward M. Kennedy . . . 54 years old . . . first elected in 1962 . . . ranking Democrat on Labor and Human Resources Committee . . . chose to concentrate on the labor committee after the Republicans took control . . . believed he could have more impact on Reagan policies as the senior minority member of that panel . . . as chairman of the committee he will be a leading Democratic spokesman on education and job programs. BUDGET

Lawton Chiles . . . 56 years old . . . served 12 years in the Florida State Legislature . . . first elected in 1970 . . . ranking Democrat on Budget Committee . . . moderately conservative Democrat who has tried to put his mark on issues ranging from the budget to drugs . . . has two fundamental arguments with the Administration: believes that new revenue of some sort will inevitably be needed but concedes that it is unlikely unless supported by the President and disagrees with Mr. Reagan's budget and spending priorities, especially his policies on education and research and development. ARMED SERVICES

Sam Nunn . . . 48 years old . . . former member of the Georgia House of Representatives . . . first elected to the Senate in 1972 . . . ranking Democrat on Armed Services Committee . . . his command of military policy has made him one of the most influential and respected lawmakers on Capitol Hill . . . strong defender of military buildup but a shrewd skeptic on specific programs . . . increasingly mentioned as a potential Presidential or Vice Presidential candidate.

APPROPRIATIONS

John C. Stennis . . . 85 years old . . . senior member of the Senate . . . first elected in 1947 . . . at end of current term, in January 1989, will be nine months short of Carl Hayden's record for Senate longevity of 41 years and 11 months . . . ranking Democrat on Appropriations Committee . . . has long been an authority on military affairs . . . as chairman will have a special relationship with the chairman of the House Appropriations Committee, Jamie L. Whitten, who is also from Mississippi. BANKING, HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS

William Proxmire . . . 70-year-old Democrat from Wisconsin . . . got Senate seat by filling the vacancy caused by the death of Joseph R. McCarthy . . . has built reputation as a leading opponent of waste in Government . . . reputation for legislative diligence . . . spoke every working morning from 1967 to earlier this year in support of the United Nations treaty against Genocide, which passed in February . . . has not missed a roll-call vote since 1966 . . . was chairman of this committee from 1975 until the Republicans took control of the Senate in 1981. FOREIGN RELATIONS

Claiborne Pell . . . 67-year-old Democrat from Rhode Island, elected in 1960 . . . served in both the Foreign Service and as the United States delegate to the United Nations General Assembly . . . was chairman of the Labor and Human Resources Subcommittee on Education, Arts and the Humanities until the Republicans took control of the Senate in 1981 . . . fought against Mr. Reagan's plan to eliminate the Department of Education . . . a vocal critic of the Administration's policies on arms control and Central American . . . supports a nuclear weapons freeze. FINANCE

Lloyd Bentsen . . . 65-year-old Democrat from Texas . . . elected in 1970 . . . was a member of the House of Representatives for three full terms, then retired to go into business before returning to politics . . . ran for the Democratic Presidential nomination in 1976 . . . this year the panel is to take up the recently passed Tax Reform Act of 1986 and the Senator has already hinted that there will be some changes in that legislation, especially in provisions he thinks are harmful to business. JUDICIARY

Joseph R. Biden Jr. . . . 43 years old . . . first elected in 1972 . . . as ranking Democrat on Judiciary Committee led the unsuccessful effort to block Mr. Reagan's nomination of Daniel A. Manion to a Federal Court of Appeals and played a prominent role in the long hearings on Justice William H. Rehnquist's nomination to Chief Justice . . . as chairman he will be at the head of any effort to oppose Mr. Reagan's judicial nominees. from appointing additional conservative judges.
