DAUMIER

Forty-five provocative lithographs depicting courtship, marriage, motherhood, divorce, politics, feminism, patriotism and other conditions of life among women in 19th-century France, as seen through the eyes of Honore Daumier, will go on exhibit today through Dec. 9 at the Neuberger Museum of the State University of New York at Purchase. Many of the pieces were originally published in the French newspapers Le Charivari and La Caricature.

''Femmes d'Esprit: Women in Daumier's Caricature'' was curated by Elizabeth Childs, an assistant professor of art history at SUNY Purchase, and Kirtsen Powell, an assistant professor of art at Middlebury College in Vermont, the show's sponsor. They will participate in a free discussion of the changing roles of women in Daumier's time, and the exhibition's relevance to today's feminist movement, at 4 P.M. on Sept. 26 in the Humanities Building Auditorium. Joining them will be Jane Kromm, an assistant professor of art history at SUNY Purchase and Carol Rifelj, professor of French at Middlebury. A public reception will follow from 5 to 6:30 P.M.

The term ''femmes d'esprit'' refers to women of intelligence and wit, and to female contemporaries of Daumier like George Sand, who rejected the traditional roles of wife, mother and housekeeper for literary and other male-dominated pursuits.

The museum, at 735 Anderson Hill Road, is open from 10 A.M. to 4 P.M. Tuesdays through Fridays, and from 11 A.M. to 5 P.M. Saturdays and Sundays. Admission is free, with a suggested contribution of $2. Call 251-6133 for more information.

HAWK WATCH

The Greenburgh Nature Center is organizing a hawk watch at Hook Mountain in Rockland County, scheduled from 9:45 A.M. to mid-afternoon on Saturday. Bruce Lowder, the center's museum curator and wildlife rehabilitator, will give a brief orientation talk in the Manor House, using resident raptors, before setting out in cars and the center's van across the Tappan Zee Bridge and Route 9W north.

An uphill hike to the watch site takes about a half-hour and is rewarded with splendid views of the Hudson River Valley as well as fly-bys of various species of south-bound birds of prey.

Binoculars, lunch and clothes that are appropriate for climbing and the weather are suggested. The fee is $3. Call 723-3470 to reserve or offer car space. The rain date is next Sunday.

COLLECTIBLES

An International Collectibles Exposition that has been popular in the Middle West for almost two decades will make its East Coast debut at the County Center in White Plains on Saturday and Sunday.

''It's a way for collectors to see what will soon be available in the marketplace,'' said Michael McClure of the McRand Company of Lake Forest, Ill., the organizer of the show. Mr. McClure described the event as ''sort of like going to a candy store but not being able to buy the candy.''

More than 160 manufacturers of limited-edition dolls, commemorative plates, lithographs and figurines, plus 40 of the artists who design them, will be on hand with examples of their products. When these items appear later in retail stores they will be priced from around $50 to several thousand dollars.

Seminars will be held by several artists, including Will Nelson, who went to China in order to properly research and reproduce his images of pandas; and David Winter, a British artist who will show his collection of ceramic English cottages and villages, represented in minute detail.

Whitey Ford, former star pitcher for the Yankees, will autograph one of the sports plates that bears his portrait, and a special exhibition will feature table settings of Presidential china used on Air Force One, at Camp David and in various United States embassies around the world.

Hours for the show are from 9 A.M. to 5 P.M. Saturday and from 11 A.M. to 4 P.M. Sunday. Admission is $6. A swap and sell session for visitors is scheduled from 9 A.M. to 1 P.M. both days in a specially marked location.

LORE LECTURES

A series of lectures by Hudson River educators and naturalists is to be offered free this month with admission to Boscobel, the restored Federal mansion at Garrison-on-Hudson.

Uta Gore starts off with a slide show and talk on ''Mystery Island on the Hudson'' from 1:30 to 3:30 P.M. today. Bannerman's Island, off the Cold Spring shore, is the site of a castle built by a Francis Bannerman, a Scottish munitions manufacturer, who used it as a summer home and arsenal.

From 1:30 to 3:30 next Sunday Roger Pannetta will discuss ''The Cultural History of the Hudson River - Nature vs. Civilization.'' The final program, on Sept. 23, will deal with ''Protecting Wetlands: From the Hudson River to Your Backyard,'' with Jim Rod, warden of Constitution Marsh, the nearby Audubon Sanctuary.

Admission to Boscobel, on Route 9D, is $5 for adults, $4 for the elderly and $2.50 for children. Call 265- 3638 for more information.

GARDEN SHOW

The New York Botanical Garden in the Bronx has a full weekend of events celebrating its Garden Harvest Festival Saturday and next Sunday from 10 A.M. to 5 P.M. Plants from North and South America will be featured.

''A View of the Tropical Rain Forest'' is the title of a display in the Enid A. Haupt Conservatory, and it includes specimens of the purple velvet plant, zebra plant and a tree covered with bromeliads and Spanish moss.

The Native Plant Garden will contain a North American meadow, areas of woodland and wetland, perennials and ferns. Three tour-talks a day will be given at both exhibitions.

An arts and crafts show will be held on the Museum Mall and will feature works created with natural materials, or inspired by nature themes. There will be jewelry, Shaker baskets, ceramics, dried flowers and pressed-flower stationery, photography and graphics. Demonstrations for adults will focus on working with natural fibers and dyes, and on flower arranging. Children will be shown how to press flowers and collect seeds. Face painting and square dancing are among the other diversions.

Admission to the 250-acre grounds is free but a $3 donation is suggested. For more information, call (212) 220-8700. $90ELEANOR CHARLES
