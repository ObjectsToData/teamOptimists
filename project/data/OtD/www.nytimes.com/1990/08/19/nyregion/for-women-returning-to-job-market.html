For Women Returning to Job Market

By ELSA BRENNER
Published: August 19, 1990

CONTINUING a 28-year practice of serving nontraditional adult students, Sarah Lawrence College is offering a new one-year program this fall to help those who earned bachelor's degrees 10, 20, even 30 years ago to update job skills for today's marketplace.

Called the Post-BA Career Exploration and Development Program, it is a two-semester course of study ''designed for women who have been out of the work force for many years and are entering or returning to the job market,'' said Alice K. Olson, director of the program.

''There are all kinds of professions that didn't exist 10 to 15 years ago,'' Ms. Olson said, citing as examples benefits planning, drug and alcohol counseling, adult literacy training, word processing, automated typesetting and desktop publishing.

What is unusual about the program at Sarah Lawrence is that entering students are not required to have a particular career direction or defined course of study in mind, the director said.

The Matter of Direction

''Most schools offer specific course work or you go to a career counselor to find out which direction to take,'' Ms. Olson said. ''We're offering both the career counseling and the preparation in one place.''

Although the program is designed mostly for women who have been at home raising a family, men who have opted for an early retirement or lost jobs through company mergers also would be good candidates for the career development and exploration course of study, said Karen Sim, a career counselor who will serve as an adjunct faculty member for the program.

The fall semester is a full-time on-campus program that includes four areas of study: an introduction to computers; statistics and related math; a noncredit career exploration seminar, and an optional liberal arts elective in the humanities, social sciences or natural sciences.

During the spring semester, students will work four days a week at an employment internship selected during the previous semester's career-exploration seminar, taught by Mrs. Sim. Throughout the second semester, students will be returning to campus every two weeks for discussions and presentations on financial management, personnel and human-resource issues, market research and modern corporate trends.

An Intensive Workshop

The final week of the program will consist of an intensive on-campus workshop during which each student will be required to prepare a ''Next-Step Plan of Action,'' Ms. Olson said.

For some that will mean planning a job search, writing a resume and training for interviews. The spring semester internship will provide a recent job experience to include on a resume and for use as references.

''Potential employers tend to ask, 'What have you done lately?' '' Ms. Olson said. ''The internship helps students who might otherwise have a resume with a many-year gap in it.''

Other students may choose to go on to graduate study and use the final week of the program to assess admissions requirements for an advanced degree.

''Those students will be able to show that it hasn't been 12 or more years since they've used their brains,'' Ms. Olson said. ''They will have earned recent college credits.'' The program is part of the college's Center for Continuing Education. In 1962, Sarah Lawrence became the first private liberal arts college to offer courses and degree programs to women past the traditional age for pursuing a post-secondary school education.

''We've been working five to six years on a program for this constituency,'' Ms. Olson said of the center's newest program.

Enrollment in the career exploration and development program is limited to 12 students. Tuition for the two semesters is $4,590; with the optional first semester liberal arts elective, tuition is $6,990.

The college is accepting applications for admission to the program, which begins the week of Sept. 10. Those interested in applying should call the center at 395-2205 to arrange for a personal interview.
