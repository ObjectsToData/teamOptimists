BUSINESS PEOPLE; 47th Street Photo Official Plans Software Business

By DANIEL F. CUFF
Published: April 9, 1990

Robert Orbach, director of business development for 47th Street Photo, is leaving to start a new business in the software industry.

Mr. Orbach, who is 37 years old, joined the New York-based discount retailer as a salesman of programmable calculators nine years ago and built it into one of the nation's largest independent sellers of computers. Because of the retailer's size, Mr. Orbach has come to play an influential role in the marketing of personal computers and software.

Mr. Orbach reached beyond the individual consumer to sell to large corporate and government customers and built relationships with major software vendors. The company is privately held, but industry estimates put 47th Street Photo's computer sales last year at $130 million, and its total revenue at more than $300 million.

Mr. Orbach, who plans to leave on April 25, declined last week to be specific about his new venture. ''Basically it involves the software industry, helping entrepreneurs with new innovative ideas,'' he said. But, he added, it went well beyond mere consulting.

Mr. Orbach said that the departure was amicable. ''After nine years, it's time to explore new avenues,'' he added.

Before it started selling computers, 47th Street Photo sold cameras and electronic equipment, and now has just about everything electronic and electric. It has three stores in New York City and one in East Meadow, L.I., and a national mail order business. The stores are known for their busy pace and their closing on Saturdays because of their Orthodox Jewish employees and owners.

Mr. Orbach moved through several jobs in the computer division and was named director of business development in 1986. He lives in New York City with his wife and three children. He holds a master's degree in jurisprudence and humanities from Beth Medrash Govoha in Lakewood, N.J.
