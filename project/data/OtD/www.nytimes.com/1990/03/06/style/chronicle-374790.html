Chronicle

By SUSAN HELLER ANDERSON
Published: March 6, 1990

LYNNE V. CHENEY will be nominated to her second four-year term as chairman of the National Endowment for the Humanities by PRESIDENT BUSH. With a budget of $156.9 million, the endowment gives money to scholars and issues reports on the teaching of the humanities. In 1987, Ms. Cheney shocked educators and parents by exposing deep gaps in high school students' knowledge of history and literature.
