    Salisbury

    Published: March 15, 1981

    To the Editor:

    In the Fred Ferretti article, ''Salisbury Cathedral's New Prisoners of Conscience Window'' (Travel Section, Feb. 22), the author writes: ''With the exceptions of Westminster Abbey and St. Paul's Cathedral ... Salisbury is perhaps the most celebrated church in England.''

    In England, certainly, Canterbury Cathedral must stand first in celebrity.

    For its stained glass, Canterbury Cathedral is not only unrivaled in England, but has further claims internationally, on a number of grounds, ranking worthily with, if after, Chartres and Bourges.

    For its other aspects of interest, treasures, lore, history, its art and architecture, Canterbury must be ranked in any group of three ''firsts'' among the English cathedrals, however one may wish to align the sequence.

    If Mr. Ferretti forgot the cathedral, he probably should not have forgotten the ''Canterbury Tales'' and Thomas a Becket. I could not bear to leave this oversight standing. HARRY BOBER, Avalon Foundation, Professor in the Humanities Institute of Fine Arts, New York University, New York
