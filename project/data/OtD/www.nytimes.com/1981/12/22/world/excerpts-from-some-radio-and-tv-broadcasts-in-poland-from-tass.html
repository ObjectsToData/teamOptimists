EXCERPTS FROM SOME RADIO AND TV BROADCASTS IN POLAND FROM TASS

Special to the New York Times
Published: December 22, 1981

WASHINGTON, Dec. 21— Following are excerpts from the Soviet press agency Tass and excerpts from domestic Polish radio and television broadcasts as transcribed and translated from the Polish here by the Foreign Broadcast Information Service, a United States Government agency Katowice Miners Strike 9 P.M. Monday, (3 P.M. Monday, E.S.T.)

Report by the Katowice correspondent of P.A.P., the Government press agency:

The difficut situation in the neighboring Ziemowit and Piast hard coal mines in Tychy continues. Since last Tuesday groups of miners there have been on strike underground.

Immense efforts are being made to resolve this dangerous conflict, using persuasion without the use of force. So far there have seen no casualties.

Despite strong pressure exercised by the strike organizers - Soldidarity extremists including persons who are not part of the work force - some miners are deciding to come to the surface.

In the Ziemowit colliery 74 miners so far have discontinued the strike. Singly, other people have seen leaving the pit this morning; 1,154 miners remained below.

The entire work force of the Ziemowit colliery totals some 10,000 people. In the nearby Piast colliery at present 1,742 miners are underground, out of a work force of 8,000. The organizers of the strike have completely restricted information. They distort news passed on by the miners' families and use intimidation.

Today they have also refused to admit doctors, experts from the mining office, as well as priests who wanted to go below. Communications are being maintained by telephone. The strikers are being supplied with food and parcels brought by families as well as nourishing soup cooked in the mines.

The directors of both mines have addressed letters to the striking miners in which they personally guarantee that no one forced to strike will be punished. ''Miners,'' read the letters, ''do not stay below any longer. In the country calm prevails. Everyboday works. End the strikes. Christmas is drawing nearer and your families await you.'' Envoy Faces Charges 10 P.M. Monday, (4 P.M. Monday, E.S.T.)

In connection with the fact that Romuald Spasowski, hitherto Polish Ambassador to the United States, has committed serious crimes against fundamental state interests, as defined in Articles 22 and 130 of the criminal code, the Minister of Foreign Affairs applied today to the head military prosecutor for criminal proceedings to be instigated.

In connection with the desertion of Romuald Spasowski, former Ambassador to Washington, the press spokesman of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs has been authorized to make the following statement:

The defection by Spasowski is an act of betraying the interests of the Polish state, deserving universal contempt and condemnation. In November of this year, Spasowski was recalled from the function of Ambassador in Washington as from the end of January 1982 with the intention of retiring him, and he knew this. He took measures to extend his stay. These were not accepted in connection with a critical assessment of the results of his work and his increasingly frequent incompetence, which was made worse by conditions of psychological depression connected with his personal affairs.

In his propaganda enunciations, Romuald Spasowski is trying to slander Poland and to dupe both employees of the Polish foreign service and also Polish and world public opinion. He is posing as a patriotic fighter. As payment for the asylum that has been granted he is voicing his views that, not by chance, happen to coincide with anti-Polish and antisocialist theses of his American principals. He is doing this for his own egotistic and narrow-minded interests, although he was indebted so much in the past to his former fatherland.

At present, when it has become clear that there will be no more privileges, he renounced his fatherland for Judas-like silver. This testifies to the fact that this renegade has abandoned all elementary theses of decency. This fact has met with the condemnation of employees of the Polish foreign service. This act of betrayal will be assessed by the competent organs of the administration of justice as it deserves. Solidarity Arrests 3:20 P.M. Monday, (9:20 A.M. Monday, E.S.T.)

English-language report by Tass, the official Soviet press agency: WARSAW, Dec. 21 - According to information provided by the press spokesman of the Prosecutor General's office, the civil and military units of Poland's Prosecutor's office were continuing their activities designed to stave off protest and strike actions. They were holding meetings and talks with factory crews. Among other things after such meetings and talks in the Katowice Province, strikes were broken off at the Z.M.P. colliery in Zory and the First of May and Anna coal mines in Modzislaw Slaski, as well as the coal industry repair works in Bytom. In Zielona Gora Province, after talks with the work force held by prosecutors and officers of the Polish army, work was resumed in nine factories.

Further summary legal actions taken among the persons arrested are: In Ostrowiec - Andrezej Dudek, member of Solidarity's National Commission; Marceli Czarnocki and Jerzy Jablowski, both members of the union's regional board, as well as Tadeusz Stasiowski, member of the Nowotki Steelworkers Solidarity Commission. They are charged with setting up a so-called Salvation Committee of the union at the works and proceeding to organize a strike. After the military commissar and prosecutor talked to the work force, the strike was discontinued.
