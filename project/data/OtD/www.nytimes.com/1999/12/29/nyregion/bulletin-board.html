BULLETIN BOARD

ANEMONA HARTOCOLLIS, KAREN W. ARENSON and JACQUES STEINBERG
Published: December 29, 1999

Diane Ravitch Drops Out

DIANE RAVITCH, the education historian and standards advocate, has resigned as education adviser to the Republican presidential campaign of GEORGE W. BUSH. Dr. Ravitch, who advised Governor Bush on three education speeches, has told associates that she resigned last month in protest after Mr. Bush said he probably would not meet with the Log Cabin Republicans, an organization of gay party members.

Dr. Ravitch, who was assistant secretary of education under Mr. Bush's father, President George Bush, declined to comment on her decision. Mindy Tucker, a spokeswoman for the Bush campaign, said of Dr. Ravitch, ''She's been a valued adviser to the campaign, and Governor Bush regrets her position.''

Changes Aplenty at City College

The trustees of the City University of New York have made change at its flagship campus, City College, one of their top priorities. If turnover in leadership is any measure, then the college has been in the midst of significant change for the past few months.

Last summer, the trustees named an acting president, Dr. STANFORD A. ROMAN Jr., above left, after pressuring YOLANDA MOSES to resign. Now the vice president for finance and management, NATHAN DICKMEYER, is leaving for Mercy College. LAURIE AUSTIN, CCNY's admissions director, is leaving to become director of recruitment for CUNY. And MARY LOU EDMONDSON, the former director of media relations at the American Museum of Natural History, has become assistant vice president of communication.

ZEEV DAGAN, a mechanical engineering professor, took over as provost. GEORGE RANALLI, a former Yale professor, became dean of the School of Architecture and Environmental Studies, and ALFRED S. POSAMENTIER, a City College education professor, was named interim dean of the School of Education. Last week, J. F. (JIM) WATTS, former chairman of the history department, was named acting dean of humanities and the arts.

Queens Checkmates Chancellors

When TERRI THOMSON, left, of Queens sealed Schools Chancellor RUDY CREW's fate last week with her vote not to renew his contract, some old-timers had a spell of deja vu. They remembered that it was a Queens member's vote that also sealed the fate of the last chancellor to be ousted by the Board of Education -- JOSEPH FERNANDEZ.

CAROL A. GRESSER of Queens cast the deciding vote in 1993 to remove Mr. Fernandez. She was the subject of intensive lobbying and was in tears when she announced her vote. This time, it was her successor, Ms. Thomson, who was urged to use her vote to end a chancellorship.

One difference between the two episodes is that the mayor in 1993 was a Democrat and Ms. Gresser bucked him. This time, Ms. Thomson sided with Mayor Rudolph W. Giuliani, a Republican whose office courted her strenuously.

''If you can see a pattern here, I'd be interested,'' Ms. Gresser said. ''To me, it just points up the fact that Queens is rather strong-minded about what it wants and doesn't want.'' ANEMONA HARTOCOLLIS, KAREN W. ARENSON and JACQUES STEINBERG
