CALENDAR; Buildings, And Trees Take Shape


Published: January 28, 1999

Images of Architecture

The New York Public Library Center for the Humanities, Fifth Avenue at 42d Street; (212) 869-8089.

''Order and Disorder: Architectural Transitions in Prints and Photographs,'' an exhibition showing buildings and other structures in all phases of their lives, from construction to decay to demolition, starts on Saturday and continues through April 3.

The 61 early-20th century prints in the show include views of New York City skyscrapers, Jacob Kainen's image of a rural town after a flood and a lithograph by Thornton Oakley of ship under construction in the World War I era.

There are also 97 photographs taken between 1852 and 1998. Included are views of ancient ruins by Maxime Du Camp; construction photographs taken by Edouard Baldus during the 1850's modern transformation of Paris; Civil War pictures by Alexander Gardner; shots of the 1906 San Francisco earthquake by George Knight, and Camilo Jose Vergara's 1980's depiction of the derelict Camden (N.J.) Public Library.

Open on Monday, and Thursday through Saturday, 10 A.M. to 6 P.M.; Tuesday and Wednesday, 11 A.M. to 7:30 P.M. No charge.

Living Sculpture

New York Botanical Garden, 200th Street and Southern Boulevard, Bedford Park, the Bronx; (718) 817-8700.

An exhibition of 200 topiaries in geometric shapes, including circles, cylinders, squares and cones, is on view at the garden's Enid A. Haupt Conservatory through April 4. Admission to the grounds, conservatory and children's garden is $6.50; students and those 65 and older, $4; children 2 to 12, $2.50. Grounds admission only, $3; students and the elderly, $2; children, $1. No charge on Wednesdays and Saturdays, 10 A.M. to noon.

Antiques at the Armory

Seventh Regiment Armory, 643 Park Avenue (66th Street); (212) 472-1180.

More than 100 dealers are to participate in an antiques show starting on Wednesday, from 4 to 9 P.M. The show will continue Feb. 4 through 6, noon to 9 P.M., and on Feb. 7, noon to 6 P.M. Admission is $10.

Exploring Stained Glass

American Bible Society, 1865 Broadway (61st Street); (212) 408-1236.

A gallery tour of the exhibition ''Glory in Glass: Stained Glass in the United States'' will be given tomorrow. The show runs through Feb. 16. The tour, at 6 P.M., will be led by Dr. Virginia Raguin, the curator.

On Saturday, from 9 A.M. to 4 P.M, a daylong symposium on preserving stained glass will take place. Conservators, restoration project directors and artisans will give successful case histories, including some featured in the exhibition. In the afternoon, a guided tour of several historic churches in the area will be given. No charge.

Summer Camp Fair

Ethical Culture School, 33 Central Park West (63d Street); (212) 712-6266.

Two camp fairs will be held this weekend. On Saturday, there will be representatives from teen-agers' summer programs, including sports, travel, community service and academic study camps. On Sunday, the focus will be on day camps, sleep-away camps and specialty camps in the New York region for children from pre-kindergarten through sixth grade. Both fairs last from noon to 3:30 P.M. and are free of charge.

Japanese Style

Japan Society, 333 East 47th Street; (212) 752-3015.

A lecture will be given tonight at 6:30 on design and style in Japan during the Edo period, from 1603 to 1867, focusing on the importance of ornamentation in ceramics, textiles and lacquerware. Admission, $10.
