PLAYING IN THE NEIGHBORHOOD

By MAUREEN C. MUENSTER
Published: April 11, 1999

FLATIRON

Swimming and Floating In New York's Waterways

As rising temperatures give rise to leisure on the water, the Van Alen Institute will offer a forum titled ''Swimming and Floating,'' which will focus on the city's waterways as recreational sites.

David Gissen, architect and curator of ''New York's Floating Bathhouses,'' an exhibition at the Lower East Side Tenement Museum through May 15, will recount a century's history of floating swimming pools. Lynette Widder, an architect, will present her own proposal, ''East River Shoreline Pool.'' And Cathy Drew, director of the River Project, a monitoring station on Pier 26 on the Hudson River, will discuss the improvement in New York's waterways and the interest in kayaking.

The image above is a finalist from the institute's ''Design Ideas for New York's East River'' exhibition and competition. It is an image of Greenpoint Piers in Brooklyn by the Dagmar Richter Studio in Berlin.

The Van Alen Institute is a civic organization devoted to projects in public architecture.

''Swimming and Floating,'' Wednesday; Van Alen Institute, 30 West 22d Street between Fifth Avenue and Avenue of the Americas; 6:30 P.M. to 8:30 P.M.; free; (212) 924-7000.

UPPER WEST SIDE

The Latest Refugees: New Faces, Same Struggle

''In a nation of immigrants and refugees, of extraordinary diversity, what is it that defines and makes a people?'' This question is posed by Jack Salzman, former director of the Center of American Culture Studies at Columbia University, as part of a photographic exhibition, ''Refuge: The Newest New Yorkers,'' on display at the New-York Historical Society, from Tuesday through Aug. 15.

The photographer, Mel Rosenthal, is photo editor of Culturefront, the magazine of the New York Council for the Humanities. His interest in refugees developed while he was photographing Cambodian and Vietnamese refugees living in the South Bronx more than 20 years ago.

Mr. Rosenthal photographed the two women pictured at left in 1995 at the Brighton Beach Ballet Theater. Dancers from Ukraine and Uzbekistan are among the many artists from the former Soviet Union who now dance and teach there.

''Refuge: The Newest New Yorkers,'' Tuesday through Aug. 15; New-York Historical Society, 2 West 77th Street, at Central Park West; Tuesdays through Sundays, 11 A.M. to 5 P.M.; $5 for adults, $3 for students and people over 65, free for children under 12; (212) 873-3400.

PROSPECT PARK

The Dodgers Aren't Back, But the Park's Ravine Is

A parade, the season's opening of the carousel and tours of the newly restored ravine will all be part of Opening Day on Saturday at Prospect Park in Brooklyn.

Two thousand Little Leaguers will march down Seventh Avenue toward the park's Long Meadow ball fields at Ninth Street, beginning at 10 A.M. Music will be provided by the Jackie Robinson Marching Steppers, the Dodgers Sym-Phony and others.

At 50 cents a ride, families can enjoy riding the hand-carved wooden goat, lion, dragon or 51 horses when the 87-year-old Coney-Island-style carousel reopens, from noon to 4:45 P.M. The Ravine tours start at 1 P.M. and 3 P.M.; they will be offered at the same times every Saturday and Sunday. One can enter the park at Ninth Street and Prospect Park West, cross the roadway, then walk down the path to the fence.

Lefferts Homestead Children's Historic House Museum will offer traditional Dutch treats, hoop games, garden activities and other events, 1 P.M. to 5 P.M.

''Opening Day at the Park,'' youth baseball parade begins at Carroll Street and Seventh Avenue; Saturday, 10 A.M.; opening ceremony 10:30 A.M.; Lefferts Homestead, in the park, near intersection of Flatbush Avenue and Empire Boulevard; free; (718) 965-8999.

ALSO AROUND TOWN

GREEN MUSIC -- The Queens Symphonic Band opens its 28th season Saturday with ''A Concert on the Village Green,'' featuring works by John Williams, Rossini, Von Suppe, Sousa and others; Queensborough Community College Theater; 56th Avenue and Springfield Boulevard, Bayside; 8 P.M.; tickets $10, $8 for those 62 or over; (718) 631-6311.

SOPRANO SELECTIONS -- Alessandra Marc, soprano, will sing selections by Verdi, Handel and Berlioz today at 2 P.M.; Walt Whitman Theater, Brooklyn College, Hillel Place and Avenue H; $30 orchestra, $25 mezzanine; part of the Brooklyn Center for the Performing Arts' Musical Masters series, (718) 951-4500.

IN STITCHES -- The whole family can learn to cross-stitch on Saturday and next Sunday, Staten Island Children's Museum; American Needlepoint Guild and Embroiderers' Guild of America will hold a beginners' class 1 P.M. to 4 P.M.; Snug Harbor Cultural Center, 1000 Richmond Terrace, near Snug Harbor Road; museum admission $4, children under 2 free; (718) 273-2060, ext. 121.
