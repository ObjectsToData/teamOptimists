CONNECTICUT GUIDE

By ELEANOR CHARLES
Published: June 26, 1994

A JULIA CHILD FEAST

If veal and pork pate with pistachios in a pastry crust, grilled boneless leg of lamb, grilled medallions of tuna with a puree of black olives and sun-dried tomatoes, roast breast of chicken with 40 cloves of garlic, and lemon meringue tart sound like your kind of picnic, call the Long Wharf Theater for reservations.

A three-day adventure in gastronomy led by Julia Child will begin with the elegant Provence-style picnic on Tuesday. Be advised that this is no come-as-you-are affair. Summer whites and gauzy gowns will prevail, as will a ticket price of $175 per person.

The festivities will begin under a tent at 5:45 with a silent auction of food-related trips, cookware, gift certificates, antiques and books. At 7:30 the assemblage will move into the Stage II Theater for dinner, to be served onstage and in the scenery dock. The feast will be prepared to Ms. Child's specifications by Cynthia and Michel Keller of Restaurant du Village in Chester.

On Wednesday at 8 P.M. and Thursday at 2 P.M. Ms. Child will give two cooking demonstrations. Among the dishes she will prepare are butterflied roast pork, granny potatoes, walnut cake, pissaladiere (quiche), salade nicoise and lemon sherbet. Tickets are $35 each day and the Williams Sonoma cookware used in the demonstrations will be auctioned off at the end of each event. Call 787-4282 for reservations. JUNIOR MARINERS

Marine oriented summer courses for children entering grades 1 through 7 will start next month, organized by the Norwalk Community-Technical College and the Norwalk Maritime Center. Three sessions are scheduled from July 5 through 15, July 18 through 29 and Aug. 1 through 12.

Mini-mariners in grades 1 through 3 will meet from 8:45 to noon at the Maritime Center on North Water Street. Science experiments, hands-on classes with marine animals, work in the wet lab, sports, crafts, games, field trips and trips aboard research vessels exploring the Norwalk River and Long Island Sound are included in the fee of $216, or $240 for the longer sessions.

Children in grades 4 through 7 may choose the Salty Sailors courses for beginners or intermediates, held at the Norwalk Sailing School at Calf Pasture Beach from 9 to 4. Sailing on a 12-foot O'Day Wigeon, crewing on a 25-foot cruising sloop and windsurfing are some of the activities for beginners. Intermediates will have additional courses in plotting, chart reading and racing, using larger boats and catamarans on trips to the Norwalk Islands. Lunch is included in the $333 and $370 fees, depending on length of the session. Call 857-7080 to enroll or obtain more information. CIVIL WAR CYCLORAMA

Brian Kammerer, who paints cycloramas, will start and finish his third chef-d'oeuvre at the New Canaan Library on Friday and Saturday from 10 A.M. to 5 P.M., when people may come and watch him work.

Two years ago he painted a large cyclorama in his studio depicting the town's traditional Fourth of July picnic in Waveny Park, and last year he did "God's Little Acre," the hilltop circle at the end of downtown that contains several historic churches. He sold them to local buyers for around $10,000 each, according to the library.

His subject this year is Pickett's Charge in the Civil War, and it will measure 20 feet long by 5 feet high. During the two days that he will be painting, and until July 10, his 20-minute video of Civil War battles, filmed with miniature soldiers and horses, will be shown several times daily, and his diorama boxes of Civil War scenes will be on display, along with a rubber stamp kit of Civil War subjects that he will make up for sale on order.

The library is located at 151 Main Street. Call 966-1985 for more information. FARMERS' MARKETS

More than 40 Connecticut farmers are harvesting their seasonal fruits and vegetables and hauling them to cities and towns for sale at weekly Farmer's Markets. According to the state's Department of Agriculture, customer interest in these markets has increased markedly in the past few years, spurring family farms to greater production.

Among those open now are Danielson's Farmers' Market at 369 Main Street, Saturdays from 9 to noon through October; New Haven Farmers' Market at Pitkin Plaza and Orange Street, Wednesdays 9 to 2 and Saturdays 9 to 1 through October; New Milford Farmers' Market, 10 Main Street at Town Hall, Saturdays 9 to noon through October; Putnam Farmers' Market, 319 Kennedy Street, Thursdays 3 to 6 through October, and Weston Farmers' Market at Weston Elementary School, Saturdays 7:30 to 10:30 until October 2.

Others will open during the first two weeks in July; a complete list is available by calling 566-4845. OATES, THE PLAYWRIGHT

Joyce Carol Oates will pay a visit to the Aldrich Museum of Contemporary Art in Ridgefield today, to attend a reading of two of her short plays and discuss her work with the audience.

Like many of her novels, the plays focus on intimate relationships. "The Key" deals with two middle-aged people who begin a courtship when they meet on a Caribbean vacation, and "The Sacrifice" is the recounting by a man and a woman of their problems, each one putting quite a different spin on the same story.

In addition to her career as a re knowned novelist, Ms. Oates is the Roger S. Berlind Professor in the Humanities at Princeton, and she has been a member of the American Academy of Arts and Letters since 1978.

The reading will begin at 3 P.M., refreshments are included in the admission price of $10. The museum is at 258 Main Street and the phone number for reservations or more information is 438-4519. ELEANOR CHARLES
