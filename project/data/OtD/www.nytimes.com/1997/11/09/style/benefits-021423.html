BENEFITS


Published: November 9, 1997

WPA Theater Evening

TONIGHT -- The WPA Theater will benefit from the final performance of ''Queen Amarantha,'' written by and starring Charles Busch, at its playhouse, 519 West 23d Street. The performance, at 7:30 P.M., will be followed by a buffet supper at Rome, 290 Eighth Avenue (25th Street). Tickets, $125, from (212) 206-0523 or at the door.

Helping Out in Harlem

MONDAY -- The Harlem Dowling-West Side Center for Children and Family Services will benefit from a dinner at Sylvia's Restaurant, 360 Lenox Avenue (126th Street). The actors Ruby Dee and Ossie Davis will be the evening's hosts. The event will begin with a cocktail reception at 6 and will feature live music and a raffle of two expense-paid trips to the Netherlands, where the winners will meet the mayor of Haarlem. Tickets, $200, from (212) 749-3656, ext. 544.

Advocates for Learning

TUESDAY -- A dinner at the University Club, 1 West 54th Street, will benefit the Public Education Association, an organization that encourages parents' involvement in their children's education. David V. B. Britt, the president of the Children's Television Workshop, will be one of those honored, and the jazz choir of Talent Unlimited High School in Manhattan will perform. The evening will begin with cocktails at 6:30. Black tie. Tickets, $500, from (212) 675-9474.

A 75-Year-Old Alliance

THURSDAY -- The Federation of Protestant Welfare Agencies will hold a gala at the Plaza to mark its 75th anniversary. The Rev. Dr. Herbert B. Anderson, the minister of Brick Presbyterian Church, will be honored. The evening will begin with a reception at 6:30. Dinner and dancing to the Michael Carney Orchestra will follow. Black tie. Tickets, $500, from (212) 675-9474.

Fancy Footworks

THURSDAY -- Sandals autographed or decorated by such celebrities as Bill Cosby, Isaac Mizrahi, Matt Lauer, Sigourney Weaver, Tom Brokaw and Todd Oldham will be among items for sale in a silent auction by the Children's Hope Foundation to raise money for children with AIDS and H.I.V. and their families. The event, from 7:30 to 11 P.M. at the Sony Plaza, 550 Madison Avenue (56th Street), will include drinks and a buffet supper. Tickets, $85, from (212) 979-9206, or $105 at the door.

50 Years in Perspective

FRIDAY -- The School of Visual Arts will celebrate its 50th anniversary with a masked ball at the 69th Regiment Armory, Lexington Avenue at 26th Street. It will run from 7 P.M. to midnight, with drinks, a buffet and musical performances by the Illinois Jacquet Big Band and the Uptown Horns. Proceeds will go to scholarships at the school. Tickets, $75, from (212) 592-2302.

Gathering for Liberty

NEXT SUNDAY -- The film maker Michael Moore will be the host of a black-tie gala at the Waldorf-Astoria benefiting People for the American Way, the civil-liberties organization. Maya Angelou will read a work written for the occasion, the critic Robert Hughes will speak and Dance Theater of Harlem will perform. Cocktails at 6 P.M. will be followed by dinner, the program and dancing to Peter Duchin and his orchestra. Tickets, $500, from (212) 944-5820.

Party at the Piers

NEXT SUNDAY -- A day of sports from 11 A.M. to 2 P.M. in the Field House at Chelsea Piers, near 23d Street on the Hudson River, will raise money for pediatric services at New York University Medical Center. Games for children and adults will include basketball, volleyball and soccer, and participants can try out gymnastics and rock-climbing equipment. Former New York Knick Walt Frazier will be on hand to sign autographs. Refreshments will be served. Tickets, $125, or $50 for those 12 and under, from (212) 263-5870.

Heritage at the Copacabana

NOV. 17 -- Ruben Blades, Marc Anthony, Tito Nieves and Ednita Nazario will sing at a black-tie dinner and dance at the Copacabana, 617 West 57th Street, in celebration of Puerto Rican Heritage Month. The evening will raise money for college scholarships for Puerto Rican students, administered by Comite Noviembre, a coalition of eight social service, cultural and economic development organizations. Cocktails at 6:30. Tickets, $250, from (212) 807-1337.

Cabaret for a Cure

NOV. 17 -- Mary Cleere Haran will sing at the Cure for Lymphoma Foundation's third annual Cabaret for a Cure dinner at the Plaza. Dr. Vincent T. DeVita, the director of the Yale Cancer Center, will be honored. The black-tie evening will begin with drinks and a silent auction at 7. Tickets, $350, from (212) 245-6570.

Library Lions

NOV. 17 -- Dr. Johnetta B. Cole, the president of Spelman College, and Robert Hughes, the art critic, will be among the ''Library Lions'' honored at a black-tie dinner to benefit the research libraries of the New York Public Library. Robert MacNeil will be the master of ceremonies for the evening at the Center for the Humanities at the library, Fifth Avenue at 42d Street. Drinks at 7. Tickets, $1,500, from (212) 930-0730.

College Aid for Indians

NOV. 17 -- Bill Richardson, the United States delegate to the United Nations, will be honored at a dinner at the Waldorf-Astoria to raise money for the American Indian College Fund. The actors Winona Ryder and Ethan Hawke will be on hand, and the Silver Cloud Singers and Bill Miller, an Indian flutist and guitarist, will perform. A reception and silent auction of Indian art will be followed by dinner and the performances. Black tie or tribal dress. Tickets, $500, from (212) 787-6312.
