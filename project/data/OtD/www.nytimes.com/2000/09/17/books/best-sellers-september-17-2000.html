BEST SELLERS: September 17, 2000


Published: September 17, 2000

Weeks

This Last On

Week Week List Fiction

1 1 2 THE BEAR AND THE DRAGON, by Tom Clancy. (Putnam, $28.95.) Aided by the antiterrorism specialist John Clark, President Jack Ryan contends with sinister forces afoot in Russia and China.

2 1 THE SWITCH, by Sandra Brown. (Warner, $25.95.) A woman serving as a legendary astronaut's media escort searches for her identical twin sister's killer.

3 4 2 OPEN HOUSE, by Elizabeth Berg. (Random House, $23.95.) A newly divorced woman attempts to rebuild her life by taking in a series of boarders.

4 3 5 WINTER SOLSTICE, by Rosamunde Pilcher. (Thomas Dunne/St. Martin's, $27.95.) As Christmas approaches, five lost souls in the north of Scotland discover the healing power of love.

5 2 2 FAITH OF THE FALLEN, by Terry Goodkind. (Tor/ Tom Doherty, $27.95.) In Volume 6 of the ''Sword of Truth'' fantasy series, Richard is captured.

6 5 5 DUST TO DUST, by Tami Hoag. (Bantam, $25.95.) Two Minneapolis police officers investigate the suspicious death of an Internal Affairs cop.

7 10 15 THE INDWELLING, by Tim LaHaye and Jerry B. Jenkins. (Tyndale, $22.99.) Volume 7 in a series about true believers who confront the Antichrist after the rapture of the saved. (+)

8 8 4 STALKER, by Faye Kellerman. (Morrow, $25.) While Peter Decker of the L.A.P.D. is distracted by a string of carjackings, someone is terrorizing his daughter.

9 7 5 PURPLE CANE ROAD, by James Lee Burke. (Doubleday, $24.95.) Dave Robicheaux, a Louisiana sheriff's deputy, searches for his mother's killers.

10 6 10 THE HOUSE ON HOPE STREET, by Danielle Steel. (Delacorte, $19.95.) A woman with five children must cope with the loss of her husband.

11 9 7 *NOT A DAY GOES BY, by E. Lynn Harris. (Doubleday, $19.95.) The tumultuous romance between a sports agent and an up-and-coming Broadway star.

12 1 FOUR BLONDES, by Candace Bushnell. (Atlantic Monthly, $24.) The love lives of glamorous Manhattan women as seen by the author of ''Sex and the City.''

13 13 6 MR. PERFECT, by Linda Howard. (Pocket Books, $24.95.) After four women compile a list of the attributes an ideal man would possess, one of them is murdered.

14 1 WILD JUSTICE, by Phillip Margolin. (HarperCollins, $26.) Is a brilliant surgeon responsible for a series of hideous crimes?

15 1 MORGAN'S RUN, by Colleen McCullough. (Simon & Schuster, $28.) A British convict, banished to Australia in 1788, finds a new life and a new love.

16 11 31 *THE BRETHREN, by John Grisham. (Doubleday, $27.95.) Three former judges, doing time at a federal prison in Florida, concoct a mail scam that goes awry.

This Last On

Week Week List Nonfiction

1 1 NOTHING LIKE IT IN THE WORLD, by Stephen E. Ambrose. (Simon & Schuster, $28.) The story of the men who built the transcontinental railroad in the 1860's.

2 4 7 LIFE ON THE OTHER SIDE, by Sylvia Browne with Lindsay Harrison. (Dutton, $23.95.) A guided tour of the afterlife, by a ''working psychic.''

3 1 152 TUESDAYS WITH MORRIE, by Mitch Albom. (Doubleday, $19.95.) The author tells of his weekly visits to his old college mentor, who was near death's door.

4 2 16 IT'S NOT ABOUT THE BIKE, by Lance Armstrong with Sally Jenkins. (Putnam, $24.95.) A memoir by the Tour de France champion and cancer survivor.

5 5 18 FLAGS OF OUR FATHERS, by James Bradley with Ron Powers. (Bantam, $24.95.) The story of the six men who raised the flag at Iwo Jima.

6 3 16 ME TALK PRETTY ONE DAY, by David Sedaris. (Little, Brown, $22.95.) A collection of autobiographical comic essays by the author of ''Naked.''

7 10 2 SELLOUT, by David P. Schippers with Alan P. Henry. (Regnery, $27.95.) The former chief Republican counsel for the House Judiciary Committee offers an account of President Clinton's impeachment. (+)

8 8 92 *THE GREATEST GENERATION, by Tom Brokaw. (Random House, $24.95.) The lives of men and women who came of age during the Depression and World War II.

9 6 13 IN A SUNBURNED COUNTRY, by Bill Bryson. (Broadway, $25.) The author of ''A Walk in the Woods'' reports on his travels through Australia.

10 12 83 THE ART OF HAPPINESS, by the Dalai Lama and Howard C. Cutler. (Riverhead, $22.95.) What Buddhism and common sense tell us about everyday problems.

11 1 THE ARROGANCE OF POWER, by Anthony Summers with Robbyn Swan. (Viking, $29.95.) An Irish journalist describes ''the secret world of Richard Nixon.''

12 11 7 AMERICAN RHAPSODY, by Joe Eszterhas. (Knopf, $25.95.) The Lewinsky affair and Hollywood, as seen by the man who wrote ''Basic Instinct'' and ''Showgirls.''

13 9 13 KITCHEN CONFIDENTIAL, by Anthony Bourdain. (Bloomsbury, $24.95.) A memoir-expose by the executive chef at Brasserie Les Halles in New York.

14 7 17 *IN THE HEART OF THE SEA, by Nathaniel Philbrick. (Viking, $24.95.) An account of the whaleship Essex, whose disastrous fate in the early 19th century would later serve as inspiration for Melville.

15 2 HOW TO READ AND WHY, by Harold Bloom. (Scribner, $25.) The Sterling professor of humanities at Yale offers instruction in how best to read literary texts.

Week Week List Children's Chapter Books

1 1 9 HARRY POTTER AND THE GOBLET OF FIRE, by J. K. Rowling. (Levine/Scholastic, $25.95.) A British boy takes part in a series of contests against other aspiring wizards. (Ages 10 and up)

2 2 52 HARRY POTTER AND THE PRISONER OF AZKABAN, by J. K. Rowling. (Levine/Scholastic, $19.95.) A British boy's life at witchcraft school. (Ages 10 and up)

3 4 91 HARRY POTTER AND THE SORCERER'S STONE, by J. K. Rowling. (Levine/Scholastic, $19.95.) A British boy attends a school for witchcraft. (Ages 10 and up)

4 3 66 HARRY POTTER AND THE CHAMBER OF SECRETS, by J. K. Rowling. (Levine/Scholastic, $19.95.) A British boy finds trouble on his return to a witchcraft school. (Ages 10 and up)

5 5 9 BACKSTREET BOYS: The Official Book, by Andre Csillag with the Backstreet Boys. (Delacorte, $14.95.) A photographic scrapbook. (All ages)

6 1 THE ROYAL DIARIES: Anastasia, the Last Grand Duchess, by Carolyn Meyer. (Scholastic, $10.95.) A fictional journal kept by the youngest daughter of Czar Nicholas II. (Ages 9 to 12)

7 9 10 BUD, NOT BUDDY, by Christopher Paul Curtis. (Delacorte, $15.95.) A 10-year-old in Depression-era Flint, Mich., sets out to find his father. (Ages 8 to 12)

8 4 OLYMPIC SUMMER GAMES 2000, by Meredith Costain. (Puffin, $3.99.) A guide to the Olympic Games in Sydney, Australia. (Ages 9 to 12)

9 6 8 THE BAD BEGINNING, by Lemony Snicket. (HarperCollins, $8.95.) The three Baudelaire siblings find misfortune everywhere they turn. (Ages 10 and up)

10 8 9 THE LITTLE PRINCE, written and illustrated by Antoine de Saint-Exupery. Translated by Richard Howard. (Harcourt, $18.) A poet's new English rendition of the French classic. (All ages)

Rankings reflect sales, for the week ending Sept. 2, at almost 4,000 bookstores plus wholesalers serving 50,000 other retailers (gift shops, department stores, newsstands, supermarkets), statistically weighted to represent all such outlets nationwide. An asterisk (*) indicates that a book's sales are barely distinguishable from those of the book above. A dagger (+)indicates that some bookstores report receiving bulk orders. Expanded rankings are available at The New York Times on the Web: www.nytimes.com/books. Best-seller lists for children's picture books, children's chapter books and children's paperbacks, respectively, are published in rotation on succeeding weeks. Publishers have provided the age designations for their best-selling children's titles. Rankings for children's paperbacks do not include serials.
