Sandra Miller and Ben Doyle

  • Sign In to E-Mail This
  • Printer-Friendly
  • Reprints
  • Save Article
Published: August 14, 2005

Sandra Miller and Ben Doyle, both poets, were married last evening in Canandaigua, N.Y. The Rev. Patricia M. Palmer, an aunt of the bride and a minister of the Church of Religious Science, officiated.

Skip to next paragraph

Forum: Weddings and Celebrations

The bride had a collection of her poems, "Oriflamme," published in March by Ahsahta Press. The bridegroom's book, "Radio Radio" (Louisiana State University Press, 2001), won the Walt Whitman Award from the Academy of American Poets in 2000. They met at the University of Iowa, where they received Master of Fine Arts degrees in poetry.

In September, Ms. Miller, who is 31 and is keeping her name, is to become a resident artist through the Greater Columbus Arts Council in Ohio, giving readings and teaching poetry workshops in the public schools there. She graduated from the University of Washington and received a master's degree in humanities from the University of Chicago. Her previous marriage ended in divorce.

The bride is a daughter of Kari Palmer Miller and Harry C. Miller Jr. of Great Falls, Va. Her father retired as a professor of urology at George Washington University, where he was also the chief of urology at George Washington University Hospital. Her mother is a consultant in Great Falls who helps clients find nursing homes.

Mr. Doyle, 32, is an assistant professor of English at Denison University in Granville, Ohio, where he teaches creative writing and literature. He graduated from West Virginia University.

He is a son of Constance Gifford Doyle and Michael J. Doyle of Cuba, N.Y. His mother teaches fourth grade at Rushford Elementary School in Rushford, N.Y. His father is the program manager for mathematics, science and technology and environmental education in the Olean, N.Y., office of the Board of Cooperative Educational Services.
