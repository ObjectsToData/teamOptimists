Andrea Straus and Paul Shurin

  • Sign In to E-Mail This
  • Printer-Friendly
  • Reprints
  • Save Article
Published: December 25, 2005

Andrea Clio Straus and Dr. Paul Anton Shurin were married on Tuesday evening at Congregation Kol Ami in White Plains. Rabbi Tom Weiner performed the ceremony.

Skip to next paragraph

Forum: Weddings and Celebrations

Mrs. Shurin, 54, is the deputy executive director of AIDS-Related Community Services, a nonprofit organization in Hawthorne, N.Y., serving people with AIDS in the Hudson Valley. She graduated from Kirkland College in Clinton, N.Y., and received a master's degree in social work from Hunter College. The bride's previous marriage ended in divorce.

She is a daughter of Rebecca and Nathan Straus III of South Salem, N.Y. Her mother retired as an associate professor of humanities at the New York University School of Continuing Education. Her father is the president of FSP International Corporation in South Salem, which distributes replacement parts for restaurant equipment.

The bride is a granddaughter of Nathan Straus Jr., who was an owner of WMCA-AM, a onetime New York radio station, and a great-granddaughter of Nathan Straus, an owner of Abraham & Straus and of R. H. Macy & Company, the department store chains.

Dr. Shurin, 64, is an associate professor of pediatrics at Albert Einstein College of Medicine in the Bronx. He is also an associate pediatrician specializing in infectious diseases at Children's Hospital at Montefiore Medical Center, also in the Bronx. He graduated from Brooklyn College and received a medical degree from Harvard. The bridegroom's two previous marriages ended in divorce.

He is the son of Sally and Bernard Shurin, who lived in Larchmont, N.Y. The bridegroom's mother was a kindergarten teacher at Public School 153 in Brooklyn. His father was a New York City firefighter in Brooklyn and later a chiropractor, who practiced in Eastchester, N.Y.
