On the Street

New York City

TIMES SQUARE SUMMER BLOCK PARTY, 46th Street, from Seventh to Eighth Avenues, Manhattan. Tomorrow, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Sponsored by the Church of Scientology.

McBURNEY Y.M.C.A. FAIR, Avenue of the Americas, from 14th to 23rd Streets, Chelsea. Tomorrow, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.

ANNUAL VILLAGE FAIR, Broadway, from Houston to Grand Streets. Sunday, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m. Sponsored by Old St. Patrick's Cathedral School.

GREAT JULY FOURTH FESTIVAL, Water Street, between Moore and Fulton Streets, Lower Manhattan. Monday, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m. Sponsored by the Pearl Street Park Association.

NEW YORK AND INDEPENDENCE DAY WALKING TOUR visits Lower Manhattan sites like St. Paul's Chapel, Fraunces Tavern and Federal Hall. Meets tomorrow at 1 p.m. at 233 Broadway, at Park Row. Led by Justin Ferate: Tours of the City. Fee: $20. Information: (212) 758-7893.

HISTORIC LOWER MANHATTAN AND THE FINANCIAL DISTRICT WALKING TOUR visits sites associated with the beginnings of New York, including the first capital building and the residence of George Washington. Meets Sunday at 1 p.m. at 1 Broadway. Sponsored by I'll Take Manhattan Tours. Fee: $12. Information: (732) 270-5559.

'HIGHLIGHTS OF HEROES OF THE AMERICAN REVOLUTION,' Lower Manhattan, visits sites of importance in the lives of Washington, Jefferson, John Adams and others. Today and tomorrow at 10 a.m., noon and 2 and 5 p.m.; Sunday at 9 and 11 a.m. and 1 and 3 p.m.; Monday at 10 a.m., noon and 2 and 4 p.m. Fee: $6; 14 and younger, $4. Sponsored by Gotham Walk. Reservations: (212) 629-1886.

THE HISTORIC (OLD) NEW YORK CITY WALKING TOUR focuses on the history and architecture of Lower Manhattan, with stops at the New York Stock Exchange, Federal Hall and St. Paul's Chapel. Meets Sunday at 2 p.m. at Trinity Church, Broadway and Wall Street. Sponsored by New York City Cultural Walking Tours. Fee: $10. Information: (212) 979-2388.

ALL NIGHT WALKING TOUR OF REVOLUTIONARY WAR NEW YORK, Lower Manhattan. Covers sites of historic and cultural importance, led by James Kaplan. Meets Monday at 2 a.m. at Broadway and Park Place, and ends at 6 a.m. in front of Fraunces Tavern Museum, 54 Pearl Street, the sponsor of the walk. Fee: $20; members, $15. A free open house at the museum is planned, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Information: (212) 425-1778.

'PATRIOTS AND REBELS,' a tour of sites relevant to the Revolutionary War, meeting Sunday at 2 p.m. in front of Bowling Green Park, at Bowling Green and Broadway, Lower Manhattan. Sponsored by Bowery and Canal Walking Tours. Fee: $12; students and 65+, $10. Information: (212) 979-8013.

'PRE-FIREWORKS PRESIDENTIAL GREENWICH VILLAGE AND GRAMERCY PARK' covers sites associated with Abraham Lincoln, Franklin D. Roosevelt, George Washington and others. Tomorrow through Monday at 2:30 and 5 p.m.; the 5 p.m. tour ends at 7:30 p.m. at Franklin D. Roosevelt Drive and 23rd Street, one of the areas from which to view the holiday fireworks display. Sponsored by NYC Discovery Tours. Fee: $13. Meeting place and reservations: (212) 465-3331.

'REVOLUTIONARY NEW YORK,' Lower Manhattan. A walking tour of sites associated with George Washington, including Fraunces Tavern, Federal Hall and places where he really did sleep. Meets Monday at 1 p.m. at City Hall Park, Broadway and Murray Street. Sponsored by Big Onion Walking Tours. Fee: $15; 63+, $12; students, $10. Information: (212) 439-1090.

'SUMMER NIGHTS, LIGHTS AND DELIGHTS; SECOND ANNUAL FREEDOM WALK' combines a look at patriotic sites in Manhattan and Brooklyn with a walk across the Brooklyn Bridge. Meets tomorrow at 7 p.m. inside the Blimpie restaurant, 38 Park Row, Lower Manhattan. Fee: $15. Sponsored by Dr. Phil: New York Talks and Walks. Information: (888) 377-4455.

Events

New York City

RIVER TO RIVER FESTIVAL 2005, lawn of Battery Park, entrance at State Street, between Bridge and Pearl Streets, Lower Manhattan. A free concert featuring Laura Cantrell, singer; Stephen Malkmus and his band, the Jicks; and the trio Yo La Tengo. Monday at 3:30 p.m.; take seating. Information: RivertoRiverNYC.com.

'MACY'S FOURTH OF JULY FIREWORKS: AMERICAN MEDLEY,' the annual display over the East River, fired from barges from East 23rd to East 42nd Streets and in Lower Manhattan. Monday about 9:20 p.m. In Manhattan, the best view is along the Franklin D. Roosevelt Drive, from 14th to 42nd Streets, and at the South Street Seaport. In Brooklyn, best views are from the Brooklyn Heights Promenade, Empire Fulton Ferry State Park and Furman Street, from Atlantic Avenue to Cadman Plaza West, and points between Joralemon and Cranberry Streets with a clear view of the sky over the East River; also in Greenpoint. In Queens, appropriate spots are those with a view of that section of the East River, including Hunters Point. In New Jersey, the display can be seen at Liberty State Park in Jersey City. The fireworks show, with recorded music performed by the New York Pops, conducted by Skitch Henderson, and recorded segments with Mariah Carey, will be broadcast on Channel 4, beginning at 9; simultaneous broadcast on 1010 WINS. Information: (212) 494-4495. For those viewing from boats: (212) 494-5243.FIREWORKS WATCH ON ROOSEVELT ISLAND Viewing of the Macy's fireworks display from the southern point of the island, beginning Monday at 9:20 p.m. Entertainment, including juggling, face-painting and music, begins at 5 p.m. Sponsored by the Roosevelt Island Operating Corporation. Tickets, at $18, can be bought at the Manhattan tram station or online, www.rioc.com. The island can be reached by tram from the station at Second Avenue and 60th Street (fare, $2 one way; under 5, free). By bus, the Q102 bus from Long Island City, Queens; by car, over the 36th Avenue Bridge at 21st Street, Long Island City; by subway, the F train to the Roosevelt Island stop. Information: (212) 832-4540.
