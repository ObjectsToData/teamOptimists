LONG ISLAND GUIDE


Published: September 10, 1989

'STRIKER UP'

It's ''striker up'' time in Old Bethpage today. No, the residents of the mid-19th century Old Bethpage Village Restoration are not walking a picket line; rather, they will be playing baseball the way it was played in the 1860's.

So at 2 P.M., when the Mineola Washingtons take to the field against the Hempstead Eurekas, they will resort to 19th-century rules and with appropriate reproduced equipment. For example, when a fly ball is caught barehanded on the first bounce (padded gloves were not in use then), it will be an out.

Admission information and directions: 420-5280. PRINTMAKING IN FRANCE

If you need a theme around which to wrap a semester of scholarly and cultural events - and Hofstra University seems to thrive on this approach - then a celebration of the bicentennial of the French Revolution is perhaps such an avenue to pursue.

Accordingly, the activities, which will eventually include a three-day academic conference, get under way this week with ''Printmaking in 19th-Century France: Selections from the Georgia Museum of Art.''

On loan from the Athens (Ga.)-based museum, the 47 works illustrate printmaking during a part of the 19th century characterized by both experimentation and refinement of earlier techniques. Included are pieces by Daumier, Redon, Toulouse-Lautrec, Cassett and Millet.

The show opens Tuesday at the Emily Lowe Gallery of the Hofstra Museum and runs through Oct. 30. A preview reception, featuring a lecture by Olivier Bernier on ''Printed Revolution: Words and Images,'' will be held today at 1 P.M.

For reservations for today's program and regular exhibition hours, call 560-5672. FILMS ON LATIN AMERICA

Using seven movies, the Humanities Institute at SUNY/Stony Brook and the Village Cinema are presenting ''Film Portraits of Latin America.''

The Monday night series of screenings and lectures, which begins Sept. 18 with ''Bye Bye Brazil,'' will be introduced tomorrow at 7:30 P.M., when Richard Pena of the New York Film Festival presents a free slide-lecture, ''Assessing Latin-American Film Cinema: A Historical Perspective.''

The program will take place at Theater Three in Port Jefferson, where all the films will be shown.

For ticket information and a complete schedule, call 632-7765. MUSIC AT BROOKHAVEN

There is more taking place at Brookhaven National Laboratory in Upton than physics, chemistry, biology and the like. Would you believe music?

In addition to the annual series that imports outstanding chamber-music ensembles from all over, the Lab's Berkner Hall also is starting to showcase local groups.

The North Shore Pro Musica is first on the list, appearing Thursday at 8:30 P.M. in a concert that includes works by Haydn, Rachmaninoff, Hindemith and Brahms.

Ticket information: 282-2345. 'MIKADO' IN MIME

The East End Special Players are indeed ''special.'' Consisting of learning disabled adults, many with speech impediments, the company performs entirely in mime.

Under Suzanne Plave's direction, the Players will bring the ''Mikado in Mime'' to the Clare Rose Playhouse at St. Joseph's College in Patchogue. The three-performance run (Friday through next Sunday) is designed to prove that ''in the arts, there is only ability, there is no disability.''

Ticket information: 654-0199. HOUSE/GARDEN TOUR

Waiting until most of the summer resort hoopla dies down, the Mattituck Historical Society will offer its annual House and Garden Tour Saturday.

Included on the list is a home on the National Register of Historic Places. It was built in 1829, added to in 1868, and, in all its Victorian glory, was visited by Theodore Roosevelt and the actress Marie Dressler.

Also on the tour are an 1860 farmhouse, an 1892 mansion, the Jim and Connie Cross home and gardens and a contemporary house.

The tour is from noon to 5 P.M. Ticket information: 298-9437. SALUTING LONG ISLAND

Since Grucci Fireworks and corporate sponsors have stepped into pick up the tab, the fifth annual ''Salute to Long Island - Grucci Style'' will be held Saturday, as originally and tentatively scheduled, at Bald Hill Cultural Park in Farmingville.

Actually, it's a two-day event that begins Friday evening, when Tommy James & The Shondells perform at a benefit ($5 a ticket) for Long Island Cultural Arts.

At 7:30 P.M. Saturday, the Long Island Philharmonic, under Christopher Keene's baton, plays Bernstein, Grieg, Liszt, J. Strauss and Wagner, followed by a 27-minute fireworks display at 9:30 P.M.

This event is free, but for every Pepsi-Cola bottle cap, Entenmann wrapper or King Kullen receipt brought to the Union Savings Bank until the end of the salute, the sponsors will contribute about 10 cents to the United Way of Long Island. READING FOR LITERACY

With a passel of big-name literary lights living within local boundaries, the Literacy Volunteers of America-Suffolk County have come up with an ideal way to raise funds for their favorite cause.

An event scheduled for Saturday will include, in addition to music and food, prominent resident authors reading from their works. Among them will be Susan Isaacs, J. Anthony Lucas, Richard Elman, Don Matheson, Stephen Gaines, Judith Rossner, David Leavitt, Brian Kates and Civ Sedering.

Introducing the authors will be Melba Tolliver of News 12, Walter Isaacson of Time magazine, Douglass Watt of The Daily News and Stewart Kampel, editor of the Long Island Weekly of The New York Times.

The benefit, at $75 a person, is from 4 to 8 P.M. at the East Hampton home of Bill and Laraine Hayes.

For reservations, call 286-1649. FESTIVE FAIRS

Put on your walking shoes because, starting Saturday, it's time to trip the fall fair-festival circuit. For example, from 10 A.M. to 5 P.M. at the Sands-Willets House on Port Washington Boulevard in Port Washington, the Cow Neck Peninsula Historical Society holds its annual Cow Neck Family Fair. Admission: $1.50 or 50 cents for those under 6. Or how about the Bellmore Family Street Fair? It's from 11 A.M. to 6 P.M. on Bedford Street, where you will pay only for what you buy. The entertainment is free.

The same pay-for-what-you-buy situation prevails at the Hempstead Seafood Festival from 11 A.M. to dusk Saturday and 11 A.M. to 6 P.M. next Sunday at Lido Beach Park.

Regular admission fees are in effect at Old Bethpage Village Restoration (420-5281), where the ''American Food Festival'' takes over from 10 A.M. to 4 P.M. Saturday and next Sunday, and at Bayard Cutting Arboretum in Oakdale, where it will cost $3 a car to catch the Fall Flower and Harvest Show from 10 A.M. to 4 P.M. Saturday and next Sunday.

Finally, in Smithtown, there is a doubleheader: ''Smithtown Historical Society Day'' from 10 A.M. to 3 P.M. Saturday, when six of the society's buildings (most of them Colonial) will be open to the public, and/or ''Nature Around Town,'' a weekend of free natural-history activities sponsored by the Smithtown Conservation Board and including nature walks, birding sessions and caravan tours.

Call 265-6768 for information about the first event and 979-6344 for the second. $90BARBARA DELATINER
