Calendar

Events in New Jersey

Published: October 29, 2010
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Single Page
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on around the state this week. Items may be submitted by mail to On the Towns, Sunday New Jersey Section, The New York Times, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405; or by e-mail to njtowns@nytimes.com.

Comedy

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center Wanda Sykes. Friday at 8 p.m. $39 to $99. Penn and Teller, comedic magic. Nov. 7 at 7 p.m. $29 to $99. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

MONTCLAIR Wellmont Theater Margaret Cho. Thursday at 8 p.m. $35 to $65. Wellmont Theater, 5 Seymour Street.

wellmonttheatre.com; (973) 783-9500.

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts Ron White. Nov. 13 at 8 p.m. $57 to $87. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater Penn and Teller, comedic magic. Thursday at 8 p.m. $35 to $75. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469;

statetheatrenj.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater Wanda Sykes. Thursday at 8 p.m. $38 to $138. Louis CK. Nov. 6 at 8 p.m. $29.50 to $49.50. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street.

countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

For Children

BLOOMFIELD Robert Van Fossan Theater, Westminster Arts Center “Hansel and Gretel” and “Goldilocks and the Three Bears,” a double bill children’s performance. Nov. 13 at 11 a.m. $8 and $10. Robert Van Fossan Theater, Westminster Arts Center, corner of Franklin and Fremont Streets. campus.bloomfield.edu/Westminster; (973) 748-9008.

MADISON Museum of Early Trades and Crafts “Teddy Bear Picnic.” Bring your teddy bear and celebrate National Teddy Bear Day. Nov. 14, 1 to 3 p.m. $3. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. (973) 377-2982; rosenet.org/metc.

MILLVILLE Wheaton Arts and Cultural Center “Halloween and Day of the Dead Celebration,” family-friendly interactive Halloween performances. Oct. 31, 1 to 8 p.m. Free. Wheaton Arts and Cultural Center, 1501 Glasstown Road (Route 55). (856) 825-6800; wheatonarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “Leo Lionni’s Swimmy, Frederick and Inch by Inch,” children’s theatrical performance presented by the Mermaid Theater of Nova Scotia. Oct. 31 at 11 a.m. $15 to $25. Ken Galipeau: “True Tall Tales of Fishing With Uncle Ish,” featuring family-inspired tall tales and silly stories. Nov. 6 at 10 a.m. and noon. Free. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. statetheatrenj.org; (732) 246-7469.

PRINCETON Paul Robeson Center for the Arts “Day of the Dead.” Make altars, skull banners, paper marigolds and more and learn traditions of the holiday. Monday, 4 to 5:30 p.m. Free. Paul Robeson Center for the Arts, 102 Witherspoon Street.

artscouncilofprinceton.org; (609) 924-8777.

Film

NEW BRUNSWICK Rutgers University-Voorhees Hall “New Jersey Film Festival Fall 2010,” new international films, American independent features, experimental and short subjects, classic revivals and documentaries. Through Thursday. $8 to $10. Rutgers University, Voorhees Hall, 71 Hamilton Street. (732) 932-8482; njfilmfest.com.

Music and Dance

BLOOMFIELD Robert Van Fossan Theater, Westminster Arts Center 4th Wall Theater presents “Miscast!” featuring the 4th Wall singers and Broadway guests. Tuesday at 8 p.m. $25. Robert Van Fossan Theater, Westminster Arts Center, corner of Franklin and Fremont Streets. (973) 748-9008; campus.bloomfield.edu/Westminster.

DEAL PARK Axelrod Performing Arts Center Jane Monheit, jazz. Nov. 13 at 8 p.m. $23 to $30. Axelrod Performing Arts Center, 100 Grant Avenue. (732) 531-9100 ext. 142;

axelrodartscenter.com.

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center Jason Bonham’s Led Zeppelin Experience. Thursday at 8 p.m. $39 to $74. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

MONTCLAIR Wellmont Theater Wolf Parade, indie rock band. Wednesday at 8 p.m. $25 and $28. Wellmont Theater, 5 Seymour Street. wellmonttheatre.com; (973) 783-9500.

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts “Russian Tales,” with the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra and the pianist Dudana Mazmanishvili, classical. Oct. 31 at 3 p.m. (800) 255-3476; njsymphony.org. “Street Beat,” featuring urban rhythms, hip-hop moves and break dance acrobatics. Nov. 7 at 3 p.m. $25. Pink Martini, pop, jazz and classical. Nov. 9 at 8 p.m. $52 to $87. New Orleans Nights with Allen Toussaint, Nicolas Payton and the Joe Krown Trio. Nov. 11 at 8 p.m. $32 to $47. Barenaked Ladies, $49 to $79. Nov. 12 at 8 p.m. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “Benise: The Spanish Guitar,” world music and dance production. Nov. 13 at 8 p.m. $27 to $62. In the Mood, a revue featuring the String of Pearls Big Band Orchestra, boogie-woogie singers and swing dancers. Nov. 14 at 3 p.m. $35 to $52. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469;

statetheatrenj.org.

PRINCETON Paul Robeson Center for the Arts “There Goes the Judge,” music, poetry and a short play by Richard Jarboe. Nov. 12 at 8 p.m. $5. Paul Robeson Center for the Arts, 102 Witherspoon Street. (609) 924-8777; artscouncilofprinceton.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater Jason Bonham’s Led Zeppelin Experience. Wednesday at 8 p.m. $45 to $169.50. The West Point Jazz Knights, performing Big Band jazz ensembles. Nov. 7 at 3 p.m. Free. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

RINGWOOD Community Presbyterian Church The Antara Ensemble with Harold Jones, flutist and conductor, and Donaldo Garcia, pianist, in a program that includes the Schumann Piano Concerto, Reinecke Flute Concerto and Mendelssohn Italian Symphony. Nov. 14 at 7 p.m. Free. Community Presbyterian Church, 145 Carletondale Road. (973) 962-7431; communitypc.org.

SOUTH ORANGE South Orange Performing Arts Center Judy Carmichael, jazz. Nov. 9 at 7:30 p.m. $8 to $25. Aimee Mann, singer-songwriter. Nov. 13 at 8 p.m. $42 to $68. Dance Theater of Harlem Ensemble. Nov. 14 at 3 and 7 p.m. $25 and $45. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-2787; sopacnow.org.

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum Spiritual Drum Circle Workshop, led by Richard Reiter, jazz musician and composer. Through Nov. 7. $5 and $10. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. (201) 836-3499; puffinculturalforum.org.

TRENTON Patriots Theater at the War Memorial “Nathan Avakian: A New Kind of Music,” featuring music ranging from classic ballads to contemporary pop. Nov. 7 at 3 p.m. $13 and $15. Patriots Theater at the War Memorial, 1 Memorial Drive. (609) 984-8400; thewarmemorial.com.

WAYNE Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University Wayne Escoffery and Carolyn Leonhart, jazz. Oct. 31 at 4 p.m. $8 and $15. Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University, 300 Pompton Road. (973) 720-2371; wpunj.edu/wplive/sheacenter.htm.

Seasonal

BELLEVILLE State Scare Factory “State Scare Factory Haunted Attraction,” featuring themed rooms of interactive terror and special effects from movies and TV. Through Oct. 31. $15 to $28. State Scare Factory, 229 Main Street. statescare.com; (973) 450-1070.

LYNDHURST whiskey cafe “Halloween Swing Dance,” featuring a swing band and dance lessons. Oct. 31 at 6 p.m. $20. Whiskey Cafe, 1050 Wall Street West. (201) 939-4889.

Spoken Word

WAYNE Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University Distinguished Lecturer Series: “Bernie Williams in Words and Music.” Mr. Williams will discuss his career as a member of the Yankees and as a jazz guitarist. Nov. 12 at 7 p.m. $45 to $125. Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University, 300 Pompton Road.

wpunj.edu/wplive/sheacenter.htm; (973) 720-2371.

Theater

ASBURY PARK ReVision Theater at the Carousel House on the Boardwalk “The Rocky Horror Show,” musical by Richard O’Brien. Through Nov. 13. $15 to $45. ReVision Theater at the Carousel House on the Boardwalk, 700 Ocean Avenue. (732) 455-3059; revisiontheatre.org.

CAMDEN Waterfront South Theater “The Old Settler,” drama by John Henry Redwood. Through Nov. 7. $15. Waterfront South Theater, 400 Jasper Street. (856) 456-2850; southcamdentheatre.org.

CAPE MAY Cape May Stage, The Robert Shackleton Playhouse “The Complete Works of William Shakespeare (Abridged),” presented by the Reduced Shakespeare Company. Through Nov. 20. $12.50 to $35. Cape May Stage, Robert Shackleton Playhouse, Bank and Lafayette Streets. (609) 884-1341; capemaystage.com.

DEAL PARK Axelrod Performing Arts Center “The Spencers,” illusion show. Nov. 14 at 2 p.m. $20 and $25. Axelrod Performing Arts Center, 100 Grant Avenue. (732) 531-9100 ext. 142;

axelrodartscenter.com.

EAST BRUNSWICK Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center “The Crucible,” drama by Arthur Miller. Through Nov. 21. $18 and $20. Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center, 715 Cranbury Road. (732) 254-3939; playhouse22.org.

HACKETTSTOWN Centenary Stage Company “Breakfast With Mugabe,” by Fraser Grace. Friday through Nov. 21. $22.50 to $25. Centenary Stage Company, 400 Jefferson Street. centenarystageco.org; (908) 979-0900.

HADDON TOWNSHIP Ritz Theater “Monty Python’s Spamalot,” musical comedy. Through Nov. 20. $17 to $30. Ritz Theater, 915 White Horse Pike. (856) 858-5230; ritztheatreco.org.

HOBOKEN Monroe Center for the Arts “The Pirates of Penzance,” by Gilbert and Sullivan, presented by the Theater Company. Thursday through Nov. 21. $15 to $25. Monroe Center for the Arts, 720 Monroe Street. thetheatercompany.org; (201) 208-7809.

JERSEY CITY The Attic Ensemble “Les Liaisons Dangereuses,” by Christopher Hampton. Nov. 12 through Nov. 21. $15 and $20. The Attic Ensemble, 83 Wayne Street. atticensemble.org; (201) 413-9200.

LONG BRANCH New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater “Character Assassins,” dark comedy by Charlie Schulman. Through Oct. 31. $25 to $60. New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater, 179 Broadway. (732) 229-3166; njrep.org.

MADISON F.M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University “The Lion in Winter,” by James Goldman. Through Nov. 14. $31 to $54. F.M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University, 36 Madison Avenue. njshakespeare.org; (973) 408-5600.

MAHWAH Berrie Center for Performing and Visual Arts “Trojan Women 2.0,” a contemporary take on Euripides’ Greek tragedy by Charles Mee Jr. Nov. 12 through Nov. 20. $5 and $10. Berrie Center for Performing and Visual Arts, 505 Ramapo Valley Road. (201) 684-7844; ramapo.edu/berriecenter/index.html.
