Events in Westchester

Published: February 12, 2010
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on in and around the Hudson Valley. Items for the guide should be sent at least three weeks in advance to westweek@nytimes.com, or by mail to Westchester Calendar, Metropolitan, The New York Times, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405.

Comedy

MAMARONECK Emelin Theater Judy Gold. Feb. 26 at 8 p.m. $30. Emelin Theater, 153 Library Lane. emelin.org; (914) 698-0098.

TARRYTOWN Tarrytown Music Hall Bobby Collins. Feb. 20 at 8 p.m. $38 and $48. Tarrytown Music Hall, 13 Main Street. (877) 840-0457; tarrytownmusichall.org.

Film

CROTON-ON-HUDSON Croton Free Library “Before They Die,” documentary chronicling the 1921 Tulsa race riot. Discussion following with one of the survivors, Olivia J. Hooker. Feb. 28 at 2 p.m. Free. Croton Free Library, 171 Cleveland Drive. crotonfreelibrary.org; (914) 271-6612.

IRVINGTON Irvington Town Hall Theater Gounod’s “Romeo and Juliette,” directed by Bartlett Sher. Feb. 14 at 1 p.m. $20. “Anvil: The Story of Anvil,” documentary by Sacha Gervasi. Feb. 24 at 8 p.m. $7. Irvington Town Hall Theater, 85 Main Street. irvingtontheater.com; (914) 591-6602.

MOUNT KISCO Mount Kisco Public Library “Amreeka,” directed by Cherien Dabis. Feb. 20 at 1:30 p.m. Free. Mount Kisco Public Library, 100 Main Street. mountkiscolibrary.org; (914) 666-8041.

PEEKSKILL Paramount Center for the Arts “Me and Orson Welles,” by Richard Linklater. Through Feb. 18. “Love in the Afternoon,” by Billy Wilder. Wednesday at 7:30 p.m. “The Messenger,” by Oren Moverman. Feb. 21 through 28. “Philadelphia Story,” by George Cukor. Feb. 24 at 7:30 p.m. All are $7 and $9. Paramount Center for the Arts, 1008 Brown Street. (914) 739-2333;

paramountcenter.org.

PELHAM The Picture House “The Yellow Handkerchief,” starring William Hurt and Maria Bello. Feb. 24 at 7:30 p.m. $20. The Picture House, 175 Wolfs Lane. (914) 738-3161; thepicturehouse.org.

PLEASANTVILLE Jacob Burns Film Center “Celebrating Chekhov” film series. Through Feb. 25. $6 to $11. “Frameworks: Art on Film” series.” Through March 3. $9 and $14. Jacob Burns Film Center, 364 Manville Road. burnsfilmcenter.org; (914) 747-5555.

For Children

CHAPPAQUA Chappaqua Library “Cloudy with a Chance of Meatballs,” part of the Read the Book, See the Movie series. Pizza will be served. Ages 9 to 12. Feb. 14, 12:30 to 3:30 p.m. Vacation week double feature: “Night at the Museum: Battle of the Smithsonian” and “Transformers: Revenge of the Fallen.” Pizza and popcorn will be served. Ages 10 and up. Thursday, noon to 4 p.m. Chess for Teens, learn new moves from a member of the World Chess Federation. Ages 10 and up. Friday, 3 to 5 p.m. All are free. Chappaqua Library, 195 South Greeley Avenue. chappaqualibrary.org; (914) 238-4779.

IRVINGTON Irvington Town Hall Theater Westchester Teen Idol competition, local singers compete for prizes before guest judges from Broadway. Ages 10 and up. Feb. 27 at 8 p.m. $22 and $27. Irvington Town Hall Theater, 85 Main Street. (914) 591-6602; irvingtontheater.com.

MOUNT VERNON Mount Vernon Public Library “Journey Into Africa,” concert presented by the Okra Dance Company. All ages. Feb. 25 at 3:30 p.m. Free. Mount Vernon Public Library, 28 South First Avenue. mountvernonpubliclibrary.org; (914) 668-1840.

OSSINING Teatown Lake Reservation Afternoon Animal Encounters: Snakes. All ages. Feb. 14 at 2 p.m. Story Sundays, stories and activities about nature, animals and ecology. Ages 4 to 7. Feb. 28, 11 a.m. Both are $5; members, free. Teatown Lake Reservation, 1600 Spring Valley Road. (914) 762-2912; teatown.org.

PLEASANTVILLE Jacob Burns Film Center “20,000 Leagues Under the Sea,” part of the Movies for Kids (and Their Families) series. Ages 6 and up. Through Feb. 14. $6 to $11. Jacob Burns Film Center, 364 Manville Road. burnsfilmcenter.org; (914) 747-5555.

PLEASANTVILLE Richard G. Rosenthal Jewish Community Center Purim Tales and Treats, stories, music, costumes and food. Ages 7 and under. Feb. 24 at 5:30 p.m. $10 and $18 per family. Richard G. Rosenthal Jewish Community Center, 600 Bear Ridge Road. (914) 741-0333; rosenthaljcc.org.

WHITE PLAINS Arts Exchange “Peter and the Wolf,” by Prokofiev. Performed by the Nigief Ensemble and presented by ArtsWestchester and the Eastchester Arts Council. Ages 4 and up. Feb. 14, 3 to 4 p.m. $10 and $15. Arts Exchange, 31 Mamaroneck Avenue. (914) 428-4220; westarts.com.

WHITE PLAINS Westchester County Center The Royal Hanneford Circus with the Flying Wallendas, three-ring circus. All ages. Through Monday. $22.50 and $28.50. Westchester County Center, 198 Central Avenue. countycenter.biz; (914) 995-4050.

BEACON Dia:Beacon Zoe Leonard: “You See I Am Here After All, 2008.” Through Sept. 6. Sol LeWitt: “Drawing Series.” Through November. $7 and $10; members and children under 12, free. Fridays through Mondays, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. Dia:Beacon, 3 Beekman Street. diabeacon.org; (845) 440-0100.

BRONXVILLE Bronxville Women’s Club Paintings by Miho Goto. Through Feb. 28. Tuesdays through Fridays, 9 a.m. to 1 p.m.; and by appointment. Bronxville Women’s Club, 135 Midland Avenue. (914) 337-3252; bronxvillewomensclub.org.

CROSS RIVER Gallery in the Park, Pound Ridge Reservation Heather Van Wolf: “Studies in Line and Character.” Through April 25. Daily, 9 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Gallery in the Park, Pound Ridge Reservation, Routes 35 and 121. (914) 864-7317; vanwolf.com.

(914) 835-0324; harrisonpl.org.

Hours: Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Sundays, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. John Jay Homestead Historic Site, 400 Jay Street. johnjayhomestead.org; (914) 232-5651.

Hours: Mondays and Thursdays, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Tuesdays and Wednesdays, 9 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Larchmont Public Library, 121 Larchmont Avenue. (914) 834-2281; larchmontlibrary.org.

LARCHMONT Mamaroneck Artists Guild “Multiple Impressions,” group show. Through Feb. 27. Tuesdays through Saturdays, noon to 5 p.m.; and by appointment. Mamaroneck Artists Guild, 126 Larchmont Avenue. (914) 834-1117;

mamaroneckartistsguild.org.

Hours: Mondays, Tuesdays and Thursdays, 9 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Wednesdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. New Rochelle Public Library, 1 Library Plaza. (914) 632-7878; nrpl.org.

Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 6 p.m.; or by appointment. Hudson Valley Center for Contemporary Art, 1701 Main Street. hvcca.com; (914) 788-0100.
