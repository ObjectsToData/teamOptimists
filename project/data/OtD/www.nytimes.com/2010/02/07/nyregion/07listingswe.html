Published: February 5, 2010

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on in and around the Hudson Valley. Items for the guide should be sent at least three weeks in advance to westweek@nytimes.com, or by mail to Westchester Calendar, Metropolitan, The New York Times, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405.

Comedy

TARRYTOWN Tarrytown Music Hall Bobby Collins. Feb. 20 at 8 p.m. $38 to $48. Tarrytown Music Hall, 13 Main Street. (877) 840-0457; tarrytownmusichall.org.

Film

IRVINGTON Irvington Town Hall Theater “The Damned United,” starring Michael Sheen. Wednesday at 8 p.m. $7. Irvington Town Hall Theater, 85 Main Street. (914) 591-6602; irvingtontheater.com.

MONTROSE Hendrick Hudson Free Library “Julie and Julia,” starring Meryl Streep. Thursday at 1:30 p.m. Free. Hendrick Hudson Free Library, 185 Kings Ferry Road.

henhudfreelibrary.org; (914) 739-5654.

PEEKSKILL Paramount Center for the Arts “Precious,” directed by Lee Daniels. Through Thursday. $7 and $9. “Stage Door,” starring Katharine Hepburn and Ginger Rogers. Wednesday at 7:30 p.m. $7. “Me and Orson Welles,” directed by Richard Linklater. Friday through Feb. 14. $7 and $9. Paramount Center for the Arts, 1008 Brown Street. (914) 739-2333; paramountcenter.org.

PELHAM The Picture House “Dr. Strangelove,” directed by Stanley Kubrick. Feb. 7 at 11:30 a.m. and Wednesday at 7:30 p.m. $5 and $9.50. The Picture House, 175 Wolfs Lane. thepicturehouse.org; (914) 738-3161.

PLEASANTVILLE Jacob Burns Film Center “Ran,” directed by Akira Kurosawa. Through Thursday. $6 to $11. “Celebrating Chekhov Film Series.” Friday through Feb. 25. $6 to $11. “FrameWorks: Art on Film Series.” Through Mar. 3. $9 and $14. Jacob Burns Film Center, 364 Manville Road. burnsfilmcenter.org; (914) 747-5555.

For Children

CHAPPAQUA Chappaqua Library “Cloudy with a Chance of Meatballs,” part of the Read the Book, See the Movie Series. Pizza will be served during the reading and discussion. Ages 9 to 12. Feb. 14, 12:30 to 3:30 p.m. Free. Chappaqua Library, 195 South Greeley Avenue. chappaqualibrary.org; (914) 238-4779.

CROTON-ON-HUDSON Croton Free Library “The Golden Bird,” musical comedy presented by the Singing Harp Theater Troupe. Storytelling and acting workshops at 2 p.m. Ages 3 and up. Feb. 7 at 3 p.m. Free. Croton Free Library, 171 Cleveland Drive. crotonfreelibrary.org; (914) 271-6612.

IRVINGTON Irvington Town Hall Theater “13,” musical comedy by Jason Robert Brown. Presented by the Clocktower Players Teen Troupe. Ages 8 and up. Feb. 7 at 2 p.m. $15 and $20. Irvington Town Hall Theater, 85 Main Street. irvingtontheater.com; (914) 591-6602.

MAMARONECK Emelin Theater “If You Give a Pig a Pancake and Other Story Books,” eight mini-musicals presented by Theatreworks U.S.A. Ages 5 and up. Feb. 13 at 11 a.m. and 1:30 p.m. $13 and $18. Emelin Theater, 153 Library Lane. (914) 698-0098; emelin.org.

OSSINING Teatown Lake Reservation “Afternoon Animal Encounters: Snakes.” Learn about natural history and ecological connections related to this animal. All ages. Feb. 14 at 2 p.m. $5; members, free. Teatown Lake Reservation, 1600 Spring Valley Road. (914) 762-2912; teatown.org.

PEEKSKILL Ford Theater “The Ugly Duckling,” a musical adaptation of the story by Hans Christian Andersen, followed by “Providence,” a play by Mehr Mansuri. Presented by The Children’s Theatre Company of Peekskill. Ages 4 and up. Through Feb. 7. $10. Ford Theater, 1031 Elm Street.

childrenstheatrecompany.org; (914) 325-1053.

PLEASANTVILLE Jacob Burns Film Center “20,000 Leagues Under the Sea.” Part of the Movies for Kids (and Their Families) series. Ages 6 and up. Through Feb. 14. $6 to $11. Jacob Burns Film Center, 364 Manville Road. burnsfilmcenter.org; (914) 747-5555.

POCANTICO HILLS Stone Barns Center for Food and Agriculture “Cooking for Kids: School Lunch,” hands-on activities. Ages 9 to 12. Feb. 13, 1 to 2:30 p.m. $25. Stone Barns Center for Food and Agriculture, 630 Bedford Road. stonebarnscenter.org; (914) 366-6200.

SCARSDALE Greenburgh Nature Center “Winter Wonderland Puppet Show.” Ages 2 and up. Feb. 13 at 10:30 a.m. $6. Greenburgh Nature Center, 99 Dromore Road. (914) 723-3470; greenburghnaturecenter.org.

TARRYTOWN Tarrytown Music Hall Disney’s “Beauty and the Beast Jr.,” presented by the Random Farms Kids’ Theater. Ages 3 and up. Through Feb. 7. $18. Tarrytown Music Hall, 13 Main Street. (877) 840-0457; tarrytownmusichall.org.

WHITE PLAINS Arts Exchange “Peter and the Wolf,” by Sergei Prokofiev. Performed by the Nigief Ensemble and presented by ArtsWestchester and the Eastchester Arts Council. Ages 4 and up. Feb. 14, 3 to 4 p.m. $15 and $20. Arts Exchange, 31 Mamaroneck Avenue. (914) 428-4220; westarts.com.

WHITE PLAINS Westchester County Center The Royal Hanneford Circus with the Flying Wallendas, three-ringed circus. All ages. Feb. 13 through Feb. 15. $22.50 and $28.50. Westchester County Center, 198 Central Avenue. countycenter.biz; (914) 995-4050.

Museums and Galleries

BEACON Dia:Beacon “Zoe Leonard: You See I Am Here After All, 2008.” Through Sept. 7. “Sol LeWitt: Drawing Series.” Through November. $7 to $10; members and children under 12, free. Fridays through Mondays, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. Dia:Beacon, 3 Beekman Street. diabeacon.org; (845) 440-0100.

BRONXVILLE Bronxville Women’s Club Paintings by Miho Goto. Through Feb. 28. Tuesdays through Fridays, 9 a.m. to 1 p.m.; and by appointment. Bronxville Women’s Club, 135 Midland Avenue. (914) 337-3252; bronxvillewomensclub.org.

CROSS RIVER Gallery in the Park, Pound Ridge Reservation “Heather Van Wolf: Studies in Line and Character,” paintings. Through Apr. 25. Daily, 9 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Gallery in the Park, Pound Ridge Reservation, Routes 35 and 121. (914) 864-7317; vanwolf.com.

DOBBS FERRY Upstream Gallery “Photography Takes Over,” group show. Through Feb. 28. Thursdays through Sundays, 12:30 to 5:30 p.m. Upstream Gallery, 26 Main Street. upstreamgallery.com; (914) 674-8548.

HARRISON Harrison Public Library “Trips and Impressions,” photographs by Lev Gogish. Through Feb. 21. Mondays and Wednesdays, 9:30 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Tuesdays, Thursdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Harrison Public Library, 2 Bruce Avenue. (914) 835-0324; harrisonpl.org.

HUDSON BCB Art “Selections,” highlights from the 2009 season and previews of upcoming shows. Through Feb. 28. Free. Thursdays through Sundays, noon to 6 p.m.; or by appointment. BCB Art, 116 Warren Street. (518) 828-4539; bcbart.com.

KATONAH John Jay Homestead Historic Site “From Oppression to Freedom: John Jay and His Huguenot Heritage,” historical objects. Through Oct. 31. $2. Wednesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Sundays, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. John Jay Homestead Historic Site, 400 Jay Street. johnjayhomestead.org; (914) 232-5651.

KATONAH Katonah Museum of Art “Steve Tobin: Katonah Steelroots.” Through Sept. 19. $3 to $5; members and children under 12, free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Katonah Museum of Art, 134 Jay Street. katonahmuseum.org; (914) 232-9555.

LARCHMONT Larchmont Public Library Jessica Wasieleski, “Unblinded,” drawings and mixed media. “Cityscapes/Innerscapes II,” photographs by Walter Kimmel. Both through Feb. 26. Larchmont Public Library, 121 Larchmont Avenue. (914) 834-2281; larchmontlibrary.org.

LARCHMONT Mamaroneck Artists Guild “Multiple Impressions,” group show. Tuesday through Feb. 27. Tuesdays through Saturdays, noon to 5 p.m. Mamaroneck Artists Guild, 126 Larchmont Avenue.

mamaroneckartistsguild.org; (914) 834-1117.

NEW ROCHELLE Brother Kenneth Chapman Gallery, Iona College Council on the Arts “Dreamscapes: New Works by Ard Berge,” portraits. Through Feb. 25. Mondays through Thursdays, noon to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 6:30 to 8 p.m.; Sundays, 2 to 5 p.m. Brother Kenneth Chapman Gallery, Iona College Council on the Arts, 715 North Avenue. (914) 637-7796; iona.edu/artscouncil.

NEW ROCHELLE New Rochelle Public Library “Faces and Places: Exploring Portraits and Landscapes,” works by Leslye William Nicholson and George Patterson. Through Feb. 28. Mondays, Tuesdays and Thursdays, 9 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Wednesdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. New Rochelle Public Library, 1 Library Plaza. (914) 632-7878; nrpl.org.

PEEKSKILL Hudson Valley Center for Contemporary Art “Double Dutch,” group show featuring contemporary Dutch installation and video art. Through July 26. $2 to $5; members, free. Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 6 p.m.; or by appointment. Hudson Valley Center for Contemporary Art, 1701 Main Street. hvcca.com; (914) 788-0100.

PELHAM Pelham Art Center “Objectified,” contemporary sculpture. Friday through April 17. Opening reception Friday at 6:30 p.m. Free. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Saturdays, noon to 4 p.m. Pelham Art Center, 155 Fifth Avenue. pelhamartcenter.org; (914) 738-2525.

POUGHKEEPSIE Vassar College, Frances Lehman Loeb Art Center “At the Heart of Progress: Coal, Iron and Steam Since 1750 — Industrial Imagery From the John P. Eckblad Collection.” Through March 21. “Harry Roseman: Hole in the Wall,” installation. Through May 18. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Vassar College, Frances Lehman Loeb Art Center, 124 Raymond Avenue. (845) 437-5632; fllac.vassar.edu.

POUND RIDGE Hiram Halle Memorial Library “Thirty-Fifth Annual Ground Glass Photo Exhibition,” works by 20 Westchester-based photographers. Through March 20. Free. Tuesdays and Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Hiram Halle Memorial Library, 271 Westchester Avenue. poundridgelibrary.org; (914) 764-5085.

Mondays, Tuesdays, Thursday and Fridays, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Wednesdays, 11 a.m. to 7 p.m.; Saturdays, noon to 4 p.m. Manhattanville College Creative Arts Center, 2900 Purchase Street. mville.edu; (914) 694-2200.

Mondays, Tuesdays, Thursdays and Fridays, 9 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Wednesdays and Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Sundays, 10:30 a.m. to 2:30 p.m. Montgomery Row Second Level, 6422-3 Montgomery Street. (845) 876-6670; montgomeryrow.com.

TUCKAHOE Westchester Italian Cultural Center “The Inventions of Leonardo Da Vinci,” contemporary models of mechanical devices. Through Apr. 16. Tuesdays and Wednesdays, 10 a.m. to 2 p.m.; Thursdays, 2 p.m. to 7:30 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 2 p.m. Westchester Italian Cultural Center, 1 Generoso Pope Place. (914) 771-8700; wiccny.org.

Mondays through Fridays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Rockland Center for the Arts, 27 South Greenbush Road.

rocklandartcenter.org; (845) 358-0877.

WOODSTOCK Woodstock Artists Association and Museum “Sketches,” group show. “Small Works,” group show. Both Feb. 13 through Mar. 5. Fridays and Saturdays, noon to 6 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Woodstock Artists Association and Museum, 28 Tinker Street. woodstockart.org; (845) 679-2940.

YONKERS Hudson River Museum “Bakelite in Yonkers: Pioneering the Age of Plastics.” “Collecting for a New Millennium: Recent Acquisitions 2000-2010.” Both through June 6. $3 to $5; members, free. Wednesdays, Thursdays, Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m.; Fridays, noon to 8 p.m. Hudson River Museum, 511 Warburton Avenue. (914) 963-4550; hrm.org.
