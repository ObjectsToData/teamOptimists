Calendar

Events in New Jersey

Published: September 10, 2010
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Single Page
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on around the state this week. Items may be submitted by mail to On the Towns, Sunday New Jersey Section, The New York Times, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405; or by e-mail to njtowns@nytimes.com.

Comedy

MONTCLAIR Wellmont Theater Aziz Ansari: Dangerously Delicious Tour. Wednesday at 8 p.m. $35 to $60. Wellmont Theater, 5 Seymour Street. (973) 783-9500;

wellmonttheatre.com.

Film

NEW BRUNSWICK Rutgers University-Voorhees Hall “New Jersey Film Festival Fall 2010,” new international films, American independent features, experimental and short subjects, classic revivals and documentaries. Through Nov. 4. $8 to $10. Rutgers University, Voorhees Hall, 71 Hamilton Street. (732) 932-8482; njfilmfest.com.

For Children

PRINCETON Paul Robeson Center for the Arts “Deception/Perception.” Mark Mitton, magician. Friday through Sept. 18 at 7:30 p.m. $10 and $15. Paul Robeson Center for the Arts, 102 Witherspoon Street.

artscouncilofprinceton.org; (609) 924-8777.

WEST WINDSOR Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College “Seussical the Musical,” presented by the Pennington Players. Sept. 24 through Oct. 3. $12 to $16. Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College, 1200 Old Trenton Road. (609) 570-3333; kelseyatmccc.org.

Music and Dance

BLOOMFIELD Robert Van Fossan Theater, Westminster Arts Center Music Exhibition, singers and musicians perform. Friday at 8 p.m. $15 and $20. Robert Van Fossan Theater, Westminster Arts Center, Corner of Franklin and Fremont Streets. campus.bloomfield.edu/

Westminster; (973) 748-9008.

CAPE MAY Cape May Stage, The Robert Shackleton Playhouse “Happy Days,” by Samuel Beckett. Through Sept. 25. $12.50 to $50. Cape May Stage, the Robert Shackleton Playhouse, Bank and Lafayette Streets. (609) 884-1341;

capemaystage.com.

HOLMDEL PNC Bank Arts Center Maze and Frankie Beverly, soul/R.&B. Sept. 18 at 6:05 p.m. $29.99. PNC Bank Arts Center, Garden State Parkway, Exit 116.

livenation.com; (732) 203-2500.

MADISON Museum of Early Trades and Crafts Downtown Concert Series: Moss Points North, rock/folk/alternative group. Friday, 1 to 3 p.m. Free. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. rosenet.org/metc; (973) 377-2982.

MONTCLAIR Alexander Kasser Theater Shanghai Quartet, classical. Sept. 12 at 3 p.m. $15. Alexander Kasser Theater, 1 Normal Avenue. (973) 655-5112; peakperfs.org.

MORRISTOWN Morristown Unitarian Fellowship Red Hen, acoustic quartet. Friday at 8 p.m. $7. Morristown Unitarian Fellowship, 21 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 335-9489; folkproject.org.

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts Tommy James and the Shondells. Sept. 18 at 8 p.m. $57 to $100. Phil Vassar, country music. Sept. 23 at 8 p.m. $32 to $55. REO Speedwagon. Sept. 24 at 8 p.m. $57 to $97. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “Glen Burtnik and Friends — Jersey Beatles Bash IV,” featuring “Let It Be,” “Rubber Soul,” and “Help!” Sept. 18 at 9 p.m. $22 to $42. New Jersey Blues and Jazz Festival, featuring Sugar Blue. Sept. 23 at 8 p.m. $30 and $40. Eddie Palmieri Afro-Caribbean Jazz Septet. Sept. 24 at 8 and 10 p.m. $40 and $50. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

NEWARK Cathedral Basilica of the Sacred Heart Organ recital with Se-Eun Lim. Sept. 19 at 4 p.m. $15 suggested donation. Cathedral Basilica of the Sacred Heart, 89 Ridge Street. (973) 484-2400; cathedralbasilica.org.

NEWARK Prudential Center “Verizon’s How Sweet the Sound,” a national choir competition. Sept. 22 at 7:30 p.m. $5 and $7. A.R. Rahman, singer. Sept. 23 at 8 p.m. $49 and $99. Prudential Center, 165 Mulberry Street. (973) 757-6000; prucenter.com.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center New Jersey Symphony Orchestra, with Jacques Lacombe, music director. Tuesday at 7 p.m. $25 to $100. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

NEWARK Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center “John Lennon 70th Birthday Celebration,” featuring the Quarrymen and a screening of the film “Nowhere Boy.” Sept. 23 at 7:30 p.m. $49.50. Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

SUMMIT Christ Church Chatham-Wood Duo, violin and piano works by Mozart, Beethoven and Brahms. Sept. 18 at 8 p.m. $5 and $15; $30 per family. Christ Church, 561 Springfield Avenue.

chathamwoodduo.com; (908) 273-5549.

WOODBRIDGE Barron Arts Center Rob Paparozzi, singer and harmonica player, and the Hudson River Rats. Thursday at 8 p.m. Free. Barron Arts Center, 582 Rahway Avenue. (732) 634-0413.

Spoken Word

FRENCHTOWN Open Space Gallery Poetry Reading and Open Mic, with Skye Van Saun, John Richard Smith and Warren Cooper. Sept. 12 at 3:30 p.m. Free. Open Space Gallery, 36 Bridge Street. (908) 392-2601; electricwindowsbeacon.com.

CLINTON The Hunterdon Museum of Ar Panel discussion and walking tour of “Katherine Mangiardi: Reflected Absence,” a two-part exhibition currently on view at the Hunterdon Art Museum and the Red Mill Museum Village. Sept. 12 at 2 p.m. Free. The Hunterdon Museum of Art, 7 Lower Center Street. (908) 735-8415; hunterdonartmuseum.org.

FLEMINGTON Hunterdon County Library Poetry Reading and Writing Contest Tips. Eileen Malone, Bay-area poet, reads from her latest collection from Ragged Sky Press, “I Should Have Given Them Water,” and gives tips on entering writing contests. Sept. 26, 2 to 4 p.m. Free. Hunterdon County Library, 314 Route 12. (908) 788-1444.

HAMILTON Grounds for Sculpture New Jersey Storytelling Festival. More than 40 storytellers will present different stories throughout the day. Sept. 12, noon to 6 p.m. $8 and $12. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. (609) 586-0616; groundsforsculpture.org.

Theater

ATLANTIC HIGHLANDS First Avenue Playhouse “Old Money,” comedy by Wendy Wasserstein. Through Sept. 25. $15 and $20. First Avenue Playhouse, 123 First Avenue. firstavenueplayhouse.com; (732) 291-7552.

BLOOMFIELD Robert Van Fossan Theater, Westminster Arts Center Rogues Company presents “Vampyre Macbeth,” a Gothic retelling of Shakespeare’s tragedy. Through Sept. 12. $15 and $20. Robert Van Fossan Theater, Westminster Arts Center, Corner of Franklin and Fremont Streets. (973) 748-9008; campus.bloomfield

.edu/Westminster.

LAKEWOOD Strand Theater “Dust in the Infield,” a one-act play by John Hillyard. Wednesday at 8 p.m. $5. Strand Theater, 400 Clifton Avenue. (732) 822-4338; strand.org.

LONG BRANCH New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater “Character Assassins,” dark comedy by Charlie Schulman. Sept. 23 through Oct. 31. $25 to $60. New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater, 179 Broadway. (732) 229-3166; njrep.org.

MADISON F.M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University “All’s Well That Ends Well,” romance by William Shakespeare. Wednesday through Oct. 10. $35 to $54. F.M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University, 36 Madison Avenue. njshakespeare.org; (973) 408-5600.

MILLBURN Paper Mill Playhouse “Hairspray,” musical with music by Marc Shaiman, lyrics by Scott Wittman. Sept. 22 through Oct. 24. $25 to $84. Paper Mill Playhouse, 22 Brookside Drive. (973) 376-4343; papermill.org.

MONTCLAIR Alexander Kasser Theater “Jet Lag,” by Jessica Chalmers. Sept. 23 through Oct. 3. $15. Alexander Kasser Theater, 1 Normal Avenue. (973) 655-5112; peakperfs.org.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Bickford Theater “Mack and Mabel,” music and lyrics by Jerry Herman, book by Michael Stewart. Sept. 23 through Oct. 17. $20 to $40. Bickford Theater, 6 Normandy Heights Road. morrismuseum.org; (973) 971-3706.

NEW BRUNSWICK Victoria J. Mastrobuono Theater “A Midsummer Night’s Dream,” romantic comedy by Shakespeare. Through Sept. 12. $10 and $15. Victoria J. Mastrobuono Theater, Mason Gross Performing Arts Center, 85 George Street.

masongross.rutgers.edu;

(732) 932-7511.

ORADELL Bergen County Players, Little Firehouse Theater “Company,” musical by George Furth, lyrics and music by Stephen Sondheim. Through Oct. 9. $21. Bergen County Players, Little Firehouse Theater, 298 Kinderkamack Road. bcplayers.org; (201) 261-4200. PARSIPPANY Parsippany Playhouse “Side by Side by Sondheim,” revue of the composer’s work. Through Sept. 26. $18 and $20. Parsippany Playhouse, 1130 Knoll Road. womenstheater.org; (973) 316-3033.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center “Aurélia’s Oratorio,” stage illusion show. Through Oct. 17. $20 to $60. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater “The King and I,” musical by Rodgers and Hammerstein. Friday through Sept. 26. $22 to $29. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

SOUTH ORANGE South Orange Performing Arts Center “Menopause the Musical,” by Jeanie Linders. Friday through Oct. 31. $45. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-2787; sopacnow.org.

UNION Zella Fry Theater, Kean University “Lost Boy Found in Whole Foods,” by Tammy Ryan. Through Sept. 19. $15 to $25. Zella Fry Theater, Kean University, 1000 Morris Avenue. kean.edu/premierestages; (908)-737-7469.

Outdoors

PALMYRA Palmyra Cove Nature Park “Beginning Birding,” Kristina Merola, naturalist, teaches participants how to spot the different species of birds while exploring the Cove’s 250 acres of woodlands and tidal wetlands. Sept. 18, 9 a.m. to noon. $10. Palmyra Cove Nature Park, 1300 Route 73 North. (856) 829-1900; palmyracove.org.

Museums and Galleries

CAPE MAY Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities “Cape May’s Stained Glass Exhibit,” featuring color photographs of 19th-century windows in Cape May, tools of the stained-glass trade and samples of the glass. Through Nov. 7. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. (609) 884-5404; capemaymac.org.

CLINTON Hunterdon Museum of Art “Botanica,” exhibition featuring contemporary art in a variety of media and materials, including painting, sculpture, photography, print making, drawing, installation and video, as well as ceramics, glass and fiber. Through Sept. 12. Donation: $3. “Katherine Mangiardi: Reflected Absence,” a two-part exhibition exploring the shared history of the mills flanking the Raritan River’s south branch in Clinton. Through Sept. 12. Donation: $3. Tuesday through Sunday, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Hunterdon Museum of Art, 7 Lower Center Street. (908) 735-8415;

hunterdonartmuseum.org.

CLINTON Red Mill Museum Village “Lights Our Lives: Early Lighting and Miniature Rooms,” exhibition of antique lighting, featuring candlesticks, lanterns, hurricane and gas lamps, and five miniature rooms depicting average lighting levels at night over the last five centuries. Through Oct. 1. $8 general admission, $6 seniors, $5 children 6 to 12. Members and children under 6 free. Nov. 1 through Dec. 21. Saturday, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Sunday, noon to 5 p.m. Tuesday through Friday, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Red Mill Museum Village, 56 Main Street. (908) 735-4101, ext. 107; theredmill

.org.

COLLINGSWOOD Perkins Center for the Arts “Through My Window,” exhibition by 25 artists from the Da Vinci Art Alliance who were each given a salvaged window with instructions to turn it into a work of art. Through Nov. 13. Tuesdays and Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 2 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 1 p.m.; or by appointment. Perkins Center for the Arts, 30 Irvin Avenue. (856) 235-6488;

perkinscenter.org.

HALEDON American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark “Harriet Tubman and the Underground Railroad Print Show: The Art of Mark Priest,” exhibition featuring prints recounting the freeing of a fugitive slave, Charles Nalle, in Troy, N.Y., in 1860. Through Dec. 31. $5. Wednesdays through Saturdays, 1 to 4 p.m., or by appointment. American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark, 83 Norwood Street. (973) 595-7953; labormuseum.org.

LAMBERTVILLE Artists’ Gallery “New Jersey Blues,” paintings and photographs of the view beyond the turnpike by Richard Harrington and John Treichler. Through Oct. 3. Free. Artists’ Gallery, 18 Bridge Street. (609) 397-4588;

lambertvillearts.com. LINCROFT Monmouth Museum “Art From Found Objects: A Juried Exhibition.” Sept. 12 through Oct. 31. $7; members and children under 2, free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Monmouth Museum, 765 Newman Springs Road. monmouthmuseum.org; (732) 747-2266.
