Theater Company Gets Aid for Classics Project

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
By PATRICK HEALY; Compiled by DAVE ITZKOFF
Published: April 7, 2010

The New York-based Aquila Theater Company has received $800,000 from the National Endowment for the Humanities to support new productions of classic Greek works that will be performed for veterans’ groups and in rural and urban areas, the theater group announced on Wednesday. The grant for Aquila’s new Ancient Greeks/Modern Lives program is the largest in the theater company’s 19-year history, an Aquila spokesman said. The performances will begin in May and run through April 2013, taking place at 100 public libraries and arts centers in communities from Harlem and Washington Heights in New York to Miami, Houston, Seattle and several other cities and small towns in at least 20 states. The productions will include scenes based on Homer’s “Odyssey” and from Sophocles’ “Ajax,” Euripides’ “Herakles” and “The Trojan Women” and Aeschylus’ “Persians,” the spokesman said. In its award, the endowment said the money was intended to begin “a national program series exploring classical literature.” Aquila regularly tours America with classics.
