Events in New Jersey

Published: January 27, 2012
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational events in New Jersey. Items for the calendar should be sent at least three weeks in advance to njtowns@nytimes.com.

Comedy

RED BANK Count Basie Theater Wanda Sykes. Feb. 9 at 8 p.m. $39 to $129. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9000; countbasietheatre.org.

Film

NEW BRUNSWICK Rutgers University-Voorhees Hall “The New Jersey Film Festival,” featuring new international films, American independent features, experimental and short subjects, revivals and documentaries. Through March 1. $8, $9 and $10. Rutgers University-Voorhees Hall, 71 Hamilton Street. njfilmfest.com; (732) 932-8482.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “Singalong Sound of Music.” A screening of the 1965 movie and singalong, with a vocal warm-up beforehand. Jan. 29 at 3 p.m. $18 and $22. “Il Trittico in HD,” broadcast of Puccini’s trilogy of one-act operas from the Royal Opera House in London. Feb. 2 at 7 p.m. $22. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. statetheatrenj.org; (732) 246-7469.

For Children

CLINTON Hunterdon Art Museum Hunterdon Museum’s 60th Anniversary Community Celebration, featuring children’s activities including a birthday hat-making project and a screening of Looney Tunes cartoons from the 1950s. Jan. 29, 3 to 5 p.m. Free. Hunterdon Art Museum, 7 Lower Center Street. hunterdonartmuseum.org; (908) 735-8415.

HAMILTON Grounds for Sculpture “Tots on Tour.” Toddlers learn about sculpture through hands-on activities. Ages 3 to 5. Feb. 4 at 11 a.m. and 1 p.m. Free with park admission, $8 to $12. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. (609) 586-0616; groundsforsculpture.org.

MADISON Museum of Early Trades and Crafts Pre-Groundhog Day, with puppets, stories and more activities about groundhogs. Feb. 1 at 4 p.m. $3 to $13; members, free. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. rosenet.org/metc; (973) 377-2982.

SCOTCH PLAINS Young People’s Theater, Terrill Middle School “Let Freedom Ring,” a mix of folk songs about American history. Feb. 4 at 1 p.m. Free. Young People’s Theater, Terrill Middle School, 1301 Terrill Road. (908) 889-5950; spfk12.org/terrillms/.

SOUTH ORANGE South Orange Performing Arts Center “Mammoth Follies,” presented by the Hudson Vagabond Puppets. Ages 4 to 10. Feb. 11 at 11 a.m. $12 and $15. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-2787; sopacnow.org.

TENAFLY Tenafly Nature Center Little Naturalists, where families explore nature with discovery stations, songs and outside journeys. Age 2 and older. Jan. 31 at 12:30 p.m. $15 and $20. Tenafly Nature Center, 313 Hudson Avenue. tenaflynaturecenter.org; (201) 568-6093.

Music and Dance

HAMILTON Grounds for Sculpture Yang Yi Guzheng Academy, ancient Chinese music. Jan. 29 at 2 p.m. Free with park admission, $8 to $12. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. (609) 586-0616; groundsforsculpture.org.

MONTCLAIR Alexander Kasser Theater Miguel Zenón, saxophonist. Feb. 4 at 8 p.m. $15. Alexander Kasser Theater, 1 Normal Avenue. (973) 655-5112; peakperfs.org.

MORRISTOWN Mayo Performing Arts Center “David Garrett: Rock Symphonies II,” performance of works that combine classical and pop. Feb. 1 at 8 p.m. $37 to $87. Paquito D’Rivera, saxophonist and clarinetist, Latin jazz. Feb. 3 at 8 p.m. $37 to $67. Super Diamond, Neil Diamond tribute band. Feb. 4 at 8 p.m. $32 to $52. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

MORRISTOWN The Minstrel, Morristown Unitarian Fellowship Al Parrish with Michael Prysock, folk. Feb. 3 at 8 p.m. $7. The Minstrel, Morristown Unitarian Fellowship, 21 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 335-9489; folkproject.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State TheaterInbal Pinto and Avshalom Pollak Dance Company present “Oyster,” with movement encompassing ballet, contemporary dance, mime and acrobatics. Feb. 1 at 8 p.m. $32 to $52. The Temptations and the Four Tops, soul. Feb. 3 at 8 p.m. $25 to $80. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum “Art After First Wednesdays,” featuring Cotton, rock band. Feb. 1, 7:30 to 8:45 p.m. Free with museum admission. Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum, 71 Hamilton Street. (732) 932-7237; zimmerlimuseum.rutgers.edu.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center Renée Fleming, soprano, with the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra, in a program that includes Ravel’s “Shéhérazade” and other works. Jan. 29 at 3 p.m. $29 to $125. Tickets: njsymphony.org; (800) 255-3476. “Romeo and Juliet,” presented by the State Ballet Theater of Russia. Feb. 3 at 8 p.m. $40 to $80. Liza Minnelli. Feb. 4 at 8 p.m. $36 to $122. “A Valentine Treat.” The New Jersey Symphony Orchestra, conducted by Thomas Wilkins, performs selections from Rodgers and Hammerstein musicals with the vocalists Gary Mauer, William Michals and Lisa Vroman. Tickets: njsymphony.org; (800) 255-3476. Feb. 11 at 8 p.m. $20 to $85. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

PATERSON Passaic County Community College The Poetry Center presents a poetry reading by Michael Cirelli and Shara McCallum. Feb. 4 at 1 p.m. Free. Passaic County Community College, 1 College Boulevard. (973) 684-6555.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center “Tosca,” by Giacomo Puccini, presented by Opera New Jersey and the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra with Valery Ryvkin, conductor. Feb. 3 at 7:30 p.m. $25 to $125. Tickets: (800) 255-3476; njsymphony.org. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

PRINCETON Richardson Auditorium Princeton Symphony Orchestra Classical Series Concert: Simply Russian, an all-Russian program. Feb. 5 at 4 p.m. $25 to $68. Richardson Auditorium, in Alexander Hall on the Princeton University Campus. princetonsymphony.org; (609) 497-0020.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater David Garrett, violinist, performs classical pieces blended with rock. Jan. 31 at 8 p.m. $35 to $65. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

SOUTH ORANGE South Orange Performing Arts Center “A Tribute to Two Latin American Masters: Ernesto Lecuona and Manuel Ponce,” with the Toomai String Quintet. Jan. 29 at 3 p.m. $15 and $20. Emanuele Segre, guitarist, and the Brooklyn Chamber Music Society perform works by Boccherini, Paganini and Tchaikovsky. Jan. 31 at 7:30 p.m. $8 to $25. “Inspired,” featuring the Carolyn Dorfman Dance Company. Feb. 4 at 8 p.m. $20 to $35. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-2787; sopacnow.org.

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum The Solidarity Singers, folk. Jan. 29 at 4 p.m. $10. “Eric and Mary Ross’s Ultimedia Concert,” show by Eric Ross, musician and composer; Mary Ross, video artist; and Atsuko Yuma, dancer. Feb. 4 at 8 p.m. $10. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. puffinculturalforum.org; (201) 836-3499.

Spoken Word

HOBOKEN Monroe Center for the Arts The Theater Company of Hoboken’s monthly Spoken Word Series presents L. A. Kuehlke, author of “Pursuit,” followed by an open mike. Feb. 5 at 3 p.m. $5. Monroe Center for the Arts, 720 Monroe Street. monroecenter.com; (201) 795-5000.

MONTCLAIR Montclair Art Museum “Creating Art: Conversations With African American Artists — Beverly McIver.” Feb. 2 at 7 p.m. Free. Montclair Art Museum, 3 South Mountain Avenue. (973) 746-5555; montclairartmuseum.org.

RED BANK Two River Theater “Behind-the-Scenes With the Cast of Jitney: A Celebration of August Wilson,” stories and memories. Feb. 6 at 7 p.m. Free. Two River Theater, 21 Bridge Avenue. (732) 345-1400; trtc.org.

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum “Open Mic Poetry Cafe,” an open mike hosted by Gha’il Rhodes Benjamin, poet and teacher. Feb. 11 at 7 p.m. $5. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. (201) 836-3499; puffinculturalforum.org.

Theater

EAST BRUNSWICK Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center “Cat on a Hot Tin Roof,” by Tennessee Williams. Feb. 10 through 26. $18 and $20. Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center, 715 Cranbury Road. (732) 254-3939; playhouse22.org.

HOBOKEN Monroe Center for the Arts “The Wild Party,” a musical by Michael John LaChiusa and George C. Wolfe, presented by the Theater Company. Feb. 10 through 19. $20 and $25. Monroe Center for the Arts, 720 Monroe Street. (201) 795-5000; monroecenter.com.

LONG BRANCH New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater “Bakersfield Mist,” comedy by Stephen Sachs. Through Feb. 5. $35 to $60. New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater, 179 Broadway. (732) 229-3166; njrep.org.

MONTCLAIR Alexander Kasser Theater “Romeo and Juliet,” by Shakespeare. Feb. 9 through 19. $15. Alexander Kasser Theater, 1 Normal Avenue. peakperfs.org; (973) 655-5112.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Bickford Theater “Veronica’s Room,” thriller by Ira Levin. Through Feb. 12. $20 to $40. Bickford Theater, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3706; morrismuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN Mayo Performing Arts Center “The Intergalactic Nemesis.” a graphic novel live on stage. Jan. 29 at 7 p.m. $27 and $37. “Romeo and Juliet,” by Shakespeare, presented by Toronto’s Classical Theater Project. Feb. 5 at 3 p.m. $20. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NETCONG Palace Theater “Mother Hicks,” by Suzan Zeder. Through Feb. 5. $14 and $18. Palace Theater, Route 183. (973) 347-4946.

NEW BRUNSWICK George Street Playhouse “Red,” a drama by John Logan. Jan. 31 through Feb. 26. $33.50 and $37.50. George Street Playhouse, 9 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7717; gsponline.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jameson Theater “Eurydice,” by Sarah Ruhl. Feb. 8 through 12. $10 and $15. Jameson Theater, Jones Avenue at Townsend Street. (732) 932-7511; masongross.rutgers.edu.

NEW BRUNSWICK Philip J. Levin Theater “Much Ado About Nothing,” by Shakespeare. Feb. 3 through 18. $15 to $25. Philip J. Levin Theater, 85 George Street. (732) 932-7511; mgsa.rutgers.edu.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “One Man Lord of the Rings,” a one-man show starring Charles Ross. Feb. 4 at 3 and 8 p.m. $15 and $35. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. statetheatrenj.org; (732) 246-7469.

ORADELL Bergen County Players, Little Firehouse Theater “Modern Orthodox,” romantic comedy by Daniel Goldfarb. Through Feb. 4. $16 and $19. Bergen County Players, Little Firehouse Theater, 298 Kinderkamack Road. (201) 261-4200; bcplayers.org.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center “The Convert,” drama by Danai Gurira. Through Feb. 12. $20 to $60. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

SOUTH ORANGE Dreamcatcher Repertory Theater at The Baird “Shooting Star,” romantic comedy by Steven Dietz, performed by Dreamcatcher Repertory Theater. Jan. 29. $25 to $30. Dreamcatcher Repertory Theater at the Baird, 5 Mead Street. (973) 378-7754 (ext. 2228); dreamcatcherrep.org.

TEANECK Jewish Center of Teaneck “Into the Woods,” a musical with music and lyrics by Stephen Sondheim and book by James Lapine; presented by the Black Box Studios Ensemble. Feb. 2 through 12. $10 and $15. Jewish Center of Teaneck, 70 Sterling Place. blackboxnynj.com; (201) 567-6664.

Museums and Galleries

ASBURY PARK Shore Institute of the Contemporary Arts “The Sum of Their Parts,” creatures from mythology and fantasy. Through Feb. 24. Shore Institute of the Contemporary Arts, 610 Cookman Avenue. (732) 483-4641; sica.org.

BEDMINSTER The Center for Contemporary Art “Revisiting Landscape: Paintings by Wes Sherman.” Through Feb. 25. The Center for Contemporary Art, 2020 Burnt Mills Road. ccabedminster.org; (908) 234-2345.

CAPE MAY Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities “Wendel White’s Schools for the Colored,” an exhibition featuring 50 photographs of the Franklin Street School. Through April 15. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. capemaymac.org; (609) 884-5404.

CLINTON Hunterdon Art Museum “Ann Tsubota: A Passion for Clay,” includes diverse groups of work by Ann Tsubota, ceramic artist. Jan. 29. “Deconstructing Nature,” group show. Jan. 29. “Nathan Skiles: The Clockmaker’s Apprentice.” Mr. Skiles will create and install 100 objects fabricated from foam rubber. Through March 25. Donation: $5. Tuesdays through Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Hunterdon Art Museum, 7 Lower Center Street. (908) 735-8415; hunterdonartmuseum.org.

ENGLEWOOD Englewood Public Library “Small Things Beautiful and Important,” an exhibition featuring works by Kurt Markgraf. Through Jan. 31. Mondays through Thursdays, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Englewood Public Library, 31 Engle Street. (201) 568-2215; englewoodlibrary.org.

GLASSBORO Rowan University Art Gallery “Willie Cole: Deep Impressions,” an exhibition of about 42 works, including prints and sculptures. Through March 10. Mondays, Tuesdays, Thursdays and Fridays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Wednesdays, 10 a.m. to 7 p.m.; and Saturdays, noon to 5 p.m. Rowan University Art Gallery, 201 Mullica Hill Road. (856) 256-4521; rowan.edu/colleges/fpa/artgallery.

HALEDON American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark “The Shirts Off Our Backs: Union T-shirt Quilts,” exhibition in which quilting patches are made from union members’ tees. Through April 17. Suggested donation: $5. Wednesdays through Saturdays, 1 to 4 p.m., or by appointment. American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark, 83 Norwood Street. (973) 595-7953; labormuseum.org.

HAMILTON Grounds for Sculpture “Aerial Roots,” outdoor exhibition of large-scale creations by Steve Tobin. Through summer 2012. “White Hot: Expressions in Iron,” more than 40 works by nine artists. Through April 8. “2012 Outstanding Achievement in Contemporary Sculpture Awards Exhibition.” Through April 22. “Instrumental Transitions,” sculptures by Michael Dunbar. Through April 22. $8 to $12; members and children under 5, free. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. (609) 586-0616; groundsforsculpture.org.

HOPEWELL Gallery 14 “Barbershop and Beauty Parlor Portraits in Ghana and Mali,” an exhibition with color photographs of advertising signs. Through Feb. 5. Free. Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Gallery 14, 14 Mercer Street. (609) 333-8511; photogallery14.com.

JERSEY CITY New Jersey City University, Harold B. Lemmerman Gallery “Margaret Murphy, A Ten-Year Survey,” featuring paintings of mass-produced objects representing reflections on consumerism, religion and gender roles. Jan. 30 through March 7. Reception: Feb. 2 at 5 p.m. Mondays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. New Jersey City University, Harold B. Lemmerman Gallery, 2039 Kennedy Boulevard. www.njcu.edu/dept/art/galleries; (201) 200-3246.

LINCROFT Monmouth Museum “Chairish the Museum Exhibition and Silent Auction Benefit.” Chairs designed by local artists and artisans will be on display at the museum and various locations in the community. Through Feb. 10. Silent auction benefit tickets: $10 per person. “33rd Annual Juried Art Exhibition,” a group exhibition featuring works in a variety of media. Through Feb. 26. $7; members and children under 2, free. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Thursdays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Fridays, 10 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Monmouth Museum, 765 Newman Springs Road. monmouthmuseum.org; (732) 747-2266.

MONTCLAIR Montclair Art Museum “George Inness: Private Treasures,” an exhibition featuring works from private collections in the Montclair area. Through April 1. “Patterns, Systems, Structures: Abstraction in American Art,” an exhibition from the permanent collection. Through May 19, 2013. $12; seniors and students, $10; members and children under 12, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Montclair Art Museum, 3 South Mountain Avenue. (973) 746-5555; montclairartmuseum.org.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Morris Museum “The Art of the Brick.” Lego sculptures by Nathan Sawaya. Through Feb. 19. “Celebrating Our Legacy,” featuring the work of the Abstract Expressionist Norman Lewis and 29 other African-American artists. Through March 18. $7 to $10. “Modern Tapestries and Floor Coverings Conceived by Picasso, Calder and Contemporaries.” Through April 15. $7 to $10. Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 11 a.m. to 8 p.m.; and Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3700; morrismuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN Gallery at 14 Maple “Remembrance of Things Past,” commemorating the 10th anniversary of the Sept. 11, 2001, attacks. Through Feb. 15. Mondays through Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Fridays, 10 a.m. to 1 p.m.; and by appointment. Gallery at 14 Maple, 14 Maple Avenue. morrisarts.org; (973) 285-5115.

MORRISTOWN Macculloch Hall Historical Museum “Gone for a Soldier: Jerseymen in the Civil War,” nearly 200 objects from the Civil War. Through July 1. $5 to $6; children 12 and under, free. Wednesdays, Thursdays and Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Macculloch Hall Historical Museum, 45 Macculloch Avenue. maccullochhall.org; (973) 538-2404.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum “Art After First Wednesdays,” features a tour of the exhibition “Rachel Perry Welty: 24/7,” on view through July 1, followed by a conversation between Rachel Perry Welty, a Boston-based conceptual artist, and Donna Gustafson, the museum’s curator. Feb. 1 at 5:30 p.m. “At/Around/Beyond: Fluxus at Rutgers,” including sculptural objects, assemblages, prints, books, films, sound works and photographs. Through April 1. “In the Search of an Absolute: Art of Valery Yurlov,” solo exhibition. Through June 3. “Popcorn and Starbaby: Children’s Book Illustrations by Frank Asch.” Through June 24. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum, 71 Hamilton Street. (732) 932-7237; zimmerlimuseum.rutgers.edu.

NEW BRUNSWICK Museum of the American Hungarian Foundation “Hungarian Masterworks: From Impressionism to Modernism,” works from the Jill A. Wiltse and H. Kirk Brown III Collection. Through Feb. 17. Donation: $5. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Museum of the American Hungarian Foundation, 300 Somerset Street. hstrial-pfazekaz.homestead.com; (732) 846-5777.

NEW MILFORD Art Center of Northern New Jersey “Women’s Issues: Controversy and Challenge,” exhibition featuring paintings, works on paper, photography, jewelry and ceramics. Through Jan. 31. Mondays through Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Fridays, 9:30 a.m. to 12:30 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, by appointment. Art Center of Northern New Jersey, 250 Center Street. artcenter-nnj.org; (201) 599-2992.

NEWARK Index Art Center “See I’m Not Paranoid, I’m Right!” Group show. Through Feb. 2. Thursdays, 6 to 9 p.m; Fridays and Saturdays, 1 to 4 p.m. Index Art Center, 585 Broad Street. (862) 218-0278; indexartcenter.org.

NEWARK The Jewish Museum of New Jersey “Claire Wagner Kosterlitz: A Bauhaus Artist in America,” an exhibition of paintings. Through March 25. The Jewish Museum of New Jersey, 145 Broadway. (973) 227-8854; www.jewishmuseumnj.org/.

NEWARK The Newark Museum “Skies Alive! Bird Migration in the Garden State,” interactive exhibition. Through January. “Generation Fit: Steps to a Healthier Lifestyle,” interactive family exhibition that explores issues of nutrition and exercise. Through Dec. 30. “From Meiji to Modern: Japanese Art Goes Global, 19th to 21st Centuries.” Through Dec. 31. “The Teapot,” an exhibition of 66 teapots. Through Dec. 31. $6 and $10. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. (973) 596-6550; newarkmuseum.org.

OCEAN CITY Ocean City Arts Center Paintings by Christine Lockwood McPartland. Through Jan. 31. Mondays through Fridays, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. Ocean City Arts Center, 1735 Simpson Avenue. oceancityartscenter.org; (609) 399-7628.

OCEAN TOWNSHIP Township of Ocean Historical Museum “Shipwrecks, Life Saving and the Story of Takanassee.” Through Jan. 31. Township of Ocean Historical Museum, 703 Deal Road. (732) 531-2136; oceanmuseum.org.

PLAINSBORO Plainsboro Public Library An exhibition of works by Tatiana Sougakova, abstract expressionist. Through Jan. 31. Plainsboro Public Library, 641 Plainsboro Road. lmxac.org/plainsboro; (609) 275-2897.

PRINCETON D and R Greenway “Textures and Trails,” exhibition encompassing quilts, metals, textiles and objects from nature. Through Feb. 10. D & R Greenway, One Preservation Place. (609) 924-4646; drgreenway.org.

PRINCETON Morven Museum and Garden “Puzzles of the Brain: An Artist’s Journey Through Amnesia,” an exhibition of works by Lonni Sue Johnson. Through June 3. $6; seniors and students, $5. Wednesdays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Morven Museum and Garden, 55 Stockton Street. (609) 924-8144; morven.org.

PRINCETON Princeton University Art Museum “Pattern/Picture,” photographs from the museum’s collection. Through Feb. 5. “Lee Friedlander: Cars and the New Cars,” photographs of cars in the American scene. Through Feb. 5. “Object of Devotion: Medieval English Alabaster Sculpture From the Victoria and Albert Museum.” Through Feb. 12. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 10 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Princeton University Art Museum, Nassau and Witherspoon Streets. artmuseum.princeton.edu; (609) 258-3788.

SHREWSBURY Guild of Creative Art “Cuisine ’n’ Art,” a member art show featuring food-related people, places and things. Through Feb. 1. Mondays through Fridays, 8 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Thursdays, 8 a.m. to 7 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Guild of Creative Art, 620 Broad Street. (732) 741-1441; guildofcreativeart.org.

SOUTH ORANGE Pierro Gallery at The Baird “Essex Under 35,” a group exhibition featuring the work of artists born after 1977 who have lived or worked in Essex County. Through Feb. 18. Wednesdays and Thursdays, 2 to 7 p.m.; Fridays, 2 to 5 p.m.; and Saturdays, 1 to 4 p.m. Pierro Gallery at The Baird, 5 Mead Street. southorange.org/thebaird/; (973) 378-7754.

SUMMIT Visual Arts Center of New Jersey “Textility,” an exhibition about contemporary artists’ use of materials, concepts and processes associated with textiles. Through April 1. $3 and $5. Visual Arts Center of New Jersey, 68 Elm Street. (908) 273-9121; artcenternj.org.

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum “The Writings on the Wall: Labor in the U.S.” an exhibition that depicts the history of labor and unions and workers’ rights. Through Feb. 7. Tuesdays through Thursdays, noon to 4 p.m. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. (201) 836-3499; puffinculturalforum.org.

TRENTON New Jersey State Museum “Remember 9.11: Reflections and Memories from New Jersey,” an exhibition that explores the impact of the terrorist attacks on the people of the state. Through Sept. 30. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 4:45 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. New Jersey State Museum, 205 West State Street. (609) 292-6464; www.state.nj.us/state/museum.

WEST WINDSOR The Studio Theater, Mercer County Community College “Surface Tension: Works by Ayami Aoyama and Florence Moonan.” Through Feb. 9. The Gallery at Mercer County Community College, 1200 Old Trenton Road. (609) 570-3333; mccc.edu/gallery.
