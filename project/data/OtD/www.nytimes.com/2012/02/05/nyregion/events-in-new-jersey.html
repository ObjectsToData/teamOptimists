Events in New Jersey

Published: February 3, 2012
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational events in New Jersey. Items for the calendar should be sent at least three weeks in advance to njtowns@nytimes.com.

For Children

BRANCHBURG Theater at Raritan Valley Community College “Aga-Boom,” physical comedy. Feb. 11 at 2 and 7 p.m. $25. Theater at Raritan Valley Community College, 118 Lamington Road. (908) 725-3420; rvccarts.org.

MADISON Museum of Early Trades and Crafts “smART stART.” Children and their parents are invited to drop in and participate in hands-on projects and story time. Feb. 13, 1 to 3 p.m. $3. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. (973) 377-2982; rosenet.org/metc.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Morris Museum “Chocolate Festival,” chocolate-making demonstrations, chocolate tasting, puppet-making. Feb. 11, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. $7 and $10. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3700;

morrismuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN Fosterfields Living Historical Farm “Winter’s Day on the Farm.” Lend a hand with cold-weather farm chores and tap maple trees to collect sap and more. Feb. 5, noon to 4 p.m. $2 to $6; children under 2, free. Fosterfields Living Historical Farm, 73 Kahdena Road. (973) 326-7645; morrisparks.net.

POMPTON LAKES Smiling Rhino Theater “Beauty and the Beast,” a family musical. Feb. 11 through 19. $18 and $22. Smiling Rhino Theater, 247 Wanaque Avenue. smilingrhinotheatre.com; (973) 248-9491.

SOUTH ORANGE South Orange Performing Arts Center “Mammoth Follies,” presented by the Hudson Vagabond Puppets. Ages 4 to 10. Feb. 11 at 11 a.m. $12 and $15. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-2787; sopacnow.org.

TENAFLY Tenafly Nature Center “Animal Sweethearts.” See live animal pairs and learn about attraction and pair bonding in the animal kingdom. Feb. 11, 12:30 to 2 p.m. $5 and $7. “Nature Story Hour.” A nature-themed story and a visit from one of the Tenafly Nature Center’s education animals. Ages 3 through 7. Feb. 18, 11:30 a.m. to 12:15 p.m. $7; members, free. Tenafly Nature Center, 313 Hudson Avenue.

tenaflynaturecenter.org; (201) 568-6093.

WEST WINDSOR Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College Disney’s “Camp Rock, the Musical,” featuring teenage actors in a music and dance showcase. Feb. 10 through 12. $10. Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College, 1200 Old Trenton Road. (609) 570-3333; kelseytheatre.net.

Music and Dance

CLIFTON Allwood Community Church Music for the Soul Concert Series presents: Roger Verdi, trombonist, and Martha Locker, pianist. Feb. 11 at 8 p.m. $15. Allwood Community Church, Chelsea and Merrill Roads. (973) 777-6360;

allwoodcommunitychurch.com.

HIGHLAND PARK Highland Park Public Library Jim Lapidus, jazz pianist, with Nathan Webb on drums and Mike Carino on the upright bass. Feb. 9, 7 to 8:30 p.m. Free. Highland Park Public Library, 31 North Fifth Avenue. (732) 572-2750; hpplnj.org.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Bickford Theater Bickford Jazz Showcase: “St. Valentine’s Day Massacre,” featuring Herb Gardner, trombonist and vocalist. Feb. 13 at 8 p.m. $15 and $18. Bickford Theater, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3706; morrismuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN Mayo Performing Arts Center “Frampton Comes Alive! 35 Tour,” with Peter Frampton, guitarist. Feb. 8 at 7:30 p.m. $57 to $97. An Evening of Romance With Michael Amante, tenor. Feb. 10 at 8 p.m. $42 to $57. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

MORRISTOWN Morristown United Methodist Church “Follow the Drinking Gourd,” a concert featuring Diane Goldsmith, pianist, with Ivan Woods and Shannon Hunt, singers. Feb. 11 at 7:30 p.m. $12 and $15. Morristown United Methodist Church, 50 Park Place. morristownumc.com; (973) 538-2132.

NEWARK John Cotton Dana Library, Rutgers University “Saxophone All Stars Jam Session,” featuring Dr.md? Leonard Brown, Ray Blue, John Petrucelli and more. Feb. 15 at 2:30 p.m. Free. John Cotton Dana Library, Rutgers University, 185 University Avenue. (973) 353-5222; libraries.rutgers.edu.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center “A Valentine Treat.” The New Jersey Symphony Orchestra, conducted by Thomas Wilkins, performs selections from Rodgers and Hammerstein musicals with the vocalists Gary Mauer, William Michals and Lisa Vroman. Feb. 11 at 8 p.m. $20 to $85. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. njsymphony.org; (800) 255-3476.

PRINCETON Richardson Auditorium Princeton Symphony Orchestra Classical Series Concert: Simply Russian, an all-Russian program. Feb. 5 at 4 p.m. $25 to $68. Richardson Auditorium, in Alexander Hall on the Princeton University Campus. princetonsymphony.org; (609) 497-0020

RED BANK Count Basie Theater “A Valentine’s Doo-Wop,” various performers. Feb. 11 at 7:30 p.m. $40 to $50. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9000;

countbasietheatre.org..

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum “The Art of Viola,” a viola recital with Piotr Kargul and Ron De Fesi. Feb. 5 at 4 p.m. $10. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. (201) 836-3499; puffinculturalforum.org.

Spoken Word

CAPE MAY Episcopal Church of the Advent Barbara Pierson, nursery manager at White Flower Farm, will speak about dahlias. Feb. 6 at 8 p.m. Free. Episcopal Church of the Advent, 612 Franklin Street. (609) 884-3065; capemayadvent.org.

HOBOKEN Monroe Center for the Arts The Theater Company of Hoboken’s monthly Spoken Word Series presents L. A. Kuehlke, author of “Pursuit.” An open-mike session will follow. Feb. 5 at 3 p.m. $5. Monroe Center for the Arts, 720 Monroe Street. (201) 795-5000;

monroecenter.com.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center James Richardson, poet, and Jonathan Franzen, novelist/essayist, will read from their works. Feb. 8 at 4:30 p.m. Free. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

RED BANK Two River Theater Behind the Scenes With the Cast of “Jitney”: a celebration of August Wilson. Feb. 6 at 7 p.m. Free. Two River Theater, 21 Bridge Avenue. (732) 345-1400; trtc.org.

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum “Open Mike Poetry Cafe,” hosted by Gha’il Rhodes Benjamin, poet and teacher. Feb. 11 at 7 p.m. $5. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. puffinculturalforum.org; (201) 836-3499.

Theater

BLOOMFIELD Westminster Center for the Arts, Bloomfield College “The Story of My Life,” with music and lyrics by Neil Bartram and book by Brian Hill. Feb. 9 through 11 at 8 p.m. $24. Westminster Center for the Arts, Bloomfield College, 449 Franklin Street. (973) 748-9008, ext. 279; 4thwalltheatre.org.

CAMDEN South Camden Theater Company, Waterfront South Theater “Tennessee’s Final Curtain,” drama by Joseph M. Paprzycki. Feb. 10 through 26. $15. South Camden Theater Company, Waterfront South Theater, 400 Jasper Street. southcamdentheatre.org; (866) 811-4111.

EAST BRUNSWICK Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center “Cat on a Hot Tin Roof,” by Tennessee Williams. Feb. 10 through 26. $18 and $20. Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center, 715 Cranbury Road. (732) 254-3939; playhouse22.org.

HOBOKEN Monroe Center for the Arts “The Wild Party,” a musical by Michael John LaChiusa and George C. Wolfe, presented by the Theater Company. Feb. 10 through 19. $20 and $25. Monroe Center for the Arts, 720 Monroe Street. (201) 795-5000; monroecenter.com.

LONG BRANCH New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater “Bakersfield Mist,” comedy by Stephen Sachs. Through Feb. 5. $35 to $60. New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater, 179 Broadway. (732) 229-3166; njrep.org.

MONTCLAIR Alexander Kasser Theater “Romeo and Juliet,” by Shakespeare. Feb. 9 through 19. $15. Alexander Kasser Theater, 1 Normal Avenue. peakperfs.org; (973) 655-5112.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Bickford Theater “Veronica’s Room,” thriller by Ira Levin. Through Feb. 12. $20 to $40. Bickford Theater, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3706; morrismuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN Mayo Performing Arts Center “Romeo and Juliet,” by Shakespeare, presented by Toronto’s Classical Theater Project. Feb. 5 at 3 p.m. $20. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NETCONG Palace Theater “Mother Hicks,” by Suzan Zeder, presented by the Growing Stage. Feb. 5. $14 and $18. Palace Theater, Route 183. (973) 347-4946.

NEW BRUNSWICK George Street Playhouse “Red,” a drama by John Logan. Through Feb. 26. $33.50 and $37.50. George Street Playhouse, 9 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7717; gsponline.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jameson Theater “Eurydice,” by Sarah Ruhl. Feb. 8 through 12. $10 and $15. Jameson Theater, Jones Avenue at Townsend Street. (732) 932-7511; www.masongross.rutgers.edu.

NEW BRUNSWICK Philip J. Levin Theater “Much Ado About Nothing,” by Shakespeare. Through Feb. 18. $15 to $25. Philip J. Levin Theater, 85 George Street. (732) 932-7511; mgsa.rutgers.edu.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center “The Convert,” drama by Danai Gurira. Through Feb. 12. $20 to $60. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

TEANECK Jewish Center of Teaneck “Into the Woods,” a musical with music and lyrics by Stephen Sondheim and book by James Lapine; presented by the Black Box Studios Ensemble. Through Feb. 12. $10 and $15. Jewish Center of Teaneck, 70 Sterling Place. blackboxnynj.com; (201) 567-6664.

WEST ORANGE Luna Stage “Reparation,” by Gino DiIorio; featuring original music by Oliver Lake, jazz musician. Feb. 9 through March 11. $20 to $35. Luna Stage, 555 Valley Road. (973) 395-5551; lunastage.org.

Museums and Galleries

ASBURY PARK Shore Institute of the Contemporary Arts “The Sum of Their Parts,” creatures from mythology and fantasy. Through Feb. 24. Shore Institute of the Contemporary Arts, 610 Cookman Avenue. (732) 483-4641; sica.org.

BEDMINSTER The Center for Contemporary Art “Revisiting Landscape: Paintings by Wes Sherman.” Through Feb. 25. The Center for Contemporary Art, 2020 Burnt Mills Road. ccabedminster.org; (908) 234-2345.

CAPE MAY Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities “Wendel White’s Schools for the Colored,” an exhibition featuring 50 photographs of the Franklin Street School. Through April 15. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. capemaymac.org; (609) 884-5404.

CLINTON Hunterdon Art Museum “Nathan Skiles: The Clockmaker’s Apprentice.” Exhibition of 100 objects made from foam rubber. Through March 25. Donation: $5. Tuesdays through Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Hunterdon Art Museum, 7 Lower Center Street. (908) 735-8415.

EDISON Performing Arts Center, Middlesex County College “Self: Scape,” group show. Through March 8. Performing Arts Center, Middlesex County College, 2600 Woodbridge Avenue.

middlesexcc.edu/theatre; (732) 548-6000, ext. 2545.

GLASSBORO Rowan University Art Gallery “Willie Cole: Deep Impressions,” an exhibition of works, including prints and sculptures. Through March 10. Mondays, Tuesdays, Thursdays and Fridays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Wednesdays, 10 a.m. to 7 p.m.; and Saturdays, noon to 5 p.m. Rowan University Art Gallery, 201 Mullica Hill Road. (856) 256-4521; rowan.edu/colleges/fpa/artgallery.

HALEDON American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark “The Shirts Off Our Backs: Union T-Shirt Quilts,” exhibition in which quilting patches are made from union members’ T-shirts. Through April 17. Suggested donation: $5. Wednesdays through Saturdays, 1 to 4 p.m., or by appointment. American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark, 83 Norwood Street. (973) 595-7953;

labormuseum.org.

HAMILTON Grounds for Sculpture “Aerial Roots,” outdoor exhibition of large-scale creations by Steve Tobin. Through summer 2012. “White Hot: Expressions in Iron,” more than 40 works by nine artists. Through April 8. “2012 Outstanding Achievement in Contemporary Sculpture Awards Exhibition.” Through April 22. “Instrumental Transitions,” sculptures by Michael Dunbar. Through April 22. $8 to $12; members and children under 5, free. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. (609) 586-0616; groundsforsculpture.org.

HOBOKEN Hoboken Historical Museum “Driving Under the Hudson: The History of the Holland and Lincoln Tunnels,” an exhibition on the history and cultural significance of the tunnels. Through July 1. $2; members, free. Tuesdays through Thursdays, 2 to 7 p.m.; Friday, 1 to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Hoboken Historical Museum, 1301 Hudson Street. (201) 656-2240; hobokenmuseum.org.

HOPEWELL Gallery 14 “Barbershop and Beauty Parlor Portraits in Ghana and Mali,” an exhibition with color photographs of advertising signs. Through Feb. 5. Free. Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Gallery 14, 14 Mercer Street. (609) 333-8511; photogallery14.com.

JERSEY CITY New Jersey City University, Harold B. Lemmerman Gallery “Margaret Murphy, a 10-Year Survey,” featuring paintings of mass-produced objects representing reflections on consumerism, religion and gender roles. Through March 7. Mondays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. New Jersey City University, Harold B. Lemmerman Gallery, 2039 Kennedy Boulevard. www.njcu.edu/dept/art/galleries; (201) 200-3246.

LINCROFT Monmouth Museum “Chairish the Museum Exhibition and Silent Auction Benefit.” Chairs designed by local artists and artisans will be on display at the museum and various locations in the community. Through Feb. 10. Silent auction benefit tickets: $10. “33rd Annual Juried Art Exhibition,” a group exhibition featuring works in a variety of media. Through Feb. 26. $7; members and children under 2, free. “Pure Expression,” an exhibition featuring paintings created by artists with disabilities in the Arts Access Program of the Matheny Medical and Educational Center. Feb. 12 through March 18. Reception: Feb. 12, 3 to 5 p.m. $7; members and children under 2, free. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Thursdays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Fridays, 10 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Monmouth Museum, 765 Newman Springs Road. monmouthmuseum.org; (732) 747-2266.

MONTCLAIR Montclair Art Museum “George Inness: Private Treasures,” an exhibition featuring works from private collections in the Montclair area. Through April 1. “Patterns, Systems, Structures: Abstraction in American Art,” an exhibition from the permanent collection. Through May 19, 2013. $12; seniors and students, $10; members and children under 12, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Montclair Art Museum, 3 South Mountain Avenue. (973) 746-5555; montclairartmuseum.org.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Morris Museum “The Art of the Brick.” Lego sculptures by Nathan Sawaya. Through Feb. 19. “Celebrating Our Legacy,” featuring the work of the Abstract Expressionist Norman Lewis and 29 other African-American artists. Through March 18. $7 to $10. “Modern Tapestries and Floor Coverings Conceived by Picasso, Calder and Contemporaries.” Through April 15. $7 to $10. Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 11 a.m. to 8 p.m.; and Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3700; morrismuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN Atrium Gallery “Seeing Art Anew,” an exhibition featuring recent photographic work by Adel Gorgy. Through March 12. Mondays through Fridays, 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. Atrium Gallery, Court Street. (973) 285-5115; artintheatrium.org.

MORRISTOWN Gallery at 14 Maple “Remembrance of Things Past,” commemorating the Sept. 11, 2001, attacks. Through Feb. 15. Mondays through Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Fridays, 10 a.m. to 1 p.m.; and by appointment. Gallery at 14 Maple, 14 Maple Avenue. (973) 285-5115; morrisarts.org.

MORRISTOWN Macculloch Hall Historical Museum “Gone for a Soldier: Jerseymen in the Civil War,” nearly 200 objects from the Civil War. Through July 1. $5 to $6; children 12 and under, free. Wednesdays, Thursdays and Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Macculloch Hall Historical Museum, 45 Macculloch Avenue. maccullochhall.org; (973) 538-2404.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum “At/Around/Beyond: Fluxus at Rutgers,” including sculptural objects, assemblages, prints, books, films, sound works and photographs. Through April 1. “In the Search of an Absolute: Art of Valery Yurlov,” solo exhibition. Through June 3. “Popcorn and Starbaby: Children’s Book Illustrations by Frank Asch.” Through June 24. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum, 71 Hamilton Street.

zimmerlimuseum.rutgers.edu; (732) 932-7237.

NEW BRUNSWICK Museum of the American Hungarian Foundation “Hungarian Masterworks: From Impressionism to Modernism,” works from the Jill A. Wiltse and H. Kirk Brown III collection. Through Feb. 17. Donation: $5. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Museum of the American Hungarian Foundation, 300 Somerset Street. hstrial-pfazekaz.homestead.com; (732) 846-5777.

NEWARK The Jewish Museum of New Jersey “Claire Wagner Kosterlitz: A Bauhaus Artist in America,” an exhibition of paintings. Through March 25. The Jewish Museum of New Jersey, 145 Broadway. (973) 227-8854; jewishmuseumnj.org/.

NEWARK The Newark Museum “Generation Fit: Steps to a Healthier Lifestyle,” interactive family exhibition that explores issues of nutrition and exercise. Through Dec. 30. “From Meiji to Modern: Japanese Art Goes Global, 19th to 21st Centuries.” Through Dec. 31. “The Teapot,” an exhibition of 66 teapots. Through Dec. 31. $6 and $10. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. (973) 596-6550; newarkmuseum.org.

NEWTON Sussex County Community College Art Gallery “Allan Gorman — Abstract Journeys,” a solo exhibition of new paintings. Through Feb. 27. Mondays through Fridays, 8 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Saturdays, 8 to 11 a.m. Sussex County Community College Art Gallery, 1 College Hill Road. (973) 300-2329.

OCEAN TOWNSHIP Township of Ocean Historical Museum “A Tribute to Norma Eberhardt Dauphin,” an exhibition about the actress, a founding member of the museum. Through May 31. Donations accepted. Township of Ocean Historical Museum, 703 Deal Road. (732) 531-2136; oceanmuseum.org.

PRINCETON DandR Greenway “Textures and Trails,” quilts, metals, textiles and objects from nature. Through Feb. 10. D & R Greenway, 1 Preservation Place. (609) 924-4646; drgreenway.org.

PRINCETON Morven Museum and Garden “Puzzles of the Brain: An Artist’s Journey Through Amnesia,” an exhibition of works by Lonni Sue Johnson. Through June 3. $6; seniors and students, $5. Wednesdays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Morven Museum and Garden, 55 Stockton Street. (609) 924-8144; morven.org.

PRINCETON Princeton University Art Museum “Pattern/Picture,” photographs from the museum’s collection. Feb. 5. “Lee Friedlander: Cars and the New Cars,” photographs of cars in the American scene. Feb. 5. “Object of Devotion: Medieval English Alabaster Sculpture From the Victoria and Albert Museum.” Through Feb. 12. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 10 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Princeton University Art Museum, Nassau and Witherspoon Streets. (609) 258-3788; artmuseum.princeton.edu.

RAHWAY Arts Guild of New Jersey “Road Trip: My America,” group show. Feb. 12 through March 15. Reception: Sunday, Feb. 12, 1 to 4 p.m. Arts Guild of New Jersey, 1670 Irving Street. (732) 381-7511; agnj.org.

SOUTH ORANGE The Baird “Essex Under 35,” the work of artists born after 1977 who have lived or worked in Essex County. Through Feb. 18. Wednesdays and Thursdays, 2 to 7 p.m.; Fridays, 2 to 5 p.m.; and Saturdays, 1 to 4 p.m. Pierro Gallery at the Baird, 5 Mead Street. (973) 378-7754;

southorange.org/thebaird.

SUMMIT Visual Arts Center of New Jersey “Textility,” an exhibition about contemporary artists’ use of materials, concepts and processes associated with textiles. Through April 1. $3 and $5. Visual Arts Center of New Jersey, 68 Elm Street. (908) 273-9121; artcenternj.org.

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum “The Writings on the Wall: Labor in the U.S.,” an exhibition that depicts the history of labor and unions and workers’ rights. Through Feb. 7. Tuesdays through Thursdays, noon to 4 p.m. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. (201) 836-3499;

puffinculturalforum.org.

Comedy

MORRISTOWN Mayo Performing Arts Center “The Starlight Series: Speakeasy,” featuring storytelling by comics. Feb. 7 at 8 p.m. $20. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater Wanda Sykes. Feb. 9 at 8 p.m. $39 to $129. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9000; countbasietheatre.org.

Film

BLOOMFIELD Bloomfield Public Library “For Love of Ivy,” a romantic comedy by Robert Alan Arthur. Feb. 13 at 12:15 p.m. Free. Bloomfield Public Library, 90 Broad Street. (973) 566-6200.

HIGHLAND PARK Highland Park Public Library “The Gang’s All Here,” a 1943 film by Busby Berkeley. Feb. 7 at 6:30 p.m. “Fifth Teen Film Festival,” a screening of original films by local teenagers. Feb. 19 at 2 p.m. Free. Highland Park Public Library, 31 North Fifth Avenue. (732) 572-2750; hpplnj.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Rutgers University-Voorhees Hall “The New Jersey Film Festival,” showing new international films, American independent features, experimental and short subjects, revivals and documentaries. Through March 1. $8 to $10. Rutgers University-Voorhees Hall, 71 Hamilton Street. njfilmfest.com; (732) 932-8482.
