Calendar

Events on Long Island

Published: June 23, 2012

A guide to cultural and recreational events on Long Island. Items for the calendar should be sent at least three weeks in advance to lical@nytimes.com.

Comedy

LEVITTOWN Governor’s Comedy Club Sherri Shepherd, co-host of “The View” on ABC, will perform a night of stand-up comedy. July 6 and 7. $22; plus two-drink minimum. Governor’s Comedy Club, 90 Division Avenue. (516) 731-3358;

govs.com.

PORT JEFFERSON Theater Three The Long Island Comedy Festival. July 6 and 7. $30. Theater Three, 412 Main Street. (631) 928-9100; theaterthree.com.

SAG HARBOR Bay Street Theater Big and Tall: An Evening With Bruce Vilanch and Judy Gold. June 30 at 8 p.m. $65 to $100. Bay Street Theater, Main and Bay Streets. baystreet.org; (631) 725-9500.

Film

HUNTINGTON Cinema Arts Center “Dark Horse,” comedy by the filmmaker Todd Solondz, who will host a discussion after the screening. June 24 at 3:30 p.m. $10 and $15. “The Crowd,” 1928 silent film directed by King Vidor. June 26 at 7:30 p.m. $9 and $14. Cinema Arts Center, 423 Park Avenue.

cinemaartscentre.org; (800) 838-3006.

PATCHOGUE The Plaza Cinema and Media Arts Center Opera in Cinema: “Aida,” a performance at the Teatro alla Scala, Milan. June 24 through 26. $15 and $20. Opera in Cinema: “Anna Bolena,” a performance at the Teatro del Maggio Musicale Fiorentino, Italy. June 24 through 26. $15 and $20. “Chely Wright: Wish Me Away,” documentary by Bobbie Birleffi and Beverly Kopf about the country-music star’s decision to come out as a lesbian. June 28 and 29 at 7:30 p.m.; June 30 at 2:30 and 7:30 p.m. $7 and $10. The Plaza Cinema and Media Arts Center, 20 Terry Street.

plazamac.org; (631) 484-7288.

ROSLYN HARBOR Nassau County Museum of Art “A Self-Portrait on the Walls,” documentary on the artist Jim Dine as he produced an exhibition in Germany, “All About Looking,” on his teaching methods. Through July 8. Free with museum admission, $4 to $10. Nassau County Museum of Art, 1 Museum Drive. (516) 484-9337; nassaumuseum.org.

For Children

COLD SPRING HARBOR Cold Spring Harbor Fish Hatchery and Aquarium “Polliwog Adventures,” celebrating exhibition animals through live shows, crafts, activities and stories. Ages 3 to 5. First and third Thursday of the month, through June 30. Registration required. Cold Spring Harbor Fish Hatchery and Aquarium, 1660 Route 25A. (516) 692-6768; cshfha.org.

GARDEN CITY Long Island Children’s Museum “Larger Than Life.” An introduction to the artists Grant Wood, Diego Rivera and Leonardo da Vinci; young visitors create their own miniatures diorama. Ages 5 and up. June 29 at 3 p.m. $2 and $3 with museum admission, $10 and $11. “Goldilocks and the Three Bears.” Ages 5 and up. June 29 through July 1. $6 and $8 with museum admission; $12 theater only. “Art Expressions for Kids,” featuring works created by young artists involved in various Nassau County mental health programs. Through June 30. “Framed: Step Into Art.” Artists’ works are recreated as three-dimensional, interactive environments in a traveling exhibition. Through Sept. 2. Long Island Children’s Museum, 11 Davis Avenue. (516) 224-5800; licm.org.

LINDENHURST Studio Theater Disney’s “High School Musical Jr.” Through July 30. $10. Studio Theater, 141 South Wellwood Avenue. (631) 226-8400;

studiotheatreli.com.

ROSLYN HARBOR Nassau County Museum of Art Family Sundays at the Museum. Family gallery guides at 1 p.m. and supervised art activities at 1:30 p.m. Sundays in the main building. Through July 8. Free with admission, $4 to $10; children 4 and under and members, free. Nassau County Museum of Art, 1 Museum Drive. nassaumuseum.org; (516) 484-9337.

Music and Dance

AMAGANSETT Stephen Talkhouse David Bromberg Quartet, folk, performing material from its latest CD, “Use Me.” June 29 at 8 p.m. $65 to $80. Caroline Jones, singer, songwriter and guitarist. June 30 at 7 p.m. $30. Ed Kowalczyk, lead singer/songwriter/guitarist of the band Live. June 30 at 9 p.m. $50. Nancy Atlas, southern rock. June 30 at 10 p.m. $30. Stephen Talkhouse, 161 Main Street. (631) 267-3117;

stephentalkhouse.com.

BAY SHORE Boulton Center for the Performing Arts The Flatlanders. June 27 at 8 p.m. $35 and $40. Barnaby Bye, pop/rock. June 30 at 8 p.m. $40 and $45. Boulton Center for the Performing Arts, 37 West Main Street. (631) 969-1101; boultoncenter.org.

DIX HILLS Dix Hills Performing Arts Center “Once Upon a Time: Music of Charles Strouse.” June 29 at 7:30 p.m. $10. Dix Hills Performing Arts Center, 305 North Service Road. (631) 656-2148; dhpac.org.

EAST MEADOW Harry Chapin Lakeside Theater “Swingin’ the Classics,” with the Swingtime Big Band, a 20-piece swing era band led by Steve Shaiman, performing a program with folk and classical themes from the big-band years and the Great American Songbook. July 2 at 7:30 p.m. Free. Harry Chapin Lakeside Theater, Eisenhower Park, Parking Fields 6 and 6A. swingtimeny.com; (516) 572-0200.

GLEN COVE St. Patrick’s Parish Hall Brother Joscephus and the Love Revival Revolution Orchestra playing New Orleans-style rhythm and jazz. July 1 at 7:30 p.m. Free. St. Patrick’s Parish Hall, Pearsall Avenue. Morgan Park Summer Music Festival presents the Swingtime Big Band, conducted by Steve Shaiman; bring seating, picnics welcome. July 8 at 7:30 p.m. Free. St. Patrick’s Parish Hall, Pearsall Avenue. morganparkmusic.org; (516) 671-0017.

HEMPSTEAD Monroe Lecture Center Theater, Hofstra University “Hollywood Live!” a collection of songs from the movies, sung by Broadway performers. Through June 24. $15 to $35. Monroe Lecture Center Theater, Hofstra University, South Campus, California Avenue. hofstra.edu; (516) 463-6644.

HEMPSTEAD Nassau Coliseum LMFAO, pop duo, with Far East Movement, the Quest Crew, Sidney Samson and more. June 26 at 7 p.m. $33 to $114.50. Nassau Coliseum, 1255 Hempstead Turnpike. (516) 794-9300; nassaucoliseum.com.

HUNTINGTON The Paramount The Wailers, Jamaican reggae, ska and rocksteady band. June 24 at 8 p.m. $22 and $45. Filter and Fuel, rock bands. July 6 at 8 p.m. $25 to $50. The Paramount, 370 New York Avenue. paramountny.com; (631) 673-7300.

NESCONSET Smithtown Library, Nesconset Branch Rhonda Denet, singer, performs jazz standards and soul classics. June 28, 7 to 8 p.m. Free. Smithtown Library, Nesconset Branch, 148 Smithtown Boulevard. smithlib.org; (631) 265-3994, ext. 135.

OYSTER BAY Christ Church Oyster Bay Oyster Bay Music Festival, classical music. June 24 through 28. Free. Christ Church Oyster Bay, 61 East Main Street. (516) 922-6377; oysterbaymusicfestival.com.

PATCHOGUE Patchogue Theater for the Performing Arts “Mickey B’s Golden Oldies Summer Spectacular.” June 30 at 7 p.m. $42 to $75. Patchogue Theater for the Performing Arts, 71 East Main Street. patchoguetheatre.com; (631) 207-1313.

SOUTHAMPTON Rogers Memorial Library Summer Benefit Concert presents the jazz pianist Judy Carmichael and her trio. June 24 at 6:30 p.m. $100. Rogers Memorial Library, 91 Coopers Farm Road. rmlfoundation.org; (631) 283-0774, ext. 582.

STONY BROOK Staller Center for the Arts “Sophisticated Interpretations: Songs That Shaped the Sound of College Radio 1977-2012,” WUSB-FM (90.1) 35th-anniversary benefit concert. June 27 at 8 p.m. $20. Staller Center for the Arts, Stony Brook University. (631) 632-2787; staller.sunysb.edu.

UPTON Brookhaven National Laboratory “Pianofest,” piano recital featuring young artists. June 27 at noon. Brookhaven National Laboratory, County Road 46. (631) 344-2345;

pianofest.com.

WESTBURY NYCB Theater at Westbury Hot Tuna, roots rock. June 29 at 8 p.m. $39.50 to $59.50. Dweezil Zappa and the Zappa Family Trust, rock. June 30 at 8 p.m. $39.50 and $59.50. NYCB Theater at Westbury, 960 Brush Hollow Road. (800) 745-3000; livenation.com.

WESTHAMPTON BEACH Westhampton Beach Performing Arts Center Shawn Colvin, folk. June 29 at 8 p.m. $40 to $70. Wayne Shorter Quartet, featuring Danilo Perez, John Patitucci and Brian Blade. June 30 at 8 p.m. $50 to $100. Westhampton Beach Performing Arts Center, 76 Main Street. (631) 288-1500; whbpac.org.

Spoken Word

HUNTINGTON Cinema Arts Center Interview, film clips, Q. and A. and book-signing with Marni Nixon, the Hollywood singing voice of Natalie Wood, Deborah Kerr and Audrey Hepburn. June 27 at 7:30 p.m. $15 to $20. Cinema Arts Center, 423 Park Avenue. (800) 838-3006;

cinemaartscentre.org.

LAWRENCE Peninsula Public Library “Rodgers With a Lot of Hart,” a lecture and performance by Harvey Granat, singer, producer and music historian, accompanied by the music director Mike Capobianco, focusing on the songs and lives of the songwriting team Richard Rodgers and Lorenz Hart. June 25 at 1 p.m. Free. Peninsula Public Library, 280 Central Avenue. (516) 239-3262;

nassaulibrary.org/peninsula.

LONG BEACH Long Beach Public Library “Chinese Poetry Reading,” Leslie Bai reads classical Chinese poems in translation and examines diverse works of art pertaining to horses. June 27 at 2 p.m. Free. Long Beach Public Library, 111 West Park Avenue. (516) 432-7201;

nassaulibrary.org/longbeach.

MANHASSET Manhasset Public Library “Portrait of China,” a discussion with Bo Wang, photographer, video artist and filmmaker. June 27 at 7 p.m. Free. Manhasset Public Library, 30 Onderdonk Avenue. manhassetlibrary.org; (516) 627-2300.

MONTAUK Montauk Library “Shakespeare on Aging,” a lecture with Maurice Charney, New York Council for the Humanities speaker in the humanities. June 24 at 3:30 p.m. Free. Montauk Library, 871 Montauk Highway. (631) 668-3377; www.suffolk.lib.ny.us/libraries/mntk/.

ROSLYN Atria of Roslyn Summer Barbecue and Lecture, on the historic architecture of Jones Beach, with Alexandra Parsons Wolfe, director of preservation services for the Society for the Preservation of Long Island Antiquities. June 27 at 6 p.m. $25 and $30. Atria of Roslyn Harbor, 100 Landing Road. atriaonroslynharbor.com; (516) 626-6900.

Theater

BELLPORT Gateway Playhouse “My Fair Lady,” a musical with book and lyrics by Alan Jay Lerner and music by Frederick Loewe. Through June 24. $25 to $58. “The Rat Pack,” musical. June 27 through July 14. $25 to $58. Gateway Playhouse, 215 South Country Road. (631) 286-1133; gatewayplayhouse.com.

CENTERPORT The Carriage House Theater at the Suffolk County Vanderbilt Museum “Entertaining Mr. Sloane,” drama by Joe Orton. Through June 24. $20 and $25. The Carriage House Theater at the Suffolk County Vanderbilt Museum, 180 Little Neck Road. (516) 293-0674; arenaplayers.org.

EAST HAMPTON Guild Hall “Luv,” comedy by Murray Schisgal. Through July 1. $38 to $80. Guild Hall, 158 Main Street. (631) 324-4050; guildhall.org.

EAST ISLIP BayWay Arts Center “Guys and Dolls,” music and lyrics by Frank Loesser and book by Abe Burrows and Jo Swerling. June 30 through July 15. $14 to $25. BayWay Arts Center, 265 East Main Street. broadhollow.org; (631) 581-2700.

FREEPORT Lantern Theater “The Cemetery Club,” drama by Ivan Menchell. Through June 24. $16 and $18. Lantern Theater, 91 North Bayview Avenue. (516) 221-4485; lanterntheatreli.com.

OAKDALE CM Performing Arts Center “La Cage aux Folles,” a musical with a book by Harvey Fierstein and lyrics and music by Jerry Herman. June 30 through July 22. $16 to $22. CM Performing Arts Center, 931 Montauk Highway. (631) 218-2810; cmpac.com.

SAG HARBOR Bay Street Theater “My Brilliant Divorce,” comedy by Geraldine Aron. Through June 24. $55 and $65. Bay Street Theater, Main and Bay Streets.

baystreet.org; (631) 725-9500.

SMITHTOWN Smithtown Center for the Performing Arts “Avenue Q: The Musical,” book by Jeff Whitty, music and lyrics by Robert Lopez and Jeff Marx. Through June 24. $45. “My Way: A Musical Tribute to Frank Sinatra,” conceived by David Grapes and Todd Olson. July 7 through 29. $25 and $35. Smithtown Center for the Performing Arts, 2 East Main Street. smithtownpac.org; (631) 724-3700.

Museums and Galleries

AMAGANSETT Sylvester and Company “Paintings,” a solo exhibition of the works by the East Hampton painter Anne Raymond. Through July 8. Sylvester and Company, 154 Main Street. (631) 267-9777; anneraymond.com.

BELLPORT Gallery 125 “Drawing From Sand: John Perreault and Mark Wagner,” an exhibition of sand art. Through July 15. Fridays, 3:30 to 9 p.m.; Saturdays, 10:30 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Sundays, 10:30 a.m. to 1:30 p.m. Gallery 125, 125 South Country Road. (631) 880-2693.

BRIGHTWATERS Bay Shore-Brightwaters Public Library “Monet and Me,” an exhibition of photographs by Holly Gordon, taken in Monet’s garden. Through June 30. Bay Shore-Brightwaters Public Library, 1 South Country Road. (631) 665-4350;

bayshore.suffolk.lib.ny.us.

EAST HAMPTON Guild Hall “The Outdoor Museum (Not Your Usual Images of New York) Exhibition.” Through July 29. “Strong-Cuevas: Premonitions in Retrospect,” featuring five pieces in the Frieda and Roy Furman Sculpture Garden, as well as 10 drawings on view in the Wasserstein Family Gallery. Through Sept. 3. Guild Hall, 158 Main Street. (631) 324-4050; guildhall.org.

EAST HAMPTON LongHouse Reserve Art Center “Accumulations: NOW Exhibition,” curated by Jack Lenor Larsen. Through Oct. 6. LongHouse Reserve Art Center, 133 Hands Creek Road. longhouse.org; (631) 329-3568.
