Events in New Jersey

Comedy

MORRISTOWN Mayo Performing Arts Center Gabriel Iglesias: Stand-Up Revolution Tour. April 19 at 8 p.m. $37 to $67. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater Margaret Cho. April 22 at 8 p.m. $35 to $60. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

Film

HIGHLAND PARK Highland Park Public Library “Toast,” directed by S. J. Clarkson. April 17 at 6:30 p.m. Free. Highland Park Public Library, 31 North Fifth Avenue. (732) 572-2750; hpplnj.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “Rigoletto in HD,” broadcast from the Royal Opera House in London. April 22 at 2 p.m. $22. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. statetheatrenj.org; (732) 246-7469.

NEWARK Index Art Center Filmideo, a festival featuring work by independent filmmakers and video artists from around the world. Through April 28. $5. Index Art Center, 585 Broad Street. (862) 218-0278; indexartcenter.org.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center “Cinematic Titanic,” live movie riffing with Mystery Science Theater 3000 featuring Crow, Dr. Forrester and Tom Servo. April 21 at 8 p.m. $28 to $38. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

PRINCETON Paul Robeson Center for the Arts “In the Name of the Father,” screening followed by discussion with Gerry Conlon, the subject of the film. April 15 at 2 p.m. $5 and $10. Paul Robeson Center for the Arts, 102 Witherspoon Street. (609) 924-8777; artscouncilofprinceton.org.

For Children

BRANCHBURG Raritan Valley Community College Earth, Moon, Sun and Coyote: Visitors listen to Native American stories and learn about lunar phases, eclipses and mysteries of space. Through April 28. $6. Space Shapes and Tycho to the Moon: Visitors learn about night and day, space travel, the Moon’s phases and features of the lunar surface, as well as meeting Tycho, a dog who wants to travel there. Through April 28. $6. Raritan Valley Community College, Route 28 and Lamington Road in North Branch. (908) 231-8805;

EAST RUTHERFORD Izod Center “Disney’s Phineas and Ferb — The Best Live Tour Ever!” April 21 and 22. $25 to $80. Izod Center, 50 Route 120. (201) 935-3900; izodcenter.com.

MORRISTOWN Mayo Performing Arts Center New Jersey Ballet’s Family Matinee of “Sleeping Beauty.” April 15 at 3 p.m. $15 to $18. “Stolen Moments: The First 100 Years of Jazz,” a program that introduces children to the history of jazz. April 18 at 10 a.m. $6. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater Sesame Street Live “1-2-3 Imagine! With Elmo and Friends.” April 15. $14 to $75. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue.

statetheatrenj.org; (732) 246-7469.

NEWARK Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center Children’s Jazz Series with the Will Calhoun Trio. April 21 at 8 p.m. Free. Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

Music and Dance

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center Best of the Gypsy Spirit, presented by the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra, led by Jacques Lacombe, with guest Kristin Lee, violinist. For tickets: njsymphony.org; (800) 255-3476. April 15 at 3 p.m. $20 to $60. Moody Blues, rock group. April 16 at 8 p.m. $79 to $149. “Swan Lake,” presented by the Moscow Festival Ballet. April 19 at 8 p.m. $19 to $65. Verismo Opera’s “Il Trovatore,” by Verdi. April 20 and 22. $10 to $55; $15 for reception. Blue Oyster Cult, rock. April 21 at 8 p.m. $19 to $59. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

MONTCLAIR Alexander Kasser Theater Danceworks 2012, performances based on the theme of “Dance and the Human Spirit” as a commemoration of 9/11. April 15. $15. Alexander Kasser Theater, 1 Normal Avenue. peakperfs.org; (973) 655-5112.

MONTCLAIR Wellmont Theater John Prine and Kevin Gordon. April 20 at 8 p.m. $39.50 and $65. Southside Johnny and the Asbury Jukes. April 21 at 8 p.m. $25 to $45. Alison Krauss and Union Station Featuring Jerry Douglas. April 22 at 8 p.m. $45 to $95. Wellmont Theater, 5 Seymour Street. (973) 783-9500;

wellmonttheatre.com.

MORRISTOWN Mayo Performing Arts Center Dance Theater of Harlem. April 20 at 8 p.m. $27 to $57. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater Cobra Starship, a synth-pop band. April 17 at 8 p.m. $25 to $55. MOMIX reMIX, dance performance choreographed by Moses Pendleton. April 20 at 8 p.m. $32 to $52. Johnny Mathis, vocalist, performing with a 31-piece band. April 21 at 8 p.m. $45 to $115. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center Garrick Ohlsson, pianist, performing an all-Liszt program. April 15 at 3 p.m. $35 and $50. Audra McDonald, vocalist, with the New Jersey Symphony Chamber Orchestra, led by Andy Einhorn. April 20 at 8 p.m. $25 to $110. For tickets: (800) 255-3476; njsymphony.org. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

NEWTON The Newton Theater The Machine, a live Pink Floyd show. April 21 at 8 p.m. $29 to $39. The Newton Theater, 234 Spring Street. thenewtontheatre.com; (973) 383-3700.

RED BANK Two River Theater “Broadway in Red Bank,” a cabaret performance. April 16, 6 to 9 p.m. $75 to $200. Two River Theater, 21 Bridge Avenue. (732) 345-1400; trtc.org.

SOUTH ORANGE South Orange Performing Arts Center Jazz in the Loft: John Dukich, vocalist, performs an evening of jazz standards and international songs. April 15 at 5 p.m. $15. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-2787; sopacnow.org.

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum “Classical Sundays at the Puffin,” with Steven Masi, pianist, performing four Beethoven piano sonatas. April 22 at 4 p.m. $10. Tuesdays through Thursdays, noon to 4 p.m. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. puffinculturalforum.org; (201) 836-3499.

Spoken Word

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater SmartTalk Connected Conversations with Cathy Rigby, first American woman to win a medal in the World Gymnastics competition. April 18 at 7:30 p.m. $25 to $75. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. statetheatrenj.org; (732) 246-7469.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center An evening with Alexander McCall Smith, author of more than 60 books, including “The No. 1 Ladies’ Detective Agency.” April 20 at 8 p.m. $25 to $35. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater Talkin’ Jazz: An evening with Gary Walker, of WBGO Jazz Radio. April 16 at 7 p.m. Free. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

Theater

CAMDEN Rutgers-Camden Center for the Arts “A Doll’s House,” drama by Henrik Ibsen. Through April 22. $5 to $10. Rutgers-Camden Center for the Arts, Third Street. (856) 225-6176; rcca.camden.rutgers.edu.

HOBOKEN Monroe TheatreSpace in Hoboken “The Pavilion,” drama by Craig Wright. Through April 29. $18 and $30. Monroe TheatreSpace in Hoboken, 720 Monroe Street. (201) 683-7014; milesquaretheatre.org.

HOBOKEN The Hudson School Performance Space “Stepping Out,” comedy presented by the Hudson Theater Ensemble. Through April 22. $15 and $18. The Hudson School Performance Space, 601 Park Avenue. hudsontheatreensemble.com; (201) 377-7014.

LONG BRANCH New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater “The Hound of the Baskervilles,” comedy by Steven Canny and John Nicholson. April 19 through May 27. $25 to $40. New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater, 179 Broadway. (732) 229-3166; njrep.org.

MAPLEWOOD Burgdorff Center for the Performing Arts “Miss Witherspoon,” comedy by Christopher Durang. Through April 29. $22 and $25. Burgdorff Center for the Performing Arts, 10 Durand Road. (973) 763-4029; thetheaterproject.org.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Bickford Theater “I Do!, I Do!” — musical with book and lyrics by Tom Jones and music by Harvey Schmidt. Through May 6. $20 to $40. Bickford Theater, 6 Normandy Heights Road. morrismuseum.org; (973) 971-3706.

NEW BRUNSWICK Crossroads Theater “The Last Five Years,” a romance written and composed by Jason Robert Brown. Through April 29. $40 to $65. Crossroads Theater, 7 Livingston Avenue. crossroadstheatrecompany.org; (732) 545-8100.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jameson Theater The Jameson Project: An Evening of One Acts. Through April 15. $10 and $15. Jameson Theater, Jones Avenue at Townsend Street. (732) 932-7511; masongross.rutgers.edu.

NEW BRUNSWICK Philip J. Levin Theater “ Intimate Apparel,” a romance by Lynn Nottage. April 20 through 28. $15 to $25. Philip J. Levin Theater, 85 George Street. (732) 932-7511; mgsa.rutgers.edu.

ORADELL Bergen County Players, Little Firehouse Theater “The Man Who Came to Dinner,” a comedy by George S. Kaufman and Moss Hart. Through April 21. $16 and $19. Bergen County Players, Little Firehouse Theater, 298 Kinderkamack Road. (201) 261-4200; bcplayers.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater “The Wizard of Oz,” presented by Phoenix Productions. April 20 through 29. $22 to $29. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

RED BANK Two River Theater “Carry It On,” a musical memoir with Maureen McGovern. Through April 22. $24 to $57. Two River Theater, 21 Bridge Avenue. (732) 345-1400; trtc.org.

TEANECK Becton Theater, Fairleigh Dickinson University “Trad,” comedy by Mark Doherty. April 19 through May 6. $32 and $37. Becton Theater, Fairleigh Dickinson University, 960 River Road. garagetheatre.org; (201) 569-7710.

WAYNE Hunziker Black Box Theater “A Matter of Dreams,” drama by Judah Skoff. Through April 29. $10 to $18. Hunziker Black Box Theater, 300 Pompton Road. (973) 720-2371; wpunj.edu/wplive/hunziker.htm.

WEST ORANGE Luna Stage “Copenhagen,” drama by Michael Frayn. April 19 through May 20. $25 and $35. Luna Stage, 555 Valley Road. (973) 395-5551; lunastage.org.

WEST WINDSOR Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College “Hairspray,” musical. Through April 22. $14 to $18. Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College, 1200 Old Trenton Road. (609) 570-3333; kelseytheatre.net.

Museums and Galleries

CAPE MAY Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities “Wendel White’s Schools for the Colored,” an exhibition featuring 50 photographs of segregated schools in the Northern states. Through April 15. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. (609) 884-5404; capemaymac.org.

CLINTON Hunterdon Art Museum “Fragmented,” works by Astrid Bowlby, Sebastian Rug, Christopher Skura and Ben Butler. Through June 3. Donation: $5. Tuesdays through Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Hunterdon Art Museum, 7 Lower Center Street. (908) 735-8415; hunterdonartmuseum.org.

DEMAREST Mikhail Zakin Gallery “Notations 21 Project,” celebrating the aesthetic and graphic beauty of contemporary illustrated musical scores. Through May 11. Reception: April 21, from 5 to 7 p.m. Mondays through Fridays, 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. Mikhail Zakin Gallery, 561 Piermont Road. (201) 767-7160; tasoc.org.

ENGLEWOOD Mark Gallery “Hyperrealism — A Moment in Time,” group show. Through May 31. Mondays through Saturdays 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Mark Gallery, 11 Grand Avenue. (201) 568-6275; mark-gallery.com.

EWING College of New Jersey “Illuminating Data: Visualizing the Information That Moves Our World,” group show. Through April 18. College of New Jersey, 2000 Pennington Road, AIMM Building, Room 319. tcnjartgallery.pages.tcnj.edu; (609) 771-2198.

GALLOWAY The Noyes Seaview Art Gallery “Intimate Landscapes,” exhibition of photographs by Albert D. Horner. Through May. 29. The Noyes Seaview Art Gallery, 401 South New York Road. Daily, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. noyesmuseum.org; (855) 894-8698.

GLASSBORO Rowan University Art Gallery “High/Low Density,” a multi-media group show. Through May 12. Mondays, Tuesdays, Thursdays and Fridays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Wednesdays, 10 a.m. to 7 p.m.; and Saturdays, noon to 5 p.m. Rowan University Art Gallery, 201 Mullica Hill Road. (856) 256-4521; rowan.edu/colleges/fpa/artgallery.

HALEDON American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark “The Shirts Off Our Backs: Union T-Shirt Quilts,” exhibition in which quilting patches are made from union members’ T-shirts. Through April 17. Suggested donation: $5. Wednesdays through Saturdays, 1 to 4 p.m., or by appointment. American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark, 83 Norwood Street. (973) 595-7953; labormuseum.org.

HAMILTON Grounds for Sculpture “2012 Outstanding Achievement in Contemporary Sculpture Awards Exhibition.” Through April 22. “Instrumental Transitions,” sculptures by Michael Dunbar. Through April 22. $8 to $12; members and children under 5, free. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. (609) 586-0616; groundsforsculpture.org.

HOBOKEN Hoboken Historical Museum “Driving Under the Hudson: The History of the Holland and Lincoln Tunnels,” Through July 1. $2; members, free. Tuesdays through Thursdays, 2 to 7 p.m.; Fridays, 1 to 5 p.m.; and Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Hoboken Historical Museum, 1301 Hudson Street. (201) 656-2240; hobokenmuseum.org.

LINCROFT Monmouth Museum “Greed + Fear = Irrational Exuberance,” collage works in mixed media. Through April 22. “Art of Illusion: A Juried Invitational exhibition,” 62 works in a variety of media. Through May 20. $7; members and children under 2, free. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Thursdays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Fridays, 10 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Monmouth Museum, 765 Newman Springs Road. (732) 747-2266; monmouthmuseum.org.

MAHWAH Mahwah Museum “Les Paul in Mahwah: A Tribute,” guitars and memorabilia. Through June. “New Jersey Fights in the Civil War — Remembering Soldiers Buried in Mahwah.” Through June 30. $3 to $5; members and children under 5, free. Wednesdays, Saturdays and Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Mahwah Museum, 201 Franklin Turnpike. (201) 512-0099; mahwahmuseum.org.

MONTCLAIR Montclair Art Museum “Patterns, Systems, Structures: Abstraction in American Art,” an exhibition from the permanent collection. Through May 19, 2013. “Viewpoints 2012, Studio Montclair’s 15th Annual Juried Exhibition,” works by 43 artists from around the country offering perspectives on contemporary life. Through April 20. $12 adults, $10 seniors and students; members and children under 12, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Montclair Art Museum, 3 South Mountain Avenue. montclairartmuseum.org; (973) 746-5555.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Morris Museum “Modern Tapestries and Floor Coverings Conceived by Picasso, Calder and Contemporaries.” Through April 15. “Harmony in Clay: the Elegance and Refinement of Song Ceramics,” a collection of Chinese ceramics. Through June 24. $7 to $10. “Molecules and Health,” science exhibition. Through July 1. $7 to $10. “Fresh Perspectives,” artworks by New Jersey high school artists. Through May 27. $7 to $10. Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 11 a.m. to 8 p.m.; and Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. morrismuseum.org; (973) 971-3700.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Therese A. Maloney Art Gallery, College of Saint Elizabeth “The Abstract Universe: Microcosm/Macrocosm,” group show. Through April 15. Tuesdays through Thursdays, 1 to 7 p.m.; Fridays, Saturdays and Mondays, 1 to 5 p.m. Therese A. Maloney Art Gallery, College of Saint Elizabeth, 2 Convent Road. (973) 290-4315; maloneyartgallery.org.

MORRISTOWN Acorn Hall “Over Here and Over There: Morris County’s Role in World War II,” uniforms, medals, photographs, jewelry, documents and other objects. Through June 18. $6; seniors, $5; students, $3. Mondays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Acorn Hall, 68 Morris Avenue. (973) 267-3465; www.acornhall.org.

MORRISTOWN Macculloch Hall Historical Museum “Gone for a Soldier: Jerseymen in the Civil War,” nearly 200 objects from the Civil War. Through July 1. “The Other Side of War: The Civil War on the Home Front,” exhibition including letters, poems and objects. Through Nov. 11. $4 to $8; children 12 and under, free. Wednesdays, Thursdays and Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Macculloch Hall Historical Museum, 45 Macculloch Avenue. maccullochhall.org; (973) 538-2404.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum “In the Search of an Absolute: Art of Valery Yurlov,” solo exhibition. Through June 3. “Popcorn and Starbaby: Children’s Book Illustrations by Frank Asch.” Through June 24. “Rachel Perry Welty: 24/7,” works by a Boston-based conceptual artist. Through July 8. “Aspects of Architecture: The Prints of John Taylor Arms,” 26 prints from 1919 to 1940. Through July 31. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum, 71 Hamilton Street. zimmerlimuseum.rutgers.edu; (732) 932-7237.

NEW BRUNSWICK Rutgers University, Douglass Library “Metamorphoses: Pictures by Audrey Flack,” prints, drawings and photographs by the American realist painter and sculptor Audrey Flack. Through June 30. Mondays through Thursdays, 8 a.m. to midnight; Fridays, 8 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; and Sundays, noon to midnight. Rutgers University, Douglass Library, 8 Chapel Drive. iwa.rutgers.edu; (732) 932-3726.

NEWARK The Newark Museum “Generation Fit: Steps to a Healthier Lifestyle,” interactive family exhibition that explores issues of nutrition and exercise. Through Dec. 30. “From Meiji to Modern: Japanese Art Goes Global, 19th to 21st Centuries.” Through Dec. 31. “The Teapot,” an exhibition of 66 teapots. Through Dec. 31. “Poetic Pastimes: Japan and the Art of Leisure,” an exhibition that includes more than 200 pieces of fine and decorative arts. Through May 6. “Romare Bearden Selections From the Newark Museum Collection.” Through Aug. 12. “Studio Glass: 1962-2012,” more than a dozen pieces by American and European glass artists from the museum’s glass holdings. Through Aug. 5. $6 to $10; members, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. (973) 596-6550; newarkmuseum.org.

NEWTONVILLE The African American Heritage Museum of Southern New Jersey “Stealing Home: How Jackie Robinson Changed America,” narrative, photos, video and artifacts. Through May 31. Exhibition about Langston Hughes, poet and playwright, featuring his works as well as artifacts and photos. Through May 31. Exhibition on Dr. Albert Forsythe, pioneer of black aviation, with artifacts and photos. Through May 31. The African American Heritage Museum of Southern New Jersey, 661 Jackson Road. (609) 704-5495; aahmsnj.org.

OCEAN TOWNSHIP Township of Ocean Historical Museum “A Tribute to Norma Eberhardt Dauphin,” an exhibition about the actress, a founding member of the museum. Through May 31. Donations accepted. Township of Ocean Historical Museum, 703 Deal Road. (732) 531-2136; oceanmuseum.org.

PRINCETON Morven Museum and Garden “Puzzles of the Brain: An Artist’s Journey Through Amnesia,” works by Lonni Sue Johnson. Through June 3. $6; seniors and students, $5. Wednesdays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Morven Museum and Garden, 55 Stockton Street. (609) 924-8144; morven.org.

PRINCETON Princeton University Art Museum “Qian Du (1764-1844) and Style in Qing Dynasty Painting,” landscape paintings from the museum’s collection and private lenders. Through April 22. “Myths, Tales and Poetry in Japanese Art,” works from the 15th to the 19th centuries from the museum’s collection. Through May 6. “John Constable: Oil Sketches From the Victoria and Albert Museum.” Through June 10. “Princeton and the Gothic Revival: 1870-1930,” exploring the Gothic Revival movement in architecture and design across America, using Princeton’s campus as a case study and launching point. Through June 24. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 10 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Princeton University Art Museum, Nassau and Witherspoon Streets. (609) 258-3788; artmuseum.princeton.edu.

PRINCETON Princeton University Library “Capping Liberty: The Invention of a Numismatic Iconography for the New American Republic,” coins, medals, bank notes and related books, manuscripts and graphic arts from Princeton University collections at the Milberg Gallery. Through July 8. “A Fine Addition: New and Notable Acquisitions in Princeton’s Special Collections.” Through Aug. 5. Mondays through Fridays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Princeton University Library, 1 Washington Road. princeton.edu/rbsc/; (609) 258-3184.

RAHWAY Arts Guild of New Jersey “Part and Parcel: The Deconstruction of the Female Form in Contemporary Art,” group show. Through April 26. Arts Guild of New Jersey, 1670 Irving Street. (732) 381-7511; agnj.org.

RED BANK Art Alliance of Monmouth County “Flip,” paintings by Rita K. Herzfeld. Through May 1. Tuesdays through Saturdays, noon to 4 p.m. Art Alliance of Monmouth County, 33 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9403; artallianceofmonmouth.org.

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum “We Must Never Forget: German Artists Reflect on the Holocaust,” group show. Through April 17. “Healing From War Through the Arts.” Handmade paper works by veterans participating in the Combat Paper Project. April 21 through May 1. Reception: April 21 at 7:30 p.m.Tuesdays through Thursdays, noon to 4 p.m. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. puffinculturalforum.org; (201) 836-3499.

UNION TOWNSHIP The Karl and Helen Burger Gallery at Kean University “Embodying the Sacred in Yoruba Art: Selections From the Newark Museum Collection.” Through April 18. Mondays through Thursdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. The Karl and Helen Burger Gallery at Kean University, 1000 Morris Avenue. kean.edu/gallery; (908) 737-0392.
