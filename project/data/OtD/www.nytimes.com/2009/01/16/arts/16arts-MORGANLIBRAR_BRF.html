Morgan Library to Digitize Gutenberg Bible

  • Sign In to E-Mail or Save This
  • Print
  • Reprints
Compiled by Julie Bloom
Published: January 15, 2009

Google isn’t the only organization taking steps to make important works of literature available online. The Morgan Library & Museum in New York has invited technicians and scholars to create a digital facsimile of one of its Gutenberg Bibles, the library has announced. The Morgan has three copies of the work, right, printed in Mainz, Germany, around 1455, and considered to be the first significant printed book in the West. The project is a collaboration between the library and the Humanities Media Interface Project of Keio University in Tokyo. Images of all 1,026 pages of the Bible will be on the library’s Web site, themorgan.org. This is the latest effort by the library to expand its audience on the Web. It has already created digital copies of Book 1 of “Paradise Lost,” as well as the delicate illustrations by Jean de Brunhoff from its recent exhibition “Drawing Babar: Early Drafts and Watercolors.” And it has begun to digitize the 183 tapes in The Paris Review’s archive, part of the Morgan’s permanent collection, including interviews with Doris Lessing, James Baldwin and Eudora Welty.
