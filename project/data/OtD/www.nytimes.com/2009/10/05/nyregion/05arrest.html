16-Year-Old Held in Killing of a 13-Year-Old in Queens

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By DERRICK HENRY
Published: October 4, 2009

The police arrested a teenager on Sunday in the death of a 13-year-old boy who was shot in the head as he headed toward a McDonald’s restaurant in Queens on Friday after school.

Kevin Miller was shot in the head as he headed toward a McDonald’s restaurant in Queens after school.

The suspect, Nnonso Ekwegbalu, 16, of 183rd Street, in Laurelton, was charged with second-degree murder, criminal possession of a firearm and assault in the death of the 13-year-old, Kevin Miller, and the injury of another teenager, the police said.

The shooting erupted during a fight between two students from Campus Magnet Humanities and the Arts High School in Cambria Heights, Police Commissioner Raymond W. Kelly said. The authorities did not immediately confirm that Mr. Ekwegbalu attended that school. He was expected to be arraigned Monday in Queens Criminal Court.

As they fought at the intersection of Linden and Springfield Boulevards in Cambria Heights, Mr. Kelly said, one of the students drew a revolver and fired twice. A shot hit Kevin, who died at Long Island Jewish Medical Center hours later. The other bullet hit a 17-year-old, who was working at a nearby car wash and who was treated and released from North Shore hospital, Mr. Kelly said.

On Sunday afternoon, friends and family gathered to grieve and to console Kevin’s mother, Donna Greaves, who sat on her front steps on 194th Street, in St. Albans, with a photo of her son that was surrounded by flowers.

“He was Mr. Popular,” said Ms. Greaves, 35. “So many people knew him and loved him.”

Kevin, a freshman at Campus Magnet High, was a junior usher at the New Jerusalem Baptist Church of Jamaica, loved basketball and enjoyed home make-over shows, his mother said.

“I’m angry,” said Betty Hood, 69, Kevin’s grandmother. “I’m hurt, but I’m just trying to trust in the Lord.”

Kevin’s wake was tentatively planned for Friday and a funeral was to follow on Saturday, the family said.

“I have to live the rest of my life with my son not coming home,” Ms. Greaves said. “I don’t know if I can adjust to that.”

Kareem Fahim and Rebecca White contributed reporting.
