New York Region, Long Island and New Jersey Home and Garden Tours

By SHELLY FREIERMAN
Published: April 27, 2011
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

WESTCHESTER AND PUTNAM COUNTIES

The Monterey, an 1875 Second Empire home that overlooks Hempstead Harbor, in Sea Cliff, N.Y., is a stop on a May 22 tour. 

Bedford Corners May 8 through June 12 A show house to benefit Cerebral Palsy of Westchester will take place at 205 West Patent Road (off South Bedford Road/Route 172). The event will feature top designers and will be open Tuesday through Friday, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Saturday, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sunday, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. No children under 6. Admission, $30. Information: (914) 937-3800 ext. 411 or cpwestchester.org.

Larchmont, May 15 Six houses will be open for a tour from 1 to 5 p.m. in the historic Pinebrook neighborhood of Larchmont, an area originally developed in the early 1900s as “Larchmont Park.” Most are former bungalows in the Craftsman, Colonial and Tudor styles that were built in the early 1900s, and have been renovated over the years. Tickets, including a reception from 3 to 6 p.m., are $55 ($45 for members of the Larchmont Historical Society, which is sponsoring the event). Same-day tickets will be available at a booth at the corner of Mayhew and Stuyvesant avenues. Information: (914) 381-2239 and larchmonthistory.org.

Other events that weekend include a cocktail party and lecture on May 14, from 6 to 8:30 p.m. at the former Larchmont residence of C. Paul Jennewein, a classical and Art Deco sculptor. Tickets for the party, tour and lecture, which will focus on Mr. Jennewein’s work, are $95 (members, $75).

Cold Spring, Mahopac, Brewster, Garrison and Putnam Valley, June 11 A tour of 10 gardens will take place from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m., to benefit Partners with PARC, a service organization for people with developmental disabilities. Tickets are $30 in advance by June 9; $40 on the day of the tour, at Butterfield Library, 10 Morris Avenue (Main Street) in Cold Spring. Information: (845) 278-7272, ext. 287 or at PartnerswithPARC.org. There will also be a private guided tour of two houses on Petra Island in Mahopac that were designed by Frank Lloyd Wright: a guest cottage built in 1951 that has recently been restored, and a 5,000-square-foot four-bedroom home built in 2007. Tickets for the Wright tour, which includes the garden tour, are $125 each in advance; if available on June 11, those tickets will be $150.

DUTCHESS AND COLUMBIA COUNTIES

Wappingers Falls and New Hamburg, June 4 The Dutchess County Historical Society will offer its 18th annual Silver Ribbon House Tour from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. The tour will include the Grinnell Library, the sixth-oldest library in New York State; the Mesier Homestead, which dates to the 18th century and is now a museum and home to the Wappingers Historical Society; and the Reese estate, the home of the founder of Scenic Hudson, as well as several private homes. Tickets, $50, at Zion Episcopal Church, Saterlee Place and Route 9D in Wappingers Falls. Information and advance tickets, $45 (members, $40): (845) 471-1630 and dutchesscountyhistoricalsociety.org.

Hyde Park and Staatsburg, June 11 Six private gardens and the Beatrix Jones Farrand gardens at Bellefield will be open for a tour from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. as part of the Hyde Park in Bloom event. Bellefield is an 18th-century house that now serves as headquarters for the National Park Service at the Roosevelt-Vanderbilt National Historic Sites. Tickets, which include breakfast from 9 to 11 a.m. and benefit the Hyde Park Visual Environment Committee, are $30 in advance. On June 11, tickets are $35 at Bellefield, which is on the site of the F.D.R. Library and Museum, 4079 Albany Post Road (Route 9) in Hyde Park. No children under 13. Advance tickets and information: (845) 229-5955 or by e-mail to hpvecboard@gmail.com.

Ancram, July 23 Five houses, some with gardens, will be open from noon to 4 p.m. along Doodletown Road, which winds through forests, wetlands and former farmland. The sites include a house built around a 19th-century silo and a 1790 house that has been restored with many “green” features and extensive gardens. The tour will be followed by a wine-and-cheese garden party featuring local produce. Tickets, $50 ($40 for members of the Ancram Preservation Group), available starting at 11 a.m. at Ancram Town Hall, 1416 County Route 7 (a quarter-mile north of the intersection with Route 82). Advance tickets before July 1, $40, and information: (518) 398-6435 or at ancrampreservationgroup.org.

Dutchess County, Oct. 15 The Country Seats Tour, featuring 9 to 12 historic farms, will take place from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. to benefit Hudson River Heritage. Tickets and information will be available closer to the event: (845) 876-2474 and hudsonriverheritage.org.

ORANGE COUNTY

Newburgh, June 11 and Sept. 10 The Historical Society of Newburgh Bay and the Highlands will offer two guided garden tours in 2011. The tours will be led by Lily Norton, a master gardener through the Cornell Cooperative Extension, and Cathy Cahill, a gardening enthusiast. Tickets, one tour, $25; both tours, $45; those under 18, no charge. The tours will begin at Crawford House, home to the society, 189 Montgomery Street (at Clinton Street). Information about the event times and gardens to be visited will be available after May 1 at (845) 561-2585 or newburghhistoricalsociety.com.

Goshen, June 18 Six gardens will be open from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. in a benefit for St. James Episcopal Church, where lunch will be served. Three of the gardens are within walking distance of the church, including a formal Empire-style garden centered around a fountain, which is planted with perennials and roses and features several statues, and another garden bordered by boxwood hedges with seating areas set along its pathways. Experts will be on hand at some sites to offer advice for home gardeners. If available, tickets will be $45 on the day of the tour, at the church, One St. James Place (at Church Street). Advance tickets, $40, and information: (845) 294-6225 or stjamesgoshen.org.

Warwick, July 9 The annual tour to benefit Warwick Valley Gardeners, with six gardens, will take place from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Rain date is July 10. Tickets, $15, at the Railroad Green, corner of Railroad Avenue and Main Street. Information by e-mail to jmeth66@gmail.com and at

LONG ISLAND

Sea Cliff, May 22 The Monterey, an 1875 Second Empire home that overlooks Hempstead Harbor, is the centerpiece of a 12-residence tour from noon to 4 p.m. The Monterey was transformed into a hotel in the North Shore resort community from 1895 to 1912, and has remained with the same extended family and their friends for 136 years. It still has most of its original lighting fixtures, ceiling plasterwork, pocket doors, mantelpieces and radiators. Some features — like the cabinets from the original basement kitchen and the mantle in a residential apartment — have been incorporated into successive renovations.

The residences also include a fully renovated cottage with a stainless steel and glass staircase that is echoed by the stainless steel railings on the house’s triple-level decks, and an 1873 house with a copper and slate roof, elaborate Victorian trim, oak floors and a hand-built fireplace made with stacked brick and no grout. No children unless carried throughout by an adult. All tickets are $35. Advance tickets before May 13 by check and SASE to Sea Cliff Landmarks Association, 19 Locust Place, Sea Cliff, NY 11579. Tickets will be sold on the day of the tour at the village green at the corner of Sea Cliff and Central Avenues. Information: (800) 963-2351.

Lloyd Harbor and Huntington Bay, May 25 Five homes will be open from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m., including a 1928 brick Georgian that is the residence of Kim Hendrickson-Radovich, an interior designer. The house has its original moldings and oak floors, a newly renovated kitchen and butler’s pantry, and a relief plaster ceiling in the living room with an animal motif of bears, foxes and whales. The designer’s studio, built over a three-car garage, will also be on the tour. Proceeds will benefit the Family Service League of Long Island. No children under 12. Tickets $55 in advance, or $60 on the day of the tour at Coindre Hall, 101 Browns Road (near Southdown Avenue), Huntington. Lunch will be available for $30, at Coindre Hall. Information and advance purchase: (631) 427-3700, extension 255, and fsl-li.org.

Southampton, June 11 and 12 Four renowned interior designers — Mario Buatta, Alexa Hampton, Charlotte Moss, and William Sofield — will speak at the Parrish Art Museum from 9 a.m. to 12:30 p.m. on June 11 to kick off the annual garden symposium and tour. The tour, on June 12, from 10 a.m. to 3 p.m., will include the private gardens of five interior designers, including those of Mr. Sofield, Steven Gambrel and John Barman. Tickets to the symposium and tour are $200 (members, $150); those contributing at a higher level, from $350, are invited to a reception on June 12. The museum is at 25 Jobs Lane. Tickets and information: (631) 283-2118, ext. 42, and parrishart.org.

Bridgehampton, July 24 through Sept. 4 This year’s edition of the Hampton Designer Showhouse will begin with a preview party on July 23 (tickets, $225), and will be open daily through Labor Day Weekend. Proceeds will benefit Southampton Hospital. Hours: 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. No children under 6. Admission is $30. More information, including the show house location, will be available on hamptondesignershowhouse.com on June 1. Information: (631) 745-0004.

NEW JERSEY

Locust, May 3 to June 12 Holly Hill, a brick country house built on the Navesink River in 1934, will be the site of a show house to benefit the Visiting Nurse Association of Central Jersey. The 12-acre property includes a richly landscaped pool area, gardens set on terraces built with brownstone salvaged from 19th-century bridges, and several mature holly trees. The house, at 813 Navesink River Road (near the Oceanic Bridge), will be open Tuesday through Sunday, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Admission is $35; senior citizens, $25. Information: (732) 224-6780 and statelyhomesbythesea.com.

Summit, New Providence and Westfield, June 4 Five residences will be open from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. to benefit the Westfield Symphony Orchestra, including a hilltop house by Henry Bacon, the Beaux-Arts architect who designed the Lincoln Memorial in Washington. Tickets, $35, at Baron’s Drugs, 243 East Broad Street (Central Avenue) in Westfield. Advance tickets, $30, and information: (908) 232-9400 and westfieldsymphony.org.

Rumson, Little Silver, Shrewsbury and Locust, June 4 and 5 Five community gardens, including the Three Girls Garden and the Grove West Organic Community Garden, will be included on the Two Rivers Garden Tour, to benefit the Jacqueline M. Wilentz Comprehensive Breast Center at Monmouth Medical Center. The garden tour hours will be 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. on June 4, and 11:30 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. on June 5. Tickets, $25, on the day of the tour at any of the sites. Advance tickets and senior citizens at all times, $20, and information: (732) 923-6886 and tworivershow.org.

Boonton, June 4 The Boonton Historical Society & Museum will sponsor a tour of eight houses from 11:30 a.m. to 4 p.m., including the Miller-Kingsland House, the oldest home in Boonton, which dates to 1740 and has been owned by only three families. Other sites include the 1894 Company House built by the Boonton Ironworks for its immigrant workers, which features its original fireplaces, mantels, ceiling beams and exposed brick, and a Sears Roebuck 1930s catalog house. Tickets, $25 at the museum, 210 Main Street (Route 202). Advance tickets, $20, and information: (973) 316-0976 or by e-mail to BoontonHistory@yahoo.com.

Hoboken, June 5 Eight to 10 private gardens will be open for guided tours from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. One garden, designed by Adam Hoppe, a landscape architect, is centered around a curving stone patio bordered with annuals and flowers and features a spherical stone fountain set before three hornbeam trees and a contemporary fire pit. Tours will leave Hoboken Historical Museum, at 1301 Hudson Street (13th Street), every half-hour. Rain date is June 12. Tickets, $25, to benefit the museum; children under 13 free. Advance ticket purchase, $20, is recommended: (201) 656-2240 and hobokensecretgardens.com.

Montclair, June 10 and 11 Seven private gardens will be open from 11 a.m. to 4 p.m., including one that combines elements of a traditional English garden with tropical flowers, and which is planted with boxwood, hakonechloa grass, clematis and Japanese fountain grass in stone beds surrounding a swimming pool. There will also be guided tours of the gardens at Van Vleck House and Gardens. Tickets, good for either day, $30, at Van Vleck, 21 Van Vleck Street (Upper Mountain Avenue). Information: (973) 744-4752 and vanvleck.org.

Cape May, June 17 through Oct. 30 Osprey Landing at Cape May Marine, which overlooks Cape May Harbor and its wetlands, will be the site of a show house by 12 designers. The four-story townhouse has a designer kitchen with Viking appliances, a full-floor master bedroom suite, three decks and a deep-water boat slip. The house, sponsored by the Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts & Humanities, will be open daily from 1 to 4 p.m. and Thursday and Friday, Sunday and Monday, 7 p.m. to 9 p.m. Cape May Marine is off of Lafayette Street, two blocks past the southern terminus of the Garden State Parkway. Admission: $15; $10, children ages 3-12.  Information: (609) 884-5404 and capemaymac.org.
