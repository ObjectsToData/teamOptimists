What You Get for ... $379,000

Michael Clevenger for The New York Times

A duplex condo in the East Market District of downtown Louisville, Ky., is on the market for $379,000. More Photos »

By MIKE POWELL
Published: December 28, 2011
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

LOUISVILLE, KY.

Multimedia
Slide Show
Homes for $379,000

WHAT: A condo with two bedrooms and two and a half baths in downtown Louisville

HOW MUCH: $379,000

SIZE: 1,840 square feet

PRICE PER SQUARE FOOT: $205.98

SETTING: This building is in a part of downtown Louisville variously referred to as the East Market District and NuLu, a portmanteau word for New Louisville. Over the past 10 years, developers and business owners have converted the neighborhood’s narrow 19th-century brick buildings into condos, galleries, boutiques and restaurants.

INSIDE: This unit is on the second and third floor of a three-story building with retail at street level. It was built in 1885 and has been used variously as a general store, a storage warehouse and an art gallery. It retains its original wood floors, exposed brick walls, living room staircase and wood ceiling beams. On the main level, there’s an open-plan living and dining area, a kitchen and a half-bathroom. The living room has three floor-to-ceiling windows with views of downtown Louisville. The kitchen was updated in 2006, when the building was converted to condos.

Both bedrooms are upstairs. The master has 20-foot ceilings and the same city views the living room has. Its bathroom features a steam shower and a double vanity. The second bedroom is down the hall from the master. Another full bathroom upstairs features a whirlpool tub. The unit also comes with one assigned parking space in an attached garage.

OUTDOOR SPACE: Tenants share a courtyard with a fountain

TAXES: $4,183.39 plus $260 a month for homeowner’s association fees

CONTACT: Tommy Arnold, Keller Williams Louisville East; (502) 442-1133; tommyarnold.com

MILWAUKEE

WHAT: A 1905 house with four bedrooms and two and a half baths

HOW MUCH: $377,500

SIZE: 2,534 square feet

PRICE PER SQUARE FOOT: $148.97

SETTING: This house is in a residential neighborhood between the Milwaukee River and Lake Michigan. The neighborhood is a collection of older single-family houses and the occasional duplex, bracketed by two parks designed by Frederick Law Olmsted: Lake Park, about four blocks east, and Riverside Park, about 10 blocks west. A campus of the University of Wisconsin is about three blocks away, and there’s a commercial corridor with restaurants, bars and markets within a few blocks. The city’s central business district is about five miles away.

INSIDE: The house was renovated by its current owners over the past six or seven years. It retains many of its original details, including hardwood floors, a bench seat in the foyer, and some built-in shelving. On the main level, there’s a living room with a fireplace, a formal dining room, a kitchen and a family room with a bay window. The kitchen has oak floors, two convection ovens, cherry cabinets, a center island and granite countertops. The house’s bedrooms are on the second and third levels. The master has an en-suite bathroom. The third level has a playroom.

OUTDOOR SPACE: Off the kitchen, there’s a deck and a backyard. There’s also a balcony off the master bedroom.

TAXES: $9,489

CONTACT: Jim Christenson, First Weber Group; (414) 745-7924; firstweber.com

PORTLAND, ORE.

WHAT: A contemporary with three bedrooms and two and a half baths

HOW MUCH: $375,000

SIZE: 2,020 square feet

PRICE PER SQUARE FOOT: $185.64

SETTING: This house is in Woodstock, a primarily residential neighborhood in southeastern Portland. Woodstock was platted in 1889 and retains many of its original houses, now alongside residences and commercial buildings built after World War II. Woodstock Boulevard, a commercial corridor with grocery stores, a library, barber shops, bars and restaurants, is about four blocks away. Reed — a small, progressive college known for its humanities programs and narrow student-to-teacher ratio — is about a mile away.

INSIDE: This house was built around 2005 by the current owners on the foundation of a 1951 ranch. One of the owners, a contractor, designed and helped built it. The main level has an open-plan great room with a kitchen and a living and dining area. The living area has a fireplace. There’s also a family room or den. Upstairs, there are three bedrooms. One is being used as an office. The master faces west and has a balcony. It’s part of a suite with a bathroom.

OUTDOOR SPACE: A flat grass backyard and a deck off of the dining area

TAXES: $4,657

CONTACT: Margie Wood, Windermere Cronin & Caplan Realty Group; (503) 936-9088; windermere.com
