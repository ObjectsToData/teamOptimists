Calendar

Events in New Jersey

Published: March 25, 2011
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on around the state. Items may be submitted by mail to New Jersey Calendar, Metropolitan, The New York Times, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405; or by e-mail to njtowns@nytimes.com.

Comedy

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center Bob Newhart. Thursday at 8 p.m. $29 to $85. Popovich Comedy Pet Theater. April 8 at 7 p.m. $15 to $35. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

HASBROUCK HEIGHTS Bananas Comedy Club Patty Rosborough. Friday at 9 p.m. $15. Sebastian Maniscalco. April 2 at 8 and 10:30 p.m. $20. Bananas Comedy Club, 283 Route 17 South. (201) 727-1090; bananascomedyclub.com.

Film

BLOOMFIELD Bloomfield Public Library “Phone Call From a Stranger,” starring Bette Davis. Thursday at 12:15 p.m. Free. Bloomfield Public Library, 90 Broad Street. (973) 566-6200; bplnj.org.

MIDDLETOWN Middletown Township Public Library “Some Like It Hot.” Monday at 7 p.m. Free. Middletown Township Public Library, 55 New Monmouth Road. (732) 671-3700; mtpl.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater “It Happened One Night,” starring Clark Gable and Claudette Colbert. Wednesday at 7 p.m. Free. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

For Children

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center “Kidz Cabaret — Funkey Monkeys!” a group of musicians and comedians. March 27 at 1 and 4 p.m. $17. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

MORRISTOWN Mayo Center for the Performing Arts “Winnie the Pooh,” presented by Sign Stage on Tour. Ages 4 to 10. April 10 at 1:30 and 4 p.m. $12. Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

RAHWAY Union County Performing Arts Center “The Three Prince Charmings,” family performances. Through March 27. $16; one child’s ticket free with each regular admission ticket. Union County Performing Arts Center, 1601 Irving Street. (732) 499-8226; ucpac.org.

ROSELAND Essex County Environmental Center Rock ’n’ Roll Geology Club, educational activities including exploring local geology, discovering what is in local soil and identifying rocks and minerals. Ages 7 to 12. Tuesday at 4 p.m. $6. Essex County Environmental Center, 621 Eagle Rock Avenue. essex-countynj.org; (973) 723-1642.

TENAFLY Tenafly Nature Center Afternoon Explorers. Children explore the Nature Center while learning about the wonders of the natural world. Through Tuesday. $15 and $20. Tenafly Nature Center, 313 Hudson Avenue. (201) 568-6093; tenaflynaturecenter.org.

WAYNE Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University Jungle Joe’s Wildlife Adventures, popular and exotic animals. April 3 at 2 p.m. Pre-show activities begin at 1:15 p.m. $10. Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University, 300 Pompton Road. wpunj.edu/wplive/sheacenter.htm; (973) 720-2371.

Music and Dance

CHATHAM Sanctuary Concerts, Presbyterian Church of Chatham Hot Club of Cowtown and the Frank Vignola Trio, western swing and gypsy jazz. April 2 at 8 p.m. $25. Sanctuary Concerts, Presbyterian Church of Chatham, 240 Southern Boulevard. (973) 376-4946; sanctuaryconcerts.org.

EAST RUTHERFORD Izod Center Smucker’s Stars on Ice 25th Anniversary Tour. April 2 at 7:30 p.m. $20.45 to $163.90. Izod Center, 50 Route 120. (201) 935-3900; izodcenter.com.

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center Pilobolus, dance theater. Friday at 8 p.m. $29 to $55. Itzhak Perlman, violinist. April 3 at 3 p.m. $39 to $150. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

HAMPTON Hunterdon Hills Playhouse “The Great American Songbook 2,” a musical-comedy revue. Through Thursday. $51.50 to $64.50. Hunterdon Hills Playhouse, 88 Route 173 West. (908) 730-8007; hhplayhouse.com.

HIGHTSTOWN Peddie School Tim Eriksen, folk and rock. Friday at 8 p.m. $10. Peddie School, 201 South Main Street. (609) 944-7500; peddie.org.

HILLSIDE Enlow Recital Hall at Kean University The Arco Ensemble performs Concerto for Guitar and String Orchestra, by Matthew Halper. April 9 at 8 p.m. $25. Enlow Recital Hall at Kean University, 215 North Avenue. enlowhall.kean.edu; (908) 737-7469.

JERSEY CITY St. Paul’s Episcopal Church Indian Music and Dance, featuring artists from the Kalavant Center. March 27 at 1 p.m. $10, suggested donation. St. Paul’s Episcopal Church, 38 Duncan Avenue. (201) 433-4922; stpaulsjc.org.

MONTCLAIR Alexander Kasser Theater “The Matter of Origins,” featuring the Liz Lerman Dance Exchange. Through March 27. $15. Alexander Kasser Theater, 1 Normal Avenue. (973) 655-5112; peakperfs.org.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Bickford Theater Nicki Parrott Trio, with Warren Vaché and Vinnie Corrao, jazz. Wednesday at 8 p.m. $15 and $18. Bickford Theater, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3706; morrismuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN St. Peter’s Episcopal Church Deniz Uz, organ. March 27 at 3 p.m. $15, suggested donation. St. Peter’s Episcopal Church, South Street and Miller Road. stpetersmorristown.org; (973) 538-0555.

MORRISTOWN Mayo Center for the Performing Arts George Winston, piano. March 27 at 3 p.m. $37 to $52. Pilobolus, dance theater. April 2 at 8 p.m. $32 to $57. The Dukes of Dixieland. April 7 at 8 p.m. $47. Rosanne Cash, singer-songwriter. April 8 at 8 p.m. $42 to $67. Itzhak Perlman, violinist. April 9 at 8 p.m. $75 to $125. Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts New Jersey Jazz Society presents Antoinette Montague, singer. March 27 at 3 p.m. $15. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater The New Jersey Symphony Orchestra with Marc-André Hamelin, pianist. March 27 at 3 p.m. $20 to $82. Jeff Beck and the Imelda May Band, rock. Tuesday at 8 p.m. $40 to $85. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. statetheatrenj.org; (732) 246-7469.

NEWARK Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center Nashville Hitmakers, featuring Brett Jones, Tony Lane, David Lee and Wynn Varble. April 9 at 8 p.m. $32. Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center Cameron Carpenter, organ. Friday at 8 p.m. $25 to $35. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater Todd Rundgren. Monday at 8 p.m. $25 to $95. Dick Fox’s Golden Boys, featuring Frankie Avalon, Fabian and Bobby Rydell. Thursday at 8 p.m. $38 to $148.“Fantasia,” film. Friday at 7 p.m. Free. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street.

countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

WAYNE Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University Works by composers from Nigeria and Uganda, presented by the William Paterson University New Music Ensemble. Monday at 7:30 p.m. Kashmir: The Led Zeppelin Show, tribute band. April 2 at 8 p.m. $16 to $25. Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University, 300 Pompton Road. (973) 720-2371; wpunj.edu/wplive/sheacenter.htm.

WEST LONG BRANCH Pollak Theater, Monmouth University Roger McGuinn, folk and rock. April 2 at 8 p.m. $33. The Klezmatics, a neo-klezmer group. April 10 at 3 p.m. $33. Pollak Theater, Monmouth University, 400 Cedar Avenue. (732) 263-6889; monmouth.edu/arts_events.

Outdoors

ROSELAND Essex County Environmental Center New Jersey Woodturners. Learn to create art from pieces of wood found in nature. Monday at 7 p.m. $8; members, free. Essex County Environmental Center, 621 Eagle Rock Avenue. (973) 228-8776; essex-countynj.org.

Theater

CAPE MAY Emlen Physick Estate “Murder at the Physick Estate,” mystery by Ray Crew. April 2 through 30. $20 and $25. Emlen Physick Estate, 1048 Washington Street. capemaymac.org; (800) 275-4278.

JERSEY CITY The Underground Theater at St. Michaels Church J City Theater presents “Private Eyes,” comedy by Steven Dietz. Friday through April 16. $20. The Underground Theater at St. Michaels Church, 252 Ninth Street. (800) 838-3006; www.jcity.org.

LEONIA Civil War Drill Hall Theater “High Society,” musical with book by Arthur Kopit and music and lyrics by Cole Porter, presented by the Players’ Guild of Leonia. Through Thursday. $18 and $20. Civil War Drill Hall Theater, 130 Grand Avenue. leoniaplayers.org; (201) 947-9606.

LONG BRANCH New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater “Puma,” by Julie Gilbert and Frank Evans. Through April 3. $25 to $40. New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater, 179 Broadway. (732) 229-3166; njrep.org.

MADISON F.M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University Lend Us Your Ears: Play Reading Series, “The Notorious Lady Susan” by Jane Austen, adapted by Tom Fontana. April 4 at 7 p.m. $10 and $15. F. M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University, 36 Madison Avenue. njshakespeare.org; (973) 408-5600.

MIDDLETOWN Navesink Library Theater “Rumors,” by Neil Simon. April 9 through 30. $17; students and seniors, $14. Navesink Library Theater, Sears and Monmouth Avenues. monmouthplayers.org; (732) 291-9211.

MILLBURN Paper Mill Playhouse “A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Forum,” with music and lyrics by Stephen Sondheim and book by Burt Shevelove and Larry Gelbart. Through April 10. $25 to $92. Paper Mill Playhouse, 22 Brookside Drive. (973) 376-4343; papermill.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK George Street Playhouse “The Fox on the Fairway,” by Ken Ludwig. Through April 17. $26.50 to $63.50. George Street Playhouse, 9 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7717; gsponline.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Mason Gross School of the Arts “Tracing Nora,” by Peter Handy. Through April 2. $15 to $25. Mason Gross School of the Arts, 33 Livingston Avenue. (732) 932-7511; mgsa.rutgers.edu.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center “Monty Python’s Spamalot,” musical comedy. Tuesday through April 3. $24 to $90. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

NEWARK Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center David Gonzalez, storyteller, performs a one-man show based on “Sleeping Beauty.” March 27 at 2 p.m. $20 and $22. “Celebrity Autobiography,” comedy, celebrity memoirs acted out by a rotating cast. April 8 at 8 p.m. $45 and $60. Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street.

njpac.org; (888) 466-5722.

POMPTON LAKES Smiling Rhino Theater “Cabaret,” by Joe Masteroff, music by John Kander and lyrics by Fred Ebb. Through April 9. $20 to $25. Smiling Rhino Theater, 247 Wanaque Avenue. (973) 248-9491; smilingrhinotheatre.com.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center “Crimes of the Heart,” comedy by Beth Henley. Through March 27. $20 to $60. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater “The Wonderful Wizard of Oz,” presented by Company of Dance Arts. Through March 27. $15 to $35. “Annie,” presented by Phoenix Productions. April 8 through 17. $22 to $29. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9000; countbasietheatre.org.

RED BANK Two River Theater “Candida,” by George Bernard Shaw, directed by David Staller. Through April 10. $24 to $60. Two River Theater, 21 Bridge Avenue. (732) 345-1400; trtc.org.

SOUTH ORANGE South Orange Performing Arts Center “Tartuffe,” comedy by Molière. Friday through April 10. $15. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-2787; sopacnow.org.

WAYNE Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University “Crumbs From the Table of Joy,” drama by Lynn Nottage. Through Thursday. $9 and $12. Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University, 300 Pompton Road. (973) 720-2371; wpunj.edu/wplive/sheacenter.htm.

WEST LONG BRANCH Pollak Theater, Monmouth University “In the Mood,” a 1940s musical revue. March 27 at 7 p.m. $35 and $42. Pollak Theater, Monmouth University, 400 Cedar Avenue. monmouth.edu/arts_events; (732) 263-6889.

WEST WINDSOR Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College “Steel Magnolias.” Through April 3. $10 to $14. Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College, 1200 Old Trenton Road. (609) 570-3333; kelseyatmccc.org.

Museums and Galleries

CAPE MAY Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities “Can I Get a Witness! A Testament to the Black Church in Cape May County,” photographs. Through April 17. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. (609) 884-5404; capemaymac.org.

CLINTON Red Mill Museum Village “The Stars and Stripes: Fabric of the American Spirit,” exhibition of antique American flags. Through June 14. $8 general admission, $6 seniors, $5 children 6 to 12. Members and children under 6, free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Red Mill Museum Village, 56 Main Street. (908) 735-4101, ext. 107; theredmill.org.

HALEDON American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark “The Line That Divides: NAFTA Trade Corridor,” exhibition of photos, paintings and video by Pamela Calore. Through April 23. $3 to $5, suggested donation; members and children under 12, free. Wednesdays through Saturdays, 1 to 4 p.m., or by appointment. American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark, 83 Norwood Street. (973) 595-7953; labormuseum.org.

HAMILTON Grounds for Sculpture “Fall/Winter Exhibition 2010,” group show. Through April 10. $8 to $12; members and children under 5, free. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. groundsforsculpture.org; (609) 586-0616.

LINCROFT Monmouth Museum “Give Peace a Chance: John Lennon and Yoko Ono’s Bed-In for Peace Exhibition,” featuring more than 40 large-format images by Gerry Deiter, photojournalist. Through May 8. $7; members and children under 2, free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Monmouth Museum, 765 Newman Springs Road. (732) 747-2266; monmouthmuseum.org.

MADISON Museum of Early Trades and Crafts “Mariners, Merchants and Pirates,” exhibition exploring early American seafaring techniques, tools and methods and the nautical navigation system of the time. Through Sept. 4. $3 and $5; members and children under 6, free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. (973) 377-2982; rosenet.org/metc.

MONTCLAIR Montclair Art Museum “Engaging Nature: American and Native American Artists (A.D. 1200-2004),” art and artifacts, including prehistoric Native American ceramics, 19th-century landscape paintings and contemporary staged photographs. Through Sept. 25. “Warhol and Cars: American Icons,” showcasing Warhol’s fascination with cars as products of American consumer society spanning his career from 1946 to 1986. Through June 19. $12 adults, $10 seniors and students; members and children under 12, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Montclair Art Museum, 3 South Mountain Avenue. (973) 746-5555; montclairartmuseum.org.

MONTCLAIR Studio Montclair “Sustainability and the Artistic Vision,” group show featuring artists who use sustainable materials. Through April 29. “Rendering Green,” featuring artworks by 18 Studio Montclair members. Through April 29. Mondays through Fridays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m., or by appointment. Studio Montclair, 33 Plymouth Street. (973) 744-1818; studiomontclair.org.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Morris Museum “Teapots by Design: From the Collection of Unjeria C. Jackson, M.D.,” exhibition of more than 150 teapots. Through April 3. $7 to $10. Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 11 a.m. to 8 p.m.; and Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3700; morrismuseum.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum “Animal Fair: Birds, Beasts and Bugs in Children’s Book Illustrations,” 28 original works of storybook animals, in various media and styles. Through June 5. “Vladimir Nemukhin: Works on Paper.” March 27 through Oct. 9. “Embodied Dreams: The Later Work of Boris Sveshnikov.” Through April 10. “Dancing With the Dark: Joan Snyder Prints 1963-2010.” Through May 29. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum, 71 Hamilton Street. (732) 932-7237; zimmerlimuseum.rutgers.edu.

NEW BRUNSWICK Rutgers University, Douglass Library Mary H. Dana Women Artists Series 40th Anniversary Exhibition: “Joan Snyder/Intimate Works,” paintings by Joan Snyder from 1965 to 2010. Through June 5. Mondays through Thursdays, 8 a.m. to midnight; Fridays, 8 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Sundays, noon to midnight. Rutgers University, Douglass Library Galleries, 8 Chapel Drive. (732) 932-3726, ext.15; libraries.rutgers.edu.

NEWARK The Newark Museum “Posing Beauty: African American Images From the 1890s to the Present,” exhibition of photographs. Through April 28. “Skies Alive: Bird Migration in the Garden State,” photographs and interactive computer programs to illustrate and explore migration. Through June 1. Free to $10. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. (973) 596-6550; newarkmuseum.org.

PRINCETON Bernstein Gallery “Retrospect,” documentary photography by Damien Schumann. Through Thursday. Mondays through Fridays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Bernstein Gallery, Robertson Hall. wws.princeton.edu/bernstein; (609) 258-8880.

PRINCETON Historical Society of Princeton “Einstein at Home,” a glimpse of the scientist’s personal side. Through Jan. 16. Tuesdays through Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Historical Society of Princeton, 158 Nassau Street. (609) 921-6748; princetonhistory.org.

PRINCETON Morven Museum and Garden “Ribbon of Life: The Delaware and Raritan Canal,” 19th- and 20th-century postcard views of the Delaware and Raritan Canal. Through June 19. $6 for adults and $5 for seniors and students. Wednesdays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Morven Museum and Garden, 55 Stockton Street. (609) 924-8144; morven.org.

PRINCETON Paul Robeson Center for the Arts “Terrace Project: John McDevitt,” steel sculptures by John McDevitt in the outdoor exhibition space of the Paul Robeson Center for the Arts. Through May 30. 102 Witherspoon Street. (609) 924-8777; artscouncilofprinceton.org.

PRINCETON Princeton University Art Museum “The Tokaido Road: 19th- and 20th-Century Journeys through Japanese Prints.” Through June 5. “Lasting Impressions of the Grand Tour: Giuseppe Vasi’s Rome.” Through June 12. “When Men and Mountains Meet: China as Land and People.” Through June 26. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 10 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Princeton University Art Museum, Nassau and Witherspoon Streets. artmuseum.princeton.edu; (609) 258-3788.

RAHWAY Arts Guild of New Jersey “Bling,” exhibition of shiny, glittery and bejeweled works of art. Through April 14. Arts Guild of New Jersey, 1670 Irving Street. (732) 381-7511; agnj.org.

SUMMIT Reeves-Reed Arboretum “Impressions of a Garden,” works by the contemporary American Impressionist painter Lisa Palombot. Through May 8. Reeves-Reed Arboretum, 165 Hobart Avenue. reeves-reedarboretum.org; (908) 273-8787.

TEANECK Fairleigh Dickinson University Art Gallery, University Hall “Earth Day Show,” mixed-media. April 2 through 22. Mondays through Fridays, 9:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Fairleigh Dickinson University Art Gallery, University Hall. (201) 692-2801; fdu.edu.
