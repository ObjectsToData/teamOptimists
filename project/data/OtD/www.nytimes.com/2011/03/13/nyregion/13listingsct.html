Events in Connecticut

Published: March 11, 2011
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on in Connecticut this week. Items for the guide should be sent at least three weeks in advance to ctcal@nytimes.com, or by mail to the Connecticut Calendar, Metropolitan, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405.

Comedy

MASHANTUCKET Comix at Foxwoods Rob Riggle. Thursday through March 19. $25 to $45. Comix at Foxwoods, 240 MGM Grand Drive. comixatfoxwoods.com; (866) 646-0609.

NEW HAVEN Joker’s Wild Comedy Club “The Next Big Thing,” comedy contest hosted by Pat Oates and Ted Steier. March 13. $10. Joker’s Wild Comedy Club, 232 Wooster Street. jokerswildclub.com; (203) 773-0733.

TORRINGTON Warner Theater Bill Engvall. March 13 at 7:30 p.m. $49.50 and $100. Warner Theater, 68 Main Street. (860) 489-7180; warnertheatre.org.

Film

HARTFORD Real Art Ways “Orgasm, Inc.,” documentary by Elizabeth Canner. Through Thursday. $4.50 to $9. “Metropolis,” the Fritz Lang film, accompanied by the Alloy Orchestra. March 19 at 8 p.m. $20 to $30. Real Art Ways, 56 Arbor Street. realartways.org; (860) 232-1006.

HARTFORD Wadsworth Atheneum Museum of Art “Berlin ’36,” opening night feature of the “15th Annual Mandell JCC Hartford Jewish Film Festival.” March 26 at 8:30 p.m. $10 to $60. Wadsworth Atheneum Museum of Art, 600 Main Street. wadsworthatheneum.org; (860) 278-2670.

NEW HAVEN New Haven Public Library “Treasures of Korean Art and Culture,” presented by the Korean Spirit and Culture Promotion Project. Includes documentary films, a traditional Korean meal and a wedding re-enactment. March 19 at 1 p.m. Free. New Haven Public Library, 133 Elm Street. (203) 946-8130; cityofnewhaven.com/library.

For Children

BRIDGEPORT Downtown Cabaret Theater “Jack and the Beanstalk,” musical comedy. Ages 3 and up. Through April 10. $18. Downtown Cabaret Theater, 263 Golden Hill Street. downtowncabaret.org; (203) 576-1636.

NEW HAVEN Peabody Museum of Natural History, Yale University “Black Holes: Space Warps and Time Twists,” interactive exhibition. All ages. Through May 1. Mondays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Peabody Museum of Natural History, Yale University, 170 Whitney Avenue. yale.edu/peabody; (203) 432-5050

NEW MILFORD New Milford Public Library “Irish Music With Tom O’Carroll,” family program of Irish music and folklore. All ages. March 13 at 2 p.m. Free. New Milford Public Library, 24 Main Street. (860) 355-1191; biblio.org/newmilford.

RIDGEFIELD Ridgefield Playhouse “The Ugly Duckling,” presented by the Bits ‘N Pieces Puppet Theater. Ages 4 and up. March 19 at 11 a.m. and 2 p.m. $15. Ridgefield Playhouse, 80 East Ridge Road. ridgefieldplayhouse.com; (203) 438-5795.

STAMFORD Stamford Museum and Nature Center “Sunday Explorers,” interactive exploration. All ages. March 13. $5 and $10. Stamford Museum and Nature Center, 39 Scofieldtown Road. stamfordmuseum.org; (203) 322-1646.

WESTPORT Westport Arts Center “WACky Family Day: Music from All Cultures,” includes crafts and educational activities. All ages. March 20 at 2 p.m. $10; Adults and under 2, free. Westport Arts Center, 51 Riverside Avenue. (203) 222-7070; westportartscenter.org.

Museums and Galleries

DARIEN Darien Nature Center “In the Bag: The Art and Politics of the Reusable Bag Movement,” group show. Through April 23. Mondays through Fridays, 9 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 1 p.m. Darien Nature Center, 120 Brookside Road. dariennaturecenter.org; (203) 655-7459.

FAIRFIELD Thomas J. Walsh Art Gallery, Fairfield University “To Honor My People,” works by Norman Gorbaty. Through March 27. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Thomas J. Walsh Art Gallery, Fairfield University, 1073 North Benson Road. (203) 254-4000, ext. 2969; fairfield.edu.

GREENWICH Bruce Museum “Cindy Sherman: Works From Friends of the Bruce Museum,” photographs from local collections. Through April 23. “Arctic Sanctuary: Images of the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge,” photographs by Jeff Jones. Through May 29. “Human Connections: Figural Art From the Bruce Museum.” Through June 5. “Three Sisters and Corn Maidens: Native American Maize Cultivation and Customs.” Through July 3. $7; seniors and students, $6; members and children under 5, free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. The Bruce Museum, 1 Museum Drive. (203) 869-0376; brucemuseum.org.

GREENWICH Cavalier Galleries Gallery Artist: Group Show. Through March 25. Mondays through Saturdays, 10:30 a.m. to 6 p.m.; or by appointment. Cavalier Galleries, 405 Greenwich Avenue. cavaliergalleries.com; (203) 869-3664.

GREENWICH Flinn Gallery “Three Visions,” group show. Through Wednesday. Mondays through Wednesdays and Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Flinn Gallery, 101 West Putnam Avenue. (203) 622-7947; flinngallery.com.

HARTFORD Connecticut Historical Society “Needlework: Women, Art and Family, 1740 to 1840,” exhibition of early American works. Through March 26. $6; seniors, students and children, $3; members and children under 6, free. Tuesdays through Fridays, noon to 5 p.m.; Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Connecticut Historical Society, 1 Elizabeth Street. (860) 236-5621; chs.org.

HARTFORD Real Art Ways Olu Oguibe, stone wall, and “Institute of Empathy,” works by Saya Woolfalk. Through March 20. “David Wojnarowicz: A Fire in My Belly,” video censored by the National Portrait Gallery. A continuing exhibition. Suggested donation: $3; members and cinema patrons, free. Sundays and Tuesdays through Thursdays, 2 to 10 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays, 2 to 11. Real Art Ways, 56 Arbor Street. realartways.org; (860) 232-1006.

HARTFORD The Mark Twain House and Museum “American Storytellers,” artifacts and prints related to Norman Rockwell and Mark Twain. Through Sept. 6. $9 to $15; children under 6, free. Mondays and Wednesdays through Saturdays, 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5:30 p.m. The Mark Twain House and Museum, 351 Farmington Avenue. (860) 247-0998; marktwainhouse.org.

HARTFORD Wadsworth Atheneum Museum of Art “The Upholstered Woman: Women’s Fashions of the 1870s and 1880s.” Through March 20. $5 to $10; members and children 12 and under, free. Wednesdays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; first Thursdays of every month, 11 a.m. to 8 p.m. Wadsworth Atheneum Museum of Art, 600 Main Street. wadsworthatheneum.org; (860) 278-2670.

LITCHFIELD Oliver Wolcott Library “Mixed Media,” works by students from the Litchfield Montessori School. Through March 24. Oliver Wolcott Library, 160 South Street. (860) 567-8030; owlibrary.org.

NEW BRITAIN New Britain Museum of American Art “WomenArtists@

NewBritainMuseum,” group show. Through March 20. “Power Boothe: Out of Order.” Through April 10. “New/Now: Interactive Crazy Quilts,” by Carol Padberg. Through April 25. “New Media: Ted Efremoff,” installation. Through June 19. $10; seniors, $9; students, $8; members and children under 12, free. Tuesdays, Wednesdays and Fridays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 11 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. New Britain Museum of American Art, 56 Lexington Street. (860) 229-0257; nbmaa.org.

NEW CANAAN New Canaan Historical Society “Carl Schmitt 1889-1989: An Artist’s Life,” paintings. Through March 25. Free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 9:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. New Canaan Historical Society, 13 Oenoke Ridge. (203) 966-1776; nchistory.org.

NEW CANAAN Rockwell Art Gallery “Resonance,” group show. Through April 8. Mondays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Rockwell Art Gallery, 9 Burtis Ave. (203) 594-7177; rockwellartgalleries.com.

NEW CANAAN Silvermine Guild Arts Center “Caithness Lines,” works by Martin Kremer. “Urban Landscape,” works by Aleksandr Razin. “Inter-Action,” by Nancy Lasar. “Line by Line,” by Lori Glavin. Through April 1. Wednesdays through Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m.; and by appointment. Silvermine Guild Arts Center, 1037 Silvermine Road. (203) 966-9700; silvermineart.org.

NEW HAVEN City Gallery “A Twist,” an exhibition of drawing, painting and sculpture by Nancy Eisenfeld. Thursday through March 28. Free. Thursdays through Sundays, noon to 4 p.m.; or by appointment. City Gallery, 994 State Street. city-gallery.org; (203) 782-2489.

NEW HAVEN Whitney Humanities Canter “James Prosek’s Expeditionary Art: Paintings, Records and Objects From Suriname.” Through June 24. Mondays and Wednesdays, 3 to 5 p.m. and by appointment. Whitney Humanities Center, 53 Wall Street. yale.edu/whc; (203) 432-6556.

NEW HAVEN Yale Center for British Art “into the light of things: Rebecca Salter, Works 1981-2010.” Through May 1. Free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Yale Center for British Art, 1080 Chapel Street. (203) 432-2800; ycba.yale.edu.

NEW HAVEN Yale University Art Gallery “Rebecca Salter and Japan.” Through May 1. “Embodied: Black Identities in American Art.” Through June 26. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays 10 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Sundays, 1 p.m. to 6 p.m. Yale University Art Gallery, 1111 Chapel Street. (203) 432-0600; artgallery.yale.edu.

Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Lyman Allyn Art Museum, 625 Williams Street. (860) 443-2545; lymanallyn.org.

Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Florence Griswold Museum, 96 Lyme Street. (860) 434-5542; flogris.org.

Tuesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Aldrich Contemporary Art Museum, 258 Main Street. (203) 438-4519; aldrichart.org.

Mondays through Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Stamford Museum and Nature Center, 39 Scofieldtown Road. stamfordmuseum.org; (203) 322-1646.
