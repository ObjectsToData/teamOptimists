Events in Connecticut

Published: March 4, 2011
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on in Connecticut this week. Items for the guide should be sent at least three weeks in advance to ctcal@nytimes.com, or by mail to the Connecticut Calendar, Metropolitan, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405.

Comedy

FAIRFIELD Fairfield Theater Company Earl David Reed. March 12 at 7:30 p.m. $22. Fairfield Theater Company, 70 Sanford Street. (203) 259-1036; fairfieldtheatre.org.

HARTFORD Bushnell Center for the Performing Arts “Ha! Ha! Hartford!” Stand-up featuring Tom Cotter, Brian Scott McFadden and Jay Black. Friday at 8:30 p.m. $25. Bushnell Center for the Performing Arts, 166 Capitol Avenue. bushnell.org; (860) 987-5900.

MASHANTUCKET Comix at Foxwoods Jamie Kennedy. Thursday through March 12. $25 to $45. Rob Riggle. March 17 through 19. $25 to $45. Comix at Foxwoods, 240 MGM Grand Drive. (866) 646-0609; comixatfoxwoods.com.

NEW HAVEN Joker’s Wild Comedy Club “The Next Big Thing,” comedy contest hosted by Pat Oates and Ted Steier. March 6 through 13. $10. Joker’s Wild Comedy Club, 232 Wooster Street. jokerswildclub.com; (203) 773-0733.

TORRINGTON Warner Theater Bill Engvall. March 13 at 7:30 p.m. $49.50 and $100. Warner Theater, 68 Main Street. (860) 489-7180; warnertheatre.org.

WATERBURY Palace Theater of Connecticut Jeff Dunham. Friday and March 12 at 8 p.m. $52.50. Palace Theater of Connecticut, 100 East Main Street. (203) 755-4700; palacetheaterct.org.

Film

HARTFORD Real Art Ways “Saint Misbehavin’: The Wavy Gravy Movie,” documentary. Through Thursday. $4.50 to $9. “Orgasm, Inc.,” documentary by Elizabeth Canner. Friday through March 17. $4.50 to $9. “Metropolis,” directed by Fritz Lang, accompanied by the Alloy Orchestra. March 19 at 8 p.m. $20 to $30. Real Art Ways, 56 Arbor Street. realartways.org; (860) 232-1006.

NEW LONDON Lyman Allyn Art Museum “Exploring Art Film Festival,” featuring Rothko. March 12 at 2 p.m. Free. Lyman Allyn Art Museum, 625 Williams Street. (860) 443-2545; lymanallyn.org.

For Children

BRIDGEPORT Downtown Cabaret Theater “Jack and the Beanstalk,” musical comedy. Ages 3 and up. Through April 10. $18. Downtown Cabaret Theater, 263 Golden Hill Street. downtowncabaret.org; (203) 576-1636.

NEW HAVEN Peabody Museum of Natural History, Yale University “Black Holes: Space Warps and Time Twists,” interactive exhibition. All ages. Through May 1. Mondays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Peabody Museum of Natural History, Yale University, 170 Whitney Avenue. (203) 432-5050; yale.edu/peabody.

NORWALK Stepping Stones Museum for Children Puppet Making Workshop with Noel MacNeal. Ages 4 and up. March 12 at 2 p.m. Stepping Stones Museum for Children, 303 West Avenue. (203) 899-0606; steppingstonesmuseum.org.

STAMFORD Stamford Museum and Nature Center Maple Sugar Sunday. All ages. March 6, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. “Sunday Explorers,” interactive exploration. All ages. Through March 13. $5 and $10. Stamford Museum and Nature Center, 39 Scofieldtown Road. stamfordmuseum.org; (203) 322-1646.

Through March 6. $10; seniors and students, $8; children under 6 and members, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Bush-Holley Historic Site, 39 Strickland Road. (203) 869-6899; hstg.org.

FAIRFIELD Thomas J. Walsh Art Gallery, Fairfield University “To Honor My People,” works by Norman Gorbaty. Through March 27. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Thomas J. Walsh Art Gallery, Fairfield University, 1073 North Benson Road. (203) 254-4000 extension 2969; fairfield.edu.

Through April 23. “Arctic Sanctuary: Images of the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge,” photographs by Jeff Jones. Mondays and Wednesdays through Saturdays, 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5:30 p.m. (Closed Tuesdays, January to March). The Mark Twain House and Museum, 351 Farmington Avenue. (860) 247-0998; marktwainhouse.org.

Wednesdays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; first Thursdays of every month, 11 a.m. to 8 p.m. Wadsworth Atheneum Museum of Art, 600 Main Street. wadsworthatheneum.org; (860) 278-2670.

KENT Ober Gallery “Family Photo Album,” works by Vladimir Kuprianov. Through March 15. Fridays and Saturdays, noon to 5 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Ober Gallery, 14 Old Barn Road. (860) 927-5030; obergallery.com.

(860) 229-0257; nbmaa.org.
