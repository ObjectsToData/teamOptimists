Events in Connecticut

Published: February 25, 2011
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on in Connecticut this week. Items for the guide should be sent at least three weeks in advance to ctcal@nytimes.com, or by mail to the Connecticut Calendar, Metropolitan, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405.

Comedy

MASHANTUCKET Comix at Foxwoods Dom Irrera. Thursday through March 5. $25 to $45. Comix at Foxwoods, 240 MGM Grand Drive. comixatfoxwoods.com; (866) 646-0609.

NEW HAVEN Joker’s Wild Comedy Club “The Next Big Thing,” comedy contest hosted by Pat Oates and Ted Steier. March 6 through 13. $10. Joker’s Wild Comedy Club, 232 Wooster Street. jokerswildclub.com; (203) 773-0733.

Film

HARTFORD Cinestudio “Another Year,” by Mike Leigh. Wednesday through March 5. $7 and $8. “The Illusionist,” directed by Sylvain Chomet. March 6 through 12. Cinestudio, 300 Summit Street. (860) 297-2463; cinestudio.org.

HARTFORD Real Art Ways “Saint Misbehavin’: The Wavy Gravy Movie,” documentary. Friday through March 10. $4.50 to $9. Real Art Ways, 56 Arbor Street. (860) 232-1006; realartways.org.

NEW LONDON Lyman Allyn Art Museum “Exploring Art Film Festival,” featuring Rothko. March 12 at 2 p.m. Free. Lyman Allyn Art Museum, 625 Williams Street. (860) 443-2545; lymanallyn.org.

For Children

HARTFORD Bushnell Center for the Performing Arts “Knuffle Bunny: A Cautionary Musical,” presented by the Kennedy Center. Ages 3 and up. Monday at 10 a.m. and noon. $12. Bushnell Center for the Performing Arts, 166 Capitol Avenue. (860) 987-5900; bushnell.org.

NEW HAVEN Peabody Museum of Natural History, Yale University “Black Holes: Space Warps and Time Twists,” interactive exhibition. All ages. Through May 1. Mondays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Peabody Museum of Natural History, Yale University, 170 Whitney Avenue. (203) 432-5050; yale.edu/peabody.

NORWALK Stepping Stones Museum for Children “Creative Kids: Ireland.” Be immersed in the rich sound of bagpipes and construct a replica. March 5 at 2 p.m. Puppet Making Workshop with Noel MacNeal. March 12 at 2 p.m. Ages 4 and up. Stepping Stones Museum for Children, 303 West Avenue. steppingstonesmuseum.org; (203) 899-0606.

STAMFORD Stamford Museum and Nature Center “Sunday Explorers,” interactive exploration. Feb. 27 through March 13. Maple Sugar Sunday. All ages. March 6, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. $5 and $10. Stamford Museum and Nature Center, 39 Scofieldtown Road. stamfordmuseum.org; (203) 322-1646.

TORRINGTON Warner Theater “The Emperor’s New Clothes,” musical based on the story by Hans Christian Andersen. Ages 3 and up. Feb. 27 at 2 p.m. $12.50. Warner Theater, 68 Main Street. (860) 489-7180; warnertheatre.org.

Museums and Galleries

CHESTER eo art lab “Imaginary Realms,” photographs by George Fellner. Through Feb. 27. Free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 6 p.m.; or by appointment. eo art lab, 69 Main Street. (860) 526-4833; eoartlab.com.

COS COB Bush-Holley Historic Site “From This Day Forward: Looking Back at Greenwich Weddings,” costumes, artifacts and photographs. Through March 6. $10; seniors and students, $8; children under 6 and members, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Bush-Holley Historic Site, 39 Strickland Road. (203) 869-6899; hstg.org.

FAIRFIELD Thomas J. Walsh Art Gallery, Fairfield University “To Honor My People,” works by Norman Gorbaty. Through March 27. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Thomas J. Walsh Art Gallery, Fairfield University, 1073 North Benson Road. (203) 254-4000 extension 2969; fairfield.edu.

GREENWICH Bruce Museum “Kashmir Shawls From the Bruce Museum Collection.” Through Monday. “Cindy Sherman: Works From Friends of the Bruce Museum,” photographs from local collections. Through April 23. “Human Connections: Figural Art From the Bruce Museum.” Through June 5. “Three Sisters and Corn Maidens: Native American Maize Cultivation and Customs.” Through July 3. $7; seniors and students, $6; members and children under 5, free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. The Bruce Museum, 1 Museum Drive. (203) 869-0376; brucemuseum.org.

GREENWICH Cavalier Galleries “Nicholas Berger: On the Waterfront.” Through Friday. Mondays through Saturdays, 10:30 a.m. to 6 p.m.; or by appointment. Cavalier Galleries, 405 Greenwich Avenue. cavaliergalleries.com; (203) 869-3664.

GREENWICH Flinn Gallery “Three Visions,” group show. Through March 16. Mondays through Wednesdays and Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Flinn Gallery, 101 West Putnam Avenue. (203) 622-7947; flinngallery.com.

HARTFORD Connecticut Historical Society “Needlework: Women, Art and Family, 1740 to 1840,” exhibition of early American works. Through March 26. $6; seniors, students and children, $3; members and children under 6, free. Tuesdays through Fridays, noon to 5 p.m.; Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Connecticut Historical Society, 1 Elizabeth Street. (860) 236-5621; chs.org.

HARTFORD Real Art Ways Olu Oguibe, stone wall, and “Institute of Empathy,” works by Saya Woolfalk. Through March 20. “David Wojnarowicz: A Fire in My Belly,” video censored by the National Portrait Gallery. A continuing exhibition. Suggested donation: $3; members and cinema patrons, free. Sundays and Tuesdays through Thursdays, 2 to 10 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays, 2 to 11. Real Art Ways, 56 Arbor Street. realartways.org; (860) 232-1006.

HARTFORD Wadsworth Atheneum Museum of Art “Evolution: Five Decades of Printmaking by David Driskell.” Through March 6. “The Upholstered Woman: Women’s Fashions of the 1870s and 1880s.” Through March 20. $5 to $10; members and children 12 and under, free. Wednesdays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; first Thursdays of every month, 11 a.m. to 8 p.m. Wadsworth Atheneum Museum of Art, 600 Main Street. wadsworthatheneum.org; (860) 278-2670.

KENT Ober Gallery “Family Photo Album,” works by Vladimir Kuprianov. Through March 15. Fridays and Saturdays, noon to 5 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Ober Gallery, 14 Old Barn Road. (860) 927-5030; obergallery.com.

MIDDLETOWN Ezra and Cecile Zilkha Gallery, Wesleyan University “Double Vision,” works by the sculptor and installation artist Jeffrey Schiff. Through Feb. 27. Free. Tuesdays through Sundays, noon to 4 p.m.; Fridays, noon to 8 p.m. Ezra and Cecile Zilkha Gallery, Wesleyan University, 283 Washington Terrace. (860) 685-3355; wesleyan.edu/cfa/events.html.

MYSTIC Mystic Seaport “Tugs!” An interactive exhibition about the past, present and future of the American tug, tow and barge industry. Through Monday. $15 to $24; children 5 and under, free. Daily, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Mystic Seaport, 75 Greenmanville Avenue. (888) 973-2767; mysticseaport.org.

NEW BRITAIN New Britain Museum of American Art “WomenArtists@ NewBritainMuseum,” group show. Through March 20. “Power Boothe: Out of Order.” Through April 10. “New/Now: Interactive Crazy Quilts,” by Carol Padberg. Through April 25. $10; seniors, $9; students, $8; members and children under 12, free. Tuesdays, Wednesdays and Fridays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 11 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. New Britain Museum of American Art, 56 Lexington Street. (860) 229-0257; nbmaa.org.

NEW CANAAN New Canaan Historical Society “Carl Schmitt 1889-1989: An Artist’s Life,” paintings. Through March 25. Free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 9:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. New Canaan Historical Society, 13 Oenoke Ridge. (203) 966-1776; nchistory.org.

NEW CANAAN Rockwell Art Gallery “Resonance,” group show. Through April 8. Mondays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Rockwell Art Gallery, 9 Burtis Ave. (203) 594-7177; rockwellartgalleries.com.

Feb. 27 through April 1. Wednesdays through Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m.; and by appointment. Silvermine Arts Center, 1037 Silvermine Road. (203) 966-9700; silvermineart.org.

NEW HAVEN City Gallery “Nests, Drawings and Dioramas,” works by Paulette Rosen. Through Feb. 27. “A Twist,” an exhibition of drawing, painting and sculpture by Nancy Eisenfeld. Thursday through March 28. Free. Thursdays through Sundays, noon to 4 p.m.; or by appointment. City Gallery, 994 State Street. city-gallery.org; (203) 782-2489.

NEW HAVEN New Haven Museum “Both Here and There: Yale-China and a Century of Transformative Encounters,” group show. Through Monday. $4; seniors, $3; students, $2; under 12, free. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Saturdays, noon to 5 p.m.; first Sunday of the month, 1 to 4 p.m. New Haven Museum, 114 Whitney Avenue. newhavenmuseum.org; (203) 562-4183.

NEW HAVEN Whitney Humanities Canter “James Prosek’s Expeditionary Art: Paintings, Records and Objects From Suriname.” Monday through June 24. Mondays and Wednesdays, 3 to 5 p.m. and by appointment. Whitney Humanities Canter, 53 Wall Street. (203) 432-6556; yale.edu/whc.

NEW HAVEN Yale Center for British Art “into the light of things: Rebecca Salter, works 1981-2010.” Through May 1. Free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Yale Center for British Art, 1080 Chapel Street. (203) 432-2800; ycba.yale.edu.

NEW HAVEN Yale University Art Gallery “Rebecca Salter and Japan.” Through May 1. “Embodied: Black Identities in American Art.” Through June 26. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays 10 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Sundays, 1 p.m. to 6 p.m. Yale University Art Gallery, 1111 Chapel Street. (203) 432-0600; artgallery.yale.edu.

Thursdays and Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, 1 to 4:30 p.m. William Benton Museum of Art, 245 Glenbrook Road. (860) 486-4520; benton.uconn.edu.

WESTPORT Nylen Gallery “Happy Chinese New Year,” paintings and monoprints by Gayle Gleckler and Tony Woolner. Through March 12. Mondays through Fridays, 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.; Saturdays, 9:30 a.m. to 5 p.m. Nylen Gallery, 606 Post Road East. (203) 227-6861; picturethisofwestport.com.

WESTPORT Westport Public Library “Cuba 2010, an Impression,” photographs by David Kalman. Through March 31. Free. Mondays through Thursdays, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Fridays, 9 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Westport Public Library, 20 Jesup Road. westportlibrary.org; (203) 291-4818.
