NEWS SUMMARY: MONDAY, AUGUST 31, 1987


Published: August 31, 1987

International A2-6, A8-9

Black miners ended their strike in South Africa after three weeks. It had been the country's largest and costliest strike, but the gold and coal miners failed to win improvement over a pre-strike wage increase.

Page A1

Iraq, stepping up renewed attacks in the Persian Gulf, bombed Iranian offshore oil sites for a second day, communiques said. The statements also said Iraqi warplanes hit two more ''large naval targets.'' A1

Israel will not build a new fighter following a vote of the Israeli Cabinet. The jet, known as the Lavi, was meant to serve as the main advanced combat aircraft in the 1990's for Israel. A1

The nation's intelligence agencies and officials have been quarreling for most of President Reagan's tenure over whether intelligence analyses prepared for him have been distorted to support Administration policies. A1 A world record in a 100-meter race, 9.83 seconds, was set by Ben Johnson of Canada in Rome. But this was no ordinary record that 64,500 people in the Stadio Olimpico were seeing with disbelieving eyes. This was a race for the ages. A1

News analysis: Many Filipinos are calling for stronger leadership, which they complain President Corazon C. Aquino is not providing 18 months after ousting a man many called a dictator. A3

President Aquino thanked the armed forces for defending her in a mutiny last week and all but dared renegade officers to try again, in a speech that kept the strong tone she took in an address at the height of the insurrection. A3

France's strained relations with Australia and New Zealand have been soured further by a dispute over the clubbing of independence demonstrators in New Caledonia, a French colony in the Pacific. A5 Foreign investment in vineyards in France is growing. At least some of the dozens of the foreign investors admit that they want the glamour of owning a real French vineyard as much as the opportunity to make money. D1

National A12-17, B4

A new booster rocket was tested. The tryout for the redesigned rocket, whose leaking seal triggered the Challenger disaster, marked a milestone for getting the space shuttle flying again, officials said. A1

A man in Boston opened fire with an automatic weapon in the neighborhood of Dorchester, killing four relatives and another woman and critically wounding two other relatives before taking his own life, the authorities said. A15 Feelings of racial inferiority may be as strong among young black children now as they were more than 40 years ago, a study shows. But the findings suggest that black children's self-esteem can be improved with proper intervention. A13

AIDS is the enemy, not three young brothers suffering from the disease, say the people in Florida who wanted the youngsters kept out of school. But the family is leaving town after their home burned. A1

Washington journal: A police effort to fight illegal drugs is perhaps the most concerted one under way in any city in the country. Over the past year, the effort has resulted in 23,000 arrests. A12

Joseph Biden's Presidential bid is seeing frustrating days. Since Mr. Biden began his campaign, he has seen the race increasingly dominated by his cooler rivals. A12

After a series of rapes of elderly women over the last four years in Homestead, Pa., the police in the depressed steelmaking community outside Pittsburgh have resorted to voluntary fingerprinting of black male residents. A12 In the trial of Jackie Presser, the teamsters' union president, and two co-defendants, a key government witness has recanted assertions that he was a phantom employee at Presser's Local 507 in Cleveland. A debate over ocean dumping of waste is being renewed with greater vigor by environmentalists and New York and New Jersey state officials in this summer of frequent beach closings and dolphin deaths. A1 Decent homes for older people in Churubusco, N.Y., a village in the northern foothills of the Adirondacks are being opened in what officials hope will help stem the problem of homeless elderly people. B1

A subway token clerk's death was taken advantage of by a thief sometime around midnight Saturday. After the clerk had a fatal heart attack, the thief reached over his body and stole more than $1,000 in cash, the police said. B1

Two men from India were injured when they tried to escape from immigration authorities holding them at Kennedy International Airport. The men fell from a hotel window while trying to climb down on a makeshift rope. B3

The death of a recording star in a shooting has shaken youths in New York City who are familiar with his rap music. Some say the death of Scott (La Rock) Sterling illustrates how violence and the hip-hop music scene seem to come together. B1 The motive for a brutal beating of a young woman has left the police baffled. Investigators are trying to determine what prompted the beating, described by some law-enforcement officers as one of the most savage they had seen. B3

Health concerns at the Meadowlands sports complex were mostly dismissed by fans and employees at Giants Stadium for the Jets-Giants preseason game. B2

A fisherman's haven may be doomed. The proposed Sheepshead Bay development project will clear out fishing shops to make way for condominiums. ''It'll probably push out all the regular people,'' one fisherman said.
