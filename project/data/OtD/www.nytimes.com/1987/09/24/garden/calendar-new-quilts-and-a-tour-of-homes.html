Calendar: New Quilts And a Tour Of Homes


Published: September 24, 1987

New Quilts Gayle Willson Gallery, 16 Jobs Lane, Southampton, L.I.; 516-283-7430.

Contemporary quilts and quilted garments, some made of unusual materials, will be on display through November 22 at the gallery.

Hours: 11 A.M to 5 P.M., Thursday through Monday; noon to 5 P.M., Sunday. Free. FRENCH DESIGNS International Design Center, Center One, 30-30 Thompson Avenue, Long Island City; 718-937-7474.

An exhibition by the designer and architect Ronald Cecil Sportes will includes furniture, video displays, Moroccan carpets and a nine-foot-high glass etching. The show will be on display through November 13.

Hours: 9 A.M. to 5 P.M., Monday through Friday. Free shuttle transportation is available from the Departure Lounge, 919 Third Avenue, at 56th Street, every 15 minutes. Free. CLINTON HILL TOUR Pratt Institute, Dekalb Avenue at Hall Street, Clinton Hill, Brooklyn; 718-636-3517.

The Society for Clinton Hill is sponsoring an exhibit of works by Pratt faculty members and alumni. The show, which opens today and runs through next Wednesday, features paintings drawings, sculpture, and photography. Hours: 10 A.M. to 5 P.M. Free.

The society is also offering a tour of Clinton Hill houses on Sunday, which will include Victorian mansions and converted apartments in the Grand Hotel. Meet at the mansion at 324 Clinton Avenue at noon; the tour lasts until 5 P.M. Tickets: $8. Information for both events: 718-789-0958. FAMILY WORKSHOPS Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street, at Central Avenue, Newark; 201-596-6550.

Children and their parents are invited to free 20-minute workshops with themes tied to the museum's collections. The workshops begin this Saturday and continue on Saturday and Sundays through December. In September and October families will be able to make Tibetan aprons in connection with an exhibition on tibetan sculpture and painting.

The workshops will run continuously from 12:30 to 3:30 P.M. Museum hours: 12 to 5 P.M., Tuesday through Sunday. Museum admission is free. ON THE WATERFRONT St. George Ferry Terminal, Staten Island; 718-447-6490.

A four-hour cruise of the Staten Island and New Jersey waterfronts from the John A. Noble ferry will be held this Saturday. The cruise will feature a narrated tour of the waterfront and a film about the noted American lithographer for whom the ferry is named. Dinner and dancing will be included.

Tickets: $50.00. The ferry will depart at 6:00 P.M. JAPANESE POTTERY Japan Society, 333 E. 47th Street; 212-472-9089.

This weekend the Japan Hands cultural organization will offer pottery program entitled ''Historic Japanese Kilns and Contemporary Potters.'' Saturday's seminar will include a presentation by Louise Cort of the Freer Gallery of Art. Admission: $40. Hours: 9:30 A.M. to 4 P.M. On Sunday, participants can take an all-day field trip to a Japanese kiln in either New York or Massachusetts. Departure is at 9:15 A.M. from Inter-Continental Hotel, 111 E. 48th St. Admission: $40. RUG SALE Armenian Church of the Holy Martyrs, 209-15 Horace Harding Expressway, Bayside, Queens; 718-225-0235.

The sale, to benefit the church, will feature handmade oriental rugs from China, Pakistan, India and Persia. Hours: Saturday, 7:30 P.M.; Sunday, 1 to 7 P.M. Free. WOMEN OF INFLUENCE Brooklyn College Student Center, Campus Road and East 27th Street, Brooklyn; 718-780-5847.

The Humanities Institute of Brooklyn College of the City University of New York is sponsoring two free lectures. ''Catherine de Medici and the Birth of Ballet,'' will held on Monday, 12:15 to 2 P.M. ''The Feminine Eye: Women and Photography,'' will be held on Wednesday at the same time. ANTIQUES SHOW Seventh Regiment Armory, Park Avenue at 67th Street; 212-772-2240.

The 10th annual five-day show opens this Wednesday, with 110 exhibitors from the United States, Canada, Austria, France and England.

Hours: 4 to 9 P.M., Wednesday; noon to 9 P.M., Thursday to Saturday; noon to 6 P.M., Sunday. Admission: $6.
