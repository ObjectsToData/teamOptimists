NEW JERSEY GUIDE


Published: September 27, 1987

OKTOBERFEST TIME Bell ringers, yodelers, ''oompah'' music and German beer will mark the return of Oktoberfest to Waterloo Village in Stanhope next weekend. The oompah will be provided by Werner Heckmann's Royal Bavarians. Although German beer and wine will flow, domestic varieties also will be offered, along with German foods. There will be continuious music and entertainment under the big tent and throughout the village from noon to 6 P.M. both Saturday and Sunday. Tickets are $7.50 for adults ($5 for the elderly and $3 for children 6 to 12; children under 6 free). To reach Waterloo Village, take I-80 to Exit 25 and then follow the brown and white Waterloo Village signs. Information: (201) 347-0900.

EBAN TO SPEAK IN WAYNE

Abba Eban, the Israeli politician, diplomat, author and historian, will open William Paterson's College's Distinguished Lecturer Series Tuesday evening, speaking at 8 P.M. in the campus's Shea Center on ''Prospects for Peace in the Middle East.'' A member of the Israeli Government for almost 30 years, Mr. Eban, 72 years old, is now chairman of the Knesset (parliament) Foreign Affairs and Defense Committee. From 1950 to 1959, he was Israel's Ambassador to the United States and representative at the United Nations. Tickets to the lecture are $10 ($8 for the elderly and students). Information: (201) 595-2971.

BOHEMIAN SINGING COWBOY

Ramblin' Jack Elliott will perform at the Turning Point in Piermont, N.Y., next Sunday at 5:30 and 8 P.M. The Brooklyn-born Mr. Elliott is described as the original Bohemian singing cowboy, a vanishing breed. He was a close companion of Woody Guthrie and a major influence on Bob Dylan and Kris Kristofferson. Mick Jagger claims Mr. Elliott as one of his earliest inspirations. Tickets are $8 and there is no minimum. Reservations are being accepted. Information: (914) 359-1089.

'DRACULA' IS IN UNION

The National Players Touring Company will present ''Dracula'' at 8 P.M. Thursday at Kean College in Union. The Players is a 14-member professional touring troupe in its 39th season. ''Dracula'' is part of this year's cultural art series at the college, which will include the National Players' production of ''Othello'' on April 7. The college's Wilkins Theater is the setting for both plays. Admission is $8.50. Information: (201) 727-2337.

FUNGUS FOR FUN

The New Jersy Mycological Assocation will hold its Fungus Fest '87 next Sunday at the Somerset County Environmental Education Center in Basking Ridge. The association and its fest are dedicated to educating the public on how to cook mushrooms and in recognizing the wild ones now in season. Those attending may bring mushrooms in for identification, and there will be a slide lecture on fungal flora, a talk on the ''foolproof four'' edible mushrooms and several guided mushroom trail walks. Books, stamps, paintings, drawings and crafts will be for sale. Fungus Fest will be held from 11 A.M. to 5 P.M. at the center on Lord Stirling Road. The suggested donation is $1.50 (75 cents for those under 16). Information: (201) 647-5740 after 4 P.M.

DOWN-TO-EARTH DOGS

A dog show at which a blue ribbon can be won for the broadest grin or the best vocalizing will be held at the Bergen County Animal Shelter next Sunday. Prizes also will be awarded for the ''Most Mixed-up Mix,'' ''Longest Dog, Nose to Tail,'' ''Saddest Expression,'' ''Most Unusual Markings,'' ''Best Trick'' and ''Dog Owner Look-alike.'' Friends of the County Animal Shelter is sponsoring the show, which will have no losers. All entries not winning top prizes will receive honorable-mention ribbons. Dog owners of any age may enter their pets in any category, but all animals must be leashed and licensed. Registration will begin at 1 P.M., with judging starting at 1:30 at the shelter, 100 United Lane, Teterboro. The entry fee is $1. There will be tours of the shelter after the show. Refreshments, crafts and other items will be offered for sale. Information: (201) 944-1847.

ALTERNATIVE CAREERS

A series of six talks on alternative careers gets under way tomorrow at Rutgers University in New Brunswick, with Rear Adm. Grace Murray Hopper (ret.), a pioneer in computer software, as the first speaker. Admiral Hopper, 80 years old, retired last year as the Navy's oldest rear admiral on active duty. She will speak on ''Hardware, Software and People: Change is a Constant,'' focusing on nontraditional career paths and preparing for career shifts. She has been involved in both the military and civilian sides of the computer field since its beginning. Assigned to a major computer project at Harvard University in the mid-1940's, she learned to program the first large-scale digital computer, the Mark I, and subsequently worked on its two successors. She later worked for the corporation that built the first commercial large-scale electronic computer, the Univac I. The admiral is a senior consultant for the Digital Equipment Corporation. Other lectures in the series will feature women prominent in business, politics and academic administration. All the talks will begin at 7:30 P.M. in Brower Commons on College Avenue. Admission is $5 a session or $25 for the six programs. Wine, cheese and other refreshments are included. Information: (201) 932-8399.

LITERARY SEMINAR

The Burlington County Cultural and Heritage Commission in Mount Holly is sponsoring a literary seminar that will include poetry, short story and novel workshops. Writers must be 18 years or older and submit a sample of their work to the commission by 4 P.M. Wednesday. Geraldine Little, Bill Higginson, Penny Harter and Lester Goldberg, all published writers, will conduct the workshops, which will begin at 9 A.M. Oct. 17 at Smithville Mansion. Information: (609) 265-5958.

DISCUSSING BOOKS

''Americans in the 30's,'' a reading and book-discussion group, will take place Wednesday at the Belleville Public Library. Dr. Jane Brown, professor of English at Trenton State College, will discuss ''For Whom the Bell Tolls,'' the 1940 Ernest Hemingway novel that came out of his experiences as a correspondent on the Loyalist side during the Spanish Civil War. Future sessions: Oct. 14 (''Their Eyes Were Watching God,'' by Zora Neale Hurston), Oct. 28 (''The Grapes of Wrath, by John Steinbeck'') and Nov. 18 (''The Best of Simple,'' by Langston Hughes). Free texts will be made available to the group, which is financed by the New Jersey Committee for the Humanities. Discussions begin at 1 P.M. and are free. The library is at 221 Washington Avenue. Information: Adrea Cohen, (201) 450-3434.

HARVEST CELEBRATION

A Harvest Festival celebrating the folkways of Colonial America will take place from 1 to 5 P.M. today at the Trailside Nature and Science Center in Mountainside. The Jersey Blues (Third New Jersey Regiment) will demonstrate 18th-century military camp life, Ars Musica Antiqua will present a concert using such old-time instruments as the krummhorn and dulcimer and the Old-Time Lakota Dancers will perform. The program also includes demonstrations, among other things, of cider-making and spinning, as well as participatory activities like corn-grinding and rug-braiding. Foods on sale will include cider, doughnuts and baked potatoes. The center is at Coles Avenue and New Providence Road. Information: (201) 232-5930.
