OUTINGS


Published: November 10, 1991

Here is a sampling of outings in the New York metropolitan region during the holiday season. Dates and times are subject to change. Today

HITCHHIKERS, PARACHUTES AND WINGS, National Audubon Society, 613 Riversville Road, Greenwich, Conn. A natural history walk focusing on seeds and how they travel. 2 P.M. Fee: $2 ($1 for children). Information: (203) 869-5272.

Nov. 16

WEST POINT FOOTBALL, Michie Stadium, West Point, N.Y. Army versus University of Akron. 1 P.M. Tickets: $17. Information: (914) 446-4996.

AUTUMN IN HECKSCHER, Heckscher State Park, at the end of the Southern State Parkway, East Islip, L.I. A 10-mile hike through the countryside, past pine groves, swamps, forests and beach area showing last signs of fall foliage. Sponsored by the Appalachian Mountain Club. Meets at 10:54 A.M. at Great River, L.I., railroad station. Fee: $2. Information: (212) 986-1430.

LIBERTY STATE PARK HIKE, through Palisades south along the Hudson River. Meets at the George Washington Bridge bus terminal at 8:30 A.M.; sponsored by the Appalachian Mountain Club. Fee: $2. Information: (212) 986-1430. Nov. 23

THANKSGIVING PREPARATION, Historic Allaire Village, Exit 18 of the Garden State Parkway, Allaire, N.J. A demonstration of food preparation from the 19th century. 1 to 4 P.M. Free. Information: (908) 938-2253.

THANKSGIVING AT BRICK HOUSE, Route 17K, Montgomery, N.Y. A tour of the seasonally decorated 1769 Georgian House owned by the Hill family for seven generations. 11:30 A.M. to 4:30 P.M. Also Nov. 24, same time. Admission: $2.50 ($1.50 for children). Information: (914) 457-5951. Nov. 24

CONCERT BY BOYS CHOIR OF HARLEM, Eisenhower Hall Theater, West Point, N.Y. A singing performance of classical, jazz and gospel music. 3 P.M. Tickets: $18 to $25. Information: (914) 938-4159. Nov. 29

THANKSGIVING CELEBRATION, at Sunnyside, West Sunnyside Road, off Route 9, Tarrytown, N.Y. A festival featuring mid-19th-century harvest and holiday food preparation. 10 A.M. to 5 P.M. Also Nov. 30, same time. Admission: $6 ($3 for students). Other activities include "Season's Greetings From the White House," an exhibition of Presidential Christmas cards from 1961 to 1990. (Dec. 31, 10 A.M. to 5 P.M.; admission: $3), and "Candlelight Tours," an English-style celebration of the holidays with caroling by bonfire, hot cider and cookies and a tour of the house, which will be decorated for the holidays (Dec. 13 to 15 and 20 to 21, 5 to 9 P.M.; admission: $7.50 ($5 for those 6 to 17 years old). Information and reservations: (914) 631-8200.

HOLIDAY DECORATING, Museum Village, off Route 17, Monroe, N.Y. Visitors can join in decorating the museum in traditional 19th-century style, by hanging holiday decorations throughout the buildings, putting up Christmas trees and constructing Victorian centerpieces. Noon to 5 P.M. Also Nov. 30 and Dec. 1, same time. Other holiday events include "Christmas in the Village," which will feature appearances by Santa, seasonal music concerts and candle-making workshops (Dec. 7 and 8, 3 to 7 P.M.). Museum admission: $5 ($4 for the elderly, $3 for those 6 to 15 years old, free for those under 3). Information: (914) 782-8247. Nov. 30

SANTA TROLLEY, Shore Line Trolley Museum, 17 River Street, East Haven, Conn. A Christmas celebration at Connecticut's largest mobile Lionel train layout, with holiday music, visits from Santa and refreshments. 11 A.M. to 5 P.M. Daily through Dec. 22. Admission: $5 ($4 for the elderly, $2 for those 2 to 11 years old). Information: (203) 467-6927. Dec. 1

WINTER PROGRAM, Greig Farm, Pitcher Lane, Red Hook, N.Y. Winter festival with such activities as cross-country skiing and ice-skating. 9 A.M. to sunset. The program continues through March 1, Wednesday through Sunday, 9 A.M. to sunset. Daily ski rental, $15 ($10 for children; hourly rental of ice skates at a price to be determined). Fee: $5 (free for those under 12). Information: (914) 758-1234. Dec. 4

SANTA CLAUS, at the New Hope and Ivyland Railroad, West Bridge Street, New Hope, Pa. A 9-mile, 50-minute, narrated, round-trip ride between New Hope and Lahaska, Pa., in vintage 1920's Reading Railroad passenger coaches, pulled by a coal-fired steam locomotive. During the ride, St. Nicholas will make an appearance. Through Dec. 22. Train trips: Wednesday, 10 A.M. to 4 P.M.; Friday and Saturday from 10 A.M. to 6:30 P.M., and Sunday noon to 4 P.M. Fee: $8.50 ($5.50 for those 3 to 11 years old). Information and reservations: (215) 862-2332.

YULETIDE TOURS, Mystic Seaport, 50 Greenmanville Avenue, Route 27, Mystic, Conn. Hourlong walking tours with costumed guides to selected tall ships and village exhibits displaying 19th-century Christmas decorations. Participants can enjoy live music and cookies along the way. Dec. 4 to Dec. 21. Tours leave Wednesday through Sunday, every 15 minutes from 9:30 A.M. to 2:30 P.M. Admission: $14 ($8.75 for those 6 to 15 years old). Also available, Lantern Light Tours, one-hour guided tours conducted by costumed guides daily (except Tuesday) 5 to 9 P.M. from Dec. 6 through Dec. 22. Admission: $16 ($8.50 for those 6 to 15 years old). Information and reservations: (203) 572-0711. Dec. 6
