Surgeon and Wife Pledge $25 Million to Yale


Published: October 4, 1991

A San Francisco surgeon and his wife have pledged to donate their entire estate, valued at $25 million, to Yale University.

Most of it will go to the School of Medicine, the largest gift in its history, Yale's President, Benno C. Schmidt Jr., said today in announcing the donation from Dr. John Anlyan and his wife, Betty.

"This is a thank-you gesture for making my life possible," Dr. Anlyan, who is 70 years old, said in a statement released today by the university. "My wife and I feel that Yale has done very well for us. I came here on a wing and a prayer on August 29, 1939, just days before Hitler marched into Poland, and I could not have made it without Yale's help."

"We feel that all alumni should voluntarily give as much as they can," he said. "The best thing we can do for the youth of the next generation is to educate them. After that they can take care of themselves."

The exact distribution of the money will be determined by Mr. Schmidt, the medical school dean and members of the Yale Corporation when the Anlyan's estate is settled after their death. Mr. Schmidt said that about $2 million will be used to endow a professorship in the humanities, the chief academic interest of Mrs. Anlyan, 67.

Dr. Anlyan was born and reared in Alexandria, Egypt, and admitted to Yale in 1939. He completed both his undergraduate and medical studies in six years, graduating from Yale College in 1942 and from the medical school in 1945. He served as an instructor in surgery at Yale from 1949 to 1951.

The Anlyans have been longtime donors to the Yale School of Medicine and the Yale Alumni Fund, according to the university's Office of Public Affairs. In 1986 they donated $1 million to the medical school for renovation of its molecular neurobiology laboratories.

Dr. Robert M. Donaldson Jr., acting dean of the medical school, said of the pledge: "This gift is particularly significant because it is unrestricted and can be used for whatever is the major need at the time. Life in the world of medicine changes so fast that it would be unrealistic for me to say it will be appointed to this effort or that."
