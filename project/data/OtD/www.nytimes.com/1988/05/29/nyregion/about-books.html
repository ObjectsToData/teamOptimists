ABOUT BOOKS

By Shirley Horner
Published: May 29, 1988

SIGNIFICANT literary or literary-related events will take place around the state in June.

Next Saturday, ''Her Infinite Variety: A Symposium on Women,'' a conference sponsored by the Fairleigh Dickinson University Alumni Association, will be held at the university's College of Dental Medicine, 140 University Plaza Drive, Hackensack.

Among the scheduled speakers are Susan Dworkin, author of ''Miss America: Bess Myerson's Own Story''; Elaine Denholtz, author of ''Playing for High Stakes: The Men, Money and Power of Corporate Wives,'' and Harry Damato, author of ''How to Collect and Restore Country Antique Furniture.''

Also scheduled is a panel discussion on ''The Cultural Explosion in New Jersey.''

The panelists include Ellen Barry, producing director of the New Jersey Shakespeare Festival at Drew University; Julie Ann Fleming, the New Jersey Symphony's director of development, and Leah P. Sloshberg, director of the State Museum in Trenton.

The moderator will be Walter T. Savage, professor of English at Fairleigh Dickinson.

Kathleen C. Gogick, president and publisher of Corporate magazine, will speak on ''For Women: The World of Publishing.''

The event, from 8:30 A.M. to 4:45 P.M., costs $25.

Information: (201) 692-2654.

Tom Stoppard, the English playwright, will lecture on his work June 14 at Seton Hall University's Theater-in-the-Round on the South Orange campus, 400 South Orange Avenue.

Mr. Stoppard, who wrote the screenplay for Stephen Spielberg's ''Empire of the Sun,'' first dazzled the theater world in 1967 with ''Rosencrantz and Guildenstern Are Dead,'' an erudite comedy about the two attendant lords in ''Hamlet.''

Tickets for the program, which begins at 8 P.M., are $5.

Information: (201) 761-9388 or (201) 678-1680.

Also on June 14, an all-day conference, ''Political Commitment in International Writing,'' will take place at the Florham-Madison campus of Fairleigh Dickinson University, 285 Madison Avenue (Route 24), Madison.

For 30 years, The Literary Review, the conference's sponsor, has been publishing English translations of poetry and fiction from around the world. The Review is based at the university.

The dilemma of the writer - must one choose between political commitment or nothing? - will be one topic.

Carolyn Forche, a poet and translator, will be the main speaker. Her work is noted for its focus on the people and politics of Central America, the third world and the Eastern bloc.

Other speakers will include Clark Blaise, co-author with his wife, Bharati Mukherjee, of ''The Sorrow and the Terror,'' the study of an Air India disaster in Canada, and Eric Sellin, director of the Center for the Study of the Francophone Literatures of North Africa.

The conference, from 9:30 A.M. to 5:30 P.M., costs $50 (including a luncheon and reception).

Information: (201) 593-8564.

In its grants for 1988-89, the National Endowment for the Humanities will give preference to proposals that carry out the theme ''New Jersey: A Past to Cherish, A Future to Choose.''

On June 14, the committee's annual dinner will be held at the Newark Public Library, 5 Washington Street, at 6:30 P.M.

The main speaker will be A. J. R. Russell-Wood, a member of the history department at Johns Hopkins University and chairman of the Conference on Latin American History's Columbus Quincentenary Committee.

This year's recipient of the Humanities Committee's annual John Witherspoon Award in the Humanities is Dr. Robert Hollander, a professor of European literature at Princeton University, a Dante scholar and a former member and chairman of the Committee for the Humanities.

Witherspoon, a theologian-philosopher, was president of the College of New Jersey, now Princeton, from 1768 to 1794. The dinner fee is $25. Information: (201) 932-7120.

The second annual New Jersey Literary Hall of Fame awards banquet will be held at 6:30 P.M. June 15 at the New Jersey Institute of Technology, 323 Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. Boulevard, Newark.

Thomas Fleming, this year's main speaker and a member of the hall, is the author of ''Time and Tide,'' a best-selling novel of Navy action in the Pacific during World War II.

In the 1960's, Mr. Fleming wrote a series of novels about Irish-American politics that somewhat resembled those of his birthplace, Jersey City.

Also scheduled is the unveiling of the Literary Wall of Fame, an exhibit of portraits of New Jersey literary figures drawn by Bil Canfield of Tinton Falls, editorial cartoonist for The Star-Ledger of Newark.

Mr. Canfield and Louis and Francene Sabin of Milltown, who have been the co-authors of more than 200 nonfiction books for juveniles, will be the evening's newly inducted members.

Since 1987, inductees have been receiving ''Michaels,'' the hall's counterpart to Oscar, Tony and Emmy. The Michael, financed by the Prudential Foundation, owes its name and form to its designer, Michael Graves, the Princeton architect.

The $30 fee includes both a reception and dinner.

Information: (201) 596-3449.
