A Look at Work by Polish Artists

By TESSA MELVIN
Published: August 28, 1988

THE first multi-media exhibition in the metropolitan area devoted exclusively to the works of contemporary Polish artists is under way at the White Plains Public Library.

Before the series ends Dec. 4, library visitors may, at no charge, hear one of the world's leading jazz pianists, see works by three of Poland's best known graphic illustrators and observe photographs that use historic printing processes. (They will also be able to learn more about those techniques by attending a free workshop.) ''There are so many wonderful artists who emigrated to the United State recently,'' said Harriet Weissman, director of the Museum Gallery at the library. ''These artists represent a segment of the population that hasn't been given its due.'' Genesis of Exhibition

The idea for the exhibition came from Barbara Hansen, the recently hired curator for the gallery. She is a ceramic artist who lives in Bronxville.

Although she frequently saw the work of Polish friends published in leading publications, Mrs. Hansen said, she realized there had never been an exhibition providing a showcase for these artists. She convinced Ms. Weissman that the library should provide it.

The Museum Gallery has mounted 70 exhibitions since it opened in l974, but the current effort, Ms. Weissman said, is the most ambitious project the gallery has undertaken. Funds for the exhibition have been provided by the City of White Plains, the Friends of the White Plains Public Library and the Westchester Arts Fund of the Council for the Arts in Westchester, an organization supported by corporate contributions and by Westchester County. Additional support, for a performance of Chopin works by the pianist Tadeusz Kerner, has come from the Polish-American Community Humanities Center.

The photographs of Daniel Kazimierski, a Croton resident and a New York University film and photography professor, are currently on display in a show entitled ''Silver, Platinum & Iron.''

Mr. Kazimierski is considered an authority in the use of these three printing methods, which produce diverse shadings and unusual results that are unattainable with commercially available photographic materials. A Photo Workshop

On Sep. l7, Dolores Metzner, a Croton photographer, will conduct a free workshop for parents and children in the cyanotype, or iron, printing process, that Mr. Kazimierski uses in the exhibit. The workshop will begin at 10:30 A.M.

Four weeks from today, on Sept. 25, ''Polish Impressions,'' an exhibition featuring three award-winning artists, will open. The selections include the graphic art of Rafal Olbinski, watercolors by Andrzej Czeczot and the illustrations of Maciek Albrecht.

Mr. Olbinski, a freelance illustrator, is a professor at the Tyler School of Art at Temple University in Philadelphia as well as an instructor at New York City's School of Visual Arts. He has won numerous awards for his animated films and commercials. In l986, Mr. Olbinski won three gold medals for his illustrations in separate international competitions. His work has been published in many news publications and his posters are in the collections of the Library of Congress, the Carnegie Foundation and the Poster Museum in Paris. An Immigrant's View

Mr. Czoczot is best known in his native Poland for his satirical sketches of Communist Party officials, work for which he said he was imprisoned in l98l although he was never formally charged. He was released in l982 and immigrated to the United States with his wife and son. His work at the White Plains library will include a series of new satirical drawings expressing his feelings about being an immigrant in New York.

The illustrations of Maciek Albrecht will include some of the work he has done for animated films and commercials. During the last five years, his work has appeared in leading magazines and newspapers, and his animated feature, ''The Soldier's Tales,'' won the l984 Emmy Award for Animation.

The musical portion of the series will begin on Sunday, Oct. 9, at 3 P.M. when Adam Makowicz, a leading European jazz pianist, will play selections from his recordings of George Gershwin and other jazz, as well as his own compositions.

In a second performance, a prize-winning performer of Frederic Chopin's music, Tadeusz Kerner, will give a recital and a lecture on Chopin on Oct. 2l at 8 P.M. Film Series Scheduled

A film series will begin Nov. 7 with a showing of ''Aria for an Athlete,'' directed by Filip Bajon. On Nov. 9, also at 7:30 P.M., the film ''Camera Buff,'' directed by Krzysztof Kieslowski, will be shown. Both films are in Polish with English subtitles and are two hours in length.

Gallery hours are 9 A.M. to 9 P.M. Mondays through Thursdays, 9 A.M. to 6 P.M. Fridays, 9 A.M. to 5 P.M. Saturdays and 1 to 5 P.M. Sundays. A reception for the artists will be held Oct. 9 from 5 to 7 P.M., to which the public is invited.
