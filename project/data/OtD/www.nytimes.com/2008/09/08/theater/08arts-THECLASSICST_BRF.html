The Classics Two Ways

  • Sign In to E-Mail or Save This
  • Print
Compiled by STEVEN McELROY
Published: September 7, 2008

The Aquila Theater Company has announced that it has been awarded $292,585 from the National Endowment for the Humanities. The money will support Aquila’s new initiative “Page and Stage: Theater, Tradition and Culture in America.” Aquila will partner with public libraries in 16 areas around the country, presenting live theatrical events, including the company’s production of Homer’s “Iliad.” There will also be reading groups and lectures in the libraries to inspire people to think about how the themes of classical Greek and Roman drama resonate in America today.
