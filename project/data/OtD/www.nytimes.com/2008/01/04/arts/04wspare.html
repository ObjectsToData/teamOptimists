AROUND TOWN

Museums and Sites

BULL RIDING INVITATIONAL Some people do yoga. Some meditate. Paulo Crimber happens to still his mind while riding a 1,500-pound bull that’s trying to throw him into a pit of hard-packed dirt in front of thousands of screaming spectators. “I always try not to think too much,” Mr. Crimber, one of several professional Brazilian riders, said in an accent that was more American ranch than South American Portuguese. “You have to ride that bull jump for jump — if you try to anticipate, that won’t work very well.”

Well, Mr. Crimber may get some time to “not think” this weekend. He is one of 45 top-division professional riders scheduled to take part in a competition that starts on Friday and will culminate in a final round on Sunday at Madison Square Garden. (Above, Guilherme Marchi, who will also be competing this weekend, in a contest last year.) The typical story arcs that accompany most sports apply here, only in exaggerated form: There are more than a few young upstarts (now 27, Mr. Crimber is in his 10th year of riding in the United States) and riders who have met great success after humble — or at least rural — beginnings. (Mr. Crimber, who has more than $1 million in earnings, rode steers and had little formal schooling at his home in the state of São Paulo.) And there’s certainly no shortage of riders trying for a comeback after being injured. The odd bruises and wounds that could come only from this sport are a reminder that riders are in competition not only with one another, but with the bulls, too. That may explain the strong ties between the men, who travel across the country together to risk life and limb.

“That’s just our second family,” Mr. Crimber said. “Sometimes we spend more time with the guys than we spend with our own family.” At least his method of finding a small dose of Zen — mounting a bull with his spiritual brethren — is equal parts entertaining and bone-chilling for the rest of us, who can root comfortably from our stable seating in the stands. (Friday and Saturday nights at 8, Sunday at 1 p.m., Madison Square Garden, 866-448-7849, pbrnow.com; $25 to $195.) MONICA DRAKE
