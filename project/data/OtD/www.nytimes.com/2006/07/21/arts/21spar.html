Forum: Artists and Exhibitions

Museums and Sites

AMERICAN MUSEUM OF NATURAL HISTORY, Central Park West and 79th Street. Through Jan. 15, “Yellowstone to Yukon,” an exhibition of more than 40 photographs by 11 photographers documenting the animals, vegetation and geology of the regions affected by one of the world’s largest wildlife conservation initiatives. Through Jan. 7, “Lizards & Snakes: Alive!,” an exhibition with live examples of more than 25 species. Through Aug. 20, “Darwin,” an exhibition devoted to the naturalist Charles Darwin, which includes fossils and live creatures as well as a reconstruction of his study at Down House. Through Jan. 2, “Voices From South of the Clouds,” a display of photographs of life in the Yunnan Province in China, taken by villagers. Also on view: “Vital Variety: A Visual Celebration of Invertebrate Biodiversity,” an exhibition of 24 large-format color photographs by the entomologist Piotr Naskrecki In the Imax theater, through Labor Day, “Journey Into Amazing Caves,” a film that documents the collaboration of Nancy Aulenbach, a cave explorer, and Hazel Barton, a microbiologist. Timed tickets to “Lizards & Snakes: Alive!” and “Darwin,” which include museum admission, are $21; $16 for students and 60+; $12 for 12 and younger. Imax tickets, which include museum admission, are $21; students and 60+, $16; children under 12, $12. Suggested museum admission: $14; students and 60+, $10.50; children, $8. Combination tickets including museum and Rose Center admission and the Hayden Planetarium space show (“Cosmic Collisions”): $22; students and 60+, $16.50; 12 and younger, $13; under 2, free; admission that includes all activities: $30; $23 for students and 60+; $19 for those 12 and younger. The Rose Center is open until 8:45 p.m. for “Starry Nights: Fridays Under the Sphere,” with a cash bar, jazz and tapas, on the first Friday of every month. Museum hours: daily, 10 a.m. to 5:45 p.m. (to 8:45 p.m. on Fridays); (212) 769-5100 or (212) 769-5200; amnh.orgHISTORIC RICHMOND TOWN, Staten Island Historical Society, 441 Clarke Avenue, Richmond Town. Tomorrow, 5 to 9 p.m., a traditional 19th-century dinner with entertainment; $45; members, $40; reservations: (718) 351-1611, Ext. 281. Hours: Wednesdays through Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Admission: $5; 62+, $4; students and 5 to 17, $3.50; children under 5 and members, free; (718) 351-1611.

MUSEUM OF THE CITY OF NEW YORK, 1120 Fifth Avenue, at 103rd Street. Sunday at 3 p.m., a staged reading of “Camp Logan,” a work about black soldiers in the early 1900’s and the Houston Riot of 1917. Through Oct. 12, “The High Style of Dorothy Draper,” a display of furniture, photographs and other items relating to the career of that noted interior decorator. Through July 30, “On the Couch: Cartoons From The New Yorker,” psychotherapy-related cartoons from the magazine. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Suggested admission: $9; 62+ and students, $5; under 12, free; families, $20; Sundays, 10 a.m. to noon, free for all. (212) 534-1672.

NEW-YORK HISTORICAL SOCIETY, 2 West 77th Street, Manhattan. Through Jan. 7, “Legacies: Contemporary Artists Reflect on Slavery,” an exhibition of works by more than 30 artists. Through Feb. 25, “Nature and the American Vision: The Hudson River School at the New-York Historical Society,” an exhibition of 100 paintings and watercolors from the society’s permanent collection, including works by Thomas Cole, Frederic Edwin Church and Jasper Francis Cropsey. Through Sept. 17., “Group Dynamics: Family Portraits and Scenes of Everyday Life,” an exhibition of portraits from the 18th and 19th centuries. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; to 8 p.m. on Fridays. $10; students and 65+, $5; under 12, free. (212) 873-3400.

NEW YORK PUBLIC LIBRARY, Wachenheim Gallery of the Humanities and Social Sciences Library, Fifth Avenue and 42nd Street. Through Aug. 5, the Declaration of Independence, handwritten by Thomas Jefferson. Tuesdays and Wednesdays, 11 a.m. to 7:30 p.m.; Thursdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Free. (212) 869-8089.

NEW YORK TRANSIT MUSEUM, Boerum Place and Schermerhorn Street, Brooklyn Heights. On view, “The Triborough Bridge: Robert Moses and the Automobile Age,” an exhibition of photographs, drawings, models and artifacts celebrating that span’s 70th anniversary. Through Aug. 27, “Upon Closer Inspection: The Art of Laura Cantor,” semiabstract pastel drawings, oil paintings and linoleum-block prints of subway-car components by an artist who is also a subway-car inspector. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. $5; 3 to 17, $3; members, 62+ and children under 3, free; (718) 694-1600.

Gardens

BROOKLYN BOTANIC GARDEN, 1000 Washington Avenue, Crown Heights. Opening tomorrow and running through Sept. 3, “Nature Unbound: Sculpture and Drawings by Nancy Blum,” an exhibition of large-scale works drawn from nature; a reception with Ms. Blum will take place tomorrow, 2 to 4 p.m. Through Sept. 3, an interactive garden walk that highlights plant conservation. Tuesdays through Fridays, 8 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Saturdays, Sundays and holidays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. $5; students and 65+, $3; under 16, free; free for all Tuesdays and Saturdays from 10 a.m. to noon; (718) 623-7200.

STATEN ISLAND BOTANICAL GARDEN, 1000 Richmond Terrace, Livingston. Two exhibitions running through Sept. 17 in the visitors’ center: “Journey to China’s Southwest,” with photographs, clothing and folk art; also, “Exploring Tibet: In Search of the Salween,” a display of rare photographs from a 1930’s expedition to southeastern Tibet to find the source of the Salween River. Admission: $3; students and 62+, $2; members, free. The one-acre Scholar’s Garden and the Secret Garden are open Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Scholar’s Garden admission: $5; 62+ and children under 12, $4. Secret Garden: $2; adults with a child, free. Grounds hours: daily, dawn to dusk; free; (718) 273-8200.

WAVE HILL, Independence Avenue and West 249th Street, Riverdale, the Bronx. Tomorrow and Sunday, 1 to 4 p.m., “Dance in the Rainforest,’’ a mask and instrument-making workshop, with music and movement, featuring Yvette Martinez, a dancer, and Nancy Friedman, a musician. Tuesdays through Sundays, 9 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; till 9 p.m. on Wednesdays in July. $4; students and 65+, $2; under 6, free; Tuesdays, free all day; Saturdays, free 9 a.m. to noon; (718) 549-3200.

EVENTS

New York City

BATTERY PARK CITY PARKS CONSERVANCY, Battery Park City, Lower Manhattan. Tonight at 6:30, “Drumming Circles for All Ages,” with djembe and conga drums, bells, shakers and sticks, featuring the drummer Pamela Patrick. Free; (212) 267-9700; bpcparks.org.

EAST VILLAGE VISITING NEIGHBORS STREET FEST, Fourth Avenue between Fifth and Eighth Streets. Tomorrow, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.

EAST SIDE SUMMER FESTIVAL, Lexington Avenue from 42nd to 57th Streets. Tomorrow, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.

NEW YORK CITY UNFOLDS STREET FAIR, Broadway from Houston to Grand Streets, Lower Manhattan. Sunday, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.

MOTION PICTURE CLUB FESTIVAL, Seventh Avenue, from 47th to 57th Streets, Manhattan. Sunday, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.

‘STARRY SKY,’ World Financial Center Winter Garden, 220 Vesey Street, Lower Manhattan. An exhibition of more than 300 handmade kites by Buteo Huang, a Taiwanese designer. Daily, 6 a.m. to midnight. Through Aug. 19. Free. (212) 945-0505.

‘FRESH FRUIT: THIS SUMMER IN NEW YORK,’ an annual celebration of lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender art and culture with plays, films, dance and a reading at locations in Midtown and Lower Manhattan. Through Sunday. Sites, schedule and other information: freshfruitfestival.com.

FLATBUSH AVENUE STREET FESTIVAL, from Parkside Avenue to Cortelyou Road, Brooklyn. Sunday, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.

COLOMBIAN DAY PARADE, Northern Boulevard from 69th to 90th Streets, Jackson Heights and Elmhurst, Queens. Sunday at noon.
