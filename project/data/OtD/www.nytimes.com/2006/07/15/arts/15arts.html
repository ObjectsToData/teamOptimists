From Head Butt to Hit

Zinédine Zidane’s hit on the chest of Marco Materazzi in the final of the World Cup between France and Italy last Sunday has inspired what looks like another hit: a song, Reuters reported from Paris. The song, “Coupe de Boule,” the French for head-butt, was written by three associates at Plage Records, a small label specializing in music for advertising, to raise their spirits after France was defeated by Italy in a penalty shootout while Mr. Zidane, the French captain, was on the sidelines as punishment for his foul. The Plage founders, Emmanuel and Sebastien Lipszyc, and the composer Franck Lascombes wrote the Caribbean zouk-influenced song, with its chorus, “Zidane, il a tapé” (“Zidane, the hit man”). It was sent by e-mail to about 50 contacts, then posted on the Internet. Next, the French radio station SkyRock put it on its play list, and record labels and ringtone sellers began fighting for the rights. Some industry sources said those rights could fetch more than $125,000. Mr. Sebastien said, “Yes, it could become lucrative, but if we can make everybody dance to it this summer, it will give us a good laugh.” LAWRENCE VAN GELDER

The Who Go Mobile

It’s back on the bus for the Who. The band will begin the North American leg of its new world tour on Sept. 12 in Philadelphia, coming the next night to Nikon at Jones Beach Theater on Long Island; other local dates include Madison Square Garden on Sept. 18 and PNC Bank Arts Center in Holmdel, N.J., on Sept. 21. The tour will continue through the United States and Canada until December, and then head to South America, Japan, Australia and Europe next year, the band announced. Besides Pete Townshend and Roger Daltrey its two surviving original members, the Who this time around will include Pino Palladino on bass, John (Rabbit) Bundrik on keyboards, Zak Starkey (son of Ringo Starr) on drums, and, on guitar and vocals, Simon Townshend, Mr. Townshend’s brother. (John Entwistle, the original bassist, died in 2002, just as the Who was beginning its last major tour, and Mr. Palladino took his place.) The band will play music from its first new album in 24 years, which is due on Oct. 23 and has the working title “Who 2.” Among the new songs: a “mini rock opera” called “Wire & Glass.”

Arts Center Reopens

On Long Island

The newly reconfigured Watermill Center, founded in 1992 by the theater director Robert Wilson as an international center for studies in the arts and humanities, began its grand reopening weekend yesterday. Mr. Wilson, its artistic director, worked with architects to design a new 35,000-square-foot building for the center, which is on six acres in Watermill, N.Y., two hours east of New York in the Hamptons. The reconfiguration also converts a former Western Union research laboratory into residency space for visual, design, performing and literary artists. After a benefit evening tonight, the doors of the center will open to the public tomorrow with a day of free guided tours of arts installations and new facilities and workshops, as well as live performances. Information: (212) 253-7484, Ext. 10.

Case of Beatles Theft

Ends in Supervision

A man who tried to sell stolen tapes of Beatles recording sessions was ordered by a London judge yesterday to undergo two years of mental health supervision, the BBC reported. Nigel Oliver, 55, was caught in an undercover police sting in 2003 trying to sell a cache of tapes from the 1969 sessions for the Beatles’ album “Let It Be” for more than $400,000. The jury was told that Mr. Oliver was a middleman in the sale, acting on behalf of two unknown men in Amsterdam. He was charged with two counts of handling stolen goods, but the judge, Jeremy McMullen, found him mentally unfit to plead. “It is necessary for the protection of the public that you take advantage of medication and treatment and counseling that you are getting,” the judge said. In a search of Mr. Oliver’s home, the police found documents giving instructions for the sale of the tapes, as well as a key to a locked suitcase that contained a stolen 1960 British passport for George Harrison.

The Warped Tour,

Behind Glass

The Vans Warped Tour, the 12-year-old bonanza of skateboards, punk rock and tattoos, is about to join the ranks of Bob Dylan, Rick Nelson and the Clash as subjects of exhibitions at the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and Museum in Cleveland. It will run from January to June, and will feature instruments, banners, clothing, backstage credentials and other memorabilia, including the special cans of Warped Tour water given to performers. The organizers are still soliciting donations from bands, but among those who have promised materials are the Mighty Mighty Bosstones, MxPx, Thursday, Gogol Bordello, the Sounds and the Vandals. When the exhibition closes, the items will be placed in a time capsule for 25 years.The Warped Tour is scheduled to arrive at the Nassau Coliseum in Uniondale, N.Y., on Aug. 5.

Pop Notes

George Winston, Alex DeGrassi, Peter Lang, Country Joe McDonald, Sean Smith, Henry Kaiser and Nels Cline are among the musicians who have contributed recordings to “The Legend of Blind Joe Death,” a tribute album to the avant-garde guitarist John Fahey who died in 2001, Billboard reported. The album, with 20 tracks, is to be released on Aug. 22 on Fahey’s former label, Takoma, with liner notes by Barry Jansen, who is better known as the radio D.J. Dr. Demento. ... “Return to Cookie Mountain,” the long-awaited new album by the critically acclaimed Brooklyn band TV on the Radio, will be released by Interscope Records on Sept. 12. The album has already been released in Europe.

Merkin’s Season

Jazz from New Orleans, the “New Sounds Live” series of contemporary classical and avant-garde music, and interpretations of the music of Radiohead and Bjork, will be part of the 2006-7 season at the Merkin Concert Hall on West 67th Street in Manhattan. It will begin on Sept. 2 with a “Crescent City Celebration” featuring Henry Butler, Maurice Brown and the Dr. Michael White Quartet. Among the other highlights of the 45 concerts through May are a tribute to Don Cherry with Dave Douglas and Roy Campbell (Sept. 16); the Section Quartet playing arrangements of songs by Radiohead, with Travis Sullivan’s Bjorkestra (Oct. 19); Gunther Schuller conducting the Mingus Orchestra (Nov. 30); Bang on a Can’s eighth annual People’s Commissioning Concert (March 1); the pianist and producer Chilly Gonzales (April 5); and the avant-garde composer Annie Gosfield (May 3). Information: merkinconcerthall.org.
