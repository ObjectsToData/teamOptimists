On the Street

New York City

FRIENDS OF LAGUARDIA PLACE STREET FAIR, Bleecker Street, between LaGuardia Place and Lafayette Street, West Village. Tomorrow, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.

BLEECKER AREA MERCHANTS AND RESIDENTS ASSOCIATION FESTIVAL, Bleecker Street, from Avenue of the Americas to Broadway, West Village. Sunday, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.

MCBURNEY Y.M.C.A. FAIR, Avenue of the Americas, from 14th to 23rd Streets, Chelsea. Tomorrow, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.

TIMES SQUARE BLOCK PARTY, 46th Street, from Seventh to Eighth Avenues, Manhattan. Tomorrow, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Sponsored by the Church of Scientology.

GREAT JULY FOURTH FESTIVAL, Water Street, from Fulton to Moore Streets, Lower Manhattan. Tuesday, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m. Sponsored by the Pearl Street Park Association.

PELOTEROS UNIDOS CARNIVAL, East 109th Street between Second and Third Avenues, East Harlem. Today through Tuesday, 4 to 11 p.m.

DOMINICAN DAY FESTIVAL, Amsterdam Avenue, between 191st and 192nd Streets, Washington Heights. Tomorrow and Sunday, 1 to 8 p.m.

'PATRIOTS AND REBELS,' a tour of sites relevant to the Revolutionary War, meeting today and Sunday at 2 p.m. in front of Bowling Green Park, at Bowling Green and Broadway, Lower Manhattan. Sponsored by Bowery and Canal Walking Tours. $12; students and 65+, $10. (212) 979-8013.

'PRE-FIREWORKS PRESIDENTIAL GREENWICH VILLAGE AND GRAMERCY PARK' covers sites associated with Abraham Lincoln, Franklin D. Roosevelt, George Washington and others. Tomorrow, Sunday and Tuesday at 2:30 and 5 p.m.; the 5 p.m. tour on Tuesday ends at 7:30 p.m. at Franklin D. Roosevelt Drive and 23rd Street, one of the areas from which to view the holiday fireworks display. Sponsored by NYC Discovery Tours. $13. Meeting place and reservations: (212) 465-3331.

'REVOLUTIONARY NEW YORK.' Lower Manhattan. Two tours on Tuesday at 1 p.m. of sites associated with the war, both meeting near City Hall Park, Broadway and Murray Street. A free two-mile walk led by the Urban Trail Conference will meet near the fountain. (The tour will also be given Sunday at 1 p.m.) (718) 352-0381. Big Onion Walking Tours will meet at the intersection of Broadway and Murray Street; $15; 63+, $12; students, $10. (212) 439-1090.

EARLY MORNING WALKING TOUR OF REVOLUTIONARY WAR NEW YORK. Lower Manhattan. Covers sites of historic and cultural importance, led by James Kaplan. Meets Tuesday at 2 a.m. at Broadway and Park Place, and ends at 6 a.m. in front of the sponsor of the walk, Fraunces Tavern Museum, 54 Pearl Street. Fee: $20; members, $15. A free open house at the museum follows, from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Reservations: (212) 425-1778, Ext. 18.

'HIGHLIGHTS OF HEROES OF THE AMERICAN REVOLUTION.' Lower Manhattan. Visits sites of importance in the lives of Washington, Jefferson, John Adams and others. Tomorrow and Monday at 11 a.m. and 1 and 3 p.m.; Sunday at 10 a.m.; and Tuesday at 10 a.m., noon, 2 and 4 p.m. $5. Sponsored by Gotham Walk. Reservations: (212) 629-1886.

'SUMMER NIGHTS, LIGHTS AND DELIGHTS: THIRD ANNUAL FREEDOM WALK' combines a look at patriotic sites in Manhattan and Brooklyn with a walk across the Brooklyn Bridge. Meets Tuesday at 5 p.m. inside the Blimpie restaurant, 38 Park Row, Lower Manhattan. $15. Sponsored by Dr. Phil: New York Talks and Walks. (888) 377-4455.

'REVOLUTIONARY WAR ROOTS OF FORT GREENE PARK.' A tour of the Brooklyn park and the surrounding area with a focus on its relevance to Washington and the Revolutionary War. Meets Tuesday at 2 p.m. in front of the Marriott, Adams and Tillary Streets, downtown Brooklyn. $25. Sponsored by Metro Tour Service. (718) 789-0430.

Events

New York City

MACY'S FOURTH OF JULY FIREWORKS: 'IT'S ABOUT U.S.' The annual display over the East River, fired from barges from East 23rd to East 42nd Streets and in Lower Manhattan. Tuesday, about 9:20 p.m. In Manhattan the best view is along the Franklin D. Roosevelt Drive, from 14th to 42nd Streets, and at the South Street Seaport. In Brooklyn best views are from the Brooklyn Heights Promenade, Empire Fulton Ferry State Park and Furman Street, from Atlantic Avenue to Cadman Plaza West, and points between Joralemon and Cranberry Streets with a clear view of the sky over the East River; also in Greenpoint. In Queens appropriate spots are those with a view of that section of the East River, including Hunters Point. In New Jersey the display can be seen at Liberty State Park in Jersey City. The fireworks show, with recorded original and patriotic music performed by the New York Pops and recorded segments with Lionel Richie and Nick Lachey, will be broadcast on Channel 4, beginning at 9; simultaneous broadcast on 1010 WINS. Information: (212) 494-4495. For those viewing from boats: (212) 494-5243. FIREWORKS WATCH ON ROOSEVELT ISLAND Viewing of the Macy's fireworks display from the southern point of the island, beginning Tuesday at 9:20 p.m. Entertainment, including juggling, face-painting and music, begins at 5 p.m. Sponsored by the Roosevelt Island Operating Corporation. Advance tickets, at $18, can be purchased online, at fireworks.rioc.com; seating is provided. Same-day tickets, $20. Service on the tram is suspended for repairs, but the island can be reached by bus (the Q102 bus from Long Island City, Queens); by car, over the 36th Avenue Bridge at 21st Street, Long Island City; and by subway (the F train to the Roosevelt Island stop). (877) 772-5425.

DECLARATION OF INDEPENDENCE, handwritten by Thomas Jefferson, on view through Aug. 5 at the New York Public Library, Wachenheim Gallery of the Humanities and Social Sciences Library, Fifth Avenue and 42nd Street. Free. Today and tomorrow, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; closed Sunday through Tuesday. (212) 869-8089.

INTREPID SEA, AIR AND SPACE MUSEUM, Pier 86, West 46th Street and 12th Avenue, Clinton. Sunday, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m., "Red, White and Blue Festival," with apple-pie-eating contests, flag-making and music. Opening Tuesday at 11 a.m., "Heroic Journey: Ordinary People, Extraordinary Stories," a new exhibition on military heroism told through letters, photographs and other items. Today and Monday, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; tomorrow, Sunday and Tuesday, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Museum admission: $16.50; veterans, reservists, students and 65+, $12.50; ages 6 to 17, $11.50; 2 to 5, $4.50; under 2, active military personnel and museum members, free; wheelchair patrons, half-price. (212) 245-0072; intrepidmuseum.org.

CITYSOL, Solar 1, Stuyvesant Cove Park, East 23rd Street and the East River, Manhattan. A free outdoor performance of solar-powered and biodiesel-amplified music. Sunday, noon to 8 p.m. Among the performers: Big A Little a, Japanther, Dame Darcy and the Wowz. Also, a green-lifestyle market. Sponsored by Green Energy, Arts and Education Center. (212) 505-6050.

'A HOLIDAY SALUTE TO GEORGE M. COHAN,' Van Cortlandt Park, Rockwood Drive Circle, Broadway and Mosholu Parkway, the Bronx. Annual concert of patriotic and show music by the Bronx Arts Ensemble, with Cathy Gale, a singer. Tuesday at 2 p.m. Free; take seating. (718) 601-7399.

'BROOKLYN BLOCK PARTY,' Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway, at Prospect Park. A free event with hip-hop music, film screenings, art workshops and lectures in celebration of the opening of the exhibition "Graffiti." Tomorrow, 5 to 9 p.m. Part of the museum's Target First Saturday series. (718) 638-5000.

ANNUAL PASTA-EATING COMPETITION, in front of Sal Anthony's SPQR Restaurant, 133 Mulberry Street, Little Italy. Tomorrow at 2 p.m. Part of the Sorrento Cheese Summer in Little Italy Festival.

ANNUAL INTERNATIONAL HOT DOG-EATING CONTEST, Nathan's Famous, Surf and Stillwell Avenues, Coney Island, Brooklyn. Tuesday at noon. The event will be televised live on ESPN.

Upstate New York

PLAYLAND PARK, Exit 19 of Interstate 95, Rye. Hours: today, tomorrow and Monday, noon to midnight; Sunday, noon to 11 p.m.; Tuesday, 11 a.m. to 1 a.m. Fireworks displays Monday and Tuesday at 9:15 p.m., then every Wednesday and Friday at 9:15 p.m. through Sept. 1, weather permitting. Parking: weekdays, $5; weekends, $7; holidays, $10. Free admission. Ride cards (for rides that are 2 to 4 points) range from $1 (for 1 point) to $20 (for 24 points) to $28 (for 36 points); in addition there is a "fun band" (a wrist band) for $35 that includes unlimited rides for six hours. Also, music, magic and children's shows. (914) 813-7000; www.ryeplayland.org.
