ON THE TOWNS


Published: April 14, 1996

An opinionated guide to cultural and recreational goings-on around the state this week. To submit items for consideration, write to On the Towns, Sunday New Jersey Section, The New York Times, 229 West 43d Street, New York, N.Y. 10036, or send a fax to (212) 556-7219.

MUSIC

BENJAMIN FRANKLIN AUDITORIUM The Art Center Chamber Music Concerts present the Ying Quartet. Tuesday at 8:30 P.M. Tickets: $20. Windsor Road, Teaneck. (201) 836-3547.

JOHN HARMS CENTER FOR THE ARTS Pinchas Zukerman, violinist, and Marc Neikrug, pianist. Tonight at 7. Tickets: $20-$34. 30 North Van Brunt Street, Englewood. (201) 567-3600.

MAURICE LEVIN THEATER The Aeolian Chamber Players. Tonight at 7:30. Tickets: $23; $21 for students and the elderly. Jewish Community Center of Metropolitan New Jersey, 760 Northfield Avenue, West Orange. (201) 736-3200, extension 253.

MAXWELL'S Lung Fish. Tonight at 6 P.M. Tickets: $5. Golden Smog. Tonight at 8 P.M. Tickets: $10. New Bomb Turks and Gas Huffer. Tuesday at 9 P.M. Tickets: $6. The Royal Crowns. Wednesday at 9 P.M. Tickets: $6. The Wrens and Grand Mal. Thursday at 9 P.M. Tickets; $5. Hyperactive. Friday at 10 P.M. Tickets: $7. Dark Carnival. Saturday at 10 P.M. Tickets: $7. 1039 Washington Street, Hoboken. (201) 798-4064.

NEW JERSEY SYMPHONY ORCHESTRA The Masterworks Series continues. Today at 3 P.M., Symphony Hall, Newark. Tickets: $15-$47. The Ovation Series, "Beethoven and Beyond," conducted by Mariusz Smolij. Thursday at 8 P.M., State Theater, New Brunswick. Friday at 8:30 P.M., Richardson Auditorium, Princeton. Next Sunday at 3 P.M., John Harms Center for the Arts, Englewood. Tickets: $12-$36. (800) ALLEGRO or (201) 624-8203.

NICHOLAS MUSIC CENTER Opera at Rutgers presents musical selections from "Rigoletto," "Madame Butterfly" and "The Barber of Seville." Today at 4 P.M. Tickets: $10; $8 for the elderly. The Rutgers Glee Club performs classical and popular favorites. Saturday at 8 P.M. Tickets: $7; $5 for the elderly. George Street at Route 18, New Brunswick. (908) 932-7511.

OHEB SHALOM CONGREGATION The Maxwell Street Klezmer Band, directed by Lori Lippitz. Today at 2 P.M. Tickets: $12. 170 Scotland Road, South Orange. (201) 762-7067.

STRAND THEATER The Shore Ballet Company present "Coppelia." Today at 2 P.M. Tickets: $10-$22. Fourth Street and Clifton Avenue, Lakewood. (908) 845-0606.

UNITARIAN SOCIETY OF RIDGEWOOD Andrew Burns, pianist, plays classical masterpieces. Friday at 8 P.M. Free; donations accepted. 113 Cottage Place, Ridgewood. (201) 444-4393. WATCHUNG ARTS CENTER The Sunday Night Series continues with Ellen Tepper and Separate Reality. Tonight at 9 P.M. Tickets: $8. Sammy Rimington, clarinetist, performs with Jon Marks, pianist, and John Russell, drummer. Friday at 8 P.M. Tickets: $10. 18 Stirling Road, Watchung. (908) 753-0190.

Theater

COUNT BASIE THEATER Phoenix Productions presents "Jesus Christ Superstar." Today at 3 P.M. Tickets: $15-$19; $2 discount for the elderly. 99 Monmouth Avenue, Red Bank. (908) 747-0014.

CORDERO SCHOOL Civic Theater of Hudson County presents "Chess." Opens Friday. Through April 28. Fridays and Saturdays at 8 P.M.; Sundays at 3 P.M. Tickets: $10; $8 for students and the elderly. 158 Erie Street, Jersey City. (201) 823-2086.

CROSSROADS THEATER COMPANY "The Screened-In Porch," by Marian X, explores the relationship of best friends living next door to each other after 35 years apart. Opens Saturday. Through May 26. Tuesdays through Fridays at 8 P.M.; Saturdays at 3 and 8 P.M.; Sundays at 3 and 7 P.M. Tickets: $22-$32. 7 Livingston Avenue, New Brunswick. (908) 249-5560.

FORUM THEATER The Forum Theater Group presents "Sophie, Totie and Belle," a musical tale about Sophie Tucker, Totie Fields and Belle Barth. Opens Friday. Through May 19. Thursdays through Saturdays at 8 P.M.; Wednesdays and Sundays at 2:30 P.M. Tickets: $23-$27. 314 Main Street, Metuchen. (908) 548-4670.

GRANGE HALL STAGE The Radburn Players present "A Streetcar Named Desire," by Tennessee Williams, directed by Ken Sheppard. Friday and Saturday at 7:30 P.M. Tickets: $10; $8 for students and the elderly. 29-20 Fair Lawn Avenue, Fair Lawn. (201) 444-5874.

HOBOKEN CITY HALL The Patchwork Theater Company presents "Nuts," by Thomas Topor. Fridays through May 3 at 7 P.M. Tickets: $5. Washington and First Streets, Hoboken. (201) 865-1436.

JOHN HARMS CENTER FOR THE ARTS Mummenschanz, the Swiss mask and mime troupe, presents "Parade," a new work. Saturday at 8 P.M. Tickets: $20-$34. 30 North Van Brunt Street, Englewood. (201) 567-5797.

LITTLE FIREHOUSE THEATER The Bergen County Players present "Lips Together, Teeth Apart," by Terrence McNally. Opens Saturday. Through May 11. Fridays and Saturdays at 8:30 P.M.; Sundays at 2 P.M. Tickets: $10-$12. 298 Kinderkamack Road, Oradell. (201) 262-0515.

LUNA STAGE "Kick the Can," by Jim Lehrer, a coming-of-age story that celebrates the open road and American innocence, lost and found. Through May 5. Thursday through Saturdays at 8 P.M.; Sundays at 2 P.M. Tickets: $20; $15 for students and the elderly. 6 Erie Street, Montclair. (201) 744-3309.
