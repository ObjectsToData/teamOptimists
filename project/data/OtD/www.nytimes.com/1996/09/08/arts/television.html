TELEVISION

By BILL CARTER
Published: September 8, 1996

Here is a selective guide to coming cultural events in New York and beyond. Events that are starred are those that Times critics consider particularly noteworthy. Unless indicated otherwise, all dates listed are for openings, all addresses are in Manhattan and all telephone numbers have a 212 area code. Addresses for locations in New York City cited more than once appear on page 80.

NEW SERIES

ABC

''DANGEROUS MINDS'' An adaptation of the 1995 hit movie, with Annie Potts (''Designing Women'') taking on Michelle Pfeiffer's role: the tough, dedicated inner-city teacher. Mondays at 8, starting Sept. 30.

''LIFE'S WORK'' Lisa Ann Walter, a comedian, plays a wife and mother who tries to juggle her family life and a new career as an assistant district attorney in Baltimore. Tuesday at 8:30, starting Sept. 17.

* ''SPIN CITY'' Michael J. Fox, most recently seen as a harried Presidential aide in the film ''The American President,'' brings his comedy talents back to weekly television in a sitcom about an endlessly harried and invetive Deputy Mayor of New York who is compelled to solve myriad problems for a nitwit of a Mayor while carrying on a romance with a tabloid reporter who covers City Hall. ABC expects Mr. Fox to deliver the new hit it desperately needs, and based on enthusiastic response to the pilot episode, he may well do it. Tuesday at 9:30, starting Sept. 17.

''TOWNIES'' Molly Ringwald, who made her reputation as a teen-ager in the films of John Hughes, heads a group of young folks from a small New England town who find themselves still hanging out together after all these years. If it sounds like a blue-collar ''Friends,'' you're thinking like a network executive. Wednesday at 8:30, starting Sept. 18.

''SABRINA, THE TEEN-AGE WITCH'' Melissa Joan Hart, formerly the star of Nickelodeon's ''Clarissa Explains It All,'' is apparently destined to be a teen-ager until at least her 30's. The show is aimed at ABC's T.G.I.F. kiddy audience, and the title says it all Fridays at 8:30, starting Sept. 27.

''CLUELESS'' Like, totally. Hoping to capture the magic of the popular movie, though minus Alicia Silverstone, Rachel Blanchard takes command of the Beverly Hills teen pack. Fridays at 9, starting Sept. 20.

''COMMON LAW'' How much comedy is inherent in the practice of a young Hispanic lawyer at a WASPy firm? ABC will find out fast. The star is Greg Giraldo, a stand-up comedian who happens to be a Harvard-educated lawyer. Saturdays at 9:30, starting Sept. 28.

* ''RELATIVITY'' Marshall Herskovitz and Edward Zwick brought ABC two memorable dramas - ''Thirtysomething'' and ''My So-Called Life'' - and this may be a third. The series will chronicle the progress of a love affair from first chance meeting in Italy by two young Americans (Kimberly Williams of the ''Father of the Bride'' movies and a newcomer, David Conrad) through a courtship complicated by the interference of family members. The mood is unabashedly romantic and may prove seductive for fans of the producer's earlier efforts. Preview on sept. 24 at 10. Saturdays at 10, starting Sept. 28.

CBS

* ''COSBY'' In the 1980's Bill Cosby was prime time's biggest star, and his return to the medium is automatically the event of the season. His new comedy may not take off instantly, but it holds plenty of promise. In it Mr. cosby is a grouchy fellow who has been laid off from his job of 30 years and is resentful, but in mostly humorous ways. Phylicia Rashad is back because she complements Mr. Cosby so well, and Madeline Kahn is a neighbor. It's Cosby, os if it's funny, that's probably sufficient. Mondays at 8, starting Sept. 10.

''INK'' Can Ted Danson recapture the ''Cheers'' magic in a newsroom comedy with his real-life wife, Mary Steenburgen? That question became even more loaded when the premiere of his highly anticipated series was postponed at the last minute because the first four episodes proved so disappointing. Origingally, Mr. Danson was to be a columnist whose editor, Ms.Steenburgen, was his exwife, but the show was sent back to the production shop for an overhaul. CBS now says that the show will have its premiere on Oct. 21, taking over the 8:30 Monday night time slot.

''PEARL'' Here's the premise: Blue-collar mom goes to college, jousts with stuffy humanities professor. Here's the payoff: Rhea Periman (''Cheers'') is the mom, and Malcolm McDowell, most recently in ''Star Trek: Generations,k'' is the prof. It's broad, it has some heart, and it may be a hit. The show will start on Sept. 16 at 8:30 and will remain on Mondays until Oct. 23, when it shifts to Wednesdays at 8:30.

''PROMISED LAND'' The members of a family set out across America to change their lives and wind up changing the lives of everyone they meet. Gerald McRaney (''Major Dad'') is the patriarch. Each episode should include a two-hankie advisory. Tuesday at 8, starting Sept. 17.

''PUBLIC MORALS'' There was a much-publicized ''debate'' about the unusually crude language used in the pilot of this comedy about a New York police vice squad. The question is, Will viewers, even fans of crude language, find much to laugh about? Wednesdays at 9:30, starting in October.
