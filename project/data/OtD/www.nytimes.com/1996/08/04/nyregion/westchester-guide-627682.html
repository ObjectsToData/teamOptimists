Westchester Guide

By ELEANOR CHARLES
Published: August 4, 1996

ART OFFERINGS

A Manhattan art consultant who buys paintings and sculptures for corporations, architects and interior designers will hold an exhibition and sale of her own collection next Saturday and Sunday from noon to 5 P.M. at the Bedford Historical Hall on Route 22 in Bedford Center.

Forty framed paintings and 100 matted works on paper and photographs by 15 emerging and established American artists will be available for $400 to $5,000. Working out of her Fifth Avenue apartment in Manhattan and her home in Pound Ridge, Maeve Gyenes calls the company she began in 1970 Samson Fine Arts. 1970.

Among the artists represented is Fernand Fonssagrives, a popular fashion photographer in the 40's and 50's, whose first wife, Lisa Fonssagrives, became an international model. ''The photographs of her are personal,'' Ms. Gyenes said. ''They were made before she became a celebrity. They are about the woman, not the clothes horse.''

Robert Valdes's long, narrow, horizontal watercolors present a panoramic view of subjects as varied as the Long Island shore or a baseball bat and ball. They are arresting not only for their shape but their meticulous technique and color.

Screen-printed serigraphs by Brian Gormley are the antithesis of the Valdes works. Abstract, layered image over image and superimposed with what appear to be doodlepad scribblings, they are one of a kind.

At 85, Malcolm McKesson continues to produce drawings of figures executed in a darkly intimate, erotic style. The remaining artists are Micaela Amato, Sonja Eisenberg, Lawrence Beck, Robert Bery, Terry Blocket, Sebastian Gross, Andre Martheleur, Elaine Marinoff, Becky Mayer, Liam Roberts and Sandra De Sando. For more information, the number to call is (212) 369-6677.

BOOKS AT WHOLESALE

A small wholesale book company in Croton Falls, Italiata Book Company, will open its doors to the public on Saturdays from 11 A.M. to 6 P.M. beginning this week, offering books at less than half retail price.

Susan Barile, the owner, will also accommodate people by appointment. The number to call is 276-3822. The shop is in a warehouse on Route 22N in the Croton River Executive Park, and customers are given refreshments.

''I carry university press books published by Yale, Columbia and the University of California, to name a few,'' she said. Also carried are books on the humanities and sciences, out-of-print novels, books on philosophy, theater, film, psychology, imports from Britain and travel books.

Eighty percent of the stock is new, the rest used or antiquarian. One of the more esoteric selections is a group of books by George Gissing, a Victorian who wrote about Charles Dickens but also wrote novels of his own.

FLYFISHING EVENT

The second yearly Women's Northeast Saltwater Flyfishing Tournament will cast off next Saturday at sunup (around 5:30) in Westport and finish at 4 P.M., with an estimated 25 or more women angling from boats around the Norwalk Islands and on land along the Long Island Sound shore.

''We had 12 competitors last year,'' Jeff Northrop, the organizer, said. So far this year entries are coming from California, Florida and Pennsylvania, as well as from New York, Connecticut and New Jersey. Mr. Northrop said the only other women's flyfishing event in the country is held in Florida.

International Game Fishing Association rules will be followed, and competitors will be on the honor system to report the number and species of fish caught. All fish must be released back into the water immediately. Bluefish count for 1 point, striped bass earn 10 points, and bonita, albacore and Spanish mackerel rack up 20 points each.

The overall winner will receive a trophy, which is passed along each year with the addition of the winner's name on its base. Second- and third-place winners will be awarded various items of fishing tackle. Prizes will be distributed at a cocktail party after the event.

Women who take part must have their own tackle and boats, but some boats will be available at Northrop Landing, a fly-fishing store owned by Mr. Northrop on Riverside Avenue, where the tournament will begin and end. An entry fee of $50 pays for the tournament expenses. For more information or to register, call (203) 226-1915.

STARLIGHT HAYRIDE

A two-hour hayride under the stars is scheduled at Muscoot Farm in Somers next Saturday, beginning at 7:45 P.M. With luck a Perseid meteor shower will appear, and Craig Stevens, resident astronomer, will be on hand to explain the phenomenon and talk about the constellations that are visible.

The free program is suitable for individuals and families with children. Blankets should be brought to sit on; a small flashlight will come in handy, and so might snacks. The farm is on Route 100, reached by taking Exit 6 off I-684 and going west on Route 35 to Route 100. Turn left to the entrance, one and a half miles farther down on the right. Call 232-7118 for more information.

FAMILY MATINEE

A special 3 P.M. matinee for families will be presented at the Towne Crier Cafe in Pawling next Saturday, enabling children to enjoy the music and Southwestern humor of Trout Fishing in America. An adult performance is scheduled at 9:30 P.M.

The Trout Fishing in America duo is made up of 5-foot-5 Keith Grimwood and 6-foot-9 Ezra Idlet. They accompany their vocals on electric bass and acoustic and 6- and 12-string guitars. They have performed together since 1979, trundling around the country in their red truck with a repertory of rhythm and blues, folk, classic rock, jazz and children's songs that appeal to adults.

Afternoon admission is $10, evening tickets, $15. The cafe is at 62 Route 22, and the phone number is 855-1300.

CANDY WORKSHOP

Patience, delicacy and manual dexterity are required to make the candied leaves and flowers that will be the subject of a one-hour workshop at the John Jay Homestead in Katonah on Thursday at 3 P.M. and repeated on Saturday at 1 P.M.

The edible plants are preserved with vegetable gums and coated with crystallized colored sugar to produce this traditional, delicious confection. Once made, the candies have a long shelf life.

The fee of $15 includes materials. Call 232-5651 to reserve a place or obtain more information. The homestead is between Katonah and Bedford at 400 Route 22. Take Exit 6 off I-684, go east on Route 35 for a fourth of a mile, then south on Route 22 for two and a half miles. ELEANOR CHARLES
