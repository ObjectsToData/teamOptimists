NEWS SUMMARY


Published: June 1, 2001

INTERNATIONAL A3-10

Key Senator to Go Slow On Bush's Antimissile Plan

The next chairman of the Senate Armed Services Committee, Carl Levin, Democrat of Michigan, said it was unlikely that missile defenses would be fielded in President Bush's current term and should not be deployed at all until tests proved their effectiveness. He predicted that diplomatic battles over the Antiballistic Missile Treaty and technological hurdles meant ''the odds are against'' deployment by the end of 2004. A1

Big Office Romance in Mexico

An unprecedented relationship between President Vicente Fox and his spokeswoman, Martha Sahagún, who has openly spoken of her love for the divorced Mexican leader, has become the subject of incessant debate, and political experts have raised questions of propriety. A3

Safeguarding Religious Sites

The General Assembly condemned all attacks on religious sites, and called on governments and international groups to take responsibility for the protection of religious and cultural treasures. It is the first concrete result of a yearlong ''dialogue among civilizations'' under way at the United Nations. A3

World Briefing A10

OBITUARIES C15

Faisal Husseini

The senior Palestinian official in Jerusalem was 60. His death from a heart attack prompted an outpouring of grief from Palestinians and from Israeli doves who considered him a man of integrity and a vital interlocutor. A6

Arlene Francis

The good-natured television personality who was a fixture on ''What's My Line?'' for 25 years was 93. C15

Helen Oakley Dance

A journalist and historian of jazz and blues, as well as a record producer, music publisher and personal assistant to musicians like Duke Ellington, Red Norvo, Chick Webb, Johnny Hodges, Bob Crosby and Earl Hines, she was 88. C15

NATIONAL A12-17

Texas Acting to Retool Its Criminal Justice System

Texas, which leads the nation in executions, is moving to overhaul its criminal justice laws in ways that supporters say will create a fairer system of capital punishment. The legislation includes measures dealing with legal representation for indigents and DNA testing. But the governor, a Republican, is undecided about a bill barring the execution of mentally retarded inmates. A1

McVeigh Seeks a Stay

Lawyers for Timothy J. McVeigh asked the federal judge who sentenced him to death for the Oklahoma City bombing to stay his execution on June 11, saying Mr. McVeigh wanted to hold the government responsible for its failure to produce thousands of pages of evidence at his trial. A1

Backwoods Impasse in Idaho

Six siblings, three boys and three girls, ages 8 to 16, held law enforcement officials at bay outside their home in Sagle, Idaho, after their mother was arrested on charges of child abuse. Officials described the children as armed and scared. A1

Spy Suspect Pleads Not Guilty

Robert P. Hanssen, a former F.B.I. agent accused of spying for Moscow for more than 15 years, pleaded not guilty to espionage charges. A14

Expedited Visas, for a Price

The I.N.S. will start charging foreign scientists, athletes, corporate executives and other special workers an extra $1,000 to speed the processing of temporary work visas. A12

New Leader for Endowment

President Bush will replace William R. Ferris as head of the National Endowment for the Humanities with an expert on Italian Renaissance art, Bruce Cole of the Hope School of Fine Arts at Indiana University. A14

Bush Daughters Charged

The police in Austin charged President Bush's 19-year-old twin daughters, Barbara and Jenna, with misdemeanor violations of Texas liquor law involving an incident at a restaurant Tuesday night. The White House urged reporters not to delve too deeply into family matters. A15

NEW YORK/REGION B1-11

Vallone Officially Joins The Race for Mayor

Peter F. Vallone, the City Council speaker, became the third major Democratic candidate to officially enter the mayoral race, saying he was the only candidate with proven experience running the city. B1

No Progress Seen on Budget

Governor Pataki warned that with the budget already two months late the impasse in Albany might lead to no budget at all, raising the possibility of the government's running indefinitely on emergency spending measures. B1

Recommendations on Profiling

The State Senate panel investigating racial profiling recommended that New Jersey become the first state to outlaw certain kinds of vehicle searches by state troopers and the first to subject troopers to the scrutiny of an outside review board. B1

SCIENCE/HEALTH

AIDS and Gay Black Men

Young gay black men are becoming infected with H.I.V. at the rate of almost 15 percent a year, federal researchers said, calling the rate alarming and urging expanded prevention efforts. The research relies on a new blood test to identify people who have recently been infected. A1

A Titan Among Titans

Paleontologists digging in Egypt have found huge fossil bones of what they say appears to have been the second-most massive dinosaur ever, Paralititan stromeri, whose length and weight could have been as much as 80 feet and 70 tons. A6

WEEKEND E1-28; E29-38

SPORTS D1-8

Devils Trail Colorado

The visiting Avalanche defeated the Devils, 3-1, to take a 2-1 lead in the four-of-seven-game Stanley Cup finals. Game 4 will be tomorrow night, also at Continental Arena. D1

BUSINESS DAY C1-16

Firestone-Ford Dispute Grows

Bridgestone/Firestone asked the Transportation Department for a safety investigation of the Ford Explorer, contending that the sport utility vehicle's design makes it prone to rollovers when a tire fails. Ford and Firestone have been bitterly arguing over whether tires or design are responsible for a rash of rollovers. C1

Shake-Up Atop Harper's

Kate Betts, two years into a three-year contract, was asked to resign as editor in chief of Harper's Bazaar. Glenda Bailey, the editor in chief of Marie Claire, will replace her. Both publications are Hearst magazines. C1

New Report on Xerox Numbers

Xerox, whose KPMG auditors had refused to sign off on the company's annual report until they had rechecked the numbers, said some accounting discrepancies had been turned up but no signs of fraud. C2

Bargain Hunting Lifts Stocks

The Dow rose 39.30 points, to 10,911.94; the Nasdaq climbed 25.99 points, to 2,110.49; and the S.& P. 500 rose 7.74 points, to 1,255.82. Technology stocks provided much of the gains, a day after a sell-off. C8
