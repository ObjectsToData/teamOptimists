BENEFITS


Published: March 25, 2001

Party at the Tea Room

THURSDAY -- A party at the Russian Tea Room given by the Young Women's Committee at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine will benefit research into cancer vaccines at the college's Comprehensive Cancer Center. The event begins at 7 p.m. with cocktails and hors d'oeuvres. Tickets, $110, from (718) 430-3804, or $120 at the door.

Merengue on 42nd Street

THURSDAY -- Olga Tañón, who recently won a Grammy Award for best merengue album, will perform at a black-tie dinner for the Leukemia and Lymphoma Society at Cipriani 42nd Street, 110 East 42nd Street (Park Avenue). Proceeds of the event go to the organization's patient service, advocacy and public education programs. Dr. Max Gomez, a medical reporter from WNBC-TV, will be the master of ceremonies. Drinks at 6:30, followed by dinner at 7 and the performance. Tickets, $500, from (212) 744-0110.

Re-establishing Communication

THURSDAY -- The National Aphasia Association, which provides patient information and sponsors conferences on aphasia, an isolating speech disorder, will benefit from a dinner and operatic performance at the Yale Club of New York, 50 Vanderbilt Avenue (43rd Street). Jan Curtis, the mezzo-soprano, will sing selections from Gershwin, Bernstein and others. The event begins with a cocktail reception at 6 p.m., followed at 7 by the performance and then dinner. Tickets, $275, from (212) 255-4329.

Aiding Those With AIDS

APRIL 2 -- A one-night-only revival of ''Elegies for Angels, Punks and Raging Queens'' at 7:30 in the Haft Theater at the Fashion Institute of Technology, 122 West 27th Street (Seventh Avenue), will raise money for the social service and advocacy programs of Momentum AIDS Project. The organization provides meals and counseling in churches and synagogues in Manhattan, the Bronx, Queens and Brooklyn. Tickets, $50, from (212) 691-8100, ext. 358.

All-Stars at Lincoln Center

APRIL 2 -- More than 70 young singers and dancers will perform in a show at the New York State Theater to raise money for the All Stars Project, which provides leadership training and helps high school students learn to express themselves through talent shows and theater productions. The black-tie evening begins at 6 with drinks. Dinner at 7, followed by the show at 8:15. Tickets, $1,000, from (212) 941-9400.

Rome to New York

APRIL 2 -- A black-tie dinner for the American Academy in Rome will support fellowships and study at the academy, where artists and scholars research art and the humanities. The evening at Cipriani 42nd Street, 110 East 42nd Street (Park Avenue), begins at 7:30 with drinks, with dinner at 8:15. David M. Childs, chairman of Skidmore, Owings & Merrill, the architects, will be honored, and the architect and designer Michael Graves will be among those paying tribute to Mr. Childs. Tickets, $500, from (212) 751-7200, ext. 27.

A Lawyer in the House

APRIL 2 -- The Legal Action Center and its Arthur Liman Policy Institute will have its annual fund-raising cocktail reception at Empire, a hotel at 44 West 63rd Street (Broadway). The organization, which does research, provides education and fights discrimination against people with histories of alcohol and drug addiction, criminal records and H.I.V. or AIDS, will honor Charles S. Dutton, the actor. The evening begins at 5 with drinks and hors d'oeuvres with the honoree, with the ceremony to begin at 6. Tickets, $1,000, or $150 for the award ceremony alone, from (212) 243-1313, ext. 29.

Transforming Through Dots

APRIL 3 -- Stacy Horn, founder of Echo, a New York-based online service, will be among the dot-com company founders who will be on hand at St. Luke's School, 487 Hudson Street (Christopher Street), to discuss how the Internet is transforming communities. Proceeds of the event, the last part of a lecture series, go to scholarships and other programs at St. Luke's School. The evening begins with coffee at 7, with the panel to begin at 7:30. Tickets, $20, from (212) 924-5960.

For Children at Bellevue

APRIL 3 -- Pediatric programs, including an early literacy program that provides free books to children as well as readings and other activities, at the Bellevue Hospital Center will benefit from a party at Windows on the World. The evening begins with wine and hors d'oeuvres at 7:30, with a live auction at 8:45 that will feature a ride on a hot-air balloon, a weekend stay at a Vermont bed and breakfast inn, and other items. Tickets, $150, from (212) 562-4124.

Help From Blue Hill

APRIL 3 -- A cocktail party and a performance of Gilbert and Sullivan's ''Patience'' by the Blue Hill Troupe, at El Museo del Barrio, 1230 Fifth Avenue (104th Street), will raise money for Urban Pathways and its vocational programs for the homeless. The evening will begin with cocktails and hors d'oeuvres at 5:30. The performance begins at 8, and dessert will be served between the musical's acts. Tickets, $200, or $40 for the show alone, from (212) 736-7385. Other performances of the show, at the museum through April 7, will also benefit Urban Pathways.

For Opera Upstate

APRIL 3 -- Members of the Glimmerglass Opera will perform at a dinner and dance at the University Club, 1 West 54th Street (Fifth Avenue), to raise money for the organization's summer apprenticeships for young singers and educational and outreach programs for schoolchildren throughout New York State. The black-tie evening begins with drinks at 7 and the performances at 7:30. Dinner and dancing to the music of the Peter Duchin Orchestra will follow at 8:30. Tickets, $500, from (212) 452-2676.
