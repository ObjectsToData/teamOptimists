SPARE TIMES


Published: July 13, 2001

ATTRACTIONS

Museums and Sites

AMERICAN MUSEUM OF NATURAL HISTORY, Central Park West and 79th Street. The Arthur Ross Terrace, a new one-acre public space with ginkgo and pagoda trees, has opened, with the same hours as the museum. ''The Genomic Revolution'' examines the genome, genetics, developments involving molecular biology and the impact on modern science, technology, natural history, biodiversity and life; through Jan. 1. ''Discovery Room,'' the newest permanent exhibition, is a learning space featuring interactive and hands-on technology. ''Ancient Microworlds,'' more than 40 color photographs by Giraud Foster and Norman Barker; through Sept. 9. Imax films include, through today, ''Ocean Oasis,'' and beginning tomorrow, ''Bears,'' along with continuing screenings of ''Shackleton's Antarctic Adventure'' and ''Lost Worlds: Life in the Balance.'' Call for screening information. Separate admission for Imax films: $15; $11 for students and 60+; $9 for children under 12. In the Imax Theater, Friday and Saturday nights at 9, ''Laser Flashback: The Psychedelic 3-D Laser Show''; at 10, ''Classic Rock in 3-D.'' Laser show tickets: $12, which includes admission to the rest of the museum. Museum hours: Sundays through Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 5:45 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 8:45 p.m. Admission (suggested donation): $10 for adults; $7.50 for students and 65+; $6 for children. Combination tickets are available. Information: (212) 769-5100; advance tickets: (212) 769-5200.

AMERICAN MUSEUM OF NATURAL HISTORY'S ROSE CENTER FOR EARTH AND SPACE, Central Park West at 81st Street. The Hayden Planetarium includes the Space Theater, presenting ''Passport to the Universe,'' and the Big Bang Theater, featuring narrated visual and audio effects simulating the beginning of the universe. The Cullman Hall, a 7,000-square-foot permanent exhibition hall on the bottom level, illustrates processes that led to the creation of the planets, stars and galaxies. ''Starry Nights: Fridays Under the Sphere,'' with cash bar and tapas; Ku-Umba Frank Lacy Quintet, through July 27, from 6 to 8 p.m. Hours: Sundays through Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 5:45 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays to 8:45 p.m. Space shows run all day. Suggested admission to the museum, which includes the Rose Center: $10; students and 60+, $7.50; children 2 to 12, $6; under 2, free. Admission to museum, Rose Center and Hayden Planetarium space show: $19; students and 60+, $14; children 2 to 12, $11.50; under 2, free. Information: (212) 769-5100. Reservations: (212) 769-5200.

HISTORIC RICHMONDTOWN, Staten Island Historical Society, 441 Clarke Avenue, Richmondtown. More than 25 buildings from the late 17th to the 19th century, restored and furnished. This village and outdoor museum depict the history of Staten Island and the surrounding region. Tomorrow from 5 to 9 p.m., a traditional dinner, with reservations required, $40; members, $35. The exhibition ''Toys!'' runs through December. Guided tours available. Hours: Wednesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays 1 to 5 p.m. Admission: $4; 62+, children 6 to 18 and students, $2.50; 5 and under, free. Information: (718) 351-1611.

INTREPID SEA-AIR-SPACE MUSEUM, Pier 86, West 46th Street at the Hudson River, Clinton. The Intrepid is a converted World War II aircraft carrier with two full decks of displays, including four theme halls: United States Navy Hall, Pioneer Hall, Technologies Hall and Intrepid Hall. ''Prepare to Dive: The 100th Anniversary of the Navy's First Submarine,'' an exhibition about the pioneering days of the submarine; ''Mission Control: From the Space Race to the Space Shuttle,'' an interactive exhibition exploring the Mercury, Gemini, Apollo and shuttle programs; continuing. Tomorrow and Sunday on the flight deck from 10 a.m. to 7 p.m., NHL Breakout 2001, the world's largest amateur off-ice hockey tournament for all ages, featuring a variety of NHL and hockey-related events, activities and entertainment for players and spectators; games are scheduled every 30 minutes, with each game consisting of three eight-minute periods. ''Korea: The Forgotten War, Remembered,'' through September. Admission: $12; 65+, veterans, reservists and ages 12 to 17, $9; ages 6 to 11, $6; ages 2 to 5, $2; under 2, free; wheelchair patrons, half price. Hours: weekdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m., with last ticket sold at 4 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, 10 a.m. to 7 p.m., with last ticket sold at 6 p.m. Information: (212) 245-0072.

MERCHANT'S HOUSE MUSEUM, 29 East Fourth Street, East Village. Sunday at 1 p.m., a walking tour of the Bond Street neighborhood, concluding with a guided tour of the museum; admission, $12, which includes a guided tour of the house. A self-guided tour is available on weekdays. Hours: Thursdays through Mondays, 1 to 5 p.m. Tours on Saturdays and Sundays. Admission: $5; students and 62+, $3; members and children under 12, free. Information: (212) 777-1089.

MOUNT VERNON HOTEL MUSEUM AND GARDEN, formerly the Abigail Adams Smith Museum, 421 East 61st Street, Manhattan. Built in 1799 as the carriage house for Abigail Adams and Col. William Smith, it was the Mount Vernon Hotel in the 1830's. Tuesday night at 6, Linda Russell and Companie perform popular 19th-century period music in the gardens, dressed in period clothing; $6, $4 for children; free for members. Museum admission: $6; $4 for members and children under 12. Museum hours: guided tours every half-hour, beginning at 11 a.m.; last tour is at 3:30 p.m. Wednesdays through Sundays, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Tuesdays until 9 p.m. in June and July only; closed Mondays. Admission: $4; students and 62+, $3; ages 12 and under, free. Information: (212) 838-6878.
