    Protests in Syrian Cities as War Rages Elsewhere

    By ROBERT MACKEY
    March 14, 2012 2:35 pmMarch 14, 2012 5:07 pm
    Video said to show Syrian students ducking for cover as the security forces opened fire at a demonstration on Wednesday in Aleppo, Syria’s second-largest city.

    Updated | 5:03 p.m. As my colleagues Anne Barnard and Rick Gladstone report, after seizing most of the northern city of Idlib on Tuesday, Syrian government forces moved on Wednesday to assert control of the southern city of Dara’a, where the uprising against President Bashar al-Assad began a year ago, activists said.

    As the fighting raged in other parts of the country, activists pointed to video that appeared to show protests by lawyers and students in Aleppo, Syria’s second-largest city and an important commercial center.

    According to Shakeeb Al-Jabri, a Syrian in Beirut who reports on the uprising for Al-Ayyam, an activist news site, video uploaded to YouTube on Wednesday showed protesters at Aleppo University’s pharmacy school chanting, “All Syrians put your hands up and say, ‘We don’t want Bashar!’”

    The Syrian government’s ban on independent reporting in the country makes it impossible to confirm the authenticity of the video posted online by activists, but the tens of thousands of clips posted online since the uprising began on March 15 last year do frequently match the descriptions of events offered by witnesses.

    Al-Ayyam reported that pro-government militiamen, known as shabiha, or “thugs,” assaulted the student protesters. One video clip of a protest march that was streamed live to Bambuser, a video broadcasting service, from a phone appeared to be punctuated by the sound of gunfire, prompting a panicked retreat.

    The activist news site added:

    The situation escalated when the students regrouped to continue their protest at the Faculty of Humanities. Armed government forces, reportedly the notorious Air Force Intelligence, arrived on the scene to back up the shabiha. They used live ammunition to disperse the protest and then laid siege to the campus, trapping all students inside. Activists report that the siege was preventing them from evacuating three students with critical gunshot wounds.

    Video uploaded to YouTube by activists appeared to show protesters at the university running as gunshots were fired.

    Activists said another video clip, also showing a protest march disturbed by gunfire, was recorded in Aleppo’s Aazamiya neighborhood, where high school students had gathered and were attacked by the security forces.

    Mr. Jabri, the activist in Beirut, said a group called the Free Lawyers issued a statement during a protest at a judicial center in Aleppo on Wednesday, in which they paid tribute to two detained colleagues and demanded that the government “reveal their fate.”

    Video that Syrian activists said was recorded on Wednesday in the Syrian city of Aleppo during a protest of the government by lawyers.

    The statement, which was recorded on video being read aloud at the protest, also condemned the “daily campaign of random arrests, forced disappearances and killing that is being practiced against the people of Syria,” according to a translation by Al-Ayyam.

    Later on Wednesday, Mr. Jabri pointed to a live video stream of a very loud opposition rally in the Damascus suburb of Douma.

    That rally apparently followed a funeral in Douma for an opposition supporter who was killed the day before, according to the Activists News Association, a Syrian group run from Cairo by the British-Syrian exile Rami Jarrah, who blogs as Alexander Page.

    The ANA Facebook page identified the dead man as Mahmoud Mohammad Saab, 28, and said he was “brutally killed yesterday by regime forces.” Video of the funeral posted on YouTube showed a large crowd of mourners.

    Video posted online by activists in other parts of the country appeared to offer brief glimpses of the Syrian military’s ongoing operation to crush dissent.

    The Web site Enduring America, which offers running coverage of the uprising each day, notes that one video clip, posted on an activist YouTube channel on Wednesday, is said to show tanks and soldiers in Dara’a.

    Another video clip, also apparently shot surreptitiously, is said to show Syrian government forces looting a district of the city of Homs they recently regained control of after intense fighting.

    Two Associated Press journalists, Rodrigo Abd and Ahmed Bahaddou, witnessed the rebel Free Syrian Army’s deteriorating grasp on Idlib earlier this week, during a brief reporting trip to the city. Mr. Bahaddou, a video journalist based in Turkey, captured dramatic images of the rebel fighters struggling to hold off advancing government forces on Sunday.

    Video of rebel fighters in the Syrian city of Idlib on Sunday, shot by Ahmed Bahaddou, a video journalist on assignment for The Associated Press.

    In an article on their experiences filed late Tuesday, Mr. Abd wrote:

    We coordinated our escape with the Free Syrian Army, the rebel force fighting to hold onto Idlib, but the situation was deteriorating quickly. The snipers, shelling and explosions were growing ever closer.

    “We are all going to be killed!” a terrified Syrian activist told me, collapsing into tears. An F.S.A. fighter said the government troops were sure to take the city back, because the rebels were running out of ammunition.

    A rebel commander said he understood if his fighters wanted to run away and save themselves. “Whoever wants to leave and not fight, lay your Kalashnikovs here,” he said. Nobody did.

    According to Fadi Mqayed, a Syrian-German activist, video recorded earlier this month and uploaded to YouTube on Wednesday showed Free Syrian Army fighters destroying a Russian-made Syrian Air Force fighter jet by driving to the outskirts of an air base near Idlib and firing a Russian-made Kornet anti-tank rocket at it.
