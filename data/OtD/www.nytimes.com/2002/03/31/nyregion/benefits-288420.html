BENEFITS


Published: March 31, 2002

Black Tie and Baseball

WEDNESDAY -- The new exhibition on baseball at the American Museum of Natural History will be on display at a dinner-dance to raise money for the museum and its education programs. The black-tie evening begins at 7:30 with cocktails, with dinner at 9 and dancing to the music of the Stingers. Tickets, $300, from (212) 769-5165.

An Auction at the Waldorf

THURSDAY -- Diana Krall and Sir Elton John will perform at a black-tie evening at the Waldorf-Astoria to raise money for the Breast Cancer Research Foundation. Paula Zahn is to be mistress of ceremonies. The evening begins with cocktails at 7, with dinner at 8 and the concert to follow. An auction will include a trip for two to Anguilla, a mink coat and other items. Tickets, $2,000, or $500 for dessert, Champagne and the concert alone at 9, from (866) 346-3228.

Starting Out in Opera

THURSDAY -- Summer apprenticeships for young singers and other educational programs of the Glimmerglass Opera will be supported at a black-tie dinner at the Waldorf-Astoria. Peter Duchin will be honored, as will Katherine Cary, a benefactor of the opera. Cocktails at 7 will be followed by dinner at 8, with performances by opera artists and dancing to the music of the Peter Duchin Orchestra. Tickets, $600, from (212) 787-4316.

Uplifting Life's Quality

THURSDAY -- A cocktail party at the W. Graham Arader III Gallery, 1016 Madison (78th Street), will raise money for the efforts of Civitas to improve the quality of life in New York, particularly on the Upper East Side and in East Harlem. Selected prints showing scenes of historic New York will be on display and for sale at the event, which begins at 6 with drinks and hors d'oeuvres. Tickets, $100, from (212) 996-0745.

Put on Your Tartan

FRIDAY -- A black-tie and full Highland dress ball at the Waldorf-Astoria will aid Gilda's Club Worldwide and Marie Curie Cancer Care in Britain, which provides in-home nursing services for patients, supports research and provides education and training for health-care workers. The 36 bagpipe players that make up the ScottishPower Pipe Band will perform, as will a Celtic harpist. Drinks at 7 and dinner at 8. Tickets, $1,000, from (917) 305-1200, ext. 230.

Gardens in Motion

FRIDAY -- A cocktail party at Spa Nightclub, 76 East 13th Street (Broadway), will benefit Earth Celebration's efforts to preserve community gardens and develop ecological awareness in New York. Drinks, hors d'oeuvres and performances by a belly dancer, Circle of Soul, and dancers garbed in costumes to invoke the elements, the seasons and garden creatures , will begin at 7, with dancing to the music of D. J. Shakey at 8:30. Tickets, $75, or $15 for dancing alone, from (212) 777-7969.

For College Scholarships

APRIL 8 -- College and vocational training scholarships supported by the Stanley M. Isaacs Neighborhood Center will benefit from a party at Tin Lizzy, 1647 Second Avenue (85th Street). The evening begins at 6 with cocktails, a buffet dinner and a raffle for prizes, among them an X-Box, hotel stays and dinners for two in New York. Tickets, $20, from (212) 360-7620, ext. 24.

See the New Symphony Space

APRIL 8 -- A series of performances at the newly renovated Peter Norton Symphony Space, 2537 Broadway (95th Street), including dance, live music and readings by Cynthia Nixon, Leonard Nimoy and Frank McCourt, will raise money for Symphony Space's education and performance programs. The evening begins at 6:30 with Champagne, hors d'oeuvres and the performances. At 8:30, Mr. McCourt and Ms. Nixon will lead a toast to the organization. Tickets, $200, from (646) 505-5175.

Bargains for the Opera

APRIL 8 -- An evening of hunting for bargains in clothing, accessories and furniture, at Casa Italiana of Columbia University, 1161 Amsterdam Avenue (117th Street), will raise money for the opera and educational programs supported by the New York City Opera Thrift Shop. Cocktails and shopping begin at 6, with dinner at 8. Tickets, $500, or $300 for shopping alone, from (212) 243-7300.

Peanut Butter, Jelly and Help

APRIL 8 -- Cookie decorating, edible art projects, storytelling and other activities for children will be featured at a party at Dylan's Candy Bar, 1011 Third Avenue (60th Street), to aid the children's social service programs of Lenox Hill Neighborhood House. The party begins at 5 with wine, hors d'oeuvres, hamburgers, hot dogs, peanut butter and jelly sandwiches and juice for children. Tickets, $100 for adults and $50 for children 15 and younger, from (212) 744-5022, ext. 1355.

From Cipriani to Rome

APRIL 8 -- A black-tie dinner at Cipriani 42nd Street, 110 East 42nd Street, will raise money for the fellowships in arts and the humanities of the American Academy in Rome. The evening begins at 7:30 with cocktails, with dinner at 8:15. Tickets, $500, from (212) 751-7200, ext. 27.

Encouraging Young Leaders

APRIL 8 -- The All Stars Project's leadership development and talent shows for teenagers, to encourage self-esteem and develop leaders, will be supported at a black-tie dinner at the New York State Theater at Lincoln Center. Dominic Chianese will be among those honored, and youths from the organization's programs will perform. Cocktails at 6 will be followed by dinner at 7, with the performances at 8:30. Tickets, $1,000, from (212) 941-9400.

Miscast? Hardly

APRIL 8 -- Christine Ebersole, Philip Seymour Hoffman, Patti LuPone and others will perform in ''Miscast,'' a musical revue, at the Supper Club, 240 West 47th Street, to raise money for Manhattan Class Company Theater. The evening begins with cocktails at 6:30, with dinner at 7:30 and the show at 9. Tickets, $250, from (212) 727-7722.

Fighting Substance Abuse

APRIL 9 -- Jamie Lee Curtis and Larry King will be the hosts of a black-tie dinner at the Waldorf-Astoria to raise money for the National Center on Addiction and Substance Abuse at Columbia University. Peter R. Dolan, chairman of Bristol-Myers Squibb, and Douglas N. Daft, chairman of Coca-Cola, will be honored. The evening begins at 6:30 with drinks, with dinner at 7:30. Tickets, $500, from (212) 627-1000.

An Auction and a Helping Hand

APRIL 9 -- A dinner at the Pierre will raise money for Federation Employment and Guidance Services, which provides programs for people with disabilities, guidance for new immigrants and other services. Cocktails at 5:45, then dinner at 7, with a live auction at 8:30 that will include a trip for two on a barge in the Burgundy region of France, tickets to the Yankees' fantasy camp and other items. Tickets, $500, from (212) 366-8551.
