SPARE TIMES


Published: July 12, 2002

ATTRACTIONS

Museums and Sites

AMERICAN MUSEUM OF THE MOVING IMAGE, 36-01 35th Avenue, at 36th Street, Astoria, Queens. One of the largest collections of moving-image artifacts. On display: ''Robert De Niro: Costume and Character,'' with more than two dozen costumes. ''The Nonconformist: Jack Nicholson on Film, 1970-1980,'' with ''Chinatown'' (1974), directed by Roman Polanski, tomorrow at 2 p.m.; ''The Last Detail'' (1973), directed by Hal Ashby, tomorrow at 4:30 p.m.; ''One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest'' (1975), directed by Milos Forman, Sunday at 2 p.m.; and ''The Fortune'' (1975), directed by Mike Nichols, Sunday at 4:30 p.m. Tomorrow and Sunday at 6:30 p.m., ''Arabian Nights'' (Italy/France, 1974), directed by Pier Paolo Pasolini. Museum hours: Tuesdays through Fridays, noon to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.; and by appointment. Film and video screenings are free with admission unless otherwise noted. Museum admission: $8.50; 65+ and students, $5.50; 5 to 18, $4.50; 4 and under, free. Information: (718) 784-0077.

MUSEUM OF TELEVISION AND RADIO, 25 West 52nd Street, Manhattan. A collection of taped radio and television broadcasts and exhibitions in other media, including drawings, posters and photographs. ''David Bowie: Sound and Vision,'' a retrospective of the singer's career, with music videos and rare film of performances, documentaries and interviews; through Sept. 15. ''Cartoon POWER!!, with a Powerpuff Girls' marathon. Museum hours: Tuesdays through Sundays, noon to 6 p.m.; Thursdays to 8 p.m.; Fridays to 9 p.m. Admission: $6; students and 62+, $4; 12 and under, $3. Information: (212) 621-6800.

Gardens

BROOKLYN BOTANIC GARDEN, 1000 Washington Avenue, Crown Heights. ''The Liberty Oaks,'' a new planting of scarlet oaks honoring the victims and heroes of the terror attacks of Sept. 11. The Butterfly and Hummingbird Trail, a self-guided tour, tomorrow through Aug. 31. Grounds are open Tuesdays through Fridays, 8 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Conservatory hours: 10 a.m to 5 p.m. when the garden is open. Suggested admission: $3; $1.50 for students and 65+ (free for 65+ on Fridays); free for children 15 and under; free for all on Tuesdays, except holidays; free from 10 a.m. to noon on Saturdays. The conservatory closes 30 minutes before the grounds. Information: (718) 623-7200.

NEW YORK BOTANICAL GARDEN, Bronx River Parkway at Fordham Road, Bedford Park, the Bronx. ''Sculpture From the Museum of Modern Art at the New York Botanical Garden,'' 15 pieces of sculpture on view within the courtyard of the Conservatory through Aug. 31, 2003. ''Victorian Ornamentals,'' with displays of coleus, cannas, and other plants; through Oct. 27. Special Summer Storytelling Series in the Everett Children's Adventure Garden, tomorrow at 2 p.m., featuring the Brothers Grinn. Hours: Tuesdays through Sundays and Monday holidays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. The Everett Children's Adventure Garden is open Tuesdays through Fridays, 1 to 6 p.m.; Saturdays, Sundays and Monday holidays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; $3; $2 for 62+ and students; $1 for children 2 to 12; free for children under 2. Garden grounds admission: $3; $2 for 62+ and students; $1 for children 2 to 12. Garden Passport: $10; $7.50 for 62+ and students; $4 for children 2 to 12. Conservatory admission: $5; $4 for students and 62+; $3 for those 2 to 12; under 2, free. Grounds admission free on Wednesdays and 10 a.m. to noon on Saturdays. Information: (718) 817-8700 or www.nybg.org.

STATEN ISLAND BOTANICAL GARDEN, 1000 Richmond Terrace, Livingston. The garden covers 83 acres. Hours: daily, 8 a.m. to dusk. Free admission to the grounds. ''Bones of Mountains: Kemin Hu's Scholars' Rock Collection,'' an exhibition of 100 rocks that have been associated with the scholars of China; through July 28. ''China's Changing Culture and Enduring Heritage,'' a display of more than 50 photographs on loan from the Chinese Consul that depict sites like the Great Wall, the Imperial Palace of the Ming and Qin Dynasties and the Classical Gardens of Suzhou; through Sept. 29. The one-acre New York Chinese Scholars' Garden is open Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Connie Gretz's Secret Garden, a walled garden with a formal maze, is open Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. (Fridays till 8 p.m.) Admission: $5; $4 for 62+ and children under 12. Admission to the Secret Garden: $2; children, $1; adults with a child, free. Information: (718) 273-8200.

WAVE HILL, West 249th Street and Independence Avenue, Riverdale, the Bronx. Gardens, greenhouses and woodlands are part of this 28-acre garden and cultural institution. ''Roll out the Landscape,'' a workshop in which participants sketch impressions of Wave Hill onto a paper movie scroll. Tomorrow and Sunday, 1 to 4 p.m., in the Kerlin Learning Center. ''The Grafter's Shack,'' a site-specific art installation about beekeeping in contemporary culture, opens Sunday at 1 p.m., with a beekeeping lecture/demonstration at 2 p.m. ''Garden Bracelet,'' a workshop in making bracelets using glass flowers and leaf beads, tomorrow, 1 to 4 p.m. Materials fee: $15; $10 for members. Tuesdays through Sundays, 9 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. Admission: $4; $2 for students; free for members and children under 6. Free for all on Tuesdays and Saturdays from 9 a.m. to noon. Information: (718) 549-3200.
