WESTCHESTER GUIDE

By ELEANOR CHARLES
Published: September 5, 1993

KENNEL CLUB DOG SHOW

Picture it: More than 2,300 dogs, all purebred, putting their best paws forward at Lyndhurst in Tarrytown next weekend in the 76th Annual Westchester Kennel Club Dog Show.

The event, held in conjunction with the Tuxedo Park Kennel Club, accepts dogs of all breeds that are registered with the American Kennel Club. Recent past winners were a German shepherd, a Doberman, and last year an English springer spaniel named Ch. Salilyn's Condor.

Tents are available for grooming, but the show ring is out in the open, rain or shine. Hours are from 8 A.M. to 5 P.M. Friday and Saturday, and 8 A.M. to around 7 P.M. Sunday. Trophies are awarded to winners in numerous categories, including individual breeds and herding, sporting and non-sporting dogs. There is a top prize for best-in-show.

Competitors tend to be familiar breeds, although the American Kennel Club periodically recognizes new breeds for eligibility. Recent additions to the list are the Shar-Pei, a Chinese dog that is best described as a bundle of wrinkles, and the hairless Chinese crested, so named because of the single wisp of hair on its head.

Admission for spectators is $5, or $3 for children younger than 12. Entry fees for dogs are $22 a dog for one category and $10 for each additional category. Lyndhurst is at 635 South Broadway. The phone number is 631-0046; the Westchester Kennel Club number is (212) 425-2320. ARTS AUCTION

One hundred artists calling themselves the "Hudson River School Today" will each put one work up for auction on Sept. 18 to raise money for the Valley Artists Association, a nonprofit organization in Peekskill.

The paintings and sculptures can be previewed on Saturday and on Sept. 17 and 18 from 10 A.M. to 3:30 P.M. at Paramount East, at 1016 Brown Street in Peekskill, where the auction will be held on Sept. 18 from 3:30 to 5:30 P.M.

Among the artists from the Westchester area contributing their work are Larry D'Amico, Sergio Gonzalez-Tornero, Hananiah Harari, Bohdan Oszycka, Deborah Hyatt and Bernie Kessler. Many styles and techniques will be offered on the Hudson Valley theme. The event has been organized by the association and J.P. Fine Arts in Peekskill, and the auctioneer will be Neil Vaughn of Cold Spring Galleries.

Sept. 18 will be a busy day in Peekskill. In addition to the auction, the Peekskill Arts and Crafts Festival will be in progress, along with the Third Annual Peekskill Farmers' Market, and more than 40 artists studios will hold open house. Call 739-6337 for more information. VETTED ANTIQUES SHOW

For the seventh year the Antiques Dealers' Association of America will bring its yearly antiques show back to the County Center in White Plains on Saturday from 11 A.M. to 7 P.M. and on Sunday from noon to 5 P.M.

Forty-five dealers from nine states will participate, focusing on 18th- and early-19th-century Americana. Formal and country furniture, textiles, glass, metal, pottery, fine and folk art, marine and scientific instruments, decoys, Shaker furniture, Oriental rugs, Art Deco items from jewelry to furniture and painted furniture are among the items to be found.

Documentation is carefully monitored for this show. Each year every piece is examined for authenticity by a committee of experts. Only members of the antiques association may enter the show, and then only after their wares have been vetted by the committee.

For the first time the event will be conducted as a benefit, with part of the proceeds aiding the new Fund for the Humanities, established by the Westchester Arts Council. The council provides grants to the county's various historical societies, sites and agencies for educational programs, exhibitions and other purposes.

There are 23 historical groups participating in the program, including Historic Hudson Valley, the Hudson River Museum, the Katonah Historical Museum, Lyndhurst, the Westchester County Historical Society, the Westchester Preservation League and the historical societies of 16 individual municipalities.

Admission to the show is $8. The County Center is at the junction of Route 119 and the Bronx River Parkway. For more information, call 285-4050. GRANGE FAIR

Westchester's rural past can be visited at the Yorktown Grange Fair, to be held for a 69th year at Rochambeau Park in Yorktown Heights.

Preview nights are Wednesday and Thursday from 6 to 10 P.M., when 1,000 4-H exhibits, plus amusement rides and food vendors will be open, and admission is free. The 4-H division in the county is open to anyone 8 through 18, and competitions range from engines to creative writing, child development, crafts, fashion, science, livestock, horticulture, carpentry and many other categories.

The complete fair, including the 4-H division, will be open on Friday and Saturday from 10 A.M. to 11 P.M. and Sunday from 10 A.M. to 9 P.M. Admission will be $3 for adults, $1.50 for children 12 and younger. People 65 and older will be charged $1.50 on Friday only.

Entries in several divisions of flower, vegetable and fruit will vie for prizes but may only be bought at an auction on Sunday at 7 P.M. Poultry, pigeons, waterfowl and livestock will compete for awards, along with needlework and other crafts. An entry fee of $2 is required only for rabbits, and there is usually a wide variety of breeds, including angoras, chinchillas, lops, and Flemish giants.

Baked goods, jellies, jams and pickles can be bought throughout the weekend, and variety shows for children, featuring magicians, acrobats, and dog acts are scheduled on Friday at 2 and 5:30 P.M., Saturday at 1 and 6 P.M., and Sunday at 1 and 5:30 P.M. Food vendors will be plentiful all weekend.

The park is on Moseman Road. Call 962-3900 for more information. STARTING A BUSINESS

The nonprofit Entrepreneurial Center, which has offices in Purchase, Manhattan and Rockland County, will offer a workshop titled "So You Want to Start a Business?" on Saturday from 8:30 A.M. to 4:30 P.M. at Reid Hall, Manhattanville College in Purchase. The cost is $89.

A comprehensive 10-week course called "What Every Entrepreneur Needs to Know" will begin at Manhattanville on Sept. 20 and continue each Monday from 6 to 9 P.M. Twenty topics will be covered in the 30 hours and certification is available. The fee will be $525.

Instructors conducting the programs are themselves successful entrepreneurs, and those along with other courses are designed for new and established small-business owners. Payment plans are available and credit cards are accepted. For more information, call 694-4947.
