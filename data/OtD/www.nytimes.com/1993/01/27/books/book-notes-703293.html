Book Notes

By Esther B. Fein
Published: January 27, 1993

Republicans' Memoirs

Robert Barnett seems to have become the fashionable literary agent for alumni of the departed Bush Administration.

In December, Mr. Barnett, a partner in the Washington law firm Williams & Connolly, began peddling the memoirs of James A. Baker 3d to New York publishers. At about the same time, Mr. Barnett started representing Mary Matalin, the deputy manager of the Bush Presidential campaign, and her Democratic archrival and paramour, James Carville, a chief strategist for the Clinton campaign, as they shop around a "he said, she said" literary look back at the 1992 campaign.

Mr. Barnett's growing list of political clients was further augmented by Marlin Fitzwater, former President George Bush's White House spokesman, and Lynne V. Cheney, who was chairwoman of the National Endowment of the Humanities during Bush's tenure.

Topping off the Barnett list of authorial politicians is former Vice President Dan Quayle, who has agreed to write his memoirs for HarperCollins, Mr. Barnett and William M. Shinker, publisher of the HarperCollins adult trade group, said yesterday. Mr. Shinker said Mr. Quayle would be working with a collaborator, who has not yet been selected.

The manuscript is to be delivered by the end of the year for publication in May or June 1994, Mr. Shinker said. The Christian Market

Cryptically describing the terms of the contract, Mr. Shinker said that HarperCollins had acquired world rights, exclusive of film rights, and that "elements of the deal make it a seven-figure deal." Other publishers and agents said such elements could include the author's advance, payment to the collaborator, bonuses for becoming a best seller and promotional guarantees.

An important element in Mr. Quayle's choosing HarperCollins, Mr. Barnett said, was the publisher's agreement to market the book not only through its regular commercial division but also through Zondervan, its religious publishing subsidiary. HarperCollins has used this strategy successfully with several other authors who the publisher believed could cross over between the Christian and mainstream markets. Among the successes were Tom Landry, the former coach of the Dallas Cowboys, and, most recently, Oliver North. Their books spent weeks on the New York Times best-seller list.

"A regular trade publisher cannot penetrate the Christian book market," Mr. Shinker said. "Christian booksellers are skeptical of books published by mainstream houses, even though, as in the case of the Quayle book, they may have an appropriate message for their readers. But it's a huge market, and with Zondervan, we have access to it." Questioning the Questioners

The book is to include an account of the Bush years, the Quayle family's religious faith and the Murphy Brown flap. In a turn of the tables, Mr. Shinker said, Mr. Quayle hopes to interview major journalists who covered and often lambasted him in his four years as Vice President.

Mr. Barnett first came to know the Quayle family after representing Mr. Quayle's wife, Marilyn, who, with her older sister, Nancy T. Northcott, wrote "Embrace the Serpent" (Crown), a thriller novel about Washington that most reviewers found extremely unthrilling.

Given his client list, Mr. Barnett is a clear contender to represent the literary fortunes of Mr. Bush, who is said to be considering writing his own memoirs.

But Mr. Shinker said that even though he would be interested in Mr. Bush's memoirs, "the Quayle book is more interesting to me."

"I think Dan Quayle has a unique perspective," he said. "It's a more unconventional book." No More Jovanovich

On March 26, Doug Brinkley's book "The Majic Bus: An American Odyssey" will become a footnote in publishing history as the first book published under the new Harcourt Brace & Company name and logo.

General Cinema, which bought Harcourt Brace Jovanovich in November 1991, has decided to "simplify and modernize the corporate identity" of the publishing house.

"Quite honestly, Jovanovich is a long name and very difficult to pronounce," said Peter Farwell, vice president for corporate relations for General Cinema. "The whole name was so long and cumbersome, and our studies showed that most people used to just say Harcourt, or Harcourt Brace, or H. B. J. But virtually nobody said the whole long name, at least not properly." (Pronounced properly, it's joh-VAHN-oh-vitch.)

The decision to jettison the Jovanovich name was made easier by the departure from the company last April of Peter Jovanovich, whose father, William, was president of the publishing house from 1954 until he unexpectedly resigned in 1990 and whose family name has been part of the company's since 1970. Peter Jovanovich is now the president and chief executive of the Macmillan/McGraw-Hill School Publishing Company, which publishes textbooks.

Mr. Farwell said General Cinema was, in fact, returning the publishing house's historical name, since the company was founded in New York in 1919 by Alfred Harcourt and Donald Brace and was then called Harcourt Brace. At some point, he said, it was called Harcourt Brace and World, but, he noted, given the publisher's long history, the name Harcourt Brace Jovanovich is really of recent vintage and not historically interesting "or practical."

Mr. Brinkley's book, the first scheduled to bear the new logo, is the true, first-person account of a Hofstra University professor who took his history students on the road to teach them firsthand about history and current events. End Notes

*G. P. Putnam's Sons has bought a biography of Audrey Hepburn, to be written by Barry Paris. Phyllis Grann, the chairman and chief executive of the Putnam Berkley Group, said yesterday that the as yet untitled manuscript was to be delivered next year and published as a Berkley paperback one year after the hard-cover edition comes out.

*Pocket Books has acquired world publishing rights to a second nonfiction book by Rush H. Limbaugh 3d, the politically conservative television and radio talk show host, whose book "The Way Things Ought to Be" has been a surprise runaway best seller for the publisher. The second book, tentatively titled "See, I Told You So," is being rushed to print for the next Christmas book season.
