    Shriver Will Speak at N.Y.U.'s 132d Commencement Today

    JUNE 10, 1964

    Inside
      Continue reading the main story
      Continue reading the main story
      Continue reading the main story Share This Page
      Continue reading the main story
      Continue reading the main story

      Sargent Shriver will deliver the address at New York Uni­versity’s 132d annual com­mencement this morning.

      The Peace Corps director will receive an honorary degree of Doctor of Laws during the exercises at the University Heights campus.

      Dr. James M. Hester, the uni­versity’s president, will also confer honorary degrees on U Thant, the Secretary General of the United Nations; Lord Har­lech, British Ambassador to the United States; James J. Rori­mer, director of the Metropoli­tan Museum of Art; Lawrence J. McGinley, former president of Fordham University; Judge Bernard Botein, presiding jus­tice of the Appellate Division of the State Supreme Court; and Warren Weaver, vice president of the Alfred P. Sloane Founda­tion.

      The university will award 3,323 advanced degrees and 3,230 baccalaureate degrees. About 12,000 persons are ex­pected at the exercises, which begin at 10:30.

      Trustees in New Gowns

      The ceremonies will start with the academic procession down the mall of the campus quadrangle.

      In the procession, the trustees of the university will have a double responsibility, part ac­ademic and part sartorial. They will have to escort the seven nominees for honorary degrees and model the university's new academic gowns.

      The violet robe has three vel­vet stripes on the sleeves and twin black velvet panels in front. The doctoral cap is eight‐skied.

      At the end of the exercises, the senior member of the fac­ulty, Professor Andre Beau­mont, will present a symbolic torch of learning to the young­est degree recipient, Miss Mar­garet Ekstrom, 19 years old, who is graduating from the Washington Square College.
