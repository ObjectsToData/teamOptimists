    GRADUATION IS SET AT CITY COLLEGE

    JUNE 17, 1964

    Inside
      Continue reading the main story
      Continue reading the main story
      Continue reading the main story Share This Page
      Continue reading the main story
      Continue reading the main story

      City College will confer un­dergraduate and graduate de­grees on 3,864 students at its 118th commencement exercises tonight in Lewisohn Stadium. The graduating class is the largest in the college's history.

      Dr. Buell G. Gallagher, pres­ident of City College, will de­liver the commencement ad­dress. The ceremonies will be­gin at 8 P.M. with the tradi­tional march along Convent Avenue into the stadium. Dr. Gustave G. Rosenberg, chair­man of the Board of Higher Education, will also speak.

      This year's graduating class will include 3,068 candidates for the baccalaureate degree. 647 candidates for the master's degree, and 149 candidates for associate certificates signifying completion of two years of study in arts or applied science. science.

      Among the graduates will be 12 students of the College's School of Engineering and Architecture who will receive the college's first Bachelor of Architecture degree. The five­year architecture program is conducted under the curriculum introduced at the college in 1961.

      63 Degrees to Policemen

      Sixty‐three members of the class are New York City police­men who will receive degrees of Associate in Applied Science, Bachelor in Business Adminis­tration or Master in Public Ad­ministration under a program administered jointly by City College's Baruch School and the Police Academy.

      Then graduates will receive their bachelor's degrees summa cum laude—with highest hon­ors. They are: Thalia A. Pan­diri, Frederick M. Cooper, Ethan J. Schreier, Charles W. Brooks, Leslie J. Freeman, David Charles Kirby, Robert A. Pad­gug, Ronald L. Lipsman, Fred­ric M. Pollack and Jack Wax­man.

      Seventy members will be graduating magna cum laude­with high honors, while 172 will receive their degrees cum laude —with honors.

      Three members of the faculty will be awarded bronze plaques in honor of 40 years of service to the college. They are Dr. Harold H. Abelson, dean of the School of Education; Prof. Wil­liam L. Finkel, chairman of the speech department; and Prof. Herbert P. Wirth of the mathe­matics department.

      Tomorrow If It Rains

      If it rains, the commencement will be held tomorrow night. Lewisohn Stadium is at 138th Street, between Amsterdam and Convent Avenues.
