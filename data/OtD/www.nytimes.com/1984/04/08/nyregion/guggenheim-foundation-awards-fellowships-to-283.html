GUGGENHEIM FOUNDATION AWARDS FELLOWSHIPS TO 283

Published: April 8, 1984

The John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation has awarded one- year fellowships to 283 scholars, artists and scientists who were chosen from 3,542 applicants in its 60th annual competition.

The grants, totaling $5,520,000 and averaging $19,500, are intended to provide one year's free time, although each recipient may spend the money any way he chooses, according to Gordon N. Ray, the foundation's president.

''Once we decide who we want, he has total freedom,'' Mr. Ray said. ''I find these days that a frequent use is to buy a word processor.''

This year's Guggenheim Fellows will be working on subjects as diverse as ''the aerodynamics of wind pollination,'' ''disorder, development and decolonization in Africa, 1935-1950'' and ''a biographical memoir of John Cheever'' by his daughter, Susan.

The foundation accepts applications in any subject except the performing arts, Mr. Ray said, because of the difficulty of holding auditions.

The fellows are chosen by a panel of seven experts, who meet each February for three days to consider the applications, Mr. Ray said. During the month before the meeting, the panelists this year reviewed a 19-foot stack of applications.

''The fellowships are awarded on the basis of demonstrated accomplishment in the past and strong promise for the future,'' he said.
