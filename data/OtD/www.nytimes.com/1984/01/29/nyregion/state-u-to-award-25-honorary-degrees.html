STATE U. TO AWARD 25 HONORARY DEGREES

Published: January 29, 1984

The State University's board of trustees has announced that it will bestow honorary degrees on 25 people for ''excellence and extraordinary achievement.''

The degrees, which will be awarded in ceremonies at 11 State University campuses beginning April 6, were approved by the trustees last week. Each degree will be conferred by a trustee or an official designated by the board.

The first award will be given at a university-wide ceremony at the State University Plaza in Albany to Dr. Ernest L. Boyer. Dr. Boyer, who will receive an honorary Doctor of Humane Letters degree, is a former United States Commissioner of Education. He was chancellor of the State University from 1971 to 1977 and is now president of the Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching.
