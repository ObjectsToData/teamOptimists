THE GUIDE

By Compiled by Barbara Delatiner
Published: October 22, 2000

Best Bet

Now that it has become a ''sport,'' courtesy of the International Olympic Committee, ballroom dancing could appeal to new audiences. Dance fans or sports enthusiasts may want to find their way to the Landmark on Main Street in Port Washington on Saturday at 8 p.m. for ''Feat First: Ballroom Dance, Olympic Style.'' With Pierre Dulaine, the founding director of the American Ballroom Theater, and a master-class format, three prize-winning dance duos will preview the swing, Latin and ballroom dancing that might be included in the 2004 or 2008 Olympics (516) 767-6444.

Film Previews

Featuring films that sometimes have not been shown at your neighborhood multiplex, the Great Neck Arts Center opens this year's ''Cinematheque'' series with ''The Yards,'' the James Caan-Mark Wahlberg-Charlize Theron drama set in New York that has been acclaimed on the film festival circuit and opened in general release last week. Sol Negrin, the cinematographer, speaks at the screening at 7:30 p.m. Thursday at the Clearview Squire Cinema on Middle Neck Road (516) 829-2570.

Keep Talking

Three women of note rule the talk circuit this week. In free appearances the author Joyce Carol Oates reads from her works at 1 p.m. Tuesday at College Center of Nassau Community College in Garden City (516) 572-7153, while the actress Rita Moreno discusses ''The Lively Arts'' at 12:40 p.m. Wednesday at the Staller Center on the Stony Brook campus of the State University (631) 632-2787. On Thursday, the journalist Linda Ellerbee talks about ''Surviving Breast Cancer and Beyond'' at 7 p.m. at the Westhampton Beach Performing Arts Center on Main Street in the final installment of Central Suffolk Hospital's ''Women's Wellness'' talks (631) 548-6080.

All That Jazz

Jazz legends make the local scene this week: Dave Brubeck performs at 8 p.m. on Friday at Tilles Center on the C. W. Post campus of Long Island University in Brookville (516) 299-3100, while Herbie Mann and Sona Terra play at 8 p.m. on Saturday at Staller Center on the Stony Brook campus of the State University (631) 631-2787.

Currier and Ives

When it comes to popular art, the output of the lithographer Nathaniel Currier and the self-trained artist James Merritt Ives is in a class all its own. So the Long Island Museum of American Art, History and Carriages has a potential coup with ''Currier and Ives, Printmakers to the American People,'' an exhibition of 72 works drawn from the Museum of the City of New York's Harry T. Peters Collection. The show opens Saturday and runs through Jan. 21 at the Long Island museum's complex on Route 25A in Stony Brook (631) 751-0066.

Good for a Laugh

Four programs this week should provide at least a few laughs. Today at 3 p.m., for example, Jackie Mason brings his kind of humor to the Westbury Music Fair on Brushollow Road. That's also where Bill Cosby performs at 8 p.m. on Friday (516) 334-0800. On Saturday at 8 p.m., Chicago City Limits offers its improvisational comedy at the Van Nostrand Theater on the Brentwood campus of Suffolk Community College (631) 851-6589, and Wali Collins and Deb Margolin, comedians, join forces in ''Laugh Out Loud! Comedy Night at the Parrish'' at 8 p.m. at the Parrish Art Museum on Job's Lane in Southampton (631) 283-2118.

Happy Halloween

While most of the week's programming marking Halloween has a familiar ring -- you know, plays about witches and walks through spooky sites -- two entries on Saturday break the mold.

In the season's first ''EAB Family Concert,'' the Long Island Philharmonic offers ''Pumpkinfest Carnival -- A Halloween Extravaganza'' featuring music from ''Phantom of the Opera'' and Dvorak's ''Carnival Overture.'' Preceded by a costume parade and contest at 1 p.m., the concert, conducted by one of the finalists for music director, David Wiley, is at 3 p.m. at Tilles Center (631) 293-2222.

At 8 p.m. in Manhasset -- and for adults -- Jon Spelman, a storyteller, offers ''Frankenstein: The Beauty of the Beast.'' It's a theater piece told from the point of the view of the monster. Accompanied by Andy Teirstein, a composer-musician, Mr. Spelman appears at the Unitarian Universalist Congregation at Shelter Rock on Shelter Rock Road in Manhasset (516) 627-6560.

Puppy Love

With two shows on the boards, today is a must-see for puppy lovers. In Massapequa at the American Legion Hall on Veteran's Boulevard, the Long Island Bulldog Club has a ''Bulldog Puppy Match'' for anyone who owns a bulldog puppy or just wants to see and learn more about the breed. There will be a handling demonstration at 11 a.m. and judging starting at 1 p.m. (516) 689-6245. Meanwhile, at Old Westbury Gardens, the Ladies Kennel Club ends its two-day ''Puppy Match'' in which more than 60 breeds are competing for honors from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. (516) 333-0048.

Chamber Music Reigns

With a concert almost every day, chamber music again is where it's at musically on the Island this week. Today there are two: the Miro String Quartet plays works by Bartok, Mendelssohn and Mozart at 4 p.m. at the Bay Street Theater in Sag Harbor (631) 725-9500, while Ridotto, the concert series with a ''touch of theater,'' offers ''Music and the Rose,'' at 4 p.m. at Old First Church on Route 25A in Huntington, with early Baroque music performed by Jolle Greenleaf, soprano; and Philip Anderson, tenor; and lute, strings and harpsichord (516) 385-0373.
