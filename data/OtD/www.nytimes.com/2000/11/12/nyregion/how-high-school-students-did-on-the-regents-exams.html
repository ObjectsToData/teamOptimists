How High School Students Did on the Regents Exams

By Kate Zernike
Published: November 12, 2000

For the first time since it required tougher English tests for high school graduation, the State Board of Regents has released scores that capture the progress of an entire class.

The data presented here are for New York City students who entered ninth grade for the first time in fall 1996. To reflect that many students did not make it to senior year in the typical four years, the state released the data both for the entire group - whose general education enrollment is tabulated below - and for the smaller group of students who were seniors last year.

The state also released data on all students who took and passed the Regents math exam, which became a condition of graduation for those entering in fall 1997 (this year's senior class).

In both the English and math exams, a passing score is 55 out of 100. Beginning with students who entered in fall 2000, the passing grade on the English exam will be 65. The passing grade on the math exam will rise to 65 for those entering ninth grade in fall 2001. KATE ZERNIKE
