PLAYING IN THE NEIGHBORHOOD

By ANDREA DELBANCO
Published: July 30, 2000

CITYWIDE

Philharmonic Begins Tour of the Boroughs

The New York Philharmonic will start its second summer tour of the boroughs this week, beginning Tuesday in Central Park. Mikko Franck will conduct the overture to ''Candide'' by Bernstein, ''An American in Paris'' by Gershwin, the ''Firebird Suite'' by Stravinsky, and a trumpet concerto by Arutunian, with Philip Smith on trumpet. Later in the week, the Philharmonic will repeat the program in Brooklyn, the Bronx and Staten Island (all dates subject to the demands of mosquito spraying). Fireworks are to follow each show.

Queens will get its due as the Queens Symphony Orchestra performs twice this week with ''Three Italian Tenors,'' Francisco Casanova, Peter Riberi and Craig Siriani, and Melinda Katz, a mezzo-soprano.

New York Philharmonic, Central Park Great Lawn, Tuesday; Prospect Park, Brooklyn, Wednesday; Van Cortlandt Park, Bronx, Thursday; all 8 p.m.; free; for more information and rain dates, (212) 875-5709; Queens Symphony Orchestra, St. John's University Great Lawn, 8000 Utopia Parkway, south of Union Turnpike; Thursday 7 p.m.; Seuffert Band Shell, Forest Park, Forest Park Drive off Woodhaven Boulevard; next Sunday 4 p.m; free, for more information and rain locations, (718) 786-8880.

LOWER MANHATTAN

Artists Whose Medium Is Puppets

''Sticks, Rags and Strings: The Art of Contemporary Puppetry'' displays the work of four artists at the World Financial Center. One, Dan Hurlin, has created a miniature proscenium animated by paper cutout characters of ketchup bottles and dishes. In contrast to the miniatures, super-size puppets and masks created by Ralph Lee will also be on display.

Jonathan Edward Cross has created small sets peopled by strange creatures made of found objects from his Lower East Side neighborhood. African-influenced masks and body puppets carved from a variety of flexible rubber pieces created by Garland Farwell will also surround the scene. These masks and body puppets, shown at left, were created in collaboration with Julio Leitao of the Harlem dance company Batoto Yetu.

''Sticks, Rags and Strings,'' through Sept. 21; Tuesdays through Thursdays 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. World Financial Center Courtyard Gallery, 225 Vesey Street; free; (212) 528-2733.

PROSPECT HEIGHTS

Forgotten Objects, Rediscovered

The damage wasn't a result of last week's rainstorms: the little house pictured at right is a construction by a Brooklyn artist, Robert Kalka. Titled ''Forget Everything,'' it is a diorama of a house under construction enclosed in plexiglass, left to decay and saturated with water vapor to stimulate the destruction. Models of trees grow up through the unfinished frame. The construction is on view at the Brooklyn Public Library through Aug. 28.

Mr. Kalka, who will give an artist's talk on Saturday afternoon, has been fascinated since childhood with rediscovering lost or forgotten objects.

''In museums,'' Mr. Kalka said, ''ordinary objects become more interesting exactly because of traces of time, such as mold, that give them a meaningful context. Libraries create the same sense of treasure.''

''Forget Everything'' through Aug. 28, Monday through Thursday, 9 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Friday and Saturday, 9 a.m. to 6 p.m.; artist's talk Saturday 2 p.m.; Lobby Gallery at the Central Library, Grand Army Plaza, Brooklyn; free; (718) 230-2122.

ALSO AROUND TOWN

HARBOR TOUR -- From the decks of the Staten Island Ferry, Anne McDonnell, a Staten Island Institute of Arts and Sciences museum educator, will lead two harbor tours. They will include Governors Island, Ellis Island, the Statue of Liberty and Robbin's Reef Lighthouse and a visit to the institute's Ferry Collection at the St. George Ferry Terminal; Friday and Aug. 18, 2 p.m.; meet at 1:45 p.m. at Visit Center of the Council on the Arts and Humanities of Staten Island in Whitehall Terminal in Manhattan; $5; (718) 727-1135, ext. 39.

SWINGING ALONG -- The musical ''Swing Dance America'' celebrates the art form from its roots in the 1920's through today; the choreographer is Robert Royston, a dancer in the Broadway musical ''Swing''; Saturday 8 p.m., before starting a 28-city tour; Williamson Theater at the Center for the Arts at the College of Staten Island, 2800 Victory Boulevard, off Staten Island Expressway; $20; (718) 982-2787.

WAR READINGS -- Readings of the works of World War II journalists at the Intrepid Sea Air Space Museum will continues the Parks Department's ''Great New York Writers in Great New York Places'' series; Wednesday 6 p.m.; Pier 86, West 46th Street at 12th Avenue; $25; (212) 360-1378. ANDREA DELBANCO
