THE GUIDE

By BARBARA DELATINER
Published: February 20, 2000

Best Bet

The avant-garde comes to Brookville Friday with Martha Clarke's ''Vers La Flamme (Toward the Flame).'' Danced by a company of seven, the theater piece is based on Chekov stories set to music by Rachmaninoff and Scriabin performed by Christopher O'Riley, pianist. The work, the final Tilles Center New Century Commission, will be performed at 8 p.m. at the theater on the C. W. Post campus of Long Island University (516) 299-3100.

Choral Weekend

If practice makes perfect, the 100 singers from all over the Eastern Seaboard who perform today at 4 p.m. at St. Peter's Episcopal Church in Bay Shore should do well. They've been working on this ''Canticles and Classics'' concert since Friday, when they signed in for a ''Choral Festival Weekend.'' The program, conducted by Suzanne Peck, includes John Rutter's ''Gloria,'' Britten's ''Festival Te Deum'' and anthems by Blow, Elgar and Vaughan Williams. The church is on South Country Road and admission is by free will offering (631) 665-0051.

Black History Month

Observances of Black History Month on the Island are in full swing this week. All things musical begin in a major way today when Doudou N'Diaye Rose and His Drummers of West Africa, a troupe of 35 from Dakar, Senegal, appear at 7 p.m. at Staller Center on the Stony Brook campus of the State University (631) 632-2787. Other programs today feature Kobla Mensa Dente's ''Talking Drums'' at 2:30 p.m. at the Hempstead Library on Nichols Court (516) 481-6990, and the J. J. Evans Specials, a gospel choir from the Christian Light Missionary Baptist Church, at 2 p.m. at the Long Beach Library on West Park Avenue (516) 432-7201. On Friday, the Long Island Voices Gospel Choir performs at 7:30 p.m. at the Student Activities Center on the Stony Brook campus of the State University (631) 444-2341. Finally, on Saturday, Leon Bates, pianist, pays a ''Tribute to Duke Ellington'' at 8 p.m. at St. John the Baptist High School on Montauk Highway in West Islip (631) 665-4350.

Today, the Cinema Arts Center on Park Avenue in Huntington opens ''Home and the World: Six Films From Africa and America,'' with ''Faraw! Mother of the Dunes,'' a prize-winning 1997 drama from Mali, at 10:30 a.m., in the ''Sunday Schmooze'' series (it will be repeated tomorrow at 7:40 p.m.), and ''Pieces D'Identite,'' a 1998 modern-day fable from the Republic of Congo and Belgium, at 1 p.m. Other entries are ''Le Grand Blanc De Lambarene (Dr. Albert Schweitzer),'' from France, Gabon and Cameroon, next Sunday and March 1; ''Journey of the Lion'' from Jamaica, next Sunday; ''Blind Faith,'' an American drama, March 5; and ''Paul Robeson: Here I Stand,'' Feb. 29 (631) 423-3456.

The spoken word also enters into the equation with lectures, poetry readings and a one-man play ''Jim Beckwourth,'' with Michael Broughton as the former slave turned frontiersman-explorer, at 3 p.m. Saturday at the Freeport Library on South Ocean Avenue (516) 379-3274. On Wednesday, Prof. Krin Gabbard, chairman of the department of comparative literature, discusses ''Goin' Up: Duke Ellington's Significance on Hollywood,'' at 7 p.m. at the Melville Library on the Stony Brook campus of the State University (631) 632-6748. Thursday brings Raymond Patterson to Humanities Hall on the C. W. Post campus of Long Island University in Brookville to read from his ''Thirteen Ways of Looking at a Black Man'' and ''Elemental Blues'' at 11 a.m. (516) 299-2391. On Saturday, in Orient at the Old Point Schoolhouse on Village Lane, Stephen Mrozowski, anthropologist and a co-leader of the excavation of Sylvester Manor grounds on Shelter Island, talks about ''Uncovering the Secrets of Northern Slave Life'' at 2 p.m. (631) 323-2480.

Chinese Gardens

Gardeners can take in two slide lectures devoted to what grows where and how in China. Today at 2 p.m. at the Planting Fields' Burns Horticultural Center in Oyster Bay, Thomas J. Nieman, professor of landscape architecture at the University of Kentucky, discusses ''The Gardens of China,'' at a meeting of the Long Island Horticultural Society (516) 922-9200. On Saturday at 10:15 a.m., the Bayard Cutting Arboretum Horticultural Society hears about ''Gardening in China'' from Caroline Kiang of the Suffolk County Cornell Cooperative Extension at the Oakdale arboretum's Manor House (631) 589-8680.

School Break

With youngsters home from school on their winter break, local museums, parks and playhouses are expanding their schedules this week. The Airport Playhouse on Knickerbocker Avenue in Bohemia offers ''Cinderella'' from tomorrow through Friday. Call the theater for schedules (631) 589-7588. In Lynbrook, the Fantasy Playhouse on Merrick Road performs ''Sleeping Beauty'' at 1 and 3:30 p.m. tomorrow through Friday and also at 11 a.m. Wednesday and Thursday (516) 599-1982. Theater Three, Main Street in Port Jefferson, offers ''The Golden Goose or The Tale of the Simple Son, Simpleton'' at 11 a.m. and 2 p.m. Wednesday through Saturday (631) 928-9100.

Nature activities include a ''School Vacation Program''' Tuesday through Thursday at the Cold Spring Harbor Fish Hatchery and Aquarium on Route 25A (631) 692-6768; ''Native American Games and Legends'' and ''Fossil Identification Days,'' both Tuesday through Friday, at Garvies Point Museum and Preserve in Glen Cove (516) 571-8010. Then, the Huntington Audubon Society holds a Bird Walk for children and their adult companions at 9:30 a.m. Wednesday, meeting at the Gerard Street Parking Lot (631) 757-3668. There are also special programs tomorrow through Friday at the Science Museum of Long Island on Leeds Pond Preserve in Manhasset (516) 627-9400; nature workshops from 9:30 to 11 a.m. Wednesday through Saturday at Connetquot River State Park in Oakdale (631) 581-1072; a ''Winter Nature Camp'' Tuesday through Friday at Theodore Roosevelt Sanctuary in Oyster Bay (516) 922-3200; and a ''Nature Craft Day'' at 10 a.m. Friday at Smith State Park in Smithtown (631) 265-1054.

Other cultural programs include ''Artistic Voices,'' an art workshop from 11 a.m. to 1 p.m. Thursday at the Heckscher Museum of Art on Prime Avenue in Huntington (631) 351-3250, and ''Family Fun Games,'' at 7 p.m. Tuesday or 2 p.m. Wednesday, and ''Circle Art: Concentric Circles and Their Artistic Patterns,'' at 10:15 a.m. Tuesday at the Goudreau Museum of Mathematics in Art and Science in the Herricks Community Center on Herricks Road in New Hyde Park (516) 747-0777. The East End Community School of the Arts on East Main Street in Riverhead offers music and art workshops tomorrow through Thursday (631) 369-2171, while the Northshore Science and Technology Center on Route 25A in Shoreham has ''Engineers Week'' Tuesday through Thursday at (631) 821-8155.

Free Choices

TODAY -- To add another dimension to its exhibition, ''The Revolutionary War: Founding the New Nation,'' the Nassau Museum of Art, off Northern Boulevard in Roslyn Harbor, offers a ''Revolutionary War Re-Enactment'' today. From noon to 3 p.m., with a battle staged at 1:30 p.m., ''soldiers'' from the 17th Light Dragoon Regiment and the 23rd Regiment of Foote will show what daily life and warfare was like for 18th-century cavalrymen from both Colonial and British forces (516) 484-9337.

WEDNESDAY -- The Avodah Dance Ensemble performs ''Let My People Go,'' a retelling of the Exodus story from African-American and Jewish perspectives, and ''Make a Change,'' with members of the college dance club, at 7 p.m. in the D'Ecclesiis Auditorium at St. Joseph's College in Patchogue (631) 447-3200.

At the Libraries

This week's musical lineup starts today with Momentum playing works by Bach, Beethoven, Mozart and Strauss at 2:30 p.m. at the Islip Library (631) 581-5933; resumes on Thursday when when Musica Dolce offers ''A Colonial Musicale'' at 2:30 p.m. at the Garden City Library (516) 742-8405; continues Friday with ''Music From China'' performed by a trio on traditional instruments at 2 p.m. at the Long Beach Library (516) 432-7201, or Chris Merwin in ''Songs From Far and Near'' at 7 p.m. at North Shore Library in Shoreham (631) 929-4488. On Saturday Opera Pro Voce stages ''Die Fledermaus'' at 8 p.m. at the Plainview-Old Bethpage Library (516) 938-0077. BARBARA DELATINER
