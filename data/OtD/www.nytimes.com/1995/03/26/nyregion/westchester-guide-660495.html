WESTCHESTER GUIDE

By ELEANOR CHARLES
Published: March 26, 1995

2-COUNTY ART EXHIBIT

Eighty-three prestigious works of art have been selected from 32 private collectors in Westchester and Fairfield Counties to make up the exhibition opening today at the Neuberger Museum of Art at Purchase College. "Crossing State Lines," 20th-century art from the contiguous areas, will remain on view through June 18.

The roster of artists reads like a Who's Who in contemporary painting, photography, ceramics, sculpture and other mediums: a mobile by Alexander Calder, an assemblage by Romare Bearden, sculptures by Alberto Giacometti and George Segal, paintings by Fernand Leger and Helen Frankenthaler, glass sculpture by Dale Chihuly and one of Red Grooms's caricaturesque paintings.

Among other familiar artists are Milton Avery, Robert Indiana, Jacob Lawrence, Marisol, Joan Miro, Robert Motherwell, Louise Nevelson, Robert Rauschenberg Frank Stella, and Andy Warhol.

In connection with the exhibition, film shorts on art collectors and their collections will be shown next Sunday from noon to 4 P.M. for $5, and a panel discussion costing $10 on April 27 from 7 to 9 P.M. requires advance reservations. Lucinda H. Gedeon, director of the museum, will moderate the program with the panelists Ralph Lerner, tax lawyer and art-as-investment expert; Harry L. Rinker, authority on art and antique collecting, and Dorothy and Herbert Vogel, collectors of art.

The exhibition may be seen free of charge or for a suggested donation of $4, $2 for children and adults 62 and older, Tuesdays through Fridays from 10 A.M. to 4 P.M. and Saturdays and Sundays from 11 A.M. to 5 P.M. The cafe is open Tuesdays through Fridays from 11:30 A.M. to 2:30 P.M. and Saturday and Sunday from noon to 4 P.M. Call 251-6133 to hear an information tape. KOCH AS KEYNOTER

So -- how's he doin' almost half a dozen years after his 12-year tenure as Mayor of one of the world's most unmanageable cities? Ed Koch will undoubtedly have provocative and informative answers to that and anything else anyone in the room asks him during his appearance at a lunch in the Rye Town Hilton on Friday.

The occasion is the Middle Atlantic Conference of the Building Owners and Managers Association of Westchester County, taking place Friday through Sunday. Hizzoner will be the keynote speaker about 12:45 P.M. on Friday. His talk will be about the future of American cities and the role that commercial real estate will play, but he will invite questions from the audience on any subject.

The development of The Westchester, White Plains's new megamall, and the turnaround in marketing that saved the Financial Center at 50 Main Street, and East Ridge Properties, a portfolio of 18 buildings will be discussed during the conference, and those attending will be taken on two tours -- one to view technical operations and the other concerned with amenity packages and marketing programs at Westchester office buildings.

In addition there will be several round-table discussions on a variety of subjects from cost containment to office cleaning by robots. To obtain more information about any or all of the events and their cost, the number to call is 328-9703. SINGLES SALON

Single people with a literary bent may want to drop in on the first Singles Salon to be held by the Greenburgh Public Library at 300 Tarrytown Road in Elmsford on Friday from 7 to 10 P.M.

Everyone who registers in advance will have a chance to take part in open-mike book talks, in which each person leads a discussion of his or her favorite book of 1994. Kenny Armstrong will provide a background of piano music from 8 to 10 P.M., and Roberta Markowitz, a social worker, will talk about relationships.

Coffee and cake will be served, and the event is free. More information can be obtained by calling 993-1600. MONTREAL PUPPETEERS

"The Dream Catchers" by Henriette Major, author of books and television shows for children in France and Canada, is about a pre-teen-age boy and girl who live out their dream fantasies, some innocent and pleasant, some terrifying and some extremely satisfying, like flying safely through space.

Theatre Sans Fils, the extraordinary puppet theater from Montreal, had adapted the story to its particular discipline and will give a performance on Saturday at 3 P.M. in Purchase College's Performing Arts Center.

Puppets ranging in height from 3 to 12 feet retell the tale, set to original music and punctuated by black light, lasers, fog and fireworks. The figures, inspired by the classic Japanese Bunraku puppets, are manipulated by black-clad members of the troupe whose presence is virtually unnoticeable against a black stage floor and backdrop.

Suitable for children 6 through 12, the show tends to expose their most common fears and then neutralize them. Tickets are $9, $13 and $17 and can be ordered by calling 251-6200. FISH-TANK LIVING

All too often one lovely little tropical fish in the tank dies, then another. The plants turn to mush, and the water refuses to stay clean and clear. Not to worry. Deal Fausel, senior naturalist at the Greenburgh Nature Center, will conduct a program today at 2 P.M. called "Tropical Aquatic Life," demonstrating everything one needs to know about setting up and maintaining a tropical aquarium.

He will indicate the types of tanks, fish, plants, food, pumps, lights, heaters and filters that are manageable for the beginner, and, using the tanks available at the center, he will invite visitors to take part in the demonstrations. Admission is $3. The center is on Dromore Road off Central Avenue. Call 723-3470. HOLOCAUST LITERATURE

Dr. Sheldon Grebstein, president of Purchase College from 1981 to 1993 and now its university professor of literature, will lecture on the "Literature of the Holocaust," the title of a course he teaches. His talk will be presented at the Purchase College Jewish Studies Program Faculty Forum on Thursday at 8 P.M. in the Humanities Building Theater at Purchase College.

His purpose is to explore the question of transforming genocide into art and whether or not the growing body of free-ranging and often highly imaginative work on the subject shocks, enrages, profanes or offers release to the survivors. Admission is free. Call 251-6550 for more information. ELEANOR CHARLES
