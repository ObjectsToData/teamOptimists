MUSIC; Summer Music Festivals Throughout the Land

By James R. Oestreich
Published: May 14, 1989

Whether their taste runs to familiar orchestral chestnuts, gatherings of avant-garde composers, elaborate exercises in early-music performance or traditional jazz, music lovers will find much to savor at the festivals planned in North America this summer.

A few events seem especially newsworthy. The biennial Boston Early Music Festival is back with an impressive array of activities, including a weekend dominated by the conductor Roger Norrington. At the PepsiCo Summerfare in Purchase, N.Y., whose daring schedule - all three of Peter Sellars' stagings of Mozart operas - is overshadowed by the knowledge that this season will be the festival's last, the final event will be a weekend devoted entirely to Beethoven's Ninth Symphony, presided over by Mr. Norrington.

Each summer seems to produce one blockbuster. This year's most sizable event takes place in Toronto in June - a one-time International Choral Festival that looks extraordinarily attractive. ARIZONA FLAGSTAFF FESTIVAL OF THE ARTS July 7-30. Symphonic and pops concerts and chamber-music brunches form part of a multi-arts program on the campus of Northern Arizona University. JAZZ ON THE ROCKS Sedona, Sept. 22-24. The main event, Saturday, in the Posse Grounds outdoor amphitheater, features Cleo Laine and John Dankworth, Bud Shank, the Woody Herman Orchestra under Frank Tiberi, others. SEDONA CHAMBER MUSIC FESTIVAL June 17-25. The Blair Quartet gives weekend concerts with guest artists Angela Cofer, David Krakauer and Thomas Scheeley. ARKANSAS MUSIC FESTIVAL OF ARKANSAS Fayetteville, June 8-25. Orchestral, chamber and jazz concerts. Carlton R. Woods is music director. Donald Erb directs the new Composer's Forum, June 17-21; Daniel Moe, the Choral Institute, June 22-25. Artists-in-residence include James Greeson and Alvin Chow. CALIFORNIA BEETHOVEN FESTIVAL San Francisco, Berkeley, June 15-July 1. The San Francisco Symphony plays mostly Beethoven under Michael Tilson Thomas. The opening concert, repeated once, re-creates a famous Beethoven program, including Symphonies Nos. 5 and 6, the Piano Concerto No. 4 and the ''Choral Fantasy.'' Soloists include Garrick Ohlssohn, who also plays a ''Candlelight Recital'' to help close the series, and Ilana Vered. CABRILLO FESTIVAL Santa Cruz and environs, July 20-30. A broad exploration of contemporary West Coast composers, including, in residence, Henry Brant, David Cope, Lou Harrison, Gordon Mumma and Chinary Ung. Janos Starker and music director Dennis Russell Davies play Beethoven's cello-piano works. Also the Kronos Quartet. CARMEL BACH FESTIVAL July 17-Aug. 6. Along with much Bach, including the Mass in B minor and Cantatas Nos. 21, 33, 34 and 189, the repertoire extends to non-Baroque composers. ''The Abduction From the Seraglio'' is the Saturday matinee attraction. The festival music director Sandor Salgo conducts. Other artists include Beverly Hoch, D'Anna Fortunato and Angel Romero. CHAMBER MUSIC WEST FESTIVAL San Francisco, Berkeley, Saratoga, June 5-21. The San Francisco Conservatory offers nine concerts featuring leading Bay Area musicians plus Robert Helps, Felix Galimir, Michael Webster and the Alexander String Quartet. George Perle is composer-in-residence; Milton Salkind, artistic director. CONCORD PAVILION June 2-Oct. 20. Performers at the outdoor amphitheater in the foothills of Mt. Diablo include Jean-Pierre Rampal, Richard Stoltzman, Diana Ross, Harry Belafonte, Rod Stewart, John Denver, Miles Davis, the Preservation Hall Jazz Band. The Concord Jazz Festival, Aug. 11-19, features Carmen McRae, Sarah Vaughan, Milt Jackson, Art Blakey and the Jazz Mesengers. HOLLYWOOD BOWL SUMMER FESTIVAL Los Angeles, July 11-Aug. 16. Among the visitors are Charles Dutoit and the Montreal Symphony, John Williams and the Boston Pops Esplanade Orchestra, Alicia de Larrocha, Barry Douglas, Vladimir Feltsman, Pinchas Zukerman, Frederica von Stade, Rosemary Clooney, Sarah Vaughan, the Dukes of Dixieland. Weekend fireworks spectaculars. The preview week, July 3-10, features a Disney symphonic extravaganza and two Mozart concerts. LAKE TAHOE SUMMER MUSIC FESTIVAL various lake sites, July 21-Sept. 2. A two-part event: July 21-29, weekend outdoor orchestral concerts and midweek ensemble performances; Aug. 22-Sept. 2, a run of ''The Best Little Whorehouse in Texas'' and a Broadway revue.
