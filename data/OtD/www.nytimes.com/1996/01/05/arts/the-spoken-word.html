The Spoken Word


Published: January 5, 1996

"THE INTERIOR BEETHOVEN: HIS CREATIVE PROCESS," Metropolitan Museum of Art, Fifth Avenue at 82d Street. Discussion among Robert Martin, dean of graduate students and professor of philosophy and music at Bard College; Scott Burnham, assistant professor of music at Princeton University; Lukas Foss, the composer, conductor and pianist, and Richard Kramer, dean of humanities and fine arts and a professor of fine arts and music at the State University of New York at Stony Brook, L.I. Tomorrow at 4:30 P.M. A performance of music by Beethoven follows at 7 P.M., featuring Mr. Foss on piano, Mr. Martin on cello, Krista Bennion-Feeney and Laurie Smukler on violin and Ira Weller on viola. Tickets: $25. Information: (212) 570-3949.

SCHIAPARELLI IN CONTEXT," Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway, at Prospect Park. A gallery talk by Rita Gottfried in conjunction with the exhibition "A Slice of Schiaparelli: Surrealism in Fashion," on display through March 24. Tomorrow at 2 P.M. Free with museum admission: $4; $2 for students; $1.50 for the elderly; free for members and children under 12. Information: (718) 638-5000.

DISCUSSION AND READING, Book Revue, 313 New York Avenue, at Main Street (Route 25A), Huntington, L.I. Colleen McCullough, author of "The Thorn Birds," will discuss her latest novel, "Caesar's Women." Sunday at 2 P.M. Free. Information: (516) 271-1442.

"DAY LILIES FROM JUNE UNTIL FROST," Bridgehampton Community House, Main Street, Bridgehampton, L.I. A slide lecture on day lilies by Dan Trimmer, owner of the Water Mill Daylily Garden. Sunday at 2:30 P.M. Admission: $5. Information: (516) 537-2223.

GALLERY TALKS, Metropolitan Museum of Art, Fifth Avenue at 82d Street. TODAY: "Renaissance Ceramics and Glass," by Marvin Schwartz, 11 A.M.; "A Closer Look: A Haida Mask," by Shirley Glubok, 12:30 P.M.; "Russian Decorative Arts at the Metropolitan Museum of Art," by Marina Nudel, 3 P.M.; "Exploring 20th-Century Art: The Painted City," by John Angeline, 6 P.M.; "Transformations: Woven Patterns for the Body, the Mind and the House in Late Antiquity," by Eunice Dauterman Maguire, a curator at the Krannert Art Museum and an assistant professor at the University of Illinois School of Art and Design, 6 P.M. TOMORROW: "Sculptors' Drawings: 1945-1990," by Deborah Goldberg, 11 A.M.; "Gifts of the Magi," by Elinor Richter, 7 P.M. SUNDAY: "Contemporary Art at the Metropolitan Museum," by Julie Reiss, 11 A.M.; "High Art and Haute Couture," by Richard Martin, 3 P.M.
