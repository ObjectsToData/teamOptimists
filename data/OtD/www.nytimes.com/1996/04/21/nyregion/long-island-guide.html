LONG ISLAND GUIDE

By BARBARA DELATINER
Published: April 21, 1996

FREE CHOICES

TODAY Pretending he is Walt Whitman, Darrel Ford, a local naturalist, reads from the poet's works at 2 P.M. at the Walt Whitman Birthplace on Old Whitman Road in Huntington Station (427-5240).

The library musical scene displays its usual variety, offering everything from jazz, via the Ron Odrich Quintet at 3 P.M. at the Port Washington Library on Library Drive (883-4400) to opera with the Island Lyric Opera performing "La Boheme" at 2:30 P.M. at the North Merrick Library on Meadowbrook Road (378-7474).

Between the two extremes, there is folk music from Dave Sear at 3 P.M. at the Shelter Rock Library, Searingtown Road in Albertson (248-7363); the Long Island Composer's Alliance offering "Shakespeare and Pianos," at 3 P.M. at the Half Hollow Hills Library, Vanderbilt Parkway, Dix Hills (421-4530), and the Cameo Quartet playing pop and classical music at 2 P.M. at the Floral Park Library on Tulip Avenue (326-6330).

TOMORROW Jem Cohen, a video maker, visits the Port Washington Library to screen and discuss his experimental travelogue, "Buried in Light: Central and Eastern Europe in Passing" at 8 P.M. (883-4400).

WEDNESDAY Ellen S. Baker, an astronaut and veteran of three space flights, visits the State University at Stony Brook to speak about her work, show exclusive NASA slides and video footage and answer questions from the audience. The Queens-born physician, whose mother is Claire Schulman, Queens Borough President, will appear at 12:40 P.M. at Staller Center (632-7230).

FRIDAY Approaching the end of its current tour with "On Golden Pond," the Other Vic Theater Company reaches the East Meadow Library on Front Street and performs the drama at 8 P.M. (794-2570).

SATURDAY The Gilbert and Sullivan Light Opera Company of Long Island performs "The Grand Duke" at the Plainview-Old Bethpage Library on Old Country Road in Plainview at 8 P.M. (938-0077).

MUSIC SANCTUARIES

Churches and synagogues on the Island are coming into their own as showcases for non-liturgical music, either as producers of series or merely as hosts for other groups. Today there are six such concerts: Kathryn A. Prinz, flutist, joined by the pianist Christine Dore and the harpist Karen Strauss, play chamber music at 3 P.M. at the Community United Methodist Church on Route 106 in East Norwich (922-0133); the United Methodist Church, Verbena Avenue, Floral Park, is host to a Piano-Ensemble Concert at 3 P.M. (354-5156), and the Garden City Chamber Music Society appears at 3 P.M. at the Unitarian Universalist Congregation, Stewart Avenue in Garden City (248-8855).

Also today, Safam, a folk-rock band specializing in Jewish-American music, performs at 7 P.M. at Temple Beth-El, Old Mill Road, Great Neck (487-0900); Ridotto, with the help of the Arcata String Quartet, explore "The Fine Art of String Playing" at 4 P.M. at Old First Church, Route 25A, Huntington (285-0373), and Serenata, a vocal-instrumental ensemble, performs Jewish music, including a work based on "The Diary of Anne Frank," at 7 P.M. at Central Synagogue of Nassau County, DeMott Avenue, Rockville Centre (766-4300).

On Friday the singers Gail Nelson and Danny Holgate recreate "The Brilliant and Dark Side of Billie Holiday" at 8 P.M. at the Huntington Unitarian Universalist Fellowship, Brown's Road (427- 9547), while on Saturday, the Main Street Singers "Celebrate Spring" at 7 P.M. at the Union United Methodist Church, Pulaski Road in East Northport (261-1303), and Dana Varelli, flutist, joined by a quartet of guest artists, offer "A Touch of Jazz" at the Greenlawn Presbyterian Church, Broadway (261-2150).

DESIGNERS' SHOWCASE

The Gables in North Hills, a red brick mansion built in the 1920's that has been refashioned into seven dwellings under a single slate roof, will be host to the 25th Anniversary edition of the Designers' Showcase. Produced by Mansions and Millionaires Inc., for the benefit of the Family Service Association, and featuring the work of 40 interior designers, the showcase begins today and runs through June 2. The homes will be open from 10:30 A.M. to 4:30 P.M. weekends and 11 A.M. to 5 P.M. weekends. Shuttle buses will transport weekday visitors from parking lots at Morley Park on Searingtown Road and weekend visitors from Lilco headquarters on Willis Avenue (626-7575).

TALK IS FREE

Devotees of the talk circuit have some traveling to do this week to catch up with all the free chats on the Island. For example, today Robert Meeropol, the son of Ethel and Julius Rosenberg, discusses "Realizing the Dream -- Overcoming a Traumatic Childhood and Corporate Law" at noon at Southampton College's Fine Art Theater. He returns tomorrow at 5 P.M. to talk about documents he believes vindicate his parents. Wednesday brings Bill Baird, the abortion rights activist, to the same campus to lecture on "The Politics of God, Government and Sex" at 9 P.M. (287-8306)

Tomorrow, Denise Brown, the sister of Nicole Brown Simpson, visits Nassau Community College's College Union in Garden City to discuss "No Excuse for Abuse: Ending Domestic Violence" at 2:30 P.M. (572-7153). Then, on Tuesday, Susan Isaacs, the noted noveist, reads from her work at 11 A.M. at the Little Theater on the Farmingdale Campus of the State University and gives an informal talk at 2 P.M. at Loft Lounge (420-2031).

FRIDAY JAZZ

"Jazz at Tilles" reappears Friday with the Paul Winter Consort, an ensemble that blends jazz with symphonic and ethnic folk styles. The concert is at 8:30 P.M. at the center on the C. W. Post campus in Brookville (299- 3100). Meanwhile in Setauket, at Neighborhood House on Main Street, the Brookhaven Arts and Humanities Council offers both visual and aural jazz: the sound comes from the Purple Toucan Trio; the sights from Tommy Thompson's watercolors. A reception for the artist, whose works can be seen through May 11, is at 6 P.M.; the cabaret-style concert at 8 P.M. (928-2664).

NATURE CALLS

Planting Fields Arborteum celebrates Arbor Day with a festival from 10 A.M.to 5 P.M. Saturday and next Sunday. There will be free tree seedlings distributed, wildlife shows, plant sales, tours, tree-planting ceremonies and children's nature activities at the State Historic Park in Oyster Bay (922-9200). Out in Southold, courtesy of the North Fork Audubon Society, weekend nature programming centers on "Raptors of Paumanok," a symposium at the local high school on Main Road. This all-day event Saturday and next Sunday features lectures, live bird demonstrations and field trips (477-9413). BARBARA DELATINER
