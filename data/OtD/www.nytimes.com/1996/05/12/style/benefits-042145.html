BENEFITS


Published: May 12, 1996

Roundabout Readings MONDAY -- The New York City School Volunteer Program will benefit from an evening of readings by John Cullum, Tony Randall, Anne Jackson, Audra McDonald, Dominick Dunne and others. The event, at the Roundabout Theater, Broadway at 45th Street, will begin with cocktails and a silent auction at 6:30 P.M. Calvin Trillin will be the master of ceremonies. Tickets, $85, from (212) 213-3370.

Supper and Songs MONDAY -- Carole King and Phoebe Snow will be among those who will perform at a concert to benefit the Blue Light Theater Company. Cocktails at 6 P.M. will be followed by a buffet supper and the show, all at Catch a Rising Star, 253 West 28th Street. Tickets, $200, from (212) 568-1580.

Neutrinos at the Whitney MONDAY -- A party at the Whitney Museum of American Art organized by its young professionals group will raise money for the museum's Lobby Gallery. The evening, from 7:30 to 10:30, will include drinks and dancing to the Flying Neutrinos, a jazz band. Tickets, $115 at the door. Information: (212) 570-3697.

Northern Lights Cabaret MONDAY -- Northern Lights Alternatives, which provides emotional support to women and children with AIDS and H.I.V., will celebrate its 10th anniversary with a dinner, dance and cabaret show at Laura Belle, 120 West 43d Street. The actor Kevin Bacon and his wife, Kyra Sedgewick, will be honored. Harvey Fierstein will be the evening's host. Cocktails at 6:30. Black tie. Tickets, $250, from (212) 557-1535.

A Dance Institute Anniversary MONDAY -- The actor Terrence Mann will be the host of a 20th-anniversary celebration for the National Dance Institute. Students of the institute will give a performance of African-American music and dance at 7 P.M. at the Kaye Playhouse at Hunter College, 68th Street and Lexington Avenue. Tickets, $100, from (212) 737-3597.

Academy of Design Gala TUESDAY -- Kitty Carlisle Hart will be honored at a black-tie dinner at the National Academy of Design, 1083 Fifth Avenue (89th Street), to raise money for the academy's exhibition and education programs. A cabaret show featuring Mary Cleere Haran at 7:30 P.M. will be followed by cocktails and dinner. Brendan Gill will be the host. Tickets, $500, from (212) 369-4880.

Helping the Blind WEDNESDAY -- Helen Keller Services for the Blind will benefit from a dinner-dance at the Pierre. Cocktails at 7 P.M. Black tie. Tickets, $300, from (212) 285-7029.

Sloan-Kettering's Society THURSDAY -- Barbara Walters will be the host at a dinner in the Winter Garden of the World Financial Center to benefit the Society of Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. The group, which is celebrating its 50th year and whose efforts support cancer research and education at the center, will honor Evelyn H. Lauder, Benno C. Schmidt, Carroll Petrie and other members of Sloan-Kettering's boards. The evening begins with cocktails at 7 P.M., followed by the dinner, a performance by Patti LaBelle and dancing to Peter Duchin and His Orchestra. Black tie. Tickets, $500, from (212) 935-1033.

New York City Ballet THURSDAY -- The New York City Ballet will hold its spring gala benefit at the New York State Theater at Lincoln Center. The black-tie evening will begin with a cocktail reception at 6:15, followed by a performance, dinner and dancing. Wynton Marsalis and His Orchestra will accompany Peter Martins's "Jazz (Six Syncopated Movements)." Tickets, $675, or $150 for the reception and performance alone, from (212) 870-5585.

Anti-Defamation League THURSDAY -- The Anti-Defamation League will honor Jerry W. Levin, Revlon's chairman, at a black-tie benefit at the Waldorf-Astoria. Cocktails at 6:30 P.M., followed by dinner and a performance the New Four Preps, a 50's-style rock band. Tickets, $1,000, from (212) 885-7868.

Dinner With Jose Carreras THURSDAY -- A benefit evening for Carnegie Hall will feature a recital of songs by the tenor Jose Carreras beginning at 8 P.M., followed at 10 by a tapas supper with Mr. Carreras at the Essex House. Tickets, $500, from (212) 903-9650.

An Auction for Hadassah THURSDAY -- An auction of Judaica organized by the New York Chapter of Hadassah will benefit Hadassah's medical programs in the United States and Israel. The event will be held from 6 to 10 P.M. at the New-York Historical Society, 2 West 77th Street, and will include drinks, hors d'oeuvres and dessert. Tickets, $85, from (212) 751-7050, or $100 at the door.

For Museo del Barrio THURSDAY -- The Museo del Barrio, Fifth Avenue at 104th Street, will hold a dinner-dance at the museum, beginning with cocktails at 7 P.M. Luis A. Ferre, who was the governor of Puerto Rico from 1969 to 1972, will be honored. Black tie. Tickets, $300, from (718) 768-1672.

A James Taylor Concert FRIDAY -- James Taylor and Shawn Colvin will perform at the Manhattan Center, 311 West 34th Street, to benefit the Natural Resources Defense Council. Spalding Gray will be the host for the event, which will celebrate the council's 25th anniversary. The evening begins with cocktails at 7 P.M. and will be followed by dinner and the show. Tickets, $500, from (212) 727-4435.

For Operation Smile FRIDAY -- Operation Smile, which provides free reconstructive surgery to children and young adults, will benefit from a scotch and cigar tasting from 7:30 P.M. to midnight at the Yale Club, Vanderbilt Avenue and East 44th Street. Tickets, $85, from (212) 366-6890.

The Griffin Ball SATURDAY -- The John Harms Theater in Englewood, N.J., will hold its annual Griffin Ball at the Marriott at Glenpointe, 100 Frank W. Burr Boulevard in Teaneck, N.J. The event will begin with cocktails at 7 P.M., followed by dinner and dancing. Black tie. Tickets, $225, from (201) 567-5797, extension 17.

Help for Selfhelp NEXT SUNDAY -- Selfhelp Community Services will benefit from a reception and concert at Alice Tully Hall, beginning at 2:30 P.M. Claude Frank and Lilian Kallir, pianists; Eugenia Zukerman, flutist, and members of the St. Luke's Chamber Ensemble will perform. Tickets, $35, $75 and $125, from (212) 971-7621.

Literary Lions Dance MAY 20 -- The New York Public Library is holding a Centennial Literary Lions dance at the main library, now officially named the Center for the Humanities. The evening, from 9 P.M. to 1 A.M., will benefit the research libraries. Dessert will be served. Black tie. Tickets, $100, from (212) 930-0730.

For Circle in the Square MAY 20 -- The Circle in the Square Theater will honor one of its founders, Theodore Mann, as part of its 45th anniversary celebration. A cocktail reception at 6 P.M. will be followed by an auction of film memorabilia and other items and by a musical revue, all at the theater, 1633 Broadway (50th Street). The performers will include Annette Bening, Philip Bosco, Ruby Dee, Elizabeth Ashley, Frank Langella and Blythe Danner. A dinner will follow at 9:15 in the atrium of the Equitable Building, Seventh Avenue at 51st Street. Black tie. Tickets, $500, or $125 for the reception and show alone, from (212) 307-2730.

Studio Museum in Harlem MAY 20 -- Ed Bradley of "60 Minutes" will be the master of ceremonies at a fund-raising dinner at the Sheraton New York for the Studio Museum in Harlem. Lois Mailou Jones, a painter, will be honored. The black-tie evening will begin with cocktails at 6:30, followed by dinner and dancing. Tickets, $350, from (212) 260-1070.

American Cancer Society MAY 22 -- David Koch will be honored at the American Cancer Society's Black and White Ball at the Pierre. Cocktails begin at 7 P.M., followed by dinner and dancing. Black tie. Tickets, $500, from (212) 237-3890.
