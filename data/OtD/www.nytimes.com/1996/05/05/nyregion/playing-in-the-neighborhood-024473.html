PLAYING IN THE NEIGHBORHOOD


Published: May 5, 1996

BEDFORD PARK Youths Tap Out A Broadway Message

Broadway is the theme this year at the fifth annual Tapmania, where 10 high school tap-dancers, known as the Bronx Hoofers, will perform to songs from musical shows including "42d Street" and "Grease." The group practices four to five times a week at the Bronx Dance Theater, whose after-school program was recently cited on the Presidential Committee for the Arts and Humanities' list of 218 exemplary programs for children at risk.

Tapmania will be performed tonight and Saturday at the Bronx Dance Theater's Performing Arts Center.

"The performance is on a professional level," said Neil Goldstein, executive director of Bronx Dance Theater. "The dancers worked really hard."

"Tapmania," at the Bronx Dance Theater's Performing Arts Center, 286 East 204th Street; 5 P.M. today and 7:30 P.M. Saturday; $10; reservations, (718) 652-7655.

UPPER EAST SIDE A New Understanding Of Antique New York

In a show for history buffs and art lovers alike, 25 paintings of 19th- and early 20th-century New York scenes are on display at the Museum of the City of New York, along with new information about their content and authorship. The meanings of many of the paintings have been reinterpreted, and the locations of the paintings have been identified.

The exhibit is titled "Revisiting the Scene: New Evidence, New Discoveries." Among the works is "Frank Work Driving a Fast Team of Trotters," an 1891 oil by John McAuliffe.

"Revisiting the Scene: New Evidence, New Discoveries," at the Museum of The City of New York, 1220 Fifth Avenue (at 103d Street); Wednesday through Saturday, 10 A.M. to 5 P.M., Sunday, 1 P.M. to 5 P.M.; through Jan. 12; $5; $4 students, seniors and children; (212) 534-1672.

BOERUM HILL Music and Movement, 5 Different Ways

In Barbara Mahler's new solo performance, "Don't Look Down," below, movements that suggest taking off into space, circling and spiraling, shedding and accumulating are presented against a score by Christopher Caines.

This piece, as well as four others -- including "Rorschach 157," by the choreogapher Ann Moradian -- will be performed as part of the Outback Series for New Dance and Performance, Friday and Saturday at the Gowanus Art Exchange.

Meg Wolfe will present the premiere of a work for seven dancers with original music by G. D. Bianciardi. Pam Geber will present her new solo piece, "No Rain Date," accompanied by the vocal performance of her sister Kristen Geber. Marie Pelletier will present "Strings Attached," performed by herself and Christina Aguirre.

"The Outback Series for New Dance and Performance," at the Gowanus Art Exchange, 295 Douglass Street (near Third Avenue); Friday and Saturday, 8 P.M.; $10; $6 low-income; (718) 596-5250.

ALSO AROUND TOWN

WORKS OF LOVE -- The world premiere of "American Love Songs," 10 commissions by composers including Ned Rorem and John Corigliano, will be on the program of the New York Festival of Song; 92d Street Y, Lexington Avenue at 92d Street; Saturday, 8 P.M.; $20; (212) 996-1100.

JERUSALEM REDISCOVERED -- Robert R. Steiglitz, an archeologist, will lecture on "Jerusalem 3000: New Discoveries in the City of Holiness," at the Jewish Museum, 1109 Fifth Avenue (at 92d street); Wednesday at 6:30 P.M.; $11; $9, students and seniors; $7, members; (212) 423-3224.

A REQUIEM IN DANCE -- "City Water Tunnel No. 3," a performance project by Marty Pottenger, will be presented by the Dance Theater Workshop and Dancing in The Streets to benefit the workers who gave their lives building the tunnel; at the Bessie Schonberg Theater, 219 West 19th Street (between Seventh and Eighth Avenues); Wednesday at 8 P.M.; (212) 989-6830.
