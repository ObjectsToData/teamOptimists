NEW JERSEY GUIDE

By Frank Emblen
Published: September 13, 1987

GERMAN FETE IN BERGEN Steuben Festival '87, a celebration of America's German heritage, is scheduled for next Sunday at New Bridge Landing Historic Park in River Edge.

From the time the festival opens at 11 A.M. until it closes at 4 P.M., vendors will sell a variety of teutonic gastronomical delights, such as bratwurst, knockwurst, weisswurst, sauerkraut and pretzels. Members of the Bergen County Historical Society, the festival's sponsor, will sell home-baked cakes and pastries.

During the afternoon, the Bergen County Militia will re-enact authentic Revolutionary War tactics - punctuated by musket volleys - which General von Steuben, Washington's Prussian drillmaster, taught the Continental Army. (Military historians credit American infantry successes against the British to these tactics, rather than the shoot-from-behind-trees guerrilla style that movie makers would have us believe was the Redcoats' undoing.)

Tours of Steuben House, the home that New Jersey presented to von Steuben in gratitude for his war efforts, will be conducted by the Historical Society.

Music will be provided by Hans Kraft and his four-piece German band; Herman Deussing, an Alpine bell ringer and accordionist; George Weber, who plays the electric organ, and a mixed vocal ensemble, the Carlstadt Singers.

Also, there will be beauty queens: Miss Scheutzen Park and Little Miss Scheutzen Park.

Craftsmen will demonstate blacksmithing, quilting, scrimshaw, woodcarving and ceramics and will sell examples of their handiwork.

Admission is $2 (no charge for children under 12).

New Bridge Landing Historic Park is at 1209 Main Street. It can be reached from Route 4 by taking the Hackensack Avenue North exit, which is also marked ''Shopping Center'' for Riverside Square, and continue a quarter-mile to the River Edge Main Street exit on the right. The park will be on the right.

Information: (201) 343-9492.

KAYAKING AND CANOEING

The second annual Jersey Shore Sea Kayaking and Bay Canoeing Show will be held from 10 A.M. to 4 P.M. Saturday on the beach in Berkeley Island County Park. There is no admission charge.

Various types of kayaks will be available for visitors to paddle. (It's a good idea to bring along what the Coast Guard calls a personal floatation device and a paddle, since there often are more demonstrator boats than paddles and life jackets available.)

The show is sponsored by the Ocean County Parks Department and the Jersey Paddler, a canoe and kayak shop in Bricktown.

To get to the park, take Exit 74 off the Garden State Parkway to Route 614 East and then go east toward Forked River. Turn left onto Route 9 North and proceed 3.1 miles before turning right onto Harbor Inn Road. Follow the road to the end.

Information: (201) 458-5777 or 370-7360.

FAIR TO AID AFGHANS

A country fair to benefit Afghan refugees will be held in Oldwick Saturday and next Sunday from 10 A.M. to 5 P.M.

The vendors will include a recently arrived Afghan who fought in his country's resistance movement; he will sell carpets and jewelry. There also will be an Afghan storyteller and musician.

Traditional Afghan foods, as well as American-style barbecued chicken, will be served.

An auction at 2 P.M. Saturday will feature antiques, artwork, Afghan carpets, furniture, designer sweaters and first editions of science-fiction by Doris Lessings.

The fair will be in the apple orchards and gardens on the grounds of the Magic Shop on Main Street, about a mile from Exit 24 of I-78.

Admission is $1 (no charge for children under 12).

Information: (201) 439-2801.

GROSHANS WORKS ON VIEW

A retrospective exhibition of oils and pastels by Werner Groshans will open Wednesday at the Jersey City Museum, where he was chairman of the Art Committee in 1966 and 1967.

Mr. Groshans, who was born in Germany in 1913, came to the United States as an adolescent and was graduated from the Newark School of Fine and Industrial Arts in 1934. He died in 1986.

His works are in many public and private collections and have been displayed in major museums, including the Metropolitan and the Whitney. His subjects were still lifes, landscapes (including New Jersey scenes) and the human figure.

The retrospective was first shown at the Butler Institute in Youngstown, Ohio, and then at the State Museum in Trenton.

A reception celebrating the Jersey City opening will be held from 4:30 to 7:30 P.M. Friday at the museum. It occupies the fourth floor of the main library at 472 Jersey Avenue, at the corner of Montgomery Street. The public is invited.

Museum hours are 11:30 A.M. to 8 P.M. on Wednesdays and 11:30 A.M. to 4:30 P.M. Thursday through Saturday.

Information: (201) 547-4514 or 451-0802.

INDIAN POWWOW

The Thunderbird American Indian Dancers will hold the group's annual New Jersey powwow next weekend at Riker Hill Park in Livingston.

Several dance contests, such as the men's fancy dance, women's traditional and the children's dance, will be among the attractions. Crafts and food will be sold.

The Thunderbird dancers were formed in 1963 to raise scholarship funds for needy American Indian students and to preserve the culture and traditions of the American Indian. Both the scholarship fund and the Riker Hill Art Fund will benefit from the powwow.

The schedule is 2 to 5 P.M. and 8 to 11 P.M. on Saturday and 1 to 4 P.M. on Sunday. Admission is $3.50 ($2.50 for children 6 to 12). The park is on Beaufort Avenue.

Information: (201) 482-5063.

CONTROVERSIAL PLAY

''The Politician Out-Witted'' was too controversial to be staged when it was written. Now the East Lynne Company of Secaucus has put it on the boards - 199 years later.

The 1788 comedy by Samuel Low tells of two young lovers forbidden marriage by their fathers, who are adamantly opposed on the day's principal political controversy: the proposed United States Constitution.

Theater history researchers could find no evidence that the play, considered by 18th-century theatrical producers to have highly inflammatory political content, ever saw the footlights.

There is a performance at 3 P.M. today at the Williams Center for the Performing Arts in Rutherford. Tickets are $12 ($10 for Bergen County residents and the elderly).

Information: (201) 863-6436.

The next presentation of ''The Politician Out-Witted'' will be at 2 P.M. next Sunday in the Dineen Theater at St. Peter's College, Montgomery Street and Kennedy Boulevard, Jersey City.

Tickets are $7 ($5 for the Friends of the Jersey City Museum, which is co-sponsoring the performance, and $3 for students and the elderly).

Information: (201) 333-4400, Ext. 266.

LITERARY SESSIONS

''Americans in the 30's,'' a free reading and book discussion program, will begin Wednesday in the Belleville Public Library, with Dr. Walter Bezanson, professor emeritus of American studies and English at Rutgers University, discussing ''All the King's Men,'' Robert Penn Warren's 1946 novel about the rise to power of a political demagogue.

Other sessions will be:

Sept. 30, ''For Whom the Bell Tolls,'' by Ernest Hemingway; Oct. 14, ''Their Eyes Were Watching God,'' by Zora Neale Hurston; Oct. 28, ''The Grapes Of Wrath,'' by John Steinbeck, and Nov. 18, ''The Best of Simple,'' by Langston Hughes.

Free texts will be made available to the group, which is financed by the New Jersey Committee for the Humanities.

Discussions begin at 1 P.M. at the library, 221 Washington Avenue.

Information: Adrea Cohen, (201) 450-3434.
