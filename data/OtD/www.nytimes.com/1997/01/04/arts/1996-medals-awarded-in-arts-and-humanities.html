1996 Medals Awarded In Arts and Humanities


Published: January 4, 1997

Edward Albee, Robert Redford and the Boys Choir of Harlem are among the winners of the 1996 National Medal of Arts. The arts medal has been awarded every year since 1984 in recognition of contributions to culture and the arts by individuals and groups. The recipients were selected by President Bill Clinton from a list of nominees that the National Endowment for the Arts solicits from the National Council on the Arts, an advisory body to the agency.

Bill Moyers, Rita Dove and three other cultural figures have been awarded the Charles Frankel Prize in the Humanities. The Frankel prize, established eight years ago, is given to five Americans who have made contributions to cultural life by bringing the humanities to a wider audience. Nominations are solicited by the endowment and narrowed to a list of finalists by the National Council on the Humanities, an advisory board to the agency. The winners are selected by Sheldon Hackney, chairman of the endowment.

The prize is named in honor of Charles Frankel (1917-1979), a professor of philosophy at Columbia University, an Assistant Secretary of State for Educational and Cultural Affairs and the first director of the National Humanities Center in Research Triangle Park, N.C.

The arts and humanities awards are to be presented by the President and First Lady Hillary Rodham Clinton at a ceremony in Washington on Thursday.

The recipients of the 1996 Medal of Arts are:

Edward Albee, playwright

Sarah Caldwell, opera conductor

Harry Callahan, photographer

Zelda Fichandler, theater director

Eduardo (Lalo) Guerrero, composer and musician

Lionel Hampton, musician and band leader

Bella Lewitzky, dancer, choreographer and teacher

Vera List, arts patron

Robert Redford, actor, director and producer

Maurice Sendak, author, illustrator and designer

Stephen Sondheim, composer and lyricist

The Boys Choir of Harlem, arts organization

The recipients of the Charles Frankel Prize are:

Rita Dove, novelist and former poet laureate of the United States

Doris Kearns Goodwin, historian

Daniel Kemmis, political philosopher

Arturo Madrid, scholar of Hispanic literature

Bill Moyers, television journalist
