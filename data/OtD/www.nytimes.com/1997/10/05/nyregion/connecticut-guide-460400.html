CONNECTICUT GUIDE

By ELEANOR CHARLES
Published: October 5, 1997

DINOSAURS AND ART

Fine art and dinosaurs sound like an odd coupling, but the Bruce Museum in Greenwich will have both next Saturday and Sunday.

''Designing Dinosaurs: Solving Prehistoric Puzzles'' is an exhibition that opened on Sept. 27 and will continue through Jan. 4, featuring programs keyed to particular aspects of the prehistoric beasts.

Today at 3 P.M. Dr. Michael J. Novacek, Provost of Science at the American Museum of Natural History in New York, will talk about his adventures in Mongolia, where he excavated dinosaur fossils. Admission is $5.

On Saturday and Sunday from 11 to 4 the museum will run a continuous Dinosaur Film Festival. Among the film titles are ''Fossil Rush: Tale of a Bone'' and ''Giant Birds of the Air: Tale of a Feather.''

The exhibition is the largest ever presented by the museum, spreading through four galleries and part of the arcade. It shows how dinosaur replicas and dinosaur art have been fashioned over the centuries, from digging up the bones to reconstructing the skeleton and covering it with flesh and skin.

Paintings and sculptures by artists in the field have been assembled, including Rudolph Zallinger's Pulitzer Prize-winning mural ''The Age of Reptiles'' from Yale's Peabody Museum. Admission to the dinosaur exhibit and the films is free today with a $3 admission to the 16th Annual Outdoor Arts Festival in progress from 11 to 5.

More than 75 artists from across the country have been selected to display paintings, sculpture, drawings, photography, and mixed media works in competition for prizes -- and all are for sale. Live music will be played from 1 to 4 both days and food will be available for purchase.

The museum is just off Exit 3 of Interstate-95. The number for more information is (203) 869-0376.

CONTEMPORARY POETS

The poet Dick Allen will lead a series of discussions on five contemporary poets at the Hartford Public Library beginning on Wednesday at 5:30, when his subject will be Gwendolyn Brooks. Her second collection, published in 1950, titled ''Annie Allen,'' won a Pulitzer Prize.

Karl Shapiro's poems about war and dehumanization are on the agenda for Oct. 15. Adrienne Rich, exploring the conflicts of women in the arts in America, will be covered on Oct. 22, and the final two poets will be Gary Soto on Oct. 29 and Rita Dove on Nov. 5.

Ms. Dove won a Pulitzer Prize in 1986 for ''Thomas and Beulah,'' based on her grandparents' lives, and Mr. Soto deals with family and friendship in difficult circumstances.

Audio cassettes of interviews with the poets will be used during the sessions along with a book of essays about contemporary American poets. All sessions begin at 5:30 at the main library, 500 Main Street, made possible by the National Endowment for the Humanities. The number for more information is (860) 543-8628.

RIVER CELEBRATION

From Thursday through next Sunday the Connecticut River Museum, 67 Main Street at Steamboat Dock in Essex, will benefit from daily events in its RiverFest '97.

RiverFare is the name of a food extravaganza on Thursday at 6 P.M. with a tab of $60. Samplings from some of the river valley's best restaurants will be served under tents on the museum lawn, supplemented by live music and an open bar.

Bee and Thistle Inn, Alforno Ristorante, Griswold Inn, Old Lyme Inn, Saybrook Point Inn, Black Seal, Cafe Routier, make up a partial list of participating restaurants.

Soap opera devotees will have an opportunity to learn more than they may want to know about what goes on behind the cameras when a panel of writers and actors from long-running series tell all over box lunches and wine on Friday at noon. Mike Malone, head writer of ''Another World,'' will moderate. The cost is $30.

''Beer, Brats and Boogie,'' on Saturday night from 6 P.M. on, features DJ dance music of the oompah variety, and Gourmet Galley's sausages and wursts for $35. And Sunday's ''Windmills and Wigwams,'' a menu of games, storytelling, a maze, treasure hunt and crafts from 1 to 4 P.M., is mostly for children through age 12, all of whom must have an adult in tow. Admission of $3 includes a tour of the museum's current exhibition, ''Before New England: The Dutch and Native Americans in the Connecticut River Valley, 1609-1650.''

The number for more information is (860) 767-8269.

ON JOHN BROWN

''John Brown: Martyr? Murderer? Madman?'' is the subject of a symposium scheduled on Saturday from 10 to 4 at the Kellogg School in Falls Village. The featured speaker will be Stephen B. Oates, author of several books about Brown and the Civil War.

His most recent book, ''The Approaching Fury: Voices of the Storm, 1820-1861'' will have a sequel, ''The Whirlwind of War,'' to be released next July. Both books tell the story of the Civil War through the eyes of several principal players.

Saturday's event begins with an orientation video at 10:30 A.M., called ''To Do Battle in This Land,'' narrated by Ossie Davis, followed by a slide-talk by Edwin N. Cotter, Jr., retired caretaker of the John Brown Farm Historic Site in Lake Placid.

A dramatic portrayal of the hardships endured by Brown's wife was written and will be performed by Kathleen Thomas at noon, after which the gathering will recess for lunch.

Professor Oates' lecture, ''John Brown, Catalyst of the Civil War'' will be presented at 1:45, followed by a discussion among the speakers and Dr. Lawrence S. Carlton of the Canton Historical Society.

The program is part of the Falls Village annual Civil War Encampment and weekend Historical Festival. Admission is by contribution of $5 for adults and is free for students. For more information, the numbers are (860) 435-2878 or (860) 824-7893.

ON U.S. GOVERNMENT

Alan Brinkley, professor of American History at Columbia University, will speak about ''The Withering of American Government: A Historian's Perspective,'' on Thursday at 7:30 at Greens Farms Academy in Westport.

Professor Brinkley's 1982 book ''Voices of Protest: Huey Long, Father Coughlin, and the Great Depression'' won the American Book Award. He is currently working on a book about Henry R. Luce to be published by Knopf, and his ''Liberalism and its Discontents: Essays on the History of Politics and the Politics of History'' will soon be published by the Harvard University Press.

Professor Brinkley serves on the National Advisory Board of the PBS television series ''The American Experience.''

Greens Farms Academy is at 35 Beachside Avenue, and admission is free. The number for more information is (203) 256-7557.

WALKING WEEKENDS

More than 50 guided walks through the forests, main streets, villages and historic sites of Connecticut's Northeast quadrant are available in the region's seventh annual Walking Weekend on next Saturday, and Monday.

Some are suitable for children, some require healthy adult stamina, and still others are pleasant strolls. Information is available in a brochure and map to be obtained free of charge by calling (860) 928-1228. ELEANOR CHARLES
