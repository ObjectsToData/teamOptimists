Corrections: November 4, 2013

Published: November 4, 2013

FRONT PAGE

An article on Thursday about the government shutdown and how it has inspired Democrats around the country to run for office misidentified the Arkansas city where Patrick Henry Hays, who announced his candidacy for the Congressional seat of a Republican who is retiring, once served as mayor. It is North Little Rock, not Little Rock. The article also misstated the last time Nebraska elected a Democrat to the House of Representatives. It was 1992, not 1994.

•

A picture caption on Friday with the continuation of an article about technology companies’ bolstering defenses of their data against government surveillance, using information from The Associated Press, misidentified the location of the Google data center shown. It is in Council Bluffs, Iowa — not in Helsinki, Finland.

•

An article on Thursday about universities that are becoming concerned that the humanities are being eclipsed misstated the area of expertise for Louis Menand of Harvard. He is a professor of English, not of history.

NATIONAL

A report in the National Briefing column on Thursday about a gunman in South Carolina who killed five people over a custody dispute and then himself included an incorrect accounting from the Greenwood County sheriff’s office about an earlier charge against him. Though burglary charges were pending against the gunman, Bryan E. Sweatt, he was not scheduled for a court appearance this week.

NEW YORK

An article in some editions on Oct. 27 about the arrest of a United Airlines passenger who tried to check several weapons as baggage at Kennedy International Airport described two of the confiscated rifles incorrectly. They were .22-caliber rifles, not .22-millimeter.

SPORTS

An article in some editions on Tuesday about the Rangers’ 2-0 loss to Montreal in their home opener misspelled the surname of a Canadiens player who missed the N.H.L. game with a concussion. He is George Parros, not Parris. The article also misspelled the given name of the Hall of Famer who participated in a pregame ceremony with Rod Gilbert, then described the ceremony incorrectly. He is Yvan Cournoyer, not Yvon, and he and Gilbert dropped the ceremonial first puck; they did not drop multiple pucks.

•

An article on Wednesday about criticism of the Buffalo Sabres over dangerous hits misidentified, in some copies, the player Buffalo’s John Scott is fighting in a picture hung above his locker stall. He is Matt Carkner — not Terry Carkner, who is a cousin of Matt’s and played in the N.H.L. from 1986 to 1999.
