Calendar

Events in New Jersey

Published: March 8, 2013

A guide to cultural and recreational events in New Jersey. Items for the calendar should be sent at least three weeks in advance to njtowns@nytimes.com.

Comedy

WEST ORANGE Pink Collar Comedy Tour, featuring four female comedians. March 14 at 8 p.m. $10. Luna Stage, 555 Valley Road. (973) 395-5551; pinkcollarcomedytour.com.

For Children

MORRISTOWN The Berenstain Bears Live in “Family Matters: The Musical.” March 10 at 1:30 and 4 p.m. $12 to $15. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

RAHWAY Sensory Friendly Theater featuring the New Jersey Ballet presents “Pinocchio,” specially designed and adapted for children with autism and related disabilities. March 10 at 2 p.m. $8. Union County Performing Arts Center, 1601 Irving Street. (732) 499-8226; ucpac.org.

VINELAND Disney’s “Alice in Wonderland Jr.,” presented by the Landis Youth Theater. March 15 through 17. $10 and $13. The Berenstain Bears Live in “Family Matters: The Musical.” March 24 at 2 p.m. $12. Landis Theater, 830 East Landis Avenue. (856) 691-1121; landistheater.com.

WAYNE “A Thousand Cranes,” play. Through March 10. $10 and $14. Hunziker Black Box Theater, 300 Pompton Road. (973) 720-2371;wpunj.edu/wplive/hunziker.dot.

Music and Dance

ENGLEWOOD Mendelssohn’s “Italian” symphony, performed by the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra, led by Marcelo Lehninger, with Joanna Mongiardo, soprano. For tickets: (973) 624-3713; njsymphony.org. March 10 at 3 p.m. $20 to $76. The Marshall Tucker Band, Southern rock. March 14 at 8 p.m. $29 to $99. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

HILLSIDE Elaine Paige, actress, recording artist, producer and broadcaster. March 10 at 3 p.m. $55 to $75. Enlow Recital Hall at Kean University, 215 North Avenue. (908) 737-7469; enlowhall.kean.edu.

MAHWAH Westchester Jazz Orchestra. March 10 at 3 p.m. $17 to $27. Fusion Tango, featuring Argentine tango dancers and musicians. March 16 at 8 p.m. $17 to $28. Berrie Center for Performing and Visual Arts, 505 Ramapo Valley Road. (201) 684-7844; ramapo.edu/berriecenter/index.html.

MANASQUAN Vladimir Zaitsev, piano virtuoso, presents “Rock Stars of Romanticism: The Music of Chopin and Liszt.” March 13 at 10 a.m. $9. Algonquin Arts Theater, 173 Main Street. algonquinarts.com; (732) 528-9211.

MONTCLAIR Via Katlehong Dance, South African dance ensemble. March 16 through 24. $15. Alexander Kasser Theater, 1 Normal Avenue. peakperfs.org; (973) 655-5112.

MONTCLAIR Deftones, alternative metal band. March 10 at 8 p.m. $33 and $36. George Thorogood and the Destroyers, blues rock. March 15 at 8 p.m. $35 and $40. Wellmont Theater, 5 Seymour Street. (973) 783-9500; wellmonttheatre.com.

NEW BRUNSWICK Beethoven Orchestra Bonn, led by Stefan Blunier. March 10 at 3 p.m. $28 to $62. Scott Weiland, former frontman of the rock band Stone Temple Pilots. March 12 at 8 p.m. $25 to $55. The Irish Tenors, featuring Finbar Wright, Anthony Kearns and Ronan Tynan. March 16 at 8 p.m. $32 to $97. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

NEWARK Celtic Woman, vocal group. March 14 at 8 p.m. $45 to $75. Zeppelin Fest, a performance of two classic rock LPs: “Led Zeppelin I” and “Led Zeppelin IV.” March 15 at 8 p.m. $20 to $40. El Gran Combo 50th Anniversary Tour, salsa orchestra. March 16 at 8 p.m. $39.50 to $95. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

NEWARK An Evening With Cowboy Junkies. March 15 at 8 p.m. $45 and $55. Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

PRINCETON Princeton Symphony Orchestra Classical Series Concert: Mystical Poetry, led by Rossen Milanov, conductor. March 10 at 4 p.m. $25 to $68. Richardson Auditorium, in Alexander Hall on the Princeton University Campus. (609) 497-0020; princetonsymphony.org.

RED BANK Steve Martin and the Steep Canyon Rangers: An Evening of Bluegrass and Comedy. March 14 at 8 p.m. $55 to $125. Summer of Love 3, featuring Glen Burtnik and the Summer of Love Experience. March 15 at 8 p.m. $20 to $99. Monmouth Civic Chorus: I Have a Dream, concert that honors the 50th anniversary of the speech by the Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. and the 150th anniversary of the Emancipation Proclamation. March 16 at 8 p.m. $25 and $30. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

SOUTH ORANGE Time for Three, with Zach De Pue, violin, Nick Kendall, violin, and Ranaan Meyer, double bass. March 13 at 7:30 p.m. $15 to $45. Frank Wess Quartet, jazz/swing. March 16 at 8 p.m. $20 to $35. The New York Voices, vocal ensemble. March 19 at 7:30 p.m. $8 to $25. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. sopacnow.org; (973) 313-2787.

STANHOPE Billy Hector, electric blues/rock. March 16 at 7 p.m. $15. The Stanhope House, 45 Main Street. (973) 347-7777; stanhopehousenj.com.

TEANECK Classical Sundays at the Puffin: “The Nature of Things,” featuring students in Bard College Conservatory’s Vocal Arts Program, presenting a program of songs exploring the natural world, love and despair. March 10 at 4 p.m. $10. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. puffinculturalforum.org; (201) 836-3499.

VINELAND Love, Lust, Fear and Freedom: The Songs of Joni Mitchell and Leonard Cohen. March 23 at 8 p.m. $22.50 to $50. Landis Theater, 830 East Landis Avenue. (856) 691-1121; landistheater.com.

WAYNE Toshiko Akiyoshi, pianist, with the Lew Tabackin Quartet. March 10 at 4 p.m. $8 to $15. Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University, 300 Pompton Road. (973) 720-2371; wplive.org.

Outdoors

TENAFLY Maple Sugaring. Visitors learn how to identify a maple tree and discover the history of syrup making and how technology has changed the gathering of sap over the centuries. March 10, 12:30 to 1:30 p.m. $5 and $10. Tenafly Nature Center, 313 Hudson Avenue. tenaflynaturecenter.org; (201) 568-6093.

Spoken Word

NEWARK Cesar Millan: The Dog Whisperer. March 10 at 7 p.m. $39.50 and $49.50. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

RANDOLPH Seventh Annual Festival of Storytelling and World Music. March 10, 12:30 to 6 p.m. $6. County College of Morris, 214 Center Grove Road. (973) 625-8548; njstorynet.org.

SOUTH ORANGE A discussion with C. K. Williams, author, poet and Pulitzer-Prize recipient. March 13 at 7 p.m. Free. Seton Hall University, 400 South Orange Avenue. (973) 761-9019; shu.edu.

TEANECK “American Musical Theater: Cultural Mirror and Mythology.” JoAnn Yeoman Tongret, theater professional and adjunct professor at Pace University, shares video clips and stories tracing the development of America’s musicals. March 17 at 4 p.m. $10. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. (201) 836-3499; puffinculturalforum.org.

Theater

BRANCHBURG “Wunderkammer,” circus, cabaret and vaudeville presented by C!RCA. March 16 at 8 p.m. $25 and $35. The Theater, Raritan Valley Community College, Route 28 and Lamington Road in North Branch. (908) 725-3420; rvccarts.org.

HACKETTSTOWN “The Cripple of Inishmaan,” dark comedy by Martin McDonagh. Through March 10. $17.50 to $27.50. Centenary Stage Company, Lackland Center, 715 Grand Avenue. centenarystageco.org; (908) 979-0900.

HAMMONTON “The Complete Works of William Shakespeare (Abridged),” comedy. Through March 23. $25 and $30. Eagle Theater, 208 Vine Street. (609) 704-5012; theeagletheatre.com.

LONG BRANCH “Ants,” comedy by Saviana Stanescu. Through March 10. $25 to $40. New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater, 179 Broadway. (732) 229-3166; njrep.org.

MAHWAH “The Shape of Things,” by Neil LaBute, presented by the School of Contemporary Arts. Through March 13. $5 and $15. Berrie Center for Performing and Visual Arts, 505 Ramapo Valley Road. (201) 684-7844; ramapo.edu/berriecenter/index.html.

MILLBURN “Lend Me a Tenor,” comedy by Ken Ludwig. Through March 10. $34 to $89. Paper Mill Playhouse, 22 Brookside Drive. (973) 376-4343; papermill.org.

PRINCETON “In the Next Room, or the Vibrator Play,” comedy by Sarah Ruhl. Through March 15. $10 and $15. Roger S. Berlind Theater, McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

RAHWAY “Eight Plays in Eight Days,” presented by the American Theater Group. Through March 10. $8. Hamilton Stage, 360 Hamilton Street. (732) 499-8226; ucpac.org.

SOUTH ORANGE The Spencers: Theater of Illusion. March 10 at 3 p.m. $25 to $40. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-2787; sopacnow.org.

TEANECK “Crossing the BLVD,” Judith Sloan and cast present the stories of immigrants from the diverse borough of Queens. March 15 at 7 p.m. $10. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. puffinculturalforum.org; (201) 836-3499.

UNION Ninth Annual Spring Readings Series, featuring four plays. March 14 through 17. Free. Premiere Stages at Kean University, 1000 Morris Avenue. kean.edu/premierestages; (908) 737-7469.

WEST WINDSOR “Avenue Q,” adult musical comedy with puppets. Through March 10. $14 to $18. Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College, 1200 Old Trenton Road.

kelseytheatre.net; (609) 570-3333.

Museums and Galleries

BOONTON “Illuminato,” exhibition of paintings by Brenda A. Edwards. Through March 31. Fridays and Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 7 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. S.H.E. Gallery, 819 Main Street. (973) 335-0943; shegallery.com.

CAPE MAY “Celebrating Community Service: Black Fraternal, Social and Civic Organizations of Cape May County,” exhibition on African-American social life. Through April 14. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. capemaymac.org; (609) 884-5404.

CLINTON “EastWest Ceramics: Clay Works Exhibition.” Through March 10. Donation: $5. Tuesdays through Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Hunterdon Art Museum, 7 Lower Center Street. hunterdonartmuseum.org; (908) 735-8415.

ENGLEWOOD “Paint Work,” solo exhibition of new works by Daniel Rios Rodriguez. Through April 14. Mondays through Thursdays, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. One River Gallery, 49 North Dean Street. onerivergallery.com; (201) 266-5244.

HAMILTON “Heaven, Hell and the History of Punch,” works by Robert Taplin. Through April 10. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. (609) 586-0616; groundsforsculpture.org.

HOBOKEN “Mapping the Territory: Hudson County in Maps, 1840-2013.” Through June 30. $2. Tuesdays through Thursdays, 2 to 7 p.m.; Fridays, 1 to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Hoboken Historical Museum, 1301 Hudson Street. hobokenmuseum.org; (201) 656-2240.

JERSEY CITY “Avatar: The Exhibition,” featuring interactive installations and original props from the movie. Through May 19. Free with admission, $12.50 and $16.75. Liberty Science Center, 222 Jersey City Boulevard. (201) 200-1000; lsc.org.

JERSEY CITY “Ray Smith: Here | Now,” featuring more than 100 works spanning three decades. Through May 5. Mana Contemporary, 888 Newark Avenue. (201) 484-1495, ext. 672; manafinearts.com.

LINCROFT “From Plate to Paper: The Art of Printmaking,” This juried exhibition features over 75 works in a variety of media that demonstrate both traditional and alternative printmaking techniques. March 10 through April 28. “Bruce Waldman: New Works,” exhibition of monoprints and etchings by the printmaker, graphic artist and illustrator. Through April 21. $7; members and children under 2, free. Tuesdays through Thursdays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Fridays, 10 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Monmouth Museum, 765 Newman Springs Road. (732) 747-2266; monmouthmuseum.org.

MAHWAH “Les Paul in Mahwah,” about the longtime Mahwah resident, inventor, musician and self-taught engineer. Through June 1. “Nike Missile Base in Mahwah: Nuclear Warheads in Our Backyard,” models of the base, which operated from 1955 to 1971, and of the missiles; vintage footage; and other artifacts of the period. Through June 30. $3 to $5; members and children under 5, free. Wednesdays, Saturdays and Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Mahwah Museum, 201 Franklin Turnpike. (201) 512-0099; mahwahmuseum.org.

MONTCLAIR “Redefining American Art: Organizers of the 1913 Armory Show in MAM’s Collection.” Through June 16. “The New Spirit: American Art in the Armory Show, 1913.” Through June 16. “Patterns, Systems, Structures: Abstraction in American Art.” Through May 19. $12; seniors and students, $10; members and children under 12, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Montclair Art Museum, 3 South Mountain Avenue. (973) 746-5555; montclairartmuseum.org.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP “Toying With Art,” juried exhibition featuring over 40 works by more than 20 artists. Through March 17. “Immortal Structures: Here and There After,” featuring the contemporary realist paintings of New Jersey artist Maria Mijares. Through March 31. “Victorian New Jersey: Photographs by Guillermo Thorn,” an exhibition of photographs from the Kean University Collection. Through March 31. “Reflections: Contemporary Studio Art Glass,” featuring 40 works by Kathleen Elliot and Paul Stankard. Through April 28.“Line, Color, and Pattern: Exquisite Textile Designs of Central and South America.” Through June 16. $7 and $10; members and children under 3 are free. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 11 a.m. to 8 p.m.; and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3700; morrismuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN “Generation Next,” African-American art. Through March 22. Mondays through Fridays, 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. Atrium Gallery, Court Street. artintheatrium.org; (973) 285-5115.

MORRISTOWN “Thomas Nast: President Maker and Campaign Breaker,” illustrating presidential campaigns from 1864 to 1884. Through June 1. “Fine Collection: Treasures From the Vault Exhibit,” featuring over a dozen Oriental rugs from the museum’s collection. Through June 30. $6 to $8; children ages 6 to 12; $4 and children under 5, free. Wednesdays, Thursdays and Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Macculloch Hall Historical Museum, 45 Macculloch Avenue. maccullochhall.org; (973) 538-2404.

NEW BRUNSWICK “Lynd Ward Draws Stories: Inspired by Mexico’s History, Mark Twain and Adventures in the Woods.” Through June 30. “Leonid Sokov: Ironic Objects,” exhibition of about 50 works. Through July 14. “Henri-Gabriel Ibels,” works on paper by the French artist. Through Sept. 8. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum, 71 Hamilton Street. (732) 932-7237; zimmerlimuseum.rutgers.edu.

NEWARK “Expanding Africa at the Newark Museum: New Visions, New Galleries,” contemporary and historical works in different media. Through May 1. “Present Tense — Arts of Contemporary Africa,” works from the permanent collection. Continuing. “In Her Eyes: Women Behind and in Front of the Camera,” photography by women that examines female identity. Through spring 2013. “African Cosmos: Stellar Arts,” exhibition about African cultural astronomy and arts. Through Aug. 11. $6 to $10; members, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. (973) 596-6550; newarkmuseum.org.

OCEAN TOWNSHIP “Loved Ones Go to War: Local Stories of World War II,” the war’s effects on local veterans and those they left behind. Through June 1. Tuesdays and Wednesdays, 1 to 4 p.m.; Thursdays, 1 to 4 p.m. and 7 to 9 p.m.; and first and second Sundays of the month, 1 to 4 p.m. Admission is free. Township of Ocean Historical Museum, 703 Deal Road. oceanmuseum.org; (732) 531-2136.

PRINCETON “The Pine Barrens: A Legacy of Preservation: Photographs by Richard Speedy.” Through April 15. $5 and $6. Tuesdays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Morven Museum and Garden, 55 Stockton Street. (609) 924-8144; morven.org.

PRINCETON “Revealing the African Presence in Renaissance Europe,” paintings, drawings, sculpture and printed books of the period. Through June 9. “Picturing Power: Capitalism, Democracy and American Portraiture,” featuring 50 portraits from the now-dispersed New York Chamber of Commerce collection. Through June 30. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 10 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Princeton University Art Museum, Nassau and Witherspoon Streets. 609) 258-3788; artmuseum.princeton.edu.

PRINCETON JUNCTION “Energy and Motion,” photography. Through April 27. West Windsor Arts Center, 952 Alexander Road. (609) 716-1931; westwindsorartscenter.org.

RED BANK “27th Annual Jean Townsend Award Exhibition,” featuring works around the themes of “In the Distance” and “Close Ups.” Through March 30. Hours: Tuesdays through Saturdays, noon to 4 p.m. Art Alliance of Monmouth County, 33 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9403; artallianceofmonmouth.org.

RED BANK “SWART: Social Work Works,” featuring artwork of New Jersey social workers and art therapists. Through March 25. Mondays through Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Gallery U, 80 Broad Street. (732) 747-6696; gallery-u.blogspot.com.

SHREWSBURY “Eyesights 2013.” Open juried photography show. Through April 3. Tuesdays, Wednesdays and Fridays, 8 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Thursdays, 8 a.m. to 7 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Guild of Creative Art, 620 Broad Street. (732) 741-1441; guildofcreativeart.org.

SUMMIT “Cordy Ryman: Shuffle/ Scrap/Echo,” site-specific exhibition featuring large-scale installations and individual works. Through March 24. Visual Arts Center of New Jersey, 68 Elm Street. (908) 273-9121; artcenternj.org.

TEANECK “Seeing the Future From the Past: Art Work by WPA Artists.” Through April 4. Tuesdays through Thursdays, noon to 4 p.m. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. (201) 836-3499; puffinculturalforum.org.

TRENTON New Jersey Artist Series — “Jon Naar: Signature Photography.” Through May 5. Tuesdays through Sundays, 9 a.m. to 4:45 p.m. Suggested admission, $5. New Jersey State Museum, 205 West State Street. (609) 292-6464; newjerseystatemuseum.org.

UNION “Advocacy Quilts: A Voice for the Voiceless,” featuring 281 artists. Through May 10. Mondays, Tuesdays and Thursdays, noon to 7 p.m.; Wednesdays and Fridays, noon to 4 p.m. Kean University, 1000 Morris Avenue. kean.edu/~gallery/; (908) 737-0392.
