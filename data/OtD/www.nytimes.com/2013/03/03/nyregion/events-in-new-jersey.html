Comedy

MAHWAH The Irish Comedy Tour, featuring Derek Richards, Mike McCarthy, Joey Carroll, Jim Campbell and Derrick Keane. March 9 at 8 p.m. $22 to $28. Berrie Center for Performing and Visual Arts, 505 Ramapo Valley Road. (201) 684-7844;

ramapo.edu/berriecenter/index.html.

MONTCLAIR Demetri Martin. March 9 at 8 p.m. $39. Wellmont Theater, 5 Seymour Street. (973) 783-9500; wellmonttheatre.com.

NEW BRUNSWICK Martin Short. March 7 at 8 p.m. $35 to $75. Artie Lange. March 9 at 8 p.m. $35 to $125. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

Film

NEW BRUNSWICK Carmen in HD, production from the Royal Opera House. March 9 at 1 p.m. $7 and $12. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

PRINCETON JUNCTION “Dersu Uzala,” 1975 film directed by Akira Kurosawa. Screening followed by discussion. March 9 at 7:30 p.m. $6 and $7. West Windsor Arts Center, 952 Alexander Road. (609) 716-1931;

westwindsorartscenter.org.

For Children

MORRISTOWN The Berenstain Bears Live! in “Family Matters: The Musical.” March 10 at 1:30 and 4 p.m. $12 to $15. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK “Clifford the Big Red Dog — Live!” Musical. March 3 at noon and 3 p.m. $15 to $35. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

NEWARK Ringling Brothers and Barnum & Bailey presents “Built to Amaze!” Through March 3. $23.50 to $153.50. Prudential Center, 165 Mulberry Street. prucenter.com; (973) 757-6000.

TENAFLY Seasonal Scavenger Hunt. Through March 9. $5 to $30. Tenafly Nature Center, 313 Hudson Avenue. tenaflynaturecenter.org; (201) 568-6093.

VINELAND “Cuentos del Arbol (Tree Tales),” a bilingual musical in a Pushcart Players production, presented by the Stages Festival of the New Jersey Theater Alliance. March 3 at 2 p.m. $5; children 14 years and under, free. Disney’s “Alice in Wonderland Jr.,” presented by the Landis Youth Theater. March 15 through 17. $10 and $13. Landis Theater, 830 East Landis Avenue. landistheater.com; (856) 691-1121.

WAYNE “A Thousand Cranes,” play. March 5 through 10. $10 and $14. Hunziker Black Box Theater, 300 Pompton Road. (973) 720-2371;

wpunj.edu/wplive/hunziker.htm.

Music and Dance

DUMONT “Showstoppers,” variety show featuring André De Shields and many other performers. March 9 at 7:30 p.m. $30 and $40. Our Redeemer Lutheran Church, 344 Washington Avenue. (201) 394-9697.

ENGLEWOOD Preservation Hall Jazz Band. March 3 at 7 p.m. $29 to $59. Paul Taylor 2 Dance Company. March 4 at 8 p.m. $39 to $59. Jewel, with special guest Lilly Hiatt. March 7 at 8 p.m. $35 to $95. Queensrÿche, heavy metal band. March 8 at 8 p.m. $29 to $99. Mendelssohn’s “Italian” symphony, performed by the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra, led by Marcelo Lehninger, with Joanna Mongiardo, soprano. For tickets: (973) 624-3713; njsymphony.org. March 10 at 3 p.m. $20 to $76. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. bergenpac.org; (201) 227-1030.

HILLSIDE “Inspired and Inspiring,” music composed by and for women. The Arco Ensemble, conducted by Anne Manson and featuring the pianist Gabriela Martinez. March 3 at 3 p.m. $15 to $25. Elaine Paige. March 10 at 3 p.m. $55 to $75. Enlow Recital Hall at Kean University, 215 North Avenue. enlowhall.kean.edu; (908) 737-7469.

MADISON Songs of the Past, with the singer and pianist Deborah Fennelly and the pianist David Maiullo. March 3 at 2 p.m. $5 and $10. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. metc.org; (973) 377-2982.

MAHWAH Westchester Jazz Orchestra. March 10 at 3 p.m. $17 to $27. Berrie Center for Performing and Visual Arts, 505 Ramapo Valley Road.

ramapo.edu/berriecenterindex.html; (201) 684-7844.

MONTCLAIR Deftones, alternative metal band. March 10 at 8 p.m. $33 and $36. Wellmont Theater, 5 Seymour Street. (973) 783-9500;

wellmonttheatre.com.

Stomp, international percussion ensemble. March 8 and 9 at 8 p.m. $47 to $77. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jewel. March 8 at 8 p.m. $35 to $75. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

PRINCETON Mark Morris Dance Group, in a program including several premieres, all performed to live music. March 5 at 7:30 p.m. $20 to $60. Paddy Moloney and the Chieftains. March 6 at 7:30 p.m. $20 to $64. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. mccarter.org; (609) 258-2787.

RED BANK Dionne Warwick — A Concert to Benefit the Count Basie Theater. March 8 at 8 p.m. $35 to $155. Mendelssohn’s “Italian” Symphony performed by the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra, led by Marcelo Lehninger, with Joanna Mongiardo, soprano. For tickets: (973) 624-3713; njsymphony.org. March 9 at 8 p.m. $20 to $76. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9000; countbasietheatre.org.

RIDGEWOOD Ars Musica presents Puccini’s “La Bohème,” featuring young artist interns in solo roles. March 8 at 8 p.m. $20 and $25. West Side Presbyterian Church, 6 South Monroe Street. (973) 628-8793;

arsmusica.org.

SOUTH ORANGE Blues in the Loft: Michael Hill’s Blues Mob. March 3 at 7 p.m. $15. Harlem Gospel Choir. March 9 at 8 p.m. $20 to $35. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-278;

sopacnow.org.

TEANECK Classical Sundays at the Puffin: “The Nature of Things.” Students in Bard College Conservatory’s Vocal Arts Program present a program of songs exploring the natural world and the nature of love and despair. March 10 at 4 p.m. $10. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way.

puffinculturalforum.org; (201) 836-3499.

TRENTON Mendelssohn’s “Italian” Symphony performed by the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra, led by Marcelo Lehninger, with Joanna Mongiardo, soprano. For tickets: (973) 624-3713; njsymphony.org. March 8 at 7:30 p.m. $20 to $76. Trenton War Memorial, Memorial Drive. thewarmemorial.com; (609) 984-8400.

WAYNE Larry Harlow with the WPU Latin Jazz Ensemble, directed by Chico Mendoza. March 3 at 4 p.m. $8 to $15. Cirque Ziva, presented by the Golden Dragons Acrobats Company. March 8 at 8 p.m. $25 and $30. Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University, 300 Pompton Road. (973) 720-2371; wplive.org.

WEST LONG BRANCH Shawn Mullins and Mindy Smith, country. March 3 at 7 p.m. $30. Tommy Sands. March 4 at 7 p.m. $25. Justin Townes Earle, with a special guest, Tift Merritt. March 9 at 8 p.m. $25 and $40. Pollak Theater, Monmouth University, 400 Cedar Avenue. (732) 263-6889;

monmouth.edu/arts_events.

Outdoors

TENAFLY Maple Sugaring. Learn how to identify a maple tree and discover the history of syrup making, and how technology has changed the gathering of sap over the centuries. March 10, 12:30 to 1:30 p.m. $5 and $10. Tenafly Nature Center, 313 Hudson Avenue. (201) 568-6093;

tenaflynaturecenter.org.

Spoken Word

HOBOKEN The prose writers Iyanna Jones, Pamela Burke and Emil Draitser will present solo readings of selected stories. March 9 at 7:30 p.m. $10. Monroe Center for the Arts, 720 Monroe Street. (201) 795-5000;

monroecenter.com.

NEWARK Cesar Millan: The Dog Whisperer. March 10 at 7 p.m. $39.50 and $49.50. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

PRINCETON “The Pine Barrens: The Past, the Politics, and the Future: A Panel Discussion,” a conversation with former Govs. Brendan T. Byrne and Jim Florio; the author John McPhee; Michele S. Byers of the New Jersey Conservation Foundation; and others. March 3 at 2 p.m. Free. Morven Museum and Garden, 55 Stockton Street. (609) 924-8144; morven.org.

RANDOLPH Seventh Annual Festival of Storytelling and World Music. March 10, 12:30 to 6 p.m. $6. County College of Morris, 214 Center Grove Road. (973) 625-8548; njstorynet.org.

BRANCHBURG Cirque Ziva, presented by the Golden Dragons Acrobats Company. March 3 at 2 p.m. $25 and $35. The Theatre, Raritan Valley Community College, Route 28 and Lamington Road in North Branch. (908) 725-3420; rvccarts.org.

bergenpac.org; (201) 227-1030.

ramapo.edu/berriecenter/index.html.

MILLBURN “Lend Me a Tenor,” comedy by Ken Ludwig. Through March 10. $34 to $89. Paper Mill Playhouse, 22 Brookside Drive. (973) 376-4343; papermill.org.

PRINCETON “In the Next Room (or The Vibrator Play),” comedy by Sarah Ruhl. March 8 through 15. $10 and $15. Roger S. Berlind Theater, Nassau Street. (609) 258-2787.

RAHWAY “Eight Plays in Eight Days,” presented by the American Theater Group. Through March 10. $8. Hamilton Stage, 360 Hamilton Street. (732) 499-8226; ucpac.org.

SOUTH ORANGE The Spencers: Theater of Illusion. March 10 at 3 p.m. $25 to $40. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-2787; sopacnow.org.

WEST ORANGE “Carnaval,” a world premiere by Nikkole Salter. Through March 17. $25 and $35. Luna Stage, 555 Valley Road. lunastage.org; (973) 395-5551.

WEST WINDSOR “Avenue Q,” adult musical comedy with puppets. Through March 10. $14 to $18. Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College, 1200 Old Trenton Road.

kelseytheatre.net; (609) 570-3333.

Museums and Galleries

BOONTON “Illuminato,” Brenda A. Edwards, exhibition of paintings. Through March 31. Fridays and Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 7 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. S.H.E. Gallery, 819 Main Street. shegallery.com; (973) 335-0943.

CAPE MAY “Celebrating Community Service: Black Fraternal, Social and Civic Organizations of Cape May County,” exhibition. Through April 14. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. capemaymac.org; (609) 884-5404.

CLINTON “EastWest Ceramics: Clay Works Exhibition.” Through March 10. Donation: $5. Tuesdays through Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Hunterdon Art Museum, 7 Lower Center Street. hunterdonartmuseum.org; (908) 735-8415.

ENGLEWOOD “Paint Work,” solo exhibition featuring new work by Daniel Rios Rodriguez. Through April 14. Mondays through Thursdays, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. One River Gallery, 49 North Dean Street. (201) 266-5244; Onerivergallery.com.

EWING “Fear and Folly: The Visionary Prints of Francisco Goya and Federico Castellon,” etchings and lithographs organized by the Kalamazoo Institute of Arts. Through March 7. Free. Tuesdays through Thursdays, noon to 7 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 3 p.m. The College of New Jersey, 2000 Pennington Road. tcnj.edu/artgallery; (609) 771-2633.

HAMILTON “Heaven, Hell and the History of Punch,” works by Robert Taplin. Through April 10. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. (609) 586-0616; groundsforsculpture.org.

HOBOKEN “Mapping the Territory: Hudson County in Maps, 1840-2013.” Through June 30. $2. Tuesdays through Thursdays, 2 to 7 p.m.; Fridays, 1 to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Hoboken Historical Museum, 1301 Hudson Street.

hobokenmuseum.org; (201) 656-2240.

JERSEY CITY “Avatar: The Exhibition,” featuring interactive installations and original props. Through May 19. Free with admission, $12.50 and $16.75. Liberty Science Center, 222 Jersey City Boulevard. (201) 200-1000; lsc.org.

JERSEY CITY “Ray Smith: Here | Now,” featuring more than 100 works spanning three decades. March 3 through May 5. Mana Contemporary, 888 Newark Avenue. (201) 484-1495, ext. 672; manafinearts.com.

LINCROFT “34th Annual Juried Art Exhibition,” national group show. Through March 3. “From Plate to Paper: The Art of Printmaking.” A juried exhibition featuring over 75 works. March 10 through April 28. $7; members and children under 2, free. Tuesdays through Thursdays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Fridays, 10 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Monmouth Museum, 765 Newman Springs Road. (732) 747-2266;

monmouthmuseum.org.

MAHWAH “Les Paul in Mahwah,” about Les Paul, longtime Mahwah resident, inventor, musician and self-taught engineer. Through June 1. “Nike Missile Base in Mahwah: Nuclear Warheads in Our Backyard,” models of the base, which operated from 1955 to 1971, and of the missiles; vintage footage; and other artifacts of the period. Through June 30. $3 to $5; members and children under 5, free. Wednesdays, Saturdays and Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Mahwah Museum, 201 Franklin Turnpike. (201) 512-0099; mahwahmuseum.org.

MONTCLAIR “Redefining American Art: Organizers of the 1913 Armory Show in MAM’s Collection.” Through June 16. “The New Spirit: American Art in the Armory Show, 1913.” Through June 16. “Patterns, Systems, Structures: Abstraction in American Art.” Through May 19. $12; seniors and students, $10; members and children under 12, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Montclair Art Museum, 3 South Mountain Avenue. (973) 746-5555; montclairartmuseum.org.

MOORESTOWN “Photography 32,” group exhibition. Through March 3. Free. Perkins Center for the Arts, 395 Kings Highway. (856) 235-6488;

perkinscenter.org.

MORRISTOWN “Generation Next,” African-American art. Through March 22. Mondays through Fridays, 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. Atrium Gallery, Court Street. artintheatrium.org; (973) 285-5115.

MORRISTOWN “Thomas Nast: President Maker and Campaign Breaker,” illustrating presidential campaigns from 1864 to 1884. Through June 1. “Fine Collection: Treasures From the Vault Exhibit,” featuring over a dozen Oriental rugs from the museum’s collection. Feb. 24 through June 30. $6 to $8; children ages 6 to 12; $4 and children under 5, free. Wednesdays, Thursdays and Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Macculloch Hall Historical Museum, 45 Macculloch Avenue. maccullochhall.org; (973) 538-2404.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP “Toying with Art,” a juried exhibition featuring more than 40 works by more than 20 artists. Through March 17. “Immortal Structures: Here and There After,” featuring the contemporary realist paintings of New Jersey artist Maria Mijares. Through March 31. “Victorian New Jersey: Photographs by Guillermo Thorn,” an exhibition of photographs from the Kean University Collection. Through March 31. “Reflections: Contemporary Studio Art Glass,” featuring 40 works of contemporary studio art glass created by Kathleen Elliot and Paul Stankard. Through April 28. “Line, Color, and Pattern: Exquisite Textile Designs of Central and South America.” Through June 16. $7 and $10; members and children under 3 are free. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 11 a.m. to 8 p.m.; and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3700; morrismuseum.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK “Prints by Mary Cassatt: In the Company of Women,” 23 works from the Zimmerli’s holdings. Through March 3. “Lynd Ward Draws Stories: Inspired by Mexico’s History, Mark Twain and Adventures in the Woods.” Through June 30. “Leonid Sokov: Ironic Objects,” exhibition of about 50 works. Through July 14. “Henri-Gabriel Ibels,” featuring works on paper by French artist Henri-Gabriel Ibels. Through Sept. 8. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum, 71 Hamilton Street. (732) 932-7237;

zimmerlimuseum.rutgers.edu.

NEWARK “Expanding Africa at the Newark Museum: New Visions, New Galleries,” contemporary and historical works in different media. Through May 1. “Present Tense — Arts of Contemporary Africa,” works from the permanent collection, continuing. “In Her Eyes: Women Behind and in Front of the Camera,” photography by women that examines female identity. Through spring 2013. “African Cosmos: Stellar Arts,” an exhibition that explores the historical legacy of African cultural astronomy and its intersection with both traditional and contemporary African arts. Through Aug. 11. $6 to $10; members, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. newarkmuseum.org; (973) 596-6550.

OCEAN TOWNSHIP “Loved Ones Go to War: Local Stories of World War II,” the war’s effects on local veterans and those they left behind. Through June 1. Tuesdays and Wednesdays, 1 to 4 p.m.; Thursdays, 1 to 4 p.m. and 7 to 9 p.m.; and first and second Sundays of the month, 1 to 4 p.m. Admission is free. Township of Ocean Historical Museum, 703 Deal Road. oceanmuseum.org; (732) 531-2136.

PRINCETON “The Pine Barrens: A Legacy of Preservation: Photographs by Richard Speedy.” Through April 15. $5 and $6. Tuesdays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Morven Museum and Garden, 55 Stockton Street. (609) 924-8144; morven.org.

PRINCETON “Revealing the African Presence in Renaissance Europe,” paintings, drawings, sculpture and printed books of the period. Through June 9. “Picturing Power: Capitalism, Democracy and American Portraiture,” featuring 50 portraits from the now-dispersed New York Chamber of Commerce collection. March 9 through June 30. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 10 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Princeton University Art Museum, Nassau and Witherspoon Streets. (609) 258-3788; artmuseum.princeton.edu.

PRINCETON JUNCTION Energy and Motion: Photography Exhibit. March 3 through April 27. West Windsor Arts Center, 952 Alexander Road. westwindsorartscenter.org; (609) 716-1931.

SHREWSBURY “Eyesights 2013.” Open Juried Photography Show. Through April 3. Mondays through Fridays, 8 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Thursdays, 8 a.m. to 7 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Guild of Creative Art, 620 Broad Street. (732) 741-1441; guildofcreativeart.org.

TEANECK “Seeing the Future From the Past: Art Work by WPA Artists,” exhibition of various media. March 9 through April 4. Reception: March 9 at 7 p.m. Tuesdays through Thursdays, noon to 4 p.m. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. (201) 836-3499; puffinculturalforum.org.

UNION “Advocacy Quilts: A Voice for the Voiceless,” featuring 281 artists. Through May 10. Mondays, Tuesdays and Thursdays, noon to 7 p.m.; Wednesdays and Fridays, noon to 4 p.m. Kean University, 1000 Morris Avenue. (908) 737-0392;

kean.edu/gallery.
