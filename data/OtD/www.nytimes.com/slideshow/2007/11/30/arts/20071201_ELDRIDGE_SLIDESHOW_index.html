      Museum at Eldridge Street

      • Sign In to E-Mail or Save This

      The exterior of the Eldridge Street Synagogue.

      The 1887 Eldridge Street Synagogue’s main sanctuary reopens on Dec. 2 after a restoration that took 20 years and cost $20 million. “Even now,” writes Edward Rothstein, “as this space’s religious function has faded and been folded into the newly named Museum at Eldridge Street, and as Irving Howe’s 'world of our fathers' on the Lower East Side in New York becomes more like the 'world of our great-grandfathers' — it is possible to be awestruck by the exotic splendor of this meticulously restored sanctuary.”
