NEWS SUMMARY


Published: September 21, 1999

INTERNATIONAL A3-14

Big Quake Hits Taiwan, Leaving Hundreds Dead

A powerful earthquake rocked Taiwan before dawn today, killing hundreds and trapping hundreds more in collapsed buildings. Many people were sleeping when the earthquake struck and may have been unable to get out of buildings in time, becoming buried under debris. Taipei, the capital, appeared to be hard hit. A1

Warning Sounded at U.N.

Secretary General Kofi Annan, in an address on the opening day of debate in the General Assembly, urged the Security Council to act faster and more effectively to meet the challenge of a world engulfed in civil wars that quickly descend into the slaughter of helpless civilians. A1

Iraq Refuses Inspections

Iraq indicated that it was not ready to go along with any of the proposals for a new system for United Nations weapons inspections. A5

Peacekeepers Arrive in Dili

More than 1,000 Australian soldiers, the vanguard of an international peacekeeping force, took positions throughout the silent and broken capital of East Timor, Dili. There was no resistance, but no welcome, either. A1

Indonesia's top military commander, Gen. Wiranto, disputed reports of thousands of deaths in East Timor and said fewer than 100 people had been killed in the bloodshed. A10

U.S. Security Threats Outlined

A Federal advisory commission said the United States would face threats in the 21st Century from economic crises and terrorism and be drawn into more military interventions. A7

Colombia Reassures U.S.

President Andres Pastrana asserted that despite his Government's request for a huge new infusion of aid, the United States would not be drawn more deeply into Colombia's 40-year fight against leftist guerrillas. A3

K.L.A. Agrees to Disband

The Kosovo Liberation Army agreed to disband and be replaced by a civilian force that would be nominally defensive and only lightly armed. A8

Hearings on Money Laundering

Congress opens hearings today on allegations of Russian corruption and money laundering. Lawmakers expect a partisan fight over President Clinton's policies, and an examination of the effects on American and international financial systems. A14

World Briefing A6

NATIONAL A18-27

2d White Man Is Convicted In Dragging Death of Black

A Texas jury found Lawrence Russell Brewer, 32, guilty of capital murder in the beating and dragging death of a 49-year-old black man last year. Mr. Brewer, above, the second of three white men charged in the killing of James Byrd Jr. to be convicted, faces death or life in prison. A18

Gulf Storm Menaces Florida

A tropical storm in the Gulf of Mexico strengthened as it headed for Florida's west coast, bringing with it high winds and expectations of flooding today. A cruise ship of the Carnival Line, with more than 1,700 aboard, managed to restart one of its two stalled engines and get under way to try to avoid the storm. A26

Lab Security Found to Improve

The Energy Department said a review had found that security at the Los Alamos National Laboratory improved but that security procedures at the nation's two other weapons laboratories, Lawrence Livermore and Sandia, still lagged. A20

Vote for Medical Marijuana

Voters in the District of Columbia learned that 10 months ago they overwhelmingly voted in favor of a measure that would allow marijuana to be used for medical purposes. Congressional opponents of the measure blocked a count of the votes until Friday, when a Federal judge ruled that the ballots could be tallied. A27

Black Farmers File Bias Claims

Nearly 15,000 black farmers have applied for a share of a multibillion-dollar Federal settlement over past discrimination, far surpassing the expected number of claims, lawyers said. A25

A Break With Tradition

William R. Ferris, whom President Clinton appointed chairman of the National Endowment for the Humanities, announced that Mr. Clinton would give the annual Jefferson Lecture on the Humanities. Because the choice is usually a leading scholar, Mr. Clinton's selection raised concern in some quarters that the endowment might be politicized. A25

Pat Buchanan's Views

The shadow competition for the Presidential nomination of the Reform Party has become embroiled in Pat Buchanan's views, expressed in his new book, that Hitler presented no threat to the United States after Nazi Germany's initial victories of World War II. A22

NEW YORK/REGION B1-9

F.B.I. Supervisor Named To Lead New Jersey Police

Governor Whitman nominated Carson Dunbar, an F.B.I. supervisor in New York and a former state trooper, to head the New Jersey State Police. Mr. Dunbar, 47, who disclosed that he was the subject of a racial-profile traffic stop several years ago, promised to strengthen the force's recruitment and disciplinary procedures and change its culture so that racial bias is not tolerated. A1

Encephalitis in Westchester

Two people in Westchester County have contracted the mosquito-borne disease, officials said, adding they would begin spraying pesticide on the southern third of the county on Thursday. The cases are the first in the current outbreak to be reported outside of New York City. A1

Lingering Effects of Floyd

Parts of New York and New Jersey remained in the sodden grip of last week's storm, with thousands still homeless or lacking utilities. B1

Comparing Biographies

The publisher of ''I Have Not Yet Begun to Fight: A Life of John Paul Jones,'' by James Mackay, delayed release for a check on its similarities to the last major Jones biography. A1

BUSINESS DAY C1-24

Warning From Apple

Apple Computer said shipments, revenue and earnings for the current quarter would fall short as a result of smaller-than-expected deliveries of microprocessor chips from Motorola. The warning, issued after the markets closed, quickly drove down share prices almost 10 points in after-hours trading, to $69.875. C1

Auto Worker Contract Details

DaimlerChrysler's tentative contract with the U.A.W. contains company concessions that may create problems when the union demands similar concessions from G.M. and Ford, judging from contract details made available by the union. C1

Oil Talks, but Not a Merger

Chevron and Phillips have discussed a joint petrochemical venture, people close to the companies said, but no merger is imminent. C2

Rolls-Royce Buys Vickers

The engine maker Rolls-Royce has agreed to acquire Vickers for $933 million, saying the deal will create a giant in the growing market for engines and other propulsion systems to push ever-bigger ships at ever-higher speeds. Vickers sold Rolls-Royce Motor Cars, which is not connected with the engine maker Rolls-Royce, to Volkswagen last year. C4

Food Maker's Overhaul

Quaker Oats announced a three-year effort to streamline its North American operations, including cutting nearly 1,200 jobs, or 10 percent of the work force, closing plants, consolidating some product lines and taking charges against earnings. C7
