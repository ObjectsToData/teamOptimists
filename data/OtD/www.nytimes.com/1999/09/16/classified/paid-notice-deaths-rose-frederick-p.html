Paid Notice: Deaths ROSE, FREDERICK P.


Published: September 16, 1999

ROSE-Frederick P., master builder and creative philanthropist, son of Samuel B. and Belle B. Rose, died at home on Wednesday, September 15, 1999. Husband of Sandra Priest Rose, father of Deborah, Jonathan F.P., Samuel Priest, and Adam Raphael Rose; brother of Daniel and Elihu; grandfather of Ariel, Rachel and Sarah leaves a legacy of enormous love, warm friendships and immense generosity. His joy of life was expressed in his work and play. The service will be held at Westchester Reform Temple, 255 Mamaroneck Road, Scarsdale, NY, on Sunday, September 19, 1999 at 10AM. The family requests no flowers, and is not receiving visitors.

ROSE-Frederick P. The Officers, Board of Directors, and staff of UJA-Federation of New York are greatly saddened by the passing of Frederick P. Rose, an extraordinary philanthropist and cherished friend of the Jewish community. He will be deeply missed. As a compassionate leader who embraced the philanthropic traditions of his exceptionally generous family, he enriched the lives of countless individuals through his devotion to Jewish causes, and his magnificent support of major cultural and educational institutions in New York and throughout the country. As a loving father, he has inspired a future generation of philanthropists through his exemplary efforts on behalf of the Jewish community. The naming of the Samuel Priest Rose Building, in memory of Mr. Rose's son, reflects the family's commitment to creating a permanent facility for the Jewish Community Center on the Upper West Side, and a lasting resource for a thriving, diverse Jewish population. The contributions of Mr. Rose and his wife, Sandra, will be felt for generations to come. Together, they have strengthened UJA-Federation's network of health and human-service agencies through their longtime leadership and their support of F.E.G.S., the Lexington School for the Deaf, the Henry Kaufmann Campgrounds, and The Mount Sinai Medical Center. From 1974 to 1977, Mr. Rose served as president of the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies of New York, a predecessor to UJA-Federation. He held numerous positions with UJA-Federation over the years. We extend our sincerest condolences to Sandra; to Mr. Rose's daughter, Dr. Deborah Rose, and his son-inlaw, Professor Jan Stolwijk; his son Jonathan and daughter-in-law, Diana; his son, Adam, and Peter McQuillan; his brother Daniel and Daniel's wife, Joanna; his brother Elihu and Elihu's wife, Susan; and the entire Rose family. Judith Stern Peck, Board Chair James S. Tisch, President Stephen D. Solender, Exec V.P.

ROSE-Frederick Phineas. The trustees and staff of the American Museum of Natural History are deeply saddened and shocked by the death of beloved trustee Frederick P. Rose. Few have given so unstintingly of themselves to make this city a more glorious place in which to live. The lives of all New Yorkers have been enriched by his extraordinary vision, integrity, and generosity. From the sciences and humanities to the performing arts, Fred Rose's wide-ranging and enlightened philanthropy has made all our lives brighter, our institutions stronger, and our city more vibrant. He was a unique New Yorker, a treasure who will be sorely missed. For all he has done for the Museum, for New York, and the world, and for his joy, enthusiasm, tenacity, professionalism, and expansive vision, we are deeply grateful. We are especially heartbroken that he was snatched from our midst on the eve of the opening of his extraordinary legacy, the Frederick Phineas and Sandra Priest Rose Center for Earth and Space, which embodies his deep love of intellectual pursuit, great architecture, and fine design. His spirit will be with us for the opening and beyond. To his devoted wife and adored member of the Museum family Sandy, daughter Deborah, and sons Jonathan and Adam, we extend our most profound sympathies, and promise that Fred will always be with us in our thoughts. Farewell dear friend and colleague, we shall miss you but count you with us always in our hearts. Anne Sidamon-Eristoff, Chairman Ellen V. Futter, President American Museum of Natural History

ROSE-Frederick P. The Horace Mann Community is deeply saddened by the death of Frederick P. Rose HM'40, its cherished friend and former student, benefactor and dedicated philanthropist. Mr. Rose's discerning wisdom, his warm personality, his love of beautiful, well-crafted buildings, and his faith in the goodness of human beings will be missed beyond measure. It is a fitting tribute to Frederick Rose, to his family, and to his two brothers that the new Middle School at Horace Mann has been named Rose Hall. Generations of students and their families will benefit from this great legacy. The 1940 HM yearbook wrote: ''Ebullient Freddy bounced into Horace Mann as a Fourth Former. He sang in the Glee Club, reported for The Record, snapped photographs for The Mannikin, took notes as Secretary of the Debating Club, and played the accordion in the Music Club.'' Fred Rose's extraordinary intelligence and grace, his spirit and fortitude, and his exceptional generosity inspire us and will continue to inspire us here at Horace Mann. What he has bestowed on the School is a testament to his vision and his dedication. Cherished friend, we will miss you-a man of extraordinary talents. It was our great privilege to merit this continuing relationship with you. We extend our heartfelt condolences to his wife, the former Sandra Priest, to his children, Deborah, Jonathan, HM'70, and Adam Raphael, HM'77 and to his brothers Daniel Rose HM'47, and Elihu Rose HM'50 and their families. Michael D. Hess '58 Chair, Board of Trustees Dr. Eileen Mullady Head of School
