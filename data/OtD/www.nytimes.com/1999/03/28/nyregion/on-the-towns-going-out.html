ON THE TOWNS; GOING OUT


Published: March 28, 1999

An opinionated guide to cultural and recreational goings-on around the state this week. Items may be submitted by mail to On the Towns, Sunday New Jersey Section, The New York Times, 229 West 43d Street, New York, N.Y. 10036; by fax to (212) 556-7219, or by e-mail to njtowns@nytimes.com.

MUSIC

CLUB BENE John (Dr. John) Valby, pianist and comedian. Saturday at 9 P.M. Tickets: $15. Route 35, Sayreville. (732) 727-3000.

FIRST PRESBYTERIAN CHURCH Christina Valdes, pianist. Today at 3 P.M. Tickets: $5. Sanctuary Building, 42 Broad Street, Elizabeth. (908) 351-2000.

GEORGE WASHINGTON MIDDLE SCHOOL The Ridgewood Public Library presents Ronny Whyte, jazz pianist and songwriter, with his sextet. Today at 2 P.M. Tickets: $8. 155 Washington Place, Ridgewood. (201) 670-5600, extension 101.

J.C.C. OF METROPOLITAN NEW JERSEY The St. Lawrence String Quartet with music of Haydn, Schumann and Shostakovich. Tonight at 7:30 P.M. Tickets: $26; $24 for the elderly and students. 760 Northfield Avenue, West Orange. (973) 736-3200, extension 253.

KEAN COLLEGE Leila Josefowicz, violinist. Tuesday at 7:30 P.M. Tickets: $15 and $20; $10 for the elderly. Wilkins Theater, 1000 Morris Avenue, Union. (908) 527-2337.

LEOPARD LOUNGE AT CLUB BENE Crescent City Maulers, swing band. Tonight at 10. Tickets: $10. Dance lessons before show. Route 35, Sayreville. (732) 727-3000.

McCARTER THEATER Vladimir Feltsman, pianist, in music of Beethoven, Bach and Mussorgsky. Tuesday at 8 P.M. Tickets: $29 and $32. 91 University Place, Princeton. (609) 683-8000.

MAXWELL'S Soaked, Fuzzbubble and The Defenders, rock. Wednesday at 9 P.M. Tickets: $6. 1039 Washington Street, Hoboken. (201) 653-1703.

NEW JERSEY PERFORMING ARTS CENTER ''Sacred Music,'' featuring The Jenny Burton Experience, rhythm and blues and gospel choir. Saturday at 8 P.M. Tickets: $25. 1 Center Street, Newark. (888) 466-5722.

PRINCETON UNIVERSITY CHAPEL Frances Nobert, organist. Wednesday at 12:30 P.M. Free. William Street and Washington Road, Princeton. (609) 258-3654.

PRINCETON UNIVERSITY TAPLIN AUDITORIUM Martin Butler, pianist, in music of Scarlatti, Debussy and Brahms. Today at 3 P.M. Free. ''Interface,'' featuring Curtis Bahn, electric bassist, and Dan Trueman, electric violinist. Saturday at 8 P.M. Free. Fine Hall, Washington Road, Princeton. (609) 258-5000.

PRIORY JAZZ SERIES Roy Crosse Trio. Today, 11 A.M. to 3 P.M. No cover charge; food or beverage purchase required. The Atrium, St. Joseph Plaza, 233 West Market Street, Newark. (973) 639-7848.

ROWAN UNIVERSITY GLASSBORO CENTER FOR THE ARTS Bertram Greenspan, violinist, in music of Copland, Ravel and Wieniawski. Tomorrow at 8 P.M. Free. Glassboro Center for the Arts, Wilson Concert Hall, Mullica Hill Road, Glassboro. (609) 256-4545.

SALEM COMMUNITY COLLEGE Appel Farm Arts and Music presents Toby Keith and Steve McCormick, country singers. Saturday at 8 P.M. Tickets: $32; $28.50 in advance. DuPont Field House, 460 Hollywood Avenue, Carneys Point. (800) 394-1211.

SHANGHAI JAZZ Marlene VerPlanck, singer. Tonight, 6 to 9 P.M. Bucky Pizzarelli, guitarist. Wednesday, 7 to 9:30 P.M. No cover; $15 to $25 minimum. 24 Main Street, Madison. (973) 822-2899.

STOCKTON PERFORMING ARTS CENTER The Bridgeton/Bay Atlantic Symphony presents ''The Spanish World, Old and New.'' Today at 4 P.M. Tickets: $18 and $23. The Duke Ellington Orchestra. Today at 4 P.M. Tickets: $18 and $24. Jim Leeds Road, Pomona. (609) 652-9000.

UNITARIAN CHURCH IN SUMMIT Matt Sullivan, oboist, in his own compositions and music of Bach, Telemann and Schumann. Today at 3 P.M. Tickets: $15; $12 for the elderly; $2 for students. Waldron and Springfield Avenues, Summit. (908) 273-3245.

UNITY CONCERTS OF NEW JERSEY The National Symphony Orchestra of the John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts in Washington, D.C., directed by Leonard Slatkin, in music of Korngold, Barber, Thomson and Dvorak. Today at 3 P.M. Tickets: $45 to $55. Montclair High School Community Auditorium, Park and Chestnut Streets, Montclair. (973) 744-6770.

THEATER

BERGEN COUNTY PLAYERS ''And the World Goes 'Round,'' a musical revue. Through April 24. Today and next Sunday at 2 P.M.; Saturday at 8:30 P.M. Tickets: $15. The Little Firehouse Theater, 298 Kinderkamack Road, Oradell. (201) 261-4200.

COUNT BASIE THEATER Phoenix Productions presents ''Jesus Christ Superstar.'' Today at 3 P.M. Tickets: $18 and $22; $16 and $20 for the elderly. 99 Monmouth Street, Red Bank. (732) 747-0014.

CROSSROADS THEATER ''Lost Creek Township,'' by Charlotte A. Gibson. Previews: Thursday through Saturday at 8 P.M.; next Sunday at 3 P.M. Tickets: $24.50 to $31.50. 7 Livingston Avenue, New Brunswick. (732) 249-5560.

ELIZABETH PLAYHOUSE ''No Time for Comedy,'' by S. N. Behrman. Through next Sunday. Today and next Sunday at 2 P.M.; Friday and Saturday at 7:30 P.M. Tickets: $8; $6 for the elderly and students. 1100 East Jersey Street, Elizabeth. (908) 355-0077.

GEORGE STREET PLAYHOUSE ''Jolson Sings Again,'' by Arthur Laurents. Today at 2 P.M. Tickets: $22 to $38. ''Collected Stories,'' starring Uta Hagen. Previews: Saturday at 8 P.M. and next Sunday at 7 P.M. Tickets: $22 to $32. 9 Livingston Avenue, New Brunswick. (732) 246-7717.

JOHN HARMS CENTER FOR THE ARTS The Chinese Golden Dragon Acrobats. Today at 3 P.M. Tickets: $20 to $36. 30 North Van Brunt Street, Englewood. (201) 567-3600.

HELEN HAYES PERFORMING ARTS CENTER ''Missing Footage,'' by Gen LeRoy. Through April 25. Thursday through Saturday at 8 P.M. Tickets: $20 to $35. 117 Main Street, Nyack, N.Y. (914) 358-6333.

LUNA STAGE The Woman's Work theater series presents ''Pandora's Box,'' by Daria Finn. Today at 2 P.M. Tickets: $20; $15 for the elderly and students. 7 Oak Place, Montclair. (973) 744-3309.
