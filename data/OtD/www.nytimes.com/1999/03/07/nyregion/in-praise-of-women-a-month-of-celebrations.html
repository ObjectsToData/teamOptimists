In Praise of Women: A Month of Celebrations


Published: March 7, 1999

March is the 13th annual Women's History Month. Here are some opportunities to join in New Jersey's observance.

BERGEN COMMUNITY COLLEGE Phoebe Snow, singer and songwriter. Friday at 8 P.M. Tickets: $23. (201) 447-7428. ''Women's Issues 2000: A Conference for Women 50 and Over.'' March 18, 9:30 A.M. to 3 P.M. Tickets: $10.(201) 447-7156. 400 Paramus Road, Paramus.

BURLINGTON COUNTY HISTORICAL SOCIETY The New Jersey Council for the Humanities presents ''Women and the Vote: Past, Present and Future,'' a lecture series. Programs include ''Alice Paul: New Jersey's Heroine for Equality,'' by Barbara Irvine, March 28 at 4 P.M.; ''African-American Women in the Struggle for Woman Suffrage,'' by Deborah Gray White, and ''The Equal Rights Amendment: Unfinished Business for the Constitution,'' by Roberta Francis, April 11 at 2 P.M.; and ''Woman Suffrage in New Jersey: Story and Satire,'' by Margaret Crocco. May 2 at 2 P.M. 457 High Street, Burlington City. (609) 695-4838.

CENTENARY COLLEGE Barbara Wind, poet, and Anne Cope Wallace, author of ''Setting Psychological Boundaries,'' Cope Wallace, author of ''Setting Psychological Boundaries.'' Today at 3 P.M. 400 Jefferson Street, Hackettstown. (908) 852-1400, extension 2374.

NEW JERSEY STATE AQUARIUM ''Women's Wave,'' an exposition featuring exhibitions by health care and corporate organizations. March 27, noon to 4 P.M. Free with aquarium admission. 1 Riverside Drive, Camden. (609) 365-3300.

NEW JERSEY STATE MUSEUM ''Our Mothers Before Us: Women and Democracy, 1789 to 1920,'' featuring women's writings to Congress, photographs, letters and memorabilia. Through April 11. Hours: Tuesdays through Saturdays, noon to 5 P.M.; Sundays, noon to 5 P.M. Free. 205 West State Street, Trenton.

(609) 292-6464.

NOYES MUSEUM OF ART A lecture by Rita Bernstein, photographer. Thursday, 1 to 3 P.M. Tickets: $3; free for members. Lily Lake Road, Oceanville. (609) 652-8848.

OLD BARRACKS MUSEUM ''Putting Women in Place: Historic Site Interpretation,'' a conference. Saturday, 9:30 A.M. to 5 P.M. Tickets: $45; $40 for members. Barrack Street, Trenton. (609) 396-1776.

RUTH L. ROCKWOOD MEMORIAL LIBRARY The New Jersey Council for the Humanities presents ''Focus on Fashion: Revealing Garments From Togas to T-Shirts.'' Lectures include ''The Foundations of Fashion: A Brief History of Underwear and Ideals,'' by Kristine Haugland, March 23 at 7:30 P.M.; ''The Fashion Makers: Haute Couture, Clothing the Masses,'' by Amy Simon Hopwood, March 30 at 7:30 P.M.; ''Fashion: Frivolous or Fundamental,'' by Dennita Sewell, April 6 at 7:30 P.M.; ''Topping It All: The Art and Craft of Millinery,'' by Tracy Carluccio and Terri Gabriell, April 13 at 7:30 P.M., and ''The Latest Mode: Dress in the Modern Age,'' by Kristina Haugland, April 20 at 7:30 P.M. 10 Robert Harp Drive, Livingston. (609) 695-4838.

RUTGERS UNIVERSITY 18th annual New Jersey Women of Achievement Awards. Tomorrow at 5 P.M. at the Neilson Dining Hall, Douglass College campus, New Brunswick. Tickets: $15, including dinner. Reservations required: (732) 932-9603. The Writers at Rutgers Series presents Alice Notley, poet. Tomorrow at 8 P.M. at Rutgers Student Center. Free. (732) 932-7213. ''National College Roundtable on Women's Health.'' Tuesday at 7 P.M. at Rutgers Student Center. (732) 932-7710. ''Private Eye,'' an exhibition featuring figurative and abstract paintings. Through March 21. ''Ahead of Her Time,'' an exhibition chronicling the life of Mary Clara Kangler. Through March 25. Both at the Mabel Smith Douglass Library. Hours: Mondays through Thursdays, 8 A.M. to 1 A.M.; Fridays, 8 A.M. to 9 P.M.; Saturdays, 10 A.M. to 8 P.M.; Sundays, noon to 1 A.M. (732) 932-9626.

TRENTON CITY MUSEUM ''Linked to Landscape: Nature and Self,'' paintings by Cassandra Kabler, Jeanne Gentry Keck and Gail Watkins. Through April 4. Hours: Tuesdays through Saturdays, 11 A.M. to 3 P.M.; Sundays, 2 to 4 P.M. Free. Ellarslie, Cad walader Park, Trenton. (609) 989-3632.
