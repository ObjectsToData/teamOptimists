    For the College-Bound, Are There Any Safe Bets?

    Introduction

    Marcin Szczepanski/University of Michigan, College of Engineering By the numbers, engineering students fare well in the job market.

    Once, a graduate degree in a practical field like law or medicine was seen as a ticket to a stable career. Now they’re no sure bet.

    So in today’s economy, what should ambitious young people pursue? Which majors and careers have a reliable “return on investment”?

    Debaters

    • Numbers: Not Just for Math Majors Anymore

      Katie Bardaro, PayScale.com

      The growing fields are in science, technology, engineering and math. You don't have to major in these; you could take a few classes and reap the benefits.

    • The Reality of College Choice

      Lynn O'Shaughnessy, author, "The College Solution"

      Most applicants don't need to study the nationwide rankings; they will attend a state school, probably within a two-hour drive of home.

    • Preparing for Citizenship, Not Just a Job

      Ellen McCulloch-Lovell, Marlboro College

      Consider a civic scorecard: Do a college's graduates vote regularly? Advocate causes? Participate in their children’s schools?

    • Think Like a Career Coach

      Nick Sedlet and Elli Sharef, co-founders, HireArt.com

      Pick a career for which demand will increase as a result of technology, not one that will be replaced because of it.

    • A Liberal Arts Foundation

      William Pannapacker, Hope College

      Assume that you will have many careers. Become the kind of person who is interested in everything and can do anything.
