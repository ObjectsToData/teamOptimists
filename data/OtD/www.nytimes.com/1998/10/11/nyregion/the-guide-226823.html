THE GUIDE

By BARBARA DELATINER
Published: October 11, 1998

Best Bet

The Oyster Festival, a major fall fair on the Island, comes to -- where else? -- Oyster Bay next weekend. The 15th annual festival features an arts and crafts show, varied entertainment, paddle boat tours, and historical exhibitions on boat and oyster farming both days; a five-kilometer run and an oyster eating and schucking contest on Saturday, and a children's hour, conducted by the Theodore Roosevelt Sanctuary on Sunday. It all takes place along the village waterfront (922-8082).

Free Choices

TODAY ''Baroque Sundays at 3,'' one of several free concert series presented at the State University at Stony Brook, resumes with a performance by Rachel Begley and Friends. Ms. Begley, a Stony Brook alumna and a leading recorder artist on the Island, performs lively dances, poignant tunes and unusual variations from the Renaissance and Baroque periods in the Staller Center's Recital Hall (632-ARTS).

WEDNESDAY Patti Davis, the daughter of Ronald and Nancy Reagan, participates in the annual ''Celebration of Survivorship'' at Adelphi University in Garden City. In the keynote speech for the program, sponsored by the Adelphi New York Statewide Breast Cancer Hotline and Support Program, Ms. Davis, whose mother had breast cancer, is to discuss ''Public Lives, Private Healing: Surviving Breast Cancer in the Family,'' at 7 P.M. in the University Center Ballroom and will be followed by a panel session featuring breast cancer survivors including Claire Shulman, the Queens Borough President. (Reservations: 877-4325.)

THURSDAY The Shenandoah Shakespeare Express, a touring troupe of 11 actors who pride themselves on performing the works as closely as possible to the way they were originally done, pulls into Nassau Community College in Garden City with a double feature showing the playwright in both his comic and tragic modes: ''The Taming of the Shrew'' at 12:30 P.M. and ''Richard III'' at 7:30 P.M. in the College Center Building (572-7185).

'Music at Hillwood'

Beethoven has been neglected lately. At least, that's what Caroline Stoessinger, the artistic director of Music at Hillwood, believes. This year, its 10th-anniversary season, the series of chamber music concerts, accompanied by commentary about the music and the music-makers from Ms. Stoessinger, will turn its attention to Beethoven. ''There's so much great Beethoven to do,'' Ms. Stoessinger said, ''that the musicians I approached about appearing in the series this year, were all happy about playing it, about illuminating what we love and what we know.''

So all four ''Music at Hillwood'' outings will feature Beethoven and Beethoven alone, beginning today at 3 P.M., when Ms. Stoessinger, a pianist, is joined by eight musicans to perform the Trio for Violin and Cello, Opus 70, No. 1 (''Ghost''), ''An Die Ferne Geliebte'' (''To the Distant Beloved''), Opus 98, and the Septet in E flat, Opus 20. The performers include Randall Scarlata, a baritone who grew up in Southampton and won the 1997 international Schubert song'' competition in Vienna, and Yeoy-Cheng Ma, a violist who isthe executive director of the Manhasset-based Children's Orchestra Society and the cellist Yo-Yo Ma's sister. The concert is in the Hillwood Recital Hall on the C. W. Post campus in Brookville (299-3100).

At the Libraries

George Gershwin's works are featured in two concerts this week: today at 2 P.M. when Christy Welty, lyric soprano; Paul Straney, bass baritone, and Christine Dore, pianist, offer '' 'S Wonderful, 'S Marvelous, 'S Gershwin,'' courtesy of the Long Beach Library, at Ye Olde Firehouse on Lido Boulevard in Point Lookout (432-7200) and Saturday at 8 P.M. when Dick Smolens and the Long Island Sound perform ''Gershwin and Friends'' at the Syosset Library on South Oyster Bay Road (921-7161).

If good old movies appeal to you, the place to be on Friday is the Half Hollow Library on Vanderbilt Parkway in Dix Hills, where ''I Married a Witch'' will be shown at 7:30 P.M.

(421-4530).

Italian Heritage

Thanks to Christopher Columbus, who landed in the Bahamas on Oct. 12, 1492, October has become Italian Heritage Month over the years. Celebrations on the Island are extensive, especially this week when

tomorrow, Nassau Community College is host to its 13th annual ''Italian Heritage Day Conference.'' This year, the subject, ''New York at One Hundred: Italian-Americans Come to Long Island,'' will be covered in lectures and discussions from 9 A.M. to 12:15 P.M. in the College Center Building on the Garden City campus (572-9649).

Wednesday brings ''Italian-American Visions: A Film Celebration'' to the Shelter Rock Library on Searingtown Road in Albertson, with Tony De Nonno, a film writer, director and producer, at 7:30 P.M

(437-0621).

The week ends Saturday with a performance by I Paesani, the Italian folk dance troupe, on Saturday at 7:30 P.M. at the New Hyde Park High School on Leonard Boulevard (746-7140).

'Humanities Month'

October is also New York State's first State Humanities Month. With a budget of $120,000 for the entire state, the New York Council for the Humanities will support 44 events at 15 different locations throughout the Island.
