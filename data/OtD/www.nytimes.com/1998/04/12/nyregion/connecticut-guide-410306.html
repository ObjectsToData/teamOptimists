CONNECTICUT GUIDE

By ELEANOR CHARLES
Published: April 12, 1998

Fighting Fire With Robots

Trinity College's annual Fire-Fighting Home Robot Contest is scheduled on Saturday from noon to 8 P.M. and next Sunday from 9 A.M. to 1 P.M. in the 2,000-seat Ferris Athletic Center on the Hartford campus.

Spectators can watch the show from bleachers and on television monitors as some 50 contestants, ranging from fourth graders to M.I.T. professors and NASA scientists put their computer-controlled robots through their paces.

Down on the floor of the arena is an 8- by 8-foot house with rooms, hallways, furniture and a lighted candle that is moved around for each new competitor to find and extinguish. ''The robot has 3 tries of 6 minutes each to find the candle,'' said Jake Mendelssohn, adjunct professor of engineering at Trinity. ''If it trips on a rug or bumps into walls and furniture it has to start over. Most of them don't find it, but the lucky ones can do it in 10 to 15 seconds.'' They must get to within 1 foot of the candle to put it out.

The prototype robots range in size from 1 cubic foot up. None of them resembles anything as cute and cuddly as R2D2. They can be programmed for heat, light, ultraviolet or whatever, but they run on computers, not hand-held remote controls. Winners receive cash prizes of up to $1,000, or useful equipment.

Admission is $4, free for students. The college is at 300 Summit Street, and the number to call for more information is (860) 233-2379.

Dance Masters

Modern dance in most of its permutations from ballet to hip hop will offered at the Shoreline Alliance for the Arts' Dance Master Class Weekend in Guilford from Friday through next Sunday at the Greene Community Center and includes a public performance on Saturday at 7:30 P.M.

The dancers, who will instruct children from the age of 8 to teen-agers, adults and professionals, include Roxane D'Orleans Juste of the Jose Limon Company, Wendy S. Brown of Marlies Yearby's Movin' Spirits Dance Theater, Adam Battelstein of Pilobolus; Leslie Browne, formerly of American Ballet Theater; David Grenke of Thingsezisee'm Dance Theater, Willie Feuer of Dances For 2, and Clyde Evans, Jr., Brandon Albright and Ron Wood of Rennie Harris Puremovement. Most of the instructors will appear in the Saturday evening performance, for which tickets are $12, $8 for children through age 18. To obtain tickets, or information on class fees and registration call (203) 453-3890.

On the Marquis de Sade

Francine du Plessix Gray will talk about the Marquis de Sade, who is the subject of her newest book entitled ''At Home with the Marquis de Sade: A Life,'' on Wednesday at 11:30 A.M. at the University of Hartford. The book will be published this summer.

In addition to her authorship of many best-sellers translated into numerous foreign languages, Ms. Gray has taught at Columbia, Yale and Princeton Universities. She is currently the Annenberg Fellow at Brown University.

Her lecture will be held in Wilde Auditorium on the campus at 200 Bloomfield Avenue, West Hartford, and admission is free. The number for more information is (860) 768-4100.

Restoring Old Houses

The Fairfield Historical Society will hold its first house restoration Show at the Burr Homestead, 739 Old Post Road in the town of Fairfield on Saturday from 10 to 4:30 and next Sunday from 11 to 4:30.

More than 20 vendors will share their expertise in decorative painting, relocating historic houses and barns, restoring 18th-century ironwork, wainscoting, paneling, flooring, chimneys, gaslights and many more projects.

Jackie Connell, an architectural consultant, and John Leeke, preservation consultant, will lecture on Saturday and Sunday, respectively, at 2 P.M., after which they will discuss slides and photographs brought by visitors.

Admission will be $5 for the show and an additional $5 for each lecture. A resource directory with detailed lists of renovation services will be given away with each ticket sold.

Admission to a catered preview party and silent auction on Friday from 6:30 to 9 P.M. will be $50 to $150. Auction items include an 11-foot L. L. Bean canoe, cocktail party for 30, tour of the Metropolitan Museum of Art with a docent, custom-designed playhouse and an eagle-watching cruise on the Connecticut River. The number is (203) 259-1598 for preview reservations, directions or other information.

Walking in Devil's Den

Three walks in the woods of the 1,746-acre Devil's Den Preserve in Weston are offered this week, free of charge. An adult hike led by two natural history guides is scheduled on Tuesday from 1 to 3 P.M., focused on spring greenery, wildflowers and birds along seldom-trod trails. No reservations are necessary.

On Friday, from 7:30 to 9:30 P.M., adults, and children no younger than 8, will search for newts, toads, frogs and salamanders risen from their muddy winter shelters to mate in ponds. Dr. Nelson Gelfman, an amphibian enthusiast, will lead the group. Waterproof footgear and flashlights are essential and reservations are needed.

A moderately strenuous eight-mile hike along the Saugautuck River will start on Saturday at 9 A.M. and end around 2 P.M. Three guides will carpool with the hikers to the west branch of the river, trek over two great ledges and stop for lunch at an overlook of the Saugatuck Reservoir and valley. Reservations, hiking shoes and bag lunches are prerequisites; cameras are optional. The number is (203) 226-4991 for reservations and directions to the Pent Road meeting place.

Russian Folk Crafts

Intricately painted and lacquered eggs, boxes, brooches and pendants, figurines, toys, nesting dolls, tableware, and handmade and embroidered textiles are some of the Russian folk crafts on display at Living Traditions in Bristol through the month of April. Prices range from $3 to $800.

The gallery, true to its name, specializes in traditional folk art from around the world, produced by contemporary artists using the techniques and materials of the past. This exhibition was assembled in conjunction with Russkaya Artists' Group Trade Association, in Marietta, Ohio. Since 1993 the association has imported Russian folk art through its Russian-based partner, Tanya Lomonosova.

The gallery is at 95 Maple Street and directions or more information are available by calling (860) 584-5585.

Assessing the Amistad

''Reflections on Amistad'' is the subject of the 23d Annual Connecticut Humanities Conference to be held on Saturday from 9:30 to 2:45 at the Connecticut Historical Society, 1 Elizabeth Street in Hartford.

Open to the public, the conference will explore the Connecticut context of the Amistad incident, methods of teaching it, ways that it is being interpreted in museums, its complex legal aspects, and the impact of the Steven Spielberg film.

Scholars who will discuss these issues include James Horton, professor of American Studies at George Washington University and director of the Afro-American Communities project at the Smithsonian Institution. His topic will be ''Celebrating the Amistad: Anti-slavery, the Underground Railroad and the Free Black Community.''

Joining him in the series of talks will be Stacy Close of Eastern Connecticut State University, Kate Steinway and Robert Richter of the Connecticut Historical Society, Fred Dalzell of Mystic Seaport, Frank Middletown of the National Park Service and Kent Newmyer of the University of Connecticut. A fee of $40 includes a box lunch and reception. The number for reservations or more information is (860) 236-5621. ELEANOR CHARLES
