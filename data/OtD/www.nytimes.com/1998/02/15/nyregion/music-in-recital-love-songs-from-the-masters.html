MUSIC; In Recital, Love Songs From the Masters

By ROBERT SHERMAN
Published: February 15, 1998

THE Fairfield Chorale continues its 35th anniversary celebrations at the Pequot Library in Southport with a recital of love songs. The 4 o'clock program has been designed as an afternoon of choreographed cabaret, with 23 members of the chorale presenting songs by Kern, the Gershwins, Porter, Rodgers and Hart and other composers of American popular music. The number to call for more information is (203) 254-1333.

In another 4 o'clock anniversary concert this afternoon, Richard Coffey, who has been minister of music at South Church in New Britain since 1972, celebrates the 25th birthday of the Gress-Miles organ there, playing works of Franck and Dupre as well as tributes to Valentine's Day and Black History Month (860) 233-7555.

The romantic intrigues chronicled with glee in Strauss's ''Fledermaus'' can be heard this afternoon at 2 at the Shubert in New Haven; the cast includes Julianne Borg, Ryan MacPherson, student artists of Yale Opera and the actor Theodore Bikel in the non-singing role of the drunken jailer (203) 624-1825.

Five trios can also be heard in concert today. When the Eroica Trio convenes for Newtown Friends of Music in a 3 o'clock recital at Edmond Town Hall, the pianist Erika Nickrenz, the violinist Adela Pena and the cellist Sara Sant-Ambrogio play Beethoven's ''Ghost'' and Ravel's A Minor Trios as well as a transcription of Gershwin's Piano Preludes (203) 426-6470.

A piano trio written last year by Ezra Laderman is a highlight of the Connecticut Trio's 3 o'clock program at the New Britain Museum of American Art, but Gerard Rosa, Julie Ribchinsky and Linda Laurent will also be offering violin, cello and piano works by Bach, Brahms and Sarasate (860) 229-0257. The program will be repeated at 12:30 P.M. on Tuesday at Central Connecticut State University (860) 832-2912.

At 4 this afternoon the music series at Avon Memorial United Methodist Church is presenting the Adaskin String Trio (a graduate ensemble in residence at the Hartt School) and the Connecticut-based Trillium Voices. The Adaskin emsemble has chosen to perform works by Beethoven, Dohnanyi and a new composition, ''The Point of Pressure,'' written for them by Ingrid Stolzel. The a cappella trio offers songs from Elizabethan madrigals to modern pop standards arranged by a member of the group, Barbara Robbins (860) 673-2111.

And in West Hartford, two members of the Camerata Ensemble -- the cellist Laura Kane and the pianist Carolyn Woodard -- have asked the violinist Peter Winograd to join them in the Trio in D Minor by Anton Arensky, the rest of today's 4 o'clock program at Seaverns Hall devoted to duos of Rachmaninoff, Cassado and Tchaikovsky (860) 521-8085.

It make take only two to tango, but when the Connecticut Classical Guitar Society throws a tango festival, four artists will be participating: the guitarist Ricardo Cobo, the flutist Lisa Arkis and the dancers Armando Orzuza and Daniela Arcuri (who starred on Broadway in ''Tango Passion'' and appeared with Madonna in the film version of ''Evita'').

The marathon Saturday schedule begins at the Connecticut Historical Society, where Mr. Orzuza and Ms. Arcuri hold a 1 P.M. tango class for beginners, intermediate level classes at 3. An 8 o'clock concert features Mr. Cobo, Ms. Arkis and the dancers. From around 10 until midnight, Mr. Orzuza and Ms. Arcuri lead a milonga, a traditional late-night tango party. Tickets for the concert are $20, for the entire festival $40 (860) 249-7041.

The Pro Arte Singers are holding what they hope will be the first annual High School Choral Festival, with more than 100 students representing choirs from Stamford, New Canaan, Newtown and other communities, participating in workshops, recitals and master classes at Western Connecticut State University in Danbury. On Saturday, beginning at 6:30 P.M. in Ives Concert Hall, the group's director, Arthur Sjogren, will lead the choirs in folk songs, spirituals and two sections of Mozart's Mass in C (203) 322-5970.

A concert at Sprague Memorial Hall on Friday at 12:30 P.M. includes performances by Yale chamber groups and an ensemble from the New Haven Arts and Humanities Cooperative High School (203) 432-4158, and three musicians are giving free recitals in Wesleyan University's Young Organ Virtuosi Festival: Joel Bacon appears at 8 P.M. on Friday, Le Ray Yu at 4 Saturday afternoon, and Tom Trenney that evening at 8, all in Memorial Chapel (860) 685-3355. And three ensembles of the Norwalk Youth Symphony perform in a ticketed recital at the Norwalk Concert Hall next Sunday afternoon at 3 (203) 866-4100.

Musicians from Marlboro are offering performances of Ravel's Violin and Cello Sonata as well as Piano Quartets by Dvorak and one of his students, Vitezslav Novak (1870-1949), to their 8 P.M. Saturday concert at the Stamford Jewish Community Center (203) 322-7900. A free recital at 8 P.M. in Sprague Hall will be given by the Yale faculty pianist Sara Laimon, playing Bach, Schumann and Copland (203) 432-4158.

A soloist and two ensembles are visiting this week as well. On Thursday evening at 8, Canadian Brass is at the Stamford Palace (203) 325-4466; the pianist Awadagin Pratt appears in recital at 8 P.M. on Friday at the Quick Center, Fairfield University (203) 254-4010, and Isaac Stern, Yo-Yo Ma, Jaime Laredo and Emanuel Ax perform at 8 P.M. on Saturday at the Bushnell (860) 246-6807.
