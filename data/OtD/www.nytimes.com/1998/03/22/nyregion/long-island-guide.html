LONG ISLAND GUIDE

By BARBARA DELATINER
Published: March 22, 1998

Free Choices

TODAY The Con Brio Ensemble performs chamber music influenced by opera at 2 P.M. at Fortunoff Hall on the Oakdale campus of Dowling College (224-5420). Also at 2 P.M, but in Locust Valley at the Library on Buckram Road, Molly Vivian Huang, a pianist, plays Chopin, Liszt, Mendelssohn and Mozart (671-1837).

TOMORROW The Interfaith Community Center of Hofstra University, joined by the American Jewish Committee, holds a symposium, ''Women and the Word: Sacred Texts, Sacred Stories,'' at 7:30 P.M. in the Student Center on the Hempstead campus (463-6920).

FRIDAY In a program called ''Piano Music by Romantic Women Composers,'' Judith Alstadter, a pianist, plays works by Clara Schumann, Fanny Mendelsohn and others at 8:15 P.M. at the Syosset Library on South Oyster Bay Road (921-7161).

For Youngsters

With five special events on the schedule, this is a good week to be young. Today there are four performances: the Brewery Troupe's ''Crowations'' appear at 1 and 3 P.M. at Chelsea Center off Route 25A in East Norwich (571-8550); Friends of the Arts' ''Children's Carousel'' offers ''Animal Tales'' by Felix Pitre at 2 P.M. in Planting Fields' Hay Barn in Oyster Bay (922-0061); the Paper Bag Players perform at 1 and 4 P.M. at Staller Center on the Stony Brook campus of the State University (632-7230), and the Rockaway-Five Towns Symphony Orchestra contributes a Young People's Concert, ''Do You Hear What I Hear?'', at 2:30 P.M. in the Lawrence Woodmere Academy on Woodmere Boulevard in Woodmere (239-7828). ''Weinerville Live!'' has the field all to itself Saturday at 11 A.M. and 2 P.M. at the Westbury Music Fair on Brush Hollow Road (334-0800).

By Youngsters

Local young musicians also entertain in three concerts this week: the North Shore Youth Orchestras appear at 4 P.M. today at Great Neck South High School on Lakeville Road (365-2599); ensembles from the Camerata String and Chamber Orchestras collaborate with dancers from the Circle of Dance and Nassau Dance Theater in a program at 3 P.M. today at the Maguire Theater on the Old Westbury campus of the State University (766-5405), and members of the Long Island Composers Alliance provide the original music for 25 young artists who play at 8 P.M. Friday at Chelsea Center in East Norwich (571-8550).

'Sustainable Development'

To promote ecologically sound and ''socially just'' development on the Island, Long Island University has organized the Institute for Sustainable Developement that will be based at its C.W. Post and Southampton campuses. The institute will spread the word through a spring series of talks and films; this week featuring two lectures: Tuesday by Bob Stein, founder of Vision Huntington, who will discuss ''Alternatives To Suburban Sprawl,'' in the C. W. Post Cinema, and Thursday by Bob Deluca of the Group for the South Fork, who will talk about his organization's continuing efforts, in Montauk Room 113 at Southampton College. Both are at 7:30 P.M. (287-8800).

'Shakespeare Festival'

For its 49th annual ''Shakespeare Festival,'' Hofstra University's theater department has polished up its duplicate of the Globe Theater and rolled it onto the stage of the John Cranford Adams Playhouse to be the host of ''All's Well That Ends Well.'' The dark comedy will run from Thursday through April 5, accompanied by programming like a symposium at 3 P.M. Wednesday in the Cultural Center; the annual High School Student Shakespearian Scene Competition, 3 P.M. Friday and April 3 in the playhouse, and its companion pieces, ''All's Swell,'' the Collegium Musicum's concert, and the comedy ''The Knight of the Burning Pestle,'' at 2 P.M. Saturday and April 4 (463-6644).

The New Millennium

To celebrate its 10th anniversary and the 40th anniversary of the State University at Stony Brook, the Humanities Institute, based on that campus, is holding a two-day conference, ''New Millennium, New Humanities.'' Scholars from all over the country will gather Friday and Saturday to explore such subjects as ''New Technologies, New Arts''; ''New Millennium, New Arts'' and ''New Millennium, New Humanities/ Science.'' Performing arts also figure in the equation with ''Fire,'' a film by Deepa Mehta, an Indian director, at 7 P.M. Friday, and the performance artist Guillermo Gomez-Pena's ''Visual Activist: Chicano Art at the End of the Century'' at 8 P.M. Saturday (632-7390).

'Cultural Survival' Tales

Carmen Agra Deedy, a Cuban-born storyteller and a regular contributor to National Public Radio's ''All Things Considered'' and ''Latino USA,'' comes to Huntington's Heckscher Museum of Art Friday at 8 P.M. with ''Overcoming Hard Times: Resilient Tales of Cultural Survival,'' the first of two storytelling concerts for adults. The program will be repeated Saturday at 10:30 A.M. at the museum on Prime Avenue (351-3250), and Ms. Deedy will conduct a storytelling workshop 2 P.M. Saturday at the Huntington Arts Council on Main Street (271-2511).

'I-Con' Again

The I-Con Science Fiction, Fact and Fantasy Convention, the largest entry of its kind on the East Coast, pulls into the State University at Stony Brook Friday for its 17th annual visit. As usual there will be visits from such space heroes as Claudia Christian, Commander Susan Ivanova in ''Babylon 5, and Nana Visitor, Major Kira in ''Star Trek: Deep Space Nine''; appearances by authors like Norman Spinrad and Frederik Pohl; displays; films, cabaret and other entertainment; vendors and even a charity auction to benefit the Long Island AIDS Action Coalition. This all goes on from 6 P.M. to 2 A,.M. Friday, 10 A.M. to 2 A.M. Saturday and 10 A.M. to 2 P.M. next Sunday at various sites on the campus (632-5045).

Jazz Benefit

At their 15th annual benefit for Programs for Living, Understanding and Services for Autistic and Other Developmentally Disabled Adults, P.L.U.S. Group Homes Inc., Arvell Shaw and the Louis Armstrong Legacy Band will be joined by Angela DeNiro, the jazz vocalist, and the Ron Aprea Quartet. The concert, which will honor Phillip Smith and Sonny Meyerwitz, is at 8 P.M. Saturday at Five Towns College in Dix Hills (338-6650).

From Peking

On its first United States tour in 10 years, the Peking Opera comes to Tilles Center on the C. W. Post campus in Brookville Saturday for two appearances. At 2 P.M., the 47-member troupe, which combines music, dance and pantomime to create what critics call a ''glittering theatrical spectacle,'' will offer a special one-hour performance geared to families as part of Tilles' ''Stage Two: The Arts Start Here'' series. The full-length performance is at 8 P.M. (299-3100). BARBARA DELATINER
