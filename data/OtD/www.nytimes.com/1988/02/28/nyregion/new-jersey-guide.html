NEW JERSEY GUIDE

By Frank Emblen
Published: February 28, 1988

POLITICAL SATIRIST Mark Russell, the political satirist whose line is ''I have 535 writers -435 in the House and 100 in the Senate,'' will appear in Sparta Saturday evening.

Mr. Russell, who writes his own material, sings topical parodies while accompanying himself on a piano. He also writes a column that appears in 100 newspapers.

His performance begins at 8 o'clock at Sussex County Community College. Tickets are $10, $15 and $20. Information: (201) 383-0027. MARCEAU IN WAYNE

Marcel Marceau, the mime of mimes, will appear next Sunday evening at the Y.M-Y.W.H.A. of North Jersey in Wayne.

Without scenery, props, special effects or a single syllable, he will ''fight howling winds, tame lions, walk a tightrope and get lost in a subway.''

The show begins at 7 o'clock. The 'Y' is at 1 Pike Drive. Tickets are $25. Information: (201) 595-0100. LAW TALK AT RUTGERS

Elizabeth Holtzman, the Brooklyn District Attorney, will speak Tuesday evening at Rutgers University in New Brunswick as part of a lecture series entitled ''The Idea of Law: Contemporary Debates.''

The lecture, which begins at 8 o'clock, is free and the public is invited.

Information: (201) 932-8426. CLASSICAL GUITARIST

The clasical guitarist Sharon Isbin will perform at the Snug Harbor Cultural Center on Staten Island Saturday evening.

Ms. Isbin, who was heard last year with Jean-Pierre Rampal and the Emerson Quartet at Lincoln Center, regularly tours Europe.

The concert begins at 8 o'clock. Tickets are $9. The center is at 1000 Richmond Terrace.

Information: (718) 448-2500. DOCTORS AND EPIDEMICS

Dr. Daniel Fox, a professor of humanities in medicine at the State University of New York at Stony Brook, will lecture on ''Physicians' Responsibility in Epidemics'' at Princeton University tomorrow evening.

The author of ''Health Policies: The Experience of Britain and America, 1911-1965,'' he will speak on how the medical profession has behaved in the last five centuries toward patients with contagious diseases.

The lecture is free and begins at 8 o'clock at 101 McCormick Hall.

Information: (609) 452-3787. BLACK LECTURE

Barbara Smith, a political activist, will lecture at Rutgers-Newark Wednesday afternoon on ''Speaking the Unspeakable: Homophobia and African-Americans.''

Ms. Smith, a co-founder of Kitchen Table: Women of Color Press, a publishing house, is a poet and the author and editor of four books, including ''All the Women Are White, All the Blacks Are Men, But Some of Us Are Brave: Black Women's Studies.''

The free lecture begins at 1 o'clock in the Robeson Center.

Information: (201) 648-5262. 'ANNA' OPENING NIGHT

Sally Kirkland, a Golden Globe award in the hand and an Oscar in the bush (Las Vegas oddsmakers favor her to win the best actress award), will return to the William Carlos Williams Center for the Arts in Rutherford Friday for the opening night of ''Anna,'' the film causing all this fuss.

Location shots for ''Anna'' were filmed at the center and Rutherford will go Hollywood for the opening, replete with searchlights, celebrities, music, wine, an autograph-photo session and tours of the Rivoli.

The movie was shown at the Film Festivals of New York and San Francisco, but the opening will take place Friday at 8 P.M. at the center, 1 Williams Plaza.

Tickets are $25 and proceeds benefit the center.

Information: (201) 445-7498 STAMPS ON VIEW

A stamp exhibition sponsored by the Association of Bergen County Philatelists will begin at noon Friday at the Ramada Inn, 100 Chesnut Ridge Road, Montvale.

Collectors from throughout the country compete and there will be a 19-dealer bourse with stamps and materials from around the world.

The United States Postal Service will have a booth and provide a special show cancellation of a souvenir card commemorating winter sports.

Show hours are noon to 7 P.M. Friday, 10 A.M. to 6 P.M. Saturday and 10 A.M. to 4 P.M. next Sunday. Admission is $1.50. Information: (201) 487-6898. MUSIC IN SUFFERN, N.Y.

The Rockland Chamber Music Society will present a concert at Rockland Community College in Suffern, N.Y., this afternoon, featuring Robert Guralnik, pianist.

The program includes Beethoven's Septet for Winds and Strings, Bartok's Contrasts for Violin, Clarinet and Piano and Faure's Quartet for piano and strings.

A wine-and-cheese reception will follow the concert.

The concert will begin at 3 o'clock in the college's Cultural Arts Theater, 145 College Road.

Tickets are $8 (students and the elderly, $6).

Information: (914) 356-1522.
