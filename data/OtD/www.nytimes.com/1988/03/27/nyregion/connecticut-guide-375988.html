CONNECTICUT GUIDE


Published: March 27, 1988

CELEBRATION OF O'NEILL Eugene O'Neill's centennial is being observed by the Yale Repertory Theater and a stellar company of performers, directors and designers.

They have assembled productions of the playwright's most antithetical works - ''A Long Day's Journey Into Night,'' the tragic story of his own family, and ''Ah, Wilderness!,'' his upbeat fantasy of a family life.

Jason Robards and Colleen Dewhurst play the leading roles of husband and wife in both plays, which run in repertory. The rest of the cast includes George Hearn, Elizabeth Wilson, Raphael Sbarge, William Cain, Jamey Sheridan, Campbell Scott, Jennifer Dundas, Steven Skybell, Nicholas Tamarkin, Kyra Sedgwick, Annie Golden and William Wise.

The director of ''Long Day's Journey'' is Jose Quintero; Arvin Brown of the Long Wharf Theater is directing ''Ah, Wilderness!'' Ben Edwards and Michael H. Yeargan provided the set designs, Jane Greenwood created the costumes and Jennifer Tipton devised the lighting.

Mr. Robards, Miss Dewhurst and Mr. Quintero have won Tonys for their interpretations of various O'Neill plays.

Performances are at 7:30 P.M. weekdays and 1:30 and 8 P.M. Saturday, through May 21, at the University Theater, 222 York Street, New Haven. Tickets are $12 to $27. Call 432-1234 for reservations and more information. BURNS, LIVE AND ON FILM

George Burns, who is booked to play the Palladium in London when he reaches the age of 100, this week will receive his first honorary degree, a doctor of humanities, during a special convocation at the University of Hartford. The 93-year-old comedian will also be featured in a three-day film festival at the university.

Beginning today, two films will be shown each night at 7 P.M. ''We're Not Dressing'' and ''Six of a Kind,'' both released in 1934, will be shown tonight; ''College Holiday'' (1936) and ''Oh, God!'' (1977) on Monday, and ''The Sunshine Boys'' (1975), for which Mr. Burns won an Oscar as best supporting actor, and ''Sergeant Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band'' (1978) on Tuesday.

The festival is free and open to the public at the Konover Campus Center. Attendance at the convocation at 5 P.M. Thursday in Lincoln Theater is by invitation. EQUESTRIAN COMPETITION

Ponies, horses and riders of all ages will fill the New Canaan Mounted Troop Equestrian Center on Saturday to compete in qualifying events for the National Horse Show at Madison Square Garden in November.

Almost half the classes are for jumpers, and up to 100 riders are expected to participate.

Spectators may come and go free of charge throughout the day to watch competitors from Connecticut, New York and New Jersey. A food concession will be open all day, and in case of heavy rain, the events will be transferred from the 120-foot-long outdoor ring to the indoor ring.

The competitions will begin at 8:30 A.M. Saturday. The center, a nonprofit horsemanship school for children, is at 22 Carter Street, off Route 106. For more information, call 966-0634. A SMALL MOUNT RUSHMORE

A scale model of Mount Rushmore, and a larger-than-life bronze of Abraham Lincoln are among the works on view in Stamford in an exhibition called ''A Tribute to Gutzon Borglum: Sculptor of Mount Rushmore.''

Other pieces include a sculpture titled ''Apaches Pursued,'' the bronze torso of a ballet dancer, a landscape in oils and framed prints.

The exhibit is from 10 A.M. to 4:30 P.M. daily except Sunday, and until 8 P.M. on Thursdays through April 30 at the Cavalier Galleries on Level A of 1 Landmark Square, Stamford. For more information, call 325-8444. HISTORY OF ILLUSTRATION

The history of illustration from the Industrial Revolution to the 1950's will be the subject of a discussion tomorrow in New Canaan.

Murray Tinkelman, a professor of illustration at Syracuse University, will discuss and show various illustrative styles of the Victorian and Art Deco eras. He will include Charles Dana Gibson's Gibson Girl, John Held Jr.'s flappers of the Roaring Twenties, Howard Pyle's mythic figures and the glamour girls of the 30's and 40's.

Mr. Tinkelman has won more than 100 awards for his illustrations. He has worked for numerous magazines, including Atlantic Monthly, Ladies' Home Journal and Field & Stream. He also illustrated the covers for a paperback set of Zane Grey novels.

After the lecture, a reception will be held to open an exhibition of illustrations by Silvermine students.

Mr. Tinkelman's slide presentation will begin at 7 P.M. tomorrow at the Silvermine School of Art, 1037 Silvermine Road, New Canaan. Admission is $4 for the lecture; the exhibit is free and is open from 9 A.M. to 10 P.M. Monday to Friday until April 8. For more information, call 966-6668. $90ELEANOR CHARLES
