A Playbill for What's On at Colleges in New York


Published: April 15, 1988

Here is a sampling of performing arts being presented on college stages in New York. Events at colleges in the City University of New York system are listed in a free calendar that may be obtained by writing to the Office of Community Relations, City University of New York, 535 East 80th Street, New York, N.Y. 10021. The Association for a Better New York also publishes a monthly calendar of performing arts and other events at New York colleges. Write to 355 Lexington Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10017. BARNARD COLLEGE, Broadway and 116th Street, 280-2079 or 280-2037. The Minor Latham Playhouse, the college's 200-seat main stage at Broadway and 119th Street, has generally free theater, dance and music throughout the school year. Each spring, the Barnard-Columbia Greek Drama Group presents Greek drama in ancient Greek. Tonight and tomorrow at 8: Aristophanes' ''Clouds.'' Tickets are $6 ($3 for students.) BARUCH COLLEGE, 17 Lexington Avenue, at 23d Street, 505-5891. Occasional free concerts include Maria Bachmann, a violinist, in Nallin Recital Hall on April 20 at 3 P.M., and ''post-modern dances'' by the Wendy Osserman Dance Company April 21 at 12:45 P.M. in the Studio Theater. BOROUGH OF MANHATTAN COMMUNITY COLLEGE, 199 Chambers Street, 618-1980. The college's Triplex theater complex has become a major downtown performance venue since opening in 1984. More than 300 professional and college events, including concerts, plays, children's theater and dance performances, are held annually in the 923-seat proscenium theater, the 282-seat Off Broadway theater and a 99-seat theater. Dance Brazil will present sambas, candomble and other folk dances tomorrow at 8 P.M., and Airjazz, a trio of dancers and jugglers, will perform April 22 and 23; tickets for both are $15 and $12. Performances geared to children are every other Saturday from October through May at 1 P.M. (the next is April 23). BRONX COMMUNITY COLLEGE, University Avenue and West 181st Street, the Bronx, 220-6312. Generally free student and professional concerts are presented in the 600-seat Hall of Fame Playhouse and the Gould Memorial Auditorium, and afternoon recitals are often held in the 280-seat Schwendler Auditorium. Coming: the Annabella Gonzalez Dance Company, April 18 and 25 at 11 A.M. and 1 P.M.; the Bronx Arts Ensemble, May 2 and 23, and Dizzy Gillespie, May 22. BROOKLYN COLLEGE, Campus Road and Hillel Place, near Flatbush and Nostrand Avenues, Brooklyn, (718) 434-1900. B.C.B.C. is short for the Brooklyn Center for the Performing Arts at Brooklyn College. About 80 performances by professional artists - presented by the center, an independent entity - and 250, mostly free recitals, chamber-music concerts and other college-sponsored events are held in the 2,500-seat Whitman Hall, the 500-seat Gershwin Theater and the 165-seat Sam Levenson Recital Hall each year. The Jazz Tap Ensemble, with Harold Nicholas, performs tomorrow at 8 and Sunday at 2 P.M.; tickets are $8 to $16. Coming: Mel Torme on May 7 (tickets, $15). CITY COLLEGE, West 133d Street and Convent Avenue, 690-4100. Aaron Davis Hall includes a 750-seat concert hall, a theater and a workshop space where professional and college performers appear throughout the year. Arthur Mitchell's Dance Theater of Harlem performs tonight and tomorrow at 8 and Sunday at 2 P.M.; tickets are $15. CITY UNIVERSITY GRADUATE CENTER, 33 West 42d Street, 642-2641. Occasional, mostly free performances are presented in the auditorium or third-floor studio, including an 18th-century dance workshop by Wendy Hilton on April 22 at 2:30 P.M.; a jazz concert by Howard Brofsky and Friends on April 26 at 8 P.M., and a staged reading of ''Othello'' by Sheer Shakespeare on April 29 at 6:30 P.M. COLLEGE OF STATEN ISLAND-ST. GEORGE CAMPUS, 120 Stuyvesant Place; SUNNYSIDE CAMPUS, 715 Ocean Terrace; (718) 390-7992 or 390-7951. Free concerts are presented many Sunday and weekday afternoons at the St. George campus's 125-seat College Hall, one block from the Staten Island Ferry; Ann Sohm, soprano, and Ed Brown, lutist and guitarist, perform Sunday at 3 P.M. The Williamson Theater at the Sunnyside campus presents regular concerts, including one called ''Shakespearean Inspirations'' by the Staten Island Symphony on Sunday at 3 P.M. (tickets are $9; $6.50 for students and the elderly). COLUMBIA UNIVERSITY, Broadway and 116th Street, 280- 2845. Plays and concerts take place in such facilities as Earl Hall, the Horace Mann Theater at Teacher's College, St. Paul's Chapel, Teatro Piccolo of Casa Italiana and Maison Francaise in Buell Hall and the Kathryn Bache Miller Theater (formerly McMillin Theater), now undergoing renovation. The Columbia 1988 Varsity Show, ''The Bonfire of Humanities,'' will be presented tonight and tomorrow at 8 (and April 21-23) at Ferris Booth Hall; tickets are $5; $3 for students. A jazz duo plays in the Faculty House today at 5 P.M.; the Barnard-Columbia Chorus performs with the University of Virginia Singers in St. Paul's tomorrow at 8 P.M., and the Columbia University Orchestra presents the original five-movement version of Mahler's First Symphony at Symphony Space, Broadway and 95th Street, Sunday at 8 P.M. COOPER UNION, 7 East Seventh Street, 353-4195. The Brooklyn Philharmonic presents a ''Meet the Moderns'' concert in the school's historic, 900-seat Great Hall tonight at 8; tickets are $7. Another regular performer, the American Jazz Orchestra, will appear May 5 at 8 P.M. FASHION INSTITUTE OF TECHNOLOGY, 27th Street and Seventh Avenue, 760-7642. The 850-seat Haft Auditorium and smaller Katie Murphy Amphitheater are used by many professional companies, including the Village Light Opera. Their ''Oklahoma!'' opens April 23; tickets are $9 to $20. The Little Orchestra Society gives a classical concert for toddlers Sunday at 1:30 and 2:45 P.M.; tickets are $20. FORDHAM UNIVERSITY AT LINCOLN CENTER, 113 West 60th Street, 841-5267. The Pope Auditorium and Studio Theater accommodate two dozen theater productions a year. ''The Wiz,'' directed by Ben Harney, runs from April 21 to 30; tickets are $10 ($5 for students and the elderly). HERBERT LEHMAN COLLEGE, Bedford Park Boulevard West, the Bronx, 960-8833. Lehman Center for the Performing Arts includes a 2,300-seat concert hall, a 500-seat theater and two smaller spaces where music, theater, dance and comedy are presented. Samuel Beckett's ''Company'' will be staged in the theater tomorrow at 8 and Sunday at 3 P.M. as part of a five-day Beckett festival; tickets are $10. The Boys Choir of Harlem will perform in the concert hall Sunday at 2 P.M.; tickets are $11 to $15. Coming later: the Aeolian Chamber Players, April 22; Pilar Rioja, April 24, and Pilobolus, April 30. HOSTOS COMMUNITY COLLEGE, 500 Grand Concourse (at 149th Street), the Bronx, 960-1009. The college is using the Teatro Puerto Rico, 490 East 138th Street, until moving into the 900-seat Savoy Manor Ballroom next winter. Coming: Dave Valentin, flutist, and Noel Pointer, jazz violinist, on May 5 at 7 P.M.; tickets are $7 and $5. HUNTER COLLEGE, 68th Street between Lexington and Park Avenues, 772-4448. Theater, dance and music can be found in the large college auditorium, the 700-seat Playhouse and the Little Theater. Euripides' ''Alcestis'' will be presented tonight and tomorrow at 8 P.M.; tickets are $6 ($3 for students and the elderly). The Hunter College Symphony and Choir will present Mozart's Requiem and 36th Symphony April 27, Thurber's ''Many Moons'' is scheduled from April 28 through May 1, and the college dance company gives its spring concert April 28 through 30. JEWISH THEOLOGICAL SEMINARY, 3080 Broadway (at 122d Street), 678-8979. A two-part concert on Sunday at 4 and 7:30 P.M., featuring Habrera Hativit - a Jewish jazz-blues group - and Shlomo Bar, an Israeli composer-musician, will open celebrations of Israel's 40th anniversary; tickets are $10 for the first part, $5 for the second. JUILLIARD SCHOOL, Broadway and 66th Street, 874-7515. More than 300 concerts - including the free ''Wednesdays at 1'' series, student recitals, and performances by two student orchestras, a chamber ensemble and a contemporary-music ensemble - are presented each year at Alice Tully and Avery Fisher Halls, and the 933-seat Juilliard Theater and the 278-seat Paul Recital Hall. The drama division's spring season continues through May 1 with four productions, including Turgenev's ''Month in the Country'' tonight, tomorrow and April 19 and 20 at 8, and Sam Shepard's ''Suicide in B Flat'' on Sunday (and April 18, 21 and 22) at 8 P.M. Tickets are $5. Four recitals are scheduled this weekend. KINGSBOROUGH COMMUNITY COLLEGE, 2001 Oriental Boulevard, Manhattan Beach, Brooklyn, (718) 934-5596. The 750-seat Performing Arts Center and Little Theater are the sites for many concerts, including free dance performances on April 22 at 3 and April 23 at 7:30 P.M. Gerard Schwarz and the Mostly Mozart Orchestra, with the pianist Horacio Gutierrez will give a concert April 30 at 8 P.M.; tickets are $16 and $15. LA GUARDIA COMMUNITY COLLEGE, 31-10 Thomson Avenue, Long Island City, Queens, (718) 482-5151. Jazz concerts to children's operas fill the La Guardia Theater during the school year. Coming attractions for children: the Kaleidoscope Dancers tomorrow at 1 P.M. and a program called ''Music From Around the World'' on April 30 at 1 P.M.; tickets are $4; $2 for children. An international dance festival featuring a half-dozen companies will be held May 7 from 11 A.M. to 5 P.M.; tickets are $5; $3 for children and the elderly. LONG ISLAND UNIVERSITY-BROOKLYN CAMPUS, Flatbush and DeKalb Avenues, Brooklyn, (718) 403-1051. Free jazz concerts are held in the Triangle Theater; the next one is Tuesday at 4 P.M. The Diane Jacobowitz Dance Theater, the college's resident company, performs May 13 through 15; tickets are $7. MANHATTAN COLLEGE, Manhattan College Parkway, Riverdale, 920-0247. The Manhattan College Players and Singers, two student groups, present plays and concerts in the large Chapel of De La Salle and His Brothers and the 450-seat Smith Auditorium. The Players' next production, John Guare's ''House of Blue Leaves,'' will run from May 5 to 8; tickets are $5; $3 for students and the elderly. MANHATTAN SCHOOL OF MUSIC, Broadway and 122d Street, 749-2802, extension 452. More than 300 (generally free) recitals and concerts are presented each year in Borden Hall and the smaller, Hubbard and Pforzheimer recital halls. The Manhattan Philharmonia, one of two student orchestras, performs music by Brahms, Mozart and Kodaly tonight at 8 in Borden, and eight recitals are scheduled this weekend. MANNES COLLEGE OF MUSIC, 150 West 85th Street, 580-0210. Concerts and recitals (generally free) by the school's orchestra, chamber ensembles or the early-music Mannes Camerata are presented almost every night from October through May in the 200-seat Mannes Concert Hall or the John Goldmark Recital Hall. The Mannes Trio performs music by Haydn, Martinu and Smetana tonight at 8, a faculty recital by the guitarist Stanley Dorn is tomorrow at 8, and the Mannes Repertory Orchestra performs Monday at 8 P.M. MARYMOUNT MANHATTAN COLLEGE, 221 East 71st Street, 517-0475. Dance and theater productions by visiting professional companies and student groups, as well as concerts and children's plays, are presented in the 249-seat Marymount Manhattan Theater, the 435-seat Great Hall, and the tiny Box Theater. Six short plays by Samuel Beckett will be presented May 4 to 8; tickets are $5 ($3 for students and the elderly). NEW SCHOOL FOR SOCIAL RESEARCH, 66 West 12th Street, 741- 5689. Each semester, jazz concerts have been presented most Fridays at 6 P.M. and classical concerts most Sunday afternoons in the 500-seat Bernhard Mayer Auditorium. The Oval Window Chamber Ensemble performs Monday at 8 P.M. (tickets are $3). NEW YORK CITY TECHNICAL COLLEGE, 300 Jay Street, Brooklyn, (718) 643-5656. Performances are in Klitgord Auditorium, where City Tech Theaterworks presents ''Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat'' on April 28 to 30 at 7:30 P.M. and May 1 at 4 P.M.; tickets are $8 ($5 for students and the elderly). NEW YORK UNIVERSITY, locations throughout Greenwich Village, 998-1212. Each year, the university's Tisch School of the Arts presents scores of mostly free theater and dance performaces at 721 Broadway, between Waverly and Washington Place; 111 Second Avenue, between Sixth and Seventh Streets, and the N. Y. U. Theater, 35 West Fourth Street. Recitals, chamber-music and jazz concerts are also presented at Loeb Student Center, 566 La Guardia Place, and Maison Francaise, 16 Washington Mews. The school's Second Avenue Dance Company presents its free spring concert tonight through Monday at 8 at 111 Second Avenue. Schnitzler's ''Ronde'' is presented by the Lee Strasberg Theater Institute at 721 Broadway tonight and tomorrow at 8 and Sunday at 2 P.M. And a free concert of music by Faure, Poulenc and Satie will be held at Maison Francaise Sunday at 3 P.M. A ''Beyond Vaudeville'' variety show, which will benefit the Coalition for the Homeless, will be held at Loeb Student Center tonight at 7:30 P.M.; admission is $2.50. The graduate acting department will present Shaw's ''Candida'' and Shakespeare's ''Comedy of Errors'' in repertory beginning April 28. PACE UNIVERSITY-CIVIC CENTER CAMPUS, Spruce Street and Park Row, 488-1715. Dozens of student and professional concerts, plays and children's shows are presented each year in the 600-seat Schimmel Theater. Euripides' ''Electra'' will be performed April 21 to 23 at 7:30 P.M. and April 24 at 2; tickets are $5 ($3 for students and the elderly). QUEENS COLLEGE, Kissena Boulevard and the Long Island Expressway, (718) 520-7322 or (718) 793-8080. Colden Center is one of the leading performing-arts centers serving Queens and Nassau County. Scores of performances by top orchestral groups, dance companies and other artists are presented annually at the 2,143-seat concert hall and 500-seat theater. Ashford and Simpson, the popular singers, appear tomorrow at 8:30 P.M.; tickets are $19 to $25. The Queens Symphony Orchestra will perform with Theodore Bikel in a ''pops'' program Sunday at 2 P.M.; tickets are $14 to $19; $10 for the elderly and $5 for students. A musical celebration of Queens College's 50th anniversary will be held Sunday at 7:30 P.M., featuring the Jimmy Heath Quintet, the Festival Alumni Orchestra, Tony Randall and soloists; tickets are $5. Coming: Cleo Laine, April 24; ''Footnotes,'' a dance concert, April 27 through May 1; the Jerusalem Symphony Orchestra, April 30; Pilobolus Dance Theater, May 1, and Itzhak Perlman, May 22. Free recitals are frequently presented in the Rathaus Recital Hall and student plays are performed in the Little Theater. QUEENSBOROUGH COMMUNITY COLLEGE, 56th Avenue and Springfield Boulevard, Bayside, Queens, (718) 631-6311. College and professional performers appear in the school's 875-seat theater. The Oakland Ballet will perform ''Giselle'' April 30 at 8 P.M.; tickets are $10 and $12. The Queensborough Orchestra will present its Spring Pops Gala April 23 at 8 P.M., and the Cremona String Quartet will appear in Kurzweil Recital Hall April 24 at 3 P.M.; tickets are $6. ST. JOHN'S UNIVERSITY, Grand Central and Utopia Parkways, Jamaica, Queens, (718) 990-6364. Classical and rock concerts are presented in the 6,000-seat Alumni Hall and the 600-seat Marillac Auditorium. The Chappell Players, a college theater troupe, will perform O'Neill's ''Moon for the Misbegotten'' April 22 and 23 at 8 P.M. and April 24 at 3 P.M.; tickets are $6. ST. JOSEPH'S COLLEGE, 245 Clinton Avenue, Brooklyn, (718) 636-6800. The college's Chapel Players present the musical ''Most Happy Fella'' April 22 and 23 at 8 P.M. and April 24 at 2 P.M.; tickets are $6. YESHIVA UNIVERSITY, Amsterdam Avenue and 185th Street, 960-5400. The university's Furst Hall will be the site for an April 24 musical celebration of Israel's 40th anniversary, featuring Yoel Sharabi, an Israeli singer, and the Shema Yisroel Orchestra. The time is 2 P.M. Tickets are $10. YORK COLLEGE, 94-20 Guy R. Brewer Boulevard, Jamaica, Queens, (718) 262-2062. Free concerts include an April 26 performance by the York College Electric Guitar Ensemble at noon, and an April 29 performance by Kenny Burrell, a jazz guitarist.
