CONNECTICUT GUIDE


Published: April 17, 1988

GOODSPEED AT 25 ''Wonderful Town,'' a Broadway hit in 1953, will open at the Goodspeed Opera House on Wednesday, as the the East Haddam theater celebrates its 25th anniversary.

The musical, adapted from the play ''My Sister Eileen,'' had a score by Leonard Bernstein, lyrics by Betty Comden and Adolph Green, and starred Rosalind Russell. It is about two sisters from Ohio who rent a basement apartment in Greenwich Village and endure the comic hassles and indignities of being newcomers in New York.

''Wonderful Town'' will run to July 8. The curtain times are 8 P.M. Wednesday to Friday, 5 and 9 P.M. Saturday, 2 and 6 P.M. Sunday and 2:30 P.M. Wednesday. Tickets are $23.50 and $24.50 and may be reserved by calling 873-8668. HARTFORD HYSTERIA SHOW

The neurologist Dr. Jean-Marie Charcot's research on mental illness in the late 1800's is the subject of a theater-dance piece to be performed today at the Austin Arts Center of Trinity College in Hartford.

Three faculty members devised ''Dr. Charcot's Hysteria Shows'' from photographs and drawings of demonstrations that Dr. Charcot held for his colleagues at the Salpetriere hospital in Paris, which treated mentally ill women.

''As we started to re-enact the movements and attitudes we saw in the iconography, we began to wonder whether the stages of hysteria that Dr. Charcot was describing were real or acted out by the women for the approval of the male doctors,'' said Judy Dworin, the chairwoman of the college's dance department.

Her co-creators were Lenora Champagne, a dance teacher and performance artist, and Dianne Hunter, an English instructor. The show's composer, Andre Gribou, assembled a score from Strauss waltzes and other music of the period.

The tickets for the 2 P.M. performance are $6, students and the elderly $4, at the door or call 527-8062 for reservations. ARTISTS AND DISEASE

''Artistic Creativity and Disease'' will be the subject of a lecture at the Yale Medical School on Thursday by Dr. Gerald Peskin, a clinical professor of surgery at the school.

''The mood of an artist, as it changes from normal to psychotic, is demonstrated most easily by van Gogh,'' Dr. Peskin said. ''When he was cheerful, he used a lot of yellows, oranges and reds, but when despondent, he went into blackbirds flying overhead and darkened shades in the fields below.''

''From a purely physical point of view, the development of arthritis and cataracts influences the way the artist draws and sees. Monet's interpretation of the same scenes before and after he was afflicted with cataracts shows a marked difference in color.''

Dr. Peskin will also present slides to illustrate the changes in the work of various artists. He plans to discuss how illness influenced the work of several poets as well.

The lecture is part of the ''Humanities in Medicine'' series at Yale. It will be held at 5 P.M. The school is at 333 Hope Street, New Haven. Admission is free. OLD HOUSE FAIR

Clem Labine, the founder of The Old-House Journal, and Patricia Poore, its editor and publisher, will be the featured speakers at the Third Annual Old House Fair next weekend in West Hartford.

Ms. Poore will give a lecture called ''The Post-Victorian House'' at noon Saturday. Mr. Labine's talk, titled ''Old House Maintenance: The Stepchild of Restoration,'' will be at 12:45 P.M. next Sunday.

Other speakers will include Jamie Gibbs, a New York landscape architect who specializes in period design, and Andrew Ladygo, the architectural conservator for the Society for the Preservation of New England Antiquities based in Massachusetts. He will discuss plaster repair.

More than 50 exhibitors will demonstrate how to restore old houses with wood turning, authentic hardware, stained glass, exterior cleaning, wall decoration, graining, floor cloth, painting on metal and the use of antique architectural materials. There will also be a free advice service for those who bring photographs of old houses.

The fair will be held from 11 A.M. to 5 P.M. Saturday and next Sunday at the American School for the Deaf, 139 North Main Street, West Hartford. Admission is $5 at the door or $4 in advance. For more information, call 525-0279. SILVERSTEIN ACTED OUT

''The Shel Game,'' a series of poems, skits, songs, dances and improvisations based on Shel Silverstein's books for children, will be performed in Waterbury on Tuesday by 13 students of the Taft School, a private high school in the city.

The students, members of the school's Taft Repertory Company, tour the state with two productions a year and have performed at schools in France, Greece, England and Canada.

On Tuesday, they will perform around the audience rather than on a stage. The show, which is about the experiences of growing up, will include pieces titled ''I'm 5,'' ''The Homework Machine'' and ''Unscratchable Itch.''

The performances will be at 12:30 and 2:30 P.M. Tuesday at the Mattatuck Museum, 144 West Main Street, Waterbury. The show is intended for children ages 5 to 13. Admission is free. For more information, call 753-0381. ANTIQUES IN WESTPORT

The house that the Westport artist Stevan Dohanos built as a contemporary ranch in the 1940's is a Georgian Colonial today, remodeled by two subsequent owners. The house is included in a tour of four homes on Friday as part of the 24th Annual Southport-Westport Antiques Show.

The other homes are an Italianate mansion overlooking Southport Harbor; a 1920's estate with a 72-foot-long pool and dog kennel, and a 1725 Colonial with a central chimney and gambrel roof that was designed in the English country-manor style. The tour will be held from 10 A.M. to 4 P.M.

The antiques show will include a lecture by Leslie B. Keno, vice president and director of Sotheby's American furniture division in Manhattan, on ''Collecting Trends in American Furniture'' at 2:30 P.M. Friday.

The show will be held from 11 A.M. to 9 P.M. Friday and from 11 A.M. to 6 P.M. Saturday and next Sunday at the Fairfield County Hunt Club, 174 Long Lots Road, Westport.

The tickets for the tour are $25, which includes a day's admission to the antiques show. Admission to the show without the tour is $6. For more information, call 227-8445. $90ELEANOR CHARLES
