By THE NEW YORK TIMES
Published: August 4, 2006

ATTRACTIONS

Museums and Sites

AMERICAN MUSEUM OF NATURAL HISTORY, Central Park West and 79th Street. Through Jan. 15, “Yellowstone to Yukon,” an exhibition of more than 40 photographs by 11 photographers documenting the animals, vegetation and geology of the regions affected by one of the world’s largest wildlife conservation initiatives. Through Jan. 7, “Lizards & Snakes: Alive!,” an exhibition with live examples of more than 25 species. Through Aug. 20, “Darwin,” an exhibition devoted to the naturalist Charles Darwin, which includes fossils and live creatures as well as a reconstruction of his study at Down House. Through Jan. 2, “Voices From South of the Clouds,” a display of photographs of life in Yunnan Province in China, taken by villagers. Also on view: “Vital Variety: A Visual Celebration of Invertebrate Biodiversity,” an exhibition of 24 large-format color photographs by the entomologist Piotr Naskrecki. In the IMAX theater, through Labor Day, “Journey Into Amazing Caves,” a film that documents the collaboration of Nancy Aulenbach, a cave explorer, and Hazel Barton, a microbiologist. Timed tickets to “Lizards & Snakes: Alive!” and “Darwin,” which include museum admission, are $21; $16 for students and 60+; $12 for 12 and younger. IMAX tickets, which include museum admission, are $21; students and 60+, $16; children under 12, $12. Suggested museum admission: $14; students and 60+, $10.50; children, $8. Combination tickets, including museum and Rose Center admission and the Hayden Planetarium space show (“Cosmic Collisions”): $22; students and 60+, $16.50; 12 and younger, $13; under 2, free; admission that includes all activities: $30; $23 for students and 60+; $19 for those 12 and younger. The Rose Center is open until 8:45 p.m. for “Starry Nights: Fridays Under the Sphere,” with a cash bar, jazz and tapas, on the first Friday of every month; tonight, the Ron Carter Quartet. The performance will also be broadcast live at 7:30 p.m. on WBGO FM (88.3). Museum hours: daily, 10 a.m. to 5:45 p.m. (to 8:45 p.m. on Fridays); (212) 769-5100 or (212) 769-5200; amnh.org

INSTITUTO CERVANTES, 211 East 49th Street, Manhattan. Through Sept. 30, “Correspondents in the Spanish Civil War, 1936-1939,” an exhibition of original documents and newspaper accounts by Ernest Hemingway, Antoine de Saint-Exupéry, John Dos Passos, George Orwell, Martha Gellhorn and others. Tuesdays through Fridays, 12:30 to 6:30 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 1:30 p.m. Free. (212) 308-7720; www.cervantes.org.

MUSEUM OF THE CITY OF NEW YORK, 1120 Fifth Avenue, at 103rd Street. Through Sunday, “On the Couch: Cartoons From The New Yorker,” psychotherapy-related cartoons from the magazine. Also through Sunday, “We Skate Hardcore: Photographs From Brooklyn’s Southside” by Vincent Cianni. Tomorrow at 2 p.m., a free inline skating clinic with Richie Velasquez, one of the subjects in the exhibition. Sunday at 2 p.m., “Big Bands,” clips from jazz films featuring famous jazz musicians and bands, including Duke Ellington, Count Basie, Benny Goodman and Dizzy Gillespie. Through Oct. 12, “The High Style of Dorothy Draper,” a display of furniture, photographs and other items relating to the career of this noted interior decorator. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Suggested admission: $9; 62+ and students, $5; under 12, free; families, $20; Sundays, 10 a.m. to noon, free for all. (212) 534-1672.

MUSEUM OF TELEVISION AND RADIO, 25 West 52nd Street, Manhattan. Through Aug. 31, “Beyond TV: New Media Art from Studio IMC,” an interactive exhibition on the technology used in video games, the Internet and cellphones. “She Made It: Women Creating Television and Radio,” a three-year project devoted to achievements of women on the creative and business sides of television and radio, with screenings and radio programming from specific shows. Tuesdays through Sundays, noon to 6 p.m.; Thursdays, to 8 p.m. $10; students and 62+, $8; children under 14, $5; (212) 621-6800.

NEW YORK PUBLIC LIBRARY, Wachenheim Gallery of the Humanities and Social Sciences Library, Fifth Avenue and 42nd Street. Through tomorrow, the Declaration of Independence, handwritten by Thomas Jefferson. Tuesdays and Wednesdays, 11 a.m. to 7:30 p.m.; Thursdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Free. (212) 869-8089.

NEW YORK TRANSIT MUSEUM, Gallery Annex, Grand Central Terminal, adjacent to Station Master’s Office. Through Oct. 29, “Many Hands: Representations of Railroad Workers,” an exhibition of photographs, tools and uniforms that illustrate the operation and maintenance of railroads, including images of rail workers by Gordon Parks. Mondays through Fridays, 8 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Free. (212) 878-0106. In the museum’s Brooklyn space, Boerum Place and Schermerhorn Street, Brooklyn Heights: “The Triborough Bridge: Robert Moses and the Automobile Age,” an exhibition of photographs, drawings, models and artifacts celebrating that span’s 70th anniversary. Through Aug. 27, “Upon Closer Inspection: The Art of Laura Cantor,” semiabstract pastel drawings, oil paintings and linoleum-block prints of subway-car components by an artist who is also a subway-car inspector. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. $5; 3 to 17, $3; members, 62+ and children under 3, free; (718) 694-1600.

Gardens

BROOKLYN BOTANIC GARDEN, 1000 Washington Avenue, Crown Heights. Through Sept. 3, “Nature Unbound: Sculpture and Drawings by Nancy Blum,” an exhibition of large-scale works drawn from nature. Through Sept. 3, an interactive garden walk that highlights plant conservation. Tuesdays through Fridays, 8 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Saturdays, Sundays and holidays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. $5; students and 65+, $3; under 16, free; free for all Tuesdays and Saturdays from 10 a.m. to noon; (718) 623-7200.

NEW YORK BOTANICAL GARDEN, Southern Boulevard and 200th Street, Bedford Park, the Bronx. Through Oct. 29, “Chihuly at the New York Botanical Garden,” an exhibition of glass sculpture by the artist Dale Chihuly. A film about Mr. Chihuly will be shown tomorrow and Sunday at 1 and 3 p.m. Other events this weekend: “The Incomparable Lotus and Waterlily,” a home gardening demonstration (tomorrow and Sunday at 1:30 p.m.); a garden highlights tour (tomorrow at 12:30 p.m.); and a forest tour (Sunday at 12:30 p.m.) Also on view, “Plants and Fungi,” a multimedia exhibition with items from around the world. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Special admission and parking fees apply during the Chihuly show. Timed admission tickets, including all admissions and a tram tour, are $20; 62+ and students, $18; 2 to 12, $5. Parking, by valet only, is $20. Tickets and parking fees can be purchased in advance through Ticketmaster: (212) 220-0503. Garden grounds admission: $6; 62+, $3; students, $2; 2 to 12, $1; under 2, free. Tram tours daily, every 30 minutes.Grounds admission free on Wednesdays, and 10 a.m. to noon on Saturdays. (718) 817-8700; nybg.org.

STATEN ISLAND BOTANICAL GARDEN, 1000 Richmond Terrace, Livingston. Two exhibitions running through Sept. 17 in the visitors’ center: “Journey to China’s Southwest,” with photographs, clothing and folk art; also, “Exploring Tibet: In Search of the Salween,” a display of rare photographs from a 1930’s expedition to southeastern Tibet to find the source of the Salween River. Admission: $3; students and 62+, $2; members, free. The one-acre Scholar’s Garden and the Secret Garden are open Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Scholar’s Garden admission: $5; 62+ and children under 12, $4. Secret Garden: $2; adults with a child, free. Grounds hours: daily, dawn to dusk; free; (718) 273-8200. .

EVENTS

New York City

BATTERY PARK CITY PARKS CONSERVANCY, Battery Park City, Lower Manhattan. Tonight at 6:30, “Drumming Circles for All Ages,” with djembe and conga drums, bells, shakers and sticks, featuring the drummer Pamela Patrick. Free; (212) 267-9700; bpcparks.org.

‘GOOD MORNING AMERICA’ SUMMER CONCERT SERIES, Bryant Park, Avenue of the Americas, at 42nd Street. Today, 7 to 9 a.m., Pharrell with Kanye West.

MANHATTAN YOUTH FAIR, Murray Street, from Broadway to Church Street, Lower Manhattan. Today, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.PRIDE IN THE CITY 2006, an annual weekend of cultural events in celebration of the city’s black lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender communities, at several locations. Events include a Family Day outdoor gathering, with music, crafts, health screenings and other activities, tomorrow from noon to 8 p.m. at Commodore Barry Park, Navy and Tillary Streets and Flushing Avenue, downtown Brooklyn, and a beach party Sunday from noon to 8 p.m. at Jacob Riis Park, Baseball Field, Bays 1 and 2, Beach 169th Street, Neponsit, Queens. www.prideinthecity.com.

GREENWICH AVENUE FESTIVAL, from Avenue of the Americas to Seventh Avenue, Manhattan. Tomorrow, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m. Sponsored by the Federation to Preserve the Greenwich Village Waterfront.

UNIVERSAL PEACE DAY, a free daylong event tomorrow at several locations, commemorating the anniversary of the bombing of Hiroshima. From 11 a.m. to 5 p.m., music, dance and poetry featuring Peter Yarrow and others in Riverside Park, at 80th Street. At 6 p.m., a screening of the film “Lost Generation,” music, a photography exhibition and candle-lighting at the New York Buddhist Church, Riverside Drive and 105th Street. At 7:20 p.m., a silent walk from the New York Buddhist Church to an interfaith service, with music, dance and speakers at the Riverside Church, Riverside Drive at 122nd Street, Morningside Heights; those taking part include Oscar Brand and John Hall. www.universalpeaceday.com.

FOURTH BRAZILIAN FILM FESTIVAL OF NEW YORK, Sunday through Aug. 13 at the Tribeca Cinemas, 54 Varick Street, at Laight Street. Features more than 20 films, panel discussions and other events. www.brazilianfilmfestival.com.

C.O.R.E. HEALTH EXPO, Seventh Avenue, from 47th to 57th Street, Manhattan. Tomorrow, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Sponsored by the Congress of Racial Equality.

FESTIVAL OF THE AMERICAS, from 47th to 56th Street, Manhattan. Sunday, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.

COLUMBUS AVENUE FAIR, from 66th to 72nd Street. Sunday, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m. Sponsored by the Lincoln Square Neighborhood Center.

CHINATOWN FESTIVAL, Bayard Street, between Elizabeth and Baxter Streets. Sunday, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Sponsored by Asian Americans for Equality.

THIRD TIGER BEER SINGAPORE CHILI CRAB FESTIVAL, an outdoor event with entertainment, games and food, in front of the Water Street Restaurant and Lounge, 66 Water Street, in the Dumbo section of Brooklyn. Sunday, noon to 6 p.m. Free admission; additional charge for food. (646) 366-1470.

JUNCTION BOULEVARD FESTIVAL, from 37th Avenue to Roosevelt Avenue, Jackson Heights, Queens. Sunday, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.

JAMAICA ARTS AND MUSIC SUMMER FESTIVAL, Jamaica Avenue, from Parsons Boulevard to 168th Street, Jamaica, Queens. Tomorrow, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.

‘STARRY SKY,’ World Financial Center Winter Garden, 220 Vesey Street, Lower Manhattan. An exhibition of more than 300 handmade kites by Buteo Huang, a Taiwanese designer. Daily, 6 a.m. to midnight. Through Aug. 19. Free. (212) 945-0505.

SUMMER ON THE HUDSON 2006, Riverside Park South, Pier 1, reachable from 68th or 72nd Street, Manhattan. A free series of events. Sunday at 6 p.m., international music and dance with the all-female group Paprika. Information: 311.

HARLEM MEER PERFORMANCE FESTIVAL, Central Park, Charles A. Dana Discovery Center, Fifth Avenue at 110th Street. Free Sunday series of concerts. Sunday at 4 p.m., Latin jazz and salsa by Ileana Santamaria, a singer who is the daughter of the percussionist Mongo Santamaria. Sponsored by the Central Park Conservancy; (212) 310-6600; centralparknyc.org.

SUMMERMUSIC 2006 Sunday, “Silks and Rags,” a performance of ragtime music with Kenneth Cooper, a pianist, and Josephine Mongiardo, a soprano, at two locations: 2 p.m. at Van Cortlandt Park, Rockwood Drive Circle, Broadway and Mosholu Avenue, Riverdale, the Bronx (rain location, Amalgamated Houses, Vladeck Hall, Hillman Avenue and Van Cortlandt Park South); 4 p.m. at Fordham University, Keating Hall Auditorium, Southern Boulevard and Fordham Road, Bedford Park, the Bronx. Free. Presented by the Bronx Arts Ensemble. (718) 601-7399.
