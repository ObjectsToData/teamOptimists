SPARE TIMES


Published: October 31, 2003

EVENTS

CITY UNIVERSITY OF NEW YORK GRADUATE CENTER, 365 Fifth Avenue, at 34th Street. ''Intimacy and Geography: The National Asian American Poetry Festival,'' with readings, panels, discussions and performances. Co-sponsored by the Center for the Humanities, the Center for the Study of Women and Society, the Poetry Society of America, the Academy of American Poets and Poet's House and the Asian American Writers Workshop. Today, 10 a.m. to 8 p.m.; tomorrow, 10 a.m. to 9 p.m. Fee for most events $7, free for students and members of the Asian American Writers' Workshop; a closing night reception is $10. Fee for all events, which includes a writing seminar, is $50, $35 for students and members of the Asian American Writers' Workshop. Information: (212) 817-8215.

RUSSIAN FILM WEEK IN NEW YORK continues through Sunday with most films at two East Village locations: Anthololgy Film Archives, 32 Second Avenue, at Second Street; City Cinemas Village East, Second Avenue, at 12th Street. One film, ''Mail Order Bride,'' a joint production of the United States and Russia starring Danny Aiello and Frank Gorshin, will be shown on Sunday at noon at Anthology Film Archives. Screening tickets are $10. Festival information: (212) 387-0299; www.nrs.com/russianfilms.

NEW YORK CITY MARATHON HEALTH AND FITNESS EXPO, Jacob K. Javits Convention Center, 11th Avenue and 35th Street, Manhattan. With exhibits, training sessions and raffles. Presented by Q 104.3 FM. Sponsored by the Dutch company ING, and the New York Road Runners Club. Today, 10 a.m. to 8 p.m.; tomorrow, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Free admission. Information: (212) 216-2000.

''HALLOWEEN EXTRAVAGANZA,'' Cathedral of St. John the Divine, 1047 Amsterdam Avenue, at 112th Street, Morningside Heights. A screening of the 1919 silent film ''The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari,'' followed by a puppet procession of ghouls by Ralph Lee. Tonight at 7 and 10. Tickets: $15; $7 for students. Information: (212) 662-2133.

27TH VILLAGE HALLOWEEN COSTUME BALL, Theater for the New City, 155 First Avenue, at 10th Street, East Village. Today, with free outdoor events beginning at 4 p.m., followed by indoor performances at 8 p.m.; doors open 7:30 p.m. Tickets to indoor show, $15; theatergoers should dress in costume or formal attire. Reservations recommended: (212) 254-1109.

30TH VILLAGE HALLOWEEN PARADE, Avenue of the Americas, from Spring Street to 23rd Street, Greenwich Village. Tonight at 7, with, as grand marshal, Audrey II, the puppet star of ''Little Shop of Horrors.''

''HAUNTED MANSION AT GRAND CENTRAL TERMINAL,'' Vanderbilt Hall. An interactive exhibition with props and costumes from ''The Haunted Mansion,'' a Disney film to open next month.Today, 7 a.m. to midnight, and tomorrow, 9 a.m. to 6 p.m.

WORLD'S LARGEST BLOCK PARTY, Madison Avenue, from 42nd to 57th Streets. Tomorrow, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m. Sponsored by the American Diabetes Foundation.

DAY OF THE DEAD CELEBRATIONS. Tomorrow, as part of ''First Saturday,'' there will be free film screenings, storytelling, music and dancing, beginning at 5 p.m. at the Brooklyn Museum of Art, 200 Eastern Parkway, at Prospect Park; information: (718) 638-5000. At the Staten Island Children's Museum, cooking class in making Halloween treats and a Halloween party are planned today, while a workshop in making dancing skeletons to shake on the Day of the Dead will be held tomorrow and Sunday beginning at 12:30 p.m.; the museum is at 1000 Richmond Terrace. Workshops are $3, plus museum admission, $5; free for children under one and members; information: (718) 273-2060.

ORIBE EXHIBITION: LIFESTYLE, CRAFTS AND SIGHTS OF GIFU, JAPAN, Grand Central Terminal, Vanderbilt Hall. Today and tomorrow, 11 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Sunday, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. With crafts, furniture and performances (today at 1 and 5 p.m.). Sponsored by the Gifu Prefectural Government.

ROCKEFELLER CENTER FARMERS MARKET, Rockefeller Center Plaza. Fridays and Saturdays, 8 a.m. to 6 p.m. Through Nov. 15. Children's activities every Saturday.

RECREATION

NEW YORK CITY MARATHON, Central Park. More than 30,000 runners from around the world participate in this 26.2-mile run through the city's five boroughs all day Sunday, beginning at 10:10 a.m.; women's race at 9:35 at the Verrazano-Narrows Bridge in Staten Island, then going through Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx and Manhattan, ending next to Tavern on the Green in Central Park. For the first time, there will be four designated areas for spectators to cheer, follow runners and watch television broadcasts of the run; they are in Brooklyn near the Brooklyn Academy of Music, 30 Lafayette Avenue, at Ashland Place, Fort Greene; and McCarren Park, 66 Bedford Avenue, at Lorimer Street, Williamsburg; in Queens, at Hunters Point Community Park, Vernon Boulevard and 48th Street, Long Island City; and, in Manhattan at the Thomas Jefferson Recreation Center, First Avenue and 113th Street. Spectators may also view the runners all day along the route. Sponsored by the Dutch company ING and the New York Road Runners Club. Information: (212) 860-4455; www.ingnycmarathon.org.
