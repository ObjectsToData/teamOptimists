59 Are Chosen for National Academy of Sciences


Published: May 10, 1992

The National Academy of Sciences, the 129-year-old private organization that advises the Federal Government on science and technology, has elected 59 new members. The election brings the academy's active membership to 1,651 scientists and engineers. Fourteen nonvoting foreign associates were also elected; their number is now 289.

Election to the academy is considered one of the highest honors that can be accorded an American scientist or engineer. The society said the new members were elected "in recognition of their distinguished and continuing achievements in original research." Following are the newly elected members and their affiliations at the time of the election.
