By THE NEW YORK TIMES
Published: September 30, 2005

For Children

Theater

NEW YORK MUSICAL THEATER FESTIVAL presents "Isabelle and the Pretty-Ugly Spell," a tale of a mixed-up fairy godmother, today and tomorrow at 1 p.m. and Sunday at 1 and 4:30 p.m. at the Lion Theater, 410 West 42nd Street, Clinton. Also, "Uncle Jed's Barbershop," a look at a Southern childhood, based on Margaree King Mitchell's book, today at 5 p.m. at the Theater at St. Clement's, 423 West 46th Street, Clinton. Tickets to each: $15. Reservations: (212) 352-3101.

'MY MAGICAL COLOMBIAN BUS' ('Mi Chivita Magica'), a bilingual play about a girl trying to adjust to city life, Saturdays at 3 p.m. through Oct. 15 at Teatro SEA, 107 Suffolk Street, Lower East Side. Admission: $12; children, $10. Reservations: (212) 529-1545.

'STARRING MOVIE MOXIE AND THE MAKE BELIEVERS,' a tribute to silent movies, with music and dance, tomorrow at 2 p.m. at Dance Theater Workshop, 219 West 19th Street, Chelsea, (212) 924-0077. Tickets: $20; children, $10.

Events

MUSIC AND CRAFTS, on Sunday, with a performance at 1 p.m. by the group Kaskeset, followed by a holiday card workshop, at the Children's Galleries for Jewish Culture, 515 West 20th Street, Chelsea, (212) 924-4500. Admission: $5.

CHESS AND FALCONS IN CENTRAL PARK Tomorrow, noon to 4 p.m., a "Falconry Extravaganza," with winged visitors in the East Meadow, Fifth Avenue and East 99th Street. Information: 311. Sunday at 10 a.m., the Annual Youth Chess Tournament, open to kindergarteners to eighth graders, at Chess and Checkers House, midpark, south of 65th Street; registration: (212) 338-5350. Both free.

'RALPH'S WORLD,' kid-friendly rock by Ralph Covert, tomorrow at 11 a.m. at Symphony Space, 2537 Broadway, at 95th Street, (212) 864-5400. Tickets: $16 and $20; children, $12.

TOUR DE PARC, for bicyclists 3 to 9, Sunday at 10:30 a.m. on the Esplanade Plaza of Battery Park City, Lower Manhattan. Free. Registration: (212) 267-9700.

'books make a home' Youngsters can create greetings to be put in books sent to families affected by Hurricane Katrina, today, 7 a.m. to 7 p.m. in Rockefeller Plaza. Sponsored by Scholastic Books and organized by Habitat for Humanities International and the Warner Music Group; scholastic.com/humanityplaza.

'DEAR SANTA,' stories and music by Bill Harley, tomorrow at 1 p.m. at Joe's Pub, 425 Lafayette Street, at Astor Place, East Village, (212) 539-8770. Tickets: $15; 12 and under, $12.

INSTRUMENT INTRODUCTION DAY, for those 6 to 12, today from 4 to 6 p.m. at the School of the Blessed Sacrament, 147 West 70th Street, Manhattan. Admission: $10 per family. Sponsored by InterSchool Orchestras of New York. Information: (212) 410-0370.
