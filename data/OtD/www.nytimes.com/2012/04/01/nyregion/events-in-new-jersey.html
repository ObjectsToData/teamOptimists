Comedy

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center Joan Rivers. April 5 at 8 p.m. $39 to $89. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center Shaq All-Star Comedy Jam. April 7 at 8 p.m. $39.50 and $49.50. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater Artie Lange. April 7 at 8 p.m. $39 to $125. Sinbad. April 13 at 8 p.m. $25 to $49.50. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9000; countbasietheatre.org.

Film

THREE LOCATIONS Three Locations “New Jersey Jewish Film Festival,” featuring 24 films at three locations: the Leon and Toby Cooperman JCC (JCC MetroWest) in West Orange; AMC Loews East Hanover; and the Bickford Theater at the Morris Museum in Morris Township. Through April 1. $10 to $25. njjff.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “Company,” musical by Stephen Sondheim, in an HD screening of a semi-staged production performed with the New York Philharmonic. April 1 at 3 p.m. $12. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

For Children

BRANCHBURG Raritan Valley Community College “Earth, Moon, Sun and Coyote.” Visitors listen to Native American stories and learn about lunar phases, eclipses and mysteries of space. April 4 through 28. $6. Raritan Valley Community College, Route 28 and Lamington Road in North Branch. (908) 231-8805; raritanval.edu/planetarium.

MADISON Museum of Early Trades and Crafts Family Fun Day, featuring family craft activities and more. April 14, 1 to 3 p.m. Free with museum admission. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. (973) 377-2982; metc.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater Sesame Street Live: “1-2-3 Imagine! With Elmo and Friends.” April 12 through 15. $14 to $75. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. statetheatrenj.org; (732) 246-7469.

TENAFLY Tenafly Nature Center Eggs Bunnydict: Visitors meet a live rabbit at the Tenafly Nature Center, create a basket using recycled paper and search for hidden candy eggs in the rabbit habitat along the woodland trails. April 14, 10 a.m. to noon. $10 and $15. Tenafly Nature Center, 313 Hudson Avenue. tenaflynaturecenter.org; (201) 568-6093.

Music and Dance

EAST RUTHERFORD Izod Center Bruce Springsteen and the E Street Band. April 3 and 4. $68 to $98. Izod Center, 50 Route 120. izodcenter.com; (201) 935-3900.

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center One Night of Queen, performed by Gary Mullen and the Works. April 13 at 8 p.m. $39 to $65. Best of the Gypsy Spirit, presented by the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra, led by Jacques Lacombe, with guest Kristin Lee, violinist. For tickets: (800) 255-3476; njsymphony.org. April 15 at 3 p.m. $20 to $60. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. bergenpac.org; (201) 227-1030.

HILLSIDE Enlow Recital Hall at Kean University Ravi Coltrane, jazz saxophonist. April 14 at 7:30 p.m. $25. Enlow Recital Hall at Kean University, 215 North Avenue. (908) 737-7469; enlowhall.kean.edu.

MONTCLAIR Alexander Kasser Theater “LeeSaar the Company: Fame,” dance performance. Through April 1. $15. Alexander Kasser Theater, 1 Normal Avenue. (973) 655-5112; peakperfs.org.

MONTCLAIR Wellmont Theater Levon Helm Band, rock. April 6 at 8 p.m. $40 to $80. Wellmont Theater, 5 Seymour Street. (973) 783-9500; wellmonttheatre.com.

MORRISTOWN Mayo Performing Arts Center The Starlight Series: Nova Chamber Ensemble, performing the Rachmaninoff Sonata for Cello and Piano. April 3 at 8 p.m. $20. The Moody Blues. April 10 and 11. $85 to $150. Mayo Performing Arts Center, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater The Moscow Festival Ballet in “Cinderella,” under the artistic direction of former Bolshoi dancer Sergei Radchenko. April 6 at 8 p.m. $32 to $52. One Night of Queen, performed by Gary Mullen and the Works. April 7 at 8 p.m. $25 to $55. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Victoria J. Mastrobuono Theater Senior Dance Concert, featuring world premieres by emerging choreographers at Mason Gross School of the Arts. April 1 at 2 and 7:30 p.m. $10 and $15. Victoria J. Mastrobuono Theater, Mason Gross Performing Arts Center, 85 George Street. (732) 932-7511; masongross.rutgers.edu.

NEWARK Prudential Center “Michael Jackson: The Immortal World Tour,” by Cirque du Soleil. Through April 1. $53 to $178. Prudential Center, 165 Mulberry Street. prucenter.com; (973) 757-6000.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center “Beethoven 3 and 5,” with the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra, led by Jacques Lacombe, conductor, performing a program of works by Beethoven and Walker. April 1 at 3 p.m. $20 to $85. For tickets: (800) 255-3476; njsymphony.org. Paco de Lucia, flamenco artist, performing jazz, pop and Latin music. April 13 at 8 p.m. $50 to $90. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. njpac.org; (888) 466-5722.

PRINCETON Paul Robeson Center for the Arts “The Power of African American Music to Inspire — A Paul Robeson Tribute Concert,” featuring Follow the Drinking Gourd with a poetry reading by Alvin Aubert. April 7 at 7:30 p.m. $10 and $15. Paul Robeson Center for the Arts, 102 Witherspoon Street. (609) 924-8777; artscouncilofprinceton.org.

PRINCETON Richardson Auditorium Jonathan Biss, pianist, performs works by Beethoven and other classical composers. April 5 at 8 p.m. $5 to $40. Richardson Auditorium in Alexander Hall on the Princeton University Campus. (609) 258-3000; princeton.edu/richaud/.

RAHWAY Union County Performing Arts Center “I’ll Be There,” a tribute to Michael Jackson, Earth, Wind and Fire and the Temptations. April 6 at 8 p.m. $25 and $35. Union County Performing Arts Center, 1601 Irving Street. (732) 499-8226; ucpac.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater Monmouth Symphony Orchestra, with Soyeong Park, violinist and winner of the orchestra’s 2011 Young Artist Concerto Competition. April 1 at 3 p.m. $35. “Video Games Live,” a concert event featuring music from video games. April 4 at 7:30 p.m. $29.50 to $75. Best of the Gypsy Spirit, presented by the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra, led by Jacques Lacombe, with guest Kristin Lee, violinist. April 14 at 8 p.m. For tickets: (800) 255-3476; njsymphony.org. $20 to $60. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

SOUTH ORANGE South Orange Performing Arts Center Adam Pascal and Anthony Rapp, cabaret. April 14 at 8 p.m. $45 to $75. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-2787; sopacnow.org.

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum Lenore Raphael, jazz pianist, with Jack Wilkens, guitarist, pays tribute to Oscar Peterson. April 14 at 8 p.m. $10. Tuesdays through Thursdays, noon to 4 p.m. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. (201) 836-3499; puffinculturalforum.org.

TRENTON Patriots Theater at the War Memorial Best of the Gypsy Spirit, presented by the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra, led by Jacques Lacombe, with guest Kristin Lee, violinist. April 13 at 7:30 p.m. $20 to $60. For tickets: (800) 255-3476; njsymphony.org. Patriots Theater at the War Memorial, 1 Memorial Drive. thewarmemorial.com; (609) 984-8400.

WAYNE Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University Paquito D’Rivera with the William Paterson Latin Jazz Ensemble, performing jazz and traditional Cuban music. April 1 at 4 p.m. $8 to $15. Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University, 300 Pompton Road. wplive.org; (973) 720-2371.

WEST LONG BRANCH Pollak Theater, Monmouth University Dead On — Live!, Grateful Dead tribute concert. April 13 at 8 p.m. $30 and $40. Pollak Theater, Monmouth University, 400 Cedar Avenue. (732) 263-6889; monmouth.edu/arts_events.

Theater

BLOOMFIELD Westminster Center for the Arts, Bloomfield College “Speech and Debate,” drama by Stephen Karam. Through April 1. $20. Westminster Center for the Arts, Bloomfield College, 449 Franklin Street. (973) 748-9008, ext. 279.

HOBOKEN Monroe TheatreSpace in Hoboken “The Pavilion,” drama by Craig Wright. April 12 through 29. $18 and $30. Monroe TheatreSpace in Hoboken, 720 Monroe Street. (201) 683-7014; milesquaretheatre.org.

MADISON F.M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University Shakespeare Live! at the Kirby: “Macbeth,” by William Shakespeare. An abridged 70-minute production geared for those in grades 6 and up. April 14 at 2 p.m. $15. F.M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University, 36 Madison Avenue. (973) 408-5600; njshakespeare.org.

MILLBURN Paper Mill Playhouse “Damn Yankees,” musical by Richard Adler and Jerry Ross. Through April 1. $32 to $87. Paper Mill Playhouse, 22 Brookside Drive. (973) 376-4343; papermill.org.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Bickford Theater “I Do!, I Do!,” musical with book and lyrics by Tom Jones and music by Harvey Schmidt. April 12 through May 6. $20 to $40. Bickford Theater, 6 Normandy Heights Road. morrismuseum.org; (973) 971-3706.

NEW BRUNSWICK George Street Playhouse “Twelve Angry Men,” a play by Reginald Rose. Through April 8. $26.50 to $41.50. George Street Playhouse, 9 Livingston Avenue. gsponline.org; (732) 246-7717.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jameson Theater The Jameson Project: An Evening of One Acts. April 11 through 15. $10 and $15. Jameson Theater, Jones Avenue at Townsend Street. (732) 932-7511; masongross.rutgers.edu.

NEW JERSEY Actors Shakespeare Company at New Jersey City University “Macbeth,” by William Shakespeare. Through April 1. $10 to $30. Actors Shakespeare Company at New Jersey City University, 285 West Side Avenue. ascnj.org; (201) 200-2390.

ORADELL Bergen County Players, Little Firehouse Theater “The Man Who Came To Dinner,” a comedy by George S. Kaufman and Moss Hart. Through April 21. $16 and $19. Bergen County Players, Little Firehouse Theater, 298 Kinderkamack Road. (201) 261-4200; bcplayers.org.

POMPTON LAKES Smiling Rhino Theater “The Odd Couple,” by Neil Simon. Through April 1. $22.47 and $24.61. Smiling Rhino Theater, 247 Wanaque Avenue. (973) 248-9491; smilingrhinotheatre.com.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center “Travesties,” a play by Tom Stoppard. Through April 1. $20 to $60. Circus Oz, featuring acrobatics, aerial acts and comedy. April 7 and 8. $40 to $55. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

RED BANK Two River Theater “In This House,” musical, with music by Mike Reid, lyrics by Sarah Schlesinger and book by Jonathan Bernstein, Ms. Schlesinger and Mr. Reid. Through April 8. $45 to $65. “Carry It On,” a musical memoir with Maureen McGovern. April 3 through 22. $24 to $57. Two River Theater, 21 Bridge Avenue. (732) 345-1400; trtc.org.

WEST WINDSOR Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College “The Wizard of Oz,” presented by the Yardley Players. Through April 1. $14 to $18. Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College, 1200 Old Trenton Road. (609) 570-3333; kelseytheatre.net.

Museums and Galleries

CAPE MAY Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities “Wendel White’s Schools for the Colored,” an exhibition featuring 50 photographs of segregated schools in the Northern states. Through April 15. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. (609) 884-5404; capemaymac.org.

CLINTON Hunterdon Art Museum “Fragmented,” an exhibition featuring works by Astrid Bowlby, Sebastian Rug, Christopher Skura and Ben Butler. Through June 3. Donation: $5. Tuesdays through Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Hunterdon Art Museum, 7 Lower Center Street. hunterdonartmuseum.org; (908) 735-8415.

DEMAREST Mikhail Zakin Gallery “Leonard Freed: The Italians,” an exhibition of photographs. Through April 6. “Notations 21 Project,” an exhibition celebrating the aesthetic and graphic beauty of contemporary illustrated musical scores. April 13 through May 11. Reception: April 21, 2012, from 5 to 7 p.m. Mondays through Fridays, 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. Mikhail Zakin Gallery, 561 Piermont Road. (201) 767-7160; tasoc.org.

EWING College of New Jersey “Illuminating Data: Visualizing the Information that Moves Our World,” group show. Through April 18. College of New Jersey, 2000 Pennington Road, AIMM Building, Room 319. tcnjartgallery.pages.tcnj.edu; (609) 771-2198.

HALEDON American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark “The Shirts Off Our Backs: Union T-Shirt Quilts,” exhibition in which quilting patches are made from union members’ T-shirts. Through April 17. Suggested donation: $5. Wednesdays through Saturdays, 1 to 4 p.m., or by appointment. American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark, 83 Norwood Street. (973) 595-7953; labormuseum.org.

HAMILTON Grounds for Sculpture “Aerial Roots,” outdoor exhibition of large-scale creations by Steve Tobin. Through summer 2012. “White Hot: Expressions in Iron,” more than 40 works by nine artists. Through April 8. “2012 Outstanding Achievement in Contemporary Sculpture Awards Exhibition.” Through April 22. “Instrumental Transitions,” sculptures by Michael Dunbar. Through April 22. $8 to $12; members and children under 5, free. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. (609) 586-0616; groundsforsculpture.org.

HOBOKEN Hoboken Historical Museum “Driving Under the Hudson: The History of the Holland and Lincoln Tunnels,” exhibition. Through July 1. $2; members, free. Tuesdays through Thursdays, 2 to 7 p.m.; Friday, 1 to 5 p.m.; and Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Hoboken Historical Museum, 1301 Hudson Street. hobokenmuseum.org; (201) 656-2240.

LAMBERTVILLE The Artists’ Gallery “Inside Out — Reflections of Human Experience in the Environment,” an exhibition of recent paintings by Materese Roche and Alla Podolsky. Through April 1. The Artists’ Gallery, 18 Bridge Street. (609) 397-4588; lambertvillearts.com.

LINCROFT Monmouth Museum “Greed + Fear = Irrational Exuberance,” an exhibition featuring collage works in mixed media. Through April 22. “Art of Illusion: A Juried Invitational exhibition,” featuring 62 artworks in a variety of media that explore the links between perception, appearance and reality. Through May 20. $7; members and children under 2, free. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Thursdays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Fridays, 10 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Monmouth Museum, 765 Newman Springs Road. (732) 747-2266; monmouthmuseum.org.

MAHWAH Mahwah Museum “Les Paul in Mahwah: A Tribute,” exhibition of guitars and memorabilia. Through June. “New Jersey Fights in the Civil War — Remembering Soldiers Buried in Mahwah.” Through June 30. $3 to $5; members and children under 5, free. Wednesdays, Saturdays and Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Mahwah Museum, 201 Franklin Turnpike. (201) 512-0099; mahwahmuseum.org.

MONTCLAIR Montclair Art Museum “George Inness: Private Treasures,” an exhibition featuring works from private collections in the Montclair area. Through April 1. “Patterns, Systems, Structures: Abstraction in American Art,” an exhibition from the permanent collection. Through May 19, 2013. $12; seniors and students, $10; members and children under 12, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Montclair Art Museum, 3 South Mountain Avenue. (973) 746-5555; montclairartmuseum.org.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Morris Museum “Modern Tapestries and Floor Coverings Conceived by Picasso, Calder and Contemporaries.” Through April 15. “Harmony in Clay: the Elegance and Refinement of Song Ceramics,” a collection of Chinese ceramics. Through June 24. $7 to $10. Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 11 a.m. to 8 p.m.; and Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3700; morrismuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN Acorn Hall “Over Here and Over There: Morris County’s Role in World War II,” an exhibition featuring uniforms, medals, photographs, jewelry, documents and other objects. Through June 18. $6; seniors, $5; students, $3. Mondays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Acorn Hall, 68 Morris Avenue. (973) 267-3465; www.acornhall.org.

MORRISTOWN Macculloch Hall Historical Museum “Gone for a Soldier: Jerseymen in the Civil War,” nearly 200 objects from the Civil War. Through July 1. $4 to $8; children 12 and under, free. Wednesdays, Thursdays and Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Macculloch Hall Historical Museum, 45 Macculloch Avenue. (973) 538-2404; maccullochhall.org.

MORRISTOWN Therese A. Maloney Art Gallery, College of Saint Elizabeth “The Abstract Universe: Microcosm/Macrocosm,” group show. Through April 15. Tuesdays through Thursdays, 1 to 7 p.m.; Fridays, Saturdays and Mondays, 1 to 5 p.m. Therese A. Maloney Art Gallery, College of Saint Elizabeth, 2 Convent Road. (973) 290-4315; maloneyartgallery.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum “At/Around/Beyond: Fluxus at Rutgers,” including sculptural objects, assemblages, prints, multiples, ephemera, books, films, sound works and photographs. Through April 1. “In the Search of an Absolute: Art of Valery Yurlov,” solo exhibition. Through June 3. “Popcorn and Starbaby: Children’s Book Illustrations by Frank Asch.” Through June 24. “Rachel Perry Welty: 24/7,” an exhibition of works by Rachel Perry Welty, a Boston-based conceptual artist who creates works on life in the 21st century. Through July 8. “Aspects of Architecture: The Prints of John Taylor Arms ,” exhibition of 26 prints dating between 1919 and 1940. April 14 through July 31. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum, 71 Hamilton Street. (732) 932-7237; zimmerlimuseum.rutgers.edu.

NEW BRUNSWICK Rutgers University, Douglass Library “Metamorphoses: Pictures by Audrey Flack,” exhibition featuring prints, drawings and photographs by the American realist painter and sculptor Audrey Flack. Through June 30. Mondays through Thursdays, 8 a.m. to midnight; Fridays, 8 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; and Sundays, noon to midnight. Rutgers University, Douglass Library, 8 Chapel Drive. (732) 932-3726; iwa.rutgers.edu.

NEWARK The Newark Museum “Generation Fit: Steps to a Healthier Lifestyle,” interactive family exhibition that explores issues of nutrition and exercise. Through Dec. 30. “From Meiji to Modern: Japanese Art Goes Global, 19th to 21st Centuries.” Through Dec. 31. “The Teapot,” an exhibition of 66 teapots. Through Dec. 31. “Poetic Pastimes: Japan and the Art of Leisure,” an exhibition that includes more than 200 pieces of fine and decorative arts. Through May 6. “Romare Bearden Selections From the Newark Museum Collection.” Through Aug. 12. “Studio Glass: 1962-2012 ,” featuring more than a dozen pieces by both American and European glass artists from the Museum’s 20th-century glass holdings. Through Aug. 5. $6 to $10; members, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. (973) 596-6550; newarkmuseum.org.

NEWTONVILLE The African American Heritage Museum of Southern New Jersey “Stealing Home: How Jackie Robinson Changed America,” an exhibition including narrative, photos, video and artifacts. Through May 31. Exhibition about Langston Hughes, poet and playwright, featuring his works as well as artifacts and photos. Through May 31. Exhibition on Dr. Albert Forsythe, pioneer of black aviation, with artifacts and photos. Through May 31. The African American Heritage Museum of Southern New Jersey, 661 Jackson Road. (609) 704-5495; aahmsnj.org.

OCEAN TOWNSHIP Township of Ocean Historical Museum “A Tribute to Norma Eberhardt Dauphin,” an exhibition about the actress, a founding member of the museum. Through May 31. Donations accepted. Township of Ocean Historical Museum, 703 Deal Road. (732) 531-2136; oceanmuseum.org.

PRINCETON Morven Museum and Garden “Puzzles of the Brain: An Artist’s Journey Through Amnesia,” an exhibition of works by Lonni Sue Johnson. Through June 3. $6; seniors and students, $5. Wednesdays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Morven Museum and Garden, 55 Stockton Street. (609) 924-8144; morven.org.

PRINCETON Paul Robeson Center for the Arts “Drawing Beyond,” an exhibition of contemporary drawing. Through April 13. “Arnold Roth: A Selection of Work From Area Collections.” Through April 7. Paul Robeson Center for the Arts, 102 Witherspoon Street. (609) 924-8777; artscouncilofprinceton.org.

PRINCETON Princeton University Art Museum “Qian Du (1764-1844) and Style in Qing Dynasty Painting,” an exhibition bringing together Qing landscape paintings from the museum’s collection and private lenders. Through April 22. “Myths, Tales and Poetry in Japanese Art,” featuring works from the 15th to the 19th centuries, selected from the museum’s collection of Japanese art. Through May 6. “John Constable: Oil Sketches From the Victoria and Albert Museum.” Through June 10. “Princeton and the Gothic Revival: 1870-1930,” exhibition exploring the Gothic Revival movement in architecture and design across America at the end of the 19th century, using Princeton’s campus as a case study and launching point. Through June 24. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 10 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Princeton University Art Museum, Nassau and Witherspoon Streets. (609) 258-3788; artmuseum.princeton.edu.

PRINCETON Princeton University Library “Capping Liberty: The Invention of a Numismatic Iconography for the New American Republic,” an exhibition of coins, medals, bank notes and related books, manuscripts and graphic arts from Princeton University collections at the Milberg Gallery. Through July 8. “A Fine Addition: New and Notable Acquisitions in Princeton’s Special Collections.” Through Aug. 5. Mondays through Fridays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Princeton University Library, 1 Washington Road. princeton.edu/rbsc/; (609) 258-3184.

RAHWAY Arts Guild of New Jersey “Part and Parcel: The Deconstruction of the Female Form in Contemporary Art,” group show. Through April 26. Arts Guild of New Jersey, 1670 Irving Street. (732) 381-7511; agnj.org.

RED BANK Art Alliance of Monmouth County “The 27th Annual Jean Townsend Award Exhibition,” an exhibition of works around the themes of “Force of Nature” and “Beasts.” Through April 4. Tuesdays through Saturdays, noon to 4 p.m. Art Alliance of Monmouth County, 33 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9403; artallianceofmonmouth.org.

SUMMIT Visual Arts Center of New Jersey “Textility,” an exhibition about contemporary artists’ use of materials, concepts and processes associated with textiles. Through April 1. $3 and $5. Visual Arts Center of New Jersey, 68 Elm Street. (908) 273-9121; artcenternj.org.

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum “We Must Never Forget: German Artists Reflect on the Holocaust,” group show. Through April 17. Tuesdays through Thursdays, noon to 4 p.m. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. (201) 836-3499; puffinculturalforum.org.

UNION TOWNSHIP The Karl and Helen Burger Gallery at Kean University “Embodying the Sacred in Yoruba Art: Selections From the Newark Museum Collection.” Through April 18. Mondays through Thursdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. The Karl and Helen Burger Gallery at Kean University, 1000 Morris Avenue. kean.edu/gallery; (908) 737-0392.
