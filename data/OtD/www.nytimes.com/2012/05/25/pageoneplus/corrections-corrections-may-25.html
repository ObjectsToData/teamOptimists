Corrections: May 25

Published: May 24, 2012

FRONT PAGE

A Political Memo article on Thursday about the risks of President Obama’s attacks on Mitt Romney’s career in private equity misstated the percentage of campaign donations given by securities and investment firms that have gone to Republicans so far in the 2012 election cycle. It is 59 percent, not 57 percent.

•

An art review last Friday about the new home of the Barnes Foundation collection, in Philadelphia, referred incompletely to the design of the lighting system. It was done by Paul Marantz and his firm, Fisher Marantz Stone, not just by Mr. Marantz.

NATIONAL

An article on Wednesday about black Mormons’ views of the presidential candidates misstated the date President Obama endorsed same-sex marriage. It was May 9 — not “last Wednesday” (May 16).

NEW YORK

An article on Monday about calls for Gov. Andrew M. Cuomo to work more assertively to raise New York State’s minimum wage misattributed a comment about an effect of the proposed legislation on families receiving the earned-income tax credit. It was the Senate majority leader, Dean G. Skelos, a Republican, who pointed out that poor families receiving the earned-income tax credit could stand to lose some benefits if the minimum wage was increased. Russell Sykes, a senior fellow at the Empire Center for New York State Policy, did not say that an increase in the minimum wage could make some families ineligible for the credit.

BUSINESS DAY

An article on Thursday about complaints by the Commission Nationale de l’Informatique et des Libertés, a French data protection agency known as C.N.I.L., that Google has not been cooperative in an investigation of its new privacy policy included an inaccurate English translation from a C.N.I.L. news release, provided by the agency. The release said that Google’s answers to a questionnaire about its policy had been “incomplete or approximate,” not “incomplete or inaccurate.” (The agency corrected its translation after the article was published.)

•

An article last Friday about the understated tastes of Silicon Valley’s wealthy referred incorrectly to the jeans favored by Jack Dorsey, a Twitter founder. While he wears jeans by the designer Scott Morrison, he does not own a pair of $1,200 custom jeans from Mr. Morrison’s 3x1 line.

•

An article on Thursday about the insider trading trial of Rajat Gupta, a former Goldman Sachs director, misspelled the surname of a Goldman co-president. He is Jon Winkelried, not Winkelreid.

•

The Advertising column on Thursday, about the expansion and linking of product placement on television to online programs, misspelled the surname of a child actor in “Touch,” a Fox TV drama. He is David Mazouz, not Masouz.

•

Because of an editing error, an article on Thursday about a study on the coverage available from health insurance policies gave an outdated name for a research center where the study’s lead author, Jon R. Gabel, works. He is a health researcher at NORC at the University of Chicago; it is no longer known as the National Opinion Research Center.

THE ARTS

A picture caption on Thursday with a dance review of Cedar Lake Contemporary Ballet, at the Joyce Theater in Manhattan, misidentified the dancer shown with Jubal Battisti. He is Oscar Ramos — not Jon Bond, another member of the company.

OBITUARIES

An obituary on Thursday about the critic and scholar Paul Fussell misstated, in some copies, the surname of the former chairman of the National Endowment for the Humanities, who interviewed Mr. Fussell in 1996. He is Sheldon Hackney, not Hackley.
