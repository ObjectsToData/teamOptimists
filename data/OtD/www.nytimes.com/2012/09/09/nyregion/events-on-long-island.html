Calendar

Events on Long Island

Published: September 8, 2012

A guide to cultural and recreational events on Long Island. Items for the calendar should be sent at least three weeks in advance to lical@nytimes.com.

Related

  • Times Topics: Long Island Arts | Long Island Dining

Connect With NYTMetro

Comedy

PATCHOGUE The Plaza Cinema and Media Arts Center Live Comedy Show: The Wilshire Comedy Group. Sept. 19 at 7 p.m. $12. The Plaza Cinema and Media Arts Center, 20 Terry Street. (631) 438-0083; plazamac.org.

WESTBURY NYCB Theater at Westbury Russell Brand. Sept. 14 at 8 p.m. $39.50 to $69.50. NYCB Theater at Westbury, 960 Brush Hollow Road. (800) 745-3000; livenation.com.

Film

EAST HAMPTON Pollock-Krasner House and Study Center Artists on Film: Motion and Emotion Film Series: “Montage Haitien (circa 1955),” and “Themes from Widow’s Walk (ca. 1961),” both by Val Telberg, surrealist photographer/filmmaker/sculptor. Sept. 14 at 7 p.m. $5; free for members. Pollock-Krasner House and Study Center, 830 Springs-Fireplace Road. pkhouse.org; (631) 324-4929.

GARDEN CITY Adelphi University Performing Arts Center “The Sleeping Beauty,” screening of a performance by the Royal Ballet, London. Through Sept. 9. $5 and $15. “La Bayadère,” screening of a performance by the Paris Opera Ballet. Sept. 12 and 16. $15. Adelphi University Performing Arts Center, 1 South Avenue. (516) 877-4000; aupac.adelphi.edu.

GARDEN CITY Ethical Humanist Society of Long Island Thursday Night Movie Series: Food and Sustainability: Challenges for a Hungry Planet: “Queen of the Sun,” a documentary about the honeybee. Sept. 13 at 7 p.m. $10. Ethical Humanist Society of Long Island, 38 Old Country Road. (516) 741-7304; ehsli.org.

HUNTINGTON Cinema Arts Center “Planeat,” a documentary by Shelley Lee Davies and Om Shlomi about eating less meat. Sept. 12 at 7:30 p.m. $10 and $15. “Your Sister’s Sister,” a comedy directed by Lynn Shelton. Sept. 13 at 7:30 p.m. $10 and $15. Cinema Arts Center, 423 Park Avenue. cinemaartscentre.org; (800) 838-3006.

PATCHOGUE The Plaza Cinema and Media Arts Center Ballet in Cinema: “Swan Lake,” screening of a performance by the Royal Ballet, London. Sept. 9 at 2 p.m.; Sept. 11 at 6 p.m. $10 and $15. The Plaza Cinema and Media Arts Center, 20 Terry Street. (631) 438-0083; plazamac.org.

ROSLYN HARBOR Nassau County Museum of Art “Artists of the 20th Century: Marc Chagall,” Chagall’s roots in Vitebsk, Belarus, as reflected in his art. Through Nov. 4. Tuesdays to Sundays at 11 a.m., noon, 1 p.m. and 3 p.m. Free with museum admission, $4 to $10; members, free. Nassau County Museum of Art, 1 Museum Drive. nassaumuseum.org; (516) 484-9337.

SAG HARBOR Bay Street Theater “Joyous Garde,” a documentary about John Steinbeck’s house in Sag Harbor. Sept. 15, noon to 4 p.m. Free. Bay Street Theater, Main and Bay Streets. (631) 725-9500; baystreet.org.

For Children

GARDEN CITY Long Island Children’s Museum “Fall Free For All,” children make a one-of-a-kind artwork. Sept. 19 through 21. (Museum closed through Sept. 14.) Long Island Children’s Museum, 11 Davis Avenue. (516) 224-5800; licm.org.

MERRICK Merrick Theater and Center for the Arts “Sleeping Beauty,” children’s performance. Weekends, Sept. 15 through Nov. 3. $9. Merrick Theater and Center for the Arts, 2222 Hewlett Avenue. (516) 868-6400;

merricktheatreandcenterforthearts.com.

Music and Dance

AMAGANSETT Stephen Talkhouse Jorma Kaukonen, guitarist. Sept. 15 at 8 p.m. $80 and $95. Stephen Talkhouse, 161 Main Street. stephentalkhouse.com; (631) 267-3117.

BAY SHORE Boulton Center for the Performing Arts Howie Day, singer. Sept. 14 at 8 p.m. $20 and $25. Larry Carlton, jazz/pop/rock guitarist. Sept. 20 at 8 p.m. $45. Boulton Center for the Performing Arts, 37 West Main Street. boultoncenter.org; (631) 969-1101.

DIX HILLS Dix Hills Performing Arts Center Ella Fitzgerald and Lionel Hampton Tribute, featuring Cat da Silva and Bryan Carrott. Sept. 9 at 3 p.m. $25 and $30. Neil Diamond Tribute with Diamond One Hot Night, a 10-piece band. Sept. 14 at 7:30 p.m. $30 and $35. Karen Oberlin’s Secret Love: A tribute to the music of Doris Day. Sept. 15 at 7:30 p.m. $40 to $50. “Stand Up and Sit Down,” with Corky Laing, drummer. Sept. 16 at 2 p.m. $20. Dix Hills Performing Arts Center, 305 North Service Road. (631) 656-2148; dhpac.org.

EAST HAMPTON Guild Hall Long Island Opera presents George Bizet’s “Carmen.” Sept. 15 at 8 p.m. $15 to $75. Guild Hall, 158 Main Street. (631) 324-4050; guildhall.org.

FREEPORT Zion Cathedral Church of God in Christ Zion Cathedral presents “Night at the Opera,” with opera singer Audrey Dubois-Harris and friends. Sept. 15 at 6 p.m. $25 and $35. Zion Cathedral Church of God in Christ, 312 Grand Avenue. (516) 378-3415; zioncathedral.org.

HEMPSTEAD Nassau Coliseum “Dralion: Cirque du Soleil,” Chinese acrobatic arts. Through Sept. 9. $55 to $163. New Edition, R&B. Sept. 19 at 8 p.m. $50 to $137.60. Nassau Coliseum, 1255 Hempstead Turnpike. (516) 794-9300; nassaucoliseum.com.

HUNTINGTON The Paramount Creed, rock. Sept. 9 at 7:30 p.m. $80 to $116.75. The Paramount, 370 New York Avenue. (631) 673-7300; paramountny.com.

OYSTER BAY Coe Hall, Planting Fields Arboretum State Historic Park “Beethoven Festival,” featuring concerts and lectures. Sept. 16 at 2 p.m. $20 and $25. Coe Hall, Planting Fields Arboretum State Historic Park, 1395 Planting Fields Road. (516) 922-8676; plantingfields.org.

PATCHOGUE Patchogue Theater for the Performing Arts Live in the Lobby: “Wonder in the Lobby,” featuring local artists performing the music of Stevie Wonder. Sept. 13 at 7:30 p.m. $10. Patchogue Theater for the Performing Arts, 71 East Main Street. patchoguetheatre.com; (631) 207-1313.

ROSLYN HARBOR Nassau County Museum of Art The Port Washington Intergenerational Klezmer Ensemble, jazz concert. Sept. 15 at 3 p.m. $5 to $15. Music at the Museum: Jazz with Lynn Stein and Jon Burr with John Hart and Warren Vaché. Through Sept. 22. $5 to $15. Nassau County Museum of Art, 1 Museum Drive. nassaumuseum.org; (516) 484-9337.

WESTBURY NYCB Theater at Westbury J. Geils Band, rock. Sept. 12 at 8 p.m. $39.50 to $59.50. NYCB Theater at Westbury, 960 Brush Hollow Road. livenation.com; (800) 745-3000.

Outdoors

GREENPORT Shelter Island Ferry Dock Jessica W. Day Lighthouse Cruise, featuring six to eight lighthouses. Sept. 10, 9 a.m. to 3 p.m. $60 to $95. Shelter Island Ferry Dock, Third Street. eastendseaport.org; (631) 477-2100.

HUNTINGTON Soldiers and Sailors Memorial Building “Old Burial Ground Tour.” Learn about the history, folk art and intriguing stories connected with the site. Sept. 20 at 2 p.m. $5 and $10. Soldiers and Sailors Memorial Building, 228 Main Street. (631) 427-7045 ext. 403.

MATTITUCK Strawberry Fields Fairgrounds East End GreenFest, an outdoor celebration of green living including vendors, music and a carnival with biodiesel-powered rides and solar-powered lights. Sept. 14 through 17. $3; children 12 and under, free. Strawberry Fields Fairgrounds, Route 48. eastendgreenfest.com; (631) 734-5894.

WEST HEMPSTEAD Hempstead Lake State Park South Shore Audubon Bird Walk. The group meets in Hempstead Lake State Park, Parking Field No. 3, Exit 18 South, from the Southern State Parkway. Sept. 9 at 9:30 a.m. Free. (516) 987-8103;

ssaudubon.org.nysparks.com/parks.

Spoken Word

GREAT NECK Great Neck Library “Bitter Herbs and Honey,” poems and stories by Yiddish, Israeli and American Jewish women writers, presented, with music, by the actress Shirley Romaine. Sept. 10 at 2 p.m. Free. Great Neck Library, 159 Bayview Avenue. (516) 466-8055; greatnecklibrary.org.

MONTAUK Montauk Library “Shakespeare on Aging,” a discussion with Maurice Charney, New York Humanities Council. Sept. 9 at 3:30 p.m. Free. Montauk Library, 871 Montauk Highway. www.suffolk.lib.ny.us/libraries/mntk/; (631) 668-3377.

SHELTER ISLAND Shelter Island Public Library Kaylie Jones, editor of the mystery book “Long Island Noir,” will discuss the book along with two of the contributing authors, Reed Farrel Coleman and Mathew McGevna. Sept. 14 at 7 p.m. Free. Shelter Island Public Library, 37 North Ferry Road. (631) 749-0042;

readshelterisland.org.

STONY BROOK Long Island Museum of American Art, History and Carriages Golden Crest Records: the rock historian John Broven will discuss the record label based in Huntington Station and founded in 1956. Free with museum admission. Sept. 9 at 2 p.m. Long Island Museum of American Art, History and Carriages, 1200 Route 25A. longislandmuseum.org; (631) 751-0066.

Theater

BELLPORT Gateway Playhouse “Phantom,” musical with music and lyrics by Maury Yeston and book by Arthur Kopit. Through Sept. 9. $52 to $58. Gateway Playhouse, 215 South Country Road.

gatewayplayhouse.com; (631) 286-1133.

EAST HAMPTON Guild Hall The Naked Stage Readings: “God of Carnage,” comedy by Yasmina Reza; translated by Christopher Hampton. Sept. 18 at 7:30 p.m. Free. Guild Hall, 158 Main Street. (631) 324-4050; guildhall.org.

LINDENHURST Studio Theater “The Best Little Whorehouse in Texas,” musical with book by Larry L. King and Peter Masterson and music and lyrics by Carol Hall. Sept. 22 through Oct. 14. $15 to $25. Studio Theater, 141 South Wellwood Avenue. (631) 226-8400; studiotheatreli.com.

MERRICK Merrick Theater and Center for the Arts “Next to Normal,” music by Tom Kitt and book and lyrics by Brian Yorkey. Through Sept. 9. $17 and $20. Merrick Theater and Center for the Arts, 2222 Hewlett Avenue. merricktheatreandcenterforthearts.com; (516) 868-6400.

NORTHPORT John W. Engeman Theater “Sweet Charity,” a musical with music by Cy Coleman, lyrics by Dorothy Fields and book by Neil Simon. Through Oct. 28. $65. John W. Engeman Theater, 250 Main Street. johnwengemantheater.com; (631) 261-2900.

OAKDALE CM Performing Arts Center “Noises Off,” comedy by English playwright Michael Frayn. Sept. 14 through Sept. 30. $16 to $22. CM Performing Arts Center, 931 Montauk Highway. (631) 218-2810; cmpac.com.

PORT JEFFERSON Theater Three “Treasure Island: A Musical Adventure.” Sept. 15 through Oct. 27. $15 to $30. Theater Three, 412 Main Street. (631) 928-9100; theaterthree.com.

Museums and Galleries

BELLPORT Circa Something Fine Art “Once Expelled, Now Propelled, Hometown Belligerence,” paintings by Eddie Rehm. Through Sept. 15. Thursdays through Saturdays, noon to 6 p.m. Circa Something Fine Art, 117A South Country Road. circasomething.com; (631) 803-6706.

BELLPORT Gallery 125 Works by Arthur Pinajian from the 1930s to 1990s. Through Sept. 9. Fridays 3:30 to 9 p.m., Saturdays, 10:30 a.m. to 9 p.m., Sundays, 10:30 a.m. to 1:30 p.m. Gallery 125, 125 South Country Road. (631) 880-2693.

EAST HAMPTON Guild Hall “Eric Fischl: Beach Life,” featuring a wide selection of paintings that span a 30-year artistic career. Through Oct. 14. Guild Hall, 158 Main Street. (631) 324-4050; guildhall.org.

EAST HAMPTON LongHouse Reserve Art Center “Accumulations: Now Exhibition,” curated by Jack Lenor Larsen, including pieces from the collection of Dena Katzenberg and works from other artists. Through Oct. 6. LongHouse Reserve Art Center, 133 Hands Creek Road. longhouse.org; (631) 329-3568.

EAST HAMPTON Pollock-Krasner House and Study Center “Men of Fire: José Clemente Orozco and Jackson Pollock,” an exhibition of paintings. Through Oct. 27. $5 and $10; members, children under 12 and CUNY/SUNY students, free. By appointment only. Pollock-Krasner House and Study Center, 830 Springs-Fireplace Road. pkhouse.org; (631) 324-4929.

GARDEN CITY Ruth S. Harley University, Adelphi University “Outdoor Sculpture Biennial,” featuring the works of 10 skilled sculptors. Through May 31. Reception: Sept. 30, 1 to 4 p.m. Ruth S. Harley University Center, Adelphi University, 1 South Avenue. (516) 877-3126; adelphi.edu.

GARDEN CITY Firehouse Plaza Art Gallery “Complejo,” featuring the paintings of M. Tony Peralta. Through Nov. 2. Mondays, Wednesdays and Thursdays, 11:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Tuesdays, 11:30 a.m. to 7 p.m.; and Saturdays 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. Firehouse Plaza Art Gallery, 1 Education Drive. firehouse.ncc.edu; (516) 572-0619.

HEMPSTEAD Hofstra University Museum “Opportunity and Impact: Works by Émigré Artists,” ranging from artists who arrived from Europe during the early 20th century to present-day emigrants from Cuba and South America, at the David Filderman Gallery. Through Sept. 9. “Toward Greater Awareness: Darfur and American Activism,” the work of Mitch Lewis, who explores the crisis of violence in Darfur through his large sculptural works, at the Emily Lowe Gallery, South Campus. Through Dec. 7. David Filderman Gallery: Daily, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Emily Lowe Gallery: Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m., Saturdays and Sundays, 1 p.m. to 5 p.m. Hofstra University Museum, 112 Hofstra University. (516) 463-5672; hofstra.edu/museum.

HEWLETT Hewlett-Woodmere Public Library “Charlotte Strongwater: The Accidental Artist,” landscape and wildlife paintings in watercolor, pastel and mixed media. Through Sept. 23. Hewlett-Woodmere Library, 1125 Broadway. (516) 374-1967;

nassaulibrary.org/hewlett.

HUNTINGTON Conklin house Gallery “All Things Conklin!” the Huntington Historical Society’s collection of heirlooms, furniture, samplers, coverlets, silver and other household wares donated by the Conklin family. Through Dec. 31. $3 to $5. Thursdays, Fridays and Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Conklin House Gallery, 2 High Street. huntingtonhistoricalsociety.org/conklin.htm; (631) 427-7045.

HUNTINGTON Fotofoto Gallery “Still Life Still,” photography by Susan Kravitz and Susan Dooley using diptychs, triptychs or both. Through Sept. 30. Fridays, 5 to 8 p.m.; Saturdays, noon to 8 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Fotofoto Gallery, 14 West Carver Street. (631) 549-0448; fotofotogallery.org.

HUNTINGTON Heckscher Museum of Art “Robert S. Neuman’s Ship to Paradise,” large-scale, mixed-media drawings. Through Nov. 25. $4 to $6; children under 10, free. “Absorbed by Color: Art in the 20th Century.” Through Dec. 2. $4 to $6; children under 10, free. Wednesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Heckscher Museum of Art, 2 Prime Avenue. heckscher.org; (631) 351-3250.

HUNTINGTON STATION South Huntington Public Library “4 Printmakers,” an exhibition featuring prints by Raisy Derzie, Stephanie Navon Jacobson, Michael Krasowitz and Angela Sanfilippo at the Alfred Van Loen Gallery. Through Oct. 3. Monday, Tuesday, Thursday and Friday, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Wednesday 10 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Saturday 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sunday 1 to 5 p.m. South Huntington Public Library, 145 Pidgeon Hill Road. shpl.info; (631) 549-4411.

JAMESPORT Jedediah Hawkins Inn Barn Gallery “Hung Out to Dry,” oil paintings by Max Moran. Through Sept. 16. Fridays, 5 to 8 p.m. and Saturdays and Sundays, 1 to 7 p.m. Jedediah Hawkins Inn Barn Gallery, 400 South Jamesport Avenue. (631) 722-2900; maxmoran.com.JAMESPORT Rosalie Dimon Gallery, Jamesport Manor Inn East End Arts Members Exhibition, featuring Dan Welden, printmaker, and Elizabeth Malunowicz, classical realist artist. Through Oct. 31. Wednesdays through Mondays, noon to 10 p.m. Rosalie Dimon Gallery, Jamesport Manor Inn, 370 Manor Road. (631) 722-0500;

jamesportmanorinn.com.

MELVILLE Art-Trium “Relationships,” group show. Through Nov. 26. Mondays through Fridays, 7 a.m. to 7 p.m. Art-Trium, 25 Melville Park Road. (631) 271-8423; huntingtonarts.org.

OYSTER BAY Planting Fields Arboretum State Historic Park “Cocktail Culture: The Glamorous Gold Coast Years From Prohibition to 1960,” clothes by designers like Mariano Fortuny and Elsa Schiaparelli, and more. Through Sept. 30. $3.50; free for members. Planting Fields Arboretum State Historic Park, 1395 Planting Fields Road. plantingfields.org; (516) 922-9200.

OYSTER BAY The Koenig Center “TR in ’12,” an exhibition featuring more than 100 objects relating to Theodore Roosevelt’s 1912 presidential campaign. Sept. 21 through Nov. 11. Reception: Sept. 21, 6 to 8 p.m. The Koenig Center, 20 Summit Street. oysterbayhistorical.org; (516) 922-5032.

PORT JEFFERSON Port Jefferson Village Center Anne P. Tuttle: A Life in Art, retrospective. Through Nov. 13. Port Jefferson Village Center, 101-A East Broadway. (631) 802-2160; pjvillagecenter.com.QUOGUE Quogue Library Art Gallery An exhibition of works by Richard Mizdal, featuring new paintings and photo/computer hybrids. Through Sept. 29. Mondays, noon to 5 p.m.; Tuesdays and Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Quogue Library Art Gallery, 90 Quogue Street. (631) 653-4224;

quoguelibrary.org.

RIVERHEAD Art Sites Gallery “Sara Cedar Miller: Light Drawings,” photographs. Through Sept. 9. Thursdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Art Sites Gallery, 651 West Main Street. (631) 591-2401; artsitesgallery.com.

RIVERHEAD East End Arts Council Gallery “Joy,” a national juried art competition and show. Through Oct. 5. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. East End Arts Council Gallery, 133 East Main Street. (631) 727-0900; eastendarts.org.

RIVERHEAD Hallockville Museum Farm and Folklife Center “Pages From Her Diary: The 1883 Courtship and Marriage of Henrietta Terry,” diary, wedding dress and personal effects illustrating the courtship and marriage customs of the North Fork’s agricultural community. Through Sept. 30. Fridays through Sundays, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. Hallockville Museum Farm and Folklife Center, 6038 Sound Avenue. (631) 298-5292; hallockville.com.

RIVERHEAD Lyceum Gallery, Suffolk County Community College “Hans Van de Bovenkamp: Shaping Space,” sculptures. Through Oct. 20. Mondays through Thursdays, 9 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Fridays, 9 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. Lyceum Gallery, Suffolk County Community College, Orient Building, 121 Speonk-Riverhead Road. (631) 548-2536; sunysuffolk.edu.

RIVERHEAD Suffolk County Historical Society “Currier and Ives: Printmakers to the American People,” 100 hand-colored lithographs from a private Long Island collection. Through Oct. 20. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 12:30 to 4:30 p.m. Suffolk County Historical Society, 300 West Main Street. (631) 727-2881;

suffolkcountyhistoricalsociety.org.

ROSLYN HARBOR Nassau County Museum of Art “Marc Chagall,” paintings and hand-colored etchings of Bible storiesthat he produced in 1957. “Sydney Chastain-Chapman/Julie Tremblay,” representational paintings and life-size sculptures. Both through Nov. 4. $4 to $10. Nassau County Museum of Art, 1 Museum Drive. nassaumuseum.org; (516) 484-9337.

SAG HARBOR Richard Demato Fine Arts Gallery “Metamorphosis of Mood,” paintings by Andrea Kowch. Through Sept. 21. Sundays, Mondays, Thursdays, Fridays, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m., and Saturdays 11 a.m. to 8 p.m. Richard Demato Fine Arts Gallery, 90 Main Street. rjdgallery.com; (631) 725-1161.

SAG HARBOR Romany Kramoris Gallery An exhibition of paintings by Herbert August and Michael Yurick. Through Sept. 20. Romany Kramoris Gallery, 41 Main Street. (631) 725-2499; kramorisgallery.com.SAG HARBOR Tulla Booth Gallery End of Summer Photography Exhibition, group show. Through Oct. 10. Mondays and Fridays through Sundays, 12:30 to 7 p.m. Tulla Booth Gallery, 66 Main Street. tullaboothgallery.com; (631) 725-3100.

SETAUKET Gallery North “Local Color II: Painting,” group show. Through Sept. 21. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Gallery North, 90 North Country Road. (631) 751-2676; gallerynorth.org.

SOUTHAMPTON Southampton Cultural Center Juried art exhibition, group show. Sept. 11 through Oct. 7. Southampton Cultural Center, 25 Pond Lane. (631) 287-4377; southamptonculturalcenter.org.

SOUTHOLD Southold Historical Society “Voices of the North Fork,” recordings of oral histories with photographs and artifacts. Through Oct. 8. $3; children under 12, free. Southold Historical Society, 54325 Main Road. (631) 765-5500; southoldhistoricalsociety.org.

STONY BROOK Long Island Museum of American Art, History and Carriages “Long Island, America’s 1950s Frontier,” exhibition. Through Dec. 31. $4 to $9; members and children under 6, free. Summer hours Thursdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Long Island Museum of American Art, History and Carriages, 1200 Route 25A. longislandmuseum.org; (631) 751-0066.

SYOSSET Syosset Public Library An exhibition of works by Ed Nussbaum, featuring paper sculptures of well-known people. Through Sept. 27. Mondays through Thursdays, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Fridays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Syosset Public Library, 225 South Oyster Bay Road. syossetlibrary.org.

WATER MILL Water Mill Museum “Lens, Chisel, Brush,” a collaboration of photography by Anthony Lombardo, stone sculpture by John Capello and oil painting by Lucille Berrrill Paulsen. Through Sept. 17. “Two Laboratories Converge,” photos from 1942 to 1992 showing the former Western Union laboratory, now the Watermill Center. Through Oct. 14. Thursdays through Mondays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays 1 p.m. to 5 p.m. Water Mill Museum, 41 Old Mill Road. watermillmuseum.org; (631) 726-4625.

WESTHAMPTON BEACH Westhampton Free Library An exhibition of paintings by JoAnn Maroldo and Jo-Ann Corretti. Through Sept. 30. Westhampton Free Library, 7 Library Avenue. (631) 288-3335; westhamptonlibrary.net.
