Calendar

Events on Long Island

Published: August 31, 2012

A guide to cultural and recreational events on Long Island. Items for the calendar should be sent at least three weeks in advance to lical@nytimes.com.

Related

  • Times Topics: Long Island Arts | Long Island Dining

Connect With NYTMetro

Follow us on Twitter and like us on Facebook for news and conversation.

Comedy

BAY SHORE Boulton Center for the Performing Arts “Big Laughs in Bay Shore,” featuring three comedians. Sept. 7 at 8 p.m. $15 and $20. Boulton Center for the Performing Arts, 37 West Main Street. boultoncenter.org; (631) 969-1101.

SHELTER ISLAND Shelter Island Public Library Laughing After Labor Day, featuring “A Comedy Writer Finds God,” a play by Tom Leopold, a writer for “Seinfeld,” and an appearance by the Emmy Award-winner Bill Persky. Sept. 7 at 7 p.m. $10. Shelter Island Public Library, 37 North Ferry Road. readshelterisland.org; (631) 749-0042.

WESTHAMPTON BEACH Westhampton Beach Performing Arts Center John Pinette. Sept. 2 at 8:30 p.m. $65 to $95. Westhampton Beach Performing Arts Center, 76 Main Street. (631) 288-1500; whbpac.org.

Film

GARDEN CITY Adelphi University Performing Arts Center “The Sleeping Beauty,” featuring the Royal Ballet from London. Sept. 8 and 9. $5 and $15. Adelphi University Performing Arts Center, 1 South Avenue. (516) 877-4000; aupac.adelphi.edu.

HUNTINGTON Cinema Arts Center “The Nashville Legends: Johnny Cash and Friends (1950-’90s),” archivist Bill Shelley presents performances by country music artists, including Bill Monroe, Linda Ronstadt and others. Sept. 4 at 7:30 p.m. $10 and $15. Cinema Arts Center, 423 Park Avenue.

cinemaartscentre.org; (800) 838-3006.

PATCHOGUE The Plaza Cinema and Media Arts Center Ballet in Cinema: “Swan Lake,” featuring the Royal Ballet, based in London. Sept. 9 through 11. $10 and $15. The Plaza Cinema and Media Arts Center, 20 Terry Street. plazamac.org. (631) 438-0083.

ROSLYN HARBOR Nassau County Museum of Art “Artists of the 20th Century: Marc Chagall,” Chagall’s roots in Vitebsk, Belarus, as reflected in his art. Through Nov. 4. Tuesdays to Sundays at 11 a.m., noon, 1 p.m. and 3 p.m. Free with museum admission, $4 to $10; members, free. Nassau County Museum of Art, 1 Museum Drive. nassaumuseum.org; (516) 484-9337.

For Children

GARDEN CITY Long Island Children’s Museum Framed: Step Into Art, art recreated as three-dimensional, interactive environments; traveling exhibition. Through Sept. 2. Long Island Children’s Museum, 11 Davis Avenue. (516) 224-5800;

licm.org.

MERRICK Merrick Theater and Center for the Arts “Sleeping Beauty,” children’s performance. Weekends, Sept. 15 through Nov. 3. $9. Merrick Theater and Center for the Arts, 2222 Hewlett Avenue. (516) 868-6400;

merricktheatreandcenterforthearts.com.

OAKDALE CM Performing Arts Center “Alice in Wonderland Jr.,” children’s musical. Through Sept. 2. $10. CM Performing Arts Center, 931 Montauk Highway. (631) 218-2810; cmpac.com.

Music and Dance

AMAGANSETT Stephen Talkhouse The Bogmen, indie rock band. Through Sept. 2. $45. Stephen Talkhouse, 161 Main Street.

stephentalkhouse.com; (631) 267-3117.

BAY SHORE Unitarian Society Julia Joseph with Milton, performing classic soul, jazz, Latin and R&B. Sept. 8 at 8:30 p.m. $10 and $13. Unitarian Society, 28 Brentwood Road. (631) 661-1278; eclecticcafe.org.

DIX HILLS Dix Hills Performing Arts Center Two Tenors and a Lady: Broadway Meets Opera, Christopher Maccio, Elio Scaccio and Simona Rodano. Sept. 8 at 7:30 p.m. $40 to $50. Ella Fitzgerald and Lionel Hampton Tribute, featuring Cat da Silva and Bryan Carrott. Sept. 9 at 3 p.m. $25 and $30. Dix Hills Performing Arts Center, 305 North Service Road. (631) 656-2148; dhpac.org.

EAST HAMPTON Guild Hall “Songbirds: A Tribute to the Ladies,” with Linda Eder performing the music of Lena Horne, Etta James, Barbra Streisand, Judy Garland, Eva Cassidy and others. Sept. 2 at 7:30 p.m. $50 to $100. Guild Hall, 158 Main Street. (631) 324-4050; guildhall.org.

GARDEN CITY Garden Stage Coffeehouse Colors of the Sun: Penny Nichols sings the early songs of Jackson Browne. Sept. 7 at 8:30 p.m. $18 and $20. Garden Stage Coffeehouse, 223 Stewart Avenue. (516) 248-8891; gardenstage.com.

HEMPSTEAD Nassau Coliseum “Dralion: Cirque du Soleil,” Chinese acrobatic arts. Sept. 5 through 9. $55 to $163. Nassau Coliseum, 1255 Hempstead Turnpike. (516) 794-9300; nassaucoliseum.com.

HUNTINGTON Heckscher Museum of Art First Friday at the Heckscher Museum of Art: Mike Ernst Band, contemporary smooth jazz. Sept. 7 at 7 p.m. Free. Heckscher Museum of Art, 2 Prime Avenue. (631) 351-3250; heckscher.org.

QUOGUE Hampton Theater Company ,Quogue Community Hall Jupiter String Quartet, performing a program of works by Beethoven, Webern and Schubert. Sept. 8 at 7:30 p.m. $5 to $100. Hampton Theater Company, Quogue Community Hall, 126 Jessup Avenue.

quoguechambermusic.com; (631) 653-8955.

REMSENBURG Remsenburg Academy “The Hidden City Orchestra” and “99 Embraces,” three concerts and an art exhibition conceived by George Cork Maul and Gina Gilmour. Through Sept. 2. Remsenburg Academy, 130 South Country Road. (631) 734-7035.

ROSLYN HARBOR Nassau County Museum of Art Music at the Museum: Jazz with Bonnie Strickman, Hillie Dolgenas and Scott Lee. Sept. 8 at 3 p.m. $5 to $15. Nassau County Museum of Art, 1 Museum Drive. (516) 484-9337; nassaumuseum.org.

WESTBURY NYCB Theater at Westbury The Marshall Tucker Band, rock. Sept. 2 at 8 p.m. $39.50 and $59.50. J. Geils Band, rock. Sept. 12 at 8 p.m. $39.50 to $59.50. NYCB Theater at Westbury, 960 Brush Hollow Road. livenation.com; (800) 745-3000.

Outdoors

BRIDGEHAMPTON Hampton Classic The 37th annual Hampton Classic Horse Show, an equestrian competition; also pony rides, petting zoo animals and more. Through Sept. 2. $10 per person and $20 per carload; free for children under 6. 240 Snake Hollow Road. hamptonclassic.com; (631) 537-3177.

MONTAUK Montauk Harbor Viking Fleet Whale Watch, a six-hour whale and marine life cruise, with the Coastal Research and Education Society of Long Island. Reservations suggested. Through Sept. 2. $49 and $75; children under 5, free. Montauk Harbor, West Lake Drive. cresli.org; (631) 668-5700.

WEST HEMPSTEAD Hempstead Lake State Park South Shore Audubon Bird Walk. The group will meet in Hempstead Lake State Park, Parking Field No. 3, Exit 18 South, from the Southern State Parkway. Sept. 9 at 9:30 a.m. Free.

ssaudubon.org.nysparks.com/parks. (516) 987-8103.

Spoken Word

MONTAUK Montauk Library “Shakespeare on Aging,” a discussion with Maurice Charney, New York Humanities Council. Sept. 9 at 3:30 p.m. Free. Montauk Library, 871 Montauk Highway. www.suffolk.lib.ny.us/libraries/mntk/; (631) 668-3377.

STONY BROOK Long Island Museum of American Art, History and Carriages Golden Crest Records: the rock historian John Broven will discuss the record label based in Huntington Station and founded in 1956. Free with museum admission. Sept. 9 at 2 p.m. Long Island Museum of American Art, History and Carriages, 1200 Route 25A. longislandmuseum.org; (631) 751-0066.

Theater

BELLPORT Gateway Playhouse “Phantom,” with music and lyrics by Maury Yeston and book by Arthur Kopit. Through Sept. 9. $52 to $58. Gateway Playhouse, 215 South Country Road. (631) 286-1133;

gatewayplayhouse.com.

EAST ISLIP BayWay Arts Center “Deathtrap,” thriller by Ira Levin. Through Sept. 2. BayWay Arts Center, 265 East Main Street. (631) 581-2700; broadhollow.org.

LINDENHURST Studio Theater “Bedtime Stories,” comedy by Norm Foster. Through Sept. 8. $15 to $25. Studio Theater, 141 South Wellwood Avenue. (631) 226-8400; studiotheatreli.com.

MERRICK Merrick Theater and Center for the Arts “Next to Normal,” music by Tom Kitt and book and lyrics by Brian Yorkey. Through Sept. 9. $17 and $20. Merrick Theater and Center for the Arts, 2222 Hewlett Avenue. merricktheatreandcenterforthearts.com; (516) 868-6400.

NORTHPORT John W. Engeman Theater “Sweet Charity,” music by Cy Coleman, lyrics by Dorothy Fields and book by Neil Simon. Sept. 6 through Oct. 28. $65. John W. Engeman Theater, 250 Main Street.

johnwengemantheater.com; (631) 261-2900.

SAG HARBOR Bay Street Theater “Big Maybelle: Soul of the Blues,” musical written and directed by Paul Levine. Through Sept. 2. $66 and $76. Bay Street Theater, Main and Bay Streets. (631) 725-9500; baystreet.org.

Museums and Galleries

BELLPORT Circa Something Fine Art “Once Expelled, Now Propelled, Hometown Belligerence,” paintings by Eddie Rehm. Through Sept. 15. Thursdays through Saturdays, noon to 6 p.m. Circa Something Fine Art, 117A South Country Road. circasomething.com; (631) 803-6706.

BELLPORT Gallery 125 Works by Arthur Pinajian from the 1930s to 1990s. Through Sept. 9. Fridays 3:30 to 9 p.m., Saturdays, 10:30 a.m. to 9 p.m., Sundays, 10:30 a.m. to 1:30 p.m. Gallery 125, 125 South Country Road. (631) 880-2693.

DIX HILLS Jeanie Tengelsen Gallery “Cultural Identity: Through the Lens,” 65 photographic works. Through Sept. 2. Mondays through Fridays, 9 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. Jeanie Tengelsen Gallery, 107 East Deer Park Road. (631) 462-5400; artleagueli.org.

EAST HAMPTON Eric Firestone Gallery “Parts and Service,” automotive artifacts, photographs and mixed-media works. Through Sept. 2. Sundays through Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 8 p.m. Eric Firestone Gallery, 4 Newtown Lane. (631) 604-2386; ericfirestonegallery.com.

EAST HAMPTON Guild Hall “Strong-Cuevas: Premonitions in Retrospect,” 5 pieces from the Frieda and Roy Furman Sculpture Garden and 10 drawings from the Wasserstein Family Gallery. Through Sept. 3. “Eric Fischl: Beach Life,” paintings that span a 30-year career. Through Oct. 14. Guild Hall, 158 Main Street. guildhall.org; (631) 324-4050.

EAST HAMPTON LongHouse Reserve Art Center “Accumulations: Now Exhibition,” curated by Jack Lenor Larsen, including pieces from the collection of Dena Katzenberg and works from other artists. Through Oct. 6. LongHouse Reserve Art Center, 133 Hands Creek Road. longhouse.org; (631) 329-3568.

EAST HAMPTON The Drawing Room Photographs by Mary Ellen Bartley. Through Sept. 3. Costantino Nivola, sculptures in marble, travertine, bronze, concrete and terra cotta. Through Sept. 3. Free. Mondays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m., Sundays 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. The Drawing Room, 66 Newtown Lane. drawingroomgallery.com; (631) 324-5016.

EAST ISLIP Islip Art Museum “The Garbage Barge Revisited: Art From Dross,” group show. Through Sept. 2. Suggested donation, $3. Wednesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Islip Art Museum, 50 Irish Lane. islipartmuseum.org; (631) 224-5402.

GARDEN CITY Firehouse Plaza Art Gallery “Complejo,” featuring the paintings of M. Tony Peralta. Sept. 4 through Nov. 2. Mondays, Wednesdays and Thursdays, 11:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Tuesdays, 11:30 a.m. to 7 p.m.; and Saturdays 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. Firehouse Plaza Art Gallery, 1 Education Drive. (516) 572-0619; firehouse.ncc.edu.

HEMPSTEAD Hofstra University Museum “Opportunity and Impact: Works by Émigré Artists,” at the David Filderman Gallery. Through Sept. 9. “Toward Greater Awareness: Darfur and American Activism,” the work of Mitch Lewis, who explores the crisis of violence in Darfur through his large sculptural works, at the Emily Lowe Gallery, South Campus. Sept. 4 through Dec. 7. Hofstra University Museum, 112 Hofstra University. (516) 463-5672;

hofstra.edu/museum.

HEWLETT Hewlett-Woodmere Public Library “Charlotte Strongwater: The Accidental Artist,” landscape and wildlife paintings in watercolor, pastel and mixed media. Through Sept. 23. Hewlett-Woodmere Library, 1125 Broadway. (516) 374-1967;

nassaulibrary.org/hewlett.

HUNTINGTON Conklin house Gallery “All Things Conklin!” the Huntington Historical Society’s collection of heirlooms, furniture, samplers, coverlets, silver and other household wares donated by the Conklin family. Through Dec. 31. $3 to $5. Thursdays, Fridays and Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Conklin House Gallery, 2 High Street. (631) 427-7045; huntingtonhistoricalsociety.org/conklin.htm.

HUNTINGTON Fotofoto Gallery “Still Life Still,” photography by Susan Kravitz and Susan Dooley using diptychs and/or triptychs. Through Sept. 30. Reception: Sept. 8, 5 to 7 p.m. Fridays, 5 to 8 p.m.; Saturdays, noon to 8 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Fotofoto Gallery, 14 West Carver Street. (631) 549-0448; fotofotogallery.org.

HUNTINGTON Heckscher Museum of Art “Robert S. Neuman’s Ship to Paradise,” large-scale, mixed-media drawings. Through Nov. 25. $4 to $6; children under 10, free. “Absorbed by Color: Art in the 20th Century.” Through Dec. 2. $4 to $6; children under 10, free. Wednesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Heckscher Museum of Art, 2 Prime Avenue. heckscher.org; (631) 351-3250.

HUNTINGTON STATION South Huntington Public Library “4 Printmakers,” an exhibition featuring prints by Raisy Derzie, Stephanie Navon Jacobson, Michael Krasowitz and Angela Sanfilippo at the Alfred Van Loen Gallery. Sept. 8 through Oct. 3. Monday, Tuesday, Thursday and Friday, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Wednesday 10 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Saturday 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sunday 1 to 5 p.m. South Huntington Public Library, 145 Pidgeon Hill Road. shpl.info; (631) 549-4411.

JAMESPORT Jedediah Hawkins Inn Barn Gallery “Hung Out to Dry,” new and recovered oil paintings by Max Moran. Through Sept. 16. Fridays, 5 to 8 p.m. and Saturdays and Sundays, 1 to 7 p.m. Jedediah Hawkins Inn Barn Gallery, 400 South Jamesport Avenue. maxmoran.com; (631) 722-2900.

JAMESPORT Rosalie Dimon Gallery, Jamesport Manor Inn East End Arts Members Exhibtiion, featuring Dan Welden, master printmaker and Elizabeth Malunowicz, classical realist artist. Through Oct. 31. Wednesdays through Mondays, noon to 10 p.m. Rosalie Dimon Gallery, Jamesport Manor Inn, 370 Manor Road.

jamesportmanorinn.com; (631) 722-0500.

MELVILLE Art-Trium “Relationships,” group show. Through Nov. 26. Mondays through Fridays, 7 a.m. to 7 p.m. Art-Trium, 25 Melville Park Road. (631) 271-8423; huntingtonarts.org.

OYSTER BAY Planting Fields Arboretum State Historic Park “Cocktail Culture: The Glamorous Gold Coast Years From Prohibition to 1960,” clothes by designers like Mariano Fortuny and Elsa Schiaparelli, and more. Through Sept. 30. $3.50; free for members. Planting Fields Arboretum State Historic Park, 1395 Planting Fields Road. (516) 922-9200; plantingfields.org.

PORT JEFFERSON Port Jefferson Village Center Anne P. Tuttle: A Life in Art, retrospective. Sept. 6 through Nov. 13. Port Jefferson Village Center, 101-A East Broadway. (631) 802-2160; pjvillagecenter.com.
