THE GOVERNORSHIP; Changing of the Guard: The Whitman Team


Published: January 23, 1994

Christine Todd Whitman will have 20 Cabinet members, one more than Gov. Jim Florio. She is restoring the chairman of the Board of Regulatory Commissioners. Mrs. Whitman has not announced her appointments for Corrections Commissioner, Adjutant General, Health Commissioner, Insurance Commissioner, Labor Commissioner and Public Advocate. Cabinet members are paid $100,225 a year. ELIZABETH RANDALL, Banking Commissioner AGE: 40. HOMETOWN: Westwood. N.J. BACKGROUND: State assemblywoman, 1985-1991. Partner, Randall, Randall & Stevens of Westwood, March 1984-February 1991. Has served in a variety of legal jobs in state and local government including assistant counsel to former Gov. Thomas H. Kean, Assistant Essex County prosecutor, municipal prosecutor in Park Ridge, and borough attorney in Hillsdale. She has been Bergen County counsel since 1991. FAMILY: Single.

ROBERT C. SHINN JR., Commissioner of Environmental Protection and Energy. AGE: 56. HOMETOWN: Hainesport, N.J. BACKGROUND: Founder of Material Handling Systems, a heavy-equipment sales firm. Elected to the Burlington County Board of Chosen Freeholders in 1977. Since 1985, he was served in the New Jersey Assembly, where he has been chairman of Assembly committees on solid waste and land use planning.

FAMILY: Divorced, two daughters. GUALBERTO MEDINA, Commissioner of Commerce and Economic Development AGE: 45. HOMETOWN: Cherry Hill, N.J. BACKGROUND: A Temple University law graduate, served in 1979-80 as an attorney for the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development in Philadelphia. Private law practice since then and has worked for accounting firms. Most recently, director of management advisory services for John Milligan & Associates, a regional accounting firm in Philadelphia. FAMILY: Married, two sons and a daughter. INTERESTS: Classical literature, classical music, running. JUDITH SHAW, Chief of Staff AGE: 50. HOMETOWN: Princeton, N.J. BACKGROUND: Deputy director for marketing of the Lottery Commission for three years, then joined the Department of Transportation and became its chief of staff in 1987. Left in 1990 and joined Public Policy Advisers, a lobbying firm in Trenton. Became a partner and vice president in 1991. FAMILY: Divorced, two stepdaughters. INTERESTS: Golf, photography. BRIAN W. CLYMER, Treasurer AGE: 46. HOMETOWN: Swarthmore, Pa. BACKGROUND: Director of the Federal Transit Administration during the Bush Administration. Most recently president and chief executive of Railway Systems Design Inc., a transportation engineering concern in Harrisburg, Pa. FAMILY: Married, a son and daughter. INTERESTS: Fishing, golf. LONNA R. HOOKS, Secretary of Stat AGE: 35. HOMETOWN: Montclair, N.J. BACKGROUND: A Howard University law graduate, clerked in the District of Columbia Court of Appeals. Left the private practice of law to become chief of staff to the State Board of Public Utilities while Mrs. Whitman was chairman. Most recently, a senior attorney for Schering-Plough in Kenilworth, N.J. FAMILY: Single. INTERESTS: Jazz, cooking, art. EDWARD D. GOLDBERG, Chancellor of Higher Education AGE: 56. HOMETOWN: Union, N.J. BACKGROUND: Joined the New Jersey Department of Higher Education in 1975 as director for community colleges. Became assistant chancellor for academic affairs in 1981 and assistant chancellor for institutional affairs in 1989. Has served as Chancellor of Higher Education since 1990. FAMILY: Married, two sons INTERESTS: Opera, theater. LEO F. KLAGHOLZ, Commissioner of Education AGE: 51. HOMETOWN: Ewing Township, N.J. BACKGROUND: Originally an elementary school teacher, taught elementary education at Trenton State College and served as assistant dean there. Joined the Department of Higher Education as assistant director for academic affairs, Office for State Colleges, in 1977. Became director of teacher preparation in 1980 and has served as assistant commissioner for the Division of Academic Programs and Standards since 1983. FAMILY: Married, no children. INTERESTS: Sailing. DEBORAH T. PORITZ, Attorney General AGE: 57. HOMETOWN: Princeton, N.J. BACKGROUND: Originally an English Teacher at Ursinus College, earned a law degree from the University of Pennsylvania. Became a Deputy Attorney General in 1977 and assistant cheif for environmental protection in the Department of Law and Public Safety in 1981. Became Deputy Attorney General in charge of appeals and later chief of the Banking, Insurance and Public Securities Section. Served Gov. Thomas H. Kean as chief counsel in 1989-90; most recently practiced law in Princeton. FAMILY: Married, two sons. FRANK J. WILSON, Commissioner of Transportation AGE: 46. HOMETOWN: Danville, Calif. BACKGROUND: Worked as a staff engineer for the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey and then as a senior transportation planner for the City of Philadelphia. Served the Southeastern Pennsylvania Transportation Authority in its capital program and, after a stint at New Jersey Transit, as its assistant general manager of operations. Was deputy executive director for operations for the Chicago Transit Authority and, most recently, general manager of the San Francisco Bay Area Rapid Transit District. FAMILY: Married, two daughters. LINDA M. ANSELMINI, Commissioner of Personnel AGE: 47. HOMETOWN: Pennington, N.J. BACKGROUND: Joined the Department of Environmental Protection in 1970 and in 1981 became its director of the division of personnel and data processing services. Was an assistant commissioner in the Department of Insurance and later in the Department of Transportation. Returned to Environmental Protection as its director of employee management and development and moved to the Division of Youth and Family Services as assistant director for fiscal and human resouces operations. Most recently, vice president for customer and information services at American College of Physicians in Philadelphia. FAMILY: Single. INTERESTS: Gardening, reading, movies, music. WILLIAM WALDMAN, Commissioner of Human Services AGE: 50. HOMETOWN: Piscataway, N.J. BACKGROUND: For Two decades worked in welfare for Essex County and Middlesex Counties. Joined the New Jersey Department of Human Services in 1987 as assistant commissioner for community relations. Became director of its Division of Youth and Family Services that same year, deputy commissioner for institutions and support in 1990, and commissioner of the department in 1992. FAMILY: Married, two sons, one daughter. INTERESTS: Hiking, reading. ARTHUR R. BROWN JR., Secretary of Agriculture AGE: 58. HOMETOWN: Egg Harbor, N.J. BACKGROUND: Worked for Seabrook Farms in Upper Deerfield for a decade, later served as an agricultural agent in Atlantic County for 11 years. Became New Jersey Secretary of Agriculture in 1982 and has since been reappointed. FAMILY: Married, four sons and two daughters. INTERESTS: Hunting, fishing, birdwatching. HERBERT H. TATE JR., Chief, Board of Regulatory Commissioners AGE: 40. HOMETOWN: West Orange, N.J. BACKGROUND: A Rutgers University law graduate, became an assistant Essex County Prosecutor in 1978. Practiced law in Roseland, N.J., then became Essex County Prosecutor in 1986. In 1991 joined the Environmental Protection Agency as assistant administrator for enforcement. Most recently, an environmental consultant. FAMILY: Single INTERESTS: Golf, tennis, theater, jazz, dance. HARRIET E. DERMAN, Commissioner of Community Affairs AGE: 50. HOMETOWN: Metuchen, N.J. BACKGROUND: A Seton Hall law graduate and senior partner in Weiner, Hendler & Derman of New Brunswick. Served as president of the Middlesex County Bar Association in 1992 and as first vice chairwoman of the taxation section of the New Jersey Bar Association that same year. A director of New Jersey Women Lawyers. FAMILY: Married, two sons and one daughter. INTERESTS: Family, reading, skiing. JANE M. KENNY, Chief of Policy and Planning AGE: 42. HOMETOWN: Highland Park, N.J. BACKGROUND: A lecturer in literature at Rutgers, became Governor Kean's director of constituent relations in 1983 and three years later his Cabinet Secretary. Most recently, vice president for corporate and community affairs, Beneficial Management Corporation and chairman of the New Jersey Committee on the Humanities. FAMILY: Married, one son, two daughters. INTERESTS: Reading, movies, swimming, sailing, skiing, ice skating PETER G. VERNIERO, Chief Counsel AGE: 34. HOMETWON: Annandale, N.J. BACKGROUND: A Duke University law graduate, practiced law in Morristown from 1985 to 1987. Became executive director of the New Jersey Republican State Committee in 1989 and returned to private law practice in 1990. FAMILY: Married, one daughter.
