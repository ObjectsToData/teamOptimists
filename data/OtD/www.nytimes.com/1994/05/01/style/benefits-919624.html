BENEFITS


Published: May 1, 1994

Spielberg Honored TONIGHT -- The director Steven Spielberg will receive a medal of honor for his contributions "to the arts, Jewish memory and continuity" from Beth Hatesfutsoth, the Nathum Goldmann Museum of the Jewish Diaspora in Tel Aviv. The black-tie dinner will start with cocktails at 6 P.M. at the Plaza. Tickets, $1,000, from (212) 339-6022. Scholarship Fund Dinner MONDAY -- The Inner-City Scholarship Fund, which raises money to help support students in schools of the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of New York, will hold a black-tie dinner at the United Nations Delegates' Dining Room. Cocktails at 6:30 P.M. will be followed by dinner and a viewing of the art at the U.N. Tickets, $175, from (212) 753-8583.

Benefit for Arts Colony MONDAY -- The Millay Colony for the Arts, a retreat for writers, composers and artists in Austerlitz, N.Y., will hold a benefit that includes the 8 P.M. performance of "Passion" at the Plymouth Theater and dinner afterward at B. Smith's restaurant, Eighth Avenue at 47th Street. Tickets, $250, or $125 for the performance alone, from (212) 496-9127. Tent Party MONDAY -- The New York Botanical Garden will hold its annual Founders Award Dinner under a tent on the grounds of the arboretum in the Bronx. Senator Daniel Patrick Moynihan, Democrat of New York, and Harry P. Kamen, chairman and chief executive of the Metropolitan Life Insurance Company, will be honored. Black tie. Cocktails at 6:30 P.M. will precede dinner and dancing. Tickets, $500, from (212) 997-0100. Humanitarian Award MONDAY -- The Humanitarian Award Concert for the T. J. Martell Foundation for Leukemia, Cancer and AIDS Research will be at Avery Fisher Hall. Eric Clapton and Rosie O'Donnell will perform. Tom Freston, the chairman and chief executive of MTV, will be honored. Dinner is at 6 P.M. in a tent at Lincoln Center, followed by the concert at 8:15, then dessert. Tickets, $750, from (212) 245-1818.

Noche de Gala MONDAY -- The Ballet Hispanico will hold its 16th annual Noche de Gala benefit at the Plaza. Cocktails at 7 o'clock will be followed by dinner and dancing and a performance by the company and by students from its school. Tickets, $300, from (212) 362-6710. Orchestral Benefit TUESDAY -- The Orchestra of St. Luke's will hold its annual benefit at the Hudson Theater. Cocktails at 7:30 P.M. will be followed by dinner, a performance by the orchestra and dancing. Tickets, $300, from (212) 840-7470. Aiding Freedom Institute TUESDAY -- Freedom Institute, a resource center for the treatment of drug and alcohol dependency, will hold a theater benefit at "Beauty and the Beast" at the Palace Theater. Cocktails and dinner starting at 6 P.M. at Barbetta, 321 West 46th Street, will precede the 8 o'clock show. Tickets, $125 and up for the performance and $55 for dinner, from (212) 874-5457. Helping the Blind TUESDAY -- The Associates' Division of the Jewish Guild for the Blind will sponsor a Rites of Spring Dance Party at the Supper Club, 240 West 47th Street, from 7 P.M. to midnight. Cash bar. Tickets, $35, at the door. Information: (212) 769-6239. Show for Very Special Arts TUESDAY -- A cocktail party and preview of an exhibition of art by Toller Cranston, the Canadian figure skater and Olympic bronze medalist, will be held from 6:30 to 9 P.M. at the Dyansen Gallery at Trump Tower, 725 Fifth Avenue at 57th Street. The event will benefit the Very Special Arts Foundation, founded by Jean Kennedy Smith to provide art and education opportunities for the disabled. There is no admission charge, but the foundation will benefit from the sale of the art and of raffle tickets. Information: (212) 754-3040.

Show for Very Special Arts Fund for 'Healthy Kids' TUESDAY -- The United Hospital Fund's New Leadership Group will hold a gallery reception from 6 to 8 P.M. at the Drawing Center, 35 Wooster Street near Grand Street. It will raise money for the fund's "Healthy Kids" program of services to children in the city's low-income neighborhoods. Tickets, $50, from (212) 645-2500. Trisha Brown Opening TUESDAY -- The Trisha Brown Dance Company will hold a benefit at its opening performance at the Joyce Theater, Eighth Avenue at 19th Street. A buffet dinner and dancing will follow the 7:30 P.M. performance at the loft of Philip Taaffe, an artist, at 124 West 30th Street. Transportation provided to the dinner. Tickets, $150, from (212) 334-9374. Dinners Around Town TUESDAY -- The Asia Society and its many cultural programs will benefit from 32 dinners being held in private homes in Manhattan and Brooklyn Heights and in some unusual spots, including the kitchen of the Waldorf-Astoria. Most start around 7:30 P.M. Tickets, $250, from (212) 288-6400, extension 235. Dance Company Benefit TUESDAY -- The modern dance company Jennifer Muller/The Works will hold its annual benefit with a 7:30 P.M. performance at the Sylvia and Danny Kaye Playhouse at Hunter College, 68th Street between Park and Lexington Avenues. A dinner-dance will follow at the Plaza. Transportation to the Plaza will be provided. Maureen McGovern will sing. Black tie. Tickets, $250, from (212) 691-3803. For Hunter College Art TUESDAY -- A 6:30 P.M. cocktail buffet and an auction of contemporary art at the faculty dining room at the Hunter College West Building, 68th Street and Lexington Avenue, will benefit the Hunter College Art Galleries. Tickets, $75, from (212) 772-4085. Human Rights Award WEDNESDAY -- Teddy Kollek, who served six terms as Mayor of Jerusalem, will receive an award from the International League for Human Rights at its benefit dinner at the United Nations Delegates' Dining Room. There will be cocktails at 6:30 P.M., then dinner and the presentation. Tickets, $500, from (212) 684-1221. 'Lady in the Dark' Reprise WEDNESDAY -- The City Center will mark its 51st anniversary and the inauguration of its series "Encores! Great American Musicals in Concert," with a performance of the 1941 show "Lady in the Dark," with lyrics by Ira Gershwin, music by Kurt Weill and book by Moss Hart. A black-tie dinner-dance follows at the Waldorf-Astoria's Starlight Roof. Tickets, $500, from (212) 921-9070. Evening for Riis Settlement WEDNESDAY -- The Jacob A. Riis Neighborhood Settlement, which provides health, recreation and education programs in Long Island City, Queens, will hold a benefit that includes the 8 P.M. performance of "An Inspector Calls" at the Royale Theater. The event will begin with a reception at 5:30 at the architectural firm of Swanke Hayden Connell, 4 Columbus Circle (at 58th Street). Tickets, $135, from (212) 874-5457. Food and Wine Cornucopia THURSDAY -- The fare of 19 restaurants and 16 wineries, along with games of chance, will be offered at a benefit for the Children's Blood Foundation. The benefit at the Racquet and Tennis Club, 370 Park Avenue near 53d Street, will be from 7 P.M. to midnight. Tickets, $125, from (212) 297-4336. For Shakespeare THURSDAY -- The New York Shakespeare Festival's Spring Benefit, to help maintain productions in Central Park, will be held at the Bethesda Fountain, in the park off 72d Street. Cocktails at 7 P.M. will be followed by dinner and entertainment. Tickets, $500, from (212) 598-7162. International Center Dinner THURSDAY -- The International Center, a volunteer organization that assists immigrants, will honor some who have become successes in the United States. They include Rocky Aoki, chairman of Benihana, who was born in Japan; John Chalsty, president and chief executive of Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities, from South Africa, and Michel P. Somnolet, executive vice president and chief operating officer of Cosmair, from France. A black-tie dinner will be held at the Essex House, starting with cocktails at 6:30 P.M. Tickets, $350, from (212) 355-6363. Italian Festa THURSDAY -- I Giullari di Piazza, an Italian folk music and theater company, will hold a Festa di Primavera at the Cathedral of St. John the Divine, Amsterdam Avenue at 111th Street, from 6:30 to 9 P.M. A buffet will follow. Tickets, $75, from (212) 431-7179. Rome Academy's 100 Years THURSDAY -- The American Academy in Rome, a center for independent study in the fine arts and the humanities, will celebrate its centennial with a black-tie dinner at the Puck Building, Lafayette and Houston streets. Cocktails at 7:30 P.M. Tickets, $500, from (212) 751-7200. Dolls for Sale THURSDAY -- Scores of dolls made by 30 of the nation's premier doll makers will be sold at a cocktail party from 6 to 9 P.M. at the Stricoff Fine Art gallery, 118 Greene Street near Prince Street. The sale will benefit God's Love We Deliver. Admission is free, but reservations are required, from (212) 219-3977. Hispanic AIDS Benefit FRIDAY -- Since its founding in 1985, the Hispanic AIDS Forum has become a major private agency offering programs in prevention of the disease, counseling and other support services. The organization will benefit from a cocktail party and auction from 6:30 until 9 P.M. and a dance that follows until 2 A.M. at the Puck Building, Lafayette at Houston streets. Tickets for the reception, auction and dance, $60, or for the dance alone, $40, from (212) 966-6563.
