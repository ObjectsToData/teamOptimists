AROUND TOWN

Museums and Sites

ST. PATRICK’S DAY FESTIVITIES Sometime during the fifth century, the teenager who would become St. Patrick was captured in his native Britain and forced into slavery in Ireland. He escaped, only to return as a missionary and convert virtually the entire country to Christianity. March 17 — the day he is said to have died — was observed first with a feast, then with parades and now with a series of pub crawls featuring drinks tinted a radioactive shade of green.

For those in New York looking to celebrate the patron saint of Ireland but who prefer a more entertaining activity than, say, monitoring the shade of your tongue as it progresses from lime to emerald with each successive beer, there are plenty of options. The first, on Sunday, is an open day at the Irish Arts Center. For the seventh year, the center has compiled a free sampler of Irish culture.

The day “allows us to showcase what Irish cultural activities we offer, with the idea of capturing the interest of New Yorkers from all cultural backgrounds,” said Rachael Gilkey, the office manager for the center. The event includes performances by students at the Niall O’Leary School of Irish Dance and the singer Ashley Davis, whose serenely clear voice recalls that of Enya. There is also an Irish Gaelic class, followed by a conversation hour to test the phrases you’ve learned. Face painters will be on the premises, but never fear. The daylong event is for people of all ages.

Of course, no St. Patrick’s Day would be complete without the requisite parade. Brooklyn holds one on Sunday at 1 p.m., proceeding through what’s called South Slope to Union Street and back. But that is a mere warm-up for the giant of all parades, the New York City St. Patrick’s Day Parade, above, on Monday at 11 a.m. An estimated 100,000 participants will accompany the grand marshal, the sportscaster Tommy Smyth, up Fifth Avenue.

After all that marching, if you should happen upon a pub whose napkins are decorated with shamrocks, well then, slainte. (Open day, Sunday from noon to 6:15 p.m., Irish Arts Center, 553 West 51st Street, Manhattan, 212-757-3318, irishartscenter.org; free. Brooklyn parade: Sunday at 1 p.m., Prospect Park West and 15th Street, saintpatricksdayparade.com; free. Manhattan parade: Monday at 11 a.m., Fifth Avenue at 44th Street, nyc-st-patrick-day-parade.org; free.) MONICA DRAKE
