Calendar

Events in New Jersey

Published: November 19, 2010
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Single Page
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on around the state. Items may be submitted by mail to New Jersey Calendar, Metropolitan, The New York Times, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405; or by e-mail to njtowns@nytimes.com.

Comedy

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts Manhattan Comedy Night, stand-up comedy showcase. Friday at 8 p.m. $25. Howie Mandel. Dec. 4 at 8 p.m. $62 to $97. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater Tracy Morgan. Nov. 27 at 8 p.m. $29.50 to $69.50. “In God We Rust,” with Lewis Black. Nov. 30 through Dec. 2. $25 to $75. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

For Children

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center Kidz Cabaret presents Mama Doni, children’s performer. Nov. 28 at 1 and 4 p.m. $17. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030;

bergenpac.org.

LEONIA Civil War Drill Hall Theater The Players’ Guild of Leonia presents “The Tall Tales of Paul Bunyan,” an original work by Lois Raff-Corwin, Teaneck puppeteer. Dec. 4 at 11 a.m. and 2 p.m. $10. Civil War Drill Hall Theater, 130 Grand Avenue. (201) 947-9606; leoniaplayers.org.

NEWARK The Newark Museum “Thanksgiving Family Festival: Design It!” Explore the building blocks of design, find out how things are made and invent something new. Friday, 12:30 to 4:30 p.m. $6 and $10. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. (973) 596-6550; newarkmuseum.org.

Music and Dance

BRANCHBURG Nash Theater, Raritan Valley Community College Faculty-Student Dance Concert. More than 100 students perform original dances choreographed by RVCC faculty and students. Dec. 1 and Dec. 2. $5 to $12. Nash Theater, Raritan Valley Community College, 118 Lamington Road. (908) 725-3420; rvccarts.org.

EAST BRUNSWICK Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center Classic standards and oldies with Patsy Palma and Friends. Friday at 8 p.m. $15. Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center, 715 Cranbury Road.

playhouse22.org; (732) 254-3939.

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center Boyz II Men, R&B. Nov. 21 at 8 p.m. $29 to $79. Paul Anka, composer. Wednesday at 8 p.m. $39 to $125. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

MAHWAH Berrie Center for Performing and Visual Arts The Shanghai Quartet: The Mendelssohn Project, performing works by Mendelssohn and others. Dec. 4 at 8 p.m. $15 to $30. The Ramapo Chorale. Dec. 5 at 3 p.m. Free. Berrie Center for Performing and Visual Arts, 505 Ramapo Valley Road. ramapo.edu/berriecenter/index.html; (201) 684-7844.

MONTCLAIR Wellmont Theater Ray Davies, performing the Kinks Choral Collection featuring the Dessoff Chamber Choir. Friday at 8 p.m. $40 to $80. Dan Zanes and Friends: Father Goose, family-friendly musician. Nov. 27 at 3 p.m. $25. Tony Bennett. Dec. 3 and Dec. 4. $75 to $125. Wellmont Theater, 5 Seymour Street. (973) 783-9500;

wellmonttheatre.com.

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts Bayanihan: The Philippine National Folk Dance Company. Nov. 21 at 3 p.m. $27 to $42. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater Will Downing and Najee, soul. Nov. 27 at 8 p.m. $48 to $72. “ABBA Mania,” tribute performance. Dec. 2 at 8 p.m. $22 to $57. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater Tony Bennett. Tuesday at 8 p.m. $55 to $149.50. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street.

countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

RIDGEWOOD Benjamin Franklin Middle School Tessera String Quartet, performing music by Beethoven, Dvorak and Ravel. Nov. 30 at 8 p.m. $25. Benjamin Franklin Middle School, 335 Van Dien Avenue.

artcenterchambermusic.org; (201) 676-2153.

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum “Classical Sundays at the Puffin,” with the Israeli Chamber Project playing classical and contemporary chamber music. Nov. 28 at 4 p.m. $10. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. puffinculturalforum.org; (201) 836-3499.

Seasonal

BRANCHBURG The Theatre, Raritan Valley Community College “A Christmas Carol,” presented by Nebraska Theater Caravan. Dec. 3 at 7 p.m. $40 and $45. The Theater, Raritan Valley Community College, Route 28 and Lamington Road in North Branch. (908) 725-3420; rvccarts.org.

BRANCHBURG Welpe Theater, Raritan Valley Community College The Raritan Valley Community College Chorale performs its annual holiday concert. Dec. 4 at 8 p.m. $5 and $10. Welpe Theater, Raritan Valley Community College, 118 Lamington Road. (908) 725-3420; rvccarts.org.

CAPE MAY East Lynne Theater Company “The Life and Adventures of Santa Claus,” adapted from L. Frank Baum’s 1902 children’s book, presented in storytelling fashion by Gayle Stahlhuth. Friday through Dec. 11. $13 and $23. East Lynne Theater Company, 500 Hughes Street.

eastlynnetheater.org; (866) 280-9211.

CAPE MAY Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities “An Old-Fashioned Christmas Exhibit,” an exhibition of holiday traditions through the years with working LGB trains, trees, toys and more. Through Jan. 2. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. (609) 884-5404; capemaymac.org.

CLINTON Red Mill Museum Village Second Annual Festival of Trees, featuring trees and wreaths decorated by local individuals, organizations and businesses. Friday through Dec. 5. Red Mill Museum Village, 56 Main Street. (908) 735-4101, ext. 107; theredmill.org.

ELMER Appel Farm Arts and Music Center “Holidaze in Hicksville!,” with Dan Hicks and the Hot Licks. Nov. 27 at 8 p.m. $28.50. Appel Farm Arts and Music Center, 457 Shirley Road. (856) 358-2472; appelfarm.org.

HAMILTON Grounds for Sculpture “Lights On!” — an annual holiday celebration with activities and workshops. Nov. 27 at 1 p.m. Free with park admission; materials fee for workshops. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road.

groundsforsculpture.org; (609) 586-0616.

MADISON Museum of Early Trades and Crafts “Crafting Chocolate: A Delectable Trade Program,” tour the museum, meet local chocolatiers, learn about chocolate, its history and the chocolate-making process. Dec. 4, 1 to 4 p.m. $5 and $10. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. (973) 377-2982; rosenet.org/metc.

MANASQUAN Algonquin Arts Center “The Nutcracker,” presented by the New York Theater Ballet. Friday through Nov. 27. $30 to $45. Algonquin Arts Center, 173 Main Street. algonquinarts.com; (732) 528-9211.

MIDDLETOWN Middletown Township Public Library The Calvin and Chapel Handbell Ringers, from the First Presbyterian Church in Red Bank, perform a holiday concert. Dec. 5 at 2 p.m. Free. Middletown Township Public Library, 55 New Monmouth Road. (732) 671-3700; mtpl.org.

MORRISTOWN Acorn Hall “Victorian Christmas at Acorn Hall,” featuring decorated period rooms, gift shop, special events, including “Festive Friday” evenings, and costumed guides. Dec. 3 through Jan. 16. $3 to $6. Acorn Hall, 68 Morris Avenue. (973) 267-3465. www.acornhall.org.

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts “Mooseltoe,” annual children’s holiday performance. Nov. 28 at 1:30 and 4 p.m. $12. Jim Brickman’s Christmas Tour 2010, pianist. Dec. 3 at 8 p.m. $42 to $72. Vienna Boys Choir, performing holiday, sacred and secular works. Dec. 5 at 3 p.m. $32 to $57. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NETCONG Palace Theatre “The Life and Adventures of Santa Claus,” by Stephen Fredericks, music and lyrics by Derek Dibbern and David Spellman, adapted from the book by L. Frank Baum. Through Dec. 19. $14 and $18. Palace Theater, Route 183. (973) 347-4946.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “Cirque Dreams Holidaze,” featuring characters performing feats that celebrate the holidays. Dec. 3 through Dec. 5. $32 to $57. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. statetheatrenj.org; (732) 246-7469.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center “The Nutcracker,” featuring the American Repertory Ballet. Friday through Nov. 28. $38 and $48. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. mccarter.org; (609) 258-2787.

PRINCETON Morven Museum and Garden “Festival of Trees,” featuring holiday trees decorated by area businesses and organizations. Dec. 1 through Jan. 9. $5 and $6. Morven Museum and Garden, 55 Stockton Street. historicmorven.org; (609) 924-8144.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater A Holiday Doo Wop, featuring Jay Siegel and the Tokens, Larry Chance and the Earls, Frankie Ford and more. Dec. 4 at 7:30 p.m. $35 to $45. “A Christmas Carol,” musical based on the book by Charles Dickens. Dec. 5 at 3 p.m. $25 to $45. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

RIDGEWOOD Benjamin Franklin Middle School Auditorium BF Players presents “A Christmas Carol.” Dec. 2 and Dec. 3. $5. Benjamin Franklin Middle School Auditorium, 335 North Van Dien Avenue. (201) 670-2780;

ridgewood.bfm.schoolfusion.us.

SOUTH ORANGE South Orange Performing Arts Center “The Nutcracker,” featuring the New York Theater Ballet. Dec. 3 and 4. $32 and $42. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. sopacnow.org; (973) 313-2787.

TRENTON Passage Theater “Santa Claus Is Coming Out,” a comedy by Jeffrey Solomon. Nov. 27 and 28. $20. Passage Theater, 205 East Front Street. (609) 392-0766; passagetheatre.org.

Spoken Word

ENGLEWOOD Englewood Public Library “Janet and Friends,” a poetry series led by Janet Ullrich, a poet from Waldwick New Jersey, presents a pre-Thanksgiving poetry program, titled “A Harvest Festival of Verse.” Tuesday at 8 p.m. Free. Englewood Public Library, 31 Engle Street. englewoodlibrary.org; (201).568-2215. -HIGHLAND PARK Highland Park Public Library Highland Park Public Library Poetry Night Series, featuring Paul Muldoon, Pulitzer-prize winning poet. Open mic and reception following reading. Tuesday at 7:30 p.m. Free. Highland Park Public Library, 31 North Fifth Avenue. (732) 572-2750; hpplnj.org.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center “Buddy Valastro: The Cake Boss.” The celebrity baker Buddy Valastro of the TLC reality show “Cake Boss” presents an interactive evening of cakes, kitchen tales and culinary fun. Tuesday at 7:30 p.m. $20 to $45. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. njpac.org; (888) 466-5722.

Theater

CAPE MAY Cape May Stage, The Robert Shackleton Playhouse “The Little Prince,” by Rick Cummins and John Scoullar. Friday through Dec. 31. $12.50 to $50. Cape May Stage, the Robert Shackleton Playhouse, Bank and Lafayette Streets. capemaystage.com; (609) 884-1341.

DEAL PARK Axelrod Performing Arts Center “Forever Plaid,” musical comedy. Through Nov. 21. $15 to $22. Axelrod Performing Arts Center, 100 Grant Avenue. (732) 531-9100 ext. 142; axelrodartscenter.com.

EAST BRUNSWICK Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center “The Crucible,” drama by Arthur Miller. Through Nov. 21. $18 and $20. Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center, 715 Cranbury Road. (732) 254-3939; playhouse22.org.
