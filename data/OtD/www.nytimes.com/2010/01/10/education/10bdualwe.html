Iona College Expands Link to High Schools

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
By WINNIE HU
Published: January 8, 2010

Iona College, a private Catholic institution in New Rochelle, has offered a dual-enrollment program called Link at Westchester and New York City high schools since the 1980s. But in the last two years, the program has expanded aggressively in Westchester, adding seven high schools for a total of 19 in the county.

Brian J. Nickerson, dean of Iona’s School of Arts and Science, said he receives more than a dozen inquiries a year from high schools that want to introduce or expand college courses. He said many of these schools were responding to parental pressure to give their children a head start on college work to ensure a smoother transition when they get to campus.

Iona offers nearly two dozen dual-enrollment courses in math, science, humanities and foreign languages. It generally limits the courses to high school seniors who maintain a grade-point average of 85 or higher, though sometimes it accepts juniors who have completed advanced work. Students pay about $75 a credit, compared with $850 a credit for an Iona college student.

At Mamaroneck High School, one of the most popular senior electives is Iona’s freshman writing course, which was added in 2007 to supplement the traditional English literature classes. About 100 students are enrolled this year.

“This class really teaches them to write about everything, not just books.” said James Short, the teacher. “They’re finally let loose to write about the things they enjoy. They can write about the Yankees, if they want. The question is how well they write, not whether they know how Hamlet develops.” WINNIE HU
