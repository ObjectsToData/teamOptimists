Calendar New Jersey

Events in New Jersey

Published: August 13, 2010
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on around the state this week. Items may be submitted by mail to On the Towns, Sunday New Jersey Section, The New York Times, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405; or by e-mail to njtowns @nytimes.com.

Comedy

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center Dennis Miller. Thursday at 8 p.m. $29 to $89. Bob Newhart. Aug. 26 at 8 p.m. $29 to $85. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

MANASQUAN Algonquin Arts Center Caroline Rhea. Aug. 28 at 8 p.m. $25 and $30. Algonquin Arts Center, 173 Main Street. (732) 528-9211; algonquinarts.com.

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts Dennis Miller. Friday at 8 p.m. $47 to $77. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater Dennis Miller. Aug. 21 at 8 p.m. $38 to $98. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9000; countbasietheatre.org.

 

For Children

BRANCHBURG Raritan Valley Community College “Laser Kids 2.” Listen to songs like “Twist and Shout” by the Beatles and “Fireflies” by Owl City while lasers draw pictures on the dome overhead. Through Aug. 26. Ages 6 to 12. $6. Raritan Valley Community College, Route 28 and Lamington Road in North Branch. raritanval.edu/planetarium; (908) 231-8805.

CAPE MAY Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities “Teddy Bear Tea Party.” Bring your own bear or doll and participate in bear-themed activities. Aug. 22 at noon. $10 and $18. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. (609) 884-5404; capemaymac.org.

EAST RUTHERFORD Izod Center “The Wiggles Wiggly Circus Live,” featuring acrobats, clowns, gymnasts, dancers, trapeze artists and more. Tuesday at 2:30 and 6:30 p.m. $18.50 to $78.50. Izod Center, 50 Route 120. (201) 935-3900; izodcenter.com.

FREEHOLD Center Playhouse “Charlotte’s Web,” by Joseph Robinette, based on the book by E. B. White. Weekends through Aug. 29. $12 and $16. Center Playhouse, 35 South Street. centerplayers.org; (732) 462-9093.

MADISON Museum of Early Trades and Crafts Summer Camp for a Day, an eight-session camp program. Through Aug. 26. Members, $25 for any single session and $65 for any three sessions. Nonmembers, $30 for a single session and $80 for three sessions. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. (973) 377-2982; rosenet.org/metc.

WEST ORANGE Turtle Back Zoo “Family Nights,” including reduced admission rates, extended zoo hours, musical entertainment and dinner specials. Every Wednesday in August. $5. Turtle Back Zoo, 560 Northfield Avenue.

(973) 731-5800; turtlebackzoo.org.

Music and Dance

CAPE MAY Cape May Stage, The Robert Shackleton Playhouse “An Evening with Karen Ziemba,” Tony Award-winning actor, singer, dancer. Monday at 8 p.m. $50. Cape May Stage, the Robert Shackleton Playhouse, Bank and Lafayette Streets. capemaystage.com; (609) 884-5404.

EAST RUTHERFORD Izod Center Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers, with My Morning Jacket. Aug. 24 at 7:30 p.m. $53 to $125. Izod Center, 50 Route 120. (201) 935-3900; izodcenter.com.

HAMILTON Grounds for Sculpture “Music and Motion: MM2 Modern Dance,” featuring a performance of their new work, “Emergence.” Aug. 15, 1 to 4 p.m. $8 to $12. Larry White and the Majestic Roots Band. Friday at 7:30 p.m. $10. New York Songwriters Circle Concert, featuring songwriters Tina Shafer, Mieka Pauley, Reed Waddle, Shaun Ruymen, Charlotte Sometimes and Toby Lightman. Friday at 7 p.m. $20 and $25. Tigertown Dixieland Band, jazz. Aug. 27 at 7:30 p.m. $10. Hours: Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. (609) 586-0616; groundsforsculpture.org.

LAKE HOPATCONG Camp Jefferson Amphitheatre Johnny Cash Tribute Concert, with Michael Patrick. Aug. 21 at 7:30 p.m. $10 and $20. Camp Jefferson Amphitheater, 81 Weldon Road. (973) 663-5590;

jeffersonhighlights.sitesvp.com.

MONTCLAIR Wellmont Theater Counting Crows Traveling Circus and Medicine Show, featuring Augustana and other guests. Tuesday and Wednesday at 8 p.m. $64. Lyle Lovett and his Large Band. Aug. 15 at 7 p.m. $40 to $75. Tears for Fears, pop rock band. Aug. 21 at 8 p.m. $45 to $95. Foreigner, rock band. Aug. 26 at 8 p.m. $40 to $70. Wellmont Theater, 5 Seymour Street. (973) 783-9500; wellmonttheatre.com.

MORRISTOWN Morristown Unitarian Fellowship Cliff Eberhardt, singer-songwriter. Aug. 27 at 8 p.m. $7. Morristown Unitarian Fellowship, 21 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 335-9489; folkproject.org.

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts Gretchen Wilson, country. Thursday at 8 p.m. $57 to $97. Donna Summer, 70s pop singer. Aug. 25 at 8 p.m. $80 to $150. Styx, rock. Aug. 27 at 8 p.m. $57 to $97. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEWARK New Jersey Performing Arts Center “Sounds of the City,” featuring Kimberly Nichole, Gordon Chambers, Darien, and DJ Sabine. Thursday at 5 p.m. Free. “Sounds of the City,” featuring David Cedeno and his orchestra, Pzazz Dance Company, Pasion Por La Vida, DJ Danny Ramirez, and DJ Jose Rodriguez. Aug. 26 at 5 p.m. Free. New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

NEWARK Prudential Center Justin Bieber, pop, with special guest Sean Kingston, pop. Aug. 28 at 7 p.m. $32.50 to $52.50. Prudential Center, 165 Mulberry Street. (973) 757-6000; prucenter.com.

OCEAN GROVE Ocean Grove Great Auditorium Organ Recitals, featuring Gordon Turk and guest organists. Through Sept. 4. Free. Ocean Grove Great Auditorium, 15 Pilgrim Pathway. (732) 775-0035;

oceangrove.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater “The Irish Tenors Reunion,” with Finbar Wright, Anthony Kearns and Ronan Tynan. Thursday at 8 p.m. $29.50 to $79.50. “Performing Arts Academy Summer Showcase,” end-of-term performance featuring aspiring actors, musicians and dancers. Aug. 24 at 6:30 p.m. $14. Styx, rock. Aug. 26 at 8 p.m. $39.50 to $85. “Rock the Basie: Adult Band Camp Showcase,” the culminating performance of Rock Band Camp, a 10-week course for adult musicians of all ages and skill levels. Aug. 27 at 8 p.m. $18. “Rockit! Live,” featuring young musicians. Aug. 28 at 7 p.m. $15. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000. SOMERVILLE Downtown Somerville Summer Concert Series, featuring swing, rock, jazz, country and more. Fridays through Aug. 27. Free. Downtown Somerville, Main and Bridge Streets. (908) 541-1600; findsomerville.com.

WEST WINDSOR Nassau Park Pavilion The Jazz Lobster, performing big band and jazz standards. Aug. 21, 6 to 8 p.m. Free. Nassau Park Pavilion, Nassau Park Boulevard (off Route 1). (609) 919-1982; westwindsorarts.org.

WOODBRIDGE Barron Arts Center “Red, White and Blues: Jazz and Blues from the Garden State Concert Series” presents Tom Timko and Friends, jazz. Thursday at 8 p.m. Free. Barron Arts Center, 582 Rahway Avenue. (732) 634-0413.

Outdoors

MORRISTOWN Acorn Hall “Annual Garden Party,” featuring Elizabeth Cherry, Victorian songs. Aug. 29, 1 to 4 p.m. $3 to $6. Acorn Hall, 68 Morris Avenue. (973) 267-3465; www.acornhall.org.

TENAFLY Tenafly Nature Center “Full Moon Silent Hike,” a tranquil, meditative hike to Haring’s Rock, where participants will stop for a rest before the return hike. Aug. 24, 7 to 9 p.m. $5 and $10. Tenafly Nature Center, 313 Hudson Avenue.

tenaflynaturecenter.org;

(201) 568-6093.

Spoken Word

CAPE MAY East Lynne Theater Company “Tales of the Victorians,” stories by American authors including O. Henry and Louisa May Alcott, Thursdays at 4 p.m. Through Aug. 26. $10. East Lynne Theater Company, 500 Hughes Street. (866) 280-9211; eastlynnetheater.org. NEW BRUNSWICK George Street Playhouse “Poetry Slam,” poets from New York City and New Jersey compete for prizes. Aug. 15 at 7 p.m. $10. George Street Playhouse, 9 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7717;

georgestplayhouse.org.

Film

JERSEY CITY Liberty Science Center “Legends of Flight,” a documentary takes the viewer inside the cockpits of planes. Through Sept. 6. $8 to $13. Liberty Science Center, 222 Jersey City Boulevard. (201) 200-1000; lsc.org.

Theater

BEACH HAVEN Surflight Theater “Cabaret,” a musical with music by John Kander and lyrics by Fred Ebb. Thursday through Sept. 4. $20 and $30. Surflight Theater, Engleside and Beach Avenues. (609) 492-9477; www.surflight.org.

CAPE MAY Cape May Stage, The Robert Shackleton Playhouse “Romance/Romance,” two one-act musicals with book and lyrics by Barry Harman and music by Keith Herrmann. Through Aug. 21. $12.50 to $35. “Happy Days,” by Samuel Beckett. Aug. 25 to Sept. 25. $12.50 to $50. Cape May Stage, the Robert Shackleton Playhouse, Bank and Lafayette Streets. (609) 884-1341; capemaystage.com.

CAPE MAY East Lynne Theater Company “The Dictator,” comedy by Richard Harding Davis. Through Sept. 4. $13 to $28. East Lynne Theater Company, 500 Hughes Street. (866) 280-9211; eastlynnetheater.org.

DEAL PARK Axelrod Performing Arts Center “Hello, Dolly!” Through Aug. 15. $10 to $25. Axelrod Performing Arts Center, 100 Grant Avenue. (732) 531-9100, Ext. 42;

axelrodartscenter.com.

LONG BRANCH New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater “Sunlight,” a new play by Sharr White. Through Aug. 15. $25 to $60. New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater, 179 Broadway. (732) 229-3166; njrep.org.

MADISON The Shakespeare Theater of New Jersey “No Man’s Land,” by Harold Pinter. Through Aug. 29. $31 to $54. The Shakespeare Theater of New Jersey, 36 Madison Avenue. njshakespeare.org; (973) 408-5600.

PRINCETON Paul Robeson Center for the Arts “The Pavilion,” by Craig Wright. Through Aug. 21. $8 and $10. Paul Robeson Center for the Arts, 102 Witherspoon Street. (609) 924-8777; artscouncilofprinceton.org.

Museums and Galleries

CAPE MAY Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities “Cape May’s Stained Glass Exhibit,” featuring color photographs of 19th-century windows in Cape May, tools of the stained-glass trade and samples of the glass. Through Nov. 7. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. (609) 884-5404; capemaymac.org.

CLINTON Hunterdon Museum of Art “Botanica,” contemporary works in painting, sculpture, photography, drawing, installation, video, ceramics, glass and fiber. Through Sept. 12. $3 donation. “Katherine Mangiardi: Reflected Absence,” two-part exhibition exploring the history of the mills flanking the Raritan River’s south branch in Clinton. Through Sept. 12. $3 donation. Tuesdays through Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Hunterdon Museum of Art, 7 Lower Center Street. (908) 735-8415;

hunterdonartmuseum.org. CLINTON Red Mill Museum Village “Lights of Our Lives: Early Lighting and Miniature Rooms,” exhibition of antique lighting along with five miniature rooms depicting average lighting levels at night over five centuries, from the 1500s. Through Oct. 3. $8 general admission, $6 seniors, $5 children 6 to 12. Members and children under 6 free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Red Mill Museum Village, 56 Main Street. (908) 735-4101; theredmill.org.

DEMAREST Mikhail Zakin Gallery “Contemporary Puppetry: The Language Inside,” exhibition showcasing the artwork of local, national and international puppetry artists. Through Sept. 8. Mondays through Fridays, 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. Mikhail Zakin Gallery, 561 Piermont Road. (201) 767-7160; tasoc.org.

HALEDON American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark “Harriet Tubman and the Underground Railroad Print Show: The Art of Mark Priest,” featuring prints recounting the freeing of fugitive slave Charles Nalle in Troy, N.Y., in 1860. Through Dec. 31. $5. Hours: Wednesdays through Saturdays, 1 to 4 p.m., or by appointment. American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark, 83 Norwood Street. (973) 595-7953; labormuseum.org.

JERSEY CITY Jersey City Museum “Hair Tactics,” works by more than 30 visual artists exploring hair as subject matter and medium. Through Aug. 22. Jersey City Museum, 350 Montgomery Street. jerseycitymuseum.org;

(201) 413-0303.

LINCROFT Monmouth Museum New Jersey Emerging Artists Series: “Seth Ruggles Hiler: Portraits.” Through Aug. 15. “The Painting World of Evelyn Leavens: A Retrospective Exhibition.” Through Sept. 5. $7; members and children under 2, free. Hours: Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Monmouth Museum, 765 Newman Springs Road. (732) 747-2266; monmouthmuseum.org.

MONTCLAIR Montclair Art Museum “Engaging with Nature: American and Native American Artists (A.D. 1200 to 2004),” a variety of art and artifacts, including prehistoric Native American ceramics, 19th-century landscape paintings and contemporary staged photographs. Through Sept. 25, 2011. $12 adults, $10 seniors and students; members and children under 12, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Montclair Art Museum, 3 South Mountain Avenue. (973) 746-5555; montclairartmuseum.org.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Morris Museum “The Shoe Must Go On,” shoes for work and play, sports and recreation, “green” shoes and cultural connections. Through Aug. 29. $7 to $10. “The Legacy of Currier and Ives: Shaping the American Spirit,” 64 hand-colored lithographs from the collection of the Michele and Donald D’Amour Museum of Fine Arts in Springfield, Mass. Wednesday through Jan. 9. $7 to $10. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3700;

morrismuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN Acorn Hall “Gowns: From Birth to Bedroom to Ballroom,” including christening gowns, ball gowns, wedding gowns, dressing gowns and nightgowns. Through Jan. 1. Adults, $6; seniors, $5; students, $3. Mondays and Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Acorn Hall, 68 Morris Avenue. (973) 267-3465; www.acornhall.org.

MORRISTOWN Macculloch Hall Historical Museum “Mr. Morristown’s Museum: 60 Years of Macculloch Hall,” exploring W. Parsons Todd’s transformation of the old home. Through Oct. 31. “Oriental Rug Exhibit: A Rare Opportunity,” unusual and rare antique rugs. Through Aug. 31. $5 to $6; children ages 12 and younger, free. Hours: Wednesdays, Thursdays and Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Macculloch Hall Historical Museum, 45 Macculloch Avenue. (973) 538-2404; maccullochhall.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum “Animal Fair: Birds, Beasts and Bugs in Children’s Book Illustrations,” 28 original illustrations in various media in a range of artistic styles. Through June 5, 2011. Tuesdays through Fridays,

10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Saturdays

and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Jane

Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum,

71 Hamilton Street. (732) 932-7237;

www.zimmerlimuseum.rutgers.edu.

NEWARK The Newark Museum “Modern Metal: Early 20th-Century American Sculpture,” featuring a dozen bronze sculptures from the Newark Museum’s collection, including works by Gaston Lachaise, Hunt Diederich, Reuben Nakian and others. Through Dec. 31. $6 to $10; members, free. Hours: Wednesdays through Fridays, noon to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. (973) 596-6550; newarkmuseum.org.

PRINCETON Morven Museum and Garden “The Kennedys: Portrait of a Family,” photographs by Richard Avedon. Through Oct. 29. Wednesdays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Morven Museum and Garden, 55 Stockton Street. (609) 924-8144; historicmorven.org.

PRINCETON Princeton University Art Museum “Starburst: Color Photography in America 1970-1980,” a historical survey of the artistic movement. Through Sept. 26. “Presence and Remembrance: The Art of Toshiko Takaezu,” featuring new works and older ones from the collections of the museum and the university. Through Sept. 11. Hours: Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 10 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Princeton University Art Museum, Nassau and Witherspoon Streets. artmuseum.princeton.edu;

(609) 258-3788.

SUMMIT Visual Arts Center of New Jersey “YoaKustoms,” works by Amanda Yoakum, faculty member at the Visual Arts Center of New Jersey. Through Sept. 3. “Material Girls,” New Jersey artists who work with materials traditionally associated with women, like glitter, lace and plastic beads. Through Sept. 3. Visual Arts Center of New Jersey, 68 Elm Street. (908) 273-9121; njcva.org.

TRENTON New Jersey State Museum “Reality and Artifice,

2010 New Jersey Arts Annual: Fine Art,” works by artists who live or work in the state. Through Oct. 31. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 4:45 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m.

New Jersey State Museum, 205

West State Street. (609) 292-6464; www.state.nj.us/state/museum.

UNION Les Malamut Art Gallery “Fine Art Photography in the Digital Age,” exhibition by Miriam Jarney. Through Aug. 30. Mondays through Thursdays, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Les Malamut Art Gallery, 1980 Morris Avenue; (908) 851-5450. lesmalamutartgallery.web.officelive.com.

WOODBRIDGE Barron Arts Center “Bodyscapes 2010,” life drawings by the members of the Barron Life Drawing Group. Through Monday. Mondays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, 2 to 4 p.m. Barron Arts Center, 582 Rahway Avenue. (732) 634-0413.
