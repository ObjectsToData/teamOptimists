Events in Westchester

Published: March 5, 2010
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on in and around the Hudson Valley. Items for the guide should be sent at least three weeks in advance to westweek@nytimes.com, or by mail to Westchester Calendar, Metropolitan, The New York Times, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405.

Comedy

KINGSTON Ulster Performing Arts Center Lisa Lampanelli. March 19 at 8 p.m. $39.75. Ulster Performing Arts Center, 601 Broadway. (845) 339-6088; upac.org.

MAMARONECK Emelin Theater Robert Klein. March 20 at 8 p.m. $45. Emelin Theater, 153 Library Lane. emelin.org ; (914) 698-0098.

Film

IRVINGTON Irvington Town Hall Theater “I Puritani,” film version of Bellini opera, presented by the Teatro Comunale di Bologna. March 7 at 1 p.m. $20. Irvington Town Hall Theater, 85 Main Street. (914) 591-6602; irvingtontheater.com.

KINGSTON Ulster Performing Arts Center “Casablanca,” starring Humphrey Bogart and Ingrid Bergman.both Friday at 7:30 p.m. $5. Ulster Performing Arts Center, 601 Broadway. (845) 339-6088; upac.org.

NEW ROCHELLE New Rochelle Public Library “The Seduction of Mimi,” directed by Lina Wertmüller. March 18 at 7 p.m. $2 suggested donation. New Rochelle Public Library, 1 Library Plaza. nrpl.org; (914) 632-7878.

PLEASANTVILLE Jacob Burns Film Center “The Cat from Outer Space,” directed by Norman Tokar. March 7 and 13 at noon. “A Prophet,” directed by Jacques Audiard. March 7 through Thursday. “The Girl With the Dragon Tattoo,” directed by Niels Arden Oplev. Wednesday at 7 p.m. “Earthbound,” directed by Bahman Farmanara. Thursday at 7:30 p.m. “Stray Dog,” directed by Akira Kurosawa. March 20 through 22. “Throne of Blood,” directed by Akira Kurosawa. March 21 through 24. All are $6 to $11. Jacob Burns Film Center, 364 Manville Road. (914) 747-5555; burnsfilmcenter.org.

TARRYTOWN Tarrytown Music Hall “Nosferatu,” directed by F. W. Murnau. March 21 at 6 p.m. $8 and $10. Tarrytown Music Hall, 13 Main Street. (877) 840-0457;

tarrytownmusichall.org.

For Children

CROTON-ON-HUDSON Croton Point Nature Center “Shelter for Survival,” construct a shelter in the woods. Ages 7 to 13. March 20 at 1 p.m. Free. Croton Point Nature Center, Croton Point Avenue. (914) 862-5927; westchestergov.com/parks.

HARRISON Harrison Public Library “Youth Art Month,” works by Harrison School pupils. All ages. Monday through March 31. Harrison Public Library, 2 Bruce Avenue. (914) 835-0324; harrisonpl.org.

KATONAH Muscoot Farm “Caring for Donkeys,” educational activities. Ages 3 to 5. March 19 at 10 a.m. and 1 p.m. $10. Muscoot Farm, 51 Route 100. (914) 864-7282; muscootfarm.org.

MAMARONECK Emelin Theater “Junie B. Jones,” play based on the story by Barbara Park, presented by TheaterworksUSA. Ages 4 to 10. Tuesday at 9:30 and 11:15 a.m. $7 and $8. Circo Comedia, acrobatics and magic. Ages 4 and up. March 20 at 11 a.m. $13 and $18. Emelin Theater, 153 Library Lane. (914) 698-0098; emelin.org.

PLEASANTVILLE Jacob Burns Film Center “The Three Lives of Thomasina,” directed by Don Chaffey. Ages 4 and up. March 20 at noon. $6 to $11. Jacob Burns Film Center, 364 Manville Road. (914) 747-5555; burnsfilmcenter.org.

POUGHKEEPSIE Bardavon Opera House “The Very Hungry Caterpillar and Other Eric Carle Favorites,” puppet show presented by the Mermaid Theater of Nova Scotia. March 13 at 11 a.m. $7.50 to $15. Black Violin, classical and hip-hop music. Ages 8 and up. March 17 at 10 a.m. and noon. $6 and $10. Bardavon Opera House, 35 Market Street. bardavon.org; (845) 473-2072.

TARRYTOWN Tarrytown Music Hall “Click, Clack, Moo,” presented by TheaterworksUSA. Ages 3 to 8. March 19 at 10 a.m. and noon. $8 and $10. Tarrytown Music Hall, 13 Main Street. (800) 497-5007; tarrytownmusichall.org.

newcastlehistoricalsociety.org ; (914) 238-4666.

Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Sundays, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. $2. John Jay Homestead Historic Site, 400 Jay Street. johnjayhomestead.org; (914) 232-5651.

(914) 232-9555; katonahmuseum.org.

Through April 11. “Within and Beyond the Premises,” paintings and photographs by Carolee Schneemann. Through July 25. Mondays through Wednesdays, noon to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, noon to 5 p.m. and 6:30 to 8 p.m.; Sundays, 2 to 5 p.m. Brother Kenneth Chapman Gallery, Iona College Council on the Arts, 715 North Avenue. (914) 637-7796;

biona.edu/artscouncil.
