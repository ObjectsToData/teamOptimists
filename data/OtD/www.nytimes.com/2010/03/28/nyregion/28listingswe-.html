Calendar

Events in Westchester

Published: March 26, 2010
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on in and around the Hudson Valley. Items for the guide should be sent at least three weeks in advance to westweek@nytimes.com, or by mail to Westchester Calendar, Metropolitan, The New York Times, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405.

Comedy

KINGSTON Ulster Performing Arts Center David Sedaris. April 9 at 8 p.m. $40 to $60. Ulster Performing Arts Center, 601 Broadway. (845) 339-6088; upac.org.

MAMARONECK Emelin Theater Jackie Mason. April 11 at 3 p.m. $45. Emelin Theater, 153 Library Lane. emelin.org; (914) 698-0098.

POUGHKEEPSIE Bananas Comedy Club Paul Virzi. Friday and April 3. $12 and $15. Bananas Comedy Club, 2170 Route 9. (845) 462-3333; bananascomedyclub.com.

Film

DOBBS FERRY Mercy College Lecture Hall The 11th Annual International Film Festival: “Cautiva,” directed by Gastón Biraben. April 5. “Not One Less” directed by Zhang Yimou. April 6. “The Son’s Room” directed by Nanni Moretti. April 7 “Takva: A Man’s Fear of God” directed by Özer Kizitlan. April 8. “Rabbit Proof Fence,” directed by Philip Noyce. April 9. All are at 6:30 p.m. All are free. Mercy College Lecture Hall, 555 Broadway. mercy.edu; (914) 741-2260.

PLEASANTVILLE Jacob Burns Film Center “Dodes’ka-den,” directed by Akira Kurosawa. April 3 at 2:55 and 8:30 p.m. $6 to $11. “Camera Obscura,” directed by María Victoria Menis. April 9 at 5 p.m. $6 to $11. Jacob Burns Film Center, 364 Manville Road. burnsfilmcenter.org; (914) 747-5555.

SCARSDALE Bendheim Performing Arts Center “Bufor (Beaufort),” directed by Joseph Cedar. April 7 at 7 p.m. $9 and $11. Bendheim Performing Arts Center, 999 Wilmot Road. (914) 472-3300; thebendheim.org.

For Children

HARRISON Harrison Public Library “Youth Art Month,” works by Harrison School pupils. All ages. Through Wednesday. Harrison Public Library, 2 Bruce Avenue. (914) 835-0324; harrisonpl.org.

KATONAH Muscoot Farm “Punch and Judy Puppet Show,” presented by the Fred Greenspan puppet troupe. Bring picnic blanket. All ages. April 4 at 1 and 2:30 p.m. Free. Muscoot Farm, 51 Route 100. muscootfarm.org; (914) 864-7282.

OSSINING Teatown Lake Reservation “Story Sundays,” seasonal stories and activities. Ages 4 to 7. March 28 at 11 a.m. “Afternoon Animal Encounters,” educational activities featuring mammals. April 11 at 2 p.m. All ages. $5; members, free. Teatown Lake Reservation, 1600 Spring Valley Road. teatown.org; (914) 762-2912.

PEEKSKILL Paramount Center for the Arts “MVP: The Jackie Robinson Story,” presented by the Dallas Children’s Theater. Ages 8 and up. April 11 at 3 p.m. $15 to $25. Paramount Center for the Arts, 1008 Brown Street. paramountcenter.org; (914) 739-2333.

PLEASANTVILLE Jacob Burns Film Center “The Three Lives of Thomasina,” directed by Don Chaffey. Ages 4 and up. March 28. “Puss in Boots,” directed by Eugene Marner. Ages 4 and up. April 3-11. Both are $6 to $11. Jacob Burns Film Center, 364 Manville Road. (914) 747-5555; burnsfilmcenter.org.

RYE Rye Arts Center “Student Studio: Emerging High School Talent,” group show. Ages 10 and up. Through Wednesday. Rye Arts Center, 51 Milton Road. (914) 967-0700; ryeartscenter.org.

SCARSDALE Weinberg Nature Center “Spring Detectives and Eco-Explorers,” wilderness exploration includes games, stories, crafts and “eco-dramas.” Ages 2 to 12. Monday through Friday. $15 to $45 per session. Weinberg Nature Center, 455 Mamaroneck Road. (914) 722-1289; weinbergnaturecenter.org.

Museums and Galleries

BEACON Dia:Beacon Zoe Leonard: “You See I Am Here After All, 2008.” Through Sept. 6. Sol LeWitt: “Drawing Series.” Through November. Fridays through Mondays, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. $7 and $10; members and children under 12, free. Dia:Beacon, 3 Beekman Street. (845) 440-0100; diabeacon.org.

CHAPPAQUA Horace Greeley House “Reader’s Digest : The Local Magazine That Conquered the World,” photographs and artifacts. Through Jan. 1. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Thursdays and Saturdays, 1 to 4 p.m. or by appointment. Horace Greeley House, 100 King Street.

newcastlehistoricalsociety.org; (914) 238-4666.CROSS RIVER Gallery in the Park, Pound Ridge Reservation Heather Van Wolf: “Studies in Line and Character.” Through April 25. Daily, 9 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Gallery in the Park, Pound Ridge Reservation, Routes 35 and 121. (914) 864-7317; vanwolf.com.

CROTON-ON-HUDSON Unitarian Universalist Fellowship of Briarcliff, Croton and Ossining Photographers of Northern Westchester Exhibit. March 28 through April 4. Unitarian Universalist Fellowship of Briarcliff, Croton and Ossining, 2021 Albany Post Road. uucroton.org; (914) 271-4283.

DOBBS FERRY Upstream Gallery “Inner Vision,” watercolors and etchings by Mitchell Goldberg. . “Then and Now,” paintings by Shirley Glasser. Both through March 28. Thursdays through Sundays, 12:30 to 5:30 p.m. Upstream Gallery, 26B Main Street. (914) 674-8548;

upstreamgallery.com.

IRVINGTON-ON-HUDSON Irvington Public Library “Landscape Watercolors,” by Ellen Hopkins Fountain. Through Wednesday. Irvington Public Library, 12 South Astor Street. irvingtonlibrary.org; (914) 591-7840.

KATONAH John Jay Homestead Historic Site “From Oppression to Freedom: John Jay and his Huguenot Heritage,” historical objects and artifacts. Through Oct. 31. $2. Tuesdays to Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Sundays, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. John Jay Homestead Historic Site, 400 Jay Street. johnjayhomestead.org; (914) 232-5651.

KATONAH Katonah Museum of Art “Katonah Steelroots,” outdoor sculpture by Steve Tobin. Through Sept. 19. “The Art of Contemporary Puppet Theater,” group show. Through June 13. “Puppetry Around the World,” includes marionettes, shadow puppets and hand puppets. Through June 13. $3 to $5; members and children, free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Katonah Museum of Art, 134 Jay Street. (914) 232-9555; katonahmuseum.org.

LARCHMONT Kenise Barnes Fine Art “Other People’s Fiction,” group show. Through April 8. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; or by appointment. Kenise Barnes Fine Art, 1955 Palmer Avenue. (914) 834-8077; kbfa.com.

LARCHMONT Pgartventure Gallery “Bruto’s Hand,” the works of Juan Delcan: animation, video and paintings. Through April 19. Wednesdays through Saturdays, noon to 5 p.m.; or by appointment. Pgartventure Gallery, 2130A Boston Post Road. pgartventure.com; (914) 834-5100.

LARCHMONT Sheldrake Environmental Center “Sharing My Journey in Nature,” an exhibition by Anthony Waring, photographs. Through May 5. Mondays through Fridays, 9:30 a.m. to 5 p.m. Sheldrake Environmental Center, 685 Weaver Street. (914) 834-1443; sheldrakecenter.org.

NEW PALTZ Samuel Dorsky Museum of Art, at SUNY New Paltz “Panorama of the Hudson River,” photographs by Greg Miller. Through March 28. “A Mother’s Journey,” photographs by Renée C. Byer. Through April 11. “Body, Line, Motion: Selections from the Permanent Collection,” drawings and sculpture. Through April 11. “Within and Beyond the Premises,” paintings and photographs by Carolee Schneemann. Through July 25. Wednesdays through Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Samuel Dorsky Museum of Art, at SUNY New Paltz, 1 Hawk Drive. newpaltz.edu/museum; (845) 257-3844.

NEW ROCHELLE Brother Kenneth Chapman Gallery, Iona College Council on the Arts “Family Matters,” paintings by Patricia Horing and Kristin McElroy. Through April 8. Mondays through Wednesdays, noon to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, noon to 5 p.m. and 6:30 to 8 p.m.; Sundays, 2 to 5 p.m. Brother Kenneth Chapman Gallery, Iona College Council on the Arts, 715 North Avenue. (914) 637-7796;

biona.edu/artscouncil.

NEW ROCHELLE New Rochelle Public Library “Utopioptix, Visions of the Future, Then and Now,” group show. Monday through Wednesday. Mondays, Tuesdays and Thursdays, 9 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Wednesdays, 9 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. New Rochelle Public Library, 1 Library Plaza. (914) 632-7878; nrpl.org.

PEEKSKILL BeanRunner Cafe “From the Heart, Intuitive Abstractions,” paintings and drawings by Susan Weinreich. Through May 16. BeanRunner Cafe, 201 South Division Street. (914) 737-1701; beanrunnercafe.com.

PEEKSKILL Hudson Valley Center for Contemporary Art “Double Dutch,” group show featuring contemporary Dutch installation and video art. Through July 26. “In.Flec.Tion.” Group exhibition by 13 New York area artists. $2 to $5; members, free. Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 6 p.m.; or by appointment. Hudson Valley Center for Contemporary Art, 1701 Main Street. (914) 788-0100; hvcca.com.

PELHAM Pelham Art Center “Objectified,” contemporary sculpture. Through April 17. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Saturdays, noon to 4 p.m. Free. Pelham Art Center, 155 Fifth Avenue. pelhamartcenter.org; (914) 738-2525.

POUGHKEEPSIE Vassar College, Frances Lehman Loeb Art Center Harry Roseman: “Hole in the Wall,” installation. Through April. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Vassar College, Frances Lehman Loeb Art Center, 124 Raymond Avenue. (845) 437-5632; fllac.vassar.edu.

PURCHASE Manhattanville College Creative Arts Center “Patterned Tactic(s),” an exhibition of drawings and paintings by Megan McNaught and Christina Pitsch. Mondays, Tuesdays, Thursdays and Fridays, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Wednesdays, 11 a.m. to 7 p.m.; Saturdays, noon to 4 p.m. Manhattanville College Creative Arts Center, 2900 Purchase Street. (914) 694-2200; mville.edu.

PURCHASE Neuberger Museum of Art Tania Bruguera: “On the Political Imaginary,” installations and performance. “Contemporary Latin American Art From the Luis Calzadilla Collection,” group show. Both through April 11. $3 and $5. Tuesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Neuberger Museum of Art, 735 Anderson Hill Road. neuberger.org; (914) 251-6100.

RYE Rye Free Reading Room “One-Man Abstract Expressionism Show,” works by Joseph Bostany. Through Thursday. Rye Free Reading Room, 1061 Boston Post Road. (914) 967-0480; ryelibrary.org.

TUCKAHOE Westchester Italian Cultural Center “The Inventions of Leonardo da Vinci,” contemporary models of mechanical devices. Through April 16. Tuesdays and Wednesdays, 10 a.m. to 2 p.m.; Thursdays, 2 to 7:30 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 2 p.m. Westchester Italian Cultural Center, 1 Generoso Pope Place. (914) 771-8700; wiccny.org.

WHITE PLAINS White Plains Public Library “The Art of Dr. Seuss: a Retrospective.” Through June 23. Mondays and Wednesdays, 10 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Tuesdays, Thursdays and Fridays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. White Plains Public Library, 100 Martine Avenue. (914) 422-1400;

whiteplainslibrary.org.

WOODSTOCK Woodstock Artists Association and Museum “The Beauty of Discord: Selections from the Permanent Collection,” group show. Through March 28. Suggested donation, $5. Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Woodstock Artists Association and Museum, 28 Tinker Street. (845) 679-2940; woodstockart.org.

YONKERS Hudson River Museum “Bakelite in Yonkers: Pioneering the Age of Plastics.” “Collecting for a New Millennium: Recent Acquisitions 2000-2010.” Both through June 6. Wednesdays, Thursdays, Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m.; Fridays, noon to 7:30 p.m. $3 to $5; members, free. Hudson River Museum, 511 Warburton Avenue. (914) 963-4550; hrm.org.

Music and Dance

BEDFORD Fellowship Hall, St. Matthews Church “Music from the Courts of Louis XVI and Napoleon III,” featuring Anthony Newman and the Bedford Chamber Ensemble. April 7 at 8 p.m. $35. Fellowship Hall, St. Matthews Church, 382 Cantitoe Street. stmatthewsbedford.org; (914) 234-9636.

IRVINGTON Irvington Town Hall Theater “Flow: In Celebration of the Hudson River,” presented by the Westchester Jazz Orchestra. April 10 at 8 p.m. $5 to $35. Irvington Town Hall Theater, 85 Main Street. (914) 591-6602; irvingtontheater.com.

IRVINGTON Irvington Public Library “Four Hand Recital,” classical piano, featuring Christopher Oldfather and Fredrica Wyman. April 11 at 4 p.m. Free. Irvington Public Library, 12 South Astor Street. irvingtonlibrary.org; (914) 591-7840.

LARCHMONT Watercolor Cafe Lori McKenna, folk. March 28 at 7:30 p.m. $25. Watercolor Cafe, 2094 Boston Post Road. (914) 834-2213; watercolorcafe.net.

MARLBORO The Falcon Benefit for Haiti: Kevin Hayes with special guests, jazz. Friday at 7 p.m. Hugh Brodie and the Cosmic Ensemble, jazz. April 3 at 7 p.m. Donations accepted. The Falcon, 1348 Route 9W. liveatthefalcon.com; (845) 236-7970.

OSSINING Ossining Eagles Hall “Zydeco Dance!” Dance lesson and performance featuring Leroy Thomas and the Zydeco Roadrunners. April 4, at 5 p.m. $20. Ossining Eagles Hall, 7 Old Albany Post Road. cajunzydeco.net/SS; (914) 960-9057.

PEEKSKILL Paramount Center for the Arts “Laurel and Hardy shorts with live jazz,” featuring Steven Bernstein’s Millennial Territory Orchestra. March 28 at 3 p.m. $10 and $20. Paramount Center for the Arts, 1008 Brown Street. (914) 739-2333; paramountcenter.org.

PLEASANTVILLE Presbyterian Church of Pleasantville Pleasantville Chamber Music Society peforms Mozart, Turina, and Brahms. March 28 at 3 p.m. Free. Presbyterian Church of Pleasantville , 400 Bedford Road. (914) 769-0458; pvillepresby.org.

POUGHKEEPSIE Bardavon Opera House Los Lobos and Leo Kottke, eclectic rock. March 28 at 7 p.m. $45 and $50. “Hudson Valley Philharmonic Pastorale,” featuring Yeol Eum Son, classical piano. April 10 at 8 p.m. $25 to $47. Bardavon Opera House, 35 Market Street. (845) 473-2072; bardavon.org.

PURCHASE Performing Arts Center “String Performance Hour,” featuring conservatory students. Recital hall of the Conservatory of Music. Wednesday at 3 p.m. Free. Performing Arts Center, 735 Anderson Hill Road. (914) 251-6200; artscenter.org.

TARRYTOWN Tarrytown Music Hall Inti-Illimani and Francesa Gagnon, Chilean folk. March 28 at 7 p.m. $32 to $45. Alejandro Escovedo and Graham Parker, rock. April 8 at 8 p.m. $35 to $48. Tarrytown Music Hall, 13 Main Street. tarrytownmusichall.org; (877) 840-0457.

VALHALLA Academic Arts Theater “Danceworks in Performance,” presented by the Westchester Community College dance troupe. Friday through April 11 at 8 p.m. $7 and $12. Academic Arts Theater, 75 Grasslands Road. (914) 606-6262; sunywcc.edu.

WHITE PLAINS Downtown Music, Grace Church The Jolles Duo, featuring Renee Jolles, violin, and Susan Jolles, harp. Wednesday at 12:10 p.m. Free. “The English Invasion,” classical, featuring Richard Weidlich, baritone. April 7 at 12:10 p.m. Free. Downtown Music, Grace Church, 33 Church Street. dtmusic.org; (914) 248-1112.

WHITE PLAINS White Plains Performing Arts Center “Jazz Trio,” a performance of favorites from the great American songbook, presented by the Westchester Philharmonic. March 28 at 2 p.m. $35. White Plains Performing Arts Center, 11 City Place. (914) 328-1600; wppac.com.

Outdoors

CROTON-ON-HUDSON Croton Point Nature Center “Spring Ornithology Workshop,” learn to identify and classify different bird families and orders. Bring binoculars. April 10 at 1 p.m. Free. Croton Point Nature Center, Croton Point Avenue.

westchestergov.com/parks; (914) 862-5927.

KATONAH Muscoot Farm “Vernal Pools,” a hike and exploration. March 28 at 1 p.m. Free. Muscoot Farm, 51 Route 100. (914) 864-7282; muscootfarm.org.

SCARSDALE Weinberg Nature Center “Native Lifestyles Program,” a demonstration of primitive ways of making fires and pottery, and tracking animals. Ages 9 and up. March 28 at 3:30 p.m. Educational hike with Walter Terrell, trained Lenapé ethnologist. Ages 9 and up. April 8 at 3:30 p.m. “Carving and Coal-Burning Wilderness Spoons,” make your own wooden spoon the primitive way. Bring a pocket knife with a “locking” blade and plain edge (not serrated.) Ages 9 and up. April 10 through May 8. All are free. Weinberg Nature Center, 455 Mamaroneck Road. weinbergnaturecenter.org; (914) 722-1289.

YONKERS Lenoir Preserve “Welcome Spring,” a walk around the preserve with Walter Chadwick. Bring binoculars. Followed by children’s crafts. April 3 at 10 a.m. Free. Lenoir Preserve, 19 Dudley Street. (914) 968-5851;

parks.westchestergov.com.

Spoken Word

KATONAH Katonah Museum of Art “The Intimate Inanimate,” a discussion with puppet masters Dan Hurlin, Roman Paska, Hanne Tierney, and curator Leslee Asch. Reservation required. March 28 at 5:30 p.m. $10; members free. Katonah Museum of Art, 134 Jay Street. (914) 232-9555; katonahmuseum.org.

MOUNT VERNON St. Paul’s Church National Historic Site “Native Americans: The Area’s Earlier Residents,” a lecture by Pat Ernest on the Native American Indians who lived in the vicinity. April 10 at 2 p.m. Free. St. Paul’s Church National Historic Site, 897 South Columbus Avenue. (914) 667-4116; nps.gov/sapa.

POCANTICO HILLS Stone Barns Center for Food and Agriculture “Meet the Farmer: Mushrooms,” led by Jack Algiere, farm manager. Thursday at 1 p.m. $12. Stone Barns Center for Food and Agriculture, 630 Bedford Road.

stonebarnscenter.org; (914) 366-6200.

POUGHKEEPSIE Vassar College “Darwin Days 2010,” an annual celebration of science and the humanities, focusing this year on women in science, with dramatic readings and presentations by Vassar faculty and staff members. Through April 22. Free. Vassar College, 124 Raymond Avenue. (845) 437-7294; arts.vassar.edu.

YONKERS Hudson River Museum “Gravity, Energy and Space,” an art and science demonstration with Tom Shannon. March 28 at 3:30 p.m. Hudson River Museum, 511 Warburton Avenue. (914) 963-4550; hrm.org.

Theater

BRIARCLIFF MANOR Hudson Stage Company, Woodward Hall Theater “I Can’t Believe it’s not Memorized,” one-act comedy by Briarcliff High School students. April 9 at 7:30 p.m. $10. Hudson Stage Company, Woodward Hall Theater, 235 Elm Road. hudsonstage.com; (877) 238-5596.

CROTON FALLS Schoolhouse Theater “Kimberly Akimbo,” comedy by David Lindsay-Abaire. Through March 28. $30 and $32. Schoolhouse Theater, 3 Owens Road. schoolhousetheater.org; (914) 277-8477.

ELMSFORD Westchester Broadway Theater “Nine,” musical by Maury Yestin and Arthur Kopit. Through April 24. $80.53 for dinner and show. Westchester Broadway Theater, 75 Clearbrook Road. (914) 592-2222; broadwaytheatre.com.
