Calendar

Events in New Jersey

Published: December 23, 2010
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on around the state this week. Items may be submitted by mail to On the Towns, Sunday New Jersey Section, The New York Times, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405; or by e-mail to njtowns@nytimes.com.

Comedy

HASBROUCK HEIGHTS Bananas Comedy Club Tina Giorgi. Jan. 7 and 8. $15. Bananas Comedy Club, 283 Route 17 South. (201) 727-1090; bananascomedyclub.com.

For Children

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Morris Museum “Winter Wonderland Family Festival,” holiday crafts and activities. Monday, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. $7 and $10. Michael DuBois, circus performer, will perform stunts like juggling, mind-reading and unicycling. Tuesday at 12:30 and 2:30 p.m. $10. Art in Motion, featuring the In Motion Dance Company. Wednesday at noon and 2 p.m. $5 and $10. Holiday Crafts: Make a Dapper Penguin Finger Puppet. Thursday, noon to 2 p.m. $5 to $10. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3700; morrismuseum.org.

RED BANK Two River Theater “Charlotte’s Web,” featuring adults, children and puppets. Through Jan. 2. $24 to $43. Two River Theater, 21 Bridge Avenue. (732) 345-1400; trtc.org.

SOUTH ORANGE South Orange Performing Arts Center Bari Koral Family Rock Band, featuring friendly tunes and interactive movement exercises. Jan. 9 at 11 a.m. and 2 p.m. $15. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. sopacnow.org; (973) 313-2787.

TENAFLY Tenafly Nature Center “Family Day Camp,” a minicamp for families featuring guided hikes with a professional naturalist, lessons about what happens to animals in winter and stories about American Indians. Tuesday and Wednesday. $15 to $60. Tenafly Nature Center, 313 Hudson Avenue. (201) 568-6093; tenaflynaturecenter.org.

Music and Dance

CHERRY HILL Croft Farm Arts Center “Music From the Movies and Classical Vinyl,” featuring members of the Philadelphia Orchestra. Jan. 9 at 3 p.m. $12 and $15. Croft Farm Arts Center, 100 Borton’s Mill Road. cherryhill-nj.com; (856) 488-7868.

COLLINGSWOOD Collingswood Community Center Denis DiBlasio, jazz saxophonist and his band. Jan. 6, 7:30 to 9 p.m. $5 to $10. Collingswood Community Center, 30 East Collings Avenue. (856) 858-8914.

MADISON Museum of Early Trades and Crafts Harp Ensemble Concert, featuring Odarka Stockert. Jan. 9 at 2 p.m. $5 and $10. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. (973) 377-2982; rosenet.org/metc.

MANASQUAN Algonquin Arts Center “The Magic of Motown,” including hits from the 1960s. Friday at 6 and 9 p.m. $30 to $50. Joy Hermalyn, Broadway singer. Jan. 8 at 7:30 p.m. $30. Algonquin Arts Center, 173 Main Street. (732) 528-9211; algonquinarts.com.

MONTCLAIR Wellmont Theater Dark Star Orchestra, playing music of the Grateful Dead. Tuesday at 8 p.m. $33. Passion Pit, pop. Thursday at 8 p.m. and Friday at 9 p.m. $43. Wellmont Theater, 5 Seymour Street. wellmonttheatre.com; (973) 783-9500.

MORRISTOWN Morristown Unitarian Fellowship Crowfoot, Canadian folk band. Thursday at 8 p.m. $7. Morristown Unitarian Fellowship, 21 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 335-9489; folkproject.org.

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts Little Feat, Southern rock-fusion band. Thursday at 8 p.m. $32 to $57. Gregg Allman, Southern blues-rock. Jan. 7 at 8 p.m. $47 to $87. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center “Video Games Live: Bonus Round,” concert featuring video game music performed by the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra and accompanied by video game imagery and lighting effects. Wednesday and Thursday. $21 to $82. “Water! From the River to the Sea,” featuring the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra. Jan. 7 at 8 p.m. $20 to $82. To buy tickets: njsymphony.org or (973) 624-3713. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. njpac.org; (888) 466-5722.

NEWARK Prudential Center “Southern Invasion,” a hip-hop concert featuring Rick Ross, Gucci Mane and Waka Flocka. Jan. 1 at 7 p.m. $29.50 to $250. Prudential Center, 165 Mulberry Street. (973) 757-6000; prucenter.com.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “Salute to Vienna,” featuring the Strauss Symphony of America. Friday at 6 p.m. $47 to $97. “Water! From the River to the Sea,” featuring the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra. Jan. 8 at 8 p.m. $20 to $82. To buy tickets: (973) 624-3713 or njsymphony.org. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

Outdoors

TITUSVILLE Washington Crossing State Park “After Holidays Walk-Off,” outdoor hike. Jan. 8 at 1 p.m. Free. Washington Crossing State Park, 355 Washington Crossing-Pennington Road. (609) 737-0623; www.state.nj.us.

TRENTON Old Barracks Museum “Patriots’ Week,” tour the museum, meet the soldiers of the battles of Trenton, and more. Dec. 26 through Tuesday. $5. Old Barracks Museum, 101 Barrack Street. (609) 396-1776; barracks.org.

Seasonal

CAPE MAY Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities “An Old-Fashioned Christmas Exhibit,” working LGB trains, trees, toys and more. Through Jan. 2. “Ghosts of Christmas Past Trolley Rides,” a member of the East Lynne Theater Company will tell Victorian holiday ghost tales on a ride through Cape May’s decorated Historic District. Jan. 1 at 8 and 9 p.m. $7 and $10. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. capemaymac.org; (609) 884-5404.

HASBROUCK HEIGHTS Bananas Comedy Club “A New Year’s Eve Three-Act Comedy Show,” starring Joe Starr, Steve Marshall and Tom Van Horn. Friday at 8 and 11 p.m. $25 and $35, including Champagne toast and noisemakers. Bananas Comedy Club, 283 Route 17 South. bananascomedyclub.com; (201) 727-1090.

JERSEY CITY Liberty Science Center “The 12 Days of Science,” featuring mental magicians, giant bubbles, live penguins and more. Through Jan. 2. Liberty Science Center, 222 Jersey City Boulevard. (201) 200-1000; lsc.org.

LINCROFT Monmouth Museum “Go Green for the Holidays,” annual exhibition with trees and trimmings decorated with ornaments made by hand using recycled materials. Through Jan. 3. Monmouth Museum, 765 Newman Springs Road. (732) 747-2266; monmouthmuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts New Jersey Ballet’s 40th Anniversary “Nutcracker.” Dec. 26. $19 to $59. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

MORRISTOWN Acorn Hall “Victorian Christmas at Acorn Hall,” featuring decorated period rooms, a gift shop and special events, including “Festive Friday” evenings in December and costumed guides. Through Jan. 16. $3 to $6. Acorn Hall, 68 Morris Avenue. (973) 267-3465; www.acornhall.org.

MORRISTOWN Macculloch Hall Historical Museum “ ’Twas the Night Before Christmas,” exhibition featuring rare Christmas toys and decorations from private collections, and the museum’s collection of holiday-themed original artwork by the caricaturist Thomas Nast. Through Jan. 31. Macculloch Hall Historical Museum, 45 Macculloch Avenue. (973) 538-2404; maccullochhall.org.

NEWARK The Newark Museum “Christmas in the Ballantine House: Feasting With Family and Friends,” featuring an installation that depicts 19th-century life and traditions. Through Jan. 2. “Holiday Family Fun Days: Celebrate Light!” hands-on workshops for families, tours of the Victorian Ballantine House decorated for Christmas and more. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. (973) 596-6550; newarkmuseum.org.

NEWARK Priory Restaurant “New Year’s Eve Jazz and Twilight Affair,” featuring Carrie Jackson and her Jazzin’ All Star Trio. Friday at 7 p.m. $15. Priory Restaurant, 233 West Market Street. (973) 242-8012.

PATERSON Lambert Castle Museum “Gingerbread Exhibit,” an exhibition of original, lavishly decorated gingerbread structures. Through Jan. 9. $4 to $8. Lambert Castle Museum, 3 Valley Road. (973) 247-0085; lambertcastle.org.

PRINCETON Morven Museum and Garden “Festival of Trees,” featuring holiday trees decorated by area businesses and organizations. Through Jan. 9. $5 and $6. Morven Museum and Garden, 55 Stockton Street. historicmorven.org; (609) 924-8144.

PRINCETON Catch a Rising Star Comedy Club Rodney Laney. Friday at 8 and 10:30 p.m. $58; includes dinner and show. Catch a Rising Star Comedy Club, Hyatt Regency, 102 Carnegie Center. (609) 987-8018; catcharisingstar.com/princeton.html.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater “Black Nativity,” a gospel musical based on the Gospel of St. Luke, using the poetry of Langston Hughes and performed by an all-black cast. Thursday at 7:30 p.m. $19.50 and $24.50. New Year’s Eve with Southside Johnny and the Asbury Jukes. Friday at 9 p.m. $29.50 to $99. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

SHREWSBURY Guild of Creative Art “All Member Holiday Show.” Through Jan. 5. Guild of Creative Art, 620 Broad Street. (732) 741-1441; guildofcreativeart.org.

WOODBRIDGE Barron Arts Center Model train exhibition, with more than 300 feet of track, a moving operating playground and three towns. Through Tuesday. Barron Arts Center, 582 Rahway Avenue. (732) 634-0413.

Spoken Word

RAHWAY Arts Guild of Rahway “The Changing Face of Reality: Realism in Art,” a lecture and discussion with Mel Leipzig, artist and art professor. Jan. 6 at 7 p.m. Arts Guild of Rahway, 1670 Irving Street. (732) 381-7511; rahwayartsguild.org.

Theater

CAPE MAY Cape May Stage, The Robert Shackleton Playhouse “The Little Prince,” by Rick Cummins and John Scoullar, adapted from the book by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry. Through Friday. $12.50 to $50. Cape May Stage, the Robert Shackleton Playhouse, Bank and Lafayette Streets. (609) 884-1341; capemaystage.com.

LONG BRANCH New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater “The Tangled Skirt,” world-premiere theater-noir crime drama by Steve Braunstein. Through Jan. 23. $25 to $60. New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater, 179 Broadway. (732) 229-3166; njrep.org.

MADISON F.M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University “I Capture the Castle,” stage adaptation of Dodie Smith’s 1948 coming-of-age novel. Through Jan. 2. $31 to $54. F.M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University, 36 Madison Avenue. njshakespeare.org; (973) 408-5600.

MILLBURN Paper Mill Playhouse “Les Misérables,” musical by Claude-Michel Schönberg based on the novel by Victor Hugo. Through Thursday. $25 to $84. Paper Mill Playhouse, 22 Brookside Drive. (973) 376-4343; papermill.org.

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts “The Magic of Bill Blagg Live,” featuring illusions, lighting and special effects. Jan. 8 at 3 and 7 p.m. $25. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center “The How and the Why,” new play by Sarah Treem. Jan. 7 through Feb. 13. $20 to $60. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

Museums and Galleries

CLINTON Hunterdon Museum of Art “Pamela Becker: Patterns and Constructs,” exhibition featuring textile constructions, baskets and photo collages. Through Jan. 16. “John Ripton: Against the Wall,” photographs of urban walls covered with signs, graffiti, public notices and commercial icons. Through Feb. 13. Donation: $3. Tuesdays through Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Hunterdon Museum of Art, 7 Lower Center Street. hunterdonartmuseum.org; (908) 735-8415.

CLINTON Red Mill Museum Village “The Stars and Stripes: Fabric of the American Spirit,” exhibition of antique American flags. Through June 14. $8 general admission, $6 seniors, $5 children 6 to 12. Members and children under 6 free. Red Mill Museum Village, 56 Main Street. (908) 735-4101, ext. 107;

theredmill.org.

DEMAREST Mikhail Zakin Gallery “Frozen in Time,” exhibition showcasing the landscapes of local and regional photographers. Through Jan. 7. Mondays through Fridays, 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. Mikhail Zakin Gallery, 561 Piermont Road. (201) 767-7160; tasoc.org.

HAMILTON Grounds for Sculpture “Fall/Winter Exhibition 2010,” group show. Through April 10. $8 to $12; members and children under 5, free. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. groundsforsculpture.org; (609) 586-0616.

HOPEWELL Gallery 14 “Monks of Burma and Laos,” exhibition of photographs by Michael Paxton. Jan. 7 through Feb. 6. Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Gallery 14, 14 Mercer Street. (609) 333-8511.

MADISON Museum of Early Trades and Crafts “Lenape: Tools and Daily Living,” exhibition exploring Lenape life before European influences. Through Jan. 1. $3 and $5; members and children under 6, free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. rosenet.org/metc; (973) 377-2982.

MONTCLAIR BrassWorks on Grove “Structural Harmonies,” solo exhibition of paintings by Allan Gorman. Through Jan. 7. BrassWorks on Grove, 105 Grove Street. (973) 744-5100; brassworksongrove.com.

MONTCLAIR Montclair Art Museum “Engaging Nature: American and Native-American Artists (A.D. 1200-2004).” Includes prehistoric American Indian ceramics, 19th-century landscape paintings and contemporary staged photographs. Through Sept. 25. “Living for Art: The Dorothy and Herbert Vogel Collection,” works produced by 27 American artists from 1967 to 2000. Through Jan. 2. $12 adults, $10 seniors and students; members and children under 12, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Montclair Art Museum, 3 South Mountain Avenue. (973) 746-5555; montclairartmuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN Acorn Hall “Gowns: From Birth to Bedroom to Ballroom,” exhibition featuring a variety of gowns, including christening gowns, ball gowns, wedding gowns, dressing gowns and even nightgowns. Through Jan. 1. $6; seniors, $5; students, $3. Mondays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Acorn Hall, 68 Morris Avenue. (973) 267-3465;

www.acornhall.org.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Morris Museum “The Legacy of Currier & Ives: Shaping the American Spirit,” exhibition featuring 64 hand-colored lithographs from the collection of the Michele and Donald D’Amour Museum of Fine Arts in Springfield, Mass. Through Jan. 9. “The Art of Shirley Winthrope Rivo,” exhibition of photography and paintings. Through Feb. 13. “Frank H. Netter, M.D.: Michelangelo of Medicine,” featuring more than 40 medical illustrations. Through Feb. 27. $7 to $10. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3700; morrismuseum.org.

NEWARK The Newark Museum “Modern Metal: Early-20th-Century American Sculpture,” bronze sculptures, including works by Gaston Lachaise, Hunt Diederich and Reuben Nakian. Through Friday. “Red Luster: Lacquer and Leatherworks of Asia.” Through Friday. “Gustav Stickley and the American Arts and Crafts Movement,” furniture, textiles and lighting. Through Jan. 2. $6 to $10; members, free. Wednesdays through Fridays, noon to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays through June, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. newarkmuseum.org; (973) 596-6550.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum “Animal Fair: Birds, Beasts and Bugs in Children’s Book Illustrations,” exhibition featuring 28 original illustrations in various media that explore the wide range of artistic styles and diverse interpretation of storybook animals. Through June 5. “Water,” exhibition of more than 100 works of art that explore the theme of water across cultures, spanning over five centuries. Through Jan. 2. “Embodied Dreams: The Later Work of Boris Sveshnikov.” Through April 10. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum, 71 Hamilton Street. zimmerlimuseum.rutgers.edu; (732) 932-7237.

PRINCETON Bernstein Gallery “Women,” artwork by Judith K. Brodsky. Through Jan. 6. Mondays through Fridays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Bernstein Gallery, Robertson Hall. princeton.edu/bernstein; (609) 258-8880.

PRINCETON Paul Robeson Center for the Arts “Raw Beauty: An Exhibition of Sculpture,” featuring works by eight artists that communicate their thoughts and feelings about the female spirit. Jan. 8 through Feb. 26. “Terrace Project: John McDevitt,” steel sculptures by the artist, in the outdoor exhibition space of the Paul Robeson Center. Jan. 8 through May. 30. Paul Robeson Center for the Arts, 102 Witherspoon Street. (609) 924-8777; artscouncilofprinceton.org.

PRINCETON Princeton University Art Museum “Nobody’s Property: Art, Land, Space, 2000-2010.” Exhibition featuring the work of seven artists. Through Feb. 20. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 10 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Princeton University Art Museum, Nassau and Witherspoon Streets. artmuseum.princeton.edu; (609) 258-3788.

UNION Les Malamut Art Gallery “Ori and the Wizards,” exhibition of monochromatic photography by five photographers. Through Friday. Mondays through Thursdays, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Les Malamut Art Gallery, 1980 Morris Avenue. lesmalamutartgallery.web.officelive.com; (908) 851-5450.
