Calendar

Events in New Jersey

Published: December 10, 2010
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on around the state. Items may be submitted by mail to New Jersey Calendar, Metropolitan, The New York Times, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405; or by e-mail to njtowns@nytimes.com.

Comedy

HASBROUCK HEIGHTS Bananas Comedy Club Billy Garan. Friday through Dec. 18. Jackie Mason. Dec. 18 at 7 p.m. $37.50. Bananas Comedy Club, 283 Route 17 South.

bananascomedyclub.com; (201) 727-1090.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater Bill Engvall. Tuesday at 8 p.m. $29.50 to $75. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9000; countbasietheatre.org.

For Children

CRANFORD CDC Theater “Stuart Little,” musical adaptation of the E. B. White novel. Dec. 12. $12. CDC Theater, 78 Winans Avenue. cdctheatre.org; (908) 276-7611.

RED BANK Two River Theater “Charlotte’s Web,” featuring adults, children and puppets. Through Jan. 2. $24 to $43. Two River Theater, 21 Bridge Avenue. (732) 345-1400; trtc.org.

WAYNE Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University “Cinderella,” presented by the American Family Theater. Dec. 12 at 2 p.m. $9. Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University, 300 Pompton Road. (973) 720-2371; wpunj.edu/wplive/sheacenter.htm.

Music and Dance

EAST RUTHERFORD Izod Center Prince, funk-rock. Wednesday and Friday at 7:30 p.m. $20.10 to $159.50. Izod Center, 50 Route 120. izodcenter.com; (201) 507-8900.

MADISON Museum of Early Trades and Crafts “Celtic Harp Concert for a Winter’s Day.” Dec. 12 at 2 p.m. $10 and $15. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. (973) 377-2982; rosenet.org/metc.

MONTCLAIR Alexander Kasser Theater Beatrice Crawford Memorial Concert, featuring Montclair State University’s Wind Symphony and Symphonic Band. Dec. 12 at 3 p.m. Free. Alexander Kasser Theater, 1 Normal Avenue. (973) 655-5112; peakperfs.org.

MONTCLAIR Wellmont Theater From Good Homes, roots-rock band. Friday and Dec. 18 at 8 p.m. $28. Wellmont Theater, 5 Seymour Street. (973) 783-9500; wellmonttheatre.com.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center BeBe and CeCe Winans, performing traditional and contemporary gospel. Friday at 8 p.m. $25 to $95. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

NEWARK Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center “Fly: Five First Ladies of Dance,” featuring five dancer-choreographers performing signature solo works. Dec. 12 at 3 p.m. $36 and $39. Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “The Magnificent Mazowsze,” featuring Polish carols and dances. Dec. 12 at 3 and 7 p.m. $32 to $57. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center Liz Callaway, singer. Dec. 18 at 8 p.m. $46. “Bach’s Complete Brandenburgs,” featuring the Chamber Music Society of Lincoln Center. Dec. 20 at 7:30 p.m. $45. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

PRINCETON JUNCTION West Windsor Arts Center Bravo Wind Ensemble featuring Nicholas Masterson, oboe; William Amsel, clarinet; and Natalya Rose Vrbsky, bassoon, presented by the Princeton Symphony Orchestra. Dec. 12 at 3 p.m. $8 and $10. Pat DiNizio of the Smithereens and Sun Palace, pop rock. Dec. 18 at 8 p.m. $18 and $20. West Windsor Arts Center, 952 Alexander Road. (609) 716-1931; westwindsorartscenter.org.

SOUTH ORANGE South Orange Performing Arts Center The Juilliard Jazz Ensemble, featuring Alicia Olatuja, guest vocalist, and Ron Blake, saxophonist. Dec. 12 at 3 p.m. $10 to $15. McCoy Tyner Trio, jazz. Dec. 18 at 8 p.m. $38 to $58. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-2787; sopacnow.org.

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum “Classical Sundays at the Puffin,” featuring the young soloists of the Ansonia Trio, performing a program of Brahms, Bruch, Debussy, and Schumann. Dec. 19 at 4 p.m. $10. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. puffinculturalforum.org; (201) 836-3499.

TOMS RIVER Ocean County Library Mona’s Hot Four, jazz. Wednesday at 8 p.m. $13 and $15. Ocean County Library, 101 Washington Street. (732) 349-6200; theoceancountylibrary.org.

WEST WINDSOR Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College “El Sueño (The Dream),” featuring the Alborada Spanish Dance Theater. Dec. 21 at 7:30 p.m. $8 and $15. Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College, 1200 Old Trenton Road. kelseyatmccc.org; (609) 570-3333.

Seasonal

BEACH HAVEN Surflight Theater The Surflight Holiday Spectacular, performance featuring traditional holiday music. Through Dec. 19. $20 and $30. Surflight Theater, Engleside and Beach Avenues. (609) 492-9477; www.surflight.org.

BRANCHBURG Raritan Valley Community College “Winter Wonder Lights,” holiday laser concert. Through Dec. 19. $6 and $11. Raritan Valley Community College, Route 28 and Lamington Road in North Branch. raritanval.edu/planetarium; (908) 231-8805.

CAPE MAY Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities “An Old-Fashioned Christmas Exhibit,” holiday traditions through the years, with working LGB trains, trees, toys and more. Through Jan. 2. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. (609) 884-5404; capemaymac.org.

EAST BRUNSWICK Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center “A Christmas Carol,” musical based on the Charles Dickens tale. Through Dec. 19. $12. Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center, 715 Cranbury Road. (732) 254-3939; playhouse22.org.

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center Leahy Irish Family Christmas, featuring fiddle-driven music, dance and vocals. Dec. 19 at 7 p.m. $29 to $69. “Love for the Holidays,” featuring Darlene Love and Freda Payne. Dec. 22 at 8 p.m. $25 to $75. Charles Dickens’s “A Christmas Carol,” performed by Theatreworks USA. Dec. 23 at 7 p.m. $10 to $28. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

HACKETTSTOWN Centenary Stage Company “The Nutcracker,” presented by the New Jersey Civic Youth Ballet. Friday through Dec. 19. $20 to $30. Centenary Stage Company, 715 Grand Avenue. (908) 979-0900; centenarystageco.org.

HOBOKEN Monroe Center for the Arts “Happy Holidaze,” comedic theatrical program featuring Joe Mantello’s stage adaptation of David Sedaris’s “The Santaland Diaries” and “Season’s Greetings.” Through Dec. 19. $15 and $20. Monroe Center for the Arts, 720 Monroe Street. (201) 208-7809; monroecenter.com.

MAHWAH Berrie Center for Performing and Visual Arts “An Eileen Ivers Christmas,” featuring fiddling and step dancers. Dec. 12 at 7 p.m. $15 to $30. A New York Voices Holiday. Dec. 18 at 8 p.m. $15 to $26. Berrie Center for Performing and Visual Arts, 505 Ramapo Valley Road. (201) 684-7844; ramapo.edu/berriecenter/index.html.

MORRISTOWN Haggerty Education Center, Frelinghuysen Arboretum “Gingerbread Wonderland,” featuring elaborate handmade structures. Dec. 12. Open daily from 9:30 a.m. to 5 p.m., and Friday until 8 p.m. Frelinghuysen Arboretum, 53 East Hanover Avenue. (973) 326-7601.

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts Handel’s “Messiah,” performed by the Masterwork Chorus and Orchestra. Tuesday at 7:30 p.m. $30 to $85. New Jersey Ballet’s 40th Anniversary “Nutcracker.” Friday through Dec. 26. $19 to $59. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

MORRISTOWN Acorn Hall “Victorian Christmas at Acorn Hall,” featuring decorated period rooms, gift shop, special events, including “Festive Friday” evenings in December, and costumed guides. Through Jan. 16. $3 to $6. Acorn Hall, 68 Morris Avenue. www.acornhall.org; (973) 267-3465.

MORRISTOWN Macculloch Hall Historical Museum “ ‘Twas the Night Before Christmas,” exhibition featuring rare Christmas toys and decorations, and the museum’s collection of holiday-themed artwork by the caricaturist Thomas Nast. Through Jan. 31. $5 to $6; children ages 12 and under, free. Wednesdays, Thursdays and Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Macculloch Hall Historical Museum, 45 Macculloch Avenue. maccullochhall.org; (973) 538-2404.

NETCONG Palace Theatre “The Life and Adventures of Santa Claus,” by Stephen L. Fredericks, music and lyrics by Derek Dibbern and David Spellman, adapted from the book by L. Frank Baum. Through Dec. 19. $14 and $18. Palace Theatre, Route 183. (973) 347-4946.

NEWARK New Jersey Performing Arts Center “Kwanzaa Marketplace,” featuring local vendors. Friday through Dec. 19. Free. New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

NEWARK The Chase Room, New Jersey Performing Arts Center “A Tribute to the Elders,” a Kwanzaa Festival event honoring the educators Dorothy Anderson and Charles Nolley. Friday at 5 p.m. $32. “Kwanzaa Children’s Festival: Honoring a Heritage,” featuring hands-on workshops for adults and children in African dance and music, storytelling, capoeira, drumming and more. Dec. 18, noon to 5 p.m. Free. The Chase Room, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpa.org.

NEWARK Science Theater (Science Park High School) “21st Annual Holiday Party and Concert,” featuring the Greater Newark Youth Orchestra. Dec. 18 at 1:30 p.m. $10. Science Theater (Science Park High School), 260 Norfolk Street. www.nps.k12.nj.us; (973) 985-5881.

NEWARK Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center “Step Afrika! The Spirit of Kwanzaa,” featuring a performance of stepping, an art form born at African-American fraternities and based in African traditions. Dec. 18 at 2 and 5 p.m. $20 and $22. Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. njpac.org; (888) 466-5722.

NEWARK Trans-Siberian Orchestra, holiday concert. Dec. 18 at 3 and 8 p.m. $28 to $72. Prudential Center, 165 Mulberry Street. (973) 757-6000; prucenter.com.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center “Handel’s Messiah Rocks,” musical presentation combining classical music and classic rock. Dec. 19 at 3 p.m. $23 to $92. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. njpac.org; (888) 466-5722.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center “Holiday Express Benefit Concert,” with special guest Joe Piscopo. Dec. 21 at 7:30 p.m. $25 to $50. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “The Nutcracker,” presented by the American Repertory Ballet. Dec. 18 and 19. $32 to $57. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Kirkpatrick Chapel, Rutgers University “Christmas in Carol and Song,” featuring the Rutgers University Glee Club and the Kirkpatrick Choir. Dec. 12. $10 and $20. Kirkpatrick Chapel, Rutgers University, George and Somerset Streets. (732) 932-7808; kirkpatrickchapel.rutgers.edu.

PRINCETON Morven Museum and Garden “Festival of Trees” decorated by area businesses and groups. Through Jan. 9. $5 and $6. Morven Museum and Garden, 55 Stockton Street. historicmorven.org; (609) 924-8144.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater “The Nutcracker,” presented by the Company of Dance Arts. Dec. 12 at 2:30 and 7 p.m. $15 to $35. “Holiday Express Benefit Concert,” featuring musicians performing holiday selections. Wednesday and Thursday at 7:30 p.m. $25 to $100. Nancy Scharff’s “Christmas Is Love” Concert, featuring the Ecumenical Choir, Children’s Choir, Gospel Choir and other guests. Friday at 7:30 p.m. $15 to $25. “Glen Burtnik’s Xmas Xtravaganza: 20th Anniversary,” concert. Dec. 18 at 7:30 p.m. $20 to $45. Monmouth Civic Chorus presents Handel’s “Messiah” and more. Dec. 19 at 4 p.m. $25 and $45. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

UNION Liberty Hall Museum Breakfast with Santa, including a photo. Dec. 12., from 8 to 10 a.m. and 10:30 a.m. to 12:30 p.m. $20 and $25. Liberty Hall Museum, 1003 Morris Avenue. (908) 527-0400.

WEST LONG BRANCH Pollak Theater, Monmouth University “A Celtic Christmas,” concert by Cherish the Ladies. Dec. 18 at 8 p.m. $35 and $42. Pollak Theater, Monmouth University, 400 Cedar Avenue. (732) 263-6889; monmouth.edu/arts_events.

WEST ORANGE Luna Stage “Holiday Songs and Stories,” a program of stories and music hosted by the actor Frankie Faison. Dec. 18 at 2 p.m. $5. Luna Stage, 555 Valley Road. (973) 395-5551; lunastage.org.

WEST WINDSOR Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College “ ’Twas the Night Before Christmas,” musical adaptation of Clement Moore’s poem “A Visit From St. Nicholas.” Dec. 12. $8 and $10. Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College, 1200 Old Trenton Road. (609) 570-3333; kelseyatmccc.org.

Spoken Word

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center “Rosie and Shmuley Come Together.” The comedian and actress Rosie O’Donnell and Rabbi Shmuley Boteach discuss a range of topics, including raising children and living life in the eye of the news media. Tuesday at 8 p.m. $25 to $99. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

Theater

CAPE MAY Cape May Stage, The Robert Shackleton Playhouse “The Little Prince,” by Rick Cummins and John Scoullar, based on the book by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry. Through Dec. 31. $12.50 to $50. Cape May Stage, the Robert Shackleton Playhouse, Bank and Lafayette Streets. (609) 884-1341; capemaystage.com.

JERSEY CITY West Side Theater “Logomaniacs,” new play by Paul Fleischman. Through Dec. 19. $15 and $30. West Side Theater, 285 West Side Avenue. (201) 200-3260; ascnj.org.

LONG BRANCH New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater “The Tangled Skirt,” a world-premiere noir crime drama by Steve Braunstein. Through Jan. 23. $25 to $60. New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater, 179 Broadway. (732) 229-3166; njrep.org.

MADISON F.M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University “I Capture the Castle,” a stage adaptation of Dodie Smith’s 1948 coming-of-age novel. Through Jan. 2. $31 to $54. F. M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University, 36 Madison Avenue. (973) 408-5600; njshakespeare.org.

MILLBURN Paper Mill Playhouse “Les Misérables,” musical by Claude-Michel Schönberg based on the novel by Victor Hugo. Through Dec. 30. $25 to $84. Paper Mill Playhouse, 22 Brookside Drive. (973) 376-4343; papermill.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK George Street Playhouse “[title of show],” music and lyrics by Jeff Bowen; book by Hunter Bell. Dec. 12. $16.50 to $71.50. George Street Playhouse, 9 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7717; georgestplayhouse.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Mason Gross School of the Arts “An Evening of One Acts,” experimental theater created by students. Dec. 12. $5 to $15. Mason Gross School of the Arts, 33 Livingston Avenue. (732) 932-7511; mgsa.rutgers.edu.

WEST ORANGE Luna Stage “And Then I Wrote a Song About It,” one-man musical starring Nick Cearley, with book by Eric H. Weinberger, music by Daniel S. Acquisto and lyrics by Sammy Buck. Through Dec. 19. $20 to $30. Luna Stage, 555 Valley Road. (973) 395-5551; lunastage.org.

Museums and Galleries

CAMDEN Rutgers-Camden Center for the Arts “Southern New Jersey Artists Redux,” exhibition featuring works from 11 artists inspired by the region. Through Friday. Mondays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Thursdays to 8 p.m. The Stedman Gallery, Rutgers-Camden Center for the Arts, 311 North Third Street.

rutgerscamdenarts.org; (856) 225-6350.

CLINTON Hunterdon Museum of Art “Pamela Becker: Patterns and Constructs,” featuring textile constructions, wall pieces, baskets and photo collages. Through Jan. 16. $3. Tuesdays through Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Hunterdon Museum of Art, 7 Lower Center Street. (908) 735-8415; hunterdonartmuseum.org.

DEMAREST Mikhail Zakin Gallery “Frozen in Time,” exhibition showcasing the artwork of local and regional photographers documenting various landscapes of today. Through Jan. 7. Mondays through Fridays, 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. Mikhail Zakin Gallery, 561 Piermont Road. (201) 767-7160; tasoc.org.

HAMILTON Grounds for Sculpture “Fall/Winter Exhibition 2010,” group show. Through April 10. $8 to $12; members and children under 5, free. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. groundsforsculpture.org; (609) 586-0616.

MADISON Museum of Early Trades and Crafts “Lenape: Tools and Daily Living,” exhibition exploring Lenape life before European influences. Through Jan. 1. $3 and $5; members and children under 6, free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. rosenet.org/metc; (973) 377-2982.

MONTCLAIR BrassWorks on Grove “Structural Harmonies,” solo exhibition featuring paintings by Allan Gorman. Through Jan. 7. BrassWorks on Grove, 105 Grove Street. brassworksongrove.com; (973) 744-5100.

MONTCLAIR Montclair Art Museum “Engaging Nature: American and Native-American Artists (A.D. 1200-2004).” Includes prehistoric American Indian ceramics, 19th-century landscape paintings and contemporary staged photographs. Through Sept. 25. “Living for Art: The Dorothy and Herbert Vogel Collection,” works produced by 27 American artists from 1967 to 2000. Through Jan. 2. $12 adults, $10 seniors and students; members and children under 12, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Montclair Art Museum, 3 South Mountain Avenue. (973) 746-5555; montclairartmuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN Acorn Hall “Gowns: From Birth to Bedroom to Ballroom,” exhibition featuring a variety of “gowns” including christening gowns, ball gowns, wedding gowns, dressing gowns, and even nightgowns. Through Jan. 1. $6; seniors, $5; students, $3. Mondays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m.: Acorn Hall, 68 Morris Avenue. (973) 267-3465;

www.acornhall.org.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Morris Museum “The Legacy of Currier & Ives: Shaping the American Spirit,” an exhibition featuring 64 hand-colored lithographs from the collection of the Michele and Donald D’Amour Museum of Fine Arts in Springfield, Mass. Through Jan. 9. “The Art of Shirley Winthrope Rivo,” exhibition of photography and paintings. Through Feb. 13. “Frank H. Netter, M.D., Michelangelo of Medicine,” featuring more than 40 medical illustrations. Through Feb. 27. $7 to $10. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3700; morrismuseum.org.

NEWARK The Newark Museum “Modern Metal: Early-20th-Century American Sculpture,” bronze sculptures, including works by Gaston Lachaise, Hunt Diederich and Reuben Nakian. Through Dec. 31. “Red Luster: Lacquer and Leatherworks of Asia.” Through Dec. 31. “Gustav Stickley and the American Arts and Crafts Movement,” furniture, textiles and lighting. Through Jan. 2. $6 to $10; members, free. Wednesdays through Fridays, noon to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays through June, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. newarkmuseum.org; (973) 596-6550.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum “Water,” featuring more than 100 works about water. Through Jan. 2. “Animal Fair: Birds, Beasts and Bugs in Children’s Book Illustrations,” 28 original illustrations in various media and styles. Through June 5. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum, 71 Hamilton Street. (732) 932-7237; www.zimmerlimuseum.rutgers.edu.
