Calendar

Events in New Jersey

Published: December 17, 2010
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on around the state. Items may be submitted by mail to New Jersey Calendar, Metropolitan, The New York Times, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405; or by e-mail to njtowns@nytimes.com.

For Children

RED BANK Two River Theater “Charlotte’s Web,” featuring adults, children and puppets. Through Jan. 2. $24 to $43. Two River Theater, 21 Bridge Avenue.

(732) 345-1400; trtc.org.

JERSEY CITY Liberty Science Center “The 12 Days of Science,” featuring mental magicians, giant bubbles and live penguins. Wednesday through Jan. 2. Liberty Science Center, 222 Jersey City Boulevard. (201) 200-1000; lsc.org.

TENAFLY Tenafly Nature Center “Family Day Camp,” a mini-camp for families featuring guided hikes with a professional naturalist, lessons about what happens to animals in winter and stories about Native Americans. Dec. 28 and 29. $15 to $60. Tenafly Nature Center, 313 Hudson Avenue. (201) 568-6093; tenaflynaturecenter.org.

Music and Dance

MANASQUAN Algonquin Arts Center “The Magic of Motown,” featuring Souled Out, performing hits from the 1960s. Dec. 31 at 6 and 9 p.m. $30 to $50. Algonquin Arts Center, 173 Main Street. (732) 528-9211; algonquinarts.com.

MONTCLAIR Wellmont Theater Dark Star Orchestra. Dec. 28 at 8 p.m. $33. Passion Pit, pop band. Dec. 30 and Dec. 31. $43. Wellmont Theater, 5 Seymour Street. (973) 783-9500; wellmonttheatre.com.

MORRISTOWN Morristown Unitarian Fellowship Crowfoot, Canadian folk band. Dec. 30 at 8 p.m. $7. Morristown Unitarian Fellowship, 21 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 335-9489; folkproject.org.

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts Little Feat, Southern band. Dec. 30 at 8 p.m. $32 to $57. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Makeda The Tia Fuller Quartet, featuring Tia Fuller, saxophonist and member of Beyoncé’s “All-Girl Band.” Thursday, 7:30 to 10:30 p.m. Makeda, 338 George Street. (732) 545-5115.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “Salute to Vienna,” with the Strauss Symphony of America. Dec. 31 at 6 p.m. $47 to $97. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. statetheatrenj.org; (732) 246-7469.

NEWARK Prudential Center “Southern Invasion,” a hip-hop concert featuring Rick Ross, Gucci Mane and Waka Flocka Flame. Jan. 1 at 7 p.m. $29.50 to $250. Prudential Center, 165 Mulberry Street. (973) 757-6000; prucenter.com.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center “Video Games Live: Bonus Round,” concert featuring video-game music performed by the New Jersey Symphony Orchestra and accompanied by video-game imagery and lighting effects. Dec. 29 and Dec. 30. $21 to $82. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (973) 624-3713; njsymphony.org.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center “Bach’s Complete Brandenburgs,” featuring the Chamber Music Society of Lincoln Center. Monday at 7:30 p.m. $45. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum “Classical Sundays at the Puffin,” featuring the young soloists of the Ansonia Trio, performing a varied program of Brahms, Bruch, Debussy, and Schumann. Dec. 19 at 4 p.m. $10. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. puffinculturalforum.org; (201) 836-3499.

WEST WINDSOR Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College “El Sueño (The Dream),” featuring the Alborada Spanish Dance Theater. Tuesday at 7:30 p.m. $8 and $15. Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College, 1200 Old Trenton Road. (609) 570-3333; kelseyatmccc.org.

Outdoors

TENAFLY Tenafly Nature Center Full Moon Silent Hike, Tenafly Nature Center educators guide a tranquil, meditative hike to Haring’s Rock. Tuesday, 7 to 9 p.m. $5 and $10. Tenafly Nature Center, 313 Hudson Avenue. (201) 568-6093; tenaflynaturecenter.org.

Seasonal

BEACH HAVEN Surflight Theater The Surflight Holiday Spectacular, performance featuring traditional holiday music. Through Dec. 19. $20 and $30. Surflight Theater, Engleside and Beach Avenues. (609) 492-9477; www.surflight.org.

BRANCHBURG Raritan Valley Community College “Winter Wonder Lights,” holiday laser concert. Through Dec. 19. $6 and $11. Raritan Valley Community College, Route 28 and Lamington Road in North Branch. raritanval.edu/planetarium; (908) 231-8805.

CAPE MAY Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities “An Old-Fashioned Christmas Exhibit,” with working LGB trains, trees and toys. Through Jan. 2. “Ghosts of Christmas Past Trolley Rides.” A member of the East Lynne Theater Company tells Victorian holiday ghost tales as visitors ride through Cape May’s decorated Historic District. Jan. 1 at 8 and 9 p.m. $7 and $10. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. capemaymac.org; (609) 884-5404.

EAST BRUNSWICK Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center “A Christmas Carol,” musical based on the book by Charles Dickens. Through Dec. 19. $12. Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center, 715 Cranbury Road. playhouse22.org; (732) 254-3939.

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center Leahy Irish Family Christmas, featuring fiddle-driven music, dance and vocals. Dec. 19 at 7 p.m. $29 to $69. “Love for the Holidays,” featuring Darlene Love with special guest Freda Payne. Wednesday at 8 p.m. $25 to $75. Charles Dickens’s “A Christmas Carol,” performed by TheatreworksUSA. Thursday at 7 p.m. $10 to $28. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

GARFIELD Our Lady of Mount Virgin Church “O Great Mystery,” the story of Christmas in a cappella music and readings, performed by the Madrigal Singers and guest flutists Jenny Cline and Susan Graham. Dec. 19 at 4 p.m. $10 and $15. Our Lady of Mount Virgin Church, 188 MacArthur Avenue. (973) 772-2295; mountvirgin.com.

HACKETTSTOWN Centenary Stage Company The New Jersey Civic Youth Ballet presents “The Nutcracker.” Through Dec. 19. $20 to $30. Centenary Stage Company, 715 Grand Avenue. (908) 979-0900; centenarystageco.org.

HAMPTON Hunterdon Hills Playhouse “Christmas at the Playhouse,” traditional carols, classic and contemporary Yuletide compositions, skits and dance numbers. Through Wednesday. $56.50 to $69.50; dinner included. Hunterdon Hills Playhouse, 88 Route 173 West. hhplayhouse.com; (800) 447-7313.

HASBROUCK HEIGHTS Bananas Comedy Club “A New Year’s Eve Three-Act Comedy Show,” starring Joe Starr, Steve Marshall and Tom Van Horn. per his Web site. Dec. 31 at 8 and 11 p.m. $25 and $35, including Champagne toast and noisemakers. Bananas Comedy Club, 283 Route 17 South. bananascomedyclub.com; (201) 727-1090.

HOBOKEN Monroe Center for the Arts “Happy Holidaze, Featuring Santaland Diaries and Season’s Greetings,” by David Sedaris and adapted for the stage by Joe Mantello. Through Dec. 19. $15 and $20. Monroe Center for the Arts, 720 Monroe Street. monroecenter.com; (201) 208-7809.

LINCROFT Monmouth Museum “Go Green for the Holidays,” an annual exhibition with trees and trimmings decorated with ornaments hand-crafted using recycled materials. Through Jan. 3. Monmouth Museum, 765 Newman Springs Road. (732) 747-2266; monmouthmuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN Acorn Hall “Victorian Christmas at Acorn Hall,” featuring decorated period rooms, a gift shop and special events, including “Festive Friday” evenings in December and costumed guides. Through Jan. 16. $3 to $6. Acorn Hall, 68 Morris Avenue. (973) 267-3465; www.acornhall.org.

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts New Jersey Ballet’s 40th Anniversary “Nutcracker.” Through Dec. 26. $19 to $59. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

MORRISTOWN Macculloch Hall Historical Museum “ ’Twas the Night Before Christmas,” an exhibition featuring toys and decorations from private collections and the museum’s collection of holiday-themed artwork by the caricaturist Thomas Nast. Through Jan. 31. $5 to $6; children ages 12 and under, free. Wednesdays, Thursdays and Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m. Macculloch Hall Historical Museum, 45 Macculloch Avenue. (973) 538-2404; maccullochhall.org.

NETCONG Palace Theater “The Life and Adventures of Santa Claus,” by Stephen L. Fredericks, music and lyrics by Derek Dibbern and David Spellman, adapted from the book by L. Frank Baum. Through Dec. 19. $14 and $18. Palace Theater, Route 183. (973) 347-4946.

NEWARK New Jersey Performing Arts Center “Kwanzaa Marketplace,” featuring local vendors. Through Dec. 19. Free. New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center “Handel’s Messiah Rocks,” combining classical music with classic rock. Dec. 19 at 3 p.m. $23 to $92. “Holiday Express Benefit Concert,” with special guest Joe Piscopo. Tuesday at 7:30 p.m. $25 to $50. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

NEWARK The Newark Museum “Christmas in the Ballantine House: Feasting with Family and Friends,” featuring an installation that depicts 19th-century life and traditions. Through Jan. 2. “Holiday Family Fun Days: Celebrate Light!” — hands-on workshops for families, and tours of the Victorian Ballantine House decorated for Christmas. Dec. 27 through Dec. 29. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. (973) 596-6550; newarkmuseum.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “The Nutcracker,” featuring the American Repertory Ballet. Through Dec. 19. $32 to $57. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

PRINCETON Catch a Rising Star Comedy Club Rodney Laney. Dec. 31 at 8 and 10:30 p.m. $58; includes dinner and show. Catch a Rising Star Comedy Club, Hyatt Regency, 102 Carnegie Center. (609) 987-8018; catcharisingstar.com/princeton.html.

PRINCETON Morven Museum and Garden Monmouth Civic Chorus presents Handel’s “Messiah” and more. Dec. 19 at 4 p.m. $25 and $45. “Festival of Trees,” featuring holiday trees decorated by area businesses and organizations. Through Jan. 9. $5 and $6. Morven Museum and Garden, 55 Stockton Street. (609) 924-8144; historicmorven.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater “Black Nativity,” a gospel musical based on the Gospel of St. Luke, using the poetry of Langston Hughes and performed by an all-black cast. Dec. 30 at 7:30 p.m. $19.50 and $24.50. New Year’s Eve concert with Southside Johnny and the Asbury Jukes. Dec. 31 at 9 p.m. $29.50 to $99. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

RIVERHEAD Long Island Science Center Family Science: “Crystals and Snowflakes.” Create an edible crystal and make a snowflake craft to take home. Dec. 27 through Dec. 30. $5; adults free. Mondays and Thursdays through Sundays, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. Long Island Science Center, 11 West Main Street. (631) 208-8000; lisciencecenter.org.

SHREWSBURY Guild of Creative Art “All Member Holiday Show.” Through Jan. 5. Guild of Creative Art, 620 Broad Street. (732) 741-1441; guildofcreativeart.org.

SUSSEX The Crescent Theater Tri-State Actors Theater presents its annual production of Charles Dickens’s “A Christmas Carol,” adapted by Paul Meacham. Through Thursday. $18. The Crescent Theater, 74 Main Street. (973) 875-2950; tristateactorstheater.org.

WOODBRIDGE Barron Arts Center Model Train Exhibit, with more than 300 feet of track, a moving operating playground and three towns. Through Dec. 28. Barron Arts Center, 582 Rahway Avenue. (732) 634-0413.

Theater

CAPE MAY Cape May Stage, The Robert Shackleton Playhouse “The Little Prince,” by Rick Cummins and John Scoullar, based on the book by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry. Through Dec. 31. $12.50 to $50. Cape May Stage, the Robert Shackleton Playhouse, Bank and Lafayette Streets. (609) 884-1341; capemaystage.com.

JERSEY CITY West Side Theater “Logomaniacs,” new play by Paul Fleischman. Through Dec. 19. $15 and $30. West Side Theater, 285 West Side Avenue.

(201) 200-3260; ascnj.org.

LONG BRANCH New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater “The Tangled Skirt,” a world-premiere, theater-noir crime drama by Steve Braunstein. Through Jan. 23. $25 to $60. New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater, 179 Broadway. (732) 229-3166; njrep.org.

MADISON F.M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University “I Capture the Castle,” a stage adaption of Dodie Smith’s 1948 coming-of-age novel. Through Jan. 2. $31 to $54. F.M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University, 36 Madison Avenue. njshakespeare.org; (973) 408-5600.

MILLBURN Paper Mill Playhouse “Les Misérables,” musical by Claude-Michel Schönberg based on the novel by Victor Hugo. Through Dec. 30. $25 to $84. Paper Mill Playhouse, 22 Brookside Drive. (973) 376-4343; papermill.org.

WEST ORANGE Luna Stage “And Then I Wrote a Song About It,” one-man musical starring Nick Cearley, with book by Eric H. Weinberger, music by Daniel S. Acquisto and lyrics by Sammy Buck. Through Dec. 19. $20 to $30. Luna Stage, 555 Valley Road. (973) 395-5551; lunastage.org.

Museums and Galleries

CLINTON Hunterdon Museum of Art “Pamela Becker: Patterns and Constructs,” an exhibition featuring textile wall pieces, baskets and photo collages. Through Jan. 16. “John Ripton: Against the Wall,” photographs of urban scenes of walls covered with signs, graffiti, public notices and commercial icons. Through Feb. 13. Donation: $3. Tuesdays through Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Hunterdon Museum of Art, 7 Lower Center Street. (908) 735-8415;

hunterdonartmuseum.org.

CLINTON Red Mill Museum Village “The Stars and Stripes: Fabric of the American Spirit,” an exhibition of antique American flags. Through June 14. $8 general admission, $6 seniors, $5 children 6 to 12. Members and children under 6 free. Through Dec. 21. Red Mill Museum Village, 56 Main Street. (908) 735-4101, ext. 107; theredmill.org.

DEMAREST Mikhail Zakin Gallery “Frozen in Time,” exhibition showcasing the landscapes of local and regional photographers. Through Jan. 7. Mondays through Fridays, 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. Mikhail Zakin Gallery, 561 Piermont Road. (201) 767-7160; tasoc.org.

HAMILTON Grounds for Sculpture “Fall/Winter Exhibition 2010,” group show. Through April 10. $8 to $12; members and children under 5, free. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. groundsforsculpture.org; (609) 586-0616.

MADISON Museum of Early Trades and Crafts “Lenape: Tools and Daily Living,” exhibition exploring Lenape life before European influences. Through Jan. 1. $3 and $5; members and children under 6, free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. rosenet.org/metc; (973) 377-2982.

MONTCLAIR Montclair Art Museum “Engaging Nature: American and Native-American Artists (A.D. 1200-2004).” Includes prehistoric American Indian ceramics, historical 19th-century landscape paintings and contemporary staged photographs. Through Sept. 25. “Living for Art: The Dorothy and Herbert Vogel Collection,” works produced by 27 American artists from 1967 to 2000. Through Jan. 2. $12 adults, $10 seniors and students; members and children under 12, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Montclair Art Museum, 3 South Mountain Avenue. (973) 746-5555; montclairartmuseum.org.

MONTCLAIR BrassWorks on Grove “Structural Harmonies,” solo exhibition of paintings by Allan Gorman. Through Jan. 7. BrassWorks on Grove, 105 Grove Street. brassworksongrove.com; (973) 744-5100.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Morris Museum “The Legacy of Currier & Ives: Shaping the American Spirit,” an exhibition featuring 64 hand-colored lithographs from the collection of the Michele and Donald D’Amour Museum of Fine Arts in Springfield, Mass. Through Jan. 9. “The Art of Shirley Winthrope Rivo,” exhibition of photography and paintings. Through Feb. 13. “Frank H. Netter, M.D., Michelangelo of Medicine,” featuring more than 40 medical illustrations. Through Feb. 27. $7 to $10. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3700; morrismuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN Acorn Hall “Gowns: From Birth to Bedroom to Ballroom,” exhibition featuring a variety of “gowns,” including christening gowns, ball gowns, wedding gowns, dressing gowns, and even nightgowns. Through Jan. 1. $6; seniors, $5; students, $3. Mondays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 4 p.m.: Acorn Hall, 68 Morris Avenue. (973) 267-3465; www.acornhall.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum “Animal Fair: Birds, Beasts and Bugs in Children’s Book Illustrations,” an exhibition featuring 28 original illustrations in various media that explore the wide range of artistic styles and diverse interpretations of storybook animals. Through June 5. “Water,” exhibition of more than 100 works of art that explore the theme of water across cultures, spanning more than five centuries. Through Jan. 2. “Embodied Dreams: The Later Work of Boris Sveshnikov.” Through April 10. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum, 71 Hamilton Street. zimmerlimuseum.rutgers.edu; (732) 932-7237.

NEWARK The Newark Museum “Modern Metal: Early-20th-Century American Sculpture,” bronze sculptures, including works by Gaston Lachaise, Hunt Diederich and Reuben Nakian. Through Dec. 31. “Red Luster: Lacquer and Leatherworks of Asia.” Through Dec. 31. “Gustav Stickley and the American Arts and Crafts Movement,” furniture, textiles and lighting. Through Jan. 2. $6 to $10; members, free. Wednesdays through Fridays, noon to 5 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays through June, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. newarkmuseum.org; (973) 596-6550.

PRINCETON Bernstein Gallery “Women,” artwork by Judith K. Brodsky. Through Jan. 6. Mondays through Fridays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Bernstein Gallery, Robertson Hall. princeton.edu/bernstein; (609) 258-8880.

PRINCETON Princeton University Art Museum “Nobody’s Property: Art, Land, Space, 2000-2010,” exhibition featuring the work of seven artists. Through Feb. 20. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 10 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Princeton University Art Museum, Nassau and Witherspoon Streets. artmuseum.princeton.edu; (609) 258-3788.

UNION Les Malamut Art Gallery “Ori and the Wizards,” an exhibit of monochromatic photography by five photographers. Through Dec. 31. Mondays through Thursdays, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Fridays and Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Les Malamut Art Gallery, 1980 Morris Avenue. lesmalamutartgallery.web.officelive.com; (908) 851-5450.
