New York Region and New Jersey Home Tours

The Putnam County Secret Garden Tour has an optional tour of a Frank Lloyd Wright-designed house on Petra Island in Mahopac, N.Y.

Published: April 28, 2010
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

WESTCHESTER AND PUTNAM COUNTIES

Related

  • A Listing of Home Tours for 2010 (April 29, 2010)
Enlarge This Image
Nancy J. Leonard

A house tour in Westfield, N.J., on June 5 will include performances from the Westfield Symphony Orchestra.

Cold Spring, Garrison, Mahopac and Carmel, June 12 A tour of 10 to 12 gardens. The Putnam County Secret Garden Tour will take place from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m., to benefit Partners with PARC, a services organization for people with developmental disabilities. Tickets are $30 in advance by June 9 and $40 on the day of the tour. Information: (845) 278-7272, extension 287, and PartnerswithPARC.org. For the second consecutive year, there will also be a private guided tour of the Frank Lloyd Wright house on Petra Island in Mahopac. Tickets for this tour, which includes the garden tour, are $90 each and must be purchased by June 9.

Westchester, Sept. 25 to Oct. 31 A show house to benefit Cerebral Palsy of Westchester in a site yet to be determined will feature more than 25 designers. Open Tuesday through Saturday, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. (Thursday until 7 p.m. and Saturday until 5 p.m.); Sunday, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. No children under 6. Tickets are $30. Information: (914) 937-3800, extension 411, and cpwestchester.org.

DUTCHESS AND COLUMBIA COUNTIES

Rhinebeck , June 12 Three residences, including a converted church, and five local gardens will be open from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. to benefit the Chancellor Livingston Chapter of the Daughters of the American Revolution and the Museum of Rhinebeck History. The rain date is June 13. Tickets, including a continental breakfast from 9:30 to 10:30 a.m., are $25 at D.A.R. House, 77 Livingston Street (off Route 9). Advance tickets by June 8, $20, by check payable to C.L.C. D.A.R., care of Brenda Klaproth, 132 East Market Street, Rhinebeck, N.Y. 12572. Information: (845) 876-2436 or rhinebeckmuseum.com.

LaGrange, June 19 The LaGrange Historical Society tour, from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m., will feature 10 to 12 sites, including a Revolutionary War-era tavern. Tickets are $25 at Freedom Plains Presbyterian Church, Route 55 and Stringham Road. Information: (845) 453-5647 or by e-mail to lynn12540@juno.com or smann2@hvc.rr.com.

Columbia County, Oct. 16 The 22nd annual Country Seats Tour, featuring 9 to 12 historic houses, will take place 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. to benefit Hudson River Heritage. Information: (845) 876-2474 and hudsonriverheritage.org .

Ancram, Oct. 23 Six private houses will be open from noon to 4 p.m. in Ancram and the surrounding towns. A wine and cheese reception will follow the tour. Tickets are $40 ($50 with a reception) and are available starting at 11 a.m. at Ancram Town Hall, 1416 County Route 7 (a quarter-mile mile north of the intersection with Route 82). Tickets before Oct. 5 are $30 ($40 with a reception). Information: (518) 398-6435 or at ancrampreservationgroup.org .

ROCKLAND AND ORANGE COUNTIES

Nyack and Upper Nyack, May 1 A green house tour of seven renovated houses, some with gardens, will take place from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. and will benefit the Blue Rock School. Each site has an environmentally friendly element like solar paneling, a saltwater pool system or an organic garden. Tickets are $40 at 219 North Broadway (La Veta Place). Advance tickets are $35. Information: (845) 267-8268 and bluerockschool.org.

Warwick, June 5 The annual tour to benefit Warwick Valley Gardeners, with seven gardens and a plant sale, will take place 9 a.m. to 3 p.m. Rain date is June 6. Tickets, which are $15, will be available the day of the tour at the starting point, the Railroad Green, corner of Railroad Avenue and Main Street. Information: by e-mail to info@warwickvalleygardeners.com and at warwickvalleygardeners.com.

Goshen, June 19 The Gardens of Goshen Tour and Luncheon features six private gardens, including an English knot garden of boxwood and barberry, and will be open from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. The event will benefit St. James Episcopal Church where lunch will be served. Tickets, if available, are $40 on the day of the tour at the church, 1 St. James Place (at Church Street). Advance tickets are $35. Information: (845) 294-6225 or stjamesgoshen.org.

LONG ISLAND

Laurel Hollow, Huntington Bay, Huntington, Centerport and Lloyd Harbor, May 26 One of the first homes on Long Island to be awarded Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design certification will also be on the Sammis Family House Tour from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Proceeds will benefit the Family Service League of Long Island. No children under 12. Tickets are $55 in advance, or $60 on the day of the tour at the Thatched Cottage, 445 East Main Street (near Washington Avenue) in Centerport. Lunch, by reservation, is available for $25. Information and advance purchase: (631) 427-3700, extension 255, and fsl-li.org.

Sea Cliff, June 12 Several gardens throughout the one-square-mile village will be open for a tour from 10 a.m. to 3 p.m., and participants are invited to a garden tea party from 11:30 a.m. to 4 p.m. The rain date is June 13. Tickets are $25 at Pirie Park, 326 Sea Cliff Avenue (near Central Avenue). Information: (516) 671-4937 and at seacliff-ny.gov.

Southampton, June 12 and 13 The annual garden symposium and Landscape Pleasures tour sponsored by the Parrish Art Museum starts at 9 a.m. on June 12 with a symposium at the museum, 25 Jobs Lane. Four private gardens in the area will be open for a tour on June 13, from 10 a.m. to 3 p.m. Tickets to the symposium and tour are $175; those contributing at a higher level, from $300, are invited to a cocktail party on June 12. Tickets and information: (631) 283-2118, extension 42 and parrishart.org.

Sag Harbor, July 25 to Sept. 5 More than 25 designers will participate in the 10th annual Hampton Designer Showhouse to benefit Southampton Hospital, which will open July 24 with a gala preview (tickets are $225). The house, at 129 Stoney Hill Road in Sag Harbor, will be open daily from 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. No children under 6. Tickets are $30. Information: (212) 980-1711 or (631) 745-0004 and hamptondesignershowhouse.com.

NEW JERSEY

Morristown, May 1 to 31 Fawn Hill Farm, a 21-room stone manor will be the location for the 15th Mansion in May Designer Show House and Garden. Tickets at the door are $25 for seniors; general admission at the door and all tickets purchased in advance online are $30. Open Monday to Saturday, 10 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Sunday, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. Shuttle buses will run from the parking lot of the Atlantic Health Corporate Offices, 475 South Street (off Spring Valley Road) in Morristown. Information: (973) 971-8800 and mansioninmay.com.

Summit, May 6 The 38th annual house tour, which will benefit several local charities and an orphanage in Cameroon , will take place from 10 a.m. to 3 p.m. Tickets are $40 at the Parish Hall of Calvary Episcopal Church, 31 Woodland Avenue (a block north of Springfield Avenue). Information: (908) 277-1814 and calvary-summit.org.

South Orange, May 15 Five residences will be open from 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. in the Montrose Park Historic District, including a 1900 brick and stone house with a Flemish gable, leaded glass windows and stone balustrades on its porches, and a Queen Anne anchored by a three-story winding staircase, with a curved dining room, Beaux Arts stained-glass windows and three fireplaces on the first floor. Tickets are $35 at Mountain Station, corner of Montrose and Vose Avenues. Advance tickets are $30. Information: (973) 763-1880 and montroseparksonj.org.

Rumson and Red Bank, May 22 and 23 The Two Rivers Garden Tour will feature seven private gardens and the Grove West Organic Community Garden. The hours will be 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. on May 22, and 11:30 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. on May 23. Tickets are $30 and may be purchased at any of the properties along the tour and at the Grove shopping center on Broad Street (Route 35) in Shrewsbury. Advance tickets are $25. Information: (732) 923-6886 and tworivershow.org.

Skillman, until May 23 Woodacres, a 1937 Georgian house on nearly seven acres in Montgomery Township, will be the site of a show house to benefit the Junior League of Greater Princeton. Tickets purchased in advance, and seniors at all times, are $20. At the door, tickets will be $25. The house, at 1438 Great Road (County Road 601) near Inverness Drive, will be open Wednesday to Saturday, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. (Friday until 8 p.m.); Sunday, noon to 5 p.m. Information: (609) 771-0525 and jlgp.org.

Westfield, June 5 The 27th annual house tour to benefit the Westfield Symphony Orchestra will take place from 10 a.m. to 3 p.m. No children under 12. Tickets are $35 at Baron’s Drugs, 243 East Broad Street. Advance tickets are $30. Information: (908) 232-9400 and westfieldsymphony.org.

Hoboken, June 6 Ten gardens will be open for the Hoboken Secret Gardens Tour. The guided tour will leave Hoboken Historical Museum and Cultural Center every half-hour from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. Rain date is June 13. Tickets, which are $25, are to benefit the museum, at 1301 Hudson Street (13th Street); children under 13 free. Buying tickets in advance (for $20) is recommended: (201) 656-2240 and hobokensecretgardens.com.

Saddle River, until June 6 The Designer Show House of New Jersey will benefit the John Theurer Cancer Center at Hackensack University Medical Center. The house, at 4 High Meadow Road (off Eckert Farm Road), will be open Tuesday to Sunday, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Tickets are $30. Information: thedsnj.com.

Montclair, June 11 and 12 The From Roses to Rock Gardens tour will feature seven private gardens, open from 11 a.m. to 4 p.m., and the gardens at Van Vleck House and Gardens. Tickets, good for either day, are $30 at Van Vleck, 21 Van Vleck Street (Upper Mountain Avenue). Information: (973) 744-4752 and vanvleck.org.

Cape May , June 18 to Jan. 2 A three-story bed-and-breakfast will be the site of the 2010 Cape May Designer Show House. The house, which is presented by the Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, will be open daily from 1 to 4 p.m. and Sunday to Friday, 7 to 9 p.m. Tickets, at 115 Ocean Street (Hughes Street), are $10, and are $7 for children ages 3 to 12. Information: (609) 884-5404 and capemaymac.org.

Morris County, Sept. 18 and 19 The historical societies of Boonton, Boonton Township, Kinnelon, Butler and Montville Township will open their museums and historic houses in northeastern Morris County. The tour, which will be Sept. 18, from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. and Sept. 19, from noon to 4 p.m., will include the Henry Doremus House in Montville, the 1740 Miller-Kingsland House in Boonton and the Oscar A. Kincaid Home of History. The event is free. Participants may start at the Oscar Kincaid Museum, 591 Powerville Road (off Rockaway Valley Road), Boonton Township. Information: (973) 334-9563 and online closer to the event, at boonton.org.

Hoboken, Oct. 24 Several residences will be open from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. for a tour to benefit the Hoboken Historical Museum. Tickets are $30, at 1301 Hudson Street; children under 13 are free. Advance tickets are $25. Information: (201) 656-2240 and hobokenhousetour.com.

A version of this list appeared in print on April 29, 2010, on page D6 of the National edition.
