Events in Connecticut

Published: June 10, 2011
  • Sign In to E-Mail

  • Single Page
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on in and around Connecticut. Items for the guide should be sent at least three weeks in advance to ctcal@nytimes.com, or by mail to Connecticut Calendar, Metropolitan, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405.

Comedy

MASHANTUCKET Comix at Foxwoods Jake Johannsen. June 23 through June 25. $20 to $40. Comix at Foxwoods, 240 MGM Grand Drive. comixatfoxwoods.com; (866) 646-0609.

MASHANTUCKET Fox Theater, Foxwoods Casino Sinbad. Friday at 8 p.m. $30 to $60. Fox Theater, Foxwoods Casino, 240 MGM Grand Drive. comixatfoxwoods.com; (800) 200-2882.

NEW HAVEN Long Wharf Theater The Second City Comedy Revue, sketch and improvisation. Wednesday through June 19. $40. Long Wharf Theater, 222 Sargent Drive. (203) 787-4282; longwharf.org.

NEW HAVEN Shubert Theater Billy Gardell. June 18 at 8 p.m. $29.50. Shubert Theater, 247 College Street. shubert.com; (203) 624-1825.

RIDGEFIELD Ridgefield Playhouse Rita Rudner. June 24 at 8 p.m. $58. Ridgefield Playhouse, 80 East Ridge Road. (203) 438-5795; ridgefieldplayhouse.com.

Film

HARTFORD Cinestudio “The Four Times,” written and directed by Michelangelo Frammartino. Through Tuesday. $7 and $8. Cinestudio, 300 Summit Street. (860) 297-2463; cinestudio.org.

HARTFORD Real Art Ways “Blank City,” documentary featuring Steve Buschemi, Jim Jarmusch, John Waters and Lydia Lunch. Through Thursday. “Circus Dreams,” documentary by Signe Taylor. Friday through June 23. “Circo,” documentary by Aaron Schock. Friday through June 23. “The Topp Twins: Untouchable Girls,” documentary by Leanne Pooley. Friday through June 25. All are $4.50 to $9. Real Art Ways, 56 Arbor Street. (860) 232-1006; realartways.org.

NEW HAVEN New Haven Public Library “Kuhle Wampe,” directed by Slatan Dudow. June 20 at 6 p.m. Free. New Haven Public Library, 133 Elm Street. cityofnewhaven.com/

library; (203) 946-8130.

OLD SAYBROOK The Katharine Hepburn Cultural Arts Center The Met Live in HD: Puccini’s “Madama Butterfly,” encore screening of the opera. Wednesday at 1 p.m. $28. “Pat and Mike,” starring Katharine Hepburn and Spencer Tracy. Thursday at 2, 4 and 7 p.m. $8. “The Connecticut Film Festival.” June 23 through June 26. $12.50 to $25. The Katharine Hepburn Cultural Arts Center, 300 Main Street. katharinehepburntheater.org; (877) 503-1286.

For Children

GLASTONBURY Connecticut Audubon Society Center at Glastonbury “Pee Wees Series 2: Spring Fever,” nature activities in Earle Park for two age groups on Tuesday. Ages 1 to 3 at 10 a.m., must be accompanied by an adult, and ages 3 to 5 at 1 p.m. $10 and $13. Connecticut Audubon Society Center at Glastonbury, 1361 Main Street. (860) 633-8402; ctaudubon.org.

NEW HAVEN International Festival of Arts and Ideas “International Festival of Arts and Ideas,” including the historical drama “Two for Freedom,” the Bossy Frog Band, a children’s film festival and more. Through June 25. Call for ticket prices. Some events are free. Festival passes are $385. International Festival of Arts and Ideas, various locations. (888) 736-2663; artidea.org.

NEW HAVEN Peabody Museum of Natural History, Yale University “Invasion of the Bloodsuckers: Bedbugs and Beyond,” exhibition featuring living specimens, films and models. All ages. Through Jan. 8. $5 to $9. Mondays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Peabody Museum of Natural History, Yale University, 170 Whitney Avenue. (203) 432-5050; yale.edu/peabody.

NORWALK Maritime Aquarium “Dinosaur Summer,” an exhibition of life-size robotic dinosaurs. All ages. June 18 through Aug. 31. $9.95 and $12.95. Maritime Aquarium, 10 North Water Street. (203) 852-0700; maritimeaquarium.org.

STORRS Connecticut Repertory Theater “Seussical the Musical,” by Stephen Flaherty and Lynn Ahrens, based on the works of Dr. Seuss. Ages 3 and up. Thursday through June 26. $10 to $39. Connecticut Repertory Theater, 802 Bolton Road. (860) 486-4226; crt.uconn.edu.

WEST HARTFORD Playhouse on Park “You’re A Good Man, Charlie Brown,” musical based on the works of Charles M. Schulz. Ages 3 and up. Wednesday through June 26. $10 to $32.50. Playhouse on Park, 244 Park Road. (860) 523-5900, ext. 10; playhouseonpark.org.

WINDSOR Bart’s Drive In String Theorie and the Rude Mechanicals, folk and rock. Bring your own blankets and lawn chairs. All ages. June 12, 2 to 6 p.m. $25; under12, free. Bart’s Drive In, 55 Palisado Avenue. hitw.org; (860) 614-5795.
