Events in New Jersey

Published: April 15, 2011
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

Comedy

HASBROUCK HEIGHTS Bananas Comedy Club Angel Salazar. Friday and April 23. $15 and $20. Bananas Comedy Club, 283 Route 17 South. bananascomedyclub.com; (201) 727-1090.

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts Wanda Sykes. April 23 at 8 p.m. $57 to $97. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater Dennis Miller. April 29 at 8 p.m. $28 to $98. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater Brian Regan. April 30 at 7 and 9:30 p.m. $29.50 to $49.50. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. countbasietheatre.org; (732) 842-9000.

Film

MONTCLAIR Montclair Art Museum “Who Killed the Electric Car?,” documentary film directed by Chris Paine. Thursday at 7 p.m. Free. Montclair Art Museum, 3 South Mountain Avenue. (973) 746-5555; montclairartmuseum.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater “Tombstone,” directed by George P. Cosmatos. April 29 at 7 p.m. Free. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9000; countbasietheatre.org.

For Children

HOBOKEN Monroe Center for the Arts “The Garden of Rikki Tikki Tavi,” presented by the Mile Square Theater. April 17 at 11 a.m. and 2 p.m. $12 and $20. Monroe Center for the Arts, 720 Monroe Street. milesquaretheatre.org; (201) 683-7014.

MADISON Museum of Early Trades and Crafts “Smart Start.” Children and their parents participate in projects and story time. Through April 25. $3. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. (973) 377-2982; rosenet.org/metc.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Morris Museum Super Science Saturdays, workshops. Ages 5 and up. Through April 30. $3; members, free. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. morrismuseum.org; (973) 971-3700.

NETCONG Palace Theater “James and the Giant Peach,” by Richard R. George, adapted from the book by Roald Dahl. April 17 at 4 p.m. $14 and $18. Palace Theater, Route 183. (973) 347-4946.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater Sesame Street Live: “Elmo’s Green Thumb.” Thursday through April 24. $14 to $40. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

RAHWAY Union County Performing Arts Center “Sleeping Beauty,” featuring the New Jersey Ballet. May 1 at 3 p.m. $15 and $22. Union County Performing Arts Center, 1601 Irving Street. (732) 499-8226; ucpac.org.

SOUTH ORANGE South Orange Performing Arts Center “Butterfly: The Story of a Life Cycle,” presented by the Hudson Vagabond Puppets. May 1 at 2 p.m. $20. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-2787; sopacnow.org.

Music and Dance

BLOOMFIELD Robert Van Fossan Theater, Westminster Arts Center Mosaic Dance Theater Company. April 29 and 30. $15 and $20. Robert Van Fossan Theater, Westminster Arts Center, corner of Franklin and Fremont Streets. campus.bloomfield.edu/Westminster; (973) 748-9008.

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center “Aida,” presented by Verismo Opera. April 17 at 3 p.m. $25 to $55. Nancy Wilson, jazz/blues singer. Thursday at 8 p.m. $39 to $89. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

GLEN RIDGE Glen Ridge Congregational Church “Passion Music,” with the Glen Ridge Choral Society and Orchestra performing “Stabat Mater” by Gioachino Rossini and “Requiem” by John Rutter. April 17 at 3 p.m. $10 to $20. Glen Ridge Congregational Church, 195 Ridgewood Avenue. glenridgecong.org/music; (973) 743-5596.

HILLSIDE Enlow Recital Hall at Kean University “Celebrating Heitor Villa-Lobos: Martha Marchena in Recital.” Wednesday at 8 p.m. $25. Ars Vitalis, contemporary chamber music. April 28 at 8 p.m. $25. Enlow Recital Hall at Kean University, 215 North Avenue. (908) 737-7469; enlowhall.kean.edu.

JERSEY CITY Margaret Williams Theater, Hepburn Hall John Pizzarelli and the New Jersey City University Jazz Ensemble, a jazz performance to benefit the NJCU Jazz and Endowed Scholarship Funds. Monday at 7:30 p.m. $15 to $100. Margaret Williams Theater, Hepburn Hall, 2039 Kennedy Boulevard. (201) 200-3280; web.njcu.edu/dept/mwt.

MONTCLAIR Wellmont Theater Snoop Dogg, hip-hop. April 28 at 8 p.m. $35 and $40. Wellmont Theater, 5 Seymour Street. (973) 783-9500; wellmonttheatre.com.

NEW BRUNSWICK Rutgers New Jersey Folk Festival. April 30, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Free. Rutgers University, George Street and Nichol Avenue. (732) 932-9500.

NEW BRUNSWICK Victoria J. Mastrobuono Theater “DancePlus Spring,” modern dance performed by students at Mason Gross School of the Arts. Thursday through April 30. $15 to $25. Victoria J. Mastrobuono Theater, Mason Gross Performing Arts Center, 85 George Street. masongross.rutgers.edu; (732) 932-7511.

NEWARK Prudential Center Lady Gaga in the Monster Ball Tour. Friday at 8 p.m. $52.50 to $178. Prudential Center, 165 Mulberry Street. prucenter.com; (973) 757-6000.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center Max Raabe and Palast Orchester. Tuesday at 8 p.m. $43 to $56. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. mccarter.org; (609) 258-2787.

RAHWAY Union County Performing Arts Center New Jersey Ballet’s “Swan Lake Act II.” April 30 at 8 p.m. $20 to $40. Union County Performing Arts Center, 1601 Irving Street. (732) 499-8226; ucpac.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater Harry Connick, Jr. and Orchestra. Wednesday and Thursday at 8 p.m. $50 to $145. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9000; countbasietheatre.org.

SOUTH ORANGE South Orange Performing Arts Center The Rodriguez Brothers, traditional Latin jazz. Tuesday at 7:30 p.m. $8 to $25. Angélique Kidjo, West African music. April 26 at 7 p.m. $38 and $48. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-2787; sopacnow.org.

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum “Classical Sundays at the Puffin,” with the harp soloist Suzanna Klintcharova performing works by Glinka, Saint-Saëns, Prokofiev, and others, and a piece for harp and electronics. April 17 at 4 p.m. $10. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. puffinculturalforum.org; (201) 836-3499.

Outdoors

HAMILTON Grounds for Sculpture “Moonlight Tour,” docent-led tours. Thursday at 9 p.m. $15 and $20. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. (609) 586-0616; groundsforsculpture.org.

Spoken Word

MORRISTOWN The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts Kevin Smith, director and producer, will host a Q & A session on the entertainment industry. April 29 at 8 p.m. $42 to $89. The Community Theater, Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

PRINCETON Richardson Auditorium, Princeton University “Princeton Poetry Festival,” a series of readings and discussions. April 29 and 30. $10 to $25. Richardson Auditorium, Princeton University, Nassau Street. (609) 497-0020; princeton.edu.

Theater

ATLANTIC HIGHLANDS First Avenue Playhouse “Love, Sex and the I.R.S.,” comedy by Billy Van Zandt and Jane Milmore. Through April 30. $15 and $20. First Avenue Playhouse, 123 First Avenue. firstavenueplayhouse.com; (732) 291-7552.

CAPE MAY Emlen Physick Estate “Murder at the Physick Estate,” mystery by Ray Crew. Through April 30. $20 and $25. Emlen Physick Estate, 1048 Washington Street. capemaymac.org; (800) 275-4278.

EAST BRUNSWICK Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center “The Merchant of Venice,” by William Shakespeare. Through May 8. $20 and $22. Playhouse 22, East Brunswick Cultural Arts Center, 715 Cranbury Road. playhouse22.org; (732) 254-3939.

LONG BRANCH New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater “Night Train,” drama by John Biguenet. April 28 through May 29. $25 to $40. New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater, 179 Broadway. (732) 229-3166; njrep.org.

MIDDLETOWN Navesink Library Theater “Rumors,” by Neil Simon. Through April 30. $17; students and seniors, $14. Navesink Library Theater, Sears and Monmouth Avenues. monmouthplayers.org; (732) 291-9211.

MILLBURN Paper Mill Playhouse “Curtains,” musical mystery-comedy by John Kander and Fred Ebb. April 27 through May 22. $31 to $91. Paper Mill Playhouse, 22 Brookside Drive. papermill.org; (973) 376-4343.

NEW BRUNSWICK Crossroads Theater “A Raisin in the Sun,” drama by Lorraine Hansberry. Through May 1. $15 to $50. Crossroads Theater, 7 Livingston Avenue. crossroadstheatrecompany.org; (732) 545-8100.

NEW BRUNSWICK George Street Playhouse “The Fox on the Fairway,” by Ken Ludwig. Through April 23. $26.50 to $63.50. George Street Playhouse, 9 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7717; gsponline.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Philip J. Levin Theater “Fires in the Mirror,” drama by Anna Deavere Smith. Through April 30. $15 to $25. Philip J. Levin Theater, 85 George Street. mgsa.rutgers.edu; (732) 932-7511.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “Lovers and Other Strangers,” romantic comedy starring Renée Taylor and Joe Bologna. May 1 at 3 p.m. $22 to $57. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

NUTLEY Nutley Little Theater “Take Me Out,” drama by Richard Greenberg. Through April 23. $15. Nutley Little Theater, 47 Erie Place. nutleylittletheatre.com; (973) 667-0374.

PARSIPPANY Parsippany Playhouse The Women’s Theater Company presents “Enchanted April,” a romantic comedy by Matthew Barber, based on the book by Elizabeth Von Amim. Through April 23. $18. Parsippany Playhouse, 1130 Knoll Road. womenstheater.org; (973) 316-3033.

PRINCETON McCarter Center “Sleeping Beauty Wakes,” new musical with book by Rachel Sheinkin, music by Brendan Milburn and lyrics by Valerie Vigoda. April 29 through June 5. $20 to $70. McCarter Center, 91 University Place. (609) 258-2787; mccarter.org.

RAHWAY Union County Performing Arts Center “The Music Man,” a musical with book, music and lyrics by Meredith Willson. April 28 at 8 p.m. $32 to $60. Union County Performing Arts Center, 1601 Irving Street. (732) 499-8226; ucpac.org.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater “Annie,” presented by Phoenix Productions. April 17 at 3 p.m. $22 to $29. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9000; countbasietheatre.org.

SOUTH ORANGE South Orange Performing Arts Center “The Gospel According to Thomas Jefferson, Charles Dickens and Count Leo Tolstoy,” by Scott Carter. Friday at 8 p.m. and April 23 at 2 p.m. Free. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. (973) 313-2787; sopacnow.org.

TEANECK Becton Theater, Fairleigh Dickinson University “The Bald Soprano,” comedy by Eugène Ionesco. Thursday through May 8. $32 and $37. Becton Theater, Fairleigh Dickinson University, 960 River Road. (201) 569-7710; garagetheatre.org.

WEST ORANGE Luna Stage “The Tallest Building in the World,” by Matt Schatz. Through May 15. $20 to $30. Luna Stage, 555 Valley Road. (973) 395-5551; lunastage.org.

WEST WINDSOR Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College “Hello, Dolly!” Tony Award-winning musical comedy based on a 1938 farce by Thornton Wilder, presented by Playful Theater Productions. April 17 at 2 p.m. $12 to $16. Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College, 1200 Old Trenton Road. www.kelseyatmccc.org; (609) 570-3333.

Museums and Galleries

CAPE MAY Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities “Can I Get a Witness! A Testament to the Black Church in Cape May County,” photographs. April 17. Mid-Atlantic Center for the Arts and Humanities, 1048 Washington Street. (609) 884-5404; capemaymac.org.

CLINTON Hunterdon Art Museum “Next Frontier: The Land and the Night Sky, Photographs by Edward Fausty.” Through June 12. Donation: $5. Tuesdays through Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Hunterdon Art Museum, 7 Lower Center Street. hunterdonartmuseum.org; (908) 735-8415.

CLINTON Red Mill Museum Village “The Stars and Stripes: Fabric of the American Spirit,” exhibition of antique American flags. Through June 14. $8; seniors, $6; children 6 to 12, $5; members and children under 6, free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Red Mill Museum Village, 56 Main Street. (908) 735-4101, ext. 107; theredmill.org.

CLOSTER Belskie Museum “The Art of the Kitchen,” art and artifacts from 20 artists and collectors. April 17 through May 8. Belskie Museum, 280 High Street. belskiemuseum.com; (201) 768-0286.

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center “Pages,” exhibition of photo collages by Irmari Nacht. Through April 28. Intermezzo Art Gallery, Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

HALEDON American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark “The Line That Divides: Nafta Trade Corridor,” exhibition of photos, paintings and video by Pamela Calore. Through April 23. $3 to $5, suggested donation; members and children under 12, free. Wednesdays through Saturdays, 1 to 4 p.m., or by appointment. American Labor Museum/Botto House National Landmark, 83 Norwood Street. (973) 595-7953; labormuseum.org.

HAMILTON Grounds for Sculpture “Spring/Summer Exhibition 2011.” May 1 through Sept. 25. $8 to $12; members and children under 5, free. Tuesdays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Grounds for Sculpture, 18 Fairgrounds Road. (609) 586-0616; groundsforsculpture.org.

HOPEWELL Morpeth Contemporary “Hetty Baiz: Wildlife,” an exhibition of animal paintings in mixed media by Hetty Baiz. Through May 8. Morpeth Contemporary, 43 West Broad Street. morpethcontemporary.com; (609) 333-9393.

LINCROFT Monmouth Museum “Give Peace a Chance: John Lennon and Yoko Ono’s Bed-In for Peace Exhibition,” featuring more than 40 large-format images by Gerry Deiter, photojournalist. Through May 8. $7; members and children under 2, free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Monmouth Museum, 765 Newman Springs Road. (732) 747-2266; monmouthmuseum.org.

MADISON Museum of Early Trades and Crafts “Mariners, Merchants and Pirates,” exhibition exploring early American seafaring techniques, tools and methods and the nautical navigation system of the time. Through Sept. 4. $3 and $5; members and children under 6, free. Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.; Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, 9 Main Street. (973) 377-2982; rosenet.org/metc.

MONTCLAIR Montclair Art Museum “Engaging Nature: American and Native American Artists (A.D. 1200-2004),” art and artifacts, including prehistoric American Indian ceramics, 19th-century landscape paintings and contemporary staged photographs. Through Sept. 25. “Warhol and Cars: American Icons,” showcasing Warhol’s fascination with cars as products of American consumer society. Through June 19. $12; seniors and students, $10; members and children under 12, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Montclair Art Museum, 3 South Mountain Avenue. (973) 746-5555; montclairartmuseum.org.

MONTCLAIR Studio Montclair “Sustainability and the Artistic Vision,” group show featuring artists who use sustainable materials. Through April 29. “Rendering Green,” featuring artworks by 18 Studio Montclair members. Through April 29. Mondays through Fridays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m., or by appointment. Studio Montclair, 33 Plymouth Street. (973) 744-1818; studiomontclair.org.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Morris Museum “On the Head and in the Hand: 200 Years of Hats and Purses From the Morris Museum Collection.” Through Sept. 25. $7 to $10. Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 11 a.m. to 8 p.m.; and Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3700; morrismuseum.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum “Animal Fair: Birds, Beasts and Bugs in Children’s Book Illustrations,” 28 original works of storybook animals, in various media and styles. Through June 5. “Dancing With the Dark: Joan Snyder Prints 1963-2010.” Through May 29. Tuesdays through Fridays, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum, 71 Hamilton Street. (732) 932-7237; zimmerlimuseum.rutgers.edu.

NEW BRUNSWICK Rutgers University, Douglass Library Mary H. Dana Women Artists Series 40th Anniversary Exhibition: “Joan Snyder/Intimate Works,” paintings by Joan Snyder from 1965 to 2010. Through June 5. Mondays through Thursdays, 8 a.m. to midnight; Fridays, 8 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Sundays, noon to midnight. Rutgers University, Douglass Library Galleries, 8 Chapel Drive. (732) 932-3726, ext. 15; libraries.rutgers.edu.

NEWARK Newark Museum “Posing Beauty: African American Images From the 1890s to the Present,” photographs. Through April 28. “Skies Alive: Bird Migration in the Garden State,” photographs and interactive computer programs. Through June 1. “Tsongkhapa: The Life of a Tibetan Visionary,” featuring a set of 15 biographical narrative paintings. Through Dec. 31. $6 to $10; members, free. Wednesdays through Sundays, noon to 5 p.m. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. (973) 596-6550; newarkmuseum.org.

NEWARK Newark Public Library “The Ironbound Series,” works in photography and mixed-media assemblage. Through April 29. Mondays, Fridays and Saturdays, 9 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.; Tuesdays through Thursdays, 9 a.m. to 8:30 p.m. Newark Public Library, 5 Washington Street. (973) 733-7784; npl.org.

PRINCETON Historical Society of Princeton “Einstein at Home,” a glimpse of the scientist’s personal side. Through Jan. 16, 2012. Tuesdays through Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Historical Society of Princeton, 158 Nassau Street. princetonhistory.org; (609) 921-6748.

PRINCETON Morven Museum and Garden “Ribbon of Life: The Delaware and Raritan Canal,” 19th- and 20th-century postcard views of the canal. Through June 19. $6; seniors and students, $5. Wednesdays through Fridays, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Morven Museum and Garden, 55 Stockton Street. (609) 924-8144; morven.org.

PRINCETON Paul Robeson Center for the Arts “Terrace Project: John McDevitt,” steel sculptures in the outdoor exhibition space of the Paul Robeson Center for the Arts. Through May 30. 102 Witherspoon Street. artscouncilofprinceton.org; (609) 924-8777.

PRINCETON Princeton University Art Museum “The Tokaido Road: 19th- and 20th-Century Journeys Through Japanese Prints.” Through June 5. “Lasting Impressions of the Grand Tour: Giuseppe Vasi’s Rome.” Through June 12. “When Men and Mountains Meet: China as Land and People.” Through June 26. Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Thursdays, 10 a.m. to 10 p.m.; Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m. Princeton University Art Museum, Nassau and Witherspoon Streets. artmuseum.princeton.edu; (609) 258-3788.

RAHWAY Arts Guild of New Jersey “Crime Story,” an exhibition of artwork inspired by crimes, curated by Lawrence Cappiello. May 1 through June 2. Arts Guild of New Jersey, 1670 Irving Street. (732) 381-7511; agnj.org.

SUMMIT Reeves-Reed Arboretum “Impressions of a Garden,” works by the contemporary American Impressionist painter Lisa Palombo. Through May 8. Reeves-Reed Arboretum, 165 Hobart Avenue. reeves-reedarboretum.org; (908) 273-8787.

SUMMIT Visual Arts Center of New Jersey “Watercolors,” featuring works by Angelito L. David. Through June 3. “Re:Design,” an exhibition featuring 14 emerging designers who repurpose or reuse discarded objects in creative and unexpected ways. Through June 5. Visual Arts Center of New Jersey, 68 Elm Street. (908) 273-9121; artcenternj.org.
