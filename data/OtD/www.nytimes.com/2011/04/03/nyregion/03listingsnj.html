Calendar

Events in New Jersey

Published: April 1, 2011
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Single Page
  • Reprints

A guide to cultural and recreational goings-on around the state this week. Items may be submitted by mail to On the Towns, Sunday New Jersey Section, The New York Times, 620 Eighth Avenue, New York, N.Y. 10018-1405; or by e-mail to njtowns@nytimes.com.

Comedy

DOVER Dover Little Theater “Out of Order,” improv comedy. April 9 at 8 p.m. $10. Dover Little Theater, Elliott Street. (973) 328-9202; doverlittletheatre.org.

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center Popovich Comedy Pet Theater. Friday at 7 p.m. $15 to $35. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

JERSEY CITY Michael Anthony’s on the Waterfront “Gilbert Gottfried Live and Uncensored!” April 14 at 7:30 p.m. $27.50 and $32.50. Michael Anthony’s on the Waterfront, 502 Washington Boulevard. (201) 798-1798; catcharisingstar.com.

Film

RAHWAY Union County Performing Arts Center “The New Jersey Short Film Concert,” an evening of international award-winning short films from the past and present. April 9 at 8 p.m. $15. Union County Performing Arts Center, 1601 Irving Street. (732) 499-8226; ucpac.org.

For Children

HOBOKEN Monroe Center for the Arts “The Garden of Rikki Tikki Tavi,” children’s performance presented by the Mile Square Theater. Through April 17. $12 and $20. Monroe Center for the Arts, 720 Monroe Street. milesquaretheatre.org; (201) 683-7014.

MORRIS TOWNSHIP Morris Museum “Super Science Saturdays,” workshops addressing the scientific method. Ages 5 and up. April 9 through April 30. $3; members, free. Morris Museum, 6 Normandy Heights Road. (973) 971-3700; morrismuseum.org.

MORRISTOWN Mayo Center for the Performing Arts “Winnie the Pooh,” presented by Sign Stage on Tour. Ages 4 to 10. April 10 at 1:30 and 4 p.m. $12. Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “Songs and Stories From Near and Far,” with Jim Rohe. April 9 at 10 a.m. and noon. Free. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. statetheatrenj.org; (732) 246-7469.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater “Imagination Movers Live! In a Big Warehouse Tour,” featuring songs, stories, skits and games. April 12 at 4 and 7 p.m. $19.50 to $39.50. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9000; countbasietheatre.org.

WAYNE Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University Jungle Joe’s Wildlife Adventures, popular and exotic animals. April 3 at 2 p.m. Preshow activities begin at 1:15 p.m. $10. Shea Center for Performing Arts, William Paterson University, 300 Pompton Road. wpunj.edu/wplive/sheacenter.htm; (973) 720-2371.

Music and Dance

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center “Feet on the Ground,” presented by Viver Brasil, featuring Afro-Brazilian traditional and contemporary dance. April 15 at 8 p.m. $25 to $59. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

MONTCLAIR Alexander Kasser Theater Dance Works 2011, modern dance performance. Wednesday through April 10. $15. Alexander Kasser Theater, 1 Normal Avenue. peakperfs.org; (973) 655-5112.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center Nacho Duato Dance Company. April 12 at 8 p.m. $42 to $56. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. mccarter.org; (609) 258-2787.

ENGLEWOOD Bergen Performing Arts Center Itzhak Perlman, violinist. April 3 at 3 p.m. $39 to $150. “Doo Wop, Love Songs and Memories.” April 9 at 8 p.m. $35 to $65. “In the Mood,” 1940s big band revue. April 13 at 3 and 8 p.m. $29 to $59. “Aida,” presented by Verismo Opera. April 17 at 3 p.m. $25 to $55. Bergen Performing Arts Center, 30 North Van Brunt Street. (201) 227-1030; bergenpac.org.

FLANDERS Crossroads Community Church Kathy Phillips, folk, pop, rock. April 9, 8 to 10 p.m. Donations accepted. Crossroads Community Church, 104 Bartley Road. (973) 584-7149; crossroadsmtolive.org.

GLEN RIDGE Glen Ridge Congregational Church “Passion Music,” with the Glen Ridge Choral Society and Orchestra performing “Stabat Mater” by Gioachino Rossini and “Requiem” by John Rutter. April 17 at 3 p.m. $10 to $20. Glen Ridge Congregational Church, 195 Ridgewood Avenue. glenridgecong.org/music; (973) 743-5596.

HILLSIDE Enlow Recital Hall at Kean University The Arco Ensemble performs Concerto for Guitar and String Orchestra, by Matthew Halper. April 9 at 8 p.m. $25. Enlow Recital Hall at Kean University, 215 North Avenue. enlowhall.kean.edu; (908) 737-7469.

MILLBURN St. Stephen’s Church Beckerath Organ Recital, by William J. Stokes, performing the music of Buxtehude, Bach, Liszt and others. April 10 at 4 p.m. $10. St. Stephen’s Church, 119 Main Street. ststephensmillburn.org; (973) 376-0688.

MORRISTOWN Mayo Center for the Performing Arts The Dukes of Dixieland. Thursday at 8 p.m. $47. Itzhak Perlman, violin. April 9 at 8 p.m. $75 to $125. Mayo Center for the Performing Arts, 100 South Street. (973) 539-8008; mayoarts.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum “Salut D’Amour,” with the sisters Min and Yoon Kwon. April 10 at 3 p.m. $5 to $15. Jane Voorhees Zimmerli Art Museum, 71 Hamilton Street. (732) 932-7237; zimmerlimuseum.rutgers.edu.

NEW BRUNSWICK Nicholas Music Center,Mason Gross Performing Arts Center Rutgers Symphony Orchestra presents “Unforgettable Mahler,” featuring Gustav Mahler’s Symphony No. 10 and his “Das Lied von der Erde” with alto and tenor, conducted by Kynan Johns. April 15 at 8 p.m. $15 to $25. Kirkpatrick Choir, Bach’s B Minor Mass, conducted by Patrick Gardner. April 16 at 8 p.m. $5 to $10. Nicholas Music Center, Mason Gross Performing Arts Center, 85 George Street. (732) 932-7511; www.masongross.rutgers.edu.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “Remember Me,” presented by Parsons Dance and the East Village Opera Company. Thursday at 8 p.m. $32 to $52. The New Jersey Symphony Orchestra with Vadim Gluzman, violin, perform works by Barber, Roumain and Tchaikovsky. April 9 at 8 p.m. Tickets: njsymphony.org; (973) 624-3713. $20 to $82. Ethan Bortnick and His Musical Time Machine. April 10 at 3 p.m. $20 to $35. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

NEWARK Prudential Center Ricky Martin, pop. April 16 at 8 p.m. $33.50 to $128.50. Prudential Center, 165 Mulberry Street. (973) 757-6000; prucenter.com.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center The New Jersey Symphony Orchestra with Vadim Gluzman, violin, perform works by Barber, Roumain and Tchaikovsky. Thursday, Friday and April 10. $20 to $82. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. njsymphony.org; (973) 624-3713.

NEWARK Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center Nashville Hitmakers, featuring Brett Jones, Tony Lane, David Lee and Wynn Varble. April 9 at 8 p.m. $32. Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. (888) 466-5722; njpac.org.

SUMMIT The Oakes Center “Songs for a New World.” A collection of songs by Jason Robert Brown. Through April 9. $20. The Oakes Center, 120 Morris Avenue. (973) 275-9836.

SUMMIT Unitarian Church in Summit Afternoon Music presents Sun Young Chang, soprano, Tia Roper-Penn, flutist, Carlo Pellettieri, cellist, and Mitchell Vines, pianist, playing Ravel’s “Chanson madécasses” and vocal and instrumental works by Haydn, Bach, Debussy, Mozart and Villa-Lobos. April 10 at 4 p.m. $15 and $20. Unitarian Church in Summit, 4 Waldron Avenue. (908) 273-3245; ucsummit.org.

TEANECK Puffin Cultural Forum “Klezmer Travels the World.” David Glukh Klezmer Ensemble performs updated klezmer repertoire and musical fusions. Friday. $10. EastRiver Ensemble, Chinese folk music with traditional instruments, costumes and props, culminating in an acrobatic act of the Monkey King. April 9 at 8 p.m. $10. The Greg Abate Jazz Quartet, songs from the American Songbook tradition and bebop standards with Bill Cunliffe, piano, Harvie S, bass, and Steve Johns, drums. April 16 at 8 p.m. $10. “Classical Sundays at the Puffin,” with international harp soloist Suzanna Klincharova performing works by the French and Russian composers Glinka, Saënt-Sains, Prokofiev, Kandov, Pierné, Caplet and Glière, and a piece for harp and electronics. April 17 at 4 p.m. $10. Puffin Cultural Forum, 20 Puffin Way. puffinculturalforum.org; (201) 836-3499.

WEST LONG BRANCH Pollak Theater, Monmouth University The Klezmatics, a neo-klezmer group. April 10 at 3 p.m. $33. Pollak Theater, Monmouth University, 400 Cedar Avenue. monmouth.edu/arts_events; (732) 263-6889.

Spoken Word

EDISON New Jersey Convention Center “The Mind Body Spirit Expo,” including lectures with Deepak Chopra, Doreen Virtue, clairvoyant, and Lisa Williams, natural health advocate. Friday, 4 to 9 p.m. $7 and $15. New Jersey Convention Center, 79 Sunfield Avenue. (215) 627-0102.

MONTCLAIR Alexander Kasser Theater Brainstorm symposium. “Dance Engaging Science,” with David Kirsh. April 12 at 1 p.m. Free. “Tools for the Imagination,” with Scott deLahunta and Philip Barnard. April 12 at 2:15 p.m. Free. “Thinking Out Loud,” with Robert Whitman, Elizabeth Streb and Michael Gordon. April 12 at 3:15 p.m. Free. Alexander Kasser Theater, 1 Normal Avenue. (973) 655-5112; peakperfs.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater Smart Talk Connected Conversations: Liz Murray, inspirational speaker. April 12 at 7:30 p.m. $25 to $75. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. statetheatrenj.org; (732) 246-7469.

NEWARK The Newark Museum “Biography and Autobiography: A Public Conversation,” with the artist Gonkar Gyatso about biographical narratives in contemporary and traditional Tibetan art. Thursday at 7 p.m. $12 and $15; $40 and $60 series fee. The Newark Museum, 49 Washington Street. newarkmuseum.org; (973) 596-6550.

Theater

ATLANTIC HIGHLANDS First Avenue Playhouse “Love, Sex and the I.R.S.,” comedy by Billy Van Zandt and Jane Milmore. Through April 30. $15 and $20. First Avenue Playhouse, 123 First Avenue. firstavenueplayhouse.com; (732) 291-7552.

CAPE MAY Emlen Physick Estate “Murder at the Physick Estate,” mystery by Ray Crew. Through April 30. $20 and $25. Emlen Physick Estate, 1048 Washington Street. capemaymac.org; (800) 275-4278.

HOBOKEN The Hudson School Performance Space “The Last Night of Ballyhoo,” comedy by Alfred Uhry. Friday through April 16. $15 to $18. The Hudson School Performance Space, 601 Park Avenue. thehudsonschool.com; (201) 659-8335.

LONG BRANCH New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater “Puma,” by Julie Gilbert and Frank Evans. April 3. $25 to $40. New Jersey Repertory Company, Lumia Theater, 179 Broadway. (732) 229-3166; njrep.org.

MADISON F.M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University Lend Us Your Ears: Play Reading Series: “The Notorious Lady Susan” by Jane Austen, adapted by Tom Fontana. April 4 at 7 p.m. $10 and $15. F. M. Kirby Shakespeare Theater, Drew University, 36 Madison Avenue. njshakespeare.org; (973) 408-5600.

MIDDLETOWN Navesink Library Theater “Rumors,” by Neil Simon. April 9 through April 30. $17; students and seniors, $14. Navesink Library Theater, Sears and Monmouth Avenues. (732) 291-9211; monmouthplayers.org.

MILLBURN Paper Mill Playhouse “A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Forum,” with music and lyrics by Stephen Sondheim and book by Burt Shevelove and Larry Gelbart. Through April 10. $25 to $92. Paper Mill Playhouse, 22 Brookside Drive. (973) 376-4343; papermill.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK George Street Playhouse “The Fox on the Fairway,” by Ken Ludwig. Through April 17. $26.50 to $63.50. George Street Playhouse, 9 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7717; gsponline.org.

NEW BRUNSWICK Philip J. Levin Theater “Fires in the Mirror.” Drama by Anna Deavere Smith. April 15 through April 30. $15 to $25. Philip J. Levin Theater, 85 George Street. mgsa.rutgers.edu; (732) 932-7511.

NEW BRUNSWICK State Theater “Ethel Merman’s Broadway,” a musical with Rita McKenzie. April 3 at 3 p.m. $22 to $57. State Theater, 15 Livingston Avenue. (732) 246-7469; statetheatrenj.org.

NEWARK Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center “Monty Python’s Spamalot,” musical comedy. Through April 3. $24 to $90. Prudential Hall, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. njpac.org; (888) 466-5722.

NEWARK Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center “Celebrity Autobiography,” comedy, celebrity memoirs acted out by a rotating cast. Friday at 8 p.m. $45 and $60. Victoria Theater, New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street. njpac.org; (888) 466-5722.

POMPTON LAKES Smiling Rhino Theater “Cabaret,” by Joe Masteroff, music by John Kander and lyrics by Fred Ebb. Through April 9. $20 to $25. Smiling Rhino Theater, 247 Wanaque Avenue. (973) 248-9491; smilingrhinotheatre.com.

PRINCETON McCarter Theater Center “The Select (The Sun Also Rises),” presented by Elevator Service Repair. April 15 and 16. $15. McCarter Theater Center, 91 University Place. mccarter.org; (609) 258-2787.

RED BANK Count Basie Theater “Annie,” presented by Phoenix Productions. Friday through April 17. $22 to $29. Count Basie Theater, 99 Monmouth Street. (732) 842-9000; countbasietheatre.org.

RED BANK Two River Theater “Candida,” by George Bernard Shaw, directed by David Staller. Through April 10. $24 to $60. Two River Theater, 21 Bridge Avenue. (732) 345-1400; trtc.org.

SOUTH ORANGE South Orange Performing Arts Center “Tartuffe,” comedy by Molière. Through April 10. $15. South Orange Performing Arts Center, 1 Sopac Way. sopacnow.org; (973) 313-2787.

WEST ORANGE Luna Stage “The Tallest Building in the World,” by Matt Schatz, directed by Troy Miller. April 14 through May 15. $20 to $30. Luna Stage, 555 Valley Road. lunastage.org; (973) 395-5551.

WEST WINDSOR Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College “Steel Magnolias.” April 3. $10 to $14. “Hello, Dolly!” Tony Award-winning musical comedy based on a 1938 farce play by Thornton Wilder, presented by Playful Theater Productions. Friday through April 17. $12 to $16. Kelsey Theater, West Windsor Campus, Mercer County Community College, 1200 Old Trenton Road. (609) 570-3333; kelseyatmccc.org.
